# Recruiting

## Overview

Recruiting is the long-horizon layer of youth coaching nobody asked you to take on, and yet — if you are coaching 14U at a nationally-recognized club — families are already asking. Who do we send film to? Can D1 coaches talk to us yet? Do we have to go to AAU Nationals? A 14U coach is not a recruiting coordinator, but you are the family's nearest authoritative voice.

The US system is unusual relative to most volleyball nations. Most countries develop elite athletes through pro-club pipelines (Brazilian SuperLiga, Italian Serie A, Turkish and European clubs). In the US, college is the developmental pipeline. Scholarships concentrate in women's NCAA Division I: women's volleyball is a "headcount" sport, so a D1 program has twelve full scholarships, each awarded to one athlete, and those twelve slots anchor the national recruiting economy. D2, D3, NAIA, and JUCO each offer meaningfully different scholarship, academic, and competitive profiles — a candid fit conversation across that ladder is among the most valuable things a club coach supplies.

Four dimensions frame the decision: **pathway** (which tier and programs match the athlete), **timeline** (when contact, commits, visits, and signing happen), **exposure** (which tournaments, camps, and film get an athlete seen), and **academic fit** (at D3 and the Ivy League these functionally gate eligibility before athletic fit is considered). 14U players at national-club programs move through exposure early via AAU, JVA, and USAV Nationals; the other three hold weight from about age fifteen forward, but understanding the architecture now prevents bad decisions later. For D1 women's volleyball specifically, coaches may not initiate communication with a recruit before **June 15 after sophomore year**, and unofficial and official visits begin **August 1 before junior year** [usav-nd-college-recruitment-timeline] — this is the current Bylaw 13 floor and differs from the "September 1 of junior year" rule that applies to several other D1 sports. NIL, transfer-portal, and dead-period rules continue to shift; verify any specific date against current NCAA.org publications [ncaa-2025-26-d1-wvb-recruiting-calendar].

## Major subtopics

- **College pathway — D1 / D2 / D3 / NAIA / JUCO** — D1 women's: twelve full scholarships (headcount). D2: equivalency (eight-max, usually split as partial awards). D3: no athletic aid — financial aid is need- and merit-based, entirely compatible at NESCAC, UAA, MIT, and Ivy schools with serious volleyball. NAIA varies widely. JUCO is legitimate for late-developing athletes, academic rebuilds, and international athletes navigating the US system.
- **NCAA timeline (D1 women's volleyball)** — coaches may first initiate communication **June 15 after sophomore year**; unofficial and official visits begin **August 1 before junior year**, with a **five-official-visit** lifetime cap [usav-nd-college-recruitment-timeline]. The NCAA compliance calendar then overlays contact / quiet / dead periods on the full year: the 2025-26 fall contact period runs September 1 - November 30 (broken by a November 10-13 AVCA-Convention dead period); December 1 - January 15 is a quiet period with an embedded dead period around the NCAA Championship; January 16 - July 31 is a spring contact period with quiet-period blocks on March / April / May club-tournament weekends [ncaa-2025-26-d1-wvb-recruiting-calendar]. Written-offer signing for women's volleyball (under "All Other D1 and D2 Sports") has a single initial signing date — **November 12, 2025** for the 2025-26 cycle — with finalization per institutional policy; the old NLI early-November-plus-April-regular split is specific to basketball under the current post-2024 framework [ncaa-2025-26-signing-dates].
- **Tournaments that matter** — AAU Nationals (Orlando, late June / mid-July) is the marquee by college-coach attendance. USAV Girls' Junior Nationals (rotates, late June / early July) is the federation pipeline event and overlaps HP season. JVA World Challenge (Louisville) and JVA Championships are the JVA showcases. Triple Crown NIT and Crossroads National Qualifier are marquee winter-spring qualifiers. Targeting matters more than volume: the gap between "tournament exists" and "right coaches are watching your rotation" is large.
- **Film standards** — recruiters want a short, curated, position-specific highlight reel plus recent full-match film access. Five to ten rallies per skill, labeled, recent, athlete identifiable (arrow, circle, jersey color). Libero: pass + dig. Outside: attack + pass + serve. Middle: block + slide/quick. Setter: hands + jump-set + transition decisions. Full-match film matters secondarily — coaches watch it after the highlight earns attention. Height, wingspan, approach-jump touch, and standing reach round out the physical-measurement set.
- **Coach communication** — concise, factually-dense emails beat mass-blasts. A good outreach has jersey + position, club team and schedule, graduation year, GPA and test scores where required, height / wingspan / touch, two or three film links, and a program-specific statement of interest. Unofficial visits: any time, family-funded. Official visits: time-limited, program-paid, junior year onward.
- **Camps & clinics** — on-campus camps at target schools are the highest-leverage exposure investment after age sixteen: head coach and assistants in the gym, athlete in the program's environment. A $400–$800 camp is a far denser exposure unit than a $6,000 travel-club season once you're at a national level.
- **Academic fit** — GPA, course rigor (AP / IB / honors), and SAT / ACT where still required define the envelope. Ivy and high-academic D3 (NESCAC, UAA, Patriot) operate under institutional academic bands that function as gates: the coach's admissions support is real but bounded. Starting in 9th grade compounds.
- **Post-college pathway (women's pro era)** — for the first time in US women's volleyball history, an athlete signing in 2025+ has a domestic professional pathway after college that pays a meaningful salary. Three competing US-based pro leagues now overlap: **[[lovb]]** (League One Volleyball, six teams, tour-and-residency format, 2025 inaugural season), **[[pvf]]** (Pro Volleyball Federation, seven teams, season-table format, 2024 inaugural with 6-of-7 women HCs), and **[[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]]** (player-led weekly-draft, individual scoring, launched 2021, rebranded Championship in 2025). For families weighing scholarship offers and program fit, the post-college horizon is no longer "play in Europe or stop playing" — flag this in any 16U-or-older pathway conversation and let the family decide how much weight to give the domestic-pro option.

## Schools of thought

- [[usa-volleyball]] — HP pathway (Regional HP tryouts → National HP training → Junior NT → Collegiate NT) runs alongside collegiate recruiting; USAV Girls' Junior Nationals and HP clinics overlap substantially with college-coach attendance.
- [[russ-rose]] — seven NCAA titles at Penn State; his "rules for club season" frames the effort, practice-standard, and chemistry baseline college programs recruit into.
- [[john-dunning]] — three NCAA titles at Stanford; "care as foundation of credibility" is especially relevant for high-academic D1 where coach-relationship carries long-horizon weight.
- [[mary-wise]] — 33 seasons at Florida; "help every player find and hone their superpower" is also a recruiting philosophy — programs that recruit for specific roles produce longer college careers.
- [[karch-kiraly]] — USA Women's NT head coach 2012–2024, USA Men's from 2024; his reads-every-skill and culture frames inform what national staff look for beyond volume stats.
- [[john-speraw]] — men's pathway is meaningfully different (far fewer D1 programs, smaller scholarship pool); Speraw's First Point Volleyball work is the best modern reference for men's recruiting.
- [[kelly-sheffield]] / [[jerritt-elliott]] / [[john-cook]] / [[dan-fisher]] — the four NCAA D1 women's program builders whose recruiting profiles, transfer-portal usage, and program-identity emphasis define what current recruiting looks like at the modern dynasty tier. Sheffield (Wisconsin), Elliott (Texas), Cook (Nebraska 2000-2024), Fisher (Pittsburgh) each recruit to a distinct positional profile — Wisconsin to serve-and-pass, Texas to pin-hitter physicality, Nebraska historically to home-grown Midwest character, Pittsburgh to relentless transfer-portal augmentation. Knowing each program's pattern shapes the fit conversation.

## Getting started

If you are a club coach supporting a family starting to think about college:

1. [[aoc-2023-rose-club-rules]] — the effort and team culture Rose describes is what college programs recruit into; club seasons are recruiting auditions even when nobody is framing them that way.
2. [[wise-2023-characterandleadership-profile]] — Wise's "superpower" frame as recruiting philosophy: the match is between a player's specific strengths and a program's specific needs, not a generic ranking for a generic slot.
3. [[dunning-2016-aoc-difference-maker]] — Dunning's Fremont HS → Pacific → Stanford arc; a recruit actually signs up for a multi-year coach-athlete relationship.
4. [[speraw-2024-usav-profile]] — the men's-side pathway for boys' volleyball futures.
5. [[usav-2026-hp-clinic-lessons-shared]] — the HP pipeline and "generalized specialists" frame national staff use to evaluate athletes.
6. [[usav-2026-coach-academy]] — Bronze/Silver/Gold curriculum; the Heart pillar covers parent communication, which is most of recruiting support at 14U–16U.
7. NCAA.org — the current recruiting calendar. Do not rely on any specific date claim in this wiki without verification; rules have evolved rapidly since 2018–2019.
