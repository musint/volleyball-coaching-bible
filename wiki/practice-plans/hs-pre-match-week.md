# HS Pre-Match Week (Mid-Season Microcycle)

## Context

The canonical HS varsity mid-season microcycle for a single-match week — Tue-Fri on-court, Friday match-eve activation, Mon reset. Assumes 2-2.5 hour weeknight practices, one Friday match, no Wednesday match. For two-match weeks, compress Tue install, drop Wed tactical to a shakeout, keep Thu walk-through. See [[age-lens-hs]] for season-context and ceilings, and [[practice-planning]] for the methodology underneath the choices below. Six hours of practice load before the whistle.

## Week objectives

- Reset the body Monday so Tuesday opens with the team fresh, not flat.
- Refine one or two technical priorities Tuesday in a focused install block — not a rebuild.
- Translate the scouting brief into rehearsed tactical patterns Wednesday.
- Walk through every serve-receive rotation Thursday with serve-receive sharpening.
- Activate Friday with [[hs-60min-match-day-activation]] — primed body, sharp first contact, no surprises.

## Day-by-day plan

### Monday — Recovery + film (off-court or 30 min light)

Off-court is the default. Optional 30-minute team film session if last week had a match: review the scouted opponent's tendencies (top 2 servers, blocker tendencies, setter location preference) so Tuesday's install is already framed. No on-court work — Monday belongs to the body. The Sanders 2025 jump-load evidence is the operational anchor: high-intensity jumps drive next-day fatigue more than total jump count, so the tightest taper-control lever the HS coach has is intensity, not volume [sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball]. Players run their own Hooper-style wellness check and report any persistent soreness [rebelo-2024-training-stress-fatigue-wellbeing].

### Tuesday — Technical install (120 min)

Open with 15 min dynamic warm-up. Skill block (50 min): [[pass-set-hit]] for 25 min — three-person unit reps building the in-system spine — then [[three-setter-rotation]] for 25 min so all three setters get matched reps at high-4, 31, and back-2 (bench depth matters this late in the season). Strategic block (35 min): [[read-blocking-progression]] phases 1-2, twelve minutes deep, then a six-on-six cooperative rally where the scoring rule is "two consecutive clean reads = wash." Close with 15 min cool-down + a one-cue install summary on the whiteboard. The Tuesday install rule: one technical priority gets a fresh look; everything else is reinforcement, not rebuild [aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently].

### Wednesday — Match-prep tactical (120 min)

Warm-up 15 min. Tactical block (90 min): the scouting brief becomes drills. [[zone-serving]] for 15 min with the zones called from the scouting report (target the opponent's weakest passers) — opens the day with serve-pressure as the lead tactical lever. Then 30 min of opponent-look 6v6: coach feeds the opponent's top two attack patterns, defense rehearses the called rotation. Then 30 min wash drill where the scoring rule rewards executing the called sub or rotation switch. Close with 15 min server-vs-passer competition where servers earn points for landing in the scouting target zones. Cool-down 15 min. Rose's compete-every-rep frame is the layer underneath all of it [aoc-2023-kiraly-competing-in-practice], [aoc-2023-rose-club-rules].

### Thursday — Rotation walk-through + serve-receive (90 min)

Shorter session by design — Thursday is sharpening, not loading. Warm-up 10 min. Rotation walk-through (30 min): each of the six SR rotations gets a 5-minute block — pre-call from the captain, dry-rep pass-set-hit, named opponent-tendency reminder, two live-serve reps. Serve-receive competition (35 min): live opposing servers, score by passer grade, run all six rotations once. Close with 15 min light setter-hitter timing and cool-down. The volume budget is intentional — Friday is a match, not a Saturday tournament, so Thursday cannot tax the legs [usav-2009-cap-game-like-training].

### Friday — Match-day activation (60 min)

Use the [[hs-60min-match-day-activation]] template wholesale. Morning or pre-match-window slot. The session is rehearsal, not training: warm-up, light skill stations, rotation walk-through, brief competition, cool-down. Players walk out in match mindset.

### Saturday — Match (or off if Friday match)

Standard match flow.

### Sunday — Off

No on-court. Players rest.

## Volume + intensity targets

Roughly 360 minutes on-court across Tue-Fri. Tuesday is the heaviest jump-load day (target 80-120 high-intensity jumps for front-row players); Wednesday tactical adds moderate jump volume (60-90); Thursday trims to 30-50; Friday is activation only (under 30 high-intensity). Front-row players accumulate more jump volume than back-row — track and balance [sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball]. Daily session-RPE should trend Tue 7-8, Wed 7, Thu 5-6, Fri 3-4 [rebelo-2024-training-stress-fatigue-wellbeing].

## Drill clusters

- **Technical spine:** [[pass-set-hit]] + [[three-setter-rotation]] anchor Tuesday — first contact and setter depth.
- **Tactical block:** [[read-blocking-progression]] (Tue strategic) + [[zone-serving]] (Wed tactical) — translate scouting into rehearsed patterns.
- **Match-day activation:** see [[hs-60min-match-day-activation]] for the Friday slate.
