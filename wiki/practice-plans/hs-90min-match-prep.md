# HS 90-Minute Match Prep

## Context

The Thursday-night practice before a Friday league match during HS varsity fall. You have a scouting brief — a serve target, one attacker to block, one rotation you expect to siege — and 90 minutes to install the plan into muscle memory without overwriting what got you here. Minimal new technique; heavy repetition on the scout-flagged tendencies; a competition block that rehearses the opponent's likeliest looks. See [[match-prep]] for the framework and [[age-lens-hs]] for how scouting-depth scales to HS resources.

## Learning objectives

- Servers execute the primary scout target at 70%+ in-zone under pressure
- Blockers read the opponent's #1 attacker's tells — approach angle, tip frequency, slide vs. quick
- Passers drill the opponent's top two servers by simulating serve-type and placement
- Setter-attacker connections rehearse the call-set menu against their block
- Team transitions scout-target → first-contact defense → designed play in one continuous rep

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (10 min)

[[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — standard dynamic series plus ball-in-hand tosses. Brisk: this is match-eve, not base-building. Jump warm-up optional — save plyometric load for match night.

### Skill development (20 min, scout-target)

[[pass-set-hit]] (8 min) — unit rhythm at match tempo. Cadence, not correction.

[[zone-serving]] (12 min) — drill the scout target. Pick two zones from the opponent's weakest passers; servers call the zone aloud before each rep. The scouting report becomes muscle memory [aoc-2024-rose-best-servers]. Any server over 25% miss-rate gets pulled for a breath-and-reset.

### Strategic (30 min, opponent plays)

[[three-setter-rotation]] (12 min) — rehearse the call-set menu rotation-by-rotation against their block. If their middle sells out to the slide, run more 31 and back-slide. If their pins stay home, attack the seam. The setter needs to know which call wins which rotation before the whistle.

[[read-blocking-progression]] (18 min) — the most important block of this practice. Use Phase 2 (live in-system) and Phase 3 (disguised tempo), stipulating opponent tendencies: if their #1 hits line 65% of the time, your pin blocker's first step is line-commit. Verbalize misreads — reading is fragile, and pressure will degrade it first [aoc-2024-kiraly-reads-every-skill][aoc-2026-bunch-read-blocking].

### Competition (25 min)

[[serve-receive-competition]] (12 min) — passers vs. servers with the scout constraint. Servers win on any ball in the target zone; passers win on any in-system pass (2.5+ on a 3-point scale). 8-point games, rotate servers each game. The side-out imperative carries through [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep].

[[six-player-defense]] (13 min) — full 6v6 with coach feeding the opponent's expected attack patterns. Assign blockers to hitters. Bonus points for defending the look you expect most (slide, line shot, high-ball bail). The dress rehearsal.

### Cool-down (5 min)

Jog, static stretch, team huddle. Coach previews warm-up timing, bus call, and the first-serve target zone. Players walk out knowing exactly what they will do.

## Coaching cues

- "Call the zone, then aim the zone" — tactical intent before mechanics on every serve
- "Ball, setter, ball, hitter" — eye-progression on blocking reads
- "Give them a chance" — the server's job is to put the team in position, not ace [aoc-2024-kiraly-serving-pressure]
- "First step wins the block" — commit foot on the ball leaving the setter's hands
- "Own your rotation" — each passer owns their zone; seams are pre-negotiated
- "Scouting changes decisions, not feelings" — the plan is information, not emotion
- "Next point" — between reps, between games, between mistakes

## Variations

- **Team size (12+):** Run two skill stations parallel during the 20-minute block; rotate. Keep 6v6 competition at full size.
- **Intensity:** If legs are heavy, cut zone-serving to 8 minutes and trim competition by 5. Match-eve volume should never cost you Friday.
- **Duration (120 min):** Add a 15-minute video block between warm-up and skill — opponent's top attacker and top server together. Add 10 minutes to competition.

## Adaptations by level

- **14U:** Drop read-blocking-progression (reading skill isn't reliably in place — use dedicated-pin alignment). Shorten strategic to 20 min, add 10 min to competition. See [[age-lens-14u]].
- **College:** Phase 3 only on read-blocking-progression. Add a 10-min video tag-along during six-player-defense for immediate review. Stats are decision-grade in-match, not just diagnostic [aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most][aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics].
