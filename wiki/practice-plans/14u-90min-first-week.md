# 14U · 90 min · First Week of Preseason

## Context

Run this plan in the opening week of a 14U club season, when roster chemistry is new, skill identity is undefined, and you need to establish tone more than install tactics. The focus is **composite**: every fundamental skill touches the ball, but the priority is ball-control reps, a shared vocabulary for passing and setting, and a visible install of base rotations. Rose's club-season framing applies — early-season is for chemistry and effort standards, not mid-season form [aoc-2023-rose-club-rules]. See [[age-lens-14u]] for the broader developmental lens and [[practice-planning]] for the methodology this plan inherits.

## Learning objectives

- Every athlete completes at least 40 controlled passing contacts (butterfly + warm-up combined).
- Setters demonstrate the hand-hinge and side-of-ball contact on ≥70% of reps in [[target-setting]].
- Team executes base rotation 1 (serve-receive and base defense) without a whiteboard by end of practice.
- Every server lands ≥5 of 10 standing-float serves into a named zone during [[serve-targets]].
- Team reaches 15 consecutive in-system sequences in [[cooperative-25-goal]] (the 14U cooperative target).

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (15 min)

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (8 min). Ball-in-hand dynamic sequence — every movement holds a ball, so proprioception and warm-up happen together. No standing lines.
- [[ball-control-warmup]] (7 min). Partner-based pass / set / control reps at low intensity. Introduce the pass/set vocabulary you will use all season (platform, midline, hand-hinge, elbows-in).

### Skill development (40 min)

- [[butterfly-passing]] (15 min). Continuous pass-catch-feed rotation; every rep ends with the athlete transitioning to the next station. Coach calls out the 4 keys cue words after each rep group, not every rep.
- [[target-setting]] (15 min). Split setters to a second court or a dedicated station; other athletes pair for simultaneous [[setting-hands]] hand-hinge reps against a wall or partner. Setters hit front, back, and high-ball targets — count makes, announce the total aloud.
- [[serve-targets]] (10 min). Standing float only this week. Call a zone before each serve; keep a personal counter. No jump serves, no topspin — the install is toss consistency and lift/step/swing tempo.

### Strategic — rotation install (15 min)

Walk the team through base rotation 1 in slow motion. Show serve-receive positions, base defense positions, and the overlap rules for that rotation. Use whiteboard diagrams once, then walk positions live two times, then run a freeze-frame at each server change for three full rotations. No ball yet — this is a vocabulary and geography install. Cue "overlap only matters at contact." Scaffold to rotations 2–6 in later sessions; one rotation this week is plenty [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners].

### Competition — cooperative (15 min)

- [[cooperative-25-goal]] (15 min). Target is 15 this week (not 25); attacks allowed as roll shots. Coach enters free balls; team passes, sets, attacks with counter visible. Miss resets the counter — but you own the restart energy. Celebrate the reset out loud [usav-2009-cap-game-like-training]. This is the competitive closer that anchors the season's FeedForward and "catch them doing it right" culture [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules].

### Cool-down (5 min)

Light mobility — calves, hips, shoulders. Team circle: each athlete names one thing a teammate did well today. The circle is the culture install.

## Coaching cues

- "Read first, platform second" — establish that reading is part of every skill from day one.
- "Shuffle, don't reach" — centering the ball on passing, not extending to it.
- "Hand-hinge at the forehead" — setter contact cue introduced on rep one.
- "Toss is the serve" — drill standing-float toss as its own skill before the swing matters.
- "Reset, we're going again" — language for a broken [[cooperative-25-goal]] count; no sighing, no scolding.
- "Overlap only matters at contact" — frees athletes to move naturally between whistle and serve.
- "Half-speed on the count" — attackers in cooperative drills swing at ~50% so ball-control drives the rep.

## Variations

- **Team size.** With 8 or fewer athletes, drop [[butterfly-passing]] to shuttle-style and run [[target-setting]] as one group.
- **Intensity.** If the group is gassed from combine/tryout, cut [[cooperative-25-goal]] to 10 minutes and extend cool-down.
- **Duration.** To compress to 75 min, cut skill development to 30 and the rotation install to 10. To extend to 120 min, add a [[two-line-passing]] block after butterfly and a second 10-minute rotation-install cycle.

## Adaptations by level

- **One level down (12U or true-beginner 14U):** drop jump-setting cues, stay in [[offense-6-6]] rotation install, and target 10 in [[cooperative-25-goal]]. Most athletes need more reps on standing-float toss alone before tracking zones.
- **One level up (HS first-week):** replace rotation-install with base offense install (4-2 or simple 6-2), add [[jump-warmup]], extend [[serve-targets]] to include zone constraints (seam 1, deep 5), and target 25 in [[cooperative-25-goal]] at full swing speed. GMS's practice-structure framing applies — a random-practice ratio that a HS group can handle exceeds what most 14Us can absorb in week one [gms-nd-structure-practice].
