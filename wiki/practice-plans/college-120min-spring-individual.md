# College 120-minute Spring Individual Development Practice

## Context

This is a spring-semester practice for an NCAA program in the supplemental window — late February through April, well after the championship and before summer. The roster is healthy, the system is installed, and the calendar finally allows the luxury most competitive seasons don't: individual skill development as the headline, not a residual. You run it two to three times per week in blocks where the strategic layer is deliberately thin so returners can refine position-specific ceilings (a middle's slide footwork, an opposite's line-angle read, a setter's jump-set release point) and freshmen can bank the reps that in-season minutes don't afford. The design draws on Kiraly's principle that practice minutes are a scarce resource — warmups are practice, not pre-practice, and random/random-blocked mixing beats pure blocked for transfer [[aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently]]. See [[age-lens-college]] for the postseason/spring planning frame and [[physical]] for the S&C integration context. This is not a conditioning session; plyometric and jumping volume are programmed by the S&C staff — your job is to supervise quality within their envelope.

## Learning objectives

- Each position-group athlete executes 80+ quality position-specific reps (setter jump-sets, attacker line-vs-angle shots, blocker swing-block footwork sequences, server jump-serve tempo reps).
- Attackers finish 4 of 5 shots to the called zone (line or angle) against a live two-player block at match tempo.
- Setters deliver 7 of 10 jump-sets to tempo-1 or tempo-2 targets from Zone 2/3 out-of-system starts.
- Blockers show clean swing-blocking footwork on 8 of 10 shuffle reps — loaded inside foot, arm swing leading elevation, hands penetrating the plane.
- Every athlete ends the practice having completed the staff-cleared [[arm-care-routine]] before leaving the gym.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (15 min)

Start with 10 minutes of [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — ball-in-hand mobility, platform shape, shoulder activation, and partner contacts. Follow with 5 minutes of [[jump-warmup]] sequenced to prime the nervous system for the position-jump volume coming in the skill block. Keep the volume at preparation levels, not conditioning. Warmups count; no standing lines.

### Individual skill development (55 min)

Split the gym into four position-group stations rotating on 13-minute cycles. Setters run [[jump-setting-progression]] — footwork-to-release rehearsal, then live balls from a passer, then out-of-system sets from Zones 2 and 3. Attackers (OH and Opp) run [[hitting-vs-block]] against a two-player live block, cueing shot selection off the block's shape. Middles and pin blockers run [[swing-block-shuffle]] — committed swing-block footwork with arm-lead movement against a live outside set [[aoc-2024-slabe-blocking]][[aoc-2024-importance-teaching-swing-blocking]]. Servers (rotating role, not a fixed position) run [[jump-serve-progression]] — toss consistency, contact point, then zone-targeted live serves. Treat this as random practice within station — mix tempos, mix targets, mix outcomes [[gms-nd-structure-practice]].

### Strategic (15 min)

Narrow to one rotation-specific installation — typically a new hybrid-5-1 wrinkle, a modified swing-block scheme, or a specific out-of-system setter release. Walk through the concept on a whiteboard for 3 minutes, run at half tempo for 5, then at match tempo for 7. Goal is understanding and rep, not a full system install.

### Competition (25 min)

Small-sided format: 3v3 or 4v4 with position-matched crossings — OH vs MB at one station, Opp vs pin blocker at another, live setter feeds. Play to 15 with wash rules so both sides have to close. Attackers call line or angle before the swing; blockers call their shuffle direction. Shot-quality feedback is immediate — coach names technique, placement, contact, or outcome in under 10 seconds, then reset. This is [[line-vs-angle-shot]] under live pressure.

### Cool-down (10 min)

Start with 5 minutes of [[reactive-jumping]] at low volume under S&C-cleared programming — quality-first, not conditioning-grind. Finish with [[arm-care-routine]] for all hitters and servers before the gym clears. Per McCutcheon's framing, championship behaviors include the unglamorous habits that compound over a four-year arc [[mccutcheon-2022-championship-behaviors]].

**Medical and S&C disclaimer.** The [[jump-warmup]] and [[reactive-jumping]] blocks prepare the athlete and train reactive quality — they are not a plyometric or conditioning program. Plyometric volume, frequency, progression, and exercise selection belong with your CSCS-credentialed strength coach and athletic training staff. Athletes with growth-plate, joint, tendon, or recent-injury concerns must be cleared before participating. Defer to sports-medicine professionals on individual loads; this plan supervises quality within their envelope, not outside it. See [[physical]].

## Coaching cues

- "Reps at the ceiling, not the floor" — spring is for refining, not rehearsing what's already mastered.
- "Random beats blocked" — mix tempos, targets, and outcomes within station.
- "Call before you swing" — attackers name line or angle before the approach.
- "Load the inside foot" — swing-blockers show clean arm-swing-led elevation.
- "Release at the top" — setters jump-set on the way up, not at the peak.
- "Quality, then volume" — stop the station when form breaks; don't chase rep counts into sloppy.
- "Arm care before you leave" — no exceptions; it's part of practice, not optional.

## Variations

- **Team size knob:** with 10 or fewer, collapse to two stations (attack + block; set + serve) on 20-minute cycles; keep the same 55-minute skill block.
- **Intensity knob:** if the roster is mid-block-fatigue, cut [[reactive-jumping]] entirely, reduce [[jump-serve-progression]] to non-jump zone serving, and extend [[arm-care-routine]] to 15 minutes.
- **Duration knob:** compress to 90 minutes by trimming warm-up to 10, skill block to 40, strategic to 10, competition to 20, and cool-down to 10.

## Adaptations by level

- **Down to HS:** shorten station cycles to 10 minutes, drop the hybrid rotation install in the strategic block in favor of a straight 5-1 refinement, and cap reactive-jumping at one short block under the school AT's clearance. See [[age-lens-hs]] and [[hs-120min-postseason-development]] for the HS postseason pattern.
- **Up to pro / national-team:** extend individual skill to 70 minutes with video-tagged feedback cued to each station, replace small-sided competition with live 6v6 rotation-specific wash, and integrate a second short plyo block if S&C programming calls for it.
