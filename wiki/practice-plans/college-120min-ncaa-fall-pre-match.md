# College 120-Minute NCAA Fall Pre-Match Practice

## Context

This is a mid-week, in-season practice for an NCAA Division I-to-III women's program in the 24-to-48 hour window before a conference match. Scouting is finished, the match-up plan is written, and the job now is to rehearse the plan under competitive load without burning the legs you need on Friday. You run this the day after film review and the day before the match-day activation session — see [[age-lens-college]] for the full microcycle it sits inside, and [[match-prep]] for the underlying stats-and-film frame the strategic block operationalizes. The competition block is load-bearing: 45 minutes of rotation-specific and scored 6v6 is where the plan either survives contact with live volleyball or gets adjusted before the opponent gets a vote.

## Learning objectives

- Side-out at 65%+ across all six rotations in the scored 6v6 block, with rotation-specific attack-efficiency holds for hot rotations.
- Execute the scouted serving plan — zone targets on the opponent's two weakest passers, three jump-serves per server per block — with error rate under 15%.
- Block and defend the opponent's primary pin attacker to a sub-.250 efficiency in the 6v6 block using the match-day block scheme (bunch-read with a pin-commit lever).
- Transition off 3+ first-contact digs into kill-or-better attacks per rotation, with the setter distributing per the scouted tendency plan.
- Close compressed-score situations (22-22 and 23-23 starts) on serve discipline rather than on late hero swings — championship behaviors tested under pressure.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (15 min)

Open with ball-in-hand [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] for 8 minutes — volleyball-specific movement and contacts, not generic conditioning — then 7 minutes of [[jump-warmup]] with med-ball hops, approach-jump blocks, and attack jumps to priming thresholds. Minutes count; warmups are practice, not pre-practice.

### Skill development (20 min)

Run [[pass-set-hit]] in three terminal-rep rounds: first pass off live serve, second pass off scouted jump-float replication, third off a coach-hit down ball. Setter runs the scouted distribution — high-ball pin on a 1, quick middle on a 2, back-row pipe on a 3. Trim to the minimum cognitive volume needed; this is activation, not skill-building.

### Strategic (30 min)

Split into two 15-minute blocks. First, [[three-setter-rotation]] to lock the setter rotation-by-rotation plan — who hits the 1 in R1, when the pipe is live, where the setter's dump is hot. Second, [[read-blocking-progression]] keyed to the scouted opponent — pins pinched on the scouted seam-setter, a commit lever on their primary middle if they carry one. Finish the strategic block by walking the serving targets and out-of-rotation adjustments at the whiteboard for 3 minutes inside the 30.

### Competition (45 min)

Three stacked blocks. Start with 10 minutes of [[pressure-serving]] to rehearse the scouted zone plan with clutch-rep pressure and the personal cue sequence. Then 10 minutes of [[transition-rally]] — only transition kills score — to harden the block-to-attack chain against live opponent-style entries. Then 25 minutes of [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] with the 23-23 validation start, two full games, scored on side-out % per rotation and rotation-specific bonuses for the match-day targets. Run it like a match: timeouts, subs, between-point rituals.

### Cool-down (10 min)

Wind down with 5 minutes of [[six-player-defense]] at 60% intensity — base rotation, coach tosses, no blockers — as a reading-reset rather than a hard rep. Then 5 minutes of static stretching, arm-care, and a 90-second coach summary of the three scouted tendencies the team just rehearsed.

## Coaching cues

- "Manage what you measure" — call side-out % by rotation between games; the number drives the conversation.
- "Play the scout, not the memory" — react to what the opponent did on film, not to what last week's opponent did.
- "Serve the zone, own the miss" — zone-target aggression with accepted error rate beats safe serves.
- "Transition is three problems" — dig location, setter access, hitter arrival; all three or nothing.
- "Compete, don't contest" — the cauldron rule; push teammates hard without tearing unity.
- "Validation is a serve" — close on discipline, not on a hero swing.
- "Read, commit, hands" — bunch-read default, pins release only when the set is unambiguously theirs.
- "Championship behaviors" — what you do in the 22-22 rep is what shows up Friday night.

## Variations

- **Team size knob:** with 10 available, run 5v5 with an OH-floater; scoring transfers cleanly to gold-medal scrimmage format.
- **Intensity knob:** if legs are heavy 48 hours out, cut [[transition-rally]] to 5 minutes and play the scrimmage to one game rather than two.
- **Duration knob:** compress to 90 minutes by dropping [[three-setter-rotation]] out of strategic and running one scrimmage game instead of two.

## Adaptations by level

- **Down to HS:** replace the 23-23 validation scrimmage with a 15-up format, simplify the scouted block scheme to dedicated-pin rather than bunch-read commit, and trim the strategic block to 20 minutes.
- **Up to professional or national-team:** add live serve-speed targeting in [[pressure-serving]] (jump-topspin at 60 mph minimum zone targets), swap in video-cued attacker disguise in [[read-blocking-progression]], and extend the scrimmage to a full three-game set.
