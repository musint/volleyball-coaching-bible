# HS 90-Minute Mid-Season Tuesday

## Context

This is the workhorse weekday practice for a US high school varsity fall program at the competitive heart of a mid-season week. You've played Friday, watched video Saturday, light recovery or off Monday, and now Tuesday is your first real training block before the Thursday light-touch and the Friday match. Ninety minutes is what most HS programs actually get — bells, shared gyms, bus schedules, and athlete-other-sport realities all compress the window. The plan is composite because mid-season Tuesdays should hit every skill at maintenance density, catch one or two specific film-flagged issues with targeted skill work, and spend the back half in game-like phase where systems get executed under scoreboard pressure. See [[age-lens-hs]] for the full HS-microcycle framing and [[practice-planning]] for the broader session-design contract this plan instantiates.

## Learning objectives

- Maintain ball-control reps on Tuesday so Thursday can be a light touch without pass degradation.
- Rehearse out-of-system setter decisions under realistic broken-play inputs, with hitters swinging a toolbox rather than only terminal shots.
- Execute a transition sequence — dig, set, swing — at game speed with blocker pressure present, not shadowed.
- Compete every rep in the back half: no free balls given, wash-drill scoring that punishes casual play.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (10 min)

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (10 min) — the standard movement-prep ball-in-hand progression. You protect the 10-minute cap here; warm-up steals thirty minutes if you let it, and on a 90-minute day that steal guts the competitive block. If your team runs a jump warm-up on heavier days, Tuesday is not that day.

### Skill development (25 min)

- [[shuttle-passing]] (12 min) — two-line or three-line shuttle passing with target-setter feeding. Served-ball entries the back half of the block so passers see a ball off a live arm, not just a coach toss. Cue platform angle over power.
- [[out-of-system-setting]] (13 min) — the film-flagged emphasis most HS setters need every week. Feed off-the-net balls, behind-the-setter balls, and chaotic second contacts. Setter chooses hitter; hitter swings within the live decision (high-ball to the pin, back-row entry, or bail set). Pair with your setter's week-specific priority from Friday's film.

### Strategic (20 min)

- [[transition-hitting]] (10 min) — dig-set-swing sequences in full 6-person rotation with a blocker up. This is where the out-of-system block becomes a team rep: the setter's choices from the previous block now live inside a defensive reset. Score on clean terminal attacks, non-score on bail-to-pin (still run it, still don't celebrate).
- [[block-touch-drill]] (10 min) — block touches over block kills this mid-season, because read-blocking gets stale without isolated touch reps. Pair middles with outsides; count touches, not kills. This drops straight into the competition block's defensive sequences.

### Competition (30 min)

- [[serve-receive-competition]] (12 min) — your Friday passing rotation under pressure serving. Scoring rewards passing quality (pass grade) plus attack efficiency off the pass, not just side-out. Kiraly's competing-in-practice ethos lives here — every rep is recorded, every rep counts.
- [[wash-drill]] (18 min) — 6v6 wash with the no-free-ball rule baked into scoring: giveaway free balls count as negative for the offending team. Rose's framing that "we don't give free balls" installs the operational standard that carries to Friday night.

### Cool-down (5 min)

- Static mobility and partner-led debrief. Two athletes name the Tuesday-to-Thursday carryover — one technical, one competitive — before you release. Thirty seconds of film preview for Thursday closes the session.

## Coaching cues

- "Call early, call loud" — team-communication discipline on every pass and dig.
- "Pass to the setter, not to the target" — platform angle serves the setter's hands, not a spot on the floor.
- "Two choices before contact" — out-of-system setters commit to a primary and a bail before the ball arrives.
- "Swing, don't survey" — hitters commit to a shot inside the approach, not at the jump.
- "Touch over kill" — blocking scoring this Tuesday is touches, not terminations.
- "No free balls" — every ball over the net attacks something, even a safe ball.
- "Compete the rep you're in" — Kiraly's frame: there are no practice reps, only competitive reps at lower stakes.

## Variations

- **Team size.** With 10–12 athletes you run the plan as written. With 7–9 you collapse shuttle-passing to two-line, swap wash-drill for a modified queen-of-the-court, and keep setter decision-volume intact.
- **Intensity.** If the previous Friday was a 5-set match or the upcoming Friday is a light opponent, cut wash-drill to 12 minutes and extend cool-down mobility to 10 minutes.
- **Duration.** On a truncated 75-minute day (shared gym), cut the block-touch-drill to 5 minutes and the serve-receive-competition to 10; preserve the full wash-drill block — the competitive close is load-bearing.

## Adaptations by level

- **Step down to 14U club.** Replace [[out-of-system-setting]] with [[target-setting]] (14U setters are not yet making out-of-system reads at speed), shorten [[wash-drill]] to 15 minutes, and add 5 minutes of [[two-line-passing]] at the front of the skill block. See [[age-lens-14u]].
- **Step up to college.** Extend the plan to 120 minutes by inserting a 15-minute [[three-setter-rotation]] block between skill development and strategic, and grow [[wash-drill]] to 25 minutes with rotation-specific scoring rules. See [[age-lens-college]].
