# 14U · 90 min · Transition

## Context

Mid-season 14U practice for a club team that sides out acceptably on the first ball but stalls after it — rallies past the first contact die because block-to-approach and dig-to-approach habits are not yet there. Transition is the highest-leverage gap in 14U practice design. Run this plan when video shows your team winning first-ball exchanges and losing second-and-third-ball exchanges, or when middles and pins stand flat after a block rather than clearing and re-approaching. See [[age-lens-14u]] and [[transition]].

## Learning objectives

- Every front-row attacker clears the net after a block and executes an approach on the next offensive touch.
- Setters release from base early — feet moving before the dig is off the platform.
- Back-row defenders dig to the setter target, not to the sky.
- Teams score transition conversions separately from first-ball conversions and see the gap on the scoreboard.
- Hitters approach on every repetition, even when the set goes elsewhere — coverage is trained inside the same pattern.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (10 min)

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (6 min) — ball-in-hand dynamic sequence with partnered ball-exchange footwork so athletes arrive warm with balls already moving.
- [[pepper]] (4 min) — partner pepper at controlled tempo emphasizing dig-set-attack as a mini-transition pattern, not a reflex drill. Keep contacts clean; this is your first transition rep of the session.

### Skill development (25 min)

- [[pass-set-hit]] (10 min) — whole-part-whole entry into the attacking pattern. Pass target is the setter, set to a pin, hitter completes the approach. Reinforces that the attacking swing starts at the passer's platform — the organizing idea of transition attack.
- [[free-ball-to-offense]] (15 min) — named-pattern reps for free-ball conversion. Coach tosses controlled free balls, team passes high, setter delivers, every attacker approaches. Rotate lineups every 8-12 reps so all six live rotations see the pattern.

### Strategic (20 min)

- [[transition-rally]] (20 min) — full 6v6 with a scoreboard that only counts transition points (block-to-attack or dig-to-attack conversions). First-ball terminations reset the rally without scoring. Coach enters free balls to alternating sides. Stop the drill between rallies to make the block-clear-and-approach point visible.

### Competition (30 min)

- [[queen-of-the-court]] (30 min) — teams of three, free-ball entries, first team to five Queen-side wins banks the session. Every player passes, sets, and attacks; no hiding in a specialist role. Track the scoreboard publicly. Coaches do not coach during reps — only between. The earlier blocks installed the pattern; this block loads it into game-speed decision-making.

### Cool-down (5 min)

- Walking recovery, partner static stretch through hip flexors, shoulders, and T-spine, verbal debrief on one thing each athlete improved in transition today.

## Coaching cues

- "Block and clear" — front-row attackers must land off the net, not hang.
- "Feet before the dig" — setters release the moment the dig comes off the platform.
- "Pass to the forehead" — dig target is the setter's face, not the sky.
- "Approach every time" — hitters run their approach even when the set is going elsewhere; coverage is the same motion.
- "Transition on the scoreboard" — conversions count, first-ball kills do not.
- "Three seconds to the next ball" — compress time between reps so the pattern lives at game tempo.
- "See setter, see ball, see block" — the B-S-B-H eye sequence applies in transition, not just in first-ball defense.

## Variations

- **Team size:** run `free-ball-to-offense` with two lineups splitting court time; run `queen-of-the-court` at 2v2 for smaller rosters or 4v4 when roster exceeds 12.
- **Intensity:** mix coach-hit down balls into `transition-rally` for higher defensive demand, or stay on free balls only for lower-passing days.
- **Duration:** compress to 60 minutes by cutting `pass-set-hit` and holding `transition-rally` and `queen-of-the-court` to 15 minutes each. Expand to 120 minutes by adding a [[wash-drill]] block between `transition-rally` and `queen-of-the-court`.

## Adaptations by level

- **One level down (12U or first-year 14U):** cut `transition-rally` to 10 minutes; replace the competition block with [[cooperative-25-goal]] so the scoreboard measures continuous rally length rather than transition wins. Same teaching point, lower pressure.
- **One level up (HS varsity):** escalate `transition-rally` entries from free balls to coach-hit attacks off a box; add swing-blocking demand; extend `queen-of-the-court` to live-serve entries per [[age-lens-hs]]. Tempo target is faster — setters release earlier, hitters approach at higher tempo, back-row attack enters the repertoire.
