# Mid-Season Tue/Thu Cycle (14U)

## Context

Run this microcycle from December through early March on the typical 14U club calendar — qualifier season, between local tournaments, when a Tue/Thu (and optional Sat tournament or scrimmage) shape is the realistic ceiling for most working coaches and traveling families. The arc assumes you have already installed your offense and serve-receive in the preseason microcycle and are now training the *adjustment loop*: scout your last tournament film on Wednesday or Sunday, fix the top one or two issues on Thursday, and bank competitive reps on Saturday. See [[age-lens-14u]] for the developmental lens this plan inherits and [[practice-planning]] for the methodology under it.

The tradeoff inherent to a 2-3-session week is real: at this volume there is no room for a separate "video day" that costs court time, and there is no room for a recovery-only practice. Every minute on the floor has to earn its keep. Kiraly's training-efficiently frame is the right reference here — meaningful touches per hour over reps-for-reps' sake.

## Week objectives

- Tuesday: full-skill volume that touches passing, setting, hitting, defense, and transition without isolating any of them as their own session.
- Thursday: convert Wednesday's video-review correction into a game-like, scoreboard-driven session; the same flaw the team showed on Saturday should be the rep being graded on Thursday.
- Saturday (when scheduled): tournament or scrimmage; treat it as the test, not the practice.
- Across the week: keep the team competing every rep without over-loading the cognitive stage — Rose's "demand effort as a constant" applies, but penalty-heavy wash drills can backfire at 14U if installed without scaffolding.

## Day-by-day plan

### Tuesday — Full-skill session (90-110 min)

The Tuesday block re-establishes ball-control identity after the weekend. It is a composite session, not a themed one; you run every fundamental with a small competitive frame, and you do not chase a tactical install.

- **Warm-up (10 min):** [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] with a ball-in-hand sequence; layer in [[pepper]] for the last 4 minutes as the first transition rep of the week.
- **Skill (30-35 min):** [[shuttle-passing]] (10 min) → [[target-setting]] (8 min) → [[pass-set-hit]] (15 min). Keep the shuttle short-distance and coach-tossed for the first 3 minutes, then transition to live-serve from a box for the rest.
- **Strategic (25-30 min):** [[free-ball-to-offense]] with named-pattern reps — pass high, setter delivers, every front-row attacker approaches. Rotate the lineup every 8-12 reps so all six rotations see the pattern.
- **Competition (20 min):** [[queen-of-the-court]] at 3v3 free-ball entries; teams of three rotate on and off; keep the scoreboard public.
- **Cool-down (5 min):** Walking recovery, partner stretch, a one-sentence reflection per athlete on what improved today.

### Wednesday — Off, or 30-minute video review

If practice is dark, this is the day to cut a 5-10 minute clip reel from the most recent tournament and post it for athletes to watch on their own. If you have a 30-minute video session available (a parent-volunteer film room, a Zoom call, or an in-person window before practice), focus on **one** correction — typically a serve-receive seam, a slow setter release out of base, or a missed block read. Coaches should watch with intent: identify the Thursday adjustment first, *then* review with the team. Do not run physical reps on Wednesday; the body's recovery window between tournaments is finite.

### Thursday — Game-like + adjustment session (90-110 min)

Thursday is the highest-leverage practice of the week. The whole session loads the scoreboard, and the design teaches the Wednesday-video correction inside competitive context.

- **Warm-up (10 min):** [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — shorter than Tuesday since tempo ramps fast.
- **Skill (15-20 min):** Targeted block addressing the Wednesday correction. If the issue is hitting against a block, run a 14U-adapted [[transition-hitting]] block — coach toss and one-blocker only, not the full HS-load version (see Adaptations below). If the issue is serve-receive, run [[shuttle-passing]] with live float serves.
- **Strategic (25 min):** [[wash-drill]] — 14U variant. Two-point wash with controlled entries, scoreboard visible, teaching points enforced between rallies, not during.
- **Competition (30-35 min):** [[queen-of-the-court]] at 3v3 with live-serve entries. The Thursday version raises pressure relative to Tuesday's free-ball version. The scoreboard is the teacher here; coaches stay quiet during reps and coach between them.
- **Cool-down (5 min):** Same shape as Tuesday.

### Saturday — Tournament or scrimmage day (when applicable)

Treat Saturday as the test, not the practice. The role of the coach shifts from instruction to in-match adjustment and post-match observation. Take notes for Wednesday's video session: which rotations broke, which serve-receive seam was attacked, which player cracked under pressure. The note-taking is the bridge from this week's microcycle into next week's microcycle.

When there is no tournament, run a 60-minute internal scrimmage with the same scoreboard discipline as Thursday's [[queen-of-the-court]] block, and leave it at 60 minutes — do not stretch it into a third full practice.

## Volume + intensity targets

- **Total court time:** 180-220 minutes across Tue/Thu, plus 0-150 minutes Saturday depending on tournament schedule. 240 minutes is a representative midpoint for a non-tournament week with a 60-minute Saturday scrimmage.
- **Touches:** Aim for 200-300 meaningful contacts per athlete across the two practices. Trim anything that does not produce a meaningful contact at game tempo.
- **Intensity ramp:** Tuesday is moderate; Thursday is high; Saturday is competitive. Wednesday is non-physical.
- **Jump volume:** moderate — most front-row attackers will jump 30-60 times across Tuesday + Thursday; Saturday adds tournament jumps. Defer programmed plyometrics to a CSCS, see [[physical]].

## Drill clusters

- **Ball-control cluster:** [[shuttle-passing]], [[pass-set-hit]], [[target-setting]], [[pepper]] — Tuesday backbone.
- **Transition cluster:** [[free-ball-to-offense]], 14U-adapted [[transition-hitting]] — Thursday corrective install when the video issue is a transition flaw.
- **Competition cluster:** [[wash-drill]], [[queen-of-the-court]] — Thursday and Saturday scoreboard reps.
- **Warm-up cluster:** [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — both practices.
