# 14U · Summer Dev · 8-Week Macrocycle

## Context

This macrocycle covers the **club-postseason / school-aligned offseason** window — typically June–August for a US 14U athlete whose national-qualifier season closed in late spring and whose fall pickup is either a HS tryout, a 15U club re-tryout, or both. The defaults are written for **14U with HS and college variants noted**; older athletes use the same arc with higher prescribed loads under qualified-staff supervision. The orienting frame is Kiraly's "training efficiently" — every minute should be volleyball-shaped movement, not bolted-on conditioning [[aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently]]. The orienting constraint is the multi-sport caveat: in matched basketball/soccer/volleyball youth comparisons, **only volleyball** showed a significant injury-risk multiplier from year-round specialization, with >8-month-per-year participation OR ~2.0 [[post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball]]. Treat this 8-week block as **deliberate, lower-density skill development**, not a second competitive season; encourage athletes to play another sport concurrently.

> **Medical / S&C disclaimer.** Jump-training, plyometrics, resistance work, and any prescription beyond movement quality and modest sport-specific reps belong to a CSCS or athletic trainer. The routines named below are coach-eye preparation only. Athletes with knee, ankle, shoulder, or back history must clear loads with a qualified professional first. Adolescent overuse is "silent" — junior-elite cohorts show jumper's-knee structural change with neovascularization in *asymptomatic* tendons [[gisslen-2005-jumpers-knee-elite-junior-volleyball]] — so monitor jump volume even where pain is absent. See [[physical]] for the broader frame and the USA Volleyball Coach Academy Body pillar [[usav-2026-coach-academy]] for scope.

See [[age-lens-14u]] for developmental priorities, [[age-lens-hs]] for the late-summer-tryout handoff, and [[season-planning]] for season-arc methodology.

## Macrocycle objectives

- Each athlete leaves the block with a written, individualized **skill-priority list** built from a Week-1–2 assessment against position-appropriate standards.
- Setter candidates demonstrate stable jump-set release on at least 60% of in-system reps in [[jump-setting-progression]] by Week 8.
- Servers extend toward a defensible **jump-float** target by Week 8 via [[jump-serve-progression]] (jump-topspin only for HS+ athletes with CSCS-cleared loads).
- Hitters read and execute **line vs. angle** off two-handed setter feeds in [[line-vs-angle-shot]] without coach prompts by Week 6.
- Total intensity-weighted jump count tracked weekly per [[sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball]] — high-intensity Jumps 50+ are the variable that drives next-day fatigue, not raw count.
- All athletes complete a daily [[arm-care-routine]] habit they will carry into fall.
- Athletes arrive at the Week-8 fall-readiness checkpoint healthier and sharper than they ended the spring — not exhausted.

## Phase structure

### Weeks 1–2: Individual skill assessment

Two short sessions per week (60–75 min) with each session opening on [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] + [[arm-care-routine]] and closing on [[jump-warmup]]. The work is **diagnostic**: video each athlete passing, setting, attacking, serving, and digging against a standard rep menu. Use Mattox's spiking checkpoints (Sight-90, Elbow-Lead, Lift-and-Whip) [[aoc-2021-teach-spiking]] as the hitting rubric. Output of these two weeks is one written skill-priority list per athlete shared with the parent and any club/HS coach in the pipeline.

### Weeks 3–5: Individual skill development

Three sessions per week, 60–90 min. Block the menu by skill: a setter-heavy day (running [[jump-setting-progression]] from catch-and-release to live in-system release), a hitting-and-attacking day ([[line-vs-angle-shot]] off controlled feeds, with shot-selection cues), and a serving-and-arm-care day ([[jump-serve-progression]] front-loaded so the shoulder is fresh). Plyometric prescription stays modest within the meta-analytic envelope (~2,000 jumps over 8 weeks at ~2 sessions/week captures the bulk of available adaptation; injury risk rises above) [[ramirez-campillo-2020-plyometric-meta-analysis]] — and *always* programmed by a CSCS, not the head coach. [[reactive-jumping]] enters here as a coach-eye landing-mechanics check, not as a max-power workout.

### Weeks 6–7: Small-group team integration

Move from individual to 2v2, 3v3, and small-sided competitive games — the GMS-style game-like ratio reasserts itself. Setters connect with hitters in live small-group reps, servers serve at receivers (not at cones), defenders dig live attacks. Two sessions per week (75–90 min). The skill-priority lists from Week 2 are now competitive constraints: every athlete's top priority gets a scoring multiplier in small-sided games. This is also where multi-sport teammates start filtering back in for shared reps without overloading the early-summer specializers.

### Week 8: Fall-readiness checkpoint

One full session that **mirrors** the Week 1–2 assessment: same rep menu, video again, same rubric. The deliverable is a one-page summary per athlete: starting skill profile → ending skill profile → carryover priorities for HS tryouts or 15U re-tryouts. Send the summary to the athlete and (with permission) to the next coach in the pipeline. Close the macrocycle with a low-intensity scrimmage and a team conversation about fall plans.

## Weekly load arc

- **Weeks 1–2:** low volume, high feedback density. ~2 sessions × ~60–75 min. Total intensity-weighted jumps near floor.
- **Weeks 3–5:** moderate volume, narrow technical focus. ~3 sessions × ~75–90 min. CSCS-programmed plyometrics if any, capped by [[ramirez-campillo-2020-plyometric-meta-analysis]] envelope.
- **Weeks 6–7:** moderate-to-higher volume, broader integration. ~2–3 sessions × ~75–90 min, with game-like reps dominating.
- **Week 8:** moderate volume, assessment-shaped. 1 full session + optional sharpness session.

Track weekly intensity-weighted jump count per athlete; pull back any week the **Jumps 50+** count climbs noticeably above the prior week's floor [[sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball]].

## Key test dates

- End of Week 2: skill-priority lists delivered.
- End of Week 5: video re-shoot for setter-release / hitter shot-selection / server jump-float metrics.
- End of Week 7: small-group competitive scoreboard reviewed with each athlete.
- End of Week 8: fall-readiness summary delivered to athlete, family, and next coach.
