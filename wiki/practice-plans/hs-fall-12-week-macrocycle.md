# HS Fall 12-Week Macrocycle

## Context

This macrocycle is the season-arc skeleton for a US high school varsity fall program — the compressed August-through-November window that owns most state calendars. Twelve weeks is the typical span from the first allowed contact day through the state-tournament ladder, with a two-week preseason install, seven weeks of conference play, two weeks of conference tournament, and a final week of state playoffs (or end-of-season for non-qualifiers). The plan assumes a 2-2.5-hour weekday session, a Friday match (occasional Tuesday/Thursday doubles), one to two volunteer assistants, and a roster of 12-14 athletes. See [[age-lens-hs]] for the developmental frame, [[season-planning]] for the broader periodization theory, and [[match-prep]] for the 24-72-hour detail layer this plan tees up week by week.

The macrocycle is the answer to the HS coach's perennial question: how do you install a 5-1, an aggressive serving standard, and swing-block as the modern default while playing two matches a week and keeping athletes off the trainer's table? The answer is staged build with intensity-weighted load monitoring, ruthless protection of the competitive-training block at the expense of warm-up creep, and a taper that starts a week earlier than coaches think they can afford.

## Macrocycle objectives

- Install 5-1 offense, libero-split serve-receive, read-blocking with swing-block overlay, and a serving standard that holds under scoreboard pressure by end of Week 2.
- Sustain a Tuesday-Thursday-Friday rhythm through Weeks 3-9 that maintains skill density without accumulating jump-load fatigue.
- Run a two-week conference-tournament block (Weeks 10-11) at reduced volume but preserved competitive intent.
- Arrive at state playoffs (Week 12) tactically sharp and physically fresh, with high-intensity-jump load tapered per intensity-weighted-jump-load guidance ([[sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball]]).

## Phase structure

### Weeks 1-2 — Preseason install (high volume, install density)

Tryouts close, roster locks. Use the Mattox early-season frame: skill-blocked scaffolding, fundamentals reset, system install in that order. Run [[hs-120min-preseason-intensity]] template Tuesday and Thursday with Saturday doubles in Week 1 only. Install priorities: 5-1 rotations and serve-receive seams (Week 1), [[swing-block-shuffle]] as blocking default and jump-topspin/jump-float serving standards (Week 2). Weekend scrimmage Week 2 against a peer program for live-rep input. Arm-care via [[arm-care-routine]] is non-negotiable from Day 1.

### Weeks 3-5 — Early conference (rising volume, system stress-test)

First conference matches. Keep 4-5 sessions per week (Tue full, Wed light, Thu medium, Fri match, occasional Sat film). Tuesday workhorse follows the [[hs-90min-mid-season-tuesday]] pattern: warm-up tight, skill 25 min, strategic 20, competition 30, cool-down 5. Insert [[transition-rally]] Wednesdays after Week 4 once the offense holds together at speed. Week 4 introduces position-specific film loops (set-error, block-touch, hitting-shot tendencies). Week 5 is the first no-easy-matchup week — load the schedule's hardest preseason opponent here, not later.

### Weeks 6-9 — Mid-season (sustained density, refinement focus)

Game-like phase dominates. The weekly arc settles: Monday recovery/film, Tuesday full session (composite, see [[hs-90min-mid-season-tuesday]]), Wednesday optional walkthrough or video, Thursday match-prep light touch ([[hs-90min-match-prep]]), Friday match. Insert [[hitting-vs-block]] competitive blocks every Tuesday Weeks 6-8 — this is when read-blocking decisions need the most live-rep volume. Pressure-serving installs the clutch-bar standard by Week 7; [[pressure-serving]] runs every Tuesday from there. Week 9 begins position-specific scouting (libero passing seams, setter dump tendencies) in prep for tournament.

### Weeks 10-11 — Conference tournament (volume taper, intent preserved)

Volume drops 25-30%, intensity holds. Cut Tuesday from 90 to 75 min by trimming skill block; preserve [[wash-drill]] competitive close. Insert [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] Wednesday Week 10 with scout-card scoring to install tournament-rep psychology. Thursday is now a true [[hs-60min-match-day-activation]] template with no jump volume above warm-up. Match-prep workload intensifies — volunteer assistants brief opponent tendencies in 3-min pre-match windows, with stat collection scoped to decisions (rotation changes, sub patterns, serve-target calls).

### Week 12 — State playoffs (peak, taper-into-readiness)

Volume cut another 20-25% from Week 11. Tuesday short, Wednesday walkthrough or off, Thursday minimal, Friday or Saturday playoff. Tactical readiness — not physical capacity — is what wins this week. Rose's "no free balls" frame holds as the operational standard. Drills are familiar (no installs); pressure-serving and wash-drill maintain the standard, not extend it.

## Weekly load arc

- **Weeks 1-2:** 6-8 sessions/week including doubles. Jump volume climbs Week 1 → Week 2 with CSCS oversight; raw jump count peaks ~150-180 per athlete in Week 2.
- **Weeks 3-5:** 4-5 sessions/week. Jump volume settles at ~80-110 per athlete per session, weighted toward lower-intensity reps. Match jumps absorb half the weekly load.
- **Weeks 6-9:** 3-4 sessions/week + 1-2 matches. Jump volume holds, but **high-intensity jumps** (Jumps 50+) climb as systems run cleaner — this is the phase where intensity-weighted monitoring matters most.
- **Weeks 10-11:** Volume drops 25-30%; high-intensity jumps drop *more* than total jump count to preserve peak readiness.
- **Week 12:** Volume drops another 20-25%. Practice jumps cap at ~40-50 per athlete per session.

Daily session-RPE plus a weekly CMJ check are the load-bearing monitoring stack at HS+ resources. Shorthand at low-resource programs: athlete self-rated fatigue (1-10) on a whiteboard before warm-up. See [[sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball]] and [[rebelo-2024-training-stress-fatigue-wellbeing]] for the underlying evidence.

## Key test dates

- **End of Week 2:** Scrimmage scrimmage vs. peer program; first 5-1 + swing-block live read.
- **Week 5:** Hardest non-conference matchup of the preseason schedule; system stress-test.
- **Week 8:** Mid-season CMJ test (if S&C resourced); first scouting-driven match-prep block.
- **Week 9 end:** Conference seeding match (or equivalent placement match).
- **Week 11:** Conference tournament final.
- **Week 12:** State first round.
