# College 90-minute In-Season Video-Driven Practice

## Context

This is a standard midweek in-season practice for a college program — Tuesday or Wednesday, 36–48 hours after the weekend slate, 48–72 hours before the next conference match. The organizing premise is that the film room and the court are one continuous loop, not parallel tracks. Jim Stone and Terry Liskevych frame video use in three buckets — scouting, match analysis, and teaching skills and tactics both online and embedded in live practice — and this plan runs the teaching bucket in the gym while the scouting bucket feeds the strategic block [aoc-2020-stone-liskevych-using-video]. The workflow described on [[match-prep]] is the upstream: tag the weekend match by rotation and error type, pull 10–15 clips clustered around the two or three highest-leverage corrections, and cue them during transitions between drills rather than in a pre-practice classroom session. Athletes see themselves, then rep the correction, then see it again — the loop compresses the delay between feedback and action that stretched-out film sessions tend to lose. See [[age-lens-college]] for the season-long context and the Kiraly rule that in-season practice minutes are the scarcest resource and must be volleyball-specific from the first whistle.

## Learning objectives

- Clear two specific video-tagged corrections from the weekend match (e.g., middle-back reach on line angle, setter hand-set contact timing) with visible change by end of practice.
- Execute opponent-specific serve targets and block-zone assignments surfaced from the scouting cut in all six rotations.
- Hold a 58%+ side-out rate across the wash-drill block against the scouted-style server feed.
- Translate one in-game adjustment principle — block-shading or serve-target shift — from film into live rep without coach prompting.
- Finish within the 90-minute block with complete cool-down; respect the microcycle load after a two-match weekend.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (10 min)

Ball-in-hand [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] for 10 minutes: pass-walk, platform-shape, shoulder activation, approach-footwork rhythm, and 15 partner forearm contacts plus 10 jump-landings each. Cue the head coach's or video assistant's quick review of the two priority clips on a courtside tablet during the last 3 minutes — athletes watch in rotating pairs while the rest continue the warm-up. No standing lines.

### Video-tagged skill correction (25 min)

Run [[shuttle-passing]] for 12 minutes with the serve-receive variant — live jump-float servers hunting the seams the scouting report flagged as opponent tendencies. Between rotations, the video assistant queues 20-second clips of the passer's matching rep from the weekend; the athlete watches, names the correction, then re-enters. Transition to 13 minutes of [[target-setting]] cued to the setter's specific weekend errors — late hands on the 10-ball, inconsistent back-set location under a slow pass. Pair each set rep with a single previous-match clip on the cart-side screen; the setter's own voice on the correction is more efficient than a coach's.

### Strategic: video-driven positional work (15 min)

Walk the team through the scouted opponent's serve-receive tendencies and attack distribution by rotation on the whiteboard for 4 minutes, then run [[block-touch-drill]] for 11 minutes with coach- or machine-fed sets keyed to the opponent's preferred tempo. Middles read the opponent's setter release tendency from cued clips between reps; pin blockers practice the bunch-read overlay against the opponent's documented seam preference. The objective is not generic block footwork — it is block footwork calibrated to Friday's hitter and Saturday's hitter specifically.

### Competition (35 min)

Run 20 minutes of [[transition-hitting]] scored as wash with both sides earning and losing washes — attack efficiency and dig-to-attack conversion tracked live. Follow with 15 minutes of [[wash-drill]] in opponent-simulated format: coach-initiated serves with the scouted server's style (jump-topspin, short-middle float, hunt-weak-passer pattern), two rotations per wash, substitution in and out of pressure rotations 1 and 6. Read out the rotation-by-rotation side-out percentage every four plays so the team sees the scoreboard the scouting report predicted.

### Cool-down (5 min)

Static mobility, shoulder and hip, while the head coach names one carry-forward from video — a specific clip the team watches again on tomorrow's off-court walkthrough. The loop closes with a preview of the next film touchpoint, not a generic "good work today."

## Coaching cues

- "Match your rep to the clip" — the athlete names the correction aloud before re-entering.
- "Scout, not generic" — block and serve targets are opponent-specific today, not universal.
- "Tempo is information" — middles read setter release before hands, not after.
- "Side-out by rotation" — read the scoreboard the way the scouting report will read it Friday.
- "Call the adjustment" — athletes voice the in-game shift before the coach does.
- "Film is practice" — a clip between reps is the drill, not a pause.
- "Correction beats repetition" — quality of the next rep matters more than count.

## Variations

- **Team size knob:** with 10 or fewer available, cut [[block-touch-drill]] to 7 minutes and run [[wash-drill]] with 4v4 plus a dedicated target so scouting pressure still lands.
- **Intensity knob:** after a heavy weekend, replace the 20-minute [[transition-hitting]] wash with 15 minutes of controlled-tempo ball-control and push the competition block to 20.
- **Duration knob:** compress to 75 minutes by trimming skill correction to 18, strategic to 10, competition to 32, retaining the video cueing at each transition.

## Adaptations by level

- **Down to HS varsity:** reduce the clip-queuing overhead — pick one priority correction, not two, and cue from a single tablet or laptop. Replace the rotation-by-rotation scouting with a single opponent-tendency (weakest passer, preferred hitter zone). Shorten [[wash-drill]] to 10 minutes; HS programs rarely have video-staff support to sustain the loop at full density.
- **Up to professional:** the loop tightens — tagged clips delivered by an analyst in real time over IFB or wrist-screen, scouting cuts generated from DataVolley/VolleyStation rather than manual tagging, and post-practice film review scheduled within 24 hours on the charter travel day. The court structure is identical; the data and delivery layer thickens.
