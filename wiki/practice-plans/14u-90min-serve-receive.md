# 14U 90-minute Serve-Receive Practice

## Context

This is a mid-season practice for a competitive 14U club team whose passing numbers have stalled against tougher float and jump-float servers in the next tournament bracket. Run it after tape or stats flag serve-receive as the bottleneck — pass quality collapsing on tough servers, shanks out of seams, or in-system percentages drifting below where the offense needs them. See [[age-lens-14u]] for the priorities that shape the ordering below: ball-control is the base, reading is the organizing skill, and serve-receive exposure rotates through the roster. The core movement pattern is [[passing-serve-receive]]; the starting formation is [[serve-receive-4-player]] since most 14U rosters are still identifying a primary passing core.

## Learning objectives

- Pass 8 of 10 tough serves to the setter's window (2-or-better on a 0-3 scale) across all three starting passers.
- Call the ball before it crosses the net on every rep — silence counts as a pass error in the competition block.
- Shuffle-not-reach on short and seam serves; no crossover steps inside 6 m.
- Read server shoulder and toss, then commit feet before the ball crosses the plane of the net.
- Finish 1 cooperative drill with 25 consecutive controlled contacts across both sides of the net.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (10 min)

Ball-in-hand [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] for 10 minutes: pass-walk, platform-shape progressions, shoulder activation, and 20 partner forearm contacts each. No standing lines. End the warm-up with 20 light serves across the net into a target zone so passers see live trajectory before the first rep.

### Skill development (30 min)

Start with 10 minutes of [[two-line-passing]] to isolate platform shape on predictable trajectory — target 6 clean 3-passes in a row before rotating. Transition to 20 minutes of [[butterfly-passing]] with the serve-receive variant: tossers move behind the endline and serve live at controlled pace. Enforce the adaptation-by-level note on the drill page — 3 m distance for newer passers, live float for stronger passers, target 6 cleans in a row before moving.

### Strategic (25 min)

Walk the team through [[serve-receive-4-player]] seams and responsibility zones on a whiteboard for 5 minutes — who takes the 1-2 seam, who owns the short middle, who releases on a deep ball. Then run [[serve-receive-3v3]] for 20 minutes with a coach or designated server hunting seams and short corners. Track pass grades aloud; the target is a 2.3 team average across the block. Rotate every passer through left-back, middle-back, and right-back positions so no one hides.

### Competition (20 min)

Run [[serve-receive-competition]] with the 14U adaptation: 2-passer receive, grade scale 0-2 only, play to 5, server must deliver a non-tossed live serve. Both sides score — the server wins on any 0 or 1 pass, the passers win on any 2 pass. After each rep, the coach names one dimension of feedback (technique, placement, contact, outcome) in under 10 seconds, then reset. Every athlete cycles through server and passer.

### Cool-down (5 min)

Finish with [[cooperative-25-goal]] — both sides work together to reach 25 consecutive controlled contacts. Cooperative endings align the team on ball-control as a shared goal and surface leftover reading gaps without penalty pressure.

## Coaching cues

- "Call before it crosses" — name the ball before it breaks the net plane.
- "Feet beat hands" — pursue and arrive stopped; no reaches inside 6 m.
- "Platform early, platform quiet" — form the platform before the ball arrives, hold it still through contact.
- "Read shoulder, read toss, commit" — three-beat eye sequence on every serve.
- "Grade honestly" — a 1 called aloud beats a polite 2; lenient grading masks the real number.
- "Target, not safe" — aim for the setter window, not just "playable."
- "Shuffle-not-reach" — especially on short and seam serves.
- "Silence is a loss" — no call equals a pass error in competition.

## Variations

- **Team size knob:** drop to 8 athletes — run two courts of 2v2 serve-receive instead of the 3v3 block.
- **Intensity knob:** if energy is low, replace [[serve-receive-competition]] with an extended [[cooperative-25-goal]] block and add a 5-minute partner-pepper restart.
- **Duration knob:** compress to 60 minutes by trimming warm-up to 8, cutting [[two-line-passing]] out of the skill block, and playing the competition to 3.

## Adaptations by level

- **Down to beginner 14U or middle-school:** widen passing seams to 5 m, allow catches on the first ball during [[serve-receive-3v3]], cap grade scale at 0-2, and serve from behind a tossed-in line rather than full serves.
- **Up to HS:** narrow seams to standard 4.5 m, require a terminated or transitioned rally for a point, enforce the full 0-3 grade scale, rotate jump-float servers through the block, and lengthen [[serve-receive-competition]] to play-to-7 with mandatory tough-serve variant.
