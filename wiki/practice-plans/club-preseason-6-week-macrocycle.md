# Club Preseason · 14U · 6-Week Macrocycle

## Context

This macrocycle spans the six weeks from a 14U club's first day of tryouts to its first weekend tournament — the most important six weeks of the season because the standards, vocabulary, and movement defaults installed here will shape everything that follows. The plan assumes 2 to 3 sessions per week of 90 minutes each, the typical national-club 14U cadence; total contact time across the macrocycle is approximately 1,620 minutes (18 sessions × 90 min). Rose's club-season framing applies throughout: invest the early season in chemistry and effort standards, not mid-season form, and demand effort as a constant even while systems are still being installed [aoc-2023-rose-club-rules]. See [[age-lens-14u]] for the developmental lens that constrains every decision below, [[season-planning]] for the broader peaking architecture this preseason hands off into, and [[14u-90min-first-week]] for the canonical Week-1 single-session template this macrocycle expands.

## Macrocycle objectives

- Identify positional fits (setter, libero, OH-pin, MB, opposite) by end of Week 2; commit to a primary lineup by end of Week 4.
- Install [[passing-forearm]] and [[setting-hands]] mechanics so that ≥80% of passes and sets meet target by end of Week 3.
- Install base offense ([[offense-4-2]] or simple [[offense-6-2]] depending on setter depth) by end of Week 4.
- Install base defense ([[defense-perimeter]]) and [[commit-blocking]] by end of Week 4.
- Reach tournament-readiness: every athlete owns one rotation, the team can side-out from any rotation, and athletes name what they did well after every match-like rep — by end of Week 6.

## Phase structure

### Week 1: Tryouts + team formation (3 sessions, 270 min)

Use the existing [[14u-90min-first-week]] template as the canonical session. Goals: assess every athlete on the four ball-control fundamentals (pass, set, serve, defense) and the two athletic markers (approach jump, lateral movement). Run [[14u-60min-tryout]] format if cuts remain; otherwise use Week 1 to set tone, vocabulary, and roster chemistry. End Week 1 with an identified setter pool, an identified libero pool, and a working roster. Mattox's early-season frame — every drill teaches a habit you will live with — is the right operating principle [aoc-2021-mattox-early-season-practice]. No tactical install yet beyond Rotation 1 walk-through [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners].

### Week 2: Fundamentals install (3 sessions, 270 min)

The skill-acquisition week. Heavy ball-control volume with [[butterfly-passing]] (15 min/session), [[target-setting]] for the setter pool (15 min/session, isolated to a side court), and [[serve-targets]] (10 min/session, standing float only — no jump serves). Introduce [[approach-and-swing]] late in the week as a 2-step "Flamingo" progression to a 3-step approach [aoc-2021-teach-spiking]. Coaching cues stay external and arm-swing-focused: "high hand, fast elbow" works on this age group [aoc-2024-attack-arm-mechanics]. Drill mix should run ≈40% ball-control, 20% small-group competitive, 30% 6v6 cooperative or grills, 10% warm-up/cool-down. End of Week 2: every athlete completes ≥40 controlled passing contacts per session and the setter pool demonstrates the hand-hinge on ≥70% of reps [aoc-2024-kids-passing-fundamentals][aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].

### Weeks 3–4: System install (6 sessions, 540 min)

Layer base offense on top of stable fundamentals. Week 3 installs [[offense-4-2]] (or simple [[offense-6-2]] if two setters are tournament-ready) — walk through all six rotations for serve-receive and base defense; introduce the audibles you intend to live with [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners]. Drills shift toward more game-like reps: [[pass-set-hit]] integrated for transition reps, [[free-ball-to-offense]] for system-execution under pressure, and [[queen-of-the-court]] as the recurring competitive closer with 3v3 to 4v4 formats. Week 4 adds defense layer: [[defense-perimeter]] base assignments, [[commit-blocking]] read scheme (commit-middle on every play simplifies the decision tree at 14U), and one or two scripted special situations (free-ball coverage, downball coverage). The drill-mix ratio shifts to ≈30% ball-control, 30% small-group competitive, 30% 6v6, 10% warm-up/cool-down — Swan's CAP game-like-training argument and GMS's "play a lot of volleyball" principle take more weight as fundamentals stabilize [usav-2009-cap-game-like-training][gms-nd-structure-practice]. End of Week 4: team executes all six rotations of base offense without a whiteboard; commit to the primary lineup.

### Weeks 5–6: First-tournament prep (6 sessions, 540 min)

The sharpening phase. Drills shift to ≈20% ball-control, 30% small-group competitive (1v1 deep-court, 3v3 short-court for read training), 40% 6v6 wash and scrimmage, 10% warm-up/cool-down. Add [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] as the recurring closer (Week 5 with cooperative scoring caps, Week 6 with full match scoring), and run one or two sessions in an explicit pre-tournament microcycle structure following [[14u-120min-pre-tournament]]. Pressure-inoculate serving with [[serve-targets]] under bracket-style consequence (miss-and-jog, make-and-celebrate) rather than free-rep counts. Walk every rotation's serve-receive against a scouted-style opponent (one reliable server, one zone-tendency). Week 6's last session is a confidence-building rehearsal — no install, no new content, just the team's sharpest version of what they already know. Rose's preseason frame: by tournament one, the team should be confident in what it does, even if there is still much to add [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep].

## Weekly load arc

| Week | Sessions | Volume | Intensity | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3 | Moderate | Low | Assess + vocabulary |
| 2 | 3 | High | Moderate | Fundamentals install |
| 3 | 3 | High | Moderate | Offense install |
| 4 | 3 | High | Moderate-High | Defense + system layering |
| 5 | 3 | Moderate-High | High | Scrimmage + pressure |
| 6 | 3 | Moderate (taper) | High | Sharpness + tournament dress rehearsal |

Volume taper into Week 6 is real but small — the limiter at 14U is tactical readiness, not fatigue. Keep [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] every session to manage warm-up time without burning practice clock.

## Key test dates

- **End Week 1:** roster set, setter and libero pools identified.
- **End Week 2:** ball-control benchmarks (40+ controlled passing contacts per athlete per session; 70%+ hand-hinge on setting reps).
- **End Week 3:** team executes Rotations 1–3 of base offense without whiteboard.
- **End Week 4:** team executes all 6 rotations + base defense; primary lineup committed.
- **End Week 5:** team passes a 25-point [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] block at full match scoring with normal recovery.
- **First tournament weekend (Week 6+):** the macrocycle's outcome marker.

## Drill cluster references

- **Warm-up cluster:** [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], [[ball-control-warmup]] (ball-in-hand, no standing lines).
- **Ball-control cluster:** [[butterfly-passing]], [[two-line-passing]], [[target-setting]], [[front-back-sets]].
- **Hitting cluster:** [[approach-and-swing]] (Weeks 2-3), [[pass-set-hit]] (Weeks 3+), [[high-ball-hitting]] (Week 4+).
- **Serving cluster:** [[serve-targets]] every session; standing float [[aoc-2025-float-serve-3-keys]] only — no jump serves at 14U.
- **Competitive cluster:** [[cooperative-25-goal]] (Weeks 1–2), [[queen-of-the-court]] (Weeks 3-4), [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] (Weeks 5–6).
- **Position-specific:** [[jump-setting-progression]] for setter pool late Week 4+ if ready.

### HS / college variant notes

- **HS preseason (~2-3 weeks instead of 6):** compress the same arc — Week 1 = tryouts + fundamentals reset, Week 2 = system install, Week 3 = scrimmage + scouted-opponent prep. Add full [[swing-blocking]] execution (not just footwork), allow [[serving-jump-float]] and [[serving-topspin]] for athletes whose shoulders support it, and run [[offense-5-1]] as the default. See [[age-lens-hs]].
- **College preseason (10-14 days under NCAA hours rules):** double-day intensity with morning tactical + afternoon scrimmage. Add [[hitting-vs-block]] live, [[jump-serve-progression]] under pressure, and [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] daily. The macrocycle's frame is identical; the dial is volume and tactical specificity. See [[age-lens-college]].
