# Postseason Testing Week

## Context

Run this microcycle in the week after the final scheduled match — typically following state, regional, or conference championship play, when the team is intact, the scoreboard pressure is gone, and you have a window to *measure* the season before athletes scatter to club, academic finals, or off-season. The frame is HS varsity (most natural fit) but scales agnostically: see [[age-lens-hs]] for the level lens, [[practice-planning]] for the methodology this plan inherits, and [[season-planning]] for where the data feeds — directly into next-season's individual development plans and the macrocycle that follows. McCutcheon's measurement frame governs the design: end-of-season testing only matters if the data shapes the next cycle [mccutcheon-2022-championship-behaviors]. Kiraly's stats-matter-most rule applies — collect numbers that will change a decision; skip numbers that won't [aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most].

## Week objectives

- Every athlete logs a measured baseline on four skill axes: serve+pass, hit+block, defense+transition, and serve-pressure.
- Every athlete leaves the week with two written individual-development goals, anchored to the data.
- Coaching staff exits with a per-athlete testing sheet ready to hand to club coaches and inform next-season's tryout calibration.
- No new system or technique is installed — this week is reassessment, not development.

## Day-by-day plan

### Monday — Assessment intro (60 min)

Whiteboard plus light ball-control. Walk the team through the week's testing protocol: drills, scoring scales, what gets measured, what each number is used for. Run a 30-min pepper / partner-pass block at low intensity. **No scoring today.** Calibration only — athletes should know exactly what "good" looks like before they get graded. Hand out the sheet so athletes own their data.

### Tuesday — Serve + pass testing (90 min)

Rose's Penn State framing applies even at HS scale: serve-and-pass is the most decisive single offensive measurement [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep]. After a 15-min warmup, run [[serve-receive-competition]] (30 min) with the standard 0–3 grade scale and target-call. Then 30 min of [[pressure-serving]] — 20 reps per athlete to called zones, missed serves and ace-or-1-pass tallied separately so the error/pressure ratio surfaces. Close with 15 min cool-down + data entry.

### Wednesday — Hit + block testing (90 min)

15-min warmup including a brief jump-prime (CSCS-supervised — postseason joints get respected). 30 min of [[hitting-vs-block]] — every attacker takes 12 swings against live 1-blocker and 12 against live 2-blocker, with kill / tool / dig / error coded behind the net. 20 min of [[jump-serve-progression]] — servers work the four-stage ladder against Tuesday's grid so serve-stage data ties to the pressure-serving baseline. 15 min of block-touch testing: middles and pins log block-touch and stuff counts. 10 min cool-down + data entry. Liskevych's frame: not "is the swing good" but "which swing-types does this athlete own" [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-hitting].

### Thursday — Defense + transition testing (90 min)

15-min warmup (skip jump load — Wednesday already taxed the ankles). 30 min of [[six-player-defense]] focused on rotations 1 and 4, with coach-fed attacks from a box at zones 4 and 2. Grade dig (0–3) and transition-attack-conversion separately so defense and transition don't blur. 30 min of [[wash-drill]] — serve team scores on side-out + transition point; receive team on side-out only. Pair-track defensive grades against wash result. Liskevych's standard: defense is a reading skill, transition is a discipline skill, both deserve their own column [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-defense]. 15 min cool-down + data entry.

### Friday — Reflection + goal-setting (30 min)

Court closed. Each athlete arrives with completed testing sheet. Sit one-on-one for 5–7 min per athlete: walk through the data, name one thing that went well, name two flags for off-season. Athletes write two individual-development goals; coaches collect a copy. Team closes with a 10-min circle — every athlete names one teammate's improvement across the season.

## Volume + intensity targets

- **Total load.** ~360 min across 4 measured sessions plus Friday reflection. Mon and Fri deliberately low.
- **Jump load.** Wednesday only; cap individual high-intensity jumps at ~50 per athlete. Skip plyometrics Tue/Thu/Fri.
- **Serving volume.** ~40 measured serves per athlete (20 pressure + 20 progression). Below mid-season; intensity preserved.
- **Competitive tempo.** Wash and 6-on-6 run live but without scoreboard stakes; the data is the scoreboard.
- **Recovery.** Mobility / arm-care in every cool-down. Friday fully closed-floor.

## Drill clusters

- **Serve + pass:** [[serve-receive-competition]] + [[pressure-serving]] — calibrated 0–3 grading, paired ace-vs-error tracking.
- **Hit + block:** [[hitting-vs-block]] + [[jump-serve-progression]] — block-touch counts and serve-stage ownership tracked side-by-side.
- **Defense + transition:** [[six-player-defense]] + [[wash-drill]] — dig grade and transition conversion as separate columns.
