# HS Preseason Intensity (120 min)

## Context

This is a preseason varsity practice for the compressed HS fall — August tryouts through the first match window — when athletes are returning from the summer base, load is climbing fast, and you have roughly two weeks to install a 5-1, a swing-block overlay, and a serving standard before the first scoreboard rep. Run this on a Tuesday or Wednesday in week two. It assumes a 2-hour slot, six-to-fourteen rostered athletes, and game-like training as the methodology backbone. See [[age-lens-hs]] for the broader HS-varsity frame and [[physical]] for the S&C layer that surrounds this block.

**Medical / S&C disclaimer.** The conditioning and jump-warmup components assume programming by a qualified strength-and-conditioning coach (CSCS or equivalent). A volleyball coach without that credential should not self-prescribe plyometric load or conditioning volume — defer load and progression decisions to the athletic trainer or S&C staff.

## Learning objectives

- Execute 5-1 serve-receive to 3-meter attack at a measurable first-ball-kill rate under serve pressure.
- Apply swing-block footwork on pin travel and shuffle on short seams without mid-rep selection errors.
- Serve with intent under heckle and scoreboard pressure while keeping error rate within tolerance.
- Finish practice with targeted conditioning load and a shoulder-protective arm-care routine.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (15 min)

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (8 min) — ball-in-hand mobility flow into partner tosses; raises heart rate and greases the shoulder capsule.
- [[jump-warmup]] (7 min) — progression from pogos to submaximal approach jumps, CSCS-programmed volume. Caps plyometric work before any conditioning load downstream.

### Skill development (35 min)

- [[weave-passing]] (12 min) — movement-first forearm passing through diagonal weave lanes. Emphasizes early-platform on the move, which is the preseason gap most returning HS passers carry from summer.
- [[three-setter-rotation]] (12 min) — setter repetitions at live tempo with position-specific read keys. Run all three setters if you carry backups, or rotate the starter through front-row and back-row reps.
- [[swing-block-shuffle]] (11 min) — swing-block-versus-shuffle selection drill per [[blocking-swing]] install. Coach reads and names the footwork call on each rep; mixed-read phase gates on clean pin-travel reps.

### Strategic (25 min)

- [[hitting-vs-block]] (25 min) — hitters-against-block competitive reps tied to tomorrow's install (quick tempo, pin attack vs. swing-block, slide vs. commit). Ties the skill block's blocking and hitting work into a shared tactical frame; scout-ready block assignments get their first live reps here.

### Competition (35 min)

- [[pressure-serving]] (10 min) — hecklers on, clutch bar set at 8, servers own a cue word. Install the preseason serving standard under a controlled pressure budget before the first match.
- [[wash-drill]] (25 min) — 6v6 wash with scoring rules that punish casual play and reward targeted behaviors (kills off serve-receive, clean sideout transition, no free balls given). Kiraly's "compete in practice" ethos is the operating standard.

### Cool-down (10 min)

- [[conditioning-court-sprints]] (7 min) — court-length sprint conditioning programmed to the team's current fitness phase. Reserved for after the volleyball blocks so quality reps do not degrade under fatigue.
- [[arm-care-routine]] (3 min) — scap and rotator-cuff protocol to close. Non-negotiable at preseason jump and serve volumes.

## Coaching cues

- "Platform first on the move." Passing quality suffers most in preseason when footwork rushes platform — call the cue on every hurried rep in weave-passing.
- "Read, then select." Swing-block versus shuffle is a reading call. If the feet pick before the eyes read, the rep is a fail even if the outcome looks clean.
- "Cue word, then toss." Every server owns their pre-serve ritual in pressure-serving. Route-repeat the cue-toss sequence on clutch reps.
- "No free balls." Rose's program-level standard scales down — every ball is a rally ball in wash-drill; giveaway frees count as team negatives.
- "Name the error, name the fix." Preseason correction budget is limited; do not repeat a problem without changing an input.
- "Compete, don't perform." Kiraly's frame — effort is the only guaranteed controllable.
- "Finish jumps with hands pressed." Hands-still-rising at contact is the preseason block tell; call it aggressively.

## Variations

- **Team size.** Below ten athletes, collapse wash-drill to 4v4 on a narrowed court and extend pressure-serving. Above fourteen, split into two courts for the skill block and rotate coaches.
- **Intensity.** Heat or early-morning session: shorten conditioning-court-sprints and extend arm-care-routine. Heavy jump day earlier in the week: replace swing-block-shuffle with a read-only variant and drop conditioning.
- **Duration.** 90-minute compression: keep the full warm-up, cut hitting-vs-block to 15 minutes, and hold wash-drill at 20 minutes. 150-minute extension: add a 30-minute video-tagged serve-receive segment between skill and strategic.

## Adaptations by level

**One level down ([[age-lens-14u]]):** drop swing-block-shuffle — swing-blocking is not age-appropriate at 14U, per the age-lens ceiling. Replace with a shuffle-and-crossover footwork drill. Reduce pressure-serving heckle intensity to clap-and-name-only, set the clutch bar at 4, and cap jump-warmup volume aggressively — plyometric load in a preseason 14U session is a CSCS-consult item. Conditioning-court-sprints halved or cut.

**One level up (college):** add a scouting input to hitting-vs-block so blockers select against a live setter's tempo call, and to wash-drill so serve-target calls come from scout cards. Pressure-serving gets a simulated score. Extend the session to 150 minutes if the calendar allows; college preseason carries a larger install volume and benefits from a dedicated transition block between strategic and competition.
