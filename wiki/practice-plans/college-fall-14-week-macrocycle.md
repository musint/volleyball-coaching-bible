# College Fall 14-Week Macrocycle

## Context

This macrocycle covers the NCAA women's volleyball fall season — preseason install through conference tournament and the NCAA tournament window. It assumes a Power-5 or comparable D1 program with a full S&C staff, video room, and 16-22-athlete roster, and scales down where noted for D2/D3/NAIA programs operating with smaller staffs and academic-first calendars. The plan operates inside the **NCAA 20-hour weekly cap** during the playing season — countable athletically related activities (CARA) include practice, competition, video review, and required strength training, with one mandatory day off per week. Spring/summer carry an 8-hour cap with skill instruction restrictions; current legislation should be verified each year [unsourced]. See [[age-lens-college]] for the priorities that frame every decision below: full-systems execution, position specialization, analytics-driven prep, and the championship-behaviors mental ceiling.

The macrocycle handshakes with [[match-prep]] (the rotation-by-rotation scout that runs from week 3 forward), [[recruiting]] (the rolling 4-year arc that determines who is on this roster and who is being onboarded), and [[season-planning]] (the underlying periodization framework). Phase decisions follow the Rose-Penn-State frame — engineer the schedule to live longer in the scoring rotations and survive the rest [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep] — and the McCutcheon championship-behaviors operating system: athletes work-learn-compete, coaches teach-coach-mentor, every drill must transfer [mccutcheon-2022-championship-behaviors].

## Macrocycle objectives

- **Install and own** a full-systems offense ([[offense-5-1]] or hybrid 5-1) and read-blocking + bunch-read overlay defense by end of week 2.
- **Side-out 65%+ team-wide** by mid-conference (week 8); diagnose and lift the lowest two rotations to within 5 percentage points of the top by week 12.
- **Serve-receive in-system 60%+** against conference-median server profiles by week 6.
- **Build the daily competitive cauldron** as the operating culture from day one — daily ranking, contest, and grade sheet — without fracturing the locker room [aoc-2013-mccutcheon-eisler-cauldron].
- **Peak into the NCAA tournament** with a sharper, shorter, scout-heavy week-13/14 taper that preserves legs and sharpens reads.
- **Protect the roster** — intensity-weighted jump caps with position-specific thresholds (front-row > back-row), session-RPE + IMU jump count + weekly CMJ + daily Hooper Index as the monitoring stack [sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball][rebelo-2024-training-stress-fatigue-wellbeing].

## Phase structure

### Phase 1 — Preseason install (Weeks 1-2)

Two-to-three weeks of intensive practice plus one or two exhibitions before conference opens. The summer conditioning carryover is assumed; the volleyball coach is not the strength coach. Anchor drills: [[butterfly-passing]], [[three-setter-rotation]], [[approach-and-swing]], [[read-blocking-progression]]. Sessions run 2.5-3 hours with a daily film block layered on top inside the 20-hour cap. The job is system installation, position assignments, and base-rotation chemistry — not match results.

### Phase 2 — Non-conference + early conference (Weeks 3-6)

Weeks 3-4 are typically non-conference travel; weeks 5-6 are conference openers. Two-match weeks (Friday/Saturday) become the rule. Practice microcycle stabilizes around a Tue-Wed-Thu pattern: heavy strategic-block on Tuesday, scout-translation Wednesday, walk-through-plus-sharpening Thursday. Anchor drills: [[pass-set-hit]], [[jump-serve-progression]], [[hitting-vs-block]], [[pressure-serving]]. DataVolley/VolleyMetrics scouts enter the loop; the coach picks the limited stat set that drives the week and ignores the rest [aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most].

### Phase 3 — Mid-conference grind (Weeks 7-10)

Body of the conference schedule; bid implications begin to compound. Practice volume drops, intensity holds, recovery rises in priority. Anchor drills: [[six-player-defense]], [[wash-drill]], [[gold-medal-scrimmage]], rotation-specific scrimmages built around scout. Match-prep film time grows; on-court time shortens. This is where the cauldron pays — daily ranking surfaces who is actually competing and who is sliding [aoc-2013-mccutcheon-eisler-cauldron][aoc-2022-rose-coach-for-life].

### Phase 4 — Conference tournament + NCAA tournament (Weeks 11-14)

Weeks 11-12 close conference + AVCA Top-25 jockeying. Weeks 13-14 are conference tournament and NCAA selection-and-first-rounds. Practice shifts to **shorter, sharper, scout-heavy** sessions — the Kiraly training-efficiently rule applies hard: minutes count, warmups are practice, every drill must transfer [aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently]. Anchor drills: [[wash-drill]] on rotation-specific scout, [[pressure-serving]] against the bracket's likely server profiles, abbreviated [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] to keep competitive teeth.

## Weekly load arc

| Phase | Weeks | On-court (hrs/wk) | Lift (hrs/wk) | Film (hrs/wk) | RPE target |
|-------|-------|--------------------|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Preseason install | 1-2 | 14-15 | 3 | 2-3 | 7-8 (high) |
| Non-conf + early conf | 3-6 | 11-13 | 3 | 3-4 | 6-7 |
| Mid-conf grind | 7-10 | 9-11 | 2-3 | 4-5 | 5-7 |
| Conf + NCAA tournament | 11-14 | 8-10 | 1-2 | 4-5 | 4-6 (taper) |

All totals stay under the 20-hour CARA cap with the mandatory day off built in. Position-specific jump caps apply: front-row attackers + middle blockers throttle first when the load monitor flags fatigue [sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load-d1-volleyball].

## Key test dates

- **End of Week 2:** scrimmage vs. exhibition opponent — first system-execution checkpoint.
- **End of Week 6:** conference quarter-mark — side-out and serve-receive baselines reviewed against pre-season targets.
- **End of Week 10:** conference three-quarters — bid scenarios assessed; lineup finalization decisions.
- **End of Week 12:** regular season close — conference tournament seeding locked.
- **End of Week 14:** NCAA tournament early rounds — terminal evaluation point of this macrocycle.

CMJ testing weekly across all 14 weeks; Hooper Index daily. Mid-conference (week 7) and pre-tournament (week 13) are the scheduled monitoring re-baselines.

## Drill cluster references

Sessions throughout the macrocycle pull from clustered drill anchors:

- **Skill maintenance cluster:** [[butterfly-passing]], [[pass-set-hit]], [[approach-and-swing]], [[three-setter-rotation]] — preseason heavy, drops to maintenance in mid-conference.
- **Strategic / scout-translation cluster:** [[read-blocking-progression]], [[hitting-vs-block]], [[out-of-system-setting]] — appears in every conference-week microcycle Tuesday-Thursday.
- **Competition cluster:** [[wash-drill]], [[gold-medal-scrimmage]], [[king-of-the-court]], [[pressure-serving]], [[six-player-defense]], [[jump-serve-progression]] — daily cauldron ranking, intensity-modulated by phase.
- **Recovery / sharpness cluster (taper):** abbreviated [[wash-drill]], [[pressure-serving]] at reduced volume, walk-through reps. Weeks 13-14 only.
