# Recovery Week Microcycle

## Context

This is the post-tournament decompression week — the deliberate pull-back after a high-stakes weekend (regional qualifier, Power League finale, state-bracket exit, NCAA conference tournament weekend, anything where Friday-Sunday already burned three days of high jumps, full-pressure rallies, and emotional load). The plan is **level-agnostic with per-level notes** because the pattern is the same at 14U club, HS varsity, and college: protect tissue, drain accumulated fatigue, restore arm and knee health, and reset the team's psychological floor before the next training block. See [[practice-planning]] for microcycle theory and [[physical]] for the load-management frame; this week is where those frames meet in practice.

> **Medical / recovery framing.** This page is a coach-side practice template, not a return-to-play protocol. Athletes reporting **pain** (not routine soreness), persistent shoulder/knee/back symptoms, or any injury sustained during the prior tournament should see an athletic trainer or physician before participating. Recovery-modality evidence base is uneven — **nutrition and sleep are the load-bearing recovery levers** with definitive volleyball-specific support; cold-water immersion and laser therapy show promise but lack settled volleyball evidence and should be deployed under qualified supervision when used [closs-2020-recovery-after-volleyball]. Defer to ATs/CSCSs/RDs on individual prescription per [[physical]] and USAV guidance [usav-2026-coach-tools].

## Week objectives

- Drain accumulated jump and swing load with **zero jump-heavy sessions** across the week.
- Restore touch and ball-control feel without scoreboard pressure.
- Reset the team psychologically — give space for the burnout-pattern signals (sport devaluation, reduced sense of accomplishment) that rise across a long competitive season [pires-2021-burnout-coping-volleyball-season].
- Run a **light** intensity ceiling: 50-60% tempo on every contact, no max swings, no full-block jumping, no conditioning punishment.
- Keep athletes connected to the gym socially while volume is intentionally low — Liskevych's "controlling feelings" frame governs the tone [aoc-2017-liskevych-controlling-feelings].

## Day-by-day plan

**Monday — Off (full rest day).** No gym, no programmed work. Walks, sleep, hydration. The hardest discipline of the week is honoring this.

**Tuesday — Mobility + ball-touch session (60 min).**
- [[cool-down-mobility]] (15 min) — extended version, run as the warm-up. Static work is appropriate as the opener this week, not a closer.
- [[partner-pepper-warmup]] (15 min) — pairs at 4-5 m, bump-bump and bump-set-bump only, no hits.
- [[ball-control-warmup]] (20 min) — Kiraly 50% rule strictly enforced; 3v3 short-court closer kept cooperative.
- [[arm-care-routine]] (10 min) — passive-range version, no resistance bands; cool-down close.

**Wednesday — Off or active recovery.** Optional cross-training (swim, yoga, walk, easy bike). Coach broadcasts it as an off day; athletes who choose movement do so individually.

**Thursday — Light competitive session (60 min).**
- [[cool-down-mobility]] opener (10 min) — mobility-first warm-up.
- [[partner-pepper-warmup]] (10 min) — full bump-set-hit at 50% tempo.
- [[target-setting]] (15 min) — coach-tossed passes only, hitters out of the picture; setters work hand-hinge mechanic without jump-set load.
- **Cooperative short-court 3v3** (15 min) — no formal drill page; same shape as the [[ball-control-warmup]] short-court block, scored by team contact streak (drop = reset), no hitter-vs-block contests, attacks capped at 50%.
- [[cool-down-mobility]] full close (10 min) — static stretch sequence + reflection huddle.

**Friday — Off.** Travel-recovery day for college; family/school day for club.

**Saturday — Optional cross-training, no formal session.** Coaches available; gym closed for team practice.

**Sunday — Off, transition into next week's block.**

## Volume + intensity targets

- **Total court time:** 120 min across 2 sessions (Tue + Thu); cap at 180 min if a third optional touch session is added Saturday.
- **Total jumps:** **<30 across the week per athlete.** No approach jumps, no jump-serves, no jump-sets, no block jumps. Athletes who feel the pull to "get a few hits in" should be redirected; the intensity-weighted jump-load research is explicit that high-intensity jumps drive next-day fatigue more than total count, and a recovery week's job is to let that signal clear ([[physical]]).
- **Tempo ceiling:** 50-60% on every contact. Any contact that crosses 60% is a dead ball.
- **Heart rate:** 50% of session time at ≤60% HRmax, matching the natural intermittent profile of low-stakes ball control. No conditioning blocks.
- **Sleep + nutrition.** Coach reminds the team daily that **sleep and nutrition are the recovery work** — the gym session is the lighter half of the week's restoration [closs-2020-recovery-after-volleyball].

## Drill clusters

- **Mobility-first cluster:** [[cool-down-mobility]] used as both opener and closer this week. Inverts the normal warm-up convention because tissue restoration is the priority.
- **Touch-restoration cluster:** [[partner-pepper-warmup]] + [[ball-control-warmup]] — tempo-capped pairs and short-court work that restores feel without volume.
- **Setter-only cluster:** [[target-setting]] off coach tosses keeps setters sharp on hand-hinge mechanics with zero hitter load on the rest of the team.
- **Arm-prep cluster:** [[arm-care-routine]] passive-range version protects shoulders that just absorbed a tournament's worth of swings.

## Adaptations by level

- **14U club ([[age-lens-14u]]):** This is the typical pattern after a Power League weekend or a regional qualifier. Tuesday and Thursday optional, not mandatory; family time and rest are the primary lever at this age. No conditioning, no make-up work, no "earn your way back" framing.
- **HS varsity ([[age-lens-hs]]):** After a state-tournament exit or quarterfinal week. Pair with academic-load relief; HS athletes are often carrying late-semester deadlines on top of physical fatigue. Consider running Tuesday as captains-led only.
- **College ([[age-lens-college]]):** After a conference tournament weekend or the final NCAA bracket weekend. Sessions stay coach-supervised; CSCS/AT staff own the recovery-modality layer (CWI, sleep tracking, soft-tissue work) — coach owns the court-side intensity ceiling and the social-floor maintenance.
