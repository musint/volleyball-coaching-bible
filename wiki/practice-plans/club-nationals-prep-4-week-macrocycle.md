# 14U Club Nationals Prep — 4-Week Macrocycle

## Context

The final month before a JVA or AAU national-qualifier endpoint for a nationally-competitive 14U club team. Core offense, serve-receive, and defense have been installed since November; rotations track without a whiteboard, the libero identity is set, and the [[offense-4-2]] or simple [[offense-6-2]] base offense is consolidated. The job over these four weeks is not installation — it is *peaking*: hardening pressure-tolerant execution, refining scout-driven tactical adjustments, and arriving at the convention center sharp rather than tired. See [[age-lens-14u]] for the developmental constraints, [[match-prep]] for the scouting-to-execution pipeline that this macrocycle feeds, and [[mental]] for the pressure-inoculation and self-talk frames that anchor Weeks 3–4.

The HS varsity adaptation compresses Weeks 1 and 2 into a single high-intensity tactical week (state-tournament arc is shorter); see [[age-lens-hs]]. The college adaptation extends Week 4 taper to 7–10 days and respects NCAA 20-hour weekly limits across the arc; see [[age-lens-college]].

## Macrocycle objectives

- Raise serving aggression-vs-error rate to a tournament-defensible band (target: ace-to-error ≥ 0.7, missed-serve rate ≤ 12%) under bench-and-heckler pressure, per Kiraly's stats-that-matter-most framing [aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most] and Rose's bad-servers / best-servers stacking [aoc-2024-rose-best-servers].
- Execute scouted defensive rotations and serve-targeting against three-to-four likely pool opponents without coach-prompted reminders.
- Arrive at Day 1 of the qualifier with legs (vertical maintained, no DOMS) and minds (composure routines automatic, self-talk scripts internalized per the 8-week sustained-block evidence — adapted to a 4-week sharpening dose) [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes].
- Hold the Rose "everything matters" standard: no free balls, no untracked reps, no easy-points-given across the four weeks [aoc-2026-rose-no-free-balls].

## Phase structure

**Week 1 — High-intensity volume.** The last "load" week before taper. Three 120-minute sessions (Tu/Th/Sa) plus one optional 60-minute Sunday film-and-walkthrough. Volume is tournament-equivalent (~6–8 hours on-court) with deliberate ball-control density and competitive scrimmage closing every session. Anchor sessions on [[wash-drill]] for scoreboard-driven 6v6 reps and [[transition-rally]] for sustained-rally conditioning; close with [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] to set the consequence-driven competitive tone for the macrocycle. Serving block uses [[serve-targets]] for accuracy in zone 1/5/6 against likely scout assignments. Use this week to spike training stress and identify any technical leak that needs tactical-week attention.

**Week 2 — Tactical refinement.** Volume drops 15–20%; intensity stays high but reps narrow. Three 120-minute sessions, organized around scouted-opponent prep. Each session opens with 15 minutes of film on a likely pool opponent (rotation-by-rotation passing weakness, primary attacker tendencies, setter dump frequency — the operational shortlist from [[match-prep]]) then runs [[six-player-defense]] keyed to that opponent's scouted attack zones. Wash-drill scoring conditions reward execution of the scouted plan (e.g., "+2 if line-D digs the cross-shot from the scouted RS"). This week is where the team transitions from "we run our system" to "we run our system *against this opponent*."

**Week 3 — Pressure inoculation.** The hardest mental week. Three 120-minute sessions; volume holds at Week 2 level but consequence-density spikes. Open every session with [[pressure-serving]] — bench-and-heckler 25-serve sets with personal reset cues required before each toss, per Kiraly's serving-pressure work [aoc-2024-kiraly-serving-pressure] and the IZOF principle that pre-serve activation needs to be individually calibrated rather than uniformly damped. Run [[queen-of-the-court]] with elimination scoring; close every session with a [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] under "loser runs" or championship-scenario consequence rules (e.g., losing team starts down 0–8 the next round). Layer in match-day scenario rehearsal: down 14–13 in set 3, opponent serving, your weakest passer in zone 5. The cognitive load is the point.

**Week 4 — Taper + sharpness.** Volume drops ~40% from Week 1 peak. Two 90-minute sessions early-week (Mon/Tue), one 60-minute activation session two days before travel, optional walkthrough Day-of. Sessions are short, sharp, ball-quality-over-quantity. Open with [[serve-targets]] for confidence-building (high makes, no max-aggression), run a tight [[six-player-defense]] block keyed to final scout, and close with shortened [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] (rally to 11) — preserving competitive bite without volume. Honor McCutcheon's behaviors-over-outcomes frame this week [mccutcheon-2022-championship-behaviors]: the goal is composure and standard, not breakthrough. Self-talk routines and pre-serve cue rehearsal happen daily.

## Weekly load arc

- **Week 1:** Volume 100%, intensity 90%, mental load 70%. Build the bank.
- **Week 2:** Volume 80%, intensity 95%, mental load 80%. Spend the bank on tactical specificity.
- **Week 3:** Volume 80%, intensity 100%, mental load 100%. Pressure peak.
- **Week 4:** Volume 50–60%, intensity 70%, mental load 50%. Sharpen, don't grind.

## Key test dates

- **End of Week 1:** Internal scrimmage with parents/older team observing — first pressure check, baseline serving stats captured for ace-to-error tracking.
- **End of Week 2:** Scout-execution rehearsal in a controlled 6v6 against the practice squad running the top scouted opponent's offense.
- **End of Week 3:** Full match simulation (best of 5) with referee, scorebook, full warmup protocol — final pressure rehearsal before taper.
- **Day -2 (Week 4):** Activation session — light, confidence-focused, no diagnostic pressure.
- **Day 0:** Tournament Day 1.
