# HS 120min Postseason Development

## Context

This plan serves a high school varsity program in the postseason window — after the state tournament ends but before players fully hand off to club. The team is intact, the scoreboard pressure is gone, and the calendar has two or three weeks to push individual skill ceilings before winter club season takes over. The focus is skill-building, not system drilling: longer skill blocks, higher-tempo progressions, and introduction of next-level mechanics (jump-setting, swing-blocking refinement, jump-serving) that mid-season competitive pressure crowded out. See [[age-lens-hs]] for the level context and [[physical]] for the strength-training frame that runs parallel to this work.

## Learning objectives

- Complete three jump-setting reps at OH target with consistent hand-hinge mechanics before setter moves to quick-tempo middle ball.
- Hit line and angle shots from Zone 4 on command in live-block rhythm, choosing the shot based on blocker positioning rather than predetermined call.
- Execute swing-block footwork (crossover-shuffle) on 5 of 6 middle-attack reads against tempo set.
- Complete 10 consecutive jump-float or jump-topspin serves in bounds with measurable float movement or topspin break.
- Win a king-of-the-court round using live-ball reading rather than predetermined play selection.

## Time blocks

### Warm-up (15 min)

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (8 min) — standard movement prep: leg swings, hip openers, shoulder arcs, lateral bounds.
- [[jump-warmup]] (7 min) — ankle stiffness progressions, pogo hops, low-box step-offs. **CSCS-credentialed staff supervision required for any plyometric load progression.** Stop at first fatigue sign; postseason is about preserving joints, not loading them.

### Skill development (50 min)

This is the block the plan exists to protect. Postseason is when you actually get time to teach the next-level version of each skill.

- [[weave-passing]] (10 min) — midline emphasis, ball-hand read. Use this as an active warmup that sharpens platform without taxing the shoulder.
- [[jump-setting-progression]] (15 min) — setters only on the net, hitters rotating through OH/Opp. Progression: stationary jump-set → lateral jump-set → jump-set off pass off the net. Hand-hinge mechanics non-negotiable.
- [[line-vs-angle-shot]] (15 min) — live block, setter feeds high-Zone-4 ball, hitter reads block position pre-jump and selects line or angle. No predetermined call. Reads-first framing [aoc-2024-kiraly-reads-every-skill].
- [[swing-block-shuffle]] (10 min) — middles on one net, pins on the other. Crossover-shuffle footwork against setter tempo ball with blocker triggering on setter hand contact, not pass trajectory.

### Strategic (15 min)

- [[jump-serve-progression]] (15 min) — athletes with jump-float or jump-topspin ambition work the full progression (toss, approach, contact, landing); athletes on standing-float use the block to refine error-tolerance. This is skill-development-adjacent, placed in strategic because the serving-pressure frame from Kiraly governs it: give the team a chance, don't chase aces in postseason.

### Competition (30 min)

- [[king-of-the-court]] (30 min) — rotating teams, live scoring, standard KOTC rules. Rose's "compete every ball" standard [aoc-2023-rose-club-rules] applies even in a no-scoreboard-stakes environment. The competition block is where the skill work gets stress-tested; if the jump-set broke down at the net earlier, it will break down here too.

### Cool-down (10 min)

- [[arm-care-routine]] (10 min) — band work, scapular stability, rotator-cuff circuit. **This routine should be built or approved by an athletic trainer or CSCS.** Postseason is when accumulated shoulder load catches up; the cool-down matters more than it did in September.

## Coaching cues

- **Read the blocker, pick the shot.** Line-vs-angle is a reading skill first.
- **Hand-hinge, not arm-push.** Jump-setters who push from the elbow lose accuracy under pressure.
- **Crossover-shuffle, don't backpedal.** Swing-block footwork covers more ground.
- **Skill over scoreboard today.** Postseason practice is for developing, not winning reps.
- **Serve to give your team a chance.** Error-tolerance over ace-chasing.
- **One ball at a time.** Compete at the ball in front of you, not the one three plays ago.
- **Cool down fully.** Shoulders don't repair themselves on the drive home.

## Variations

- **Team size.** If only 8-10 players are available, collapse king-of-the-court to queen-of-the-court (4v4) and extend skill block by 10 min.
- **Intensity.** For the week immediately after state run, cut jump-warmup and jump-serve-progression by 50%, extend arm-care, and use the skill block for lower-impact technique (ball control, setting targets).
- **Duration.** If compressed to 90 min: cut strategic to 10, competition to 20, keep skill at 50. Skill development is the reason the plan exists.

## Adaptations by level

- **College level (spring individual).** See [[college-120min-spring-individual]] for the same season-phase principle with longer individual-skill blocks and more CSCS-governed physical work.
- **14U level.** Postseason development at 14U is less about individual-skill ceilings and more about cross-training, rest, and transitioning to a different phase of the club calendar. See [[age-lens-14u]] — at that level, reducing volume and varying activity is the priority, not pushing jump-serve progressions.
