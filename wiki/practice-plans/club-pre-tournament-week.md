# Club Pre-Tournament Week (14U Microcycle)

## Context

This is the Mon-through-Sun lead-in to a Saturday-Sunday club tournament for a nationally-competitive 14U team running a typical 4-practice club week (Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu) with Fri travel/light activation and Sat-Sun match play. Core systems are installed; this week sharpens execution, layers in scout-specific reps, and peaks tactical readiness without burning legs. See [[age-lens-14u]] for the developmental ceilings and [[match-prep]] for the scouting and video frame the week's content rests on. The microcycle handshake to [[practice-planning]] applies — Mon-Thu sessions are operationalized scout, not generic skill work. HS varsity adapts the same shape to a Tue-Fri pre-tournament week (see Adaptations); college collapses Wed video into the team film room and lengthens Tue-Thu sessions to 120 min.

## Week objectives

- Walk into Game 1 Saturday with every athlete naming her rotation-specific tactical focus and pre-serve cue word.
- Pass 50%+ in-system against the scouted opponent's primary server profile under simulated pressure.
- Side-out 60%+ across the team's two strongest rotations in Thursday's wash blocks.
- Close at least three [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] end-game scenarios (18-18 or 20-20) with a clean Kiraly-style validation point [aoc-2024-kiraly-validation-6v6].
- Log every session's competition-block stats publicly so Friday's walkthrough has an evidence base, not vibes [aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics].

## Day-by-day plan

### Monday — Technical refinement (90 min)

Open the week recovering legs and sharpening platforms. [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (12 min) → [[butterfly-passing]] (20 min) on full-rotation platforms with target zones — pick the seam your scout exploits and force-feed it. Setting block (20 min) on hand-hinge and side-of-ball contact. Hitting (20 min) approach + line/angle differentiation. Close with cooperative 4v4 on a small court (15 min) — touch volume, no scoreboard. Three Coach cues: "early platform," "high contact," "shoulders square to target." No pressure stats today; Monday's job is repolish, not heat.

### Tuesday — Strategic install + competition (90 min)

Today is where the scout becomes practice content. [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (10 min) → [[six-player-defense]] (20 min) rehearsing the specific attack-zone tendencies of Saturday's first opponent — coach feeds from a box, defense calls line/angle audibly before contact. [[pressure-serving]] (15 min) zoned to the scouted weakest passer with hecklers and a clutch bar at four; track ace-to-error publicly. [[wash-drill]] (35 min) 6v6 — serve team must win two consecutive rallies (serve + transition) to score; receive team washes on one. Run with rotations matched to the opponent's strongest server, so your worst-passing rotation gets the most reps. Closing huddle (10 min) — every athlete names her rotation focus.

### Wednesday — Video review + walkthrough (60 min)

Stone and Liskevych frame video into scouting, match analysis, and teaching skills/tactics [aoc-2020-stone-liskevych-using-video]; this Wednesday block is mostly the first two with a teaching tail. Whiteboard + film (25 min): walk the opponent's primary rotations, attacker tendencies, server profile, and weakest passer; show clipped 14U-self-tape from last week's tournament against any common-opponent comparison. Walk-through on court (25 min): rotation-by-rotation overlap, base positioning vs. scouted attack zones, timeout flow. Light closer (10 min) — partner pepper, no jump volume. Wednesday is intentionally short because Thursday is the heat day and Saturday is the reason.

### Thursday — Activation + final competition (90 min)

Use [[14u-120min-pre-tournament]] as the single-session backbone, compressed to 90. [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] (10 min) → [[pressure-serving]] (10 min) zone-targeted, every server saying her cue word aloud. [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] (45 min) — three compressed end-game games starting at 18-18, validation-point bonus, scout-rotated opponents, public side-out tracker. Close with [[wash-drill]] tightened to 5-point banks (15 min). Cool-down circle (10 min): each athlete names her Saturday Game 1 tactical focus aloud. Walk out confident, not fatigued — Rose's club-season rule applies, peaking is tactical readiness, not max effort [aoc-2023-rose-club-rules].

### Friday — Travel / light activation (20 min on-court if at venue, else off)

If pool play starts Saturday morning, Friday is travel + 20-min activation at the hotel ballroom or first venue check-in: ball-in-hand mobility, partner pepper, three rounds of go-to serves, walkthrough of Game 1 rotation order. No jump volume, no full court. If the team is local, Friday is fully off — sleep is the lever.

### Saturday-Sunday — Tournament play

Pool then bracket. The staff scouts in real time during pool; between matches, the head coach owns the next-point environment while a bench assistant tracks rotation-by-rotation side-out and serve-error rates [aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics]. Apply Rose's Penn State frame — your in-match adjustments are downstream of how cleanly you scouted in pool [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep]. Validate process over result in Sunday's bus-ride debrief.

## Volume + intensity targets

- **Total court hours (Mon-Thu):** ~5.5 hours, declining intensity Mon→Wed, peak Thu.
- **Jump exposure:** Tue and Thu are the only meaningful jump-volume days; Wed is jumps-near-zero by design.
- **Competition-block share:** rises across the week (Mon ~15% → Thu ~70%) as you peak.
- **Stat tracking:** Tue and Thu publicly. Mon and Wed coached, not graded.

## Drill clusters

- **Ball-control + serve-receive:** [[butterfly-passing]], [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — Mon and Tue backbone.
- **Pressure + serving:** [[pressure-serving]] — Tue and Thu.
- **Tactical + defensive:** [[six-player-defense]], [[wash-drill]] — Tue and Thu, scout-rotation matched.
- **Competition peak:** [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] — Thursday's centerpiece.
