# Club-Ops: Practice + Tournament Scheduling

## Purpose

Design a defensible season schedule for a club program: which tournaments the team enters, how practices stack against school and family conflicts, and how the calendar is communicated to athletes and parents. The output is a published, frozen schedule that absorbs the inevitable mid-season turbulence (school playoffs, illness clusters, gym closures) without unraveling the season's competitive arc. This page is for the club director or head coach setting the master calendar; per-week practice content lives on [[practice-planning]] and per-tournament prep lives on [[match-prep]]. Season-arc strategy lives on [[season-planning]].

## Process

### 1. Build the tournament calendar first (before practices, before fees)

The tournament calendar drives everything downstream — practice tempo, taper weeks, travel costs, hotel bookings, fee timing. Build it before you publish anything else.

**Step 1.1 — Pick a sanctioning pathway.** Decide USAV-only, JVA-only, or hybrid before scheduling any events. **USAV path:** local junior tournaments → regional championships (your USAV region) → National Qualifiers → Girls' / Boys' Junior Nationals (the bid-chase calendar). **JVA path:** JVA-sanctioned tournaments (often higher per-event quality at lower travel cost) → JVA World Challenge in the spring as the championship event. **Hybrid:** most competitive 14U-and-up clubs run hybrid, using JVA events to fill weekends between USAV bid tournaments. A team chasing a Nationals bid needs USAV qualifier access; a team prioritizing match volume and competition density at lower cost is often better served by JVA.

**Step 1.2 — Slot the peak event(s) first.** For a national-level 14U team, that is usually the National Qualifier the team is bid-chasing at, plus Junior Nationals if a bid is earned. For a regional-level team, it's the regional championship. Anchor the peak first, then build the build-up backwards.

**Step 1.3 — Fill the regular calendar.** Aim for **one event every 2–3 weekends** in the competitive block (typically mid-November through April for club). More than that produces under-trained, over-played teams; less than that produces under-tested teams.

**Step 1.4 — Mark recovery weeks deliberately.** Schedule **at least one no-tournament weekend per month** as a planned recovery week. Mark it on the calendar before parents see the document, so the gap is communicated as deliberate (not as forgetfulness). The intensity-weighted jump-load research shows accumulated mechanical drift over a competitive block is real and position-specific (front-row > back-row); recovery weeks are physiological infrastructure, not slack.

### 2. Build practices around school and family conflicts

**Practice frequency by level (typical national-club ranges):**
- 14U: 2 practices/week, 90–120 min each
- 15U–16U: 2–3 practices/week, 120 min each
- 17U–18U: 3 practices/week, 120–150 min each, plus optional position skill or S&C session

**Conflict mapping checklist:**
- **School athletics seasons.** In most US states, fall = school volleyball for HS athletes; mid-fall through winter pulls multi-sport athletes into basketball / soccer / track. Survey the roster's school sports calendars *before* publishing practice times.
- **High-school playoffs.** Block the first two weeks of November (HS state-tournament window in most states) — many clubs run optional-only practice during HS playoff runs.
- **School holidays + standardized testing.** AP/SAT/ACT weekends, finals weeks, school breaks. Default to lighter practice load during finals.
- **Religious observances.** Survey the roster for Sabbath/Sunday observance, Ramadan, major holidays.
- **Sibling logistics.** Two-athlete-family carpools shape practice-time choices more than coaches expect; align practice end-times across age groups when possible.

### 3. Communication cadence

- **Pre-tryout:** Publish the tentative season calendar before tryouts so families can opt in with informed eyes.
- **Post-team-selection:** Publish the *frozen* season calendar within 7 days of team selection. The frozen calendar lists every practice, every tournament, every recovery week, and every blackout date through the end of the season.
- **Weekly:** Sunday-evening practice + travel email for the upcoming week. Include start time, end time, location, what to bring, and weather-contingency.
- **Tournament weeks:** A separate Wednesday tournament-logistics email with hotel info, departure time, schedule, and meal plan.

## Templates

### Season-schedule template (publish to parents at team-selection)

```
[Club Name] — [Team Name] — [Season Year] Season Calendar

PRACTICES (frozen):
  [Day], [Start time]–[End time], [Location]
  [Day], [Start time]–[End time], [Location]
  [...]

TOURNAMENTS:
  [Date], [Tournament name], [City], [Sanctioning: USAV/JVA], [Travel: day-trip / overnight], [Anticipated peak: Y/N]
  [...]

PLANNED RECOVERY WEEKS (no tournament, light or optional practice):
  Week of [date]
  Week of [date]

BLACKOUT DATES (no club activity):
  [School-finals window]
  [Major holidays]

FEES & DEADLINES:
  [Tournament-fee installment dates]
  [Travel-deposit deadlines]

CONTACT:
  Head coach: [name, email]
  Team manager: [name, email]
```

### Tournament-decision matrix (use when evaluating each candidate event)

| Criterion | Weight | Score (1–5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bid value (does it offer a Nationals bid?) | High | | USAV qualifier vs. non-qualifier |
| Competition quality (level of opponents) | High | | Past-year results, division strength |
| Travel cost + time | High | | Drive vs. fly; nights of hotel |
| Calendar fit (recovery before / after) | High | | Days since last event; days to next event |
| Sanctioning pathway alignment | Medium | | Matches USAV / JVA / hybrid plan |
| Team-development fit (right level of challenge) | Medium | | Avoid sandbag events and unwinnable events |
| Conflicts with school / family blackouts | Hard filter | | Any conflict = drop |

Apply the matrix at season-planning time to a long list of candidate tournaments; keep the top-scoring set that fits the budget and the load plan.

## Common pitfalls

- **Overscheduling tournaments.** Six tournaments in eight weeks looks impressive on the schedule, produces a tired, over-played, under-coached team by March. Cap the competitive block at **one event per 2–3 weekends** for 14U–16U; **one event per 2 weekends max** for 17U–18U bid chasers.
- **Conflicting with school athletics.** Multi-sport athletes are the rule, not the exception, at 14U. A mandatory practice during the HS volleyball state-tournament week destroys roster trust. Build the season calendar around the HS season, not over it.
- **Not building in recovery weeks.** A schedule with no marked recovery weeks signals to parents (and athletes) that the program does not believe in recovery. Mark recovery weeks deliberately, communicate them as deliberate, and protect them.
- **Late publication of the frozen schedule.** Families budget around the schedule. Publishing it later than 7 days post-team-selection forces families into mid-season conflicts that could have been avoided.
- **Sanctioning-pathway drift.** Mixing too many USAV qualifiers, JVA events, and one-off invitationals without a coherent path produces a calendar that doesn't actually qualify the team for anything in particular. Pick the pathway, then plan.
