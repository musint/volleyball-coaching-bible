# Tryout Rubric: 12s

## Purpose

This rubric translates the 27 promotion criteria from [[12s]] `## Promotion criteria` into an 8-row, 1-5 evaluation grid usable in a 60-90-minute tryout for entering 12s players (rising 12s) or returning 12s players being evaluated for 13s readiness. It is intended for club tryouts at the 14U age band; cross-reference [[age-lens-14u]] for program-level context, and [[12s-drills]] for the drill catalog the rubric assumes evaluators are pulling stations from.

The rubric's load-bearing column is **column 5 = 13s-ready** — a player scoring 5 across most rows is demonstrating early-13s ([[13s]]) readiness and should be flagged for a higher-team conversation. Column 3 is the on-track baseline: consistent demonstration at age-appropriate level. Column 1 means the criterion is not yet observable.

## Evaluation criteria

| Skill | 1 — Not yet | 2 — Occasional | 3 — Consistent age-appropriate | 4 — Above age-appropriate | 5 — 13s-ready |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Passing** (forearm + overhead) | Reaches across body, no platform formation, hops into contact, no overhead pass attempt | Platform forms inconsistently; sometimes stops, often hops; overhead only on emergency | Stops & balances, thumbs together, sets angle on tossed ball; overhead on free balls; calls "mine" | Late-platform formation reliable; shuffle footwork without prompt; overhead clean to target | Serve-receive-overhead emerging; reads server pre-contact; passes 7'+ to setter target consistently |
| **Setting** | Palms-up, ball goes straight up; underhand-set fallback; eyes locked on ball | Hand-hinge attempted, side-of-ball contact rare; front-set only | Hand-hinge with side-of-ball contact; front and back set distinguishable; eyes-on-target on tossed ball | Live-set without catch scaffold; locating to OH/opp zones; tempo 3 reliably | Tempo 2 emerging on right pass; jump-set introduction; setter-as-second-ball confidence |
| **Hitting** | No approach pattern; one-foot takeoff; arm-only swing; lands moving backward | Three-step inconsistent; bow-and-arrow load missing; wrist-snap habit visible | L-R-L approach with start-slow-accelerate; bow-and-arrow load; contact out front; sticks landing | Big cross-court swing with high contact; closes the heel; consistent target hitting | Tip/roll-shot introduction; line-shot attempt; jump-touch standard rising |
| **Blocking** | Doesn't reach over net; crosses feet; ball hits chest | Hand-press shape inconsistent; outside drifts middle on tempo-3 sets | Static-and-shuffle middle/pin; thumbs-up press; outside-foot-high stance held | Penetrates over net plane consistently; reads set before commit; recovers off net | Swing-block footwork introduction; reads hitter angle pre-contact |
| **Serving** | Underhand only or overhand fails to clear net; toss across body | Float clears net 3-5/10; toss isolated drill not yet repeatable; no target awareness | Standing float clears net 8/10, lands in zone 6/10; lift-step-swing visible; isolated toss repeatable | Zone serving 8/10; aim-small-miss-small mindset; aggressive contact out front | Standing topspin viable; jump-float introduction; targets seam consistently |
| **Defense** | Upright stance, weight on heels; no platform on hard-driven; no ball-call | Three P's attempted; hips inconsistent; calls "ball" sometimes | Get-back/get-low/get-touch on coach down-ball; hips under hard-driven dig; calls before arrival | Reads hitter shoulder pre-contact; emergency overhead pass clean; perimeter base instinctive | Look/See/Decide operationalized; rotation-defense concept understood; pursue-through-the-floor |
| **Transition** | Static after contact; no recovery footwork | Free-ball-to-offense in cooperative drill only; no live transition | Free-ball pass-set-hit cleanly in cooperative drill; recognizes free-ball call | Block-to-approach footwork visible; dig-to-approach attempted in live play | Setter-second-ball protocol on bad pass; libero overhead from behind 3m introduction |
| **Mental / coachability** | No self-correction; eyes wander during instruction; emotional bail after miss | Self-corrects sometimes; repeats cue inconsistently; off-rep disengaged | Self-corrects between reps; repeats cue accurately; engages teammates' reps; FeedForward-receptive | Initiates teammate cue; coach-modeled self-talk visible; competes in low-stakes pressure games | Pressure-tolerant in Munciana-style 6v6 (Biggie Smalls/Finish Strong); sustained self-talk pattern |

## Scoring

Each evaluator scores all 8 rows per athlete on the 1-5 scale. **Promotion threshold to 13s: score 3 or above on at least 7 of 8 rows, with no score-1 row in passing, setting, or serving.** Score 4-5 on 4+ rows indicates ahead-of-age-band development — flag for higher-team conversation. Score 1 in passing/setting/serving is a return-to-11s signal regardless of other rows.

The 27-criterion granular checklist in [[12s]] `## Promotion criteria` remains the source of truth for what each cell observes; the 8-row table is the operational instrument evaluators carry on a clipboard.

## Calibration notes

**Session structure (60-90 min).** Run stations: 15 min passing (tossed + easy-served), 10 min setting (target station), 10 min hitting (approach + tossed-set), 8 min serving (zone targets), 10 min defense (coach down-ball + hard-driven), 15 min 6v6 cooperative-then-low-stakes-competitive observing transition, blocking, mental.

**Inter-rater agreement.** Use 2-3 evaluators per athlete; pre-tryout meeting walks the 8 rows and anchors examples for column 3 and column 5. Score independently, reconcile differences >1 column post-session. Sample 3 athletes mid-session to check evaluator drift.

**Common biases to avoid.** Height-halo (tall ≠ skilled at 12s — Munciana flags this). Friend-of-coach bias. Recency bias (last station scored harshly). Confusing volume of effort with skill quality — a quiet, balanced platform outscores enthusiastic flailing every time.

**Recency lean.** Per the program preference for modern training, score against AOC/Munciana cue language and hand-hinge setting standards rather than legacy underhand-set or wrist-snap-spike framings.
