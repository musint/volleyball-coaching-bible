# Club-Ops: Conflict Resolution

## Purpose

Give head coaches and club directors a single, repeatable process for handling parent / athlete / coach conflicts — playing-time complaints, perceived favoritism, peer disputes between athletes, philosophical disagreements about tactics or selection. The goal is not to eliminate conflict (a competitive program will produce it weekly) but to route it predictably, document it defensibly, and prevent it from compounding into board-level escalations or SafeSport reports. Aligns with the [[philosophy]] hub on coach-athlete-parent triad management and the code-of-conduct posture introduced in [[aoc-2014-denney-10-steps-start-club]].

## Process

### The 24-hour rule

**No substantive conversations between a coach and a parent (or athlete) within 24 hours of a match or selection decision.** Emotion is highest, recall is most distorted, and content said in that window is the content most likely to be regretted. The rule is bidirectional: coaches do not initiate, parents are told at the season-kickoff meeting that the rule applies to them too. If a parent emails / texts / corners you in the parking lot inside the 24-hour window, the only acceptable response is the meeting-request template below.

The rule has one carve-out: medical / safety issues (suspected injury, mental-health concern, transportation problem) are handled immediately. Everything else — playing time, set selection, substitution patterns, refereeing decisions, tournament outcomes — waits.

### Meeting structure

A formal conflict-resolution meeting runs **30 minutes maximum**, in a neutral on-site space (club office, empty classroom, never a parking lot or DM thread), with these blocks:

1. **Listen (10 min).** The complaining party speaks first and uninterrupted. Coach takes notes. No defending, no contextualizing. Goal is to make the parent / athlete feel fully heard before any response.
2. **Clarify (5 min).** Coach asks fact-finding questions only — not rebuttal questions. "When did this start?" "Who else is involved?" "What outcome would you consider resolved?"
3. **Respond (10 min).** Coach states the program's view, the data behind any selection / playing-time decision, and what the coach will and will not change. Russ Rose's framing applies: effort is non-negotiable, role-fit is determined by performance evidence, and chemistry investments take time ([[aoc-2023-rose-club-rules]]).
4. **Action items (5 min).** Three specific outputs: what the coach will do, what the parent / athlete will do, and the follow-up date. Written down in front of both parties on the meeting-summary template.

A **second adult on the coach's side** is required for any meeting with a parent of the opposite gender, or any meeting concerning an athlete under 18 where SafeSport considerations apply ([[usav-2026-coach-academy]]). The assistant coach, club director, or program manager fills this role; not another parent.

### Escalation pathway

Conflicts escalate one rung at a time and stop at the first rung that resolves them:

1. **Athlete → assistant coach.** Peer disputes, minor playing-time questions, drill-level concerns. Resolved within one practice cycle.
2. **Athlete or parent → head coach.** Selection decisions, sustained playing-time concerns, perceived favoritism, philosophical disagreements. Resolved within one week, documented per the meeting-summary template.
3. **Parent → club director.** Head-coach-level decisions the parent disputes after the head-coach meeting; allegations of unfair treatment that the head coach is a party to; financial-aid or scheduling disputes. Director investigates with both sides, documents, decides.
4. **Parent → board.** Director-level decisions disputed after the director meeting; conduct issues involving the director; SafeSport-adjacent matters that warrant board oversight. Written submission required; board chair acknowledges within 48 hours.

**Skipping rungs is not allowed** unless SafeSport requires it (any abuse / misconduct allegation goes directly to the U.S. Center for SafeSport and the club's MAAPP officer; do not run those through the coach).

## Templates

### Meeting-request response (coach → parent who messaged inside the 24-hour window)

```
Subject: Re: <parent's subject> — let's meet this week

Hi <Parent>,

Thanks for reaching out. I want to give your concern the time it deserves,
and program policy is to handle these conversations in person rather than
over text or email, and outside the 24-hour post-match window.

I'm available Tuesday <date> at 6:00pm or Wednesday <date> at 5:30pm at
<facility>. Either time, please plan on 30 minutes. <Athlete> is welcome
to attend if you'd like — that's your call.

Looking forward to talking.

<Coach>
```

### Meeting-summary (coach completes during meeting, sends within 24 hours)

```
Conflict-resolution meeting summary

Date: <date>     Location: <facility>
Attendees: <names + roles>
Topic (one sentence): <complaint as the parent / athlete stated it>

What was heard:
- <bullet, in their words>
- <bullet>

Program response:
- <bullet, factual + brief>
- <bullet>

Action items:
- Coach will: <specific, dated>
- Parent / athlete will: <specific, dated>
- Follow-up: <date / trigger>

Signatures (digital ok):  Coach _______   Parent _______   Athlete _______

Filed to: <club shared drive path>      Cc: <director if escalated>
```

## Common pitfalls

- **Not documenting.** A meeting that produced no written summary effectively did not happen. Untraceable verbal commitments become "you said you'd do X" disputes a month later. File every summary to a shared drive the director can audit.
- **Resolving over text or DM.** Tone collapses, nuance vanishes, and the record becomes a screenshot weapon. If a parent insists on text, respond once with the meeting-request template and stop typing.
- **Favoritism perception.** Track playing-time minutes by athlete per match in the same sheet you use for stats; data-back any selection conversation. Do not have private dinners / rides / gifts with individual athletes — group settings only, per [[usav-2026-coach-academy]] minor athlete abuse prevention policies (MAAPP).
- **Skipping the second adult.** Any meeting involving a minor athlete or any concern that could become a SafeSport matter requires a second adult on the coach's side. No exceptions.
- **Letting a conflict skip the head coach.** Parents who go directly to the director on an issue the head coach has not yet heard get routed back. Directors who end-run the head coach erode the staff's authority for the rest of the season.
