# Tryout Rubric: 11s

## Purpose

This rubric evaluates 11U athletes against the [[11s]] promotion criteria — specifically, what an 11U player must demonstrate to graduate into [[12s]]. Column 5 ("12s-ready") is the operational definition of promotion-readiness on each criterion. Use this rubric for end-of-season evaluations, mid-year level checks, and tryout placement at the 11U/12U boundary.

The rubric inherits the wiki's recency-leaning operating model from [[age-lens-14u]]: modern AOC and Munciana coaching language is the assumed cue vocabulary, simple [[offense-4-2]] is the assumed entry-level offense, and "introduction phase transitioning toward fundamentals" is the assumed developmental window. Score at age-appropriate effort and complexity — Column 3 ("consistent at age-appropriate level") is the on-track 11U mid-season state, not a deficiency.

## Evaluation criteria

| Criterion | 1 — does not demonstrate | 2 — occasional | 3 — consistent at age-appropriate level | 4 — above age-appropriate | 5 — 12s-ready |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Passing** | No platform shape; arms apart, reaches without moving feet. | Platform shape on centered balls only; reaches on off-center balls. | Recognizable platform on most contacts; shuffles toward the ball; thumbs together. | Stable platform on off-center balls; consistent shuffle-not-reach; passes tossed balls to setter target. | Platform stable on every contact; passes tossed/slow-served balls to setter target with shape intact in majority of reps; "stopped and balanced" before contact. |
| **Setting** | No hand triangle; thumbs collide; palms catch. | Triangle attempted but inconsistent; double-contact violations frequent. | Catch-and-set with hand triangle, no thumb collision; recognizes overhead vs. forearm territory. | Hand-hinge mechanism present in catch-and-set; setter-track candidates live-set tossed balls inconsistently. | Hand triangle clean above forehead; setter-track candidates live-set a tossed ball 5-10 feet above the net to target; legs-extend power source visible. |
| **Hitting** | No swing shape; pushes the ball; no approach. | Down-ball arm swing attempted; no approach footwork. | Down-ball swing from puppet position (elbow up, contact in front); 2-step Flamingo approach attempted. | 2-step approach onto an accurate toss with isolated jump; contact in front consistently. | 2-step approach with isolated jump on tossed ball; 3-step approach attempted by season's end; "start slow, accelerate" tempo visible. |
| **Blocking** | Hands below net; backs up at the net; net contact. | Hands at net height occasionally; jump uncommitted. | Hands above net pre-jump; thumbs up, fingers spread; controlled stationary jump. | Net awareness consistent; recognizes when to block vs. back up. | Pre-jump posture stable across reps without net contact; reads when to block vs. back up to defense; no retreat from the net. |
| **Serving** | Underhand serve does not clear net reliably; no overhand attempt. | Underhand reliable; overhand attempted but inconsistent contact. | Reliable underhand from baseline; overhand attempt at developmentally appropriate distance; toss inconsistent. | Underhand from baseline; overhand contact successful in ~30% of attempts; toss landing in repeatable zone. | Reliable underhand; overhand serve attempted at appropriate distance with successful contact in at least 30% of attempts; toss arc consistent each rep; serves at a target, not just over the net. |
| **Defense** | Stands flat-footed; no pursuit. | Ready position only when reminded; pursues without touching. | Ready position during opponent's contact; pursues with intent; recovery touches present. | Touches pursued balls consistently; recognizes free ball vs. attack at concept level. | Ready position automatic; "get back, get low, get a touch" Three P's visible; recovery touches keep rallies alive; reads attack vs. free ball. |
| **Transition** | Admires the ball after contact; no recovery. | Recovers slowly; no pass-set-hit shape. | Recovers to ready spot after contact; participates in pass-set-hit shape cooperatively. | Pass-set-hit shape executed with teammates; urgency awareness present. | Recovers automatically after every contact; pass-set-hit shape executed without freezing; concept-level urgency between contacts. |
| **Mental & coachability** | Does not attempt feedback; protests repetition. | Inconsistent attempts at feedback; tolerates repetition reluctantly. | Attempts feedback on next rep; tolerates repetition; engages teammates positively. | Self-corrects within rep cycle; asks clarifying questions; positive teammate engagement. | Self-correction reliable; asks coachability-signal questions; rep-volume tolerance high; "reps equal confidence" mindset evident. |

## Scoring

Per athlete, record one score (1-5) per criterion. Total = 8-40. Reference bands:

- **Total 32-40 (avg 4.0+):** 12s-ready. Promote with high confidence; player meets or exceeds promotion criteria across the board.
- **Total 24-31 (avg 3.0-3.9):** On-track for 12s. Most criteria at age-appropriate level. Promote unless 1-2 criteria score 1-2 (revisit those before final placement).
- **Total 16-23 (avg 2.0-2.9):** Repeat 11s recommended. Multiple criteria not yet at age-appropriate level. Continue 11U-development focus on the lowest 2-3 criteria.
- **Total 8-15 (avg <2.0):** Reassess fit. Either developmental support gap, calendar-age mismatch (younger 10U-aligned athlete), or pre-introduction-phase. Discuss with parent and explore [[10s]] placement or developmental track.

A single 5 does not promote; a single 1 does not demote — promotion is a pattern across the rubric. Serving and passing are weighted higher in any tiebreak because they carry the load-bearing 11U-specific milestones (overhand-serve attempt; recognizable platform).

## Calibration notes

**Session design.** Run the rubric across a 60-90-minute session: warm-up + footwork (10 min, scores movement quality and pursuit signals); passing + serving stations (20 min, scores Passing + Serving); setting + hitting stations (20 min, scores Setting + Hitting); blocking + defense (10 min); cooperative 4v4 or 6v6 (15-20 min, scores Transition + Mental & coachability + Systems-recognition).

**Inter-rater agreement.** With 2+ evaluators, score independently then compare. Discrepancies of 2+ points trigger a re-watch (or re-rep) before resolving — never average without discussion. Anchor each evaluator on Column 3 ("consistent at age-appropriate level") as the calibration baseline before tryouts begin.

**Common biases to avoid.**
- **Size bias:** taller 11U athletes look more "12s-ready" but vertical reach is not a rubric criterion at this age. Score the skill, not the body.
- **Multi-sport athlete bias:** athletes with limited volleyball reps but strong general athleticism may score 2-3 on technique while showing 4-5 movement quality. Per [[11s]] athletic profile, multi-sport play is evidence-backed injury prevention; do not penalize for it.
- **Loud-energy bias:** the quiet self-correcting athlete often outranks the loud one on Mental & coachability. Score on rep-by-rep behavior, not volume.
- **Cue-language over-engineering:** Moran et al. 2023 found no significant cue-type advantage in youth populations. Do not score down an athlete who responds to a different cue family — score the resulting motor behavior.

**Forward link.** Athletes scoring at 12s-ready (Column 5) on most criteria graduate into the [[12s]] context where the platform must become stable, standing-float serve becomes the default, basic 4-2 must be executable in real games, and overhead passing is added.
