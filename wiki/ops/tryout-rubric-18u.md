# Tryout rubric — 18u

## Purpose

This rubric translates the [[18s]] `## Promotion criteria` into a graded 1-5 evaluation tool for an 18u tryout. The 18u year is the **college bridge** — see [[age-lens-college]] for the parent context. The unifying frame: column 5 ("college-ready") is calibrated to "would survive a college preseason on August 1," not "completed an 18s season." For recruiting-active athletes, [[recruiting]] adds a parallel evaluation layer (target-list discipline, film standards, signing-date awareness) that runs alongside the on-court rubric.

The rubric reads against an 80-90 minute combined tryout session: 20 min warm-up + skill-station rotation, 30 min controlled drill (serve-receive + transition + 6v6 wash), 30 min open scrimmage with end-game pressure rules, 10 min interview + measurement station (height / wingspan / standing reach / approach jump touch / vertical).

## Evaluation criteria

| Criterion | 1 — Does not demonstrate | 2 — Occasional | 3 — Consistent at age | 4 — Above age | 5 — College-ready |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Passing** | Cannot reliably platform jump-float; no seam communication. | Platforms jump-float; jump-topspin passes drift; seam calls absent. | Libero-split serviceable vs. jump-float and slow topspin; pre-serve seam call audible. | Passes high-velocity topspin in libero-split; calls four-passer adjustment; consistent 2.0 tournament rating. | 2.0+ rating vs. college-tier topspin; pre-serve seam ownership instant; pass grades tied to **what offense ran**. |
| **Setting** | 5-1 only in three rotations; back-set absent; OOS dumps. | 5-1 across six rotations; jump-set inconsistent; OOS one-handed. | Full 5-1; jump-set from Zone 2/3; OOS two-handed redirect functional. | Back-set disguise; tempo-call audible (Munciana T1/T2/T3); rotational drift correcting late-match. | Real-time tempo audibles; OOS two-handed consistent; rotational-component repeatability sustained under fatigue. |
| **Hitting** | 3-step approach; arm-pull contact; in-system only. | 4-step approach; high-hand inconsistent; line-only or angle-only toolkit. | Modern 4-step mature; line/angle/tip in-system; back-row Zone 6 reliable. | Cut/roll/seam shots; OOS swing toolkit; reads block-and-defender pre-contact. | College-tier OOS swing; approach-velocity and penultimate-step trained as personal levers; Zones 6/1 in-system reliable. |
| **Blocking** | Reach-blocking; run-back landings default. | Read-blocking footwork; arm-swing penetration intermittent. | Swing-blocking footwork operational; step-back default; controlled-stick landing. | Bunch-read overlay install-ready; commit situationally vs. dominant middles. | Bunch-read installed; hand-press penetration; landing mechanics audited every set; commit-call instant. |
| **Serving** | Standing-float only or unreliable jump-float. | Jump-float in; aim drifts; jump-topspin attempted but inconsistent. | Jump-float reliable to two zones; aim-small framing internalized. | Jump-topspin emerging; ace/miss balance internalized; pressure-rehearsal evident. | Jump-topspin a recruiting-relevant differentiator; approach-velocity refined; five-before-ten architecture rehearsed and applied. |
| **Defense** | Reactive ball-watching; no rotation scheme. | Rotation-specific scheme partial; pursuit footwork breaks down. | Libero-in-6 default; Three P's audible; reads ball, not hitter. | Reads hitter pre-contact; pursuit-and-recovery automatic; late-match valgus drift correcting. | Rotation-one tactical levers (bunch-read / commit / shading / short-serve disrupt) callable; third-set form intact. |
| **Transition** | Block lands and stops; dig-to-approach undecided; OOS bail absent. | Block-to-approach inconsistent; setter second-ball protocol breaks down. | Block-to-approach automatic; OOS detect-call-bail functional; setter second-ball clean. | Dig-to-approach pre-decided; libero second-ball overhead from behind 3m operational. | Third-transition-rally execution at college-bridge floor; OOS-to-pin instant; setter second-ball clinical. |
| **Mental / coachability** | Blames; freelances role; quiet under pressure. | Accepts feedback post-drill; pre-performance routine absent. | Ownership audible; self-correction within drill; explicit role-acceptance. | Self-talk and imagery scaffolded; pressure routines visible; sense-of-accomplishment self-reports. | Championship behaviors operational [mccutcheon-2022-championship-behaviors]; 8-week self-talk install evident; pressure-routine durable; superpower self-defined. |

## Scoring

Each evaluator scores all eight criteria independently 1-5, then a position-specific weighting yields the composite:

- **Setter:** setting × 2, defense × 1.5, all others × 1.
- **Libero / DS:** passing × 2, defense × 2, all others × 1, blocking × 0.5, hitting × 0.5.
- **OH / Opp:** hitting × 2, passing × 1.5 (OH only), all others × 1.
- **MB:** blocking × 2, hitting × 1.5, transition × 1.5, passing × 0.5.

Composite cuts: **40+ college-ready**; **32-39 returning 18s starter**; **24-31 development-track**; **<24 misplacement** (consider 17s).

Athletic-profile measurements (four-number physical card per [[usav-nd-college-recruitment-timeline]]) are recorded but not scored — they feed [[recruiting]] conversations, not the on-court composite.

## Calibration notes

**Inter-rater agreement.** Use 2-3 evaluators; average independent scores; flag any criterion with >2-point spread for live review. The 1-5 anchors are cell-text-specific — read the column header, not the number, when scoring.

**Tryout-session arc.** Warm-up + skill-station scores criteria in isolation (passing, setting, hitting, serving). Controlled drill scores blocking, defense, transition under cooperative load. Scrimmage with end-game pressure (Munciana Biggie Smalls / Prove It / Finish Strong style) scores mental/coachability and stress-tests every other criterion [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Mental score weights scrimmage observation 70 / interview 30.

**Common biases.**
- **Halo from height.** Tall MBs default-score 4 on blocking; force the cell-text read (penetration, hand-press, landing).
- **Recency bias.** Last-rally errors anchor whole-tryout impressions; require evaluators to fill the rubric in two passes (mid-session + end).
- **Recruiting halo.** A committed athlete is not automatically a 5; column 5 is observable execution, not commitment status.
- **Familiarity bias.** Returning club athletes default-score higher; calibrate against the cell text, not against last year.

**Recruiting overlay.** For the recruiting-active subset, [[recruiting]] criteria (target-list discipline, film standards, coach-communication, camp investment, signing-date awareness — women's volleyball single initial signing date 2025-26 is November 12, 2025) run as a parallel evaluation. A college-ready scorecard with a chaotic recruiting process is a coachable gap, not a tryout-failure gap.
