# Tryout Rubric: 17s

## Purpose

This rubric scores 17s tryout candidates on eight skill and behavior rows using a 1-5 scale. It is the operational instrument that translates the [[17s]] `## Promotion criteria` section into evaluator-graded scores during a 60-90-minute tryout session. The 17s year is the **advanced** phase of the club arc and overlaps directly with the NCAA Division I women's volleyball recruiting calendar — D1 contact opens June 15 after sophomore year, unofficial and official visits open August 1 before junior year, and the November 12 initial signing date sits inside the HS varsity → club transition. The implication for tryout calibration: column 5 is not "best 17s in the gym," it is **18s-ready / college-bridge** — the candidate could step onto an [[18s]] roster tomorrow and be evaluable by college coaches at AAU Nationals. Column 3 is age-appropriate 17s. Column 1 is below-band and triggers a retention conversation per [[aoc-2025-cc-tryouts]] and [[aoc-2025-cc-playing-time-roles]]. Coaches running 17s tryouts should pair this rubric with the [[recruiting]] documentation expectations and the [[age-lens-hs]] / [[age-lens-college]] parent-context pages.

## Evaluation criteria

| Skill | 1 — does not demonstrate | 2 — occasional | 3 — consistent at 17s level | 4 — above 17s level | 5 — 18s-ready / college-bridge |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Passing** | SR average <1.5; platform formed early; no overhead pass; seam-shrug visible. | SR ~1.5-1.8; inconsistent late-form; overhead pass tentative; calls "mine" but late. | SR ≥2.0 in match-pace reps; read-then-form sequence visible; overhead used as co-equal tool; calls "mine" early and loud; free-ball reflexively to a high-2. | SR ≥2.3; tilted-pursuit posture mature; free-ball passing to tempo trigger when supported; libero-rate passing across all six rotations. | SR ≥2.5 against jump-topspin; libero-rate passing under pressure; reads server release point pre-contact; can pass at college-recruit baseline at AAU Nationals. |
| **Setting** | Plant-and-push every ball; no jump-set; no OOS hand-set; no audibles called. | Jump-set on easy passes only; bump-set every OOS ball; rare audibles. | Jump-set on every front-row pass to target; hand-set OOS to within 1m on passes within 2m of net; Stotsenberg two-handed-redirect on bumps; audibles on tight/medium/off. | Tempo-1 connection ≥40% in scrimmage; backset disguise (no shoulder turn); slide and 31 in repertoire; calls audible based on dig height. | Tempo-1 ≥50% in match; in-rotation OOS protocols reflexive; runs hybrid-5-1 if roster supports; college-recruitable setter touch under fatigue. |
| **Hitting** | Approach jump touch <reach + 18"; hits one shot (angle); no back-row attack; no shot toolbox. | Touch reach + 18-22"; cut and line emerging; back-row attempt rare; pre-contact intention missing. | Touch reach + 24" working baseline; shot-toolbox (cut/line/angle/tip/roll/high-line) catalogued and called; back-row pipe or D-attack reliable; efficiency ≥0.200. | Touch reach + 28"; reads block pre-contact and chooses shot intentionally; middle-tempo (1, 31, slide) reliable for MB; efficiency ≥0.250. | Touch reach + 32"+; range-based decision-making at college-evaluable level; back-row attack a primary scoring option; female-spike-biomech-sound penultimate-step + approach velocity; college-recruitable kill rate against college-style block. |
| **Blocking** | No swing-block footwork; run-back landing (knee-valgus risk); guesses every rep. | Swing-block emerging on outside; reads setter occasionally; run-back landing intermittent. | Swing-blocking footwork mature with controlled stick or step-back landing; reads setter and hitter every rep; bunch-read pinches and releases on seam sets; press over net with hand discipline. | Closes the seam with pin partner consistently; commit-block called situationally; lateral footwork to slide cleanly; hand-press disciplined under pressure. | Forms walls against college-pace tempo offense; bunch-read pin blocker reads and releases at college-evaluable speed; commit-block tool against dominant MBs; ACL-safe landing every rep. |
| **Serving** | No jump-serve; error rate >25%; no target call; pulls intent on big points. | Jump-float emerging; error rate 20-25%; targets called occasionally; aggressive intent inconsistent. | Jump-topspin or jump-float as primary serve by physical profile; error rate <15%; targets called pre-serve; pre-serve routine visible; serve-streak >3 once per set. | Error rate <12%; ace rate ≥8%; serves to scout-driven targets; pressure-server mentality on game-points. | Jump-topspin reliable with approach velocity at college-recruitable level (Bari U-19 baseline); ace rate ≥10%; serves to seams under match pressure; weapon-server profile. |
| **Defense** | Forms platform pre-contact; no pursuit; no Three P's; no read. | Reads ball not hitter; pursuit emerging; seam responsibilities unclear. | Read-then-arrive-stopped-then-platform sequence visible; Three P's executed (get back, get low, get a touch); seam responsibilities clear and called; pursuit footwork to digs outside primary zone. | Up-defense available situationally against tip-heavy opponents; libero-in-6 mature; rotation-specific defensive schemes called. | Reads hitter pre-contact at college-evaluable speed; pursuit-and-touch on every off-block ball; defensive captain calls; college-recruitable libero / DS profile. |
| **Transition** | No block-to-approach recovery; OOS hitter crowds setter; no setter second-ball protocol. | Block-to-approach footwork emerging; OOS bail-to-pin inconsistent; setter second-ball ad-hoc. | Out-of-system bail-to-pin on broken digs (Booth framing); block-to-approach recovery footwork on every block-to-attack sequence; setter second-ball protocol reflexive; in-rotation OOS protocols installed and called. | Audibles from setter trigger correct hitter response without coaching cue; dig-to-approach tempo maintained; OOS attack converts. | Transition offense scores at college-evaluable rate; OOS attack a primary weapon, not a survival reflex; reads broken-pass tempo and selects high-2 vs tempo trigger pre-contact. |
| **Mental & coachability** | Phone-checks on bench; blames teammates; defensive to corrections; slumped between points. | Pre-performance routines absent; mixed body language; corrections received passively. | Pre-performance routines visible on serve and pass; encourages teammates after errors; adjusts to coaching corrections without defensiveness; purposeful between-point body language; engaged bench presence. | Pressure-acclimatization visible on game-points; self-talk audible and constructive; imagery routines coachable; locker-room leader behaviors emerging. | Championship-Behaviors profile (athlete: works/learns/competes); coaches the bench; recruiter-evaluable carriage at AAU Nationals; college-recruitable mental profile. |

## Scoring

Each evaluator scores all 8 rows independently for every candidate. Sum across rows for a composite (range 8-40). Apply these decision thresholds aligned to the [[17s]] promotion criteria:

- **Composite 32-40 with 4+ on three or more rows including hitting/setting/passing:** college-bridge candidate; place on recruiting-track roster and align documentation per [[recruiting]].
- **Composite 24-31 with at minimum a 3 on every row:** age-appropriate 17s; standard roster placement.
- **Composite 16-23 or any single row at 1:** retention conversation candidate. Frame per [[aoc-2025-cc-tryouts]] and [[aoc-2025-cc-playing-time-roles]] — athletic-fit issue, not character issue.
- **Composite <16:** below-band; offer development pathway or alternate program.

Position-weight the composite when needed. For a libero candidate, weight passing + defense + transition double; for a setter, weight setting + transition + mental-coachability double; for a MB, weight blocking + hitting double.

## Calibration notes

**Session structure (60-90 minutes).** Block 1 (15 min): warm-up + ball-control — score passing baseline only. Block 2 (15 min): position-specific work — score the position-relevant rows under controlled conditions. Block 3 (20 min): scripted 6v6 with audible-required scoring — score setting, hitting, blocking, transition under match-like pressure. Block 4 (15 min): pressure-serving + king-of-the-court — score serving under stake. Block 5 (10 min): scripted OOS scenarios + bench rotation — score transition + mental/coachability with explicit coaching corrections introduced to test response.

**Inter-rater agreement.** Use ≥2 evaluators per court. After each block, evaluators compare scores on 2-3 anchor candidates before continuing — this catches drift early. Disagreements >1 point trigger a brief calibration discussion. Per [[aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most]], stat-driven anchors (SR average, error rate, kill rate) should override impression where data is available.

**Common biases to avoid.** (1) **Returning-player halo** — a 16s returner gets a 4 on coachability because she knows the staff; score the behavior, not the relationship. (2) **Height-bias on hitting** — tall candidates get inflated hitting scores; the rubric is approach-touch + shot-toolbox, not standing reach alone. (3) **Loud-passer bias** — vocal candidates get inflated coachability; quiet competence (Lingenfelter accountability-passing frame) is a 4. (4) **Recency bias** — last 5 reps weight too heavily; use the full block. (5) **Munciana five-before-ten on serving** — do not penalize aggressive serving errors that fit the aim-small-miss-small frame; penalize timid serving that fits no frame.

The 17s rubric is the most college-recruiter-relevant of the age-band rubrics. Run it as if a college coach is watching, because at AAU Nationals one is.
