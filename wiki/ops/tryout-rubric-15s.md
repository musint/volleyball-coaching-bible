# Tryout Rubric: 15s

## Purpose

This rubric translates the [[15s]] `## Promotion criteria` section into a 1-5 scoring scale a coaching staff can apply consistently across a single 60-90 minute tryout session. Use it to evaluate athletes graduating from [[14s]] into the 15s specialization year, and to triangulate which 15s players are tracking 16s-ready by season end. The rubric is calibrated against the [[age-lens-hs]] parent context — most 15s tryout candidates are simultaneously HS freshmen on a JV or varsity roster, and the operational standards there are the standards a serious 15s club trains to.

The rubric's design unifying-principle: **Column 5 (next-age-ready) describes a 16s starting-roster baseline.** A player scoring 5 across most rows is ready to start at 16s today. A player scoring 3 across most rows is age-appropriate at 15s. Below 2 sustained across multiple rows indicates the player is still tracking 14s milestones and may need a developmental rather than competitive 15s placement.

## Evaluation criteria

| Criterion | 1 — Does not demonstrate | 2 — Occasional | 3 — Consistent at age-appropriate level | 4 — Above age-appropriate | 5 — 16s-ready |
|-----------|--------------------------|-----------------|-----------------------------------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Passing** | Platform breaks under jump-float; passes drop below 1.5 grade; cannot verbalize what she saw | Platform stable on standing-float; serve-receive grade 1.5-1.9; reads the ball but not the server | Forearm platform stable across serve types; pass-grade 2.0+ on age-peer servers; uses overhead pass on shoulder-height balls; B-S-B-H read articulable when prompted | Pass-grade 2.2+; stays in passing rotation under pressure; adjusts pre-serve positioning to opponent server tendencies | Libero-track pass discipline (2.3+ grade); owns every pass without deflection per Lingenfelter framing; tracks server zones across full opponent lineup |
| **Setting** | Palm-sets or wrist-flicks; backset telegraphed; cannot run a clean 5-1 rotation | Hand-hinge intermittent; basic 4-2 reliable; jump-set attempted but ball position inconsistent | Hand-hinge with side-of-ball contact locked in; basic 5-1 across three rotations; jump-set on at-net tempo balls; backset without shoulder dip | Complex 6-2 with audibles viable; out-of-system bail-to-pin reliable; tempo varied (1/2/3) on demand | Two-handed OOS redirects consistent; setter reads block before contact; runs hybrid 5-1/6-2 cleanly across all six rotations |
| **Hitting** | Constant-tempo approach; no closing footwork; one-shot hitter (cross-only); contact below peak reach | Modern 4-step approach emerging; two reliable shots; arm mechanics inconsistent under pressure | 4-step approach with start-slow / accelerate-the-last-two; three reliable shots (cross/line + one of angle/tip/roll); MacDonald high-hand on most reps; pipe attempted | Penultimate-step length matches physical profile; reads block to choose shot; back-row attack from Zone 1 or 6 emerging | Four+ shot types deployable; back-row attack reliable in transition; approach velocity is a leverage point against age-peer blocks |
| **Blocking** | Run-back landings habitual; cannot articulate spread vs bunch; no swing-block footwork | Step-back landing intermittent; spread block default; swing-block footwork attempted dry | Stick or step-back landing default per [[zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus]]; bunch-read concept articulable; swing-block footwork clean without jump | Jump-added swing-block emerging on outside; reads setter hand release; bunch-read applied situationally against real middle threats | Bunch-read default scheme viable; swing-block jump-execution reliable on outside; commit-block deployed against dominant middles |
| **Serving** | Standing-float only; tosses inconsistent; serves "just over the net" with no target | Standing-topspin emerging; jump-float attempted but toss unreliable; some target awareness | Jump-float as primary serve; standing-topspin retained as backup; aim-small-miss-small target accountability | Jump-float zone-accurate at 70%+ in-zone rate; jump-topspin attempted and landing in court; five-before-ten mentality visible | Jump-topspin reliable as primary serve; serves to 2-inch target areas under pressure; no-gut-serving aggressive default |
| **Defense** | Cannot identify her defensive system; no Three P's pattern; arrives unbalanced | Three P's intermittent; perimeter defense only; hard-driven balls bounce off | Stone hips-under-ball framework readable on hard-driven digs; Three P's executed under pursuit; identifies her role inside team's defense | Defense-rotation reads (libero in 6) executed; Look/See/Decide diagnostic articulable; arrives stopped-then-platform | Middle-back defense default; reads hitter-not-ball; libero-track reads execute against age-peer hitters across all six rotations |
| **Transition** | Block-to-approach and dig-to-approach happen by accident; OOS undetected | Block-to-approach footwork emerging; OOS detect-call inconsistent | Block-to-approach and dig-to-approach automatic; OOS detect-call-bail-to-pin reliable on bail-to-pin half | OOS two-handed redirects attempted; setter-second-ball protocol executed; libero overhead set from behind 3m emerging | Full OOS toolkit consistent under pressure; transition attack from any defensive starting position; tempo varied through transition |
| **Mental & coachability** | Blames serve/floor/teammate; cannot articulate what she saw; visible defensiveness on correction | Owns occasional reps; takes correction without protest but does not adapt | Sustained engagement with self-talk or pre-performance routine over multi-month block; differentiates process vs result feedback; takes hard correction in front of teammates without defensiveness | Coaches teammates; demonstrates Munciana five-before-ten reset between reps; verbalizes reads back to coach unprompted | Generalized-specialist mental model per McCutcheon framing; owns the result of every rep across full match; pressure-acclimatized through deliberate routine work |

## Scoring

Sum all eight rows for a composite 8-40 score. Reference bands:

- **34-40 (avg 4.25+):** 16s starting-roster ready. Consider varsity-track placement and accelerated curriculum.
- **27-33 (avg 3.4-4.1):** Tracking 16s by season end. Standard 15s competitive placement.
- **20-26 (avg 2.5-3.3):** Age-appropriate 15s. Standard 15s placement with development emphasis on 1-2 weakest rows.
- **15-19 (avg 1.9-2.4):** 14s milestones still resolving. Consider developmental 15s, second team, or extended 14s if program structure allows.
- **Below 15 (avg <1.9):** Not yet 15s-ready. Direct conversation with player and family about appropriate placement.

Position-weighted scoring is acceptable: for a libero candidate, double-weight Passing and Defense; for a setter candidate, double-weight Setting and Mental; for a middle candidate, double-weight Blocking and Hitting. Document the weighting before tryouts begin so it is consistent across evaluators.

## Calibration notes

**Session structure.** A 60-90 minute tryout cannot evaluate eight criteria with equal depth. Run a 15-minute warmup (observe Athletic profile / movement quality without scoring), 20 minutes of skill stations (Passing / Setting / Hitting rotated through), 20 minutes of small-sided play (Defense / Transition / Blocking observed in 3v3 or 4v4), and 20 minutes of 6v6 wash drill (Mental / coachability foregrounded; cross-validate skill scores under live conditions). Score during 6v6, not during stations — stations isolate technique but the rubric describes game performance.

**Multi-evaluator consistency.** Two evaluators per court minimum. Score independently, then reconcile rows where you differ by 2+ points before composite. The most common inter-rater disagreement at 15s is on Mental & coachability — anchor it to the observable behaviors in the rubric (does she own the rep? does she articulate the read? does she reset between attempts?), not to evaluator gut-feel.

**Common biases to avoid.** Early-maturer halo (a tall, physically mature 15s often scores high on athletic eye-test but row-3 on Mental); recency bias (the last drill block is overweighted); position-mismatch bias (a libero-track athlete evaluated against an OH-shaped rubric will under-score). The rubric's 1-5 scale describes observable behaviors at each level, not coach impressions; if the column-3 description fits, score 3.

**ACL-mechanism red flags** override scoring. If an athlete demonstrates run-back block landings with knee-valgus collapse [sassi-2025-acl-volleyball-systematic-review][zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus], flag for the ACL-prevention curriculum regardless of composite score. Mechanism risk is a coaching priority, not a placement decision.
