# Club-Ops: SafeSport Compliance

## Purpose

SafeSport is the U.S. Center for SafeSport's mandated abuse-prevention program for the U.S. Olympic & Paralympic movement, and USA Volleyball requires every member coach (and most adult-staff roles around minor athletes) to clear three baseline items before they touch the gym: SafeSport training, IMPACT, and a background check. SafeSport must be completed before any athlete interaction. This doc gives a club director (and the coaches she leads) a single checklist for staying compliant: training cycles, reporting duties, the policies that govern locker rooms, one-on-one interactions, and transportation, plus a parent/athlete acknowledgment template that documents the policy was communicated.

This page assumes the reader is operating a USAV-affiliated junior club. See [[usa-volleyball]] for the broader USAV organizational context, and [[recruiting]] / [[philosophy]] for adjacent club-leadership responsibilities.

## Process

**1. Initial training (every new coach, every season).** Every coach, every paid staff member with regular athlete contact, and every adult chaperone on club travel must complete the SafeSport Trained Core course before athlete interaction begins. Course is delivered through the SportsEngine Member Management System tied to the coach's USAV membership. Block time during onboarding — do not let "I'll do it next week" turn into a coach running practice while non-compliant. Staple SafeSport completion to the same onboarding gate as IMPACT and the background check; one trio, one due date.

**2. Annual refresher.** SafeSport completion expires; refresher courses are required each subsequent membership year. The refresher is shorter than the Core course but is non-negotiable — a lapsed certificate makes a coach ineligible to coach. Build refresher reminders into the membership-renewal sweep (typically late summer for fall club seasons).

**3. Background checks.** USAV mandates a background check on every member coach at registration and renewal. Treat the background check as a separate workflow with its own renewal cadence; do not assume that SafeSport renewal carries it.

**4. Reporting requirements.** Coaches and club staff are mandatory reporters under SafeSport's Mandatory Reporting policy: any reasonable suspicion of sexual misconduct involving a minor must be reported to the U.S. Center for SafeSport (800-790-2226 or report online) AND to law enforcement. Other forms of misconduct are reported to the U.S. Center for SafeSport or, in some categories, to USA Volleyball directly. Train every coach and director to know the reporting line by heart; do not let "I'll ask the head coach first" become an excuse for delay.

**5. Documentation.** Maintain a per-coach compliance binder or shared sheet with: SafeSport completion date, SafeSport expiration date, IMPACT completion, background-check status and expiration, signed staff acknowledgments of club SafeSport/MAAPP policies. Keep documentation for the duration of employment plus a retention tail (consult counsel for jurisdiction-specific retention windows).

**6. Locker-room and changing-area policy.** Adopt a written policy aligned with the USOPC Minor Athlete Abuse Prevention Policies (MAAPP): no closed-door one-adult/one-minor in a locker room or changing area; two-deep adult supervision when adults must be present; no recording devices in locker rooms or changing areas. Communicate the policy to athletes and parents in writing at season start.

**7. One-on-one interaction policy.** Default to "observable and interruptible" — any one-on-one between an adult and a minor athlete (instruction, mentoring, conversation, transportation) must occur in a setting where another adult could see or hear. Avoid private rooms with closed doors, private vehicles, private DMs, and private social-media follows. If individual instruction is necessary, do it on an open court with another adult present.

**8. Transportation policy.** Club-sanctioned travel must be parent- or guardian-driven, parent-of-another-athlete-driven (with written consent), or a multi-adult chartered bus. No coach drives a single minor athlete alone — even short distances. If a parent fails to pick up after practice, two adults wait with the athlete; the coach does not drive the athlete home solo.

**9. Electronic communication policy.** Group communications only — team-wide texts, parent group chats, team-management apps with parents on the thread. No private DMs to minor athletes on Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, or personal phone. Document the policy and have parents acknowledge it.

## Templates

### SafeSport Policy Acknowledgment (parents + athletes, season start)

> **[Club Name] SafeSport & Athlete Safety Acknowledgment — [Season Year]**
>
> Our club follows USA Volleyball SafeSport requirements and the USOPC Minor Athlete Abuse Prevention Policies (MAAPP). The policies below apply to all coaches, staff, chaperones, parents, and athletes:
>
> 1. **Coach training.** All [Club Name] coaches and staff with athlete contact have completed SafeSport training, IMPACT, and a background check.
> 2. **Reporting.** Any concerns involving athlete safety should be reported to the head coach, club director, or directly to the U.S. Center for SafeSport at 800-790-2226 or safesport.org. Coaches are mandatory reporters of suspected sexual misconduct involving minors.
> 3. **One-on-one interactions.** Coach/athlete interactions occur in observable, interruptible settings. No closed-door private meetings, no private vehicles, no private DMs.
> 4. **Locker rooms.** No recording devices. No one-adult/one-minor closed-door arrangements. Two-deep adult supervision when adults are present.
> 5. **Transportation.** Athletes are transported by their parent/guardian, by another team parent (with written consent), or by chartered group transport. No coach transports a single minor athlete alone.
> 6. **Electronic communication.** Coaches communicate with athletes through team-wide channels with parents included. No private DMs.
>
> **Athlete signature:** _____________________ **Date:** _________
> **Parent/Guardian signature:** _____________________ **Date:** _________

Distribute at the season-kickoff parent meeting, collect signatures, retain in the coach/athlete file.

## Common pitfalls

- **Missing renewal deadlines.** A SafeSport certificate that lapsed three days ago is a non-compliant coach today. Calendar refresher and background-check expirations 30 days before the deadline; do not rely on the SportsEngine email reminder alone.
- **Informal one-on-one situations.** "She just stayed after to ask about her arm swing" turns into a closed-gym, one-adult-one-minor situation. Make the policy operational: practice ends, doors stay propped open, a second adult lingers until every athlete is picked up.
- **Transportation gaps.** The pickup falls through, the coach drives the athlete home "just this once," and the policy is broken. Pre-game the failure mode: two adults always close out practice; the coach calls a second parent before driving anyone.
- **Treating SafeSport as a compliance checkbox.** The training is the floor, not the ceiling. The point is athlete safety; the documentation exists so a parent, regional commissioner, or investigator can verify it later.
