# Match-Prep — In-Match Adjustment Decision Aid

## Purpose

In-match adjustments are the decisions [[match-prep]] cannot make in advance. The pre-match scouting report tells you what you expect to see; this template tells you what to do when the score, the rotation chart, or the body language tells you something different. It is built for a head coach managing the next point while a bench assistant tracks rotation-by-rotation patterns — the standard division of labor at competitive club, HS, and college matches.

The frame is **scoring-milestone triggers** (5-point, 12-point, 20-point) plus **always-watching triggers** (momentum, rotation collapse, individual breakdown). At each trigger you ask the same three questions: what does the observation actually say, what adjustment options does it open, and what does each option cost — because timeouts, subs, and lineup pivots all spend a finite resource (timeouts, sub count, player confidence, or roster trust). Pressure-management overlaps with [[mental]]: the timeout is a defensive weapon against momentum as much as a tactical one.

## Inputs

- Pre-match scouting form ([[match-prep-scouting-form]]) and rotation tendency form ([[match-prep-opponent-tendency-form]]) on the bench clipboard.
- Live stat sheet ([[match-prep-stat-collection-sheet]]) updated by an assistant or a stats-trained parent volunteer — minimally side-out percentage by rotation, FBSO, hitter efficiency, server ace-to-error.
- A sub plan: pre-set defensive sub points, serve-receive subs, and one or two "break-glass" lineup pivots prepared in the 48-hour window.
- Two timeouts per set (USAV indoor) plus the technical pause if applicable. **Treat them as scarce.**

## Form

The decision aid is a single table you read down the leftmost column, scanning for whichever trigger fired most recently.

| Trigger (when) | Observation needed (what) | Adjustment options | Cost / risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **5-point check (early-set)** | Are we siding out in the rotations the scout said we'd struggle in? Is the opponent serving the rotation we predicted? Is our serving aggressive-vs.-error tracking the plan? | Confirm plan is intact and stay quiet, **or** flag one rotation to watch more carefully, **or** reset a server's risk band ("take 5% off your serve") | Low. The 5-point check is mostly diagnostic; the temptation is to overreact to a 2-3 deficit on small-sample randomness. |
| **12-point check (mid-set)** | Side-out percentage by rotation, hitter efficiency by hitter, opponent's serving target patterns. Is one of our hitters being targeted on serve and breaking down? Is one rotation under 50% side-out? | First defensive sub if pre-planned; serve-receive sub for a struggling primary passer; targeted serving call (attack the worst passer, per Rose's bad-servers frame) [aoc-2018-rose-bad-servers]; rotational defense shift against a hot opponent hitter [aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics] | Medium. Subs are limited (USAV indoor ~6 per set with re-entry rules). Defensive subs spend trust if reversed quickly. |
| **20-point check (late-set leverage)** | Score margin, momentum direction, who is at the line for the opponent on the next big point. Are we within 2-3 with a rotation flip coming, or are we trading sideouts in a stable margin? | High-leverage timeout to break opponent server's rhythm; setter-dump or slide call at the right rotation; pinch-hitter sub for a serving specialist; defensive shift on the opponent's biggest finisher [aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics] | High. Timeouts are nearly gone. A wrong sub here costs a set. The cost of *no* adjustment is also high — late-set passivity is a Rose anti-pattern [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep]. |
| **Momentum trigger (always)** | Three-point opponent run, body-language collapse, repeated unforced errors in one rotation, or audible bench-tone shift. | Timeout (stop the run, reset routine), not a strategic timeout — call it on **momentum** rather than scoreline alone [aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics]. Optional: serve-receive shuffle to put your best passer on their best server. | Medium. Spends a timeout but the alternative is conceding a 4-5 point swing. |
| **Server-pressure trigger (always)** | Your server at the line on a big point — the side-out-to-break or set-point moment. | Cue the server to their training-level routine and pre-decided risk band; **do not** add complexity. Pressure compresses skill — the move is to simplify, not to ask for more [aoc-2024-kiraly-serving-pressure]. | Low. Cost is verbal — get the cue right or skip it. |
| **Rotation-collapse trigger (always)** | One rotation is under 40% side-out across two sets, or one player is the clear failure point in that rotation. | Defensive sub, serve-receive sub, or rotation-skip via timed substitution. If a starter is the failure point, McCutcheon's frame applies: the standard of competitive excellence is more important than protecting one player's minutes [mccutcheon-2021-volleybrains-ep30]. | High emotional cost (player trust); high competitive cost of *not* adjusting. Choose deliberately, not reflexively. |
| **Self-character trigger (always)** | Are we still playing our game? Defensive intensity, swing aggressiveness, bench engagement — the markers your team uses to identify itself. | Reset cue ("come back to who we are"), short timeout, or a captain-led huddle. The Dunning frame: a team that loses its character before the big point loses the big point [aoc-2017-dunning-stanford-character]. | Low. The cost of skipping this trigger is large but invisible until later. |

## Workflow

1. **Pre-match (locker room):** review the scouting and tendency forms; pre-decide the first defensive sub, the first serve-receive sub, and the "break-glass" lineup pivot.
2. **5-point check:** confirm plan; one-sentence cue to the team if needed.
3. **12-point check:** first sub window; first targeted-serving call.
4. **20-point check:** leverage decisions; reserve at least one timeout for momentum.
5. **Always-watching:** momentum, server-pressure, rotation-collapse, self-character — these fire on event, not on score.
6. **Between sets:** quick rotation-by-rotation read with the bench assistant; one adjustment to install for the next set, not three.
7. **Post-match:** flag any trigger you missed for the [[match-prep-video-review-workflow]] session.
