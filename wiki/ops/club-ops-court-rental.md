# Club-Ops: Court Rental

## Purpose

Securing and managing practice and tournament-host court space is the largest controllable line item in a junior club budget and the single biggest constraint on roster size, training volume, and tournament-hosting revenue. This doc covers how a club director identifies venues, negotiates rental terms, satisfies insurance requirements, and engineers backup capacity so a single facility loss does not cancel a season. It links to [[philosophy]] (program identity drives venue choice), [[recruiting]] (visible, accessible courts recruit athletes), and [[club-ops-fee-structure]] (court hours are the dominant fee component).

## Process

### 1. Identify candidate venues

Three venue archetypes cover the junior-club market:

- **Public and private high schools.** Most common path. Rates run $25–$60/court/hour in most US markets, sometimes free if a club coach is also on staff. Pros: low cost, geographic spread, parent familiarity. Cons: school activities take priority — playoffs, dances, graduation, finals all displace volleyball; summer access is often unavailable; nets and standards may be sub-spec.
- **Dedicated volleyball / multi-sport facilities.** Volleyball-specific or court-club facilities (4–8 courts under one roof). Rates $40–$120/court/hour. Pros: spec equipment, predictable schedule, room for tournament hosting, marketing visibility. Cons: cost; many markets do not have one; landlord clubs often run their own competing program.
- **Community centers, churches, recreation departments.** Rates $15–$45/court/hour. Pros: community goodwill, often available off-peak. Cons: shared use with basketball / pickleball / events; floor type and ceiling height variable; nets and antennae may not meet USAV spec.

Build a venue inventory spreadsheet listing: facility name, contact, rate card, ceiling height, floor type, court count, available hours by day-of-week, and a 1–5 score on parking, locker rooms, and restroom adequacy. Refresh annually. Revenue-positive clubs typically run 60–80% of practice hours in 1–2 anchor venues with the remainder spread across overflow sites.

### 2. Negotiate rental terms

Lock these terms in writing every season — verbal arrangements drift:

- **Rate.** Per-court-hour for practice; flat day rate or per-match rate for tournaments. Negotiate multi-team / multi-night discounts (10–20% reductions are common for clubs taking 200+ hours per season).
- **Block reservation.** Lock recurring days and times for the full season at signing. Without a block, you are competing every week with school events and other tenants.
- **Cancellation rights.** The facility's right to bump you for school events should be capped (e.g., no more than 4 cancellations per season) and obligate replacement dates or fee refunds.
- **Equipment.** Specify whose nets, antennae, standards, and pads will be used. If the club brings equipment, secure storage on-site.
- **Access window.** Doors-open and gym-locked times that bracket the rental window — coaches and players need 15+ minutes for setup and teardown.
- **Custodial / event-fee adders.** Many high schools tack on a custodian or AD fee per use; these can double the published rate. Get the all-in number.

### 3. Insurance requirements

Most facilities require the club to carry general liability insurance and to name the facility as additional insured. USA Volleyball junior club membership includes a baseline general liability policy that satisfies most public-school requirements [[usav-2026-resources-for-coaches]]. Verify per-occurrence limits ($1M minimum is common; some private and university facilities require $2M+). Carry a copy of the certificate to every site visit during the season; produce on request. For tournaments — especially when hosting non-USAV teams or using non-USAV facilities — a separate event-day rider is often required.

### 4. Backup-venue planning

Treat single-venue dependency as an existential risk. A boiler failure, a roof leak, a school playoff run, or a contract dispute can erase your facility two weeks before nationals. Mitigate:

- Identify and pre-qualify a Plan B and a Plan C facility before the season starts. Run at least one practice per season at each so the keys, contacts, and access logistics are tested.
- Keep one community-center or rec-department contract on retainer even if you do not use the hours — small monthly minimum, large optionality.
- Build a parent-driven car-pool tree for emergency relocations; do not rely on individual outreach.

## Templates

### Facility-use agreement template

> **Facility-Use Agreement**
>
> This agreement is between **[Facility Name]** ("Facility") and **[Club Name]** ("Club").
>
> 1. **Term:** [Start date] through [End date].
> 2. **Block reservation:** Club reserves [N] courts on [days] from [start time] to [end time].
> 3. **Rate:** $[X] per court-hour, payable monthly in arrears. Total estimated season cost: $[Y].
> 4. **Cancellation by Facility:** Facility may cancel up to [4] dates per season for school events with [10] days' notice. Cancelled dates will be made up at a mutually agreeable time or refunded.
> 5. **Cancellation by Club:** Club may cancel any session with [48] hours' notice without fee.
> 6. **Insurance:** Club carries $1M general liability via USA Volleyball; certificate attached. Facility is named as additional insured.
> 7. **Equipment:** [Specify nets, antennae, standards, pads — owner of each, and on-site storage.]
> 8. **Access:** Doors open 15 minutes before each session; building locked 15 minutes after.
> 9. **Custodial / event fees:** $[Z] per session, included in the per-court-hour rate above.
> 10. **Termination:** Either party may terminate with [30] days' written notice.
>
> Signed: _______________ (Facility) _______________ (Club Director)

### Cancellation / weather policy template

> **Inclement Weather and Cancellation Policy**
>
> Practices follow the host facility's closure decisions. If [School District / Facility] closes due to weather, practice is cancelled — no separate decision is made by the club. Notification by [6:00 PM] via team app.
>
> Coach-discretion cancellations (illness outbreak, equipment failure) are sent by [4:00 PM] via team app and email. Cancelled sessions are not automatically rescheduled; if total cancellations exceed [3] in a season, makeup dates will be coordinated.
>
> Tournament play follows USAV / host-tournament weather policy; the club does not independently cancel tournament participation absent a closure or USAV directive.

## Common pitfalls

- **Inadequate insurance.** Assuming a school's insurance covers the club is a recurring expensive mistake. Always carry your own policy and add the facility as additional insured. The USAV junior-club policy is the cheapest baseline path [[usav-2026-resources-for-coaches]].
- **No backup venue.** Clubs that lose their anchor facility mid-season without a Plan B routinely refund tuition or fold. Pre-qualify alternatives before the season starts, not after the cancellation email arrives.
- **Conflicting use during peak seasons.** Late-October through early March is the peak collision window — high-school season, basketball overlap, holiday closures, and exam periods all compete. Negotiate make-up windows in advance and assume that 10–15% of school-rented hours will move.
- **Verbal-only deals with familiar contacts.** A friendly AD changing roles can erase 200 court hours overnight. Get every term in writing every season.
- **Ignoring board competence.** Court-rental contracts are operational legal documents. The club board should include someone with contracting or property-management experience [[aoc-2014-denney-10-steps-start-club]].
