# Opponent Tendency Form (Rotation-by-Rotation Scout)

## Purpose

The rotation-grid companion to the player-profile [[match-prep]] scouting brief. The player-profile form captures who the opponent's hitters are; this form captures what they actually do in each of the six rotations — serve-receive base, preferred first-tempo set, where the back-row attack lives, and the defensive base they collapse into after a kill or free ball. Russ Rose's rotational-sideout frame makes the case: side-out and FBSO are not team-level metrics, they are six separate metrics, and the rotation is the only useful scouting unit. Karch Kiraly's "stats that matter most" caveat applies — capture the two or three that drive your next decision (block call, defense base, serve target), not the full grid.

Use during pool play, video review, or a pre-match scout. Fill what you have, leave the rest blank, and let the gaps tell you what to watch for in the first set.

## Inputs

- Opponent roster with rotation order (setter location for R1; rotation flow follows clockwise from there).
- Either: (a) live pool-play observation by an assistant or volunteer, (b) prior-match video, or (c) bracket-pool scout from an adjacent court.
- The companion player-profile form (see [[match-prep]] hub) so individual hitter tendencies feed the rotation cells.
- Knowledge of the three serve-receive bases — [[serve-receive-3-player]], [[serve-receive-4-player]], [[serve-receive-libero-split]] — so the SR-base column is meaningful rather than a guess.

**Cross-rotation patterns to flag:**

- Rotations where opponent's SR base changes (e.g., libero-split only when libero is in a passing rotation).
- Rotations where setter is front-row — does the team accept the loss of the front-row attacker, or does the opposite swing? Does the setter still dump?
- Weakest two rotations by sideout (the "bad servers" target — the rotations you are serving aggressively into per Rose's bad-servers frame).
- Rotations where the second-tempo OH option disappears (e.g., the OH is the primary passer in a 3-player base and cannot get to a high-ball approach in time).

## Workflow

1. **Pre-match (T-72 to T-24h).** Pull prior video or last pool-play notes. Fill what you can; mark unknowns explicitly. The unknowns become live-watch priorities in the first set.
2. **Warm-up + first set (T-0 to set-1 end).** Bench assistant fills in remaining cells in real time. Head coach is not filling this — they are coaching. The form is the assistant's job.
3. **Set break.** Bench coach hands the form to the head coach during the 3-minute break. Head coach scans for serve-target rotation, block-load adjustments, and defensive base mismatches. Two or three usable observations is the bar — not a full grid completion.
4. **Mid-match adjustment.** Cross-reference the rotation grid against your own [[match-prep]] stat collection (sideout-by-rotation) so you are matching their weak rotation to your strong serving rotation, not just hunting in the abstract.
5. **Post-match.** File the completed form to your match-prep folder; update if the opponent recurs in a later bracket round. The second meeting is where the form pays off.
