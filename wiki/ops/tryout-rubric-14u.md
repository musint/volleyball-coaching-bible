# Tryout Rubric — 14U (14s)

## Purpose

This rubric scores tryout candidates against the [[14s]] `## Promotion criteria` contract on a 1-5 scale. It is the **operational counterpart** to that age-guide section: every row maps directly to a promotion criterion, and column 5 in every row describes a 15s-ready athlete. Use it for your nationally-ranked club 14U tryout, and reuse it mid-season as a re-evaluation form to track movement toward 15s readiness. The voice and operational depth follow [[mike-lingenfelter]] and [[munciana-volleyball-club]] — short windows, observable behaviors, no projection beyond what the candidate demonstrates in the gym today. Cross-reference [[age-lens-14u]] for the developmental ceilings the rubric should not over-reach.

## Evaluation criteria

| Criterion | 1 — Does not demonstrate | 2 — Occasional | 3 — Consistent at 14s (on-target) | 4 — Above age-appropriate | 5 — 15s-ready |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Passing** | Reach-and-pop platform; chest-trap on jam balls; no pass-and-cover habit | Forearm pass occasionally lands in target zone (<40%); hops back inconsistently; quiet platform under tossed balls only | ≥60% in-system off moderate-pace standing-float; quiet platform with hop-back on jam; pass-and-cover sprint visible after contact; deliberate forearm-vs-overhead read | ≥75% in-system off live-paced float; consistent under jump-float; recovers cleanly off seam balls; coverage-spot habit reflexive | ≥75% in-system off jump-float, including off-platform pass-and-recover; reads server tendency; takes ownership of seam communication mid-rally |
| **Setting** (setter candidates only; non-candidates: score N/A here, weight Hitting/Defense more) | Chest-trap on overhead set; setter pushes through with wrists rather than hinging; no Tempo vocabulary | Hand-hinge inconsistent; outside set delivers a hittable ball ~half the time; backset telegraphs (turns shoulders early) | Hand-hinge with side-of-ball contact; outside set hittable from in-system pass; backset competent without telegraphing; demonstrates Tempo 3 footwork on tossed balls | Tempo 2 sets executable on in-system passes; jump-set off tight passes; varies tempo to opposing block; Tempo 1 footwork demo'd | Tempo 1 executable in 6v6; jump-set default in-system; reads block and varies set-location intent on call; runs a slide or back-quick from in-system |
| **Hitting** | Two-step or flat-tempo approach; flat sidearm swing; no shot inventory beyond "hit hard" | Three-step approach inconsistent; arm swing with low elbow; one shot in inventory | Three-step approach with visible "start slow, accelerate the last two" tempo; elbow-lead, high-hand contact; ≥2 of {big cross, line, tip} usable; female-specific approach-velocity coachable from cue | Approach reliable from out-of-system tosses too; full {big cross, line, tip} inventory situational; angle-vs-line decision pre-contact | Approach + arm swing reliable off jump-set tempos; reads block before contact; deceptive shot selection; backrow-attack-introducible |
| **Blocking** | No outside-foot-high closing position; flat-foot block jumps; default landing is run-back (knee-valgus risk) | Footwork inconsistent; hand penetration shallow; lands stick *or* run-back, no default | Static-and-shuffle reliable; outside-foot-high closing; hand-press with net penetration; default landing is stick or step-back, **never run-back**; demonstrates Rosen 10-foot-line swing-block footwork without jump | Swing-block footwork with low jump; closes seam reliably; reads setter eyes pre-jump; controlled penetration off opposing tempo | Full swing-block execution off jump; reads setter location pre-set; lands controlled stick on every block attempt; bunch-read introducible |
| **Serving** | Toss inconsistent; clears net <60% on standing-float; no target intent — only "deep" | Standing-float reliable to half-court; standing-topspin attempted but inconsistent; aim is general zone, not specific spot | Standing-topspin to zone reliable (≥60% in target zone); jump-float toss consistent (contact a bonus); aim-small target accountability — names spot before serve | Jump-float reliable in matches at moderate pace; standing-topspin ≥75% to call zone; runs short-deep mix on serve | Jump-float at match-default pace; standing-topspin to specific 2m boxes; jump-topspin introducible; aggressive Munciana five-before-ten mentality visible under score pressure |
| **Defense** | Static feet on contact; reaches with platform high; no read on shot-vs-swing; no pursuit on tips | Three P's inconsistent; platform formed too early or too late; reads ball, not hitter | Three P's (get back, get low, get a touch) habitual, not coach-prompted; Look/See/Decide read on shot-vs-swing pre-contact; platform formation late, not early | Reads hitter approach-shape pre-contact; pursues tips from off-position; arrives stopped before platform forms | Reads hitter eye-cue and approach-line; libero-anchor identity in 6v6; covers tip/roll/seam off teammates' platforms |
| **Transition** | Run-back landing default after blocks; no recovery footwork off dig; panic-sets with hands when tight pass arrives | Block-to-approach footwork inconsistent; OOS recognition slow; setter dump or hand-set tight balls | Block-to-approach footwork (step-back default); dig-to-approach footwork live for OHs/opposites; OOS recognition triggers forearm-set or bump-to-teammate, not panic-set | Sub-2-second block-to-approach recovery; OOS-detect-call-bail-to-pin protocol clean; libero-second-ball overhead set from behind 3m | Full block-to-approach into in-system swing; OOS converts to attackable sets at pace; setter-second-ball protocols reliable in 6v6 |
| **Mental & coachability** | Defensive reactivity to feedback; effort drops with score; phone-checks during teammate reps; no in-rally communication | Compliant but silent on cue; effort uneven; communicates retrospectively, not in real time | Self-corrects after **one** cue (not three); consistent effort independent of score; communicates seams/switches/balls in-rally; engaged during teammate reps | Coach-style self-feedback emerging; teammate-correcting in-rally; visibly resets after errors within one rep | Captain-grade communication and self-feedback; raises team baseline by presence; runs Munciana wash-drill culture without coach prompting |

## Scoring

- **Per-row scoring:** every evaluator scores each row 1-5 independently. Setter row scores N/A for non-setter candidates; in that case redistribute weight evenly to Hitting and Defense rows so the candidate's total is comparable.
- **Promotion threshold (15s-ready):** target is **3 across all rows** with Mental & coachability **non-negotiable** — a candidate below 3 on coachability does not promote regardless of skill scores. Reflects the [[14s]] promotion contract: skill ceilings are coachable; coachability deficits compound and gate everything else.
- **Scores of 4-5** flag a candidate for accelerated grouping — pull her into a 15s rep pool during specialized blocks even if rostered at 14s.
- **Scores of 1** in any row trigger a one-cue re-evaluation rep before final scoring; a candidate may be misreading the drill format, not the skill.

## Calibration notes

- **Run a 60-90-minute tryout** with these blocks: dynamic warmup (10) → passing block off tossed + standing-float serves (15) → setter-segregated tempo demo + non-setter hitting block (15) → blocking footwork + landing-pattern check (10) → serving target work, both standing-float and jump-float toss (10) → defense Three P's + Look/See/Decide reads (10) → 6v6 wash-drill segment for transition + mental scoring (15-20). Score after the block ends, not during reps.
- **Inter-rater agreement:** ≥2 evaluators per court; reconcile any score that differs by ≥2 between raters before finalizing. Use the column-3 anchor description as the calibration target — "what would a 14s-on-target athlete look like in this drill right now" — to anchor the disagreement conversation.
- **Common biases to avoid:** (a) projection — scoring physical upside instead of demonstrated skill; (b) returner-bias — over-scoring known athletes from prior years on memory; (c) growth-spurt confound — under-scoring an awkward-phase athlete whose mechanics will re-stabilize within a season; (d) one-skill halo — strong server gets a 4 in passing because she "looks like a player." Score what the gym shows today, write your one-line rationale, and let row-totals speak.
- **Run-back landing is a hard rule, not a scoring nuance.** Any candidate landing run-back-default after blocks scores ≤2 on Blocking row regardless of footwork quality elsewhere [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus]. This is an injury-prevention floor, not a stylistic preference.
- **Female-specific approach-velocity is coachable, not strength-bound.** Do not down-score short-stature candidates whose approach-velocity is a technique gap rather than a power gap [fuchs-2019-male-female-spike-biomechanics] — score her column-3 if technique is corrigible from cue.
- **Munciana operational depth applies directly here:** short windows, fast scoring resets, drill volume in tight geometric formats — the rubric is designed for the format Munciana runs, not for long-line waiting [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. If your tryout uses long passing lines, the rubric scores noise.
