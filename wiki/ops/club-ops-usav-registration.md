# USAV Club + Member Registration

## Purpose

This document is the operating reference for a club director running USA Volleyball club registration and member registration each season. It walks through the annual cycle, names the deadlines that move every year, and codifies the SafeSport, IMPACT, and background-check sequencing that gates eligibility for athletes, coaches, and team officials. The wikilinked anchor is [[usa-volleyball]] — that page is the methodology context; this page is the operational checklist that turns federation policy into a club's annual rhythm.

The SportsEngine Member Management System is the single system of record for everything below. Every athlete, coach, and team official has a SportsEngine profile attached to a club code, and every status check (SafeSport completion date, background-check expiration, IMPACT certificate, age-grade) flows through that profile. When the staff knows where to look in SportsEngine, the rest of the work is calendar discipline.

## Process

**Step 1 — Club registration (early summer).** USAV club registration opens through the home Region. Each Region sets its own opening date (typically late June through early August) and its own fee — there is no single national club-registration window. The club director submits the club application through the Region's portal, pays the club fee, and receives the club code that every member registration will attach to. Insurance enrollment runs in parallel; verify the policy covers all practice and tournament venues before publishing schedules. **Do not advertise tryouts before the club code is active** — athletes can technically register without it, but their memberships will not roll up to your roster.

**Step 2 — Coach eligibility (before any contact with athletes).** Every coach on the staff must clear three components before stepping on the court: **IMPACT** (Increased Mastery and Professional Application of Coaching Theory) — the four-hour on-demand baseline course, free for members, taken through the USAV Academy [usav-2026-coach-education]; **SafeSport** training, which must be completed before any interaction with athletes [usav-2026-coach-education]; and a **background check** processed through SportsEngine. Block this sequence into August at the latest — every year a few coaches discover their certificates lapsed mid-tryout-week and cannot legally run their court.

**Step 3 — Tryout-window athlete registration.** Athletes register their USAV memberships through SportsEngine before tryouts. The membership year runs September 1 through August 31. The tryout-window timing is governed by the Region (most Regions release their first allowable tryout date in mid-September for the 13U-and-up age groups). Confirm the Region calendar before publishing your tryout invitation; running an unsanctioned early tryout can cost the club its sanctioning. The membership purchase produces a digital member card that doubles as the athlete's tournament-eligibility credential.

**Step 4 — Roster lock and team-official assignment.** After team selection, each athlete's profile is added to the team roster inside SportsEngine. The head coach plus any assistants and a team representative are listed as team officials. Tournament directors verify rosters at check-in by pulling the SportsEngine roster, not paper documents — keep it current.

**Step 5 — Ongoing renewal cadence.** SafeSport renews annually; the renewal training is shorter than the initial. Background checks renew on a USAV-defined cycle (currently every two years). IMPACT does not require national renewal, but Regions or clubs may impose stricter standards [usav-2026-coach-education]. The Coach Academy ladder (Bronze / Silver / Gold) is optional continuing education, not a renewal requirement [usav-2026-coach-academy]; legacy CAP certifications continue to count under the new points-and-badges system [usav-2026-coach-academy].

```
- [ ] IMPACT certificates verified or scheduled (USAV Academy)
- [ ] SafeSport training renewed for every coach

- [ ] Tryout invitation published with link to USAV registration
- [ ] Memberships verified for every confirmed-attendance athlete
- [ ] Roster locked in SportsEngine within 72 hours of selection

- [ ] Mid-season SafeSport audit (any coach added after August)
- [ ] Roster snapshot before each qualifier
- [ ] Pre-Nationals roster lock per event-specific deadline
```

## Common pitfalls

**Late registration.** Tournament directors will not seat unregistered athletes, and same-day fixes routinely fail because SafeSport completion can take longer than the registration window. Run the parent-onboarding email at least ten days before first practice and audit the roster again forty-eight hours before each tournament.

**SafeSport compliance gotchas.** The most common failures are (1) a new assistant coach added mid-season whose SafeSport completion was skipped during onboarding; (2) a renewal that lapsed silently on the anniversary of the previous year's training; and (3) coaches who completed an unrelated SafeSport course (school district, scouting org) and assumed it counted — only the U.S. Center for SafeSport course logged through SportsEngine satisfies USAV. Audit every SafeSport date in SportsEngine before each qualifier.

**Membership-card sharing.** Membership cards are individual credentials, not club property. Athletes occasionally swap cards or lend their digital card to a sibling — both invalidate the membership and can trigger a sanctioning review. The parent-onboarding email above explicitly says "memberships are individual, not transferable" for that reason; reinforce it at the parent meeting.

**Region-vs-national confusion.** USAV is a federation of Regions; club registration, tryout windows, and many fees are Region-set rather than national [usav-2026-resources-for-coaches]. Check the home Region's calendar each year — assumptions from last year's dates have caused more than one club to miss its window.

**Background-check expiry blind spot.** SportsEngine flags expired SafeSport visibly but is less aggressive about background-check expirations. Add a recurring quarterly task to the operations calendar to pull a SportsEngine staff report and check every background-check date.
