# Munciana Turn and Burn (Out-of-Middle)

## Setup
Groups of three: a defender, a setter, and a middle (or any back-row attacker). Run on the middle initially, then optionally pull middles and setters out and run with three DS's hitting back-row only. Coach side with a cart.

## Execution
1. Coach hits a tough ball into the back row.
2. The defender pursues and digs, often diving or rolling to control the ball.
3. The setter sets a back-row attack ball — typically pipe / D / red — to the middle (or DS).
4. The middle approaches and **hits out of the back row** even though the dig was bad.
5. Goal: **[00:49:09]** "We want to teach our guys no matter how bad the ball is, we can still run the middle."
6. Continuous rotation through the three-player group; coach feeds the next ball within a beat.

## Coaching points
- The drill name is the philosophy: turn (defender pursues) and burn (hitter still attacks even off a junk dig).
- **Middles must be good here.** **[00:48:54]** "We're going to force the middles to be good here." Mike's framing: middles often disengage when the play goes off-system; this drill forces them to stay engaged and run the seam every time.
- Hitter shot selection: line is rare on a back-row attack; hit angle hard or hit deep cross.
- Setter trains to deliver under pressure — a 5x5 set into a moving back-row hitter is a high-skill rep.

## Variations
- Pull setters out and have the digger set the ball for a peer DS hitter.
- Add a blocker against the middle so the hitter must read at least one arm.
- Run on left or right pin instead of middle — same pursuit-then-attack dynamic.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: not appropriate as written. Use [[munciana-mia-drill]] for out-of-system hitting at this age.
- **HS**: standard rules; middles run the back-row seam.
- **College**: add a blocker; live setter; hitter must terminate against a single arm.
