# Jump Setting Progression

## Setup

Setter at the net in standard setter position. Coach at mid-court with a ball cart. One or two hitters at Zone 4 to receive the set (or a hoop target if no hitters available). Shaggers behind the target. Drill runs as a four-stage progression — every setter must clear each stage before advancing.

## Execution

Four stages, 8-10 reps per stage, advance when the setter is clean on 7 of 10:

1. **Stationary set.** Setter receives a bucket-pass tossed directly to her hands and sets to Zone 4 without jumping. Baseline [[setting-hands]] check — if this is not clean, do not progress.
2. **Step-and-set.** Setter takes one shuffle-step to the ball, then sets stationary. Tests footwork and the "beat the ball" habit.
3. **Approach-and-jump-set.** Coach tosses a clean pass to a fixed spot; setter approaches, plants, jumps, and sets at the peak. No horizontal movement beyond the planned approach. Tests timing of the gather and release.
4. **Jump-set from live pass.** Passer delivers a real serve-receive pass; setter reads the pass, decides to jump or stay down, executes. Decision is part of the rep.

Rotate setters after completing all four stages or after 40 total reps.

## Coaching points

- The jump set is the same hands as the standing set, elevated — same hand-hinge, same held follow-through, same ball-shaped contact. Dan Fisher's "up the chimney" release cue applies in the air identically to on the ground.
- "Beat the ball" still applies: feet arrive at the takeoff spot, then the jump, then the hands. A setter who is still shuffling at takeoff is late and has already given up the tempo gain.
- Release at the peak of the jump, not on the way up or on the way down. The whole point of the jump set is tempo; releasing early loses it.
- Body stays vertical — no drift forward across the net plane. Plant directly under the intended contact point.
- On stage 4, grade the decision as well as the execution. A jump-set off a low or off-net pass is a coachable error even if the ball arrives at the target.

## Variations

- Add a middle attacker on stage 3 to introduce middle-timing.
- Alternate front-set and back-set in the air once stage 4 is clean.
- Second-contact attack option on one rep in three at the college level.

## Adaptations by level

- **HS:** Stages 1-3 only in the early season; introduce stage 4 once the setter is reliable at 3. Jump-set only on clean passes.
- **College:** All four stages; live pass variation; middle-timing built in; second-contact attack threat introduced.

Jump setting is not a 14U drill for most club setters — introduce only when [[setting-hands]] is stable and the setter has the leg strength to reach above the net with trunk control. See [[setting-jump]] for the readiness check.
