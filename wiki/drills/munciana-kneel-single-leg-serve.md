# Munciana Kneel & Single-Leg Serve Toss

## Setup
Pair up across the net. One server kneels on the dominant-side knee (right-handers on the left knee, lefties on the right) with the back knee high; partner targets at the opposite endline as catcher. Servers start near the 10-foot line. After the kneel block, players stand single-leg (right-handers stand on the left foot, lefties on the right) and step back as far as they comfortably reach contact. Roughly one minute per posture; end-line servers are usually back at the in-line by the end.

## Execution
1. From the kneel, the server tosses, swings, and serves the ball over the net to the catcher.
2. Every clean over-the-net serve earns a half-step back; partner shags and sends the ball back.
3. Switch to single-leg standing serves on Mike's call and repeat the same back-up rule.
4. A bad toss reads instantly because the server "wobbles and tends to fall over" — treat the wobble as the diagnostic.
5. Run roughly one minute per posture, then move into the 5-6 seam target work that follows.

## Coaching points
- The whole point is the toss. As Mike puts it, **[00:24:24]** "you just want to work on a good toss... you can't cheat with your feet."
- Punch the ball — no body lean, no hand steering. Contact is what carries the ball, not the legs.
- "Just want to put the ball on a dot" **[00:24:55]** — the toss is the dot the contact aims for.
- Servers who can't get off the 10-foot line need targeted serving work, not more reps of bad toss [unsourced — Mike's diagnostic; sourced from 25:06].

## Variations
- Hold the kneel posture until five clean serves over the net before standing up.
- Swap to a contact-knee-down posture for athletes still learning the swing path.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: stay inside the 10-foot line; success metric is "ball clears the net cleanly," not zone.
- **HS**: full backup to in-line in single-leg posture; pair into [[munciana-5-6-seam-noodle]] as the next block.
- **College**: stretch the single-leg posture beyond the in-line and contact float trajectory off a single foot.
