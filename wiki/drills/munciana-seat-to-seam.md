# Seat to Seam

## Setup
A box (or chair) sits in the back-court behind the defender. Coach with two balls at the net. Run on the left, middle, and right of the back-court — Mike: "we do this drill on the left, the middle on the right today" **[00:20:05]**. A holder steadies the box: "make sure you have somebody to hold the box" **[00:20:43]**.

## Execution
1. Defender plays a first ball from the coach.
2. Defender immediately retreats and "they got to tap their tush on the box" **[00:19:55]** — the seat must touch the box behind them.
3. Coach delivers a second ball into the seam — "coach gets two balls at a time" **[00:20:21]** — and the defender plays it.
4. Two-ball cycle, then rotate the next defender in.

## Coaching points
- The drill's three-P frame: "Seat to seam is teaching posture, position, and then passion. Now, the three P's of defense, they're going to get back" **[00:19:45]**.
- Tap the tush = position is correct (back where they should be), and posture (low) is correct.
- The second seam ball tests passion — chasing through the seam, not standing.
- "That tells me they understand position, that tells me they understand posture, and it also tells me on the second ball that they understand passion" **[00:19:58]**.

## Variations
- Use a chair instead of a box: "you can use the chair. You can use the box" **[00:20:39]**.
- Three balls instead of two for advanced groups — extend the chase.
- Run all three positions back-to-back (left → middle → right) as a single block.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** lower box, coach delivers softer first ball; emphasize the seat tap as a positional cue.
- **HS:** standard box; second ball into a real seam.
- **College:** harder first feed, second ball into emergency-pursuit zones; defenders chase through the seam, not just to it.
