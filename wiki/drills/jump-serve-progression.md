# Jump Serve Progression

## Setup
One or two servers at the endline with a feeder and cart. Focused skill block — no crossing servers. Post the four stages on a whiteboard:

1. Standing float
2. One-step float
3. Two-step jump-float
4. Jump-topspin (full approach)

Each server works the lowest stage they cannot yet execute cleanly. A stage is "owned" when the server lands 7 of 10 to a called zone on two consecutive sets.

## Execution
1. Server picks the lowest stage not yet owned.
2. Take 10 reps; coach scores each for toss, contact, and landing zone and names the missing element after each rep.
3. Toss-only reps are legitimate between stages: 10 tosses in a row with a consistent release point must precede advancing.
4. Once a stage is owned across two sets, move up. Miss the 7-of-10 bar and repeat.
5. Block ends at 10 minutes; servers log their top stage and resume there next session.

## Coaching points
- Toss is the gate at every stage. Isolate toss reps whenever contact degrades.
- Step timing: weight transfer completes as contact happens (Lift-Step-Swing). When rhythm breaks, reset the stage.
- Stage 4 uses the hitter's approach — same footwork, same arm pull — linking serving to hitting-approach training.
- Do not skip stages. The cost of installing bad timing at stage 4 is rebuilding from stage 2.

## Variations
- **Left-right split**: right- and left-handers work parallel queues; feeder calls the stepping foot.
- **Video capture**: phone on tripod behind the server for side-view review between sets.
- **Zone add-on**: once a stage is owned, pair it with [[zone-serving]] targeting.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: most live at stages 1-2; stage 3 only for players near HS-ready. No stage 4.
- **HS**: most at stages 2-3; stage 4 for servers with stable approach and shoulder health.
- **College**: stages 3-4; stage 4 standard for opposites and select outsides; pair with jump-topspin specialty work.
