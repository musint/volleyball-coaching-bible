# Three-Person Serve Receive

## Setup
Three passers in standard three-person serve-receive alignment (left, middle, right). Server at the endline opposite. Setter or target at the setter position.

## Execution
1. Server serves.
2. Whichever passer is in the zone of the serve takes it; other two hold their positions.
3. Pass to target, target catches and hands to next server.
4. No rotation — passers hold zones for a set count (e.g., 15 serves) to reinforce zone reads.

## Coaching points
- Zone calling happens early: the covering passer calls "mine" at the top of the serve's arc, not after it crosses.
- Non-receiving passers hold their zones and verbally confirm the cover — "yours" — so the passer knows they own it.
- Seam serves are the teaching moment: two passers are adjacent, the ball arrives between them; both call, one commits, the other clears.
- Target rates every pass aloud.

## Variations
- **Live jump-serve:** add topspin and jump-float at HS / college level.
- **Short-serve variant:** servers move inside the 3m line and attack short zones.
- See [[wave-serve-receive]] for a rotating variant that adds zone-transition demands.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** tosses or underhand serves; passers hold a set zone for the full rep count.
- **HS:** float serves; add one topspin per five serves as a difficulty injection.
- **College:** full live jump-float and topspin serves; a passer who takes a ball outside their zone (poach) forfeits the pass.
