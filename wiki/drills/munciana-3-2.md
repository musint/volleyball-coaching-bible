# Munciana 3-2 (Three Players, Two Contacts)

## Setup
Three players on each side. Each side gets only **two contacts** to play the ball. All attacks come **out of the back row** — there is no front-row attack option in this drill. Coach with a cart sideline.

## Execution
1. Coach enters a ball; the receiving side passes (1) and attacks back-row (2). No third contact.
2. The opposing side digs (1) and attacks back-row (2). No third contact.
3. **[01:00:26]** "It's all back row attack." Standard 3v3 with two contacts per side.
4. Make-it-take-it scoring; not cooperative — **[01:00:24]** "Not a cooperative game, you're playing the score."
5. Play to a target — typically 5 or 7.

## Coaching points
- "I always wanna be on attack." **[01:00:36]** Two contacts means every digger must be ready to attack the next ball within a heartbeat. There is no setter to bail you out.
- Hitters live attack-ready. **[01:00:48]** "Hitters are always ready to hit. Gotta be ready to hit. Gotta be ready to hit."
- Defenders dig **to target** — meaning they dig to a position the next attacker can hit out of, not just up.
- This is a back-row attack volume drill more than a defensive drill — the dig has to be hittable.

## Variations
- Three contacts permitted (downgrades back-row demand); softer entry version.
- Replace hits with controlled chops at younger levels.
- Score: only back-row attacks that land in opposite corners earn points.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: not appropriate as written; back-row attacking at 14U is uncommon. Substitute [[munciana-usa-33]] for a 3-contact variant.
- **HS**: standard two-contact rule; play to 5.
- **College**: two-contact rule; play to 7; live serves to enter; back-row attack must be inside the antenna geometry.
