# Munciana Pursuit Attack (25 in 5)

## Setup
Two players inside in pursuit posture (defenders), and two players on the outside ready to hit. Coach with a ball cart on the sideline. Mike's frame: **[00:44:38]** "We're going to get into some cooperative drills now."

## Execution
1. Coach hits or tosses the first ball.
2. The two pursuit defenders chase; one digs the ball, the other sets it (regardless of where the dig went). **[00:44:53]** "One will chase the ball and play it. The other will set it regardless."
3. The two defenders then move to the outside; one of them becomes a hitter on the next round.
4. One of the **outside hitters** hits a controlled cross-court ball.
5. The **other outside hitter** sprints under the net and digs that hit. The ball is dug, set, and caught — that completes one rep.
6. Goal: **25 successful sequences in 5 minutes** as a team. **[00:45:25]** "They have to get 25 in five minutes."

## Coaching points
- This is a **cooperative** drill — competing against the clock and the team's own count, not against another group. **[00:45:31]** "It's a cooperative drill. That means we work together for one goal."
- Ball control is everything. **[00:45:48]** "If you can't hit the ball at a player, you're probably not going to be able to hit a ball away from a player."
- The hitter is a teaching partner. Their job is to put a hittable ball into the digger's defensive zone, not to terminate.
- A lot of talk: "breeze under" calls, "mine," "in/out" reads — communication is what keeps 25-in-5 alive.

## Variations
- 50-in-10: harder ceiling, longer block.
- Add a third pursuit defender so two chase and one sets.
- Replace the controlled hit with a roll-shot for a softer reading challenge.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: 10 in 5; toss-pursuit instead of hit-pursuit; hitter tosses controlled ball over.
- **HS**: 25 in 5 standard.
- **College**: 25 in 4; hitter swings at full pace.
