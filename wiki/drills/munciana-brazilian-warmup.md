# Munciana Brazilian Warm-Up

## Setup
Three players on each side of the net. Coach has a ball cart sideline. Mike's frame: **[00:47:40]** "Brazilian warm-up is a hustle drill, it's a communication drill, it's a cooperative drill again."

## Execution
1. Coach puts the first ball in as a hit (chop-down) into one side.
2. The receiving side passes (the **first passer** is the player who sprints under the net to the other side and centers as the new setter on the next ball).
3. The second player chops a controlled ball at the next side.
4. Ball is dug, set, and chopped back — continuous rotation; the chopper after each contact runs under to become the next side's center.
5. **[00:48:23]** "You've got to be able to pass that hit in this game to be successful. Whoever digs the first one, skint, gets over, calls the ball."
6. Goal: keep the ball alive for one minute (or 10 touches) without it hitting the floor.

## Coaching points
- "Got to move. Got to move." **[00:48:18]** Movement under the net is half the drill — players can't be late getting to the other side or the chop will land before they arrive.
- "Be ready to go." Anticipation reads — read the chop early, sprint early.
- Pass-the-hit is the core skill. **[00:48:21]** "You've got to be able to pass that hit in this game to be successful." The whole drill simulates one rotation of pass-set-hit-pass-set-hit traffic.
- This can be entered as passing, setting over, or any modality — Mike's framing is flexible. **[00:48:30]** "You can do whatever you want."

## Variations
- 10 touches before the ball can drop.
- Add a tip on every fourth contact to create reading variety.
- Three-and-go: third side joins the rotation when the second side completes the pass-chop.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: Brazilian-style chop replaced with a slow down-ball; goal is 5 touches without the ball hitting the floor.
- **HS**: standard hustle pace; 10 touches.
- **College**: live swings instead of chops; 90 seconds continuous.
