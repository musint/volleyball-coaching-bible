# Go Stay

## Setup
Three DS / back-row defenders in left-back, middle-back, right-back. Coach at the net with a ball. Mike: "Go stay is another communication and move drill" **[00:22:24]**.

## Execution
1. Coach throws to one of three zones.
2. **Wing throw (left or right):** "if I throw the ball to one of the two wings, they move. They adjust" **[00:22:27]** — example given: "if I go to Ramsey, Emma and Ramsey will switch" **[00:22:31]**.
3. **Middle throw:** "if I throw it to the middle, they all stay" **[00:22:36]**.
4. Defenders read the throw, call it, and either move (wing) or stay (middle).
5. Continuous live feed with verbal calls every rep.

## Coaching points
- "They've got to talk. They've all got to read. And they all got to be ready to run" **[00:22:38]** — the three-pillar checklist.
- "Keep it low" **[00:22:50]** — between reps, no standing up.
- The drill is named for the read: "go" on the wings, "stay" on the middle. Defenders verbalize the call before moving.
- "It's all about defensive talk" **[00:23:10]** — the talking is the point; the dig is downstream of the call.

## Variations
- Add a second ball after the first (one wing, one middle) for compound reads.
- Coach can clap to mix in a non-throw start for advanced groups.
- Pair with [[munciana-figure-eight-defense]] in the same DS communication block.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** slow tosses; require the verbal call before moving — half the value is hearing the team talk.
- **HS:** faster tosses, no walk-throughs; team scored on missed calls.
- **College:** add a third throw direction (overpass) for an additional read; coach grades comm honesty.
