# Pressure Serving

## Setup
Servers line up behind one endline with a full ball cart. The rest of the team takes the receiving side as hecklers behind the attack line in a horseshoe — nobody crosses the 3 m line or touches live balls. Coach at the scorer table. Post a clutch bar (e.g., 8 to win; missed clutch = -2).

## Execution
1. Each server must land two consecutive in-serves before rotating out. A miss resets their counter.
2. Hecklers may talk, stomp, or call fake zones starting when the server picks up the ball.
3. Every fourth rep the coach declares "clutch": that serve is +2 for in-zone, -2 for netted or long.
4. First server to clear the 8-point clutch bar earns a team perk (picks cooldown game or next receive pair).
5. A server may invoke their cue word before the toss; coach enforces 3 seconds of silence from hecklers to rehearse the reset.

## Coaching points
- Every server owns a personal cue word. Reid Priddy's "Just me and the ball" is the canonical example — say it, breathe, toss.
- Accept misses. If you never miss, you aren't hitting hard enough; serving is point-scoring offense.
- Watch for pressure tells: rushed toss, hand steering contact, feet creeping off the line. Call them by name.
- Route-repeat the ritual. The pre-serve sequence should look identical on a clutch rep and a warmup rep.

## Variations
- **Sideout-only**: server must win the rally, not just the serve.
- **Double-or-nothing**: a clean in-zone serve lets the server bank or double stake on the next rep.
- **Silent heckle**: hecklers may only stare — trains visual-trigger servers.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: hecklers clap and shout names only; reset is one clean serve; clutch bar is 4.
- **HS**: standard rules; include jump-float for servers developing it.
- **College**: add a simulated score ("20-20, your serve"); servers call the zone aloud before each clutch rep.
