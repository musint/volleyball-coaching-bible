# Hitting vs Block

## Setup
Live 1- or 2-blocker front at the net. Setter at zone 2 (or coach-toss replacement). Hitters at zone 4 (optionally zone 2). Ball cart behind the setter; 1-2 shaggers; no full back-row defense — the drill isolates the block read. Net at match height.

## Execution
1. Coach or passer initiates a controlled ball to the setter.
2. Setter delivers a live set to zone 4 (or zone 2).
3. Blockers show a front — single, two-blocker line closed, two-blocker angle closed, or split. Randomize.
4. Hitter reads the block during the last two steps and chooses a shot: hard cross, line, high-hands tool, tip deep, or roll.
5. Blockers block live. Hitter win on any kill or tool out; block win on stuff or hitter error.
6. Rotate every 6-8 swings.

## Coaching points
- Read hands, not bodies. Hands sealed = line closed; hands drifting inside = tool available.
- Commit to the shot by the plant — do not swing uncertain.
- Against a two-blocker seam, attack the seam or use high-hands off the outside blocker.
- Match shot to swing motion. A tip from the full arm swing disguises; a jabbed tip telegraphs.

## Variations
- **Late-front:** block moves into position only after the set releases — forces true mid-approach reading.
- **Score by shot type:** bonus points for line, deep tip, or tool off outside hand.
- **Block-adjustment:** block takes one shot away each rep; hitter attacks the open option.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** skip the live contest; use hands-up passive block as a visual cue. Train block-reading, not battles.
- **HS:** live 1-blocker first, 2-blocker once reading habits are in place.
- **College:** live 2-blocker with randomized fronts; video-reviewed.
