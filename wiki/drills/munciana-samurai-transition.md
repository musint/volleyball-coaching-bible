# Munciana Samurai Transition (Seven-Touch)

## Setup
Three DS's on the back-row defensive side. One coach feeding from across the net. A roller (ball-shagger) ready behind the cart. Mike's frame: **[00:43:38]** "Same thing. Learning to move."

## Execution
1. Coach starts the rally by tossing/hitting a single ball into the back row.
2. The three DS's dig and pass continuously — the ball stays alive until **all three players touch it for a total of seven good contacts**.
3. **[00:43:55]** "They can't go until all three guys touch the ball."
4. Only one ball is in play at a time. If the ball drops, the count resets.
5. Crash and cover on every contact. **[00:44:01]** "Crash to cover. Crash to cover."
6. After the seventh good contact (with all three players having touched), the group exits and the next group rotates on.

## Coaching points
- Same teaching as [[munciana-tight-triangles]] and [[munciana-sam-dixette]]: defense is **forward movement** to where the next ball will be, not retreating to a base.
- The "seven contacts with all three touching" rule is what forces movement. A passive defender who lets one teammate handle every ball will never escape the drill.
- Set-and-go: after each pass the player moves; defense is never static. **[00:44:16]** "Always go. Ball. Set and go."

## Variations
- Five-touch variant for younger players (still requires all three to touch).
- Live coach hits instead of tosses; harder reads, longer pursuits.
- Pair with a setter in the front row who must put the seventh ball over the net for an attempted attack.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: five touches with all three touching; coach tosses, no live hits.
- **HS**: seven touches, coach mixes tips/hits/down-balls.
- **College**: nine touches with attack required on the final touch.
