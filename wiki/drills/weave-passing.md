# Weave Passing

## Setup
Three passers across the back third of one side at the 5, 7, and 9 m seams. A tosser/coach at the net with a ball cart. A target (setter) at target area. Passers move in a continuous weave — pass, sprint behind the next passer, receive again — so every rep starts with the passer already in motion.

## Execution
1. Coach tosses (or short-serves) a ball at the lead passer's seam.
2. Lead passer plays a forearm pass to the setter target, then weaves behind the others to the far end.
3. The second passer, already moving into the vacated slot, receives the next toss immediately.
4. Cycle continues without pause. 60-90 seconds per set, three sets.
5. Target grades each pass aloud on a 0-3 scale.

## Coaching points
- Pursue on the move — platform forms late, feet arrive early.
- Finish the pass before the weave; do not drift before contact.
- Eyes track the server/coach, not the teammate in front; trust the pattern.
- Short quick steps in the weave — no crossover sprints.

## Variations
- **Target window:** setter holds a hoop; only passes through the window count.
- **Competitive count:** team gets a point per 3-pass, loses one per shank; first to 10 wins.
- **Random feed:** coach randomizes which passer gets the next ball, eliminating the predictable rotation.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** two passers instead of three, toss only, target 20 clean passes in 90 seconds.
- **HS:** three passers, mix of tosses and serves, 30 clean in 90 seconds.
- **College:** three passers with a live jump-server, no resets, 1 m target window.
