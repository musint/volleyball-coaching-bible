# Munciana Left vs Left (Cross-Court)

## Setup
Three back-row defenders on each side. Setter in each back row. **One blocker up at left front** on each side — the blocker matches the cross-court hitter. **No middle.** This is a left-on-left cross-court contest.

## Execution
1. Coach enters a free ball to one side.
2. The receiving side passes, sets, and the **left front** hits cross-court at the opposite team.
3. The opposite team digs the hard cross, sets, and counter-attacks with their own left front cross-court swing.
4. **[00:57:11]** "Teaches people to dig the hard cross-court, teaches hitters to look for the hard cross-court."
5. Score make-it-take-it: 3-point game. **[00:57:11]** "Usually play make-it-take-it, three-point game."
6. Best of three games per pairing.

## Coaching points
- Hitters look for the **hard** cross-court. **[00:57:25]** "Teaches your hitters to hit a one-on-one ball."
- The single blocker forces the hitter to read one arm. Keep the swing aggressive — high-line over the block or angle hard around it.
- Diggers position deep cross-court — the geometry of the swing makes the seam/angle predictable.
- The drill rewards swing volume and read accuracy, not finesse.

## Variations
- Right vs right (mirror).
- Add a middle blocker so the cross-hitter sees a two-block.
- Play to 5 with rotation between rallies.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: not appropriate as written; cross-court swing pace is too high for 14U back-row defense as the only line.
- **HS**: standard rules; 3-point games, best of three.
- **College**: add a libero anchoring deep cross; live blocks; 5-point games.
