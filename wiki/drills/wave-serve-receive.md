# Wave Serve Receive

## Setup
Three passers in standard serve-receive alignment on side A. Servers at the endline of side B. Ball cart at side B endline. A target player at the setter position on side A.

## Execution
1. Server serves to side A.
2. After the pass, the three passers rotate one position clockwise (right → middle → left → right) — the "wave."
3. New serve comes immediately — no stop between serves.
4. Passers cycle through all three zones as the serves continue.
5. Hold a pass-rating target (aggregate of 2.0+ on a 0-3 scale) across a set of 15-20 serves.

## Coaching points
- Transitioning to the next zone has to happen fast — a passer who hasn't set feet by the time of the next serve can't read the server.
- Passers call early and loud in each zone; communication does not reset between zones.
- Target grades every pass aloud so feedback is immediate and compounding.
- Don't rotate on a miss-pass — the drill's teaching value is in stacking reps under rotation fatigue, not in tidy reset.

## Variations
- **Counter-wave:** rotate counterclockwise to force passers out of their dominant-hand-side reads.
- **Live-serve:** add jump-serves or top-spin serves to raise difficulty.
- See [[three-person-serve-receive]] for a related three-person pattern without rotation.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** tosses or underhand serves only; slow the rotation to let passers arrive before the next serve.
- **HS:** mix float serves and topspin; target rating 2.0+ across 20 serves.
- **College:** full live jump-float and topspin serves; target rating 2.3+ across 20 serves.
