# Cool Down Mobility

## Setup
Run immediately after the last drill or match of the session. Players spread in a half-circle facing the coach or captain. No equipment. The goal is a short, routine-based cool-down — the serving-ritual principle applied to end-of-practice recovery: a consistent closing ritual helps players manage load and transition out of competitive mode [aoc-2017-rose-serving-ritual]. The [[defense-platform]] link is nominal — this is a recovery page, tagged for catalog purposes.

## Execution
1. **Easy jog, one length of the court.** Shake out legs and arms.
2. **Walking cool-down laps.** Two full laps, slow breathing.
3. **Static stretch sequence (5-6 minutes).** One hold of 20-30 seconds each: hamstring, quad, hip flexor (kneeling lunge), piriformis/figure-4, thoracic rotation, lat and triceps overhead, chest/doorway pec, forearm and wrist.
4. **Closing huddle.** One-sentence reflection per player or coach-led closer ("best contact today" or "one thing I'll carry into tomorrow"). Mirrors serving-ritual discipline — close the rep on your own terms.

## Coaching points
- Static stretching goes **after** practice, not before. Dynamic work lives in [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]].
- Breathing is the point — two or three relaxed breaths per hold.
- Keep the sequence routine. Consistency beats variety for recovery; ritual is the teaching point [aoc-2017-rose-serving-ritual].
- **Medical disclaimer.** Consult an AT/CSCS on recovery programming specifics — hydration, nutrition, sleep, soft-tissue work, and return-to-play after injury are outside the coach's purview. Athletes reporting pain (not routine soreness) should see medical staff rather than self-managing through stretching. USAV directs coaches to lean on qualified specialists for anything outside general practice design [usav-2026-coach-tools].

## Variations
- **Arm-care close.** Pair with a trimmed [[arm-care-routine]] (passive range only) on hitting-heavy days.
- **Partner-assisted stretch.** Gentle resistance on hamstring and shoulder holds; coach communication ("that's enough").
- **Breathing-only close.** On doubles days, trim to breathing and individual foam-roll work.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** short version (6-8 min), coach leads every hold, reflection closer.
- **HS:** full 10 minutes; captains lead midway through the season.
- **College:** individual recovery pre- and post-team sequence; team cool-down is the social-ritual floor.
