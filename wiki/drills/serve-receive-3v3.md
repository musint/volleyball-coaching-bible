# Serve-Receive 3v3

## Setup
Split the court lengthwise with cones so each half is roughly 4.5 x 9 m. Three passers on the receiving side in a small serve-receive seam (left-back, middle-back, right-back). Three players on the serving side who serve, block, and transition. Ball cart behind servers' endline. Extras shag and rotate in.

## Execution
1. Server delivers a live serve into the 3-passer seam.
2. Receiving team plays out the point: pass → set → attack (no blocker, or one).
3. Serving team tries to terminate or force a free ball back.
4. Run sets of 7 serves per rotation. Track clean passes (3s and 2s) and kill/no-kill on the receiving side.
5. Rotate through base positions; swap sides after each set.

## Coaching points
- Passers call the seam before contact ("mine," "out," "short," "deep") — silence is a loss.
- Target the setter window, not just "good enough" — a 2 locks the team into slow tempo.
- Read the server's shoulder and toss, then commit feet; late reach is the most common error.
- Servers hunt seams and short corners — this is a serving drill too.

## Variations
- **Rotate servers:** swap server every 2 serves to force re-reads.
- **Libero-focus:** run the seam as libero plus two DS-style passers.
- **Pass-to-score:** only clean 3-passes count toward rally points; dirties playable but free.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** widen seam to 5 m, allow catches on first ball, keep feedback verbal.
- **HS:** standard seam, enforce 3-0 pass scale, require terminated or transitioned rally for a point.
- **College:** full-court 3-passer seam against jump-float/topspin, block and back-row scoring separately.
