# Munciana Prove It

## Setup
Full 6v6 on a regulation court. Whiteboard tracks small counts and big points. Mike calls it **[00:53:34]** "the cousin of Biggie Smalls" — same scoring chassis but with a service requirement.

## Execution
1. Same Biggie Smalls structure: three small points (each = one rally won) builds toward a big.
2. **The change:** **[00:53:39]** "Now the only way to get your point is you've got to serve and score to get the big point."
3. After three smalls, the team must go back and serve. If they win that rally with their serve, **then** they bank one big.
4. If they lose the serve rally, all three smalls evaporate and the opposing side may build their own run.
5. Play to 3 or 5 bigs. Make-it-take-it on the small side as in Biggie Smalls.

## Coaching points
- The "prove it" framing is psychological: you don't get the point until you can serve and score under pressure. **[00:53:39]** "You've got to serve and score to get the big point."
- This is the bridge between the serving block and the 6v6 block — every serving rep in the day's earlier drills now has match consequence.
- Servers at the 3-small mark are facing the same psychology as a server at 24-23 in a real set. Treat it that way.
- Coach calls the score aloud every rally to anchor the moment.

## Variations
- **Two smalls + serve rally = one big** (lower bar; HS pre-tournament).
- **Five smalls + serve rally = one big** (top of the difficulty scale).
- Combine with [[munciana-no-gut-serving]] — gut serves on the prove-it serve cost the run.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: not as written; the cognitive load (3 small + serve rally) breaks down at this age. Run [[munciana-biggie-smalls]] instead.
- **HS**: standard three smalls + serve rally; play to 3 bigs.
- **College**: three smalls + serve rally; play to 5 bigs; serve must be jump-float or jump-topspin.
