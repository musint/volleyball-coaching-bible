# Out of System to Attack

## Setup
Six players on one side in their serve-receive formation. Coach on the opposite side with a ball cart. Target cones on the coach's side mark an "acceptable finish" zone — back-row 5-meter area plus the deep corners. Scripted bad-pass locations marked with tape: shallow (in front of the 3m), deep behind the attack line, and off-the-net overpasses.

## Execution
1. Coach tosses a ball at one of the scripted bad-pass zones — the "pass" the team has to play from.
2. Libero or setter emergency-sets a high outside ball (or a right-side for out-of-system).
3. Outside or right-side attacker runs a high-hands swing or shot into the target zone.
4. If the setter can reach the ball with their hands, they must — bump-set is the fallback, not the default.
5. Run 10-15 reps, alternating the three bad-pass zones; rotate attackers every 4-5 reps.

## Coaching points
- Out-of-system does not mean swing-for-a-kill — it means a high, controlled set to the pin and a smart shot.
- Setters get to the ball early enough to use hands when possible; a hand-set out-of-system is a weapon.
- Hitters change the shot menu: tip, roll, high-hands off the block, deep corner — the shot the defense is not expecting.
- Communicate "out" early so the off-hitters release to cover; coverage is part of the drill.
- No-swing errors on out-of-system reps are correctable; hitting into the block is a shot-selection failure.

## Variations
- **Libero-setter:** setter is forced to take the first ball so the libero sets — see [[aoc-2021-kiraly-libero-set-team-drill]].
- **Blocker live:** add a blocker on the coach's side; hitters must see the block.
- **Scored:** +1 for a kill in the target zone, -1 for hitting the block or out of bounds.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** simplify to one bad-pass zone (shallow); accept any controlled attack over the net.
- **HS:** full three-zone rotation with pin attackers.
- **College:** add middle-back tipping by the coach, blocker on the coach's side, strict target zones.
