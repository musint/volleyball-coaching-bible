# Munciana Tight Triangles

## Setup
Three defenders form a tight triangle facing the coach (close enough that platforms nearly touch). Coach stands a few steps in front holding the ball at head height. Mike's framing: **[00:40:27]** "Passing balls only to my head."

## Execution
1. Coach calls "shuffle" — defenders shuffle as a unit (all three move together) without breaking the triangle shape.
2. Coach drops the ball or hits it down at one of the three; that defender digs the ball back up to the coach's hands.
3. After each dig, the triangle shuffles to a new defensive spot and resets.
4. **[00:40:46]** "I'm going to put the ball on the floor. You find the defensive spot to play. You're going to dig the ball to me. Get back into tight triangles."
5. Drill cycles continuously for ~45 seconds before rotating the next group of three on.

## Coaching points
- Coverage logic Mike calls explicitly **[00:41:45]**: "Defending the left, covering the middle, defending the middle, covering the left, defending the right." The triangle's job is to cover whichever zone the next-most-likely attacker is **not** standing in.
- Quiet platform — the platform should be still on contact. Movement before contact is footwork, not the platform.
- "Crash and cover, crash and cover" **[00:41:21]**: the triangle slides toward the ball each rep, not back. Defense is forward.
- Talk constantly. **[00:41:33]** "You got to talk."

## Variations
- Replace the toss with a roll-shot from a coach standing at the 10-foot line so the triangle has to read the contact.
- Add a fourth player at target so each dig must arrive at a setter window (not just at the coach).
- Live transition: the triangle digs the ball and a fourth player sets it back to a hitter; team plays out the swing.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: keep it slow; coach drops the ball with a defined zone call so the triangle knows where to shuffle.
- **HS**: standard pace; live coach hits.
- **College**: add a 25-touch goal — 25 clean digs to coach in a row before the drill ends.
