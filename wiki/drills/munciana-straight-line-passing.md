# Straight Line Passing (Free Tilt)

## Setup
Passers in a line at the back of the passing zone. Tosser at the net with a cart. Target at setter position. Mike: "let's go straight line passing... You'll go left and right. Start on the left" **[00:03:48]**.

## Execution
1. Passer enters from the left side, running straight at the tosser.
2. As the passer moves forward, the body is already tilted toward target — Mike's name for the posture: "we call it free tilt. She goes here" **[00:04:05]**.
3. Tosser releases as the passer arrives in the passing window.
4. Passer plays a quiet-platform pass to target with the body already shaped to send it.
5. Repeat from the right side; alternate continuously.

## Coaching points
- "Move low. Move low. Quiet platform" **[00:04:33]** — the cardinal cues.
- "The idea is quiet platform still working on tilt" **[00:04:42]** — angle the ball with body, not by chopping the arms.
- "Your players get in trouble when they swing their platform" **[00:04:45]** — passers who steer the ball with the arms are the ones who shank.
- Free tilt is preset; passers should arrive with the body already aimed at target before the ball lands.

## Variations
- Move to [[munciana-directional-pass]] next — same tilt principle from six different start zones.
- Increase the run-in distance and add a defensive sprint before the pass.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** 4-5 m run-in only; emphasize stopping before contact rather than running through.
- **HS / college:** longer run-in, ball delivered while the passer is still moving — closure on a moving target.
