# Emergency Pursuit

## Setup
One defender at a time on the court, starting from a neutral base near zone 6. Coach stands on the other side with a ball cart; assistant (or second coach) stands off-court with a cart to feed the pursuit phase. Use the full half-court. Mark the out-of-bounds lines clearly.

## Execution
1. Coach hits or tosses a ball off-the-body — shanks, tips to the sideline, overpasses to the attack line, balls behind the defender.
2. Defender reacts to any ball that is not already in control: dive, sprawl, barrel roll, run-through, or collapse platform.
3. Goal is simply to keep the ball alive — a reachable second contact anywhere on the court counts.
4. On the successful save (or miss), defender pops up, resets in zone 6, and the next rep arrives within 3-5 seconds.
5. Rotate defenders after 6-8 pursuit reps each.

## Coaching points
- "The floor is not scary" — commit to the ground; tentative dives cause injuries, full dives do not.
- Keep the hips low between reps so the first step is explosive, not upright.
- Long arms, single-hand pancake, J-stroke — use whichever emergency skill fits the ball; do not force forearm platform on every pursuit.
- Pop back up and relocate — pursuit is not one touch, it is stay-alive-until-the-rally-ends.

## Variations
- **Two-ball pursuit:** second feed arrives immediately after the first, so the defender must reset and chase again.
- **Partnered pursuit:** second player covers from the sideline to bump-set the saved ball toward a target.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** emphasize safe falling (run-through, J-stroke, sprawl); skip full layouts until floor-contact is taught.
- **HS:** full emergency arsenal; add partner coverage.
- **College:** two-ball sequences, scored on clean second-contact recovery.
