# Munciana USA 33

## Setup
Split a court in half lengthwise — drape a noodle along the midline antenna or move the antennas to mark the half. Three players on each half, on opposite sides of the net (so 3v3 on a half-court). Mike runs it on both sides simultaneously. Standard rotation through the team.

## Execution
1. Coach puts the first ball into play to one side.
2. Each side plays the ball with **three** contacts (pass, set, hit/over) and sends it back over.
3. The first passer becomes the center setter on the next ball; the previous center moves out; a new player slides in.
4. **[00:46:54]** "Eva will handle the first ball. The ball will be set back to Eva right around the 10-foot line. Eva will put a ball in play. It's a cooperative drill. We don't want the ball to ever die. Eva then becomes the center."
5. Goal: keep the ball off the floor for as long as possible. The drill scores by clean back-and-forth, not by terminations.

## Coaching points
- Stay big. **[00:47:08]** "Stay big. Stay big." Athletic posture between contacts; no resetting to upright.
- The "USA 33" name is the half-court 3v3 format — three players per side, three contacts per side, one shared half-court.
- The constant rotation (passer → center → out) means every athlete plays every role within 30 seconds.
- Use the half-court walls (or noodle) honestly — a ball that crosses the half is a dead ball; reset.

## Variations
- Run on **both** halves simultaneously for full-court traffic if balls are well-managed.
- Score: longest rally wins; team that hits 30 contacts in a row wins the block.
- Replace one contact with a tip for the third (over) ball to add shot variety.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: one team only on one half; coach feeds in the first ball as a slow toss.
- **HS**: full 3v3 on both halves; one ball each.
- **College**: jump-set and live attack on the third contact; balls in play for two minutes continuously.
