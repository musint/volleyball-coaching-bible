# Munciana Biggie Smalls

## Setup
Full 6v6 on a regulation court. Coach with a ball cart sideline. Whiteboard tracks each team's "small" count and "big" count. Standard match rotations.

## Execution
1. Coach enters the first ball as a free ball.
2. Make-it-take-it: the team that wins the rally gets the next ball. **[00:53:01]** "Meaning whoever wins the point gets the ball."
3. Each rally won = one **small** point. **[00:53:04]** "One small. You've got to get three small points in a row."
4. **Three smalls in a row = one big.** That's the only way to bank.
5. Anytime the other team scores, the streak breaks and resets that side to zero smalls.
6. Play to **3 or 5 bigs**. **[00:53:24]** "We usually play to three or five."
7. If a team is at "two smalls" and the opponent terminates, the opponent flips to one small and starts their own Biggie Smalls run.

## Coaching points
- Three-in-a-row is hard at any level. The drill rewards consistency, not flashes.
- Make-it-take-it means service-line consequences are real — a missed serve hands the streak back and the opponent's run continues.
- Coverage and ball control matter most — a single bad swing kills three rallies of work.
- Use the score psychology: at "two smalls" everyone is on edge; that pressure is the teaching tool.

## Variations
- **Prove it**: stricter version — see [[munciana-prove-it]].
- **Five smalls = one big** (harder ceiling).
- Coach varies the entry: free ball, down-ball, or live serve to change the texture.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: two smalls = one big; play to 3 bigs.
- **HS**: standard three smalls = one big; play to 5 bigs.
- **College**: three smalls plus a scoring-side rule (only the side that started the run can score the big).
