# Butterfly Passing

## Setup
Mirror halves on either side of the net. On each side, place a passer deep (5-6 m off the net), a tosser/server at the opposite endline, and a target at the setter position. Ball cart at each endline. Players circulate through three stations in a figure-eight pattern: pass → target → shag → tosser.

## Execution
1. Tosser on side A sends a controlled toss or easy serve to the passer on side B.
2. Passer B plays a forearm pass to target B at the setter position.
3. Passer B sprints to become the new target; old target B shags the ball, jogs to side A, becomes the new tosser.
4. Old tosser A rotates forward to become the next passer on side A.
5. Side A mirrors the sequence simultaneously. Rotate continuously for a set time or a target count of clean passes.

## Coaching points
- Passer calls "mine" early; platform forms stable before the ball crosses the net.
- Feet beat hands — pursue and arrive stopped; do not reach.
- Target grades every pass 0-3 aloud so feedback is immediate.
- Tossers keep height and tempo honest; a soft feed defeats the read-and-move purpose.

## Variations
- **Competitive scoring:** team stacks passes graded 3+ toward a running count; a miss resets.
- **Serve-receive variant:** tossers move behind the endline and serve live.
- **Short-serve warmup:** servers pinch inside 6 m and attack seams; pairs with [[two-line-passing]].

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** tosses only, 3 m distance, target 6 cleans in a row.
- **HS:** mix tosses and float serves; 2 m target window; 10 in a row.
- **College:** live jump-float or topspin, 1 m window, shag turnover counted as an error.
