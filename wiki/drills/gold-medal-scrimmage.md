# Gold Medal Scrimmage

## Setup
Full 6v6 on a regulation court. Coach at the sideline with a ball cart and a whistle. Scorers track side-out % per rotation, kills, errors, and "gold medal" outcomes (coach-defined positive plays — block kills, free-ball kills in the target zone, setter dumps, swing-blocks that land). Clock runs; game is 25 minutes.

## Execution
1. Start score at 22-22 or 23-23 — compressed end-game per Kiraly's "validation" format.
2. Live serves; play full matches to 25, validation point required to close.
3. Coach can blow the whistle and enter a scripted situation: "Team A, reset 24-24"; "Team B, two rotations to win."
4. Coach awards bonus points for "gold medal" plays — a block kill, a swing at the seam, a serve-receive three at the setter when the serve was a bomb.
5. At the game's end, run a second game immediately; three games in the time block.

## Coaching points
- This is the closest thing to a match you can run in practice — treat it like one. Timeouts, substitutions, between-point rituals.
- End-game reps are the gold: starting at 22 means every team gets 3-4 match-closing situations per session.
- Rose's "everything matters" applies — track side-out %, celebrate the player who dug the ball that started the transition, not just the attacker who finished.
- Kiraly's validation point: the team must win it on a clean, flat serve — rewards serve discipline at match point.
- Coaches do not over-coach during rallies; they call timeouts and run halftime-style reset talks.

## Variations
- **Rotation challenge:** team must win every rotation before they can close; losing a rotation resets the scoreboard by 3.
- **Serve constraint:** every serve must be flat, no rainbow floaters; a rainbow serve surrenders a point.
- **Side-out % game:** win condition is 80% side-out across all rotations.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** start at 18-18, no validation-point requirement, award bonus for in-system kills.
- **HS:** Kiraly validation format, 22-22 start.
- **College:** 23-23 validation with serve-discipline constraint, three games per block.
