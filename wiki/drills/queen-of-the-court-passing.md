# Queen of the Court (Passing)

## Setup
Full court with net. Split players into teams of two or three. One team holds the "queen" side; challenger teams line up behind the opposite endline. Coach at the net on the queen side with a ball cart; a target/setter lives on the queen side for pass scoring. Extras shag and rotate in.

## Execution
1. Coach initiates a rally by serving (or tossing) a ball to the challenger side.
2. Challenger team must deliver a cleanly graded pass (a 3 on the 0-3 scale) to their own side before attacking.
3. Queens play the rally normally; if challengers win AND their initial pass was a 3, they move to the queen side.
4. If challengers win with a dirty pass, they rotate off.
5. If queens win, challengers rotate off. First team to hold the queen side for 5 straight rallies wins the block.

## Coaching points
- The pass-grade gate is the point: a kill built on a dirty pass is still a process failure.
- Target grades immediately before the rally resolves; the rule must be visible.
- Queens hunt the weakest passer — targeted serving wins rallies before they start.
- Every rally starts with a called seam; silent receptions lose the slot even on a kill.

## Variations
- **Pass-for-points (Diane Flick):** only pass grade counts — challengers score by stacking 3s, not by winning rallies.
- **Two-touch queen:** challengers must pass and set cleanly before attacking; a bump-set over the net is a loss.
- **Libero-stay:** rotate the libero across every team; she stays on the queen side for continuity.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** 2-pass is acceptable to stay, coach-tossed serves, teams of three rotate in after any loss.
- **HS:** full 0-3 scale with 3s required to stay, live float serves, teams of three.
- **College:** teams of two or three, live jump serving, 3-pass plus kill to stay; stat-grade every rally.
