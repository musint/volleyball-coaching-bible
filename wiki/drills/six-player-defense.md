# Six-Player Defense

## Setup
Six defenders on one side in their base defensive alignment (team's standard rotation — perimeter, rotation, or middle-back). Coach on a box at zone 2 or zone 4 on the opposite side with a ball cart and a shagger. Optional: two blockers on the defenders' side working in live.

## Execution
1. Coach calls the attack zone ("four," "two," "pipe," "slide") and attacks from that position.
2. Blockers (if live) front the attack; back-row defenders read, shift, and defend.
3. Defense must dig, set, and attack (or free ball) into a target zone for the rep to count.
4. Coach immediately throws a second ball if the first rep dies — no resets until 4-6 plays run continuously.
5. Rotate attack positions and defensive rotations every 2-3 minutes.

## Coaching points
- Eyes on the hitter — the ball tells you late; the hitter's shoulder and approach angle tell you early.
- Line/angle responsibilities must be vocalized before the attack — silence is a coverage gap.
- Blockers seal the line or the angle on command; the rest of the defense plays around the block.
- Transition starts the instant the ball is dug; no one stands and watches.
- Call out who is leaving the floor to swing so the setter knows who is hot.

## Variations
- **Wash scoring:** team must complete two consecutive digs-to-kill before earning a point — see [[wash-drill]].
- **Fixed attack zone:** coach hits only one position for 3 minutes to drill that rotation's read.
- **No-block:** remove blockers so back-row defenders see the full court — stresses reading.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** coach tosses or down-balls; simplify to one rotation and one attack zone per block.
- **HS:** live attack from a box, full team defense + transition.
- **College:** live block-and-defend, transition to a counter-attack scored against a second coach-fed ball.
