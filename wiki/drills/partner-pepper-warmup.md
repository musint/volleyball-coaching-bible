# Partner Pepper Warmup

## Setup
Pair players up, one ball per pair, 4-6 m apart along the sidelines or any space with open overhead clearance. No net required. Use this as the ball-contact bridge from dry movement warmup into live ball work. The full drill family — competitive pepper, three-person pepper, group pepper — lives on [[pepper]]; this page is the warmup-specific usage.

## Execution
1. **Bump-bump (60 seconds).** Forearm passes back and forth, starting close, backing out to 4-5 m.
2. **Bump-set-bump (60 seconds).** Second contact overhead with the hands — a clean [[setting-hands]] rep, not a cheat bump-set.
3. **Bump-set-hit (3 minutes).** Full pepper, cooperative first, then a rally target (e.g., 20 contacts without a drop).
4. **Angle pepper (2 minutes).** Feeder tosses off-platform; passer plays from a lateral angle to train shape under movement.
5. **Reset rally block (3 minutes).** Restart on every drop; count team total clean cycles.

## Coaching points
- Warmup pepper emphasizes controlled touch — clean platform, clean hands, clean swing, not kill shots.
- Hit tempo at 50-60% — loads the body without overloading cold tissue.
- Call contacts every rep ("mine," "up," "out").
- Feet beat hands — arrive stopped, per Liskevych's ASICS passing teach [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-passing].

## Variations
- **Full competitive pepper.** See [[pepper]] for scored versions with three-person variants.
- **Ball-control roll-in.** Move straight into [[ball-control-warmup]] when the pepper block closes.
- **Three-person pepper.** Middle sets; outside players alternate dig and hit.
- **Silent pepper.** Remove verbal calls; read body language only (advanced).

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** hold at bump-bump and bump-set-bump. Add the hit only when bump-set-bump holds 10+ contacts.
- **HS:** full partner pepper at warmup tempo; 20+ contacts expected.
- **College:** compressed distance (3-4 m), flatter trajectories, 30-second continuous rally blocks.
