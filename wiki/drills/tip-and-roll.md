# Tip and Roll

## Setup
Setter or coach at zone 2 delivering controlled sets to zone 4. Cones mark two target zones on the opposite court: "tip deep" just behind the 3-meter line between zones 2 and 3, and "roll" in a deep corner (zone 1 or 5). Optional two-box static block at zone 4. Ball cart behind the setter.

## Execution
1. Coach calls "tip" or "roll" before the set.
2. Setter delivers a repeatable ball to zone 4.
3. Hitter runs the full approach and executes the called shot.
4. Score: 1 in zone, 0 in-play outside zone, −1 for a pounded swing — this is shot-work.
5. 10-15 called reps, then a "coach's choice" round — hitter picks and announces before the approach.

## Coaching points
- Tip: same approach, same arm motion until the final instant. Change only the hand — open palm, fingers firm. A telegraphed tip fails the rep.
- Tip deep, not short. Aim behind the defender's feet, not in front of the block.
- Roll: brush the top of the ball with a wrist snap; carry deep with topspin, not float.
- Eyes on the target zone just before the shot, not on the ball.
- Both shots come off the same approach as a hard swing — disguise is the goal.

## Variations
- **No call, read-based:** static block covers cross-court; hitter tips behind it or rolls to the open corner.
- **Tip-only / roll-only rounds:** isolate one shot before mixing.
- **Scoring ladder:** three in-target shots in a row advances to a smaller cone area.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** coach-toss sets; generous zones (6x6 ft); emphasize tip deep.
- **HS:** live setter; tighter zones; add the soft two-box block.
- **College:** live setter, live block, mandatory disguise — if the bench can call the shot, the rep does not count.
