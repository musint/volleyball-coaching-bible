# Figure Eight Defense

## Setup
Three DS-style defenders in left-back, middle-back, and right-back. Coach with a cart. Targets receive the dig. Mike calls the drill: "Figure eight. Left back. Middle back. Right back. Short long" **[00:21:08]**.

## Execution
The chase pattern alternates short and long balls in a figure-eight rhythm.

1. Coach throws a **short ball** — defender plays it and tosses to target.
2. Coach throws a **long ball** — defender plays and tosses.
3. After playing **short**, the defender goes to the **middle**: "after you pass short, you go to middle" **[00:21:31]**.
4. After playing **middle** (which is in the direction they just came from), they "got to go long" **[00:21:35]**.
5. Walk through it first catch-and-toss, then play it live: "go ahead and play live. Short ball. Long ball... no, no, play it live. Go" **[00:21:41]**.
6. "We go for a designated number of perfect passes" **[00:22:06]** — count quality reps, not time.

## Coaching points
- This is a **teacher drill** — the geometry teaches the habit. Mike: "it's a teacher, guys, to move on defense. We want defenders to learn to cross, not stand and look" **[00:22:10]**.
- Crossers, not watchers — the figure-eight forces every defender to cross the court between contacts.
- Catch-and-toss the first round so the pattern is internalized; live ball comes after.

## Variations
- Short-long only (skip the middle phase) for shorter blocks.
- Add a fourth zone (extend the eight) for advanced groups.
- Pair with [[munciana-go-stay]] for the same DS group's communication block.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** catch-and-toss only, shorter zones; emphasize the cross pattern.
- **HS:** live short and long balls, target rep count of 8-10 perfect passes.
- **College:** live ball at speed, target 15+ perfect passes, no resets.
