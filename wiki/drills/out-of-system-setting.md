# Out-of-System Setting

## Setup

Six-player side. Setter in standard Zone 2 position; libero in Zone 5; three front-row hitters at their pins; DS in Zone 1 or Zone 6. Coach at mid-court with a ball cart capable of tossing scripted bad passes. Cones mark three bad-pass landing zones: **too short** (5-6 feet off the net), **too long** (over the setter's head, past the antenna), **too wide** (Zone 1 or outside Zone 5).

## Execution

Coach calls the bad-pass shape aloud — "short," "long," "wide" — and tosses the ball so it lands in the named zone. The second-contact player (setter on short passes, libero or outside hitter on long/wide passes where the setter cannot get there) selects an appropriate tool:

- **Short pass:** overhead set to outside, or bump-set to outside if the ball is below waist.
- **Long pass:** setter bails — usually the libero bump-sets across the net plane to the opposite or a back-row attacker.
- **Wide pass:** whoever reaches it first bump-sets or two-handed redirects to the far pin.

Hitters run live approaches and finish the rally. Rotate the second-contact role after every 10 reps so the libero, DS, and outside hitter all get second-contact volume. Borrowing the Kiraly libero-set drill structure — two full teams is the ideal configuration, scored as a point per finished kill, so the drill compounds into live-play reps.

## Coaching points

- The call comes first. "Out" or "OOS" from the first-contact player before the second ball arrives, so hitters adjust approach angle.
- Tool selection is part of the rep. Coach grades the decision, not just the outcome — a smart bail that gets a kill blocked is a better rep than a lucky jump-set that scores.
- The libero needs volume here. Kiraly's libero-set framing is explicit: liberos are de-facto setters whenever the setter takes the first ball, and teams rarely train this. Build the reps in practice.
- Default bail is the high-outside — but do not let the defense pre-commit. Every fifth rep go to the opposite or a back-row attack to keep the block honest.
- No jump-setting off out-of-system passes. Full stop.

## Variations

- Live dig instead of coach toss — hard-driven ball at the setter, setter digs, libero sets. Trains the same pattern with real perceptual information.
- Validation scoring — only a clean, not-scramble kill counts; a tipped-in finish does not advance the score.
- Back-row-only attack finishes to force a tempo variation off the libero set.

## Adaptations by level

- **14U:** Short and long shapes only; bump-set to outside as the only target; coach-tossed feeds; finish with catches rather than live attacks until tool selection is reliable.
- **HS:** All three shapes; live finishes; opposite and back-row targets introduced mid-season.
- **College:** Live dig origin, full tool inventory, validation scoring, film review of decision quality by second-contact player.
