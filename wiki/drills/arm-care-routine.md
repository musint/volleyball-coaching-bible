# Arm Care Routine

## Setup
Individual or small-group. Each player has a light resistance band (tied to a net post, door jamb, or held by a partner). Run as the second half of [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], or as a standalone arm-prep block before a serving- or hitting-heavy session. This is a thrower-style routine adapted to volleyball's overhead demands — preparation, not rehab.

## Execution
One set of 10-15 reps per station, slow and controlled:

1. **Scap squeezes.** Arms at sides, squeeze shoulder blades back and down; 2-second hold.
2. **Band rows.** Band chest-high; pull elbows to ribs, retract scaps.
3. **External rotation at 90/90.** Upper arm parallel to floor, elbow 90 degrees; rotate hand back over ear.
4. **Internal rotation at 90/90.** Reverse direction; hand returns forward under control.
5. **Y-T-W.** Thumbs up: Y overhead, T out to sides, W elbows pulled back hands up.
6. **Full can raises.** Thumbs up, arms raised to shoulder height 30 degrees off midline.
7. **Passive range.** Cross-body, overhead reach, sleeper stretch — 15-20 seconds each.

## Coaching points
- Slow tempo is the point. Fast reps train momentum, not tissue.
- The routine loads rotator cuff and scap stabilizers before [[hitting-arm-swing]] and [[serving-float]] work.
- Band tension light (yellow or red), breathing relaxed.
- **Medical disclaimer.** Arm care should be supervised by an athletic trainer for any player with a shoulder history (prior injury, surgery, persistent pain); this is a general-prep example, not rehab. Athletes reporting shoulder pain during or after should see medical staff — do not "push through." Build routines with input from qualified professionals per USAV's guidance [usav-2026-coach-tools]. Kiraly's point on psychological safety applies: players must feel safe reporting pain honestly [aoc-2024-kiraly-things-learned].

## Variations
- **Partner manual resistance.** Replace bands with partner-provided resistance for travel or space-limited settings.
- **Cool-down arm care.** Trim to passive range only; pair with [[cool-down-mobility]].
- **Position-specific.** Middles and opposites repeat the 90/90 external rotation series.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** bodyweight-only variants early in the season; coach cues slow tempo and shape.
- **HS:** light band resistance; 8 minutes pre-hitting.
- **College:** individualized prehab built by ATs and S&C; team routine is a floor.
