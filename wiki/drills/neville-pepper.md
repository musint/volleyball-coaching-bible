# Neville's Pepper Drill

## Setup
Two full teams of six on a standard indoor court. Attacks come only from the back row — no front-row attacking for the duration of the drill. Attribute points to each team's running total as described below.

## Execution
1. Standard 6v6 scoring with back-row-attack-only constraint.
2. Scoring rules (per Kiraly / Slabe):
   - **+1 for each successful dig.**
   - **+1 for winning the rally.**
   - **-1 if a defender digs the ball over the net** (shank into the opponent's floor counts as a loss of control).
   - **-all points if a player does not pursue a ball** (penalty for not giving effort on a chase).
3. Rotate attackers through back-row positions; libero plays zone 5 throughout.
4. Optional: all first attacks directed at zone 5 to further stack defensive load on the libero.

## Coaching points
- Pursuit is scored harder than a dig — the design rewards effort on balls that look unreachable.
- Digs over the net cost points; defenders cue hips back, knees bent, platform angled toward own-side target.
- Back-row-attack-only keeps the hitter's contact point reachable to a back-row defender — no roof kills, no unreachable slide-attacks.
- Libero coordination with surrounding defenders is cue-called: "mine-up" (libero takes) or "help" (DS takes the seam).

## Variations
- **Libero-stacked variant:** all first attacks target zone 5 — libero digs every attack for an entire set.
- **Three-hit maximum:** limit each side to three contacts per rally regardless of touch count, forcing faster transition.
- See [[blitz-the-bro]] for a related libero-stacked drill on the serve-receive side.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** not recommended as pictured — remove the point penalties for non-pursuit, replace with a coach-noted "effort star" per rally so the reward stays positive.
- **HS:** run full scoring but keep attackers at half-speed until platform control stabilizes; then ramp up.
- **College:** full scoring, full live back-row attack intensity; libero substitution every set.
