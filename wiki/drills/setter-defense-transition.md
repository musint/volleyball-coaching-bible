# Setter Defense Transition

## Setup

Six-player side. Setter in base Zone 2 front-row position. Libero and DS in back-row defensive alignment. Three front-row hitters (outside, middle, opposite) in transition-ready positions. Coach on a box across the net with a ball cart, capable of hitting or tossing a driven ball at the setter's defensive zone.

## Execution

Coach attacks a front-row ball that falls into the setter's coverage area — a tip over the block, a roll-shot into Zone 2, or a hard-driven ball at the setter's hip. The setter digs the first ball with the platform. The libero (pre-trained as second-contact setter per [[out-of-system-setting]]) delivers the set to a front-row hitter. The three hitters finish the rally with an attack against a defensive wash.

Twenty reps per set; rotate setter and libero positions halfway through.

## Coaching points

- The setter is a defender first on any ball in her zone. Platform mechanics, body behind the ball, angle the dig up and toward the middle-front for the libero's approach.
- The libero needs reps at this — Kiraly's training-libero work frames libero-as-setter as a trained position expectation, not an emergency improvisation. Train the bump-set to outside as the default bail.
- Hitters communicate loud and early. "Out" on the setter's dig tells hitters to adjust approach angle for a higher, slower set.
- After the dig, setter transitions into cover position behind the hitter, not back to the setting target — in out-of-system the setter is no longer the second-contact player.
- Validate finishes. Borrowing from Kiraly's validation-6v6 framing, only clean finishes count toward the drill's rep quota; a scrambled kill that lands in does not advance the count.

## Variations

- Coach drives the ball at the libero instead, forcing an outside hitter or opposite to take the second contact.
- Second-contact attack allowed — the libero can tip or push-ball on contact two instead of setting.
- Extend to full rally after the attack: winner stays, loser runs a sprint.

## Adaptations by level

- **HS:** Start with tips and roll-shots at the setter; progress to hard-driven balls after digs are consistent. Outside hitter as primary bail target.
- **College:** Full-speed coach attack from the box. All out-of-system tools in play — back-row attack, tempo variation, second-contact attack by the opposite.
