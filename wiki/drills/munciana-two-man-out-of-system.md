# Munciana Two-Man Out of System

## Setup
Two players in a single back-corner per side; the rest of the team in lines off-court. In match-day Munciana use, **block off the corners** so swings stay legal cross-court; in this video Mike runs it without the corner block. Coach with a cart sideline. Setters and DS's stack in distinct lines so role tracking is clear.

## Execution
1. Coach sends the first ball over to side A.
2. The two-player team plays it: **the player who digs leaves**; the other plays setter and feeds a hittable ball.
3. The non-setter (whoever set the ball) leaves; the player who didn't set stays for the next round.
4. **[00:36:33]** "Whoever sets leaves, whoever doesn't, stays for the next round."
5. Always set to the **opposite** side from the dig.
6. Run to 15 in the corners (or whatever ceiling fits the time block).

## Coaching points
- "It's one thing to learn how to set it. It's another thing to hit it." **[00:36:51]** Hitting a 5x5 in-corner ball is hard — use the drill to teach hitter shot selection on these feeds.
- Setting opposite forces the dig-setter to drive the ball across the body, which is harder than setting back to the side they dug from. That's by design.
- Visual cue Mike uses: **[00:36:43]** "You can see who the setters are. They come in front of the 10. You can see who the DS's are. They stay behind." Use this to hold setters accountable to closing on the ball, not catching it deep.

## Variations
- Play a long side instead of a corner to give the two players more spread.
- Add a blocker on the receiving side so the hitter must read at least one arm.
- Three-man variant: a third player shags and rotates in after each rep.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: catch-and-set the first ball (no dig); focus on the opposite-side set rule.
- **HS**: standard rules to 15.
- **College**: add a single blocker plus a defender; play out the full rally and award points only on terminations.
