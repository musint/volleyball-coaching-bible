# Munciana Momentum

## Setup
Full 6v6 on a regulation court. Whiteboard visible to both sides. Coach with a ball cart at the sideline. Mike's frame: **[00:55:08]** "Momentum is a simple momentum drill."

## Execution
1. Coach enters the first ball as a free ball — point value = 1.
2. The team that wins the rally banks 1 point and **the next ball is worth +1 point** (so 2 points the next rally if the same team wins again).
3. Each consecutive point in a row escalates the value: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5...
4. Any rally won by the **other** side breaks the run and resets the next ball to 1 point — but the other side now begins their own run starting at 1 (and the next consecutive win for them is worth 2).
5. **[00:55:25]** "I throw the ball in on team A. They score. It's now one to nothing. They get the make-it-take-it ball. So if I throw them, the next ball is worth two."
6. Play to 25.

## Coaching points
- Streaks compound. A team that strings three points together swings the score by 1+2+3 = 6, not 3.
- Mike's example arc shows it: 1-0 → playing for 2 → 3-0 → playing for 3 → 6-0 → playing for 4. Five consecutive scores = 1+2+3+4+5 = 15-point swing.
- This is a mental drill as much as a physical one. The value-on-the-line builds match-relevant tension.
- Coverage matters at the high-value end: blowing a single 5-point ball can flip the score.

## Variations
- Cap the escalator at 3 (so the most a single rally is worth is 3 points) for shorter games.
- Reset the value to 1 after every five consecutive points so streaks don't cement the outcome.
- Pair with serve-only entry: the team in possession serves the ball; missed serve = opponent gets a streak start.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: cap escalator at 3; play to 15.
- **HS**: standard rules; play to 25.
- **College**: uncapped escalator; play to 25 with live serves.
