# Blitz the Bro

## Setup
Two full teams of six on a standard indoor court. Identify the libero on each side as the primary target. Servers stack serves deliberately at the libero's zone (typically zone 5). Ball cart at each endline; coach and a server at each end ready to feed rapid serves.

## Execution
1. Play 6v6 rally scoring with standard game rules.
2. Every serve is targeted at the libero's zone — short, deep, seam-between-passers with the libero centered under the ball.
3. Rally plays out normally after the serve. Score like a regular 6v6 game; rotate servers so every floor player gets a serving turn at the libero.
4. Karch Kiraly notes the USA WNT runs this drill roughly once a week to challenge the libero against a long volume of hard-targeted serves.

## Coaching points
- Libero's first read is on the server's toss — the body-position cues for short/deep/seam reads start before contact.
- Surrounding passers are allies, not bystanders — they call loud and early to give the libero the zone and the target.
- Post-pass, the libero must reset to defensive base, not drift — the rally immediately puts defensive demands on the same player the serve just taxed.
- Coach tracks libero's pass quality per set; a drop in platform stability as the set progresses is the drill's central teaching moment (fatigue-under-load).

## Variations
- **Defensive variant:** after the pass, the opposing team gets the dig-set-hit and all first attacks go to zone 5 — stacking serve-receive and defensive load on the same libero.
- **Tracking variant:** coach records pass quality per 10 serves; libero sees the score live and competes against their own earlier blocks.
- See [[neville-pepper]] for a related back-row-attack drill that stacks defensive load on the libero.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** not recommended — the drill assumes a mature platform and reading skill; substitute with simpler libero-targeted serve-receive patterns like butterfly or three-person passing.
- **HS:** run with slowed serves (float serves, not jump-serves) to keep the platform-read challenge without over-taxing arm recovery.
- **College:** full live serves including jump-serves; rotate libero substitutions every 10-15 serves to keep quality high without fatigue masking the teaching moments.
