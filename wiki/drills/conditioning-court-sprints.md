# Conditioning Court Sprints

## Setup
Single half-court. Two cones on the end line (one per sideline) and one at the net. Line players at the end line in waves of 3-4 for shared rest intervals. Coach stands at the net with a whistle. Run at the end of a skill or tactical block, not as a cold opener — athletes should already be warm from [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] and ball-work.

## Execution
The pattern is a repeated anaerobic interval. One rep:

1. **Whistle 1** — sprint end line to net, touch the net tape.
2. **Whistle 2** — lateral shuffle sideline to sideline in defensive posture, palms low.
3. **Whistle 3** — backpedal to the 3-meter line, then sprint back to the end line.

Run 6 reps on roughly 30-40 seconds work / 30-40 seconds rest, level-dependent. Groups alternate so each wave gets full rest while the next works.

## Coaching points
- Shuffles stay in [[defense-pursuit]] posture — low, hands ready, shoulders square.
- Forward sprint mechanics rehearse the [[hitting-approach]] acceleration — knees up, arm swing, eyes forward.
- Backpedal is controlled; heels down, chest up.
- **Medical disclaimer.** Sprint conditioning loads and intensity should be programmed by a CSCS or sport-science staff — USAV's coaching resources direct coaches to credentialed specialists for anything outside general drill design [usav-2026-coach-tools]. This is an example pattern, not a prescribed program. Athletes with cardiovascular, orthopedic, or recent-injury history should be cleared by medical staff before participating.

## Variations
- **Reactive trigger.** Replace the whistle with direction calls (left/right/back); pairs with [[reactive-jumping]].
- **Ball-integrated.** Close each rep with a coached down-ball pursued to the floor.
- **Rotation-specific.** Start players in their rotation's home zone so each rehearses their pursuit path.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** 4 reps maximum, longer rest, posture over speed; stop on form break.
- **HS:** 6 reps at a preseason-appropriate work-to-rest ratio; build over preseason weeks.
- **College:** volume and ratio set by S&C staff; this pattern is a drill template, not the program.
