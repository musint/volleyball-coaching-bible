# King of the Court

## Setup
Teams of 3 or 6. One side is the "King" side — the team holding court. Up to three challenger teams queue on the opposite side (if 3-per-team) or one at a time (if 6-per-team). Coach with a ball cart at the sideline. Challengers enter the serve; King side always receives.

## Execution
1. Challenger serves the King side.
2. Teams play out the rally. If the King side wins, they stay and bank one point on the team scoreboard.
3. If the challenger wins, the King team is dethroned — they go to the back of the challenger line; the winning challenger rotates onto the King side.
4. Three consecutive King wins and the team is forcibly rotated off to give challengers a chance — bracketed challenge format.
5. Run 15 minutes or to a target King-side point total.

## Coaching points
- Holding court is mental — the team that stays most is the team that handles a bad first contact and still wins the rally.
- Challengers own the first contact (the serve) — use it; no easy entries.
- Shot selection on the King side wins: the King team sees every opponent's block and every defensive gap, but must cash in.
- Rotate aggressively; this drill dies if one team holds court for 20 minutes — cap at three straight wins.
- Talk between rallies is coaching's job, not players' — play is continuous, so feedback is post-drill.

## Variations
- **6v6:** full-team King with live subs; rallies are longer but stakes are higher.
- **Bracket mode:** 4 challenger teams in a formal bracket; each dethronement advances the challenger, loser drops to next bracket slot.
- **Attack-only points:** termination counts only if it's a clean attack, not a serve ace or service error.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** skip — use [[queen-of-the-court]] with 3-per-team and free-ball entries instead.
- **HS:** teams of 3 with live serves, three-win rotation cap.
- **College:** 6v6 King-of-the-court, bracket mode.
