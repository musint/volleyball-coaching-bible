# Physio Reaction (Reaction Drill)

## Setup
A coach stands behind a large physio (Swiss) ball held at chest height by a partner (Mike: "I'm going to put you in charge of holding this" **[00:16:24]**). DS or back-row defender stands a few meters in front. Mike runs this drill from "the left side... the middle... the right" **[00:16:33]** — multiple zones in the same block.

## Execution
1. Defender holds a stopped ready position on the coach's call.
2. Coach releases the ball off the physio at unpredictable angles — "they release, boom" **[00:16:49]** — short, long, hard, soft.
3. Defender reads the trajectory off the bounce and pursues / digs.
4. Coach: "I just make sure they're released. I make sure they're stopped" **[00:17:01]** — every rep starts from a planted, stopped base, not a drift.

## Coaching points
- Stopped-and-ready is the prerequisite — Mike: "we want to make sure our players are stopped and ready to react" **[00:16:39]**.
- "We get rid of players guessing and they have to read" **[00:17:09]** — the physio's unpredictability forces reaction over anticipation.
- Vary the release every time: "I change it every time" **[00:17:21]** — predictability defeats the drill.
- Reps from left, middle, and right — defenders learn to read from every base position.

## Variations
- Two-ball: coach releases a second ball as soon as the defender resets.
- Pair-defenders: two players share a zone and must communicate who takes the read.
- Without the physio: hand-toss with hidden release; less unpredictable but easier to set up.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U:** soft releases, defender starts already low; emphasize stopping before reading.
- **HS / college:** harder releases, defender has to recover from drift, two-ball variant.
