# Munciana 5-6 Seam Bounce (One-Hop Deep)

## Setup
Identical to the 5-6 seam strike, except the box is now placed **on** the receiving in-line (3 m / attack line) of the deep zone — typically 1, 5, or 6 — to demand a deep, falling-trajectory serve. Servers behind the endline with a ball cart. Run all three deep zones across the block.

## Execution
1. Servers must serve a ball that bounces **once** and then strikes the box. **[00:28:19]** "They want to serve a ball that bounces once, hits the box."
2. Drilled across the three deep zones (1, 5, 6); typically five box hits per zone before moving on.
3. Coach stands beside the cart and calls "deep" or names the zone.
4. Shaggers clear balls back to the cart between reps.

## Coaching points
- The bounce shape is the read: a serve dropping into the back third looks falling-fast on contact. **[00:29:00]** "Push the ball deep... you want them attacking the deep ball."
- Deep serves and very short serves are the two **hazard balls**. **[00:29:03]** "The ball served deep, and the ball served extremely short are two hazard balls. Those are balls we want to be able to serve effectively in the game."
- "The best way to practice what you want in the game is to practice it in practice." **[00:29:12]**
- Watch contact path — a deep ball comes from a contact slightly behind the server's striking shoulder, not in front; correct toss before chasing rep counts.

## Variations
- Pair with a short-serve target inside zones 2/3/4 to alternate deep and short on every rep.
- Add a passer at the box who must read and play the ball cleanly; the server scores when the passer cannot route to target.

## Adaptations by level
- **14U**: any one-hop into the deep third counts (no specific box); target three per server.
- **HS**: box-on-line; five per zone, three zones.
- **College**: box-on-line plus jump-float deep; servers must call zone aloud before each rep.
