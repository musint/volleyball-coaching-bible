# Mental skills curriculum

## Overview

This page is the operational counterpart to [[mental]]. Where [[mental]] answers *why* mental work matters — culture, leadership, motivation, post-loss philosophy — this page answers *how* to deliver it: which skill goes in this week's plan, at this age, in what dosage, with what cue.

The curriculum is built from ten Tier-1 sources in the Track B.5 sport-psychology research wave. Five were measured on female cohorts directly relevant to a girls club program (Lola 2012, Görgülü 2021, de Arruda 2022, Slovák 2023, Billing 2014); Pourmohseni 2025 used collegiate volleyball cohorts; the remaining four were mixed or unspecified (Moran 2023, Mesagno 2017, Walter 2019, Pires 2021) plus Coussens 2024 (sex unspecified) — the synthesis flags cohort sex where it changes the application.

You teach mental skills the way you teach passing: introduce in age-appropriate form, refine across multiple seasons, never cram them into tournament week, and never expect a one-shot intervention to leave a lasting trace. The single largest mistake at the club level is treating mental work as a separate Saturday-morning lecture instead of a 10-minute block at the top of every Tuesday practice.

## Core curriculum tracks (8 topics)

### 1. Imagery and visualization

Collegiate volleyball players use imagery heterogeneously — wide within-team variance in frequency, content (technical/motivational/strategic), and timing — and without coach scaffolding, practice stays idiosyncratic; content also shifts across the season toward strategic-cognitive late and recovery-motivational during heavy weeks [pourmohseni-2025-imagery-collegiate-volleyball-season]. "Visualize success" is not an instruction. Imagery becomes coachable only when you supply scripts (what to see), prompts (when), and vividness training.

**Operationally:** Issue a written imagery script per role (libero pre-serve-receive, setter pre-rotation, hitter pre-approach); prompt at fixed points (warm-up close-out, between-set huddles); refresh content weekly.

### 2. Pre-performance routines

PPRs on the volleyball serve do not automatically improve accuracy. Billing's club-volleyball thesis found no effect of PPR presence on accuracy and no relationship between competitive anxiety and routine maintenance — women maintained routines more consistently than men, but that didn't translate into better serving [billing-2014-pre-performance-routines-volleyball]. The correction: PPR *components* (breath, ball bounces, focal point, cue word) likely matter more than presence/absence. Mesagno's review confirms PPR has empirical support as one anti-choking intervention [mesagno-2017-choking-models-interventions]; the volleyball-specific signal says coach what's *inside* the routine.

**Operationally:** Build a 4-component routine (breath in, two ball bounces, eyes lock target, one cue word). Coach the components, not the wrapper.

### 3. Attentional focus — internal vs. external vs. neutral

Four findings:

- **External cues help young female spikers' arm-swing velocity.** Slovák 2023 (n=12, ~13.6 yr) — external ("crack a whip") produced faster hand/forearm/arm velocities than internal or control across wind-up and acceleration; internal performed at control level [slovak-2023-external-focus-volleyball-spike].
- **Sequential cues hurt skilled setters.** de Arruda 2022 (n=32 female setters: 16 novice, 16 skilled) — stacked sequential cues degraded skilled setters' accuracy and crushed inter-joint coordination variability *regardless* of direction; novices unaffected on accuracy [dearruda-2022-sequential-focus-volleyball-setting].
- **Implicit-then-explicit wins serve decision-making at 10–12.** Lola 2012 (n=60 female novices, 10–12 yr) — sequential implicit-then-explicit beat implicit beat explicit on speed AND accuracy at retention [lola-2012-implicit-explicit-volleyball-serving].
- **Cue type doesn't matter for youth whole-body explosive tasks.** Moran 2023 (n=173 youth, multi-site) — no advantage for external/internal/analogy vs. neutral on vertical jump or 20-m sprint; neutral *beat* internal on vertical jump [moran-2023-verbal-coaching-cues-youth].

**Operationally:** External cues for arm-swing at 14s+. One cue per rep for skilled setters. Implicit-first reps at 10s/12s. No universal cue rule.

### 4. Choke recovery and pressure-management

Choking is a sudden performance decrement *despite the athlete possessing the requisite skill*. Mesagno identifies two complementary theoretical families — attention-based (distraction; Masters's reinvestment: under pressure, skilled performers revert to step-by-step monitoring of automated skills) and self-presentation (anxiety from judgment) — with replicable personality vulnerabilities (high trait anxiety, self-consciousness, perfectionism, fear of negative evaluation) and empirically supported interventions (pressure acclimatization, PPRs, attentional cueing, left-hand contraction, quiet-eye) [mesagno-2017-choking-models-interventions].

Görgülü 2021 tested Wegner's ironic processes in 43 female teenage players: "don't serve into the net" did **not** produce the predicted ironic-error spike under verified anxiety manipulation; distinct attentional-control disruption emerged instead [gorgulu-2021-avoiding-instructions-volleyball-pressure].

**Operationally:** Pressure is a training stimulus. Use Munciana rehearsal (Biggie Smalls, Prove It, Finish Strong, no-gut serving). Don't catastrophize negative-instruction language. Address attention and self-presentation simultaneously.

### 5. Mental toughness — IZOF and MTQ48

Mental toughness as instrumented rests on Clough's 4Cs (Challenge, Commitment, Control, Confidence). The original MTQ48 has well-documented factorial-validity issues; Kawabata derived an 18-item Short MTQ and a 6-item Very Short MTQ from two large samples (N=2,186; N=3,209) that fix the measurement model without abandoning the theory [kawabata-2021-mtq48-refinement]. Use Short MTQ post-season, Very Short MTQ for in-season weekly check-ins.

The IZOF model says optimal pre-match anxiety differs across athletes. Nogueira-Filho confirmed this in seven male professional volleyball players: optimal anxiety zones differed by 2–12 points across teammates; within-zone matches outperformed out-of-zone for the same athlete; the logic extended to self-efficacy [nogueira-2019-izof-volleyball-anxiety]. Cohort is male professional — transfer to women's club is defensible inference.

**Operationally:** At 16s+, profile each starter's pre-match state across ≥5 matches. Personalize pre-match interventions. Stop uniform "calm down"/"fire up" speeches.

### 6. Team cohesion and collective efficacy

Spink surveyed elite and recreational volleyball teams. The cohesion-efficacy correlation was **level-dependent**: positive elite, absent recreational. *Task cohesion* (shared goals, role clarity) tied more strongly to collective efficacy than *social cohesion* (interpersonal liking) [spink-1990-cohesion-collective-efficacy-volleyball]. Pasta dinners are not the load-bearing intervention — clear roles are.

Coach-athlete relationship is the mediator. Coussens (n=537, sex unspecified): perceived support (availability) predicts self-confidence; received support (delivery) predicts psychological wellbeing; both mediated by relationship quality [coussens-2024-coach-support-confidence-wellbeing]. The two support types aren't interchangeable.

**Operationally:** Define roles explicitly. State availability for confidence. Deliver during stressors for wellbeing.

### 7. Self-talk

Walter 2019 randomized 117 junior sub-elite athletes (ages 13–23) into 1-week ST, 8-week ST, or control. The 8-week training reduced somatic anxiety, raised self-confidence and self-efficacy with sustained follow-up gains, improved volitional self-optimization, and produced better *coach-rated* performance. The 1-week training was transient only [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes]. Cohort sex unspecified; age band overlaps HS+ club volleyball directly.

**Operationally:** Self-talk works as an 8+ week block, not a match-week tool. Embed in the season plan.

### 8. Burnout prevention and season-arc psychological load

Pires 2021 tracked 54 professional volleyball athletes (28 men, 26 women) across four measurement points spanning 10 months. **Reduced sense of accomplishment** and **sport devaluation** rose progressively; **physical/emotional exhaustion stayed stable**; **coping strategies did not change** while stress rose. Confidence and motivation showed moderate inverse correlations with burnout dimensions throughout [pires-2021-burnout-coping-volleyball-season]. Burnout in volleyball is erosion of meaning and efficacy, not primarily fatigue.

**Operationally:** Build a confidence/motivation check-in into mid- and late-season staff rhythm. When a player goes quiet at week 12, that's the leading indicator — not visible exhaustion at week 20.

## Per-age operational matrix

Tracks are *introduced*, *refined*, or *not yet appropriate* at each age band, with one specific operational example per row.

### 10s — introduction phase ([[10s]])

- **Introduce:** None as instrumented work. Outcome metric is fun retention; explicit mental-skills programming subtracts from it.
- **Refine:** None.
- **Not yet:** PPRs (no closed-skill substrate), attentional cueing (mixed youth evidence; movement discovery is the right default [moran-2023-verbal-coaching-cues-youth]), self-talk (8-week blocks consume the attention budget [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes]), MTQ/IZOF instruments, burnout monitoring.
- **Example:** Coach-modeled affect — you say "good ball, next one" out loud after a miss. No worksheets.

### 11s/12s — fundamentals ([[11s]], [[12s]])

- **Introduce:** Implicit-then-explicit serve cue ordering — live targets first, "where to serve" rules later [lola-2012-implicit-explicit-volleyball-serving]. Coach-modeled self-talk (you narrate your own resets) so athletes internalize the model.
- **Refine:** Nothing — too early.
- **Not yet:** Formal PPR drills, instrumented MT assessment, IZOF profiling, season-long burnout monitoring, high-stakes pressure rehearsal.
- **Example:** 12s serving block runs as live-target reps for 10 minutes before any verbal "rule" is taught.

### 13s/14s — late-fundamentals → specialization onset ([[13s]], [[14s]])

- **Introduce:** External-focus cueing for arm-swing (Slovák's cohort matches this band directly [slovak-2023-external-focus-volleyball-spike]). Pre-serve routine *components* — breath, two ball bounces, target-pick, cue word — as habit [billing-2014-pre-performance-routines-volleyball][mesagno-2017-choking-models-interventions]. Low-stakes pressure rehearsal (shortened Biggie Smalls, Finish Strong). Athlete-owned self-talk under coach modeling [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes].
- **Refine:** Implicit-then-explicit ordering from 12s, now against tougher serves.
- **Not yet:** Stacked sequential cues for setters [dearruda-2022-sequential-focus-volleyball-setting], formal IZOF profiling, Short MTQ as graded instrument, full 8-week self-talk block (4-week intro is the right dose), multi-season burnout tracking.
- **Example:** 30-second pre-serve routine — breath, two ball bounces, eyes lock target, one cue word — coached as habit for 8 weeks. Grade on consistency, not accuracy.

### 15s/16s — specialization ([[15s]], [[16s]], [[age-lens-hs]])

- **Introduce:** Full 8-week self-talk block in spring [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes]. IZOF profiling for starters across ≥5 matches, then personalized pre-match interventions [nogueira-2019-izof-volleyball-anxiety]. Imagery scripts per role with prompts at warm-up close-out and between sets [pourmohseni-2025-imagery-collegiate-volleyball-season]. Short MTQ end-of-season as returning-player conversation tool [kawabata-2021-mtq48-refinement]. Full-stakes pressure rehearsal — 6v6 with carry-over scoring.
- **Refine:** PPR components from 14s, now monitored for component consistency. External-focus cueing pruned to one cue per rep for skilled setters [dearruda-2022-sequential-focus-volleyball-setting].
- **Not yet:** Multi-season longitudinal burnout monitoring (most athletes lack the exposure window).
- **Example:** 16s setter's coach prunes from three sequential cues to one per rep, rotating the load each session [dearruda-2022-sequential-focus-volleyball-setting].

### 17s/18s — college bridge ([[17s]], [[18s]])

- **Introduce:** Very Short MTQ as in-season weekly check-in [kawabata-2021-mtq48-refinement]. Multi-season burnout monitoring — accomplishment and devaluation tracked across ≥2 club years; confidence/motivation as leading indicator [pires-2021-burnout-coping-volleyball-season]. Role-clarity as load-bearing cohesion lever [spink-1990-cohesion-collective-efficacy-volleyball]. Dual-support architecture: state availability + deliver consistently [coussens-2024-coach-support-confidence-wellbeing].
- **Refine:** All seven prior tracks at operational depth.
- **Example:** 18s setter has a personal IZOF profile, a written imagery script she edits monthly, an embedded pre-match self-talk routine, and a Very Short MTQ every two weeks.

### College-bridge ([[age-lens-college]])

- **Refine:** Multi-season burnout monitoring extends into college years [pires-2021-burnout-coping-volleyball-season]. Imagery work matches Pourmohseni's collegiate within-season heterogeneity finding [pourmohseni-2025-imagery-collegiate-volleyball-season]. No new curriculum tracks — consolidation only.

## Coach session-design templates

### Mental skills warm-up block (10 min)

Run weekly at the top of practice, after the dynamic warm-up.

- **10s/12s:** 5 min cooperative passing game with coach narrating affect ("good ball, next one") + 5 min serving with live target only. Modeling, not instruction.
- **14s/16s:** 3 min imagery script (role-specific image for the day's primary skill) + 4 min routine-component drill (breath, two ball bounces, target-pick, cue word — cold, no serve contact) + 3 min low-stakes pressure rep (3-in-a-row banks a point).
- **18s:** 2 min IZOF self-rating (anxiety + self-efficacy 1-10) + 3 min imagery script + 5 min pressure rep with carry-over scoring.

### Pre-tournament mental prep (15 min)

Run the day before. Refresh trained tools; don't introduce new ones.

- **10s/12s:** Skip the formal block. Run a fun 4v4 grill with no scoring stakes — reduce salience, don't add tools.
- **14s/16s:** 5 min role-clarity huddle (each athlete states her two responsibilities for tomorrow's first match) [spink-1990-cohesion-collective-efficacy-volleyball] + 5 min imagery (walk through first three rotations) + 5 min one-cue-per-rep serve block at tournament distance and target.
- **18s:** 3 min IZOF self-check + 5 min role-clarity huddle + 4 min imagery + 3 min one-cue serve block under one-strike pressure.

### Post-loss debrief (10 min)

Pires 2021's pattern says the slow-burn risk is loss of meaning, not visible exhaustion [pires-2021-burnout-coping-volleyball-season]. Post-loss conversations either compound that or interrupt it.

- **10s/12s:** Two questions. "What did you do that you're proud of?" "What's one thing you'll work on next practice?" No video, no stats. Preserve fun retention.
- **14s/16s:** 4 min process review (three things executed, three to refine — specifically not "should have won") + 3 min individual written reflection (one process goal) + 3 min coach-modeled self-talk reset.
- **18s:** 4 min process review with stat anchor (kill % by rotation, pass grade by rotation) + 3 min IZOF re-anchoring + 3 min role re-affirmation (each athlete restates her two primary responsibilities for the next match).

## Anti-patterns

- **Don't pile sequential cues on a skilled setter.** Stacked cues degraded skilled female setters' accuracy *regardless* of direction, and crushed the inter-joint coordination variability that makes them accurate [dearruda-2022-sequential-focus-volleyball-setting]. One cue per rep at 16s+.
- **Don't run a one-shot self-talk session before a tournament.** 1-week ST produced only transient effects; 8-week ST produced durable change with coach-rated performance gains [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes]. Pre-match speech is not the lever.
- **Don't assume "build a routine" improves the serve.** Billing found no PPR effect on serving accuracy at the club level [billing-2014-pre-performance-routines-volleyball]. Coach the components, not presence.
- **Don't catastrophize "don't miss" instructions.** Görgülü tested Wegner's prediction in 43 female teenagers — the predicted ironic-error spike did not appear under verified anxiety manipulation [gorgulu-2021-avoiding-instructions-volleyball-pressure]. The dogma that negative instructions cause uniquely catastrophic errors is not supported in volleyball serving.
- **Don't substitute team-bonding events for role clarity.** Task cohesion (shared goals, role clarity) tied more strongly to collective efficacy than social cohesion at elite levels [spink-1990-cohesion-collective-efficacy-volleyball]. Role definition is the work.
- **Don't run uniform "calm down"/"fire up" pre-match talks.** Optimal pre-match anxiety differs substantially across teammates — a single team script misses the IZOF architecture by design [nogueira-2019-izof-volleyball-anxiety]. At 16s+, pre-match talk is individualized cueing.
