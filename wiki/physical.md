# Physical

## Overview

> **Medical disclaimer.** This page is curricular orientation for coaches, not programming prescription. Defer to qualified professionals — athletic trainers (ATs), Certified Strength and Conditioning Specialists (CSCSs), and physicians — for individual programming, injury rehab and return-to-play, concussion management, nutrition, and any high-intensity conditioning work.

Physical training is the infrastructure of volleyball. Every rally is a chain of short, high-force actions — jump, land, cut, swing, pursue, block, reset — and the athletes still producing those actions in the third set of the last match of a tournament weekend are the ones whose bodies are prepared. The four anchoring qualities at indoor 6s are **jump**, **shoulder durability**, **lateral/linear speed**, and **aerobic-plus-repeated-sprint endurance**. Trunk/hip stability, ankle/knee integrity under landing, and the cervical/scapular base for overhead work sit underneath all four. Sport-specific match physiology grounds the prescription: in elite female match play, mean heart rate runs ~117 bpm with mean blood lactate ~2.7 mmol/L, with **liberos running highest** (130.6 bpm; 17.9% of match at ≥80% HRmax) and **setters lowest** (101.3 bpm), and over 50% of match time spent at ≤60% HRmax — i.e., the sport is intermittent, with most time spent recovering between rallies, and conditioning prescription should be position-specific rather than uniform [akarcesme-2022-heart-rate-lactate-volleyball-match].

The modern stance is explicit: **most sport-specific physical preparation is not the head coach's job to prescribe**. USA Volleyball's Coach Academy frames the coach's share through the **Body pillar** (health, safety, nutrition, motor learning) inside the Bronze/Silver/Gold model [usav-2026-coach-academy] — *recognize*, *modulate*, *collaborate*, not design strength programs. At club and youth level the orientation is **age-appropriate, LTAD-informed** movement through small-sided and game-like play rather than formal resistance training [usav-2026-growing-kids-volleyball][usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]. At HS and college, where a CSCS or AT is on staff, the head coach communicates sport demands and owns court-side load (jump volume, practice intensity, rally density) so specialists periodize everything else. Ecological-dynamics research reinforces this: preparation isolated from the perception–action couplings of real play is less transferable than training embedded in representative game contexts [pinder-2011-representative-learning-design][woods-2020-sport-ecology-designers].

## Major subtopics

- **Strength & conditioning foundations** — *(medical disclaimer applies — CSCS-prescribed at HS+)*. Posterior chain, unilateral lower-body, trunk stability, scapular control, deceleration. At 14U the emphasis is general athleticism and play-based loading — USAV's age-appropriate framing treats body-weight, multi-sport exposure, and small-sided games as the primary developmental lever [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]. HS+ is where formal periodized S&C arrives.
- **Jump training** — *(medical disclaimer applies)*. Total jump count across practice and competition is the largest physical variable a coach controls — and recent IMU + force-plate work in NCAA D-I volleyball shows that **high-intensity jumps (Jumps 50+) drive next-day fatigue more than total jump count**, with mechanical drift visible as force decreases / velocity increases at the same jump height; jump caps should therefore be intensity-weighted, not raw-volume, and **front-row players accumulate more high-intensity jumps than back-row** [sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load]. Approach-and-landing quality is a primary injury-prevention lever — knees-over-toes absorption, even bilateral landing, no backward lean; Mattox's spiking cues are the coaching-eye version [aoc-2021-teach-spiking]. Plyometric prescription belongs to a CSCS, and the meta-analytic evidence base supports a moderate dose: ~2,000 jumps over 8 weeks at 2 sessions/week captures the bulk of available adaptation (pooled effect size on volleyball vertical jump d = 0.82), with diminishing returns and rising injury risk above that envelope [ramirez-campillo-2020-plyometric-meta-analysis]. Architecturally, **vastus lateralis cross-sectional area** and **lateral gastrocnemius fascicle length** are the highest-leverage muscle-architectural targets for sport-specific attack-jump (R² = 0.73 for combined VL-ACSA + LG-FL + training age), and the attack jump is biomechanically distinct from generic CMJ / SJ / DJ — it is its own training target [jiang-2024-muscle-structure-jump-prediction]. **Jump-volume monitoring** remains the coach's job.
- **Mobility & flexibility** — *(medical disclaimer applies)*. The volleyball-specific menu: thoracic extension/rotation, shoulder external rotation, hip flexion/internal rotation, ankle dorsiflexion. A **volleyball-specific dynamic warm-up** that flows into ball-contact beats a generic cardio-and-stretch — Kiraly's "minutes count" frame argues warm-ups should be volleyball-shaped movement [aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently]. Static stretching belongs post-practice; arm-care is the staple closer. The shoulder-specific case for arm-care is grounded: in matched-cohort studies of volleyball attackers, the playing shoulder shows depression, scapular lateralization, posterior-capsule tightness, and rotator-cuff imbalance, with every adaptation more pronounced in symptomatic athletes than in matched-asymptomatic peers — supporting **posterior-shoulder mobility plus rotator-cuff strengthening** as the prevention targets [kugler-1996-shoulder-pain-volleyball-attackers].
- **Injury prevention** — *(medical disclaimer applies — defer to ATs for individual injury risk, rehab, return-to-play)*. Highest-volume concerns: **overuse shoulder pathology** [kugler-1996-shoulder-pain-volleyball-attackers], **patellar tendinopathy** (jumper's knee — junior-elite cohorts show structural change *plus* neovascularization on color Doppler in clinically symptomatic tendons, *and* in 10/57 asymptomatic tendons; "no pain yet" is not "no problem yet" at the adolescent elite level [gisslen-2005-jumpers-knee-elite-junior-volleyball]), **ankle sprains** (cluster-randomized RCT evidence supports balance-board work in warm-ups for any player with prior sprain history; ~€444 per sprain prevented [verhagen-2005-balance-board-ankle-prevention]), **ACL** (volleyball ACL injury is overwhelmingly a non-contact spike-landing event — 97% non-contact, ~62% during landing-after-attack, with female athletes 2-4× the male rate, **adolescent female outside hitters** the highest-risk subgroup, and 15-20-min neuromuscular training programs progressing bilateral-to-unilateral with sport-specific spike-landing simulations the prevention target [sassi-2025-acl-volleyball-systematic-review]), and **low-back stress** (elite jump-serving cohorts show high prevalence of disc pathology and grade-4 spondylolysis at L5-S1, with the repetitive serve mechanism plausibly driving lumbar shear [kulling-2014-lumbar-spine-beach-volleyball]). **Concussion** is a smaller but rising concern: NCAA women's volleyball concussion rates increased 2015/16-2018/19, with 39% of women's-sports concussions caused by contact with **equipment or apparatus** (ball, net, post, floor) — meaning prevention vectors are ball-to-head awareness, floor-defense technique, and net-pole padding, not "tackling mechanics" [chandran-2022-ncaa-concussion-epidemiology]. Coach-owned levers: manage jump/swing volume, teach and reinforce landing mechanics [aoc-2021-teach-spiking], enforce arm care, keep warm-ups specific, and recognize that **late-match fatigue is a real biomechanical phenomenon** — fatigue plus surface instability shifts knee strategies sex-asymmetrically (women toward higher knee-valgus angles), so injury-prevention work should respect the late-set risk window [prieske-2017-fatigue-instability-jump]. Liskevych's defensive material implicitly covers safe floor-pursuit mechanics as 14U athletes learn to dive and extend [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-defense]. **Return-to-play** prognosis for ACL in women's collegiate volleyball is favorable in absolute terms (93.1% return, 65.7% playing in 35+ sets) but the position concentration is striking — **attackers** are 78.8% of injuries vs. 54.7% of the control population, mirroring the spike-landing mechanism [smith-2025-acl-return-to-play-volleyball]. Specialization is also a coach-owned lever for adolescent overuse: high-specialization volleyball youth show OR ~2.3 for overuse injury (p=0.02), and >8-month-per-year volleyball participation OR ~2.0 — the only sport in matched basketball/soccer/volleyball comparisons where specialization significantly raised overuse risk; **multi-sport encouragement is an evidence-backed injury-prevention lever** in this population, not just developmental philosophy [post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball].
- **Load management** — *(medical disclaimer applies)*. The coach owns court-side monitoring: jump count, practice minutes, rally density, cumulative weekly intensity. The systematic-review-supported monitoring stack: **daily session-RPE** for internal load (used in 77% of volleyball studies), **daily jump count / height** for external load via wearable or video (IMU adoption rising at 16%), **weekly countermovement-jump (CMJ)** for neuromuscular fatigue, and **daily Hooper Index** for well-being; HR is unreliable for prescription in volleyball given the anaerobic profile, and uniform programs systematically over-load middle blockers vs. other positions [rebelo-2024-training-stress-fatigue-wellbeing]. Ecological dynamics treats fatigue and adaptation as coupled to informational context — high-decision reps produce a different fatigue signature than blocked reps at the same heart rate [davids-2013-ecological-dynamics-talent][woods-2020-sport-ecology-designers]. **Heat stress** has a sport-specific evidence base: in elite acclimated beach-volleyball populations across 51 FIVB events, only 1 heat-related medical forfeit occurred even with 9 events exceeding the Navy Black Flag threshold (WBGT > 32.3°C) — but the conclusion does *not* generalize to junior, club, or non-acclimated indoor populations, for whom WBGT > 31-32°C remains a meaningful flag [bahr-2012-fivb-heat-stress-monitoring]. AOC's "9 reasons teams fail" recognizes underprepared-body and fatigue-accountability breakdowns as failure modes [aoc-2019-nine-reasons-teams-fail]. Club/HS rules of thumb: count approach jumps (intensity-weighted via Sanders et al. — Jumps 50+ matter more than total count), taper 24–48 hours before tournament peaks, build recovery days after three-day qualifiers [sanders-2025-early-season-jump-load]. The recovery-modality evidence base is uneven: nutrition and sleep show strong, definitive evidence for volleyball, while cold-water immersion and laser therapy show promise but lack volleyball-specific definitive evidence — adoption is reasonable but not settled best practice [closs-2020-recovery-after-volleyball].
- **Sport-specific conditioning** — *(medical disclaimer applies)*. Most **rally-length and repeated-sprint capacity** is built through the sport when practice is dense with live rallies. "Training efficiently" — volleyball-specific warm-ups, more 6v6, more live-ball contact — is itself a conditioning choice [aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently]. Where HS/college S&C staff add conditioning, it is interval-based on work-rest ratios matching the 5–12-second rally / 15–25-second between-rally profile [akarcesme-2022-heart-rate-lactate-volleyball-match], not long continuous aerobic work. The strength-and-power evidence base supports specific targets: **horizontal jumping (broad jump, bounding)** is the highest-leverage power target for change-of-direction (correlations of -0.589 to -0.729 with pro-agility shuttle), vertical jump alone won't carry early acceleration (no significant correlation with 10-m), and most volleyball court movement happens in 3-5 m windows so 20-m / pro-agility tests should be supplemented with shorter-distance sprints [lockie-2020-power-speed-cod-volleyball]. **Periodization model is a smaller lever than consistency** at the junior-male level — 8-week block vs. linear periodization produced equivalent gains on jump, agility, power, and aerobic endurance in matched RCT, with block winning narrowly only on arm/grip strength [tavakkoli-2022-block-vs-traditional-periodization]. Concurrent training (combining strength and endurance) carries a real **interference effect** but it is manageable: 2 sessions/week of 20-30-min endurance is more strength-friendly than 3+ × 50-60 min, HIIT does not produce the same penalty as steady-state (and HIIT mirrors volleyball's match-intensity profile), resistance-before-endurance and 6-24 hour separation between modes reduce interference [wang-2024-concurrent-training-strength-endurance]. The **intervention research base** more broadly supports cluster resistance training over traditional resistance for several strength outcomes, sand training for agility transfer, and core-stability training for CMJ / vertical-jump / pro-agility gains, though the evidence quality is "low" overall — small samples, but consistent direction [keoliya-2024-volleyball-training-interventions].
- **Youth scaling** — *(medical disclaimer applies; youth programming should not be over-loaded or over-specialized)*. USAV LTAD is clear: **multi-sport exposure, movement play, and age-appropriate court/ball/net scaling** beat early specialization [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules][usav-2026-growing-kids-volleyball]. The injury-epidemiology case is direct: in matched basketball/soccer/volleyball youth comparisons, specialization significantly raised overuse-injury risk **only in volleyball** (OR ~2.3), and >8-month-per-year volleyball participation OR ~2.0 [post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball]. At 14U: extensive small-sided games, minimal formal "conditioning," body-weight/balance work only when prescribed, landing-mechanics emphasis as jump volume ramps. The "iY" generational-fitness frame — declining baseline fitness on arrival — reinforces loading patiently [aoc-2018-coaching-iy-generation]. Practitioner-knowledge research finds the LTAD framework is widely known but **inconsistently and incompletely implemented**: only 2.7% of practitioners incorporated all four required components of "athleticism," and three pillars showed weak adherence — accommodating individualized non-linear development, monitoring/assessment tools, and systematic progression [till-2022-ltad-practitioner-knowledge]. The implication for coaches: maturity-status assessment, individualized loading, and explicit progression frameworks are the underused levers.

- **Female-athlete fueling and RED-S** — *(medical disclaimer applies; defer to RDs and physicians for nutritional and medical assessment)*. Volleyball's leanness pressure makes Female Athlete Triad and Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S) coach-relevant. The clinical operational definition: **low energy availability** below ~30 kcal/kg fat-free-mass per day damages metabolic rate, immunity, protein synthesis, GI, cardiovascular, growth, and psychological function — i.e., LEA is a performance problem, not just a health problem; performance impact includes reduced endurance, strength, training response, coordination, judgment, and increased injury risk [coelho-2021-female-athlete-triad-reds]. The IOC RED-S Clinical Assessment Tool (CAT) is the screening instrument; first-line treatment is nutritional rehabilitation (300-600 kcal/day above current intake) plus interdisciplinary care. Coaches see meal patterns and practice intensity that nutritionists never see — recognition is the coach's share, prescription is not.

## Schools of thought

- [[usa-volleyball]] — governing-body frame: age-appropriate rules, LTAD-aligned progressions, and the Coach Academy's **Body pillar** as the coach's share of the physical-preparation space [usav-2026-coach-academy]. The shift from legacy CAP/IMPACT to Bronze/Silver/Gold.
- [[japanese-training]] — the historical intensity tradition (Daimatsu-era high-volume, high-precision training) is part of the physical-training heritage. Worth understanding for what it built and the adult-body cost it has since been critiqued for. Context, not a template for 14U.
- [[ecological-dynamics]] — frames preparation through **representative learning design**: conditioning should sample the information-movement couplings of real play so training fatigue and decision load match match-day couplings [pinder-2011-representative-learning-design][woods-2020-sport-ecology-designers][davids-2013-ecological-dynamics-talent]. "Game-like" and "blocked" reps at the same heart rate are not the same stimulus.
- [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] — eclectic practitioner frame; strongest contribution here is **training-efficiency** thinking [aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently] and coaching-eye cues for landing and arm mechanics [aoc-2021-teach-spiking][aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-defense].

## Getting started

If you are new to the physical-preparation side of coaching and want to get the scope right without overreaching:

1. [[usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]] and [[usav-2026-growing-kids-volleyball]] — USAV's age-appropriate and LTAD-aligned framing. Establishes what 14U-and-younger training should look like and why formal resistance training is not the entry point.
2. [[usav-2026-coach-academy]] — the Bronze-tier **Body pillar** defines the coach's share: recognize, modulate, collaborate.
3. [[aoc-2024-kiraly-training-efficiently]] — Kiraly's "minutes count" frame tells you how to make warm-ups and practice carry conditioning load rather than bolting on extra running.
4. [[aoc-2021-teach-spiking]] — Mattox's landing-mechanics cues are the coach's primary injury-prevention tool as jump volume climbs.
5. [[aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-defense]] — safe floor-pursuit mechanics matter as 14U athletes learn to play above the floor.
6. **Consult a CSCS** before building any formal strength or plyometric program, and partner with an **AT** for injury-prevention screening, landing assessment, and in-season care.
