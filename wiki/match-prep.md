# Match Prep

## Overview

Match prep is the tactical layer on top of [[practice-planning]] and [[season-planning]]. Practice decides what the team can do; season planning decides what it is training toward; match prep decides what it will actually do against this opponent, in this rotation, at this moment in the bracket. It is the 24-to-72-hour window where scouting, stat review, video, and lineup decisions converge into a plan, and then the three-second window mid-third-set where that plan either survives contact with the opponent or gets overwritten by what the coach now sees.

The modern coach operates on an unprecedented data-and-video stack. DataVolley (and its more recent integrations like VolleyMetrics and VolleyStation) generates rotation-by-rotation attack and pass stats at the professional and top-college level; Hudl tagging and similar platforms give even club-level coaches serve-receive and swing data their predecessors could only estimate; phone cameras and cloud uploads mean a coach walks to the car with a full match film in their pocket. The craft has not changed — know your opponent, know yourself, pick the fights you can win — but the evidence base is denser by an order of magnitude than twenty years ago. The skill is no longer getting numbers; it is knowing which numbers matter and which ones will mislead a team if over-coached.

Karch Kiraly frames it as "you can't manage what you don't measure" — while warning that taking the right, limited set of stats matters more than taking more of them [aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most]. Russ Rose's Penn State model pushes farther: analyze your own team's tendencies as aggressively as the opponent's, and let the match-day plan shape in-match decisions [aoc-2017-rose-pennstate-prep]. John Dunning's Stanford frame adds a third dimension — how to prepare for the most important match of the year without breaking your own character or the team's [aoc-2017-dunning-stanford-character].

## Major subtopics

- **Opponent scouting** — rotations, serve-receive strengths and weaknesses by rotation, primary attackers and their tendencies (line vs. angle, high-ball vs. quick, preferred set location), blocking scheme, setter dump frequency, weakest servers (the rotations you expect to score in, per Rose's bad-servers frame). At club level, scouting is often a single pool-play match plus whatever video you can record from the adjacent court.
- **Self-scouting** — what the opponent is seeing when they scout you. Rotation-by-rotation passing percentages, side-out percentage by rotation, first-ball sideout (FBSO) percentage, attack efficiency by hitter and set location, serving error rate. The honest self-scout is the precondition for choosing which fights to pick. See Rose on the impact of passing — passing quality is the single biggest lever on the whole offense [aoc-2018-rose-impact-of-passing].
- **Stats that matter** — the limited set a coach actually uses in-match. Kiraly calls out serving and passing as the highest-priority stat families [aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most]. The operational shortlist: side-out percentage by rotation, FBSO, serve-receive quality distribution, serving aggressive-ness vs. error rate (ace-to-error ratio, missed-serve percentage), attack efficiency by hitter and zone. Everything else is diagnostic context — useful after the match, rarely decision-grade in the middle of a set.
- **Video prep workflow** — Stone and Liskevych organize video use into three buckets: scouting, match analysis, and teaching skills and tactics (both online and embedded in live practice) [aoc-2020-stone-liskevych-using-video]. The modern workflow: tag the match, pull clips organized by rotation or tactic, watch with the team in 10-to-20-minute blocks, and translate to practice design and the match-day scouting report.
- **In-match adjustments** — the decisions the plan can't make for you. Kiraly's in-game-tactics corpus covers rotating defense against a hot hitter, adjusting serve targets to attack the weakest passer-in-this-match, calling timeouts on momentum shifts rather than scoreline shifts, and pulling the setter-dump or slide look at the right leverage points [aoc-2024-kiraly-in-game-tactics]. His serving-pressure work extends the frame to the individual server at the line on a big point [aoc-2024-kiraly-serving-pressure]. The standard move is a bench assistant watching rotation-by-rotation while the head coach manages the next point.
- **Tournament prep (pool-to-bracket)** — the club-season reality. Pool play is where scouting happens in real time; bracket play is where the report cashes in or gets rewritten. Week-before practice installs the base plan; the morning-of walkthrough previews the opponent; between matches the staff re-scouts while players recover. Hugh McCutcheon frames this as committing to a standard of competitive excellence — the operational discipline carries a team through the bracket, not the scouting binder [mccutcheon-2021-volleybrains-ep30].

## Schools of thought

- [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] — the richest match-prep corpus in the wiki: Kiraly's stats-that-matter-most, in-game-tactics, and serving-pressure work; Rose's Penn State match-prep, bad-servers, and best-servers game-planning pieces [aoc-2024-rose-best-servers][aoc-2018-rose-bad-servers]; Dunning on character-preservation through match analysis; Stone-Liskevych on video. The AOC thesis is that reading is the underlying skill of match prep as much as of passing: collect deliberately, watch carefully, adjust quickly.
- [[gold-medal-squared]] — data-informed practice design is the match-prep upstream. GMS emphasizes measuring what you want to improve and building practice blocks around the targeted metric; the in-match stat discipline is downstream of a practice culture that already counts everything that matters.
- [[japanese-training]] — scouting precision is a distinguishing feature of the Japanese tradition. Multiple camera angles, frame-by-frame review, and the cultural expectation that coaches and players watch hours of opponent video together predate the DataVolley era by decades.

For the coach upgrading match prep, read in this order:

1. [[aoc-2024-kiraly-stats-matter-most]] — start with Kiraly's framing. Fewer, better stats beats more stats.

3. [[aoc-2024-rose-best-servers]] and [[aoc-2018-rose-bad-servers]] — stack your servers, exploit theirs.
