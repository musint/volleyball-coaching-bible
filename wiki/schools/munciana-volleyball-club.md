# Munciana Volleyball Club

## Overview

Munciana Volleyball Club is a nationally-recognized girls' club program headquartered in Muncie, Indiana, co-directed by Alyssa and Mike Lingenfelter [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide]. Munciana's distinguishing program choice is **club-wide system alignment** — a single unified skill-training and systems framework that applies from the youngest teams through 18U so players never have to relearn technique or rotations as they move up age groups [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide]. The program's public coaching voice arrives through three complementary channels: Mike Lingenfelter's [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] video series on middle-attack tempo, accountability-driven passing, and youth warmup design [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-attack-tempo] [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing] [aoc-2021-lingenfelter-short-ball-long-ball]; the September 2025 AOC Coach Connection LIVE session with Mike and Alyssa Lingenfelter [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide]; and a 2022 internal camp-drill library — a 64-minute video tutorial where Mike walks Munciana camp staff through the program's full drill inventory in the order it would be coached [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Together those sources document the most operationally-detailed coaching framework on this wiki for a multi-team nationally-recognized club, which makes Munciana the most context-relevant program reference for coaches running similar Indiana-caliber clubs (Song's immediate context).

## Core principles

**1. Club-wide system alignment.** A single unified skill-training and systems vocabulary applies across every age group in the club [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide]. Players never have to relearn technique or system shapes as they move from youngest teams through 18U. This is a club-architecture choice more than a practice-planning choice — and it contrasts directly with the more common club pattern of each team running its own head coach's preferred system.

**2. Rep-based confidence.** "Reps equal confidence. The more I can rep, the more I feel good" [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing]. The skill-training design follows from this: high-volume reps with named focal points (balance, quiet platform, pass-and-cover, avoiding jammed passes) inside short-window drills, rather than low-volume drills with abstract objectives [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing] [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**3. Accountability built into every drill.** "Every passing drill should have a winner and a loser" [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing]. Munciana drills are competitive by design — scoring is tracked, someone wins, someone loses. The cultural implication is structural rather than rhetorical: the drill itself creates the pressure; the coach does not have to manufacture it [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing] [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**4. Pursuit and recovery as the foundation.** The Munciana camp library opens with a two-man shuffle drill — hold low, shuffle down and back, no ball — pursuit footwork as the first thing camp athletes do [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. The principle that emerges across the camp library is that footwork and pursuit are the unstated prerequisites for every other skill, drilled before the ball is even introduced.

**5. Closure on the ball, not reaching for the ball.** The transition from pursuit drills into passing drills is governed by "simple closure on the ball" — the platform stays quiet, the body steers the angle, the passer finishes on balance [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. This same principle re-emerges in defensive drills: arrival behind the ball precedes platform formation.

**6. Middle-tempo discipline.** The three-tempo middle-attack framework is indexed by left-foot plant timing relative to setter contact — Tempo 3: foot plants after the ball leaves the setter; Tempo 2: foot plants while the ball is in the setter's hands; Tempo 1: middle is already airborne while the ball is in the setter's hands [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-tempo]. The coaching cue is anchored to two observable events, which makes the framework adoptable verbatim by other clubs [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-attack-tempo] [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-tempo].

**7. Aim small, miss small for serving.** Munciana's serving philosophy is target-driven from day one. "All too often in serving we just serve to serve, and it becomes somewhat of a dumpster" [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Instead, every serve targets a finite area — down to a 2-inch target on a noodle or a single floor box — so athletes hold themselves accountable to placement, not just clearing the net [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**8. Out-of-system as default.** "You're going to be out of system about 80 percent of the time" [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. The setting and hitting block trains non-setter setters, dig-then-set patterns, and team out-of-system flows on the assumption that a perfect first-contact pass is the exception, not the rule [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**9. 6v6 as psychology rehearsal.** The competitive 6v6 culmination of every Munciana camp progression is engineered around different end-game pressures — Biggie Smalls (3-in-a-row), Prove It (must serve and score), Momentum (escalating point values), Tug of War (shared rope), Finish Strong (19-19 reset on miss) [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. The chassis of each game varies; the technique stays constant. The principle is that competitive 6v6 trains psychological habits that show up in real matches, not just tactical patterns.

## Methodology

Munciana's methodology operationalizes those principles in a single coherent practice arc that is documented end-to-end in the 2022 camp library [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. The arc — pass → defend → serve → set → hit → block → 6v6 — mirrors the practice flow Munciana would run with its own teams; the camp library is the club's drill library, not a separate camp-only inventory [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**Always run left and right.** Almost every passing drill is mirrored — symmetry is the default, not an option. Drills like Three-Man Tilt, Speed Close, and Directional Pass run in both directions [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**The Three P's of defense — get back, get low, get a touch.** Sometimes also articulated as posture-position-passion. Stated explicitly inside the seat-to-seam drill at 19:45 of the camp tape, the Three P's are a coaching shortcut for the defensive ready position and pursuit decision [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**Outside foot is high.** Defensive footwork law — "this doesn't happen by accident, it has to be taught" — surfaces inside Three-Way Release at 14:06 of the camp tape [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Munciana drills the outside-foot-high stance as a teachable invariant, not as a player-discovered habit.

**Read and run, not watch and stand.** Speedball is named explicitly as "the intro to defense" in the camp progression — defense is taught from a games-based ball-up-everywhere starting point, not from isolated platform work [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Munciana's defensive identity is movement-first.

**Teacher drills.** Mountain D, Campfire D, Figure-Eight Defense, Seat-to-Seam, and Pursuit-Attack are all framed as drills where the *mechanic* teaches the habit — failure resets to a costly position so the body learns. The coach is the feeder; the geometry of the drill carries the lesson [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**Drill volume in tight windows.** "We'll touch more balls in a five minute period than some people will pass in an hour" [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Rep density is a stated design principle: drills are short, scoring resets fast, and athletes accumulate reps inside small geometric formats (triangles, tilt-chains, two-ball patterns) rather than long lines.

**Aggressive-mentality serving.** Drills like No-Gut Serving and Five Before Ten are designed to harp on errors as evidence of intent rather than evidence of poor mechanics — "I want that aggressive serving mentality" [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Munciana's serving culture is closer to the [[karch-kiraly]] tough-serving stance than to a clean-percentage stance.

**Triangles as the multi-skill format.** Triangle Passing, Triangle Passing off a Box B, and the later Tight Triangles setting/hitting drill all use the same three-corner geometry to combine multiple skills in a small space. The geometry is a Munciana signature [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

## Contrasts with other schools

Munciana sits closest to [[gold-medal-squared]] in its emphasis on rep-based skill-training and to [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] in its competing-in-practice culture, but the contrast is at the *unit of analysis*: GMS speaks to elite-team motor-learning principles; AOC speaks to coach-by-coach drill design; Munciana speaks to **club-wide system architecture across age groups**. The closest analog on this wiki at the same unit of analysis is the [[brazilian-school]]'s club-to-NT pipeline framing, though Munciana operates at the youth-club tier while CBV operates at the national-development tier.

Compared to the looser methodology common at non-aligned clubs — where each team runs its own head coach's preferred system and players relearn vocabulary as they age up — Munciana's choice is the structural opposite: every age group, every drill, every coaching cue is shared. The cost of this choice is constraint on individual head coaches at each age; the benefit is that a 16U athlete arriving on an 18U team already speaks the same coaching language [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide].

## Critiques and limitations

The Munciana methodology assumes a club operating at scale large enough to maintain coordinated coaching across age groups — multiple teams per age, full-time program leadership, dedicated court time. Smaller clubs may not be able to sustain the alignment cost. Additionally, Munciana's public-facing coaching record runs almost exclusively through Mike Lingenfelter's voice; Alyssa Lingenfelter co-directs the program but is less documented in the sources on this wiki [unsourced]. A future ingest of additional Munciana materials — particularly Alyssa Lingenfelter's voice and the club's 14U-and-younger curriculum — would round out the program's documented identity.
