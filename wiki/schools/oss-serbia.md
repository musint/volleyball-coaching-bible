# Volleyball Federation of Serbia (Odbojkaški savez Srbije, OSS)

## Overview

Federation stub for the Volleyball Federation of Serbia (Odbojkaški savez Srbije, OSS) — the CEV-affiliated governing body (federation code: SRB) for indoor volleyball in Serbia [oss-serbia-2026-wikipedia]. OSS is recognized by FIVB and CEV as the inheritor of the records of the Volleyball Federation of Yugoslavia (1948-1991) and the Volleyball Federation of Serbia and Montenegro (1992-2006), and operates as the standalone OSS from 2006 with Serbian independence [oss-serbia-2026-wikipedia]. The federation administers SuperLiga (men's and women's), domestic cup competitions, and national-team selection across senior, junior, youth, and beach categories. Member of the European Volleyball Confederation since the post-Yugoslav re-admission. As of 2022 CEV / FIVB ranking context: Serbia 7th CEV men's / 13th globally; 5th CEV women's / 11th globally [oss-serbia-2026-wikipedia]. The institutional context for the [[serbian-school]] coaching tradition built under [[zoran-terzic]]'s long women's-NT tenure and the men's-NT continuity through Kolaković, Grbić, Kovač, and Crețu — see the [[serbian-school]] page for the doctrinal and result-record content. ## Notable practitioners

- [[zoran-terzic]] — Serbia women's NT head coach 2002-2022 and 2025-present; the federation's longest-tenure head coach across both genders.
- [[slobodan-kovac]] — Serbia men's NT head coach 2019-2021; 2019 European Championship gold; player on the 2000 Sydney FRY gold-medal squad.
- [[igor-kolakovic]] — Serbia men's NT head coach 2007-2014 and 2022-2024; 2010 World Championship bronze, 2011 European Championship gold.
- [[nikola-grbic]] — Serbia men's NT head coach 2014-2019; 2016 World League gold, 2017 European Championship bronze; player on the 2000 Sydney FRY gold-medal squad; current head coach of [[polish-school]] (Poland NT) 2022-present.
