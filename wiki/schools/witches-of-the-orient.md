The "Witches of the Orient" (Toyo no Majo / 東洋の魔女) was the international nickname given to the Japan women's national team that won the 1962 FIVB World Championship and the 1964 Tokyo Olympic gold medal under head coach Daimatsu Hirobumi. The core of the team came from [[daimatsu-hirobumi]]'s [[nichibo-kaizuka]] club. The team became a global cultural phenomenon and is the anchor reference point for the legacy of the traditional [[japanese-training]] methodology.
