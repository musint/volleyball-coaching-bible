# Federación Cubana de Voleibol (FCV)

## Overview

Federation/league stub for the **Federación Cubana de Voleibol (FCV)**, the institution responsible for organizing and regulating volleyball in Cuba and for managing the country's national-team programs across categories [fcv-cuba-2025-fivb-election]. The FCV is **FIVB-affiliated** and operates within **NORCECA** (North, Central America and Caribbean Volleyball Confederation) jurisdiction [fcv-cuba-2025-fivb-election]. The federation is the institutional carrier of the [[cuban-school]] dynasty era and of the Cuban men's program, including the 1976 Montreal Olympic bronze [cuba-men-nt-2026-wikipedia], the 1989 World Cup men's gold [cuba-men-nt-2026-wikipedia], and the women's three-Olympic-gold dynasty (1992, 1996, 2000) under [[eugenio-george]] [cuba-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]. As of the 2025-2028 cycle the federation reports **15 NORCECA Championship titles for the men's program** [cuba-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] and **eight Pan American Games golds plus the 1991-2000 eight-tournament FIVB winning streak for the women's program** [cuba-women-nt-2026-wikipedia].

The 2025–2028 FCV leadership cycle was elected at a General Assembly in Havana on **9 May 2025**, presided over by Cuban Olympic Committee President Roberto León Richards and Cuban Sports Minister Osvaldo Vento Montiller [fcv-cuba-2025-fivb-election]. **Osvaldo Idel Martínez Arias**, who previously served as FCV Secretary General and brings over four decades of Cuban-volleyball experience as PE instructor, coach, and federation administrator, was elected **president** [fcv-cuba-2025-fivb-election]. **[[mireya-luis]] Hernández** — three-time consecutive Olympic gold medallist (1992 Barcelona, 1996 Atlanta, 2000 Sydney) and concurrent **FIVB Executive Vice President** with positions on the FIVB Board of Administration and Executive Committee — was elected **vice president** [fcv-cuba-2025-fivb-election] [luis-2026-wikipedia-biography]. The FCV Executive Committee: **Yumilka Ruíz** (1998-2000 dynasty roster member) as Board Member and National Commissioner; **Ricardo Borroto** as Secretary General; **Lorenzo Martínez** as Treasurer; **Jorge Sosa** and **Jorge Luis García** as Board Members [fcv-cuba-2025-fivb-election]. The dynasty-players-into-federation-governance pipeline (Mireya Luis, Yumilka Ruíz) is one of the federation's documented continuity patterns post the dynasty era.

## Notable practitioners

- [[eugenio-george]] — Cuban women's NT head coach 1968-1996, assistant 1996-2008; FIVB Best Women's Volleyball Team Coach of the Twentieth Century
- [[antonio-perdomo]] — Cuban women's NT assistant 1973-1998, head coach 1998-2007 (2008 Beijing)
- [[mireya-luis]] — three-Olympic-gold dynasty captain; concurrent FCV vice president and FIVB Executive Vice President
