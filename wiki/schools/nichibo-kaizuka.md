The Nichibo Kaizuka (Dai Nippon Spinning Company) women's team under Daimatsu Hirobumi (1954-1965) became the dominant force in Japanese industrial-league volleyball — the club that provided the core of the 1964 Tokyo Olympic gold-medal Japan team. Famously nicknamed the [[witches-of-the-orient]], Daimatsu's Nichibo won 258 consecutive matches and pioneered the high-volume, technically exacting training methodology that would later generalize into the modern [[japanese-training]] tradition.
