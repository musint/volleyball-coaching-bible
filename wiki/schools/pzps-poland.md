# Polish Volleyball Federation (PZPS)

## Overview

Stub for a federation/institution page — paired with the principal-tradition page at [[polish-school]]. The **Polish Volleyball Federation** (*Polski Związek Piłki Siatkowej*, PZPS) is the national governing body for volleyball in Poland. Founded in **1928**, headquartered in Warsaw, the PZPS joined FIVB in 1947 and CEV in 1963 [pzps-2026-wikipedia]. The federation oversees 16 provincial volleyball associations and organizes "all the men's domestic competitions from professional to amateur as well as all women's domestic championships from First tier to the lowest one" — including PlusLiga (men's first tier), Tauron Liga (women's first tier), Polish Cup, and Polish SuperCup [pzps-2026-wikipedia]. PZPS manages senior + U23 + U21 + U19 national teams across both genders [pzps-2026-wikipedia]. Current president since 2021 is Sebastian Świderski, a former Polish men's NT player; notable historical leadership includes Tadeusz Brzósko (1957-1969) and Mirosław Przedpełski (2004-2015) [pzps-2026-wikipedia]. The PZPS-administered men's national-team program is the structural carrier of the [[polish-school]] tradition: imported head coaches (Italian Anastasi, French Antiga, Belgian Heynen, Serbian Grbić) plug into PZPS-employed Polish staff and a player core developed through PlusLiga, with the federation as the institutional continuity through coaching transitions [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. Modern competitive results under PZPS administration: World Championship gold in 2014 + 2018, silver in 2022; European gold 2009 + 2023; 2024 Paris Olympic silver — Poland's first Olympic medal since 1976 [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The federation's training-centre infrastructure is anchored at Spała.

## Notable practitioners

- [[andrea-anastasi]] — Polish men's NT HC February 23, 2011 - October 2013; first imported HC of the modernization era; lifted program from FIVB rank 11 to medal-contending status.
- [[stephane-antiga]] — Polish men's NT HC October 24, 2013 - 2016; 2014 World Championship gold (host).
- [[vital-heynen]] — Polish men's NT HC February 7, 2018 - 2021; 2018 World Championship gold (back-to-back defense).
- [[nikola-grbic]] — Polish men's NT HC January 12, 2022 - present; 2024 Paris Olympic silver, 2023 European gold, 2023 + 2025 Nations League gold.
