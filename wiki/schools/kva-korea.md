# Korea Volleyball Association (KVA)

## Overview

The **Korea Volleyball Association (KVA, 대한민국배구협회)** is the governing body for non-professional volleyball in South Korea. Founded **March 1946** with headquarters in Bangi-dong, Songpa-gu, Seoul, the federation has been a member of the FIVB since **1959** and is a member of the Asian Volleyball Confederation (AVC) [kva-2026-wikipedia]. KVA's 2007 incorporation as a corporation aggregate formalized its institutional governance structure under current president Oh Han-nam and secretary Cho Young-gu [kva-2026-wikipedia].

KVA's jurisdictional scope covers the senior men's and women's national teams, the U-21 / U-19 / U-17 youth categories, and amateur / non-professional domestic competitions; it also coordinates training of players, referees, and operational personnel [kva-2026-wikipedia]. The federation's competitive history at the senior women's level — 1976 Montreal Olympic bronze, 2012 London + 2020 Tokyo Olympic 4th-place finishes, 1994 + 2014 Asian Games gold — anchors the [[korean-school]] page [korea-women-nt-2026-wikipedia].

KVA must be distinguished from **KOVO (Korea Volleyball Federation, 한국배구연맹)**, the separate body governing the V-League professional men's and women's competitions launched in 2005 [kva-2026-wikipedia] [vleague-korea-2026-wikipedia]. The KVA-vs-KOVO governance separation is unusual relative to most national federations and reflects the historical sequencing in which national-team / amateur volleyball governance (1946) preceded professional-league formation (2005) by nearly six decades. Foreign head-coach appointments at the senior women's NT level (Lavarini 2019-2021, Hernández González 2022-2024) are KVA decisions; V-League foreign-player and foreign-coach decisions are KOVO and individual-club decisions.
