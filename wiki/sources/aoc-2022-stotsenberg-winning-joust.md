# Winning the joust in volleyball

## Summary
Jed Stotsenberg's golden rule of jousting: "The player who pushes last wins." Technique: big hand (to feel the block), apply pressure, push through. If blocker pressure is weak, push straight. If strong, push straight first then redirect to the side. A short setter who can joust can score a lot of points on tight passes.

## Key claims / ideas
- "The player who pushes last wins" — last-in-time beats tall in a joust.
- Big hand on the ball to feel contact with the block.
- Pressure + push through. Weak pressure → straight; strong pressure → straight first, then side.
- Jousts are a meaningful source of setter kills, especially for shorter setters.
