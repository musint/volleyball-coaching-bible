# Jim Stone: Biomechanical passing analysis – simpler is better

## Summary
Jim Stone breaks down passing mechanics using Kayla Banwarth as the exemplar: arms extended, palms up early, "one move to the ball with the platform," quiet upper body, most motion done with the legs before platform presentation. Pre-decide the seam. Practice R→L, L→R, and midline.

## Key claims / ideas
- Arms extended and palms up early — not at contact.
- "One move to the ball with the platform" — minimize mid-contact adjustments.
- Quiet, calm upper body; most movement is done by the legs in shuffle steps before contact.
- Present platform early and angle to target.
- Pre-decide seam ownership with adjacent passer — avoid mid-play confusion.
- Train all three contact directions: right-to-left, left-to-right, midline.
