## Summary

Tod Mattox's 1v1 dynamic warmup splits the team into groups of four per court. Two players face off across the net behind the 10-foot line; two wait at the post. Each rep is two contacts — bump to self, then hit over the net. Missed returns sub out. Mattox insists on full swings rather than tips or lollipops: the whole point is to challenge the opposing player with a real driven ball. A ladder format layers on competitive pressure: winners climb courts, losers drop. Great for pre-match game-like touches and for conditioning.

## Key claims / ideas

- 1v1 drills maximize touches per minute and work as high-density warmup.
- The 2-contact constraint (bump-to-self, then drive) keeps the rep game-like and legal.
- Full swings are required — roll shots and tips violate the intent of the drill.
- Ladder format (winners up a court, losers down) layers in competition without a complex scoring system.
- Works pre-match for "game-like contacts" and as in-practice conditioning.
