# Men's passing: Formations to protect your hitters

## Summary
Ben Josephson (Texas associate HC) frames men's serve-receive formation design around one rule: set up your best attackers for success. Keep the dominant back-row hitter deep to preserve attack range; park confident overhead passers just behind the 10-foot line.

## Key claims / ideas
- Formation design starts from: who are your best attackers, and how do we protect their approach?
- Dominant back-row hitter receives deep to keep their full attack range available
- Tall middle or outside hitters confident in overhead passing cover territory behind the 10-foot line
- Every formation choice cascades from the offense, not the pass
- At minimum 5 players, half a court for the drill

- Overhead passing roles
