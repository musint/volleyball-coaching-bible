# The Liskevych 5

## Summary
Liskevych describes a rolling in-match evaluation heuristic: judge a player by her last five contacts on the ball, not her cumulative stats, to detect who is hot or cold right now.

## Key claims / ideas
- Full-match hitting percentages obscure within-match momentum swings.
- Looking at the most recent five contacts reveals current form (starting strong and fading, starting slow and building, etc.).
- In-the-moment hot/cold reads drive substitution and set-distribution decisions.
- Small-sample contextual evaluation beats cumulative stat tracking for tactical adjustments.

## Topics covered
- In-match player evaluation and scouting
- Rolling-window statistical reads vs. cumulative stats
- Substitution and set-distribution decisions
- Coaching philosophy on momentum
