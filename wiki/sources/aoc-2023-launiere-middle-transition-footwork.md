# Middle hitter transition footwork: 3- and 4-step approach

## Summary
University of Utah head coach Beth Launiere (1990-2025) prescribes exact step-by-step transition patterns from block to attack, with left- and right-side specific sequences and a "full circle" pivot on the right side to keep the middle open to the court.

## Key claims / ideas
- Left-side transition (4-step approach): after blocking, turn inside-court — right, left, right, pivot to net — then approach with left, right, left.
- Right-side transition (4-step approach): after blocking, turn inside-court — left, right, left, hop landing with right behind/left forward — then approach left, right, left.
- "Full circle" on right-side transition: keeps the middle's eyes on the court through the turn, giving them more time to read.
- Launiere specifies this came from her own study — she presents it as a researched best practice, not a heuristic.

## Topics covered
- Middle transition footwork from block to attack
- Asymmetric step patterns for left vs. right transitions
- Open-to-court pivot philosophy
- 4-step approach mechanics
