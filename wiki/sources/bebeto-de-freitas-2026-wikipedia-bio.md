# Bebeto de Freitas — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article for **Paulo Roberto de Freitas**, known as
**Bebeto de Freitas** — head coach of the Brazil men's national volleyball
team during the 1980s and the architect of the **1984 Los Angeles Olympic
silver** ("Silver Generation"). Born January 15, 1950; played for Brazil
at the 1972 Munich and 1976 Montreal Olympics; died March 13, 2018 (age
68, heart attack at the Atlético Mineiro training center). Bebeto is the
direct-predecessor link in the Brazilian men's NT coaching lineage to
Bernardinho — who served as his **assistant at the 1988 Seoul Olympics**
and later succeeded him in the coaching tree. Inducted into the
International Volleyball Hall of Fame in 2015.

The article's distinctive value for the wiki is its **direct documentation
of Bebeto as head coach of the Italian men's national team, winning the
1998 FIVB World Championship gold** — a concrete data point placing Bebeto
at the top of the Italian-Brazilian methodology-exchange axis at the
head-coach level, in the Velasco "Generazione di Fenomeni" succession
window.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Born January 15, 1950; died March 13, 2018** (age 68) at the Atlético
  Mineiro training center (heart attack).
- **Played at the 1972 Munich and 1976 Montreal Olympics** as a Brazil
  men's NT player.
- **1982 World Championship silver** — Bebeto listed as Brazil men's NT
  coach.
- **1984 LA Olympic silver** as Brazil men's NT HC — the "Silver
  Generation" team that included Bernardinho and Renan Dal Zotto as
  players.
- **1988 Seoul Olympics** — Bebeto remained HC; Bernardinho served as
  assistant coach.
- **1998 FIVB World Championship gold as HC of the Italian men's NT** —
  one of the few coaches in history to win World Championships with two
  nations. Directly evidences the Italian-Brazilian methodology-exchange
  axis at head-coach level.
- **International Volleyball Hall of Fame induction: 2015**.
