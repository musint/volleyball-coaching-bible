# Volleyballmag — Exciting times for LOVB approaching inaugural 2025 pro season (June 26, 2024)

## Summary
Volleyballmag's preview of LOVB's 2025 inaugural pro season, published roughly six months before launch. Confirms LOVB's single-entity ownership model (per COO Rosie Spaulding), the six launch markets (Austin, Atlanta, Houston, Madison, Omaha, Salt Lake), head-coach hires, the SPANX-as-first-sports-partnership detail, and the nine 2024 USA Olympic-roster signings. The most authoritative pre-launch summary outside LOVB's own press releases.

## Key claims / ideas
- **LOVB is a single-entity league** — parent organization owns all teams. Quote from COO Rosie Spaulding: "That's the way we believe is best to start to be sustainable."
- **Six 2025 launch markets:** Austin, Atlanta, Houston, Madison, Omaha, Salt Lake
- **Two more teams planned for 2027** (later announced as LA, Minnesota, San Francisco)
- **Nine 2024 USA Olympic-roster members signed** for the 2025 season — Poulter, Carlini, Washington, Ogbogu, Larson, Robinson-Cook (and others)
- **Two USA Olympic team assistants among LOVB head coaches:** Matt Fuerbringer (Madison), Tama Miyashiro (Salt Lake)
- **SPANX:** first-ever sports partnership in the brand's history
- **Adidas** (uniform supplier, June 2024) and **Mikasa** (ball supplier)
- **ESPN** broadcast inventory of 10 matches in 2025 (later expanded)
- Specific salary figures not disclosed; "veteran stars" earn more than rookies
