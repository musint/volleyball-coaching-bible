# Andrea Anastasi — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical record for **Andrea "Nano" Anastasi** — Italian outside hitter (Italy NT 1981–1991, 141 caps; 1990 World Championship gold as player) who became one of the most institutionally significant European men's NT head coaches of the 2000s–2020s. Anastasi head-coached three different national teams (Italy 1999–2003 + 2007–2010, Spain 2005–2007, Poland 2011–2013, plus a brief Belgium stint in 2018) and won European Championships with all three of his three primary national programs, the only coach to do so in modern men's volleyball. His Polish tenure (Feb 23, 2011 – Oct 2013) is the direct anchor for the Italian-methodology import into Polish men's volleyball: Anastasi inherited a team ranked 11th in the FIVB and produced **three medals in 2011 alone** (World Cup silver, World League bronze, European Championship bronze), then 2012 World League gold and London Olympic 5th place. Subsequent club-coaching success in Poland (Trefl Gdańsk, Projekt Warsaw) and Italy (Sir Safety Perugia 2022 FIVB Club World Championship gold, Gas Sales Piacenza) extended his coaching footprint into the 2020s.

## Key claims / ideas

- Born October 8, 1960, in Poggio Rusco, Italy.
- Played outside hitter for Italy 1981–1991 (141 caps); won 1990 FIVB World Championship gold; 1989 European Championship gold; 1990 + 1991 World League golds.
- Italy men's NT head coach 1999–2003 + 2007–2010: 1999 European Championship gold; 1999 + 2000 World League gold; **2000 Sydney Olympic bronze**; 2003 European Championship gold.
- Spain men's NT head coach 2005–2007: **2007 European Championship gold (Russia)**.
- Poland men's NT head coach 2011–2013: appointed Feb 23, 2011 with team ranked 11th in FIVB; **three medals in 2011** (World Cup silver, World League bronze, European Championship bronze) — described in the article as "unprecedented" for Polish volleyball; 2012 World League gold; London 2012 Olympic 5th place; dismissed October 2013.
- Brief Belgium men's NT stint in 2018.
- Club coaching highlights: Trefl Gdańsk (2014–2019, Polish Cup + SuperCup); Projekt Warsaw (2019–2022); **Sir Safety Perugia 2022 FIVB Club World Championship gold**; Gas Sales Piacenza (2023–2025).
- Three coach-of-the-year honors: 2000 Italian Coach of the Year; 2011 + 2012 Polish Coach of the Year.
- Andrea Gardini served as Anastasi's assistant in Poland.
