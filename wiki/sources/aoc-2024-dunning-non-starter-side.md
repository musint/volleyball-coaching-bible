# John Dunning: Motivating the non-starter side of the net

## Summary
Dunning credits Stanford's 2016 championship partly to the non-starter side of the net bringing it in practice every day — and describes the incentive structure (win a drill and switch sides to play with the starters) that made it work.

## Key claims / ideas
- Stanford's 2016 title run was driven in part by the non-starter side showing up with belief and excitement daily.
- Drill incentive: if the non-starters beat the starters, a player gets to switch sides and play with starters — making every rep matter.
- Competitive gym environment benefits both sides: starters get pushed, non-starters see their contribution is real.
- Non-starter engagement is a managing-a-program responsibility, not an afterthought.

- Non-starter motivation
- Practice design with side-switching incentives
- Program management and depth
- Competitiveness as daily practice culture
