# The 'sweet spot' is the foundation of good passing

## Summary
Ben Josephson: the foundation of good passing is contacting the ball on the "sweet spot" — the fleshy part of the forearms. Target 8/10 passes on the sweet spot before shifting focus to location; once sweet-spotting is mastered, accuracy follows.

## Key claims / ideas
- Best passers "sweet spot" the ball on the fleshy part of the forearm
- 8/10 sweet-spot hit rate is the threshold where location becomes the main focus
- Once sweet-spotting is mastered, location accuracy comes naturally
- Drill: one passer, free balls to setter target, setter moves around to make passer adapt

## Topics covered
- Contact point on the platform
- Sweet-spot vs. location sequencing in teaching
- Free-ball-to-setter accuracy drill
