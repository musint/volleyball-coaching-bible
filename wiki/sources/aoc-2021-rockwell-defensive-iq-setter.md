## Summary

Four cues for reading the opposing setter on defense: (1) pass location reveals setter options (off-net = no middle; tight = quick/dump); (2) setter body language (chest to net = dump threat; backpedal = back-pin set); (3) running-to-ball setter rarely sets back-pin; (4) as a coach, vary toss locations/tempos to setters in drills.

## Key claims / ideas

- Pass location is the single biggest tell for what's coming next.
- Setter body language is teachable as a defensive cue.
- Coaches should vary toss origin + tempo — not just spot-feed — to train real defensive reads.
