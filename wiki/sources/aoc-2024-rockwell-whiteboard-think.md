## Summary

Write 4-5 play sets on a whiteboard (OH, middle, RS, back row). Each team must score on all listed sets; first to check off every set wins. Initiate each rally with a down-ball or free-ball. Setters must track what's been scored and call plays that haven't. Drill morphs into a defensive drill late as teams defend the remaining uncompleted sets.

## Key claims / ideas

- Constraining which sets must score forces setter IQ and team communication.
- Late-drill phase becomes defensive IQ: opposition anticipates remaining sets.
- Rockwell: "I love this drill because it keeps players thinking."

## Topics covered

- Setter decision-making
- Team-level communication
- Defensive anticipation via constraint
