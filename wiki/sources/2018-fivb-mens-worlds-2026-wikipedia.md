# 2018 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia tournament reference for the 19th FIVB Men's Volleyball World Championship — September 9–30, 2018, jointly hosted by Italy and Bulgaria. **Poland defeated Brazil 3–0 (28–26, 25–20, 25–23) in the final** under head coach Vital Heynen, winning their third World Championship overall. Bartosz Kurek won tournament MVP with 171 total points and best blocker. The final was the third Brazil-Poland Worlds final in the previous four editions.

## Key claims / ideas

- Tournament dates: September 9–30, 2018; co-hosts Italy + Bulgaria (first multi-nation Worlds host).
- Format: 24 teams, three-round pool system + semifinals/final.
- Poland's path: third seed; semifinal 3–2 over USA (25–22, 20–25, 23–25, 25–20, 15–11); final 3–0 over Brazil (28–26, 25–20, 25–23).
- **Final result:** Poland def. Brazil 3–0; **Poland's third World Championship title overall**.
- Medal podium: Gold Poland (HC Vital Heynen); Silver Brazil; Bronze USA (def. Serbia 3–1); 4th Serbia.
- Tournament MVP: **Bartosz Kurek** (Poland) — also leading scorer (171 points) and best blocker.
- Notable record-set: Russia's 25–6 set vs. Tunisia became the all-time lowest set-point record in a Worlds match under modern rally scoring.
