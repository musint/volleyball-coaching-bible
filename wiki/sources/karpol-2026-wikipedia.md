# Nikolay Karpol (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article on **Nikolay Vasiliyevich Karpol** (b. 1 May 1938, Bereznica), longtime head coach of VC Uralochka-NTMK (Yekaterinburg / Sverdlovsk) and of the USSR / Russia women's national volleyball team. Establishes the longest continuous single-club head-coaching tenure documented in professional team sport — **51 consecutive years** at Uralochka from 1969 through at least 2020 — concurrent with Soviet / Unified Team / Russian national-team leadership across **five Olympic Games** (1980, 1988, 1992, 2000, 2004). Five Olympic medals total (two gold — 1980 Moscow and 1988 Seoul — three silver); nine European Championship golds across the Soviet-to-Russian transition; eight CEV Women's Champions League titles with Uralochka. International Volleyball Hall of Fame inductee (2009).

## Key claims / ideas

- **Tenure 1969–at least 2020** at VC Uralochka-NTMK — world-record single-club head-coaching continuity (51+ years).
- **Concurrent USSR / Russia women's NT head coach** across five Olympics: 1980 Moscow (gold), 1988 Seoul (gold), 1992 Barcelona (silver, Unified Team), 2000 Sydney (silver), 2004 Athens (silver).
- **1990 FIVB World Championship gold** (USSR); bronze at 1994, 1998, 2002 (Russia).
- **Nine European Championship gold medals:** 1977, 1979, 1985, 1989, 1991 (USSR); 1993, 1997, 1999, 2001 (Russia).
- **Eight CEV Women's Champions League titles with Uralochka:** 1981, 1982, 1983, 1987, 1989, 1990, 1994, 1995.
- **FIVB Grand Prix gold:** 1997, 1999, 2000, 2002.
- **Nickname "The Howling Bear"** — well-documented public sideline persona of intense, audible coaching.
- **International Volleyball Hall of Fame induction 2009.**
