# Set the middle drill to encourage high-percentage attacks

## Summary
Tod Mattox (The Bishop's School) pitches the middle attack as underused in juniors. He uses a "Push" (hybrid 1/2, second tempo, antenna high) set to one of three locations — center, 4ft right, 4ft left — and reinforces with a 6v6 drill that awards 3 points per middle kill vs 1 for everything else.

## Key claims / ideas
- Left- and right-side sets are predictable for blockers and back row; middles force decisions.
- "Push" set = hybrid between 1 and 2: second tempo, antenna high.
- Push timing: set when right-handed middle is coming off the left step, just before step-close.
- Three push locations: center of net, 4 ft right, 4 ft left.
- Teams rarely practice blocking each of those zones → middle hitting percentages are much higher than pins.
- Drill: 6v6, middle kill = 3 points, other kills = 1 point, play to 10 (adjustable).
- Blockers dislike leaning off the pins → if middle is set, opposing blockers either help (pin opens) or don't (middle wins) — either way back row is in a bind, which opens tips.

- Push set mechanics (tempo, location, timing)
- Scoring-incentive drill design for offensive priorities
- Strategic effect on opposing block and back row
