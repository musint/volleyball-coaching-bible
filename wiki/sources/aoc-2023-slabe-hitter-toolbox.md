# What hitters can do with bad sets: Using their toolbox

## Summary
Luka Slabe (USA W-NT) walks through the hitter's OOS toolbox for imprecise sets (tight, off, too inside/outside): the tool/swipe/wipe off the blocker's hands, the high-hands deep ball, and the non-negotiable mechanics cue — keep elbow and hand high or get stuff-blocked.

## Key claims / ideas
- On imprecise sets — tight, far, or pin-miss — the tool is a primary option.
- Tool = wrist angle off blocker's hands; also called swipe or wipe.
- High-hands alternative: deflect high off hands so the ball lands past diggers.
- All these OOS shots require high elbow and hand; dropping the elbow invites a stuff block.

- OOS hitting decisions
- Tool / swipe / wipe technique
- High-hands deep-ball shot
- Elbow-height mechanics under pressure
