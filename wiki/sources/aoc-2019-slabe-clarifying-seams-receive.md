# Clarifying the seams on serve receive

## Summary
Luka Slabe's seam drill isolates serves down the middle to force passer communication and hand-off conventions. A common rule: left-deep and right-short belong to the left passer because players generally pass better to their left.

## Key claims / ideas
- The seam ball (between two passers) is the highest-ambiguity pass and needs a rehearsed call system.
- Teams should share a categorization rule for left/right/deep/short seam balls.
- Heuristic: "left deep and right short" goes to the left-side passer because passers are generally stronger to their left.
- 6+ players required: two passers, servers, and a target on the other side.

- Seam-ball ownership rules
