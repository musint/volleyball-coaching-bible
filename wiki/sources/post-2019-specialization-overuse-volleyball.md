# Sport Specialization and Overuse Injury in Youth Volleyball (Post et al. 2019)

## Summary

Post and colleagues (2019, *Sports Health*) addressed a critical question for youth sport: does sport specialization carry the same injury risk across sports, or are some sport-sex combinations especially dangerous? Their cross-sectional study of 716 youth athletes (ages 12–18) from basketball, soccer, and volleyball produced a striking sport-specific result: specialization was significantly associated with overuse injury *only in volleyball*, not basketball or soccer. The volleyball-specific odds ratios are large enough to drive coaching policy: high specialization yielded an OR of 2.3 for overuse injury (p=0.02), and year-round (>8 months) volleyball participation yielded an OR of 2.0 (p<0.05). Nearly half (45.7%) of volleyball athletes reported prior overuse injuries — the highest among the three sports. Volleyball also had the highest specialization rates of the three sampled sports, compounding the harm. For 14U club, HS, and college coaches the implication is direct: multi-sport encouragement is not just developmental philosophy, it is an evidence-backed injury-prevention lever specifically for volleyball-playing adolescents. Year-round single-sport participation roughly doubles overuse injury risk in this population. For the wiki, Post 2019 anchors any claim about youth specialization, multi-sport benefit, and the volleyball-specific dose-response between annual sport months and overuse injury.

## Key claims / ideas

- Specialization is associated with overuse injury *specifically in volleyball*, not in basketball or soccer.
- High specialization in volleyball: OR 2.3 for overuse injury.
- Year-round (>8 months/year) volleyball: OR 2.0 for overuse injury.
- 45.7% of youth volleyball athletes report prior overuse injury — highest of the three sports studied.
- Volleyball had the highest specialization rates among the three sports — compounding injury risk.
- Multi-sport encouragement is evidence-backed injury prevention for youth volleyball, not just a philosophical preference.
