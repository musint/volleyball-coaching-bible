# China Women's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia results-and-history reference for the China women's national volleyball team — the program that anchors the [[chinese-volleyball-tradition]] and is one of only three women's national teams (with the USA and USSR) to hold three Olympic gold medals. The article documents 10 major international titles: 3 Olympic golds (1984 Los Angeles under Yuan Weimin's player-development pipeline, 2004 Athens under [[chen-zhonghe]], 2016 Rio under [[lang-ping]]), 2 FIVB World Championship golds (1982, 1986), and 5 FIVB World Cups (1981, 1985, 2003, 2015, 2019). The 1980s "Five Consecutive Championships" generation (1981 World Cup → 1982 Worlds → 1984 Olympics → 1985 World Cup → 1986 Worlds) made volleyball a national sport in China and produced the dynasty Lang Ping later inherited as head coach. As of June 2025 the team sits FIVB-ranked #6 with Zhao Yong (2024-present) as head coach following Lang Ping's retirement after Tokyo 2020.

## Key claims / ideas

- China women hold 3 Olympic golds (1984 Los Angeles, 2004 Athens, 2016 Rio), 1 silver (1996 Atlanta), and 2 bronzes (1988 Seoul, 2008 Beijing) across 11 Olympic appearances 1984-2024.
- Two FIVB World Championship golds: 1982 (Peru, 8-2 record, 24-6 sets) and 1986 (Czechoslovakia, perfect 10-0, 30-3 sets) under the 1980s dynasty.
- Five FIVB World Cup titles: 1981 (perfect 7-0), 1985 (perfect 7-0, 21-1 sets), 2003 (perfect 11-0 under Chen Zhonghe), 2015 (10-1 under Lang Ping), 2019 (perfect 11-0 under Lang Ping).
- The 2003 World Cup → 2004 Athens Olympic gold pair establishes Chen Zhonghe's program-building run; the 2015 World Cup → 2016 Rio Olympic gold pair establishes Lang Ping's second head-coach tenure (2013-2021) as the modern dynasty re-establishment.
- 12 Asian Championship golds and 10 Asian Games golds anchor regional dominance.
- Three Nations League medals: 2018 bronze, 2019 bronze, 2023 silver.
- 2020 Tokyo Olympics produced the program's worst Olympic result on record (9th, eliminated in pool play, 2-3) under Lang Ping in her final tournament — a contradiction worth noting against the dynasty narrative.
- 2022 Worlds 6th-place result and 2024 Paris 5th-place finish reflect the post-Lang-Ping rebuild under successor Cai Bin and current HC Zhao Yong.
- Wikipedia frames the program as "one of the leading and most successful squads in women's international volleyball" and emphasizes it as a culturally significant national sport in China; CCTV "Best Team of the Year" recognition in 2011, 2014, 2015, 2016 confirms the public-prestige dimension.
