# Yuichi Nakagaichi — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
Reference-level biography of Yuichi Nakagaichi (born 1967, Fukui). Establishes two distinct careers: outside hitter on the Japan men's national team **1989–1998** (Barcelona 1992, 6th; 1994 Asiad gold; 1998 Worlds 16th), then head coach of the Japan men's national team **2017–2021** (2017 Asian champion; 2019 World Cup 4th; Tokyo 2020 Olympics 7th).

**Important correction to this cluster's research brief:** the brief described Nakagaichi as "Japan men's '72 Olympic gold architect." That is incorrect — Nakagaichi was born in 1967, five years before the Munich 1972 gold. The Japanese men's 1972 head coach is not identified in the sources captured for this cluster and remains [unverified]; a dedicated source should be acquired before any wiki page asserts that attribution.

## Key claims / ideas
- Nakagaichi's coaching tenure (2017–2021) was the first Japan men's Olympic return in 12 years (Tokyo 2020)
- Stated ambition: end Japan's Olympic men's medal drought dating to Munich 1972
- Playing-era pedigree (1990s NT) shapes his authority as coach
