# Playing defense against a 3-hitter rotation

## Summary
Mike Sealy explains that against a 3-hitter rotation, middles usually can't close shoulder-to-shoulder with the pin blocker because offenses are too fast; teams should choose between reaching over to seal the seam or leaving a deliberate seam covered by a back-row digger, and should cheat toward the stronger pin hitter.

## Key claims / ideas
- Middles rarely close shoulder-to-shoulder against fast 3-hitter offenses.
- Reaching over can close the low seam.
- Alternatively, leave the seam open and defend with a well-positioned back-row digger.
- Stable middle block (that doesn't chase wide to close) makes defense easier than an unstable, closer block.
- Cheat toward the stronger pin hitter — most teams have an imbalanced pair.

## Topics covered
- Defending 3-hitter / fast offenses.
- Block closing decisions (reach-over vs. stable).
- Back-row positioning behind an intentional seam.
- Scouting-based block cheat.
