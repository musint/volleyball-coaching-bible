# Giovanni Guidetti — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Biographical and career reference for Giovanni Guidetti (b. 1972, Modena), the Italian coach who has anchored VakıfBank Istanbul since 2008 and compiled the longest tenured women's head-coaching career in modern European club volleyball. Tracks his Italian club origins (Volley 2000 Spezzano through Chieri), his Italy national-team vice-coach apprenticeship under the Italian-school senior staff (1997–2000, 1998 World Championship and 2000 Sydney Olympics), and his unusual serial-national-team career: Bulgaria (2003–04), Germany (2006–14), Netherlands (2015–16), Turkey (2017–22), Serbia (2023–24), Canada (2025–present). At VakıfBank he has won the CEV Champions League in 2010–11, 2012–13, 2016–17, 2017–18, and 2021–22, plus FIVB Club World Championships in 2013, 2017, 2018, and 2021 — widely cited as the defining European women's club dynasty of the 2010s–2020s.

## Key claims / ideas

- VakıfBank 2012–13 season: 47–0 record across Turkish League, Turkish Cup, and CEV Champions League — one of the most cited unbeaten seasons in women's club volleyball.
- VakıfBank 2010–11 Champions League run: 12–0 undefeated, SF vs. Fenerbahçe Acıbadem 3–2, final vs. Rabita Baku 3–0.
- Netherlands with Guidetti reached European silver (2015) and finished 4th at Rio 2016 — peak modern result for the Dutch women's program.
- Turkey with Guidetti (2017–22) compiled three European Championship medals (2017 bronze, 2019 silver, 2021 bronze) plus 2018 Nations League silver and 2021 Nations League bronze.
- Guidetti coached star cross-federation rosters at VakıfBank — Tijana Bošković, Zehra Güneş, Zhu Ting, Lonneke Slöetjes, Gabriela Guimarães, Isabelle Haak, Kelsey Robinson — and is therefore a rare Italian-school coach with extended, direct coaching exposure to Serbian, Turkish, Chinese, Dutch, Brazilian, Swedish, and American elite players inside a single club program.
- His career spans six national-team federations, making him one of the most-traveled head coaches in the modern women's game; the serial-national-team pattern itself is a data point about how VakıfBank is structured relative to federation cycles.
