## Summary

This is AOC curricular content; defer to qualified AT/CSCS/MD on programming specifics. Abe Avender's rule: never stretch a tissue before performance — it both raises injury risk and reduces output. Replace pre-performance static stretching with blood-flow movement (e.g., walking from endline to net while moving arms). Post-practice/match is the right window for gentle stretching. Adjust warmup volume based on recent activity: bench players need more warmup on back-to-back matches than starters who just played.

## Key claims / ideas

- Pre-performance static stretching increases injury susceptibility and decreases performance.
- Dynamic blood-flow work (light movement with arm motion) is the correct pre-performance pattern.
- Post-performance is the correct window for static stretching and yoga-style movement.
- Warmup dosing should vary by recent activity — bench players on match day need more warmup than starters who just came off.
- Starters in a 2nd/3rd match of the day need less, not more, warmup.
