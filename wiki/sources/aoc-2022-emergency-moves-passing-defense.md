# Emergency moves for passing and defense

## Summary
Jen Flynn Oldenburg (Ohio State) teaches "center-based" emergency defensive and passing moves — the Double Knee Down and Shin Dig — used when a ball is too close for a traditional floor move but too aggressive to play on the feet.

## Key claims / ideas
- Emergency moves work from "GO posture," face the action, and get belly button (center) to and under the ball; "attack the attacker."
- Double Knee Down: controlled move to block the ball with body when a hard-driven ball lands in front of defender below the waist.
- Shin Dig: one shin slides, other knee touches down, finishes in Double Knee Down — used for balls aggressively attacked left or right but not far enough for a traditional floor move; analogy is a catcher blocking a wild pitch, but the defender attacks the ball.
- Passing's One Knee Down creates a solid foundation for getting center under a low ball.
- 20 & 10 Concept: when moving forward to a low serve, allow the "perfect" pass to be more like a dig than a 3-pass to minimize overpasses.

## Topics covered
- Center-based emergency defensive footwork
- Double Knee Down and Shin Dig mechanics
- One Knee Down passing technique
- Managing forward momentum on serve receive (20 & 10 concept)
- Libero / back-row defensive decision making
