# Cassidy Lichtman — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

The Wikipedia biography of Cassidy Lichtman documents the Stanford-trained outside hitter who became the architectural athlete-leader of Athletes Unlimited Pro Volleyball [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. Born May 25, 1989 in Poway, California, Lichtman played at Stanford 2007–2011, earning first-team AVCA All-American honors in her junior and senior seasons and four Pac-10 championships while completing dual Stanford degrees (B.A. political science, M.A. history) in four years [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. She represented USA Women's NT 2011–2016 across the 2014 FIVB World Grand Prix, the 2011 and 2015 Pan American Games, and multiple Pan American Cups [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. Five years of overseas pro volleyball followed (Poland, Switzerland, Azerbaijan, France, China; final stop Sichuan 2015) [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. From 2021 she was a founding athlete of Athletes Unlimited Pro Volleyball, finishing fourth in Season 2 (2022); she became the **first athlete to sit on the AU Board of Directors** and was promoted to AU Director of Volleyball in 2022 — the same year AdWeek named her among its Most Powerful Women in Sports [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. In 2026 she was elected Chair of the USA Volleyball Board [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. She co-founded the nonprofit P/ATH in 2020 [lichtman-wikipedia-bio].
