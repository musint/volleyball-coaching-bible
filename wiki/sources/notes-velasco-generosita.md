# La generosità è la base della vita — Julio Velasco

## Summary

*La generosità è la base della vita* ("Generosity is the foundation of
life") is Julio Velasco's first-person, book-length articulation of the
philosophical framework underlying his 35-year coaching career. The
book is published in Italian (no English translation as of 2026-04-24)
and was released in the wake of Velasco's return to the Italy women's
national team head-coach role (2024 Olympic gold, 2025 FIVB Women's
World Championship gold — making him the first coach ever to win both
the men's and women's World title).

For the wiki, the book is the primary-source Velasco voice that
underpins the [[italian-school]] and [[julio-velasco]] coach pages.
The wiki's existing Velasco sourcing leans on Mark Lebedew's English-
language retrospectives and the MOK Umag "10 cult phrases" interview
piece — useful but secondary capture (coach commentary on Velasco's
commentary). This book is analogous in role to what Bernardinho's
*Transformando Suor em Ouro* (2006) is for the [[brazilian-school]] page.

This citation key also covers Velasco's earlier "clinic books" — the
cluster of typeset transcripts of Italian coaching seminars and
federation-distributed methodology handbooks produced over the
1990s–2010s. Those materials remain Italian-language only.

## Key claims / ideas

Drawn from previously-ingested Velasco material (Lebedew 2015, Lebedew
2020, MOK Umag 2020) plus Italian-press coverage around the book's
release. Italian primary-text claims tagged `[translation-needed]` until
a working pass exists.

- **Generosity as foundational coaching value.** The title is the
  thesis: generous behavior toward teammates, opponents, officials, and
  the game itself is what makes high-performance team culture
  durable. [translation-needed]
- **The Theory of Alibis.** Champions refuse to attribute failure to
  external causes. The book is expected to develop this signature
  Velasco teaching in book-length form with Italian and Argentine
  examples. (Already cited on [[julio-velasco]] and [[italian-school]]
  via lebedew-2015-velasco-thinker-of-game and
  mokumag-2020-velasco-10-cult-phrases.)
- **Leader-as-teacher framing.** "A leader is more a teacher than a
  leader." Velasco's philosophy degree from National University of La
  Plata is the explicit grounding for his rejection of authoritarian
  command in favor of pedagogical leadership.
- **Method as anti-conflict device.** "The more established the method
  the lower the chance of conflict."
- **Defense as cultural thermometer.** "Defence is the thermometer of
  the soul of a team."
- **Internal accountability — "spikers don't talk about the set."**
  Each position solves its own problem rather than litigating upstream
  teammate contribution.
- **"Champions seek solutions; losers seek alibis."**
- **The "square" — team unity over specialization.** The 1980s
  specialization trend Velasco actively pushed back on; book-length
  philosophical grounding rather than the tactical claim already
  documented on [[italian-school]]. [translation-needed]
- **Generazione di Fenomeni as case study.** Italy men's 1989-1996 run,
  expected to detail the brotherhood framing alluded to in
  [mokumag-2020-velasco-10-cult-phrases]. [translation-needed]
- **Crossover to non-volleyball leadership.** Pep Guardiola's
  documented multi-hour drive to meet Velasco after seeing him on TV
  is the canonical signal of Velasco's transferability outside
  volleyball.

- Crossover applicability of team-leadership principles outside
