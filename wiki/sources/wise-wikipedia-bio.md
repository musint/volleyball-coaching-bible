# Mary Wise — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
The Wikipedia biography of Mary Wise compiles her coaching résumé at Iowa State (1981–1984, first head-coaching job, youngest D-I head coach in history at 21), Kentucky (1986–1990, graduate assistant to associate head coach), and Florida (1991–2024, 34 seasons, 987–150, .868). It records her career totals — 1,068 wins, described on the page as the most by any female D-I women's volleyball head coach — and enumerates her AVCA and SEC awards. The page serves as a secondary-source anchor for Wise's award record where primary AVCA pages don't surface individual coach history.

## Key claims / ideas
- Three AVCA National Coach of the Year awards: **1992, 1996, 2017**.
- **AVCA All-Time Great Coach (2006)** — distinct from a formal Hall of Fame induction, which is not listed.
- **13 SEC Coach of the Year awards** (1991–1993, 1995–1996, 1998–2002, 2007, 2014, 2017).
- Eight NCAA Final Four appearances across 1991–2024: **1992, 1993, 1996, 1997, 1998, 2002, 2003, 2017**. The gap between the 2003 and 2017 national championship finals is 14 years — the page lists both appearances without framing the gap as notable.
- Florida record 987–150 (.868); overall career 1,068–213; 19 SEC regular-season titles, 12 SEC tournament titles per the Wikipedia page (the Character-and-Leadership profile reports 25 SEC regular-season titles — the discrepancy is unresolved in the reviewed material and likely reflects different definitions of "regular-season title" vs. "shared regular-season titles").
- 2003 Florida won 105 consecutive matches.
- Player background: Purdue setter 1977–1980; two-time All-Midwest Regional; Big Ten Conference titles 1979, 1980.
