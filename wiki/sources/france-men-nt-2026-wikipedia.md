# France Men's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia reference for the France men's national volleyball team, tracing the program from its mid-20th-century founding through the modern Tillie- and Giani-led golden era. Establishes France as a current top-tier men's program: two consecutive Olympic golds (Tokyo 2020 and Paris 2024) plus two Volleyball Nations League titles (2022, 2024) within a four-year window.

## Key claims / ideas

- France won gold at Tokyo 2020, defeating the Russian Olympic Committee in the final — the program's first Olympic men's volleyball title.
- France retained the Olympic title at Paris 2024, defeating Poland 3-0 in the final on home soil.
- World Championship best result is bronze in 2002 (Argentina).
- 2014 World Championship: 4th place (semifinal loss).
- 2015 European Championship: gold.
- FIVB World League titles in 2015 and 2017.
- FIVB Volleyball Nations League gold in 2022 and 2024.
- FIVB world ranking: 3rd (1 January 2023 reference).
- Coaches: Philippe Blain (2001-2012); **Laurent Tillie (2012-2021)**; Bernardinho (2021-2022, one-year stint); **Andrea Giani (2022-present)**.
- Captain: Benjamin Toniutti (setter).
- Earvin N'Gapeth: outside hitter, MVP of Tokyo 2020.
- Kévin Tillie (son of Laurent Tillie) is also on the national team.
