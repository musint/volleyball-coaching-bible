## Summary

Salima Rockwell's (Notre Dame) whiteboard drill. The coach writes 4-5 specific play sets on a whiteboard (outside, middle, right-side, back-row). Each team must score on every listed set. First team to check off all sets wins. Rallies start with a down ball or free ball. The setters must track what's already been scored to call sets that haven't. As the drill evolves, it becomes a defensive drill — because teams can predict which sets the opponent still needs.

## Key claims / ideas

- Explicit checklist of required sets forces setter decision-making beyond "best hitter, best set."
- Late in the drill it flips into defensive IQ training — defenders and blockers read which sets the opponent still needs to check off.
- The whiteboard is a lightweight cognitive-constraint tool that changes offensive variety without changing scoring rules.
- The drill rewards setter creativity, blocker anticipation, and team-level play-calling simultaneously.
- Works as a 6v6 team drill with down-ball or free-ball entry.
