## Summary

Mark Rosen (Michigan '99-'22) argues that transition training must deliberately mix in-system and out-of-system reps so players handle either scenario competently in games. Filmed at 2016 MIVCA Clinic.

## Key claims / ideas

- Training only in-system transition produces players who freeze when a pass goes off the net.
- Training only out-of-system transition produces players who miss normal-rhythm attacks.
- Intentional mix of both is the baseline; the mix ratio can be tuned by opponent scouting.

## Topics covered

In-system vs out-of-system transition training; mixed-scenario practice design; offensive transition variety.
