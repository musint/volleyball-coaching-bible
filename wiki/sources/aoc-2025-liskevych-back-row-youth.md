# Training back-row attack with younger players

## Summary
Former USA women's national team coach Terry Liskevych advocates teaching back-row attacks to youth players as a way to ingrain proper contact on the back of the ball rather than the top.

## Key claims / ideas
- Back-row attacking reinforces the correct contact point — striking the back of the ball, not the top.
- "If you hit the top of the ball, it goes straight down" — only appropriate when the hitter is well above the net, which young players rarely are.
- Progression: start with no jump (rhythm and contact), then approach-jump-hit off a coach toss.
- At early ages, first goal is simply landing the ball in the court; pace and placement come later.
- Positions back-row attack as a developmental fundamental, not an advanced add-on.

## Topics covered
- Back-row attack as a youth technique teaching tool
- Contact-point geometry for non-elevated hitters
- Jump vs. no-jump arm-swing progression
- Scaling expectations by developmental stage
