## Summary

Christy Johnson-Lynch (Iowa State) presents her go-to transition drills: hitter-vs-hitter (block-to-attack transition), in-a-row (consecutive kill attempts), coach-on-six (transition from specific defenses), and focused 6-on-6 variants.

## Key claims / ideas

- Competitive format is core to transition training — Johnson-Lynch explicitly loves competition in transition drills.
- Hitter-vs-hitter trains the block-to-attack transition chain specifically.
- In-a-row drills train consecutive-kill habit under fatigue.
- Coach-on-six drills isolate defensive-to-offensive transition out of a specific defense.
- 6-on-6 transition drills pinpoint a single element (setting a specific player, attacking after setter dig, middle-behind-setter) rather than training transition generically.

## Topics covered

Transition drill taxonomy; competitive 6-on-6 design; block-to-attack chain; middle transition behind setter.
