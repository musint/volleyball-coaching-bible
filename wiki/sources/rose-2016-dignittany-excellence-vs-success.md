# A Conversation with Russ Rose — Part 1

## Summary
A June 2016 long-form interview with Penn State's Russ Rose built around three books he recommends to coaches: *When Pride Still Mattered* (Maraniss on Lombardi), *Wooden on Leadership* (Wooden/Jamison), and *Geno: In Pursuit of Perfection* (Auriemma/MacMullan). Part 1 tackles the distinction between excellence (a process/developmental goal) and success (a perception players hold). Rose notes that coaches can often capture a team's attention more effectively after losses than wins — which reveals how success and excellence can diverge.

The conversation connects directly to Angela Duckworth's finding that "effort counts twice": effort builds skill, and skill multiplied by effort produces achievement. Rose uses this to argue that will alone can win a match but only skill-plus-effort sustains a season.

## Key claims / ideas
- Excellence vs. success: excellence is the process of elevating performance; success is how players feel about outcomes.
- Lombardi actually demanded excellence — correcting mistakes even after wins.
- Wooden: success is "peace of mind" from maximizing potential.
- Will wins matches; skill + effort wins seasons.
- Expectations matter — different seasons have different definitions of success.
