# Karch Kiraly on training reading as part of every skill

## Summary
Kiraly frames reading as a skill embedded in every volleyball action and offers a three-question framework — What do I see? What do I know? How do I respond? — using the B-S-B-H (ball-setter-ball-hitter) eye-work sequence for defense and blocking.

## Key claims / ideas
- Reading is not separate from skill; it is part of every skill.
- B-S-B-H sequence (ball → setter → ball → hitter) trains the eyes to the right cues.
- "Eyes on the right things longer" is a trainable attention habit.
- Knowledge layer matters: opponent distribution tendencies, attacker tendencies, body behavior.
- Response layer (react/move) comes after seeing and knowing; coaches should ask questions to develop it.

## Topics covered
- Reading and perceptual-cognitive training
- Defensive eye-work (B-S-B-H)
- Blocking read cues
- Questioning as a coaching method
