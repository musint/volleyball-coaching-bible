# Hitting drill: Train players to take more aggressive swings

## Summary
Emporia State's Ken Murczek runs an end-of-practice drill (used daily for three weeks) that rewards hard swings that land, penalizes net errors, and explicitly forbids the net as an acceptable error direction.

## Key claims / ideas
- Philosophy: "Hit it as hard as they can all the time. Once you hit it hard, then you can start playing soft."
- Velocity does two things: (1) shrinks defense reaction time, (2) makes off-speed changes more effective.
- Drill progression: hitting lines → transition (block-pull-off-attack) → position-specific sets (Slide R, 2 M, Go L).
- Rotation cycle (1→4→2→5→3→6) spreads jump load.
- Scoring: +1 hard kill in, 0 for out/tape, **-1 for net**. Net errors are treated as a significant problem.
- Block/defense overlay only for higher-level teams.

## Topics covered
- Velocity-first hitting philosophy
- Heat-before-finesse sequencing
- Net-error penalty scoring
- Jump-load management across rotations
