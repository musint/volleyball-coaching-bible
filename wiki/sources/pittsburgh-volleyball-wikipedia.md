# Pittsburgh Panthers women's volleyball — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia's program-history page for Pittsburgh Panthers women's volleyball. Used to corroborate the head-coach lineage from Mary Kromer through Dan Fisher, the dramatic Fisher-era ascent (350–73 in 13 seasons; five consecutive Final Fours 2021–2025), the seven ACC regular-season titles, Olivia Babcock's back-to-back AVCA National Player of the Year honors (2024, 2025), Fisher's NAIA national title at Concordia Irvine in 2012 prior to his Pitt hire, and the program's home-venue history (Fitzgerald Field House → planned Victory Heights Arena 2026).

## Key claims / ideas
- Dan Fisher hired 2013 concurrent with Pitt's move from Big East to ACC. At Concordia Irvine 2011–2012, Fisher went 75–2 with the 2012 NAIA national championship.
- Five consecutive Final Fours (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025) — none yet have produced an NCAA championship-match appearance.
- Seven ACC regular-season titles (2017 tie; 2018; 2019; 2022 tie; 2023 tie; 2024; 2025 tie).
- 2024 record: 33–2; ranked No. 1 in the AVCA poll for 12 consecutive weeks; lost to Louisville 1–3 in the NCAA semifinal.
- 2019: 18–0 ACC conference season.
- Olivia Babcock: 2023 AVCA National Freshman of the Year; 2024 and 2025 AVCA National Player of the Year (one of only five NCAA players ever to win the award twice).
- Dan Fisher: 2024 AVCA Division I National Coach of the Year.
- Mike Hebert was Pitt's HC 1976–1979 — 13th place nationally in 1978 (EAIAW Championship). Hebert later became one of the GMS-affiliated college coaching figures and authored *Thinking Volleyball*.
- Home venue: Fitzgerald Field House (capacity 2,864) since 1982; auxiliary use of Petersen Events Center (12,508) for NCAA tournament rounds; planned move to Victory Heights Arena in 2026.
