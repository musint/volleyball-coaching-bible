# Growing Kids' Volleyball

## Summary

This USA Volleyball resource collects ~40 years of 12-and-under coaching experience into concrete practice formats designed to replace the "one kid touches the ball while 23 watch" pattern that dominates line-based drill structures. The central move is to run the entire practice in pairs over a net, rope, or ribbon, with receive/set/attack sequences, cooperative two-vs-zero net-crossing games, and modified net games like "Loser Becomes the Net" that work with anywhere from 3 to 12 players.

For a youth-focused coach, this page operationalizes the age-appropriate and small-sided-play philosophies USAV advocates elsewhere. It offers tactical answers — four-nets-on-a-rope setups for 8–16 doubles teams on standard courts, sitting volleyball when space is limited, older-kids-teaching-younger-kids peer coaching, coed play to accelerate development, field trips to high-school or college matches — that let a single coach keep a large group continuously engaged. The page is undated in its current form; the 2026 year reflects the fetch date rather than an original publication date.

## Key claims / ideas
- Pair-based practice over a net keeps every athlete active, solving the attention/engagement problem that standing-line drills create.
- Two vs. zero cooperative net-crossing games build genuine ball control before competition is introduced.
- "Loser Becomes the Net" scales from 3 to 12+ players and teaches net awareness without needing an actual net.
- Four-nets-on-a-rope setups allow 8–16 doubles teams to play round-robin simultaneously on one standard court.
- Two-contact games introduce chaos that increases the number of reading opportunities per minute.
- Sitting volleyball is a legitimate fallback when gym space is constrained.
- Peer coaching (11–12-year-olds teaching 7–10-year-olds) accelerates learning for both groups.
- Coed play accelerates skill development via mixed-experience competition.
- Field trips to higher-level matches inspire continued participation — a direct retention lever.
