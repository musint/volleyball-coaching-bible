# Training the middle hitter for serve receive

## Summary
Jim Stone argues that middles who can absorb short serves expand the offense — a middle on short serve frees the slide and makes the pipe a live attack option rather than a pass-bailout.

## Key claims / ideas
- Training middles to avoid all serve receive is a common but limiting default.
- A middle hitter who handles a short serve is "dynamite" — it keeps the middle attack and pipe live.
- Stone's coverage assignment: middle covers short serve in her starting spot plus ~5 feet to her right.
- Freeing the middle from long-serve receive preserves the slide attack.

## Topics covered
- Middle blocker serve-receive responsibilities
- Slide attack preservation
- Pipe as an in-system option
