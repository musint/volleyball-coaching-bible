## Summary

Tod Mattox (The Bishop's School) lists 20 practice-energy tactics in four categories: cagey practice planning, time manipulation, competition-as-ally, and coach-behavior management. Includes a downloadable printable.

## Key claims / ideas

- Always start practice with a competition, not a static warmup.
- Switch partners frequently to prevent social ruts.
- Never use punishment as a drill consequence — it poisons effort.
- Limit any drill to 10 minutes maximum.
- Communicate a clear drill end — by time, reps, or points.
- For every missed serve in a drill, initiate play with a thrown-in ball to preserve tempo.
- Short competitive games beat long ones for maintained intensity.
- Queen of the Court format rewards top performers and sustains motivation.
- Record individual and team scores; visibility of scores drives effort.
- Use player models (not verbal descriptions alone) to convey skill.
- Celebrate small improvements explicitly; it reinforces belonging.
