# Volleyball serve receive fundamentals: Team communication

## Summary
Joe Sagula (UNC HC 1990-2022) runs a two-passer serve-receive simulation drill that trains movement, position-switching, and — most importantly — seam communication. A rule-of-thumb for seam assignments is explicit: short seam goes right, deep seam goes left.

## Key claims / ideas
- Drill purpose: train movement and communication fundamentals for an SR unit, especially in the seam.
- Drill mechanics:
  - Setup: two passers, a target, a tosser on the opposite side of the net.
  - Sequence: toss → pass → catch → repeat. After each pass, the two passers switch positions and return to ready.
  - Mirrors SR scenarios; emphasizes inter-passer communication and consistent return-to-ready habits.
- Seam-communication variation:
  - Add short and deep tosses to challenge seam decisions.
  - Sagula's seam convention: **short seam to your right, deep seam to your left**.
  - Forces verbal call-outs about who takes the seam ball.
- Drill acts as a feeder for other AOC SR content: footwork patterns, passing angles, competitive passing drills (cross-linked in original).

## Topics covered
- Two-passer SR simulation drill
- Seam responsibility conventions (short right / deep left)
- Inter-passer communication habits
- Return-to-ready discipline
