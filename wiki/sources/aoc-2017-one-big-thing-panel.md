# "One Big Thing" Panel Talk With Four Renowned Coaches

## Summary

An AOC clinic panel from July 2017 moderated among four senior coaches: John Dunning (Stanford, retired), Terry Liskevych (U.S. Women's National Team, retired), Glenn Hoag (Canada Men's National Team, retired), and Laurie Eisler (University of Alberta, active). The stated through-line is "one big thing" each coach would emphasize — framed around team inspiration, program growth, and the role of social media in the modern coach's job. Intended audience per AOC's own categorization: high school, club, and youth coaches looking for cross-generational philosophy.

The full video is AOC Premium, so only the public landing-page description is captured here. The panel's value as a source is mostly archival — it documents a cross-pollination event between two national-team voices (Liskevych on the women's side, Hoag on the men's side, across borders), a founding college voice (Dunning), and a mid-career college voice (Eisler).

## Key claims / ideas

- Panel topic framing: "one big thing" — one overriding principle per coach.
- Themes explicitly listed by AOC: team inspiration, growth in volleyball, social media issues.
- Cross-federation composition: U.S. women's NT (Liskevych), Canadian men's NT (Hoag), U.S. college (Dunning), Canadian college (Eisler).
- Audience targeted at HS/club/youth coaches — implying the panel was pitched as foundational philosophy, not elite-only tactics.
