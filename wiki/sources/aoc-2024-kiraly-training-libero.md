# Training your libero with Karch Kiraly

## Summary
Kiraly and Luka Slabe walk through a set of libero/defender drills used by the USA Women's National Team, including "Blitz the Bro," "Fire Drill," "Neville's Pepper Drill," a libero-as-setter variant, and a libero–block coordination game-plan exercise.

## Key claims / ideas
- "Blitz the Bro" — 6v6 game where all serves target the libero; USA National Team runs it about once per week.
- "Fire Drill" — 6v6 where players rotate to every position each point (right-back always sets); builds empathy for every role and uses every skill at half-speed attack.
- "Neville's Pepper Drill" — all back-row attacks; point per dig, point per rally; digging over the net loses a point; not pursuing a ball loses all points; modify by funneling first attacks to Zone 5.
- Slabe: "You should always be rewarded for a good dig." Score design must honor dig quality.
- A second-contact variant has the libero set whenever the setter takes the first ball.
- Libero must coordinate with the block — trained through a game-planning exercise.

## Topics covered
- Libero-focused 6v6 drill design
- Defense and reading drill progressions
- Libero as emergency setter
- Libero–block coordination
