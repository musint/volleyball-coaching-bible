# Igor Kolaković (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article on **Igor Kolaković** (b. 4 June 1965, Titograd / now Podgorica, SR Montenegro, SFR Yugoslavia), Montenegrin setter-turned-coach whose two stints as Serbia men's national-team head coach bracket a cross-national career across Slovenia, France, Iran, and Poland. The biography establishes Kolaković as the architect of Serbia's **2011 European Championship gold (perfect 6-0)** and **2010 World Championship bronze**, an Iran-men's-NT tenure (2017-2020) that delivered a 2019 Asian Championship gold and 2017 World Grand Champions Cup bronze, and a long Budućnost Podgorica club tenure (1997-2009) preceding the international NT era.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Player career 1979-1997 as setter** — Budućnost Podgorica (multiple stints) and Partizan Beograd (1987-1991, 1993-1994); Yugoslav championships 1989-90 and 1990-91 with Partizan; CEV Challenge Cup silver 1989-90.
- **Budućnost Podgorica head coach 1997-2009** — multiple Montenegrin championships (2007, 2008).
- **Serbia men's NT head coach 2007-2015 (first stint)** — replaced Ljubomir Travica.
- **2011 European Championship gold** with Serbia (perfect 6-0 record), Austria/Czech Republic.
- **2010 World Championship bronze** with Serbia (Italy host).
- **2008-2009 World League silver medals** with Serbia.
- **European Championship bronze** with Serbia 2007 and 2013.
- **ACH Volley Slovenia 2010-2012** — Slovenian championships and cups (2011, 2012); concurrent with Serbia NT role.
- **AS Cannes 2013-2016** (French SuperLeague).
- **Iran men's NT head coach 2017-2020** — 2019 Asian Championship gold; 2017 World Grand Champions Cup bronze.
- **Warta Zawiercie Poland 2020-2022** (PlusLiga).
- **Serbia men's NT head coach 2022-2024 (second stint)** — return appointment.
- **Halkbank Ankara Turkey 2024-2025.**
- **ACH Volley return 2025-present.**
- **Cross-national-coach pattern:** worked with five different national teams (Serbia twice, Slovenia [via club], France [via club], Iran, Poland [via club], Turkey [via club]).
