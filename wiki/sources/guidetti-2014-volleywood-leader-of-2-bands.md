# Volleywood (2014) — Guidetti: The Leader of 2 Bands

## Summary

Interview with Giovanni Guidetti originally published in the Danish Volleyball Magazine (August 2014) and reprinted on Volleywood. At the time Guidetti was concurrently head coach of Germany's women's national team and of VakıfBank Istanbul. Primary value for the wiki: a direct Guidetti quote identifying **offensive speed / quickness** as the single largest change in modern volleyball — the cleanest source available for the claim that his VakıfBank system centers tempo.

## Key claims / ideas

- Direct Guidetti quote: "Modern volleyball is stronger and much quicker. Speed (quickness in offense) in modern volleyball remains as the biggest change I have ever seen. Modern volleyball is 3 times quicker than 20 years ago."
- Guidetti defines a "good coach" as a teacher, not a winner: "A good coach is the one who can teach everyday something new to all their players and make them better every day."
- Context at time of interview: simultaneous head coach of Germany women and VakıfBank — a dual role he held from 2008 through 2014 and the structural precedent for his later serial-national-team pattern (Turkey 2017–22, Serbia 2023–24, Canada 2025–).

## Topics covered

- Modern volleyball evolution — speed and power
- Offensive quickness as the dominant change in the game
- Coaching philosophy (teaching over winning)
- Dual-role management: club + national team in the same year
