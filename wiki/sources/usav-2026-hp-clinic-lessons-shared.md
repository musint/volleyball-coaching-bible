# HP Clinic Lessons Shared

## Summary

John Kessel's field notes from the USA Volleyball High Performance Coaches' Clinic held at the Olympic Training Center, featuring presentations by Hugh McCutcheon, Tom Hogan, and Peter Vint. The clinic articulated three foundational concepts that the national-team staff treats as the measurable dimensions of skill development: Repeatability (consistent execution across competition levels, not just on good days), Variance (minimizing unnecessary movement — "Simpler is better, consistency comes with such economy of movement"), and Risk Management (positional choices that enlarge the acceptable-outcome window, e.g., setting the setter's target five to six feet off the net rather than tight).

The article also captures two higher-level arguments from McCutcheon that reshape how a youth or club program should think about its player-development pipeline: coaching effectiveness rests on foundational science rather than on any one coach's style, and the development goal is "generalized specialists" — players skilled across all game areas but exceptional in one or two — not early specialization. McCutcheon's retort on gender differences in training ("Don't we live on planet Earth and the laws of physics apply?") is a widely quoted line from this clinic. ## Key claims / ideas
- Three measurable dimensions of skill development: Repeatability, Variance, Risk Management.
- Repeatability = consistent execution across varying competition levels, not just occasionally.
- Variance reduction = economy of movement; "Simpler is better, consistency comes with such economy of movement."
- Risk Management example: setter target five to six feet off the net enlarges the margin for error while keeping balls playable.
- Coaching effectiveness depends on foundational sport science, not on individual coaching style or personality.
- The development target is "generalized specialists" — broadly skilled, exceptional in one or two areas — not early specialization.
- Training principles are gender-universal: "Don't we live on planet Earth and the laws of physics apply?" (McCutcheon).
- Elite opponents excel specifically at: executing fundamentals, maintaining biomechanical efficiency, making in-system adjustments, and making tactical decisions — these become the training targets.
- Fun and positive reinforcement drive long-term retention and therefore long-term development.
- Over-the-net, game-like training is the most effective format at the HP level as well as youth.
