# The World: "Move over soccer" (2021)

## Summary

Journalistic feature published on **The World** (public-radio program
distributed by PRX / PRI) on August 6, 2021, during the Tokyo 2020
Olympics. Michael Fox reports on Brazilian volleyball's cultural position
as the country's second-most-popular sport after soccer, quoting academic
and practitioner voices on what the sport means in Brazilian society. The
article is the wiki's primary source for two otherwise hard-to-cite
Brazilian-school claims: (1) volleyball's second-sport-after-soccer
ranking (grounded in a **2015 Ministry of Sport report** the article
cites — top sport for women; second for men after soccer), and (2) the
Olympic-funding retreat under the Bolsonaro administration (abolition of
the Ministry of Sport in 2019) as a macro-level structural-risk data
point.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Volleyball is Brazil's second sport after soccer** — grounded in a
  2015 Ministry of Sport report the article cites. Volleyball is the top
  sport for women and second for men after soccer.
- **"We are considered the soccer country — but volleyball is number
  two."** — Elisa Dell'Antonio (physical education professor), quoted in
  the article.
- **"Same passion"** — Dell'Antonio frames volleyball crowds as matching
  the energy of Brazilian soccer crowds; "huge crowds pack stadiums for
  volleyball games with vibrant chants."
- **Free-to-air TV coverage** is substantial; Brazilian viewers plan
  weekly schedules around volleyball matches — supports the household-
  names claim for NT volleyballers.
- **Olympic-funding context:** Carolina Fernandes da Silva (sports
  historian) quoted: "If you want Brazil to do well at the Olympics, you
  need investment and public policies, and they are in decline right
  now." Article notes President Bolsonaro abolished the Ministry of
  Sport in 2019 — structural-risk data point for the post-Rio 2016
  rebuild editorial.
- **Olympic medal narrative:** 1984 LA silver ("The Silver Generation"),
  1992 Barcelona gold (first men's gold), nine Olympic indoor medals
  total across both programs since 1992.
