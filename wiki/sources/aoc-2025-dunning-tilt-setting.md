# Tilt setting for emergency plays

## Summary
John Dunning teaches the tilt set — an advanced emergency technique for when the setter can't square shoulders to the target in time, or when the pass is pushed too tight to the net. Keep shoulders squared to the ball, then tilt to follow through toward target without facing it. Do not arch the back.

## Key claims / ideas
- Tilt setting is for emergencies — pass too tight to net, or not enough time to square to target.
- Mechanics: run to ball, keep shoulders squared to the ball's direction, tilt shoulders to follow through toward target without fully facing target.
- Avoid arching the back.
- When pass is pushed tight to the net: turn back to the net and tilt shoulders to target, avoiding net contact.
