# Training players to read: Look, see, decide

## Summary
Ben Josephson (University of Texas associate head coach) breaks reading into three compartmentalized steps — look, see, decide — and describes how each can be taught separately to raise player IQ.

## Key claims / ideas
- "The look" is the teachable technique: where to watch — approach angle, hips, shoulder angle, hand.
- "The see" is the intangible interpretation step; coaches probe it by asking "What did you see?" and then telling players what to see.
- "The decide" is the response choice that follows, made after identifying the opponent's intent.
- Compartmentalizing the three steps lets coaches diagnose where reading breaks down.

## Topics covered
- Reading mechanics for defense and blocking
- Player IQ / game-sense development
- Coaching cues for attacker-intent cues (hips, shoulders, hand)
