# Training tips for setters: Using repeatable form

## Summary
John Dunning's system for teaching setters to set consistently centers on repeatable hand form before, during, and after contact: hands above the forehead, big, elbows out, wrists cocked; push through the ball to the target; follow through with hands facing target and arms extended.

## Key claims / ideas
- Good setters share one trait: repeatable form before and after ball contact.
- Before: hands above forehead, shaped to the ball, big hands, elbows out, wrists cocked.
- During: push from the cocked position through the ball to the target.
- After: follow through with hands facing target and arms extended.
- Consistent form drives consistent sets.
