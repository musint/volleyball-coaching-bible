# Hyung-Sil Kim — Volleybox coach profile

## Summary

Volleybox database structured biographical record for Kim Hyung-sil (김형실, born July 21, 1951), the South Korean volleyball coach who served as head coach of the Korean women's national team during the 2011-2012 cycle culminating in the 4th-place finish at the 2012 London Olympics — Korea's first Olympic semifinal appearance in 36 years (since the 1976 Montreal bronze medal). The record includes his playing position (setter), an extended assistant-coaching phase (1978/79–1985/86), and his most recent club-level tenure as head coach (2021/22–2022/23) and Sports Director (2023/24) of the AI Peppers / Pepper Savings Bank women's V-League team in Gwangju.

The Volleybox record is structurally reliable for dates and titles but does not preserve coaching-philosophy material in Kim's voice. Korean-language reporting from the 2012 London Olympic cycle and the 2021–2024 AI Peppers tenure is the path to deeper philosophy and tactical-method documentation.
