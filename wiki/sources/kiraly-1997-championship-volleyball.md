# Karch Kiraly's Championship Volleyball: Play Your Best, On and Off the Beach

## Summary
*Championship Volleyball* is Karch Kiraly's earliest full-length coaching / technique book, written as he transitioned from the 1984/88 indoor gold-medal US men's teams into his dominant beach volleyball career. Published by Simon & Schuster under the Fireside imprint (first edition July 1990; revised 1996 edition with Jon Hastings), the book is pitched as a comprehensive skills course covering both indoor and beach volleyball — captured in its subtitle, "Play Your Best, On and Off the Beach."

The book's scope is broad: fundamental techniques (passing, setting, attacking, serving, blocking, digging), team strategy and plays, training/workout routines, coaching principles, and team management. Reviewers consistently note that it addresses players across levels from recreational through advanced. It remains the only book-length technical treatise Kiraly authored during his playing career and is the earliest public document tying his name to the forearm-pass and platform mechanics he would later formalize in AOCVB video content.

For the wiki, this source is the technical foundation of the Kiraly corpus — paired with his later book *Chasing Greatness* (2023), which focuses on the mental/developmental side of his coaching system.

## Key claims / ideas
- Volleyball fundamentals — especially passing platform, footwork, and ball control — transfer between indoor and beach with only modest adjustments; mastering them for one discipline makes a player stronger in the other.
- Team play flows from skill mastery: setter-hitter timing, offensive patterns, and defensive schemes all depend on reliable first contacts.
- Training should be structured around workout routines and deliberate reps, not just scrimmage volume.
- The coach's role extends beyond X's and O's to team management and culture.
- Beach volleyball is a legitimate laboratory for indoor skill — a belief Kiraly later carried into his USA WNT system (e.g., recruiting beach-background athletes, using sand training, and insisting on all-around skill).

## Topics covered
- Forearm pass and platform technique; overhead passing
- Setter fundamentals; offensive plays (indoor and beach-adapted)
- Attacking: approach, armswing, shot selection on both surfaces
- Serving — float and jump, plus placement
- Blocking and individual defense
- Team strategies / rotations
- Workout and conditioning routines
- The coach-player relationship and team management
