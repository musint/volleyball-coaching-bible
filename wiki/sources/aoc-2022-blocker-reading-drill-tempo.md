# Blocker reading drill: Tempo, eye-progression, footwork

## Summary
Jed Stotsenberg (Wave VBC / Pacific Ridge) describes a blocker-reading drill using a three-person setting triangle on one side to feed rapid sets while trios of blockers on the other side read, block, and peel — training tempo, eye-progression, and loud communication.

## Key claims / ideas
- A setting triangle (libero → setter → left → libero) generates continuous reads without actual attacks.
- Three blockers per wave: two block, one peels as off-blocker.
- Three progressions: (1) all sets go outside left, (2) outside left or right, (3) outside left, outside right, or back to libero (simulating a middle/bic).
- Drill goals: tempo, eye-progression, watching the play.
- Blockers must yell out the read — "Outside, outside!" "Back, back!" — loudly enough for the whole gym; communication strengthens block-defense.
- Bonus: non-blocking side gets multiple setting reps.

## Topics covered
- Block reading / set-direction identification
- Blocker eye-progression and tempo
- Off-blocker peel mechanics
- Block-defense communication
- Setter reps via triangle
