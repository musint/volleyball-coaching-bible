# Yasutaka Matsudaira — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
Reference-level biography of Yasutaka Matsudaira (松平康隆, 1930–2011). Complements the Hall of Fame record [[matsudaira-1998-hall-of-fame]] with fuller career chronology: education at Keio University (law degree, 1952), professional playing career at Nippon Kokan, Soviet study trip post-retirement to learn six-a-side rules (Japan having long played the nine-player domestic game), coaching tenure as Japan men's NT head coach 1965–1972, and a decades-long post-coaching trajectory through FIVB and JVA administration culminating in JVA presidency (1989–1995) and FIVB First Vice Presidency (1994–1996).

Used here as the **secondary corroborating source** for the 1972 Munich head-coach identity and the full career-chronology. The Hall of Fame record is the Tier 1 anchor; Wikipedia adds biographical granularity the institutional record does not (Keio, Nippon Kokan, Soviet study trip, family lineage, death specifics) at the cost of trust-tier.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Japanese name**: 松平康隆
- **Japan men's NT head coach 1965–1972**
- 1968 Mexico City Olympics — silver medal
- **1972 Munich Olympics — gold medal**, "through a big turnaround in the semi-finals"
- Post-retirement Soviet study trip for six-a-side volleyball techniques
- Descendant of the Daini Matsudaira family (late Edo period feudal retainers; lords of Komatsu Castle)
- Also taught volleyball in Brazil and Argentina in administrative-era travels
- Post-1972 institutional career: JVA Executive Director (1979), Asian Volleyball Confederation President (1985–1996 per one source), JVA President (1989–1995), FIVB Executive VP (1983–1994), FIVB First VP (1994–1996)
