## Summary

Jim Stone (Ohio State '82-'07, USA Youth National Team '08-'19) walks through establishing a practice-priority hierarchy when taking on a new team, so that each practice attacks the skills that will move the team forward fastest. Part of AOC's High School Prep Series.

## Key claims / ideas

- Every new team needs a priority hierarchy before practice design begins.
- Some skills should be practiced every single day; others rotate in.
- Skill prioritization is a diagnostic task, not a one-size-fits-all checklist.

## Topics covered

Practice priority hierarchy; daily-touch skills; HS-team diagnosis.
