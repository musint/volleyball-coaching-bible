# Training outside hitters – Shot work

## Summary
Kirsten Bernthal Booth (LOVB, formerly Creighton) enumerates four outside-hitter shots and maps each to a specific defensive read: deep zones 5/6, sharp angle, seam hit, and cross-body.

## Key claims / ideas
- Deep to Zones 5 and 6: effective vs. rotation defense (corners typically uncovered).
- Sharp angle: effective vs. rotation defense; requires getting on the ball quickly to cut inside the block.
- Seam hit: use when the opposing middle is late getting to the pin — hit into the gap between the outside blocker and middle.
- Cross-body: used when line is uncovered; ~70% tempo over the block to the deep line corner.
- Each shot tied to a specific defensive cue — shot selection is reactive, not rehearsed.

## Topics covered
- Outside-hitter shot arsenal
- Reading rotation defense and exploiting deep corners
- Sharp-angle cutting technique
- Seam and cross-body shot decision rules
