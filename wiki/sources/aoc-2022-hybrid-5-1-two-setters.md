# Hybrid systems: 5-1 with two setters

## Summary
Tod Mattox presents a hybrid 5-1 that rotates two specialized setters — a tall setter for front-row blocking and a quick setter for back-row defense — with a drill for practicing the role swap.

## Key claims / ideas
- Traditional 5-1 uses one setter; using two by specialty can be an edge.
- Tall setter plays when in front row to add a bigger blocking presence.
- Quick setter plays in back row to help with back-court defense.
- Drill: tall setter starts front row, coach tosses, play live; sub quick setter into back row after a few rallies and continue.
- Requirements: 12+ players, ball cart, coach, full court.

## Topics covered
- Hybrid 5-1 offensive system
- Role-specialized setters (tall vs. quick)
- Setter blocking vs. back-row defense
- Practice drill design for system reps
