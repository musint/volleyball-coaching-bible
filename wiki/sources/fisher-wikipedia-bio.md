# Dan Fisher — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
The Wikipedia biography of Dan Fisher compiles his coaching arc from European pro stops (Belgium, Switzerland) through US-based assistant work (Pacific, USF, Hawai'i) and a 2-season NAIA head-coaching run at Concordia Irvine (75–2 overall; 2012 NAIA national champions undefeated 38–0) into his 13-season Pittsburgh head-coaching tenure (2013–present) [fisher-wikipedia-bio]. The page enumerates Pitt's transformation from a program that hadn't been to the NCAA tournament since 2004 into one with five consecutive NCAA Final Four appearances (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025), seven ACC championships (2017–19, 2022–25), and Fisher's 2024 AVCA Division I National Coach of the Year award [fisher-wikipedia-bio]. The bio also notes the historical bracket comparison: Fisher is the eighth head coach with a .800+ winning percentage in his first 10 D-I women's volleyball seasons, and the first to do so in the 21st century — joining Dave Shoji, Terry Pettit, and Russ Rose [fisher-wikipedia-bio].

## Key claims / ideas
- Fisher was born March 19, 1976, Goleta CA; BA Sociology (Pacific 1999); MA Sport Science (Pacific 2008).
- Career head-coaching record: 425–76 (.848). Concordia Irvine 75–2; Pittsburgh 350–74.
- Pittsburgh tenure (2013–present): 350–74 (.825).
- 2012 NAIA national champions at Concordia Irvine, undefeated 38–0.
- 5 consecutive NCAA Final Fours at Pittsburgh: 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025.
- 2024: 33–2 overall, 19–1 ACC; outright ACC champions; began season 15–0 (45–1 in sets); national #1 seed; lost NCAA semifinal to Louisville.
- 2024 AVCA Division I National Coach of the Year.
- 7x ACC Champions: 2017, 2018, 2019, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025.
- 5x AVCA East Region Coach of the Year (2017, 2018, 2022, 2024, 2025); 3x ACC Coach of the Year (2017, 2018, 2024).
- USA roles: Gold 2015 Pan American Games; Gold 2023 Pan American Cup U21; Bronze 2016 Pan American Cup.
- Eighth head coach with .800+ winning percentage in first 10 D-I women's volleyball seasons; first to do so in the 21st century.
