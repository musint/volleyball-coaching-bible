# Hagglund — Defensive Court Positioning: Middle vs Left Back (AOC, 2015)

## Summary

The canonical AOC reference for **cross-shading** — the within-rotation movement where a libero anchored at LB shifts toward MB based on the opposing attack's read, then returns to her base. Natalie Hagglund (former USC libero, three-time AVCA All-American, USA NT pool) instructs the framework: not "MB vs LB" as a binary base choice, but "where do you base AND when do you shade."

## Key claims / ideas

- **Default base: LB** (modern collegiate norm at women's level).
- **Cross-shade toward MB** when the opposing OH is angled cross or the set is off-the-net.
- **Return to LB** when the opposing attacker is line-pressing or setter is on the right side.
- **The shade is read-driven, not pre-set.**
- The MB-vs-LB choice is therefore not "pick one, stay there" — it's "pick a base, drill the shade."
