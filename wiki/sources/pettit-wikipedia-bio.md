# Terry Pettit — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

The Wikipedia biography of Terry Pettit compiles the 23-season Nebraska head-coaching tenure (1977–1999) of the program's second head coach and architect of the modern Nebraska Cornhuskers women's volleyball identity [pettit-wikipedia-bio]. The page documents his 694–148–12 (.820) Nebraska record, the 1995 NCAA national championship, two NCAA runner-up finishes (1986, 1989), three Final Four appearances (1990, 1996, 1998), and 21 conference championships across the Big Eight and early Big 12 eras [pettit-wikipedia-bio]. It enumerates his 2x AVCA National Coach of the Year awards (1986, 1994), 2004 USA Volleyball All-Time Great Coach Award, AVCA Hall of Fame induction, and University of Nebraska Athletic Hall of Fame induction (2020) [pettit-wikipedia-bio]. The page also notes the September 6, 2013 dedication of Terry Pettit Court at the renovated Bob Devaney Sports Center — institutional acknowledgment that the program identity predates Cook's tenure [pettit-wikipedia-bio]. Pettit is the program-foundation anchor on the Nebraska coaching lineage that runs Pettit → John Cook → Dani Busboom Kelly.

## Key claims / ideas

- Born 1946; B.S. English (Manchester University); M.F.A. creative writing (Arkansas); graduate study in theology; published poet
- Pre-Nebraska: English teacher and volleyball/tennis coach at Louisburg College, North Carolina
- Nebraska head coach 1977–1999 (23 seasons); record **694–148–12 (.820)**
- **NCAA national champion 1995** (defeated Texas in the final)
- 2x NCAA runner-up: 1986, 1989
- 3x NCAA Final Four (semifinal): 1990, 1996, 1998
- 19 consecutive NCAA tournament appearances (1982–1999)
- 21 conference championships: 18 Big Eight + 3 Big 12
- 2x AVCA National Coach of the Year: 1986, 1994
- 9x conference Coach of the Year; 6x regional Coach of the Year
- USA Volleyball All-Time Great Coach Award (2004); AVCA Hall of Fame; University of Nebraska Athletic HoF (2020)
- Coached two AVCA National Players of the Year: Karen Dahlgren (1986), Allison Weston (1995)
- Post-coaching: leadership-academy direction at Creighton, Denver, Colorado State; books *Talent and the Secret Life of Teams* (2008), *A Fresh Season* (2013)
- Terry Pettit Court dedicated September 6, 2013 at the Bob Devaney Sports Center
