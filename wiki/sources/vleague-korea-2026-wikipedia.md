# V-League (South Korea) — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia institutional record for South Korea's V-League — the country's top professional men's and women's volleyball competition, founded 2005 and governed by KOVO (Korea Volleyball Federation, distinct from the KVA national-team body). Documents 7 men's clubs, 7 women's clubs, title-sponsor history, and notable foreign players. Anchors the player-development pipeline below the national-team level for the [[korean-school]] page.

## Key claims / ideas
- Founded **2005**; first season 2005-06
- Governed by **KOVO** (separate from KVA)
- Men's: 7 clubs (Hyundai Capital, Samsung Fire, Korean Air, Woori Card, KEPCO, KB Insurance, OK Savings)
- Women's: 7 clubs (Heungkuk Life, GS Caltex, Hyundai Hillstate, IBK, CheongKwanJang, Korea Expressway, Pepper)
- 2019-20 cancelled (COVID context)
- Foreign-player pipeline includes Cubans (Leyva, Simón) on men's side; Bethania de la Cruz, Olesia Rykhliuk on women's side
