# Russia men's national volleyball team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Reference-level biographical and results article covering the Russia men's national volleyball team, with FIVB-recognized record inheritance from the Soviet Union (1948–1991) and the Commonwealth of Independent States (1992). Establishes the program's medal census across Olympics, World Championships, World Cups, and European Championships — 41 gold / 21 silver / 24 bronze / 86 total across all FIVB events. Anchors the Soviet-to-Russian dynasty as one of the two longest continuously dominant men's volleyball programs in the sport's history (alongside the United States men's NT if extended back to the Beal-Dunphy era, and alongside Brazil from the Bernardinho era forward).

## Key claims / ideas

- **USSR Volleyball Federation joined FIVB in 1948;** in 1949 the USSR won the inaugural FIVB Men's World Championship 8-0.
- **Four Olympic gold medals:** 1964 Tokyo (USSR), 1968 Mexico City (USSR), 1980 Moscow (USSR), 2012 London (Russia).
- **Four Olympic silver medals:** 1976 Montreal, 1988 Seoul, 2000 Sydney, 2020 Tokyo.
- **Three Olympic bronze medals:** 1972 Munich, 2004 Athens, 2008 Beijing.
- **Six FIVB World Championship gold medals:** 1949, 1952, 1960, 1962, 1978, 1982.
- **Six FIVB World Cup gold medals:** 1965, 1977, 1981, 1991, 1999, 2011.
- **Fourteen European Championship gold medals** spanning 1950–2017; five in a row under Platonov-era leadership (1977–1985, corroborated via the Platonov bio).
- **2022 suspension:** FIVB and CEV suspended Russia from international competition on 28 February 2022 pursuant to IOC guidance.
