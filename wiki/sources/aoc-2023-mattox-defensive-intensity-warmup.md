## Summary

Tod Mattox uses a simple coach-driven toss drill to ignite defensive mindset during warmup. A coach or player at the net tosses balls across the backcourt; defenders dive to dig. A point scores only when the dig stays up and a teammate catches it. Mattox plays it to 7. The drill rehearses what he calls the real defensive lesson — defense is a mindset, translated as "go for it."

## Key claims / ideas

- Defense is mostly mindset; warmups should rehearse that mindset under pressure.
- Scoring only when a teammate catches the dig enforces playable height, not just touch-and-pray reaches.
- Random backcourt toss patterns force anticipation and explosive first step.
- 7-point games keep intensity high without exhausting the team before real drills.
- Warmup is a legitimate venue for developing pursuit, not only for physiological prep.
