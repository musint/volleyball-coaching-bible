# Why the setter dump should be part of your offense

## Summary
Jed Stotsenberg (Wave Volleyball) argues the setter dump's value isn't only in its kills — a dump quota (e.g., 10 dumps per match) keeps the defense honest and opens angles for hitters. Even back-row setters must remain offensive threats. References Dunning's point that successful dumps require holding the setting hand-shape long enough to avoid early defensive reads.

## Key claims / ideas
- Setter dumps matter even when they fail — the threat keeps the block honest and opens attacker angles.
- "Even when setters are in the back row, you always want them to be offensive."
- Dump quotas (e.g., 10 dumps per match) force setters to exercise the option.
- Disguise depends on keeping setting hand shape until the last moment (per Dunning).
