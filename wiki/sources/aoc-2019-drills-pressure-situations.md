## Summary

Harvey Dulberg PhD (via Championship Performance) argues practice should simulate competitive pressure weekly through "dress rehearsals" — end-of-match situational drills and deficit-start scenarios — so players carry a "been there, done that" composure into real games.

## Key claims / ideas

- Weekly dress rehearsals of competitive-pressure situations are a training fundamental, not a luxury.
- Deficit-start scenarios (down 5-0 to begin a drill) teach comeback capability.
- Cross-sport examples (football 40-yard-FG-with-3-seconds, tennis serving-for-match, Miami-Florida-State 80-yards-in-45-seconds) show the same principle works in any sport.
- Goal: make players feel "been there, done that" in real game pressure.

Pressure-situation drill design; deficit-start drills; cross-sport examples of clutch training.
