# Sport Practitioners as Sport Ecology Designers

## Summary

Woods and colleagues (2020) is a recent open-access synthesis in *Frontiers in Psychology* that reframes the coach's role under an ecological-dynamics view. Rather than treating skill as a motor memory that the athlete acquires and stores, the authors argue athletes develop **adaptive relationships with competitive environments** through practice ecologies that integrate performer, task, and environment constraints. The practitioner becomes a **sport ecology designer** — someone who shapes informational and task constraints so athletes can self-organize functional solutions — rather than an instructor who prescribes movement templates.

The paper is a useful bridge between the older ecological-dynamics theoretical statements (Davids, Araújo, Renshaw) and applied coaching practice in 2020s team sports. It takes a strong rhetorical line — even the term "skill acquisition" is argued to be misleading — which is useful for framing the ecological-dynamics school on the wiki in contrast with Gold Medal Squared's technique-first pedagogy. Open-access; full abstract and key claims accessed.

## Key claims / ideas

1. **Skill as adaptation, not acquisition.** "Skill acquisition" misrepresents learning; athletes develop functional relationships with performance environments rather than storing reproducible motor memories.
2. **Practitioner-as-designer.** Coaches function as learning-ecology designers who facilitate self-organized athlete–environment interactions rather than deliver standardized movement templates.
3. **Representative design is central.** Practice tasks must authentically simulate competitive informational constraints so athletes can attune to relevant affordances.
4. **Functional movement variability.** Variability is adaptive in skilled performers and should not be uniformly treated as error.
5. **Self-organization via constraint manipulation.** Athletes exploit self-organizing tendencies when constraints are designed well, enabling emergent problem-solving across varied performance contexts.
