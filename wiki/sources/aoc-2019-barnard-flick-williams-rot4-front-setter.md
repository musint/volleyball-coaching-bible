## Summary

Two strategies for a rotation with a front-row setter: (1) stack the slide-capable middle by the setter and front-left-stack all front-row players to disguise the offense; (2) if the right-front is a good passer, they can pass from left-back as long as they're in front of right-back — serving team should target the front-row non-defender.

## Key claims / ideas

- Stacking forces the opposing middle to shadow, freeing back-row attack possibilities.
- Positioning rules for passing: right-front can pass from left-back *if* they stay in front of right-back.
- Serving strategy: target the opposing front-row non-designated-defender.
- Back-row attacks are essential weapons in rotation-4 when setter is front row.
