# Hit more line, score more points

## Summary
Kyle Mashima (Rotate 123 / SoloStats) uses shot-chart data to argue HS/club teams overhit cross-court and undertrain line. His fix: make line hits mandatory in drills, switch OHs from an angled approach to a more straight-ahead approach with a higher/more-vertical/inside set. His team moved from ~10:1 cross:line to ~55:45 and kill rates jumped 5-10%.

## Key claims / ideas
- Common ratio: 10 cross-court to 1 line — unbalanced because drills set line as a goal, not a requirement.
- 200+ swings (likely more) needed to reliably hit a spot.
- At 20% line rate you need 1,000 sets to get 200 line reps; at 80% (required) you need 250.
- Fix: drills where line is mandatory regardless of set quality; bumping or 2-handed push to zone 1 counts.
- Traditional angled approach uses a fast, low, cross-court-biased set — high lateral speed demands precision timing, hitters default to cross or miss-hit.
- Line approach: straighter approach + higher, more-inside, more-vertical set → hitter times jump, not lateral movement.
- Line defenders are often weaker because the setter digs the line.
- High-line to Zone 1 corner: even vs. formed block, ball tends to deflect off top of block in.
- Target ratio: 2:1 cross:line (up from ~10:1).
- Reported results after structured training: 55% cross / 45% line; kill rate up 5-10%.
- Drill examples: 4-person Columbus with line-only scoring; 3-person receive + setter requiring 5 line kills per pin to complete.
