## Summary

Terry Liskevych's six-stage ball-control digging sequence for paired players. One player starts on the 10-foot line with back to the net; the other starts a few steps up from the endline. The pair works through progressively more demanding sequences: (1) toss-hit-dig-catch, (2) toss-hit-dig-hit, (3) 2-person pepper (dig-set-hit), (4) 2-person pepper with jump attack, (5) 2-person pepper with all overhand digs (forcing flatter hitter trajectory), (6) dig-to-self, set-to-self, hit across — then partner repeats. Coaches switch orientations. Accountability is enforced with rep count ("10 perfect contacts in a row") or time ("15 seconds no errors") targets.

## Key claims / ideas

- 2-person pepper can be treated as a progression, not a single drill — six clear steps from simple to demanding.
- Overhand-dig pepper forces the hitter to hit a flatter trajectory, which is a closer game match for real attacks.
- Self-dig / self-set / hit across is a useful isolation progression toward live pepper.
- Accountability thresholds matter: "10 perfect in a row" or "15 seconds clean" hold players to a standard.
- Players must swap orientations so both sides get back-to-net and net-facing reps.
