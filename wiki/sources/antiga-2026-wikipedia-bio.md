# Stéphane Antiga — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical record for **Stéphane Antiga** — French outside hitter (France NT 1998–2010, 306 caps, captain) and the head coach who delivered Poland's **2014 FIVB Men's World Championship gold on home soil** (September 21, 2014; 3–1 over Brazil; Mariusz Wlazły MVP). Born February 3, 1976, Suresnes, France; height 2.00 m. As player: 2002 World Championship bronze, 2003 + 2009 European Championship silvers, 2006 World League silver, 2004 Athens Olympics participant; CEV Champions League gold with Paris Volley 2000–01; multiple Polish championships with Skra Bełchatów. As coach: Poland men's NT 2014–2016 (three-year contract appointed October 24, 2013), Canada men's NT 2017–2018 (2017 World League bronze, Curitiba), then Polish-club career including Onico Warsaw (2017–2019), Developres Rzeszów (2019–2024, including Polish Championship gold 2024–25), Pallavolo Scandicci (2024), and LUK Lublin (2025–present). Knight's Cross of the Order of Merit of Poland (2014). Co-recipient "Coach of the Year" 2014.

## Key claims / ideas

- Born February 3, 1976, Suresnes, France; height 2.00 m; outside hitter; France NT captain 1998–2010, 306 caps.
- Player medals: 2002 World Championship bronze; 2003 + 2009 European Championship silvers; 2006 World League silver.
- Club: Paris UC, Paris Volley (CEV Champions League 2000–01), Skra Bełchatów (5 Polish championships), Delecta Bydgoszcz, FIVB Club World Championship silvers 2009 + 2010 with Skra.

- Canada men's NT 2017–2018: 2017 FIVB World League bronze (Curitiba).
- Polish club career: Onico Warsaw 2017–2019 (Polish Championship silver 2018–19); Developres Rzeszów 2019–2024 (Polish SuperCup 2021–22, 2022–23; Polish Cup 2021–22; Polish Championship gold 2024–25); Pallavolo Scandicci 2024; LUK Lublin 2025–present (Polish SuperCup + Cup 2025–26).
- 2014 awards: Knight's Cross of the Order of Merit of the Republic of Poland (Bronisław Komorowski); Médaille d'or de la jeunesse, des sports et de l'engagement associatif (France); co-recipient "Coach of the Year" 2014; Polish Men's Volleyball Team named "Best Team of 2014."
