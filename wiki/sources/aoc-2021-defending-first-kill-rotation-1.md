# Defending the first swing: Rotation 1

## Summary
Mike Sealy (UCLA HC 2010-2022) and former USA national team player Salima Rockwell dissect defensive coverage for Rotation 1 — the typical rotation where the opposing offense runs a hut, a one, and a high-ball right-side attack. They walk through blocker shading choices and serving adjustments to disrupt the offense.

## Key claims / ideas
- Opponent's attack set in R1 for this breakdown: hut (outside), one (quick middle), and a high back set to RS.
- Four defensive levers:
  - (1) Bunch read — pin blockers pinch in, prepared to release to the pins on an outside set.
  - (2) If the setter is pulled to either pin due to a poor pass, the MB shades toward that blocker but stays capable of transitioning to the opposite pin if the setter pushes it back.
  - (3) If the opposing middle is strong and crafty, commit the MB entirely to defending the middle.
  - (4) Serving lever — use a short serve to disrupt the opponent's in-system offense and slow the tempo.
