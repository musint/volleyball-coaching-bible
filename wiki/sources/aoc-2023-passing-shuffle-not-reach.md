# Passing movement: Shuffling instead of reaching

## Summary
Ron Kordes (Assumption HS / KIVA VBC) argues that passers should shuffle to center the ball in the body rather than reach with arms, and offers a simple two-tosser shuffle drill to train the habit.

## Key claims / ideas
- Reaching with the arms to finish a pass is a common but suboptimal habit.
- Shuffling to center the ball gives greater accuracy and control than reaching.
- Drill: two tossers stand 10-15 feet apart; the passer shuffles back and forth between them, playing tosses in front — for reps or time.

## Topics covered
- Passing footwork (shuffling vs. reaching)
- Body centering on contact
- Simple repeatable passing-movement drill
