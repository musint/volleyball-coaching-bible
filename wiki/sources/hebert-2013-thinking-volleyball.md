# Thinking Volleyball (Mike Hebert, 2013)

**DISAMBIGUATION**: Mike Hebert's *Thinking Volleyball* (Human Kinetics, 2013, ISBN 9781450442626) shares its title with Hugh McCutcheon's *Thinking Volleyball* (2022). Always cite with author and year. This source is `hebert-2013-thinking-volleyball`.

## Summary
Mike Hebert's final book is a philosophical and programmatic treatise rather than a drill compendium. Of its 10 chapters, only two (offense and defense) are narrowly tactical; the remaining eight cover program structure, team policy, goals, event management, team playing style, gym culture, trust-building, communication systems, match coaching, and statistics. The book's central theme is being "ready, willing, and able to think beyond the conventional" — grounded in a real understanding of why convention exists, not reckless contrarianism.

Hebert spent 35 years coaching at Pittsburgh, New Mexico, Illinois, and Minnesota (952–392 career record, five Final Fours, 1985 AVCA Coach of the Year, AVCA President 1987–1990). The book channels that experience into sections on reading defense (his signature tactical contribution), learned optimism, managing injured players, and whether the time cost of detailed statistics actually pays back.

## Key claims / ideas
- Think beyond convention — but only after understanding why convention exists.
- Gym culture, trust, and communication rival tactical systems in determining program outcomes.
- Reading defense: players read set/hitter tendencies and posture accordingly rather than playing a rigid rotational base.
- Statistics skepticism — an initial "stats evangelist" later questioning ROI on detailed stat collection.
- Program management (policy, goals, events) is a first-class coaching skill.

## Topics covered
- Program philosophy and management
- Reading defense and offensive/defensive systems
- Gym culture, trust, team chemistry, learned optimism
- Statistical analysis and its limits
- Match coaching and in-game adjustments
