# Brazil women's national volleyball team — Wikipedia article

## Summary

Reference-level Wikipedia article on the Brazil women's national volleyball team — the *Canarinhas* — a back-to-back Olympic gold-winning program (Beijing 2008, London 2012) with **46 total titles** across competitions. The article anchors the women's program to two head-coach eras: **Bernardo Rezende's early-success stint 1990–2000** (Atlanta 1996 and Sydney 2000 bronzes, 1994 World Championship silver) and **José Roberto Guimarães's 2003/2004-present dynasty** (2008 and 2012 Olympic golds, 2020 Tokyo silver, 2024 Paris bronze). As of June 2025 the team is ranked 2nd in FIVB World Rankings, with 12 FIVB World Grand Prix golds — a competition record.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Olympic medal record.** Gold 2008, 2012. Silver 2020. Bronze 1996, 2000, 2024. (Six Olympic medals total across 12 Olympic appearances since 1980.)
- **Olympic-run details.** Beijing 2008: undefeated 8-0. London 2012: 6-2. Tokyo 2020: 6-2.
- **World Championship ceiling.** 18 appearances since 1956; no gold; four silvers (1994, 2006, 2010, 2022) and two bronzes (2014, 2025).
- **World Cup.** Three silvers (1995, 2003, 2007), one bronze (1999), no championships.
- **Grand Prix dominance.** 12 championships — a competition record.
- **Nations League.** Four silvers (2019, 2021, 2022, 2025); winless in finals.
- **Two-era coaching arc.** Bernardinho 1990–2000 → Guimarães 2003/2004-present.
- **Other titles.** 23 South American Championship golds; 4 Pan American Games golds; 2 World Grand Champions Cup golds.
- **Playing style.** Team "emphasizes technical skill and aggressive attacking, characteristics developed through sustained club competition and consistent international program investment."
- **Current status.** Ranked 2nd in FIVB World Rankings (June 2025); Zé Roberto as current head coach.
- **Nickname.** *Canarinhas* ("Little Canaries").
