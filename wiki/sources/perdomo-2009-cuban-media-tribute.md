# Antonio "Ñico" Perdomo — Cuban-media obituary content (2009)

## Summary
Aggregated obituary content from Spanish-language Cuban volleyball media after Perdomo's November 2009 death. Direct fetches of cubaencuentro.com and the YouTube tribute video were blocked or rate-limited; the factual content here is reconstructed from web-search-result excerpts of those obituaries. **The career chronology is consistent across all reachable obituary fragments**: 1966 assistant alongside George with the men's NT, 1969 women's junior team, 1973 elite women's team alongside George, assistant during the Barcelona/Atlanta/Sydney Olympic golds, head coach during the late-1990s World Championship and World Cup wins, and Cuban Volleyball Federation advisor at his death.

Used on [[antonio-perdomo]] as the **primary career-chronology source** that links the Olympedia 2008-Beijing-only record to Perdomo's much longer assistant-and-head-coach trajectory.

## Key claims / ideas
- **1966 — assistant alongside Eugenio George, Cuban men's NT.**
- **1969 — coached the women's junior NT.**
- **1973 — assumed leadership of the elite women's NT alongside George.**
- Assistant during George's Barcelona (1992), Atlanta (1996), and Sydney (2000) Olympic-gold tenures.
- Cuban Volleyball Federation advisor at the time of his death.
- Died suddenly from respiratory failure in November 2009 at age 66.
- "Contributed significantly to the **creation of the Cuban Volleyball School** and its record of achievements."
- Nickname: **"Ñico."**
