# Hitter training: Attacking vs. single, double and triple blocks

## Summary
Oregon State's Mark Barnard runs a competitive hitter drill progression: two hitters compete against single, double, then triple blocks with a kill/play/error scoring system (+1/0/-1).

## Key claims / ideas
- Progressive block density trains hitters to finish against increasing defensive pressure.
- Scoring: kill = +1, ball in play = 0, error = -1; winner earns 1 or 2 depending on margin.
- Drill extends to left, right, back row; can add pre-pass from serve or digging start from net.
- Simulates in-match swing conditions against single, double, and triple blocks.

## Topics covered
- Attacking under block pressure.
- Block density training.
- Competitive two-hitter drill format.
