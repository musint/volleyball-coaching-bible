# Pro Volleyball Federation (PVF) — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia institutional record for the Pro Volleyball Federation — the U.S. women's pro indoor league announced November 17, 2022, with first match January 24, 2024. Inaugural seven-team format (Atlanta Vibe, Columbus Fury, Grand Rapids Rise, Omaha Supernovas, Orlando Valkyries, San Diego Mojo, Vegas Thrill) plus 2025 expansion to Indianapolis. Independent franchise ownership, traditional season-table structure, January-through-May calendar. Notable founders include Trent Dilfer, with Cecile Reynaud as VP of Volleyball Operations. Six of seven inaugural head coaches are women. Champions: Omaha Supernovas (2024), Orlando Valkyries (2025). Anchors the [[pvf]] page.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Announced Nov 17, 2022; first match Jan 24, 2024**
- **Founders:** Dave Whinham (Columbus, OH), Stephen Evans (Frisco, TX); Trent Dilfer joined as partner
- **Cecile Reynaud (USA Volleyball Hall of Fame, 26 yrs Florida State HC):** VP Volleyball Operations
- **Inaugural seven teams** — Atlanta Vibe, Columbus Fury, Grand Rapids Rise, Omaha Supernovas, Orlando Valkyries, San Diego Mojo, Vegas Thrill
- **Inaugural head coaches** — Todd Dagenais (Atlanta), Angel Perez (Columbus), Cathy George (Grand Rapids), Shelton Collier (Omaha), Amy Pauly (Orlando), Tayyiba Haneef-Park (San Diego, Olympian), Fran Flory (Vegas, longtime LSU HC)
- **Six of seven HCs are women**
- **Indianapolis expansion 2025** (Indianapolis Ignite)
- **Franchise ownership** — independent ownership groups per team (contrasts with LOVB single-entity)
- **Calendar:** January–May; ~16-game regular season; top-four playoff
- **$1M championship prize**
- **Salary range 2025:** $60K–$175K including benefits; individual awards $3K–$15K
- **Inaugural-match attendance record:** 11,624 (Atlanta vs. Omaha, Jan 24, 2024); broken at 12,090 (Omaha home opener)
- **USA Volleyball alliance** announced 2024
- **Champions:** Omaha Supernovas (2024 def. Grand Rapids Rise); Orlando Valkyries (2025)
