# Viacheslav Platonov (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article on **Vyacheslav Alekseyevich Platonov** (21 February 1939 – 26 December 2005), head coach of the USSR men's national volleyball team through its 1977–1985 peak. The article establishes Platonov as the architect of the Soviet men's program during the era in which it won the 1978 and 1982 World Championships, the 1980 Moscow Olympic gold, and five consecutive European Championships (1977–1985). Platonov's club tenure with Automobilist Leningrad (later the organizational precursor to Zenit Saint Petersburg) produced USSR Championships in 1983 and 1989. International Volleyball Hall of Fame inductee (2002).

## Key claims / ideas

- **Playing career 1954–1967** with SKIF, SKA, and Spartak — Russian-domestic club pipeline before moving into coaching.
- **USSR head coach 1977–1985** — primary tenure.
- **USSR head coach 1990–1991** — second tenure through the dissolution year.
- **Unified Team head coach 1992; Russia head coach 1995–1997** — post-Soviet continuity.
- **Gold at 1978 FIVB World Championship** (Italy).
- **Gold at 1980 Summer Olympics** (Moscow).
- **Gold at 1982 FIVB World Championship** (Argentina).
- **Five consecutive European Championships 1977–1985** as head coach.
- **USSR Championship with Automobilist Leningrad 1983 and 1989.**
- **International Volleyball Hall of Fame induction 2002.**
