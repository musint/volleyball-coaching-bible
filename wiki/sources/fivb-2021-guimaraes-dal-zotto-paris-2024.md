# FIVB 2021: Guimarães + Dal Zotto to Paris 2024

## Summary

Official FIVB announcement (October 21, 2021) confirming both **José
Roberto "Zé Roberto" Guimarães** (Brazil women's NT) and **Renan Dal Zotto**
(Brazil men's NT) as head coaches of their respective programs through the
Paris 2024 Olympic cycle. The announcement is the canonical Tier-2
source for Dal Zotto's tenure as the post-Bernardinho Brazil men's NT
rebuild-cycle head coach — establishing that he **took charge in 2017**
(replacing Bernardinho after the Rio 2016 home gold) and that the CBV
extended his tenure through the three-year Paris-cycle truncated in 2023
when Bernardinho returned.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Dal Zotto took charge of the Brazil men's NT in 2017.** Direct FIVB-
  federation confirmation of the year — resolves the [unverified] tag on
  the Brazilian-school page.
- **Guimarães has coached the women's team since 2003.** Corroborates
  Wikipedia's tenure-start date for Guimarães's women's-program run.
- **2021 CBV endorsement of both coaches for the Paris cycle** signals
  institutional continuity as the structural commitment of the Brazilian
  tradition: same two architects carried across Olympic cycles with
  federation support.

## Direct quotes

**Adrián Behar, CBV Chief Executive:**
> "We are very happy to have Jose Roberto Guimaraes and Renan Dal Zotto
> for another Olympic cycle."

**José Roberto Guimarães:**
> "It is a new challenge, with a three-year cycle and with great
> expectations."
>
> "I'm proud to represent my country, it is the dream of every coach,
> athlete and manager."

**Renan Dal Zotto:**
> "We have a shorter cycle, starting with the World Championship next
> year, which is very important."
