# Terry Liskevych: "There Are No Shortcuts"

## Summary
A VolleyBrains episode in which Liskevych reflects on his twelve years running the USA Women's National Team (1985–1996) and his post-coaching work as AOC co-founder and editor of *The Volleyball Coaching Bible* (Human Kinetics, AVCA series). Two themes anchor the conversation: failure as tuition ("failure is tuition for the future") and the patience required to build anything real ("everything takes longer than you think, and nothing in life that is worth doing is easy").

Liskevych recounts inheriting a USA NT program in 1985 that had been gutted by retirements after the 1984 silver-medal team — losing all seven matches and no sets against Korea's third-ranked club team. Within a decade the team reached world #1, earned bronze at Barcelona 1992 and the 1990 World Championships, and took gold at the 1995 World Grand Prix.

## Key claims / ideas
- Failure is tuition — treat losses and skill errors as development, not setbacks.
- There are no shortcuts; sustained effort over realistic timelines beats silver bullets.
- Mentoring is the highest-leverage later-career activity: "I've learned so much about how not to do things that I can help people cut down on some of the errors."
- Building a program from the ashes is possible if you hold the long timeline.
