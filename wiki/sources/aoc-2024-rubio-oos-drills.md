# Drills for out-of-system scenarios

## Summary
Dave Rubio (Arizona '92-'22) argues that college teams should train out-of-system more than in-system, and shares drills that force hitters to adapt to imperfect passes, imperfect sets, emergency scenarios, dig-to-attack transitions, and libero-sourced sets.

## Key claims / ideas
- Out-of-system = all three attackers not available to attack.
- Practice ratio should lean more OOS than in-system, especially at the college level.
- Hitters must adapt to imperfect passes, imperfect sets, and emergency scenarios as a teachable skill.
- Approach angle and depth of attack matter more than usual when the set is off.
- Drill: outside attacker digs, then quickly transitions to attack in the same rally.
- Drill: attackers adapt to libero-sourced OOS sets.

## Topics covered
- Out-of-system practice philosophy (OOS-heavy ratio)
- Dig-to-attack transition for OHs
- Hitter adaptation to libero sets
- Approach angle / attack depth adjustments
- OOS drill taxonomy
