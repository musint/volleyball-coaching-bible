## Summary

Jen Flynn Oldenburg's Ohio State drill alternates in-system and out-of-system offense. Defense side fields 3 front-row plus 1 back-row player; offense side runs 6. A free ball initiates the in-system rep. Then the coach bounces a ball deep and the offense has two touches — forcing an out-of-system bump set from behind the 10-foot line. In-system and out-of-system scenarios alternate. The offense tries to earn 6 points in a row. Oldenburg's coaching point: hitters must still rip it, and whoever sets out-of-system (setter or not) must be bold with the contact.

## Key claims / ideas

- Alternating in-system and out-of-system reps keeps the drill match-like because real games oscillate between the two constantly.
- Out-of-system offense requires bold bump-setting from any player, not just the setter — so every attacker must rehearse it.
- Hitters are coached to swing hard even on the out-of-system ball — reduced-effort hitting is not the goal.
- 6-in-a-row target creates sustained pressure without being an endless block.
- Defense-to-offense ratio (4 defenders vs. 6 attackers) is asymmetric by design — the defenders are expected to work harder per rep.
