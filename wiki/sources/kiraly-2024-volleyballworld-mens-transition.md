# Kiraly switches to coach USA men ahead of LA 2028

## Summary

Published on Volleyball World (en.volleyballworld.com), the FIVB's own news site, this article covers the October 2024 announcement that Karch Kiraly would become head coach of the U.S. Men's National Team for the LA 2028 cycle. It complements the USA-facing coverage (USAV story page, VolleyballMag.com) by framing the transition for an international audience and by capturing two specific Kiraly quotes that do not always appear in the U.S.-facing versions.

The article confirms Kiraly was 63 at time of appointment, that this is his first time coaching the men's side, and that the opening was created when John Speraw moved to USAV president and CEO. It also provides the short strategic context: the U.S. men ranked third in the world, had just earned bronze at Paris 2024, and are auto-qualified for LA 2028 as host nation. Kiraly's first competition with the men was slated for the 2025 VNL.

## Key claims / ideas

- Kiraly quote on the new role: "I am thrilled beyond words at the opportunity to coach the U.S. Men's National Team."
- Kiraly quote on the transition: "For one-quarter of my life, I've poured everything I have into the U.S. Women's Team...Now, it's time for new adventures."
- His 12-16 year women's tenure collected: 2020 Tokyo gold, 2014 World Championship, three consecutive VNL titles (2018, 2019, 2021).
- U.S. men auto-qualified for LA 2028 as host, removing qualification pressure from Kiraly's first cycle with them.
- First men's competition under Kiraly: 2025 VNL.
