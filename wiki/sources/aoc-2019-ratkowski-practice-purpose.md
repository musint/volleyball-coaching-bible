## Summary

Catie Ratkowski (Divine Savior Holy Angels HS) argues every practice element must have a named purpose — long-term goal, short-term goal, opponent-specific tactic, or diagnosed skill gap — and team must be made aware of those goals (whiteboard, group text, locker-room post).

## Key claims / ideas

- Every drill needs a named purpose, not only a label.
- Goals must be visible to players (whiteboard, group text, locker room) to align effort.
- Practice flow: individual skills first, then apply in game-like situations (scrimmage).
- Goal-sharing is a coach responsibility, not a bonus.

## Topics covered

Goal-visibility mechanisms; practice flow (skills → application); purpose-driven drill selection.
