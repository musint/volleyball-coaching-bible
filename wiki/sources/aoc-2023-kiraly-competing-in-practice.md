# Karch Kiraly: Gain a competitive advantage by competing in practice

## Summary
Kiraly argues the USA women's composure in high-pressure matches is built by competing intensely every day in practice, and that most programs that don't compete in the gym are handing his team a competitive edge.

## Key claims / ideas
- Composure under pressure is trained, not innate.
- Daily competition in practice teaches both how to win and how to lose.
- Kiraly quote: "Lots of coaches don't have their teams compete in their gyms. They're handing us a competitive advantage."
- Cited example: Rio pool match vs. Netherlands — down 1-2, held composure, won.

## Topics covered
- Competitive practice culture
- Pressure training
- Mental composure / dealing with losing
- Practice design lever
