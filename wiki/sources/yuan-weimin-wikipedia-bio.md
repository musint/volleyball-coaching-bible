# Yuan Weimin — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

The Wikipedia biography of Yuan Weimin (袁伟民) documents the head coach who built the modern Chinese women's volleyball tradition out of post-Cultural-Revolution conditions [yuan-weimin-wikipedia-bio]. Appointed to the China women's NT head-coach role in 1976, Yuan transformed a team ranked 16th in the world (1974) into "a super power" — winning the 1981 FIVB World Cup gold, 1982 FIVB World Championship gold, and 1984 Los Angeles Olympic gold across a three-event sequence that established the lineage [yuan-weimin-wikipedia-bio]. Chinese-language coverage adds 1985 World Cup and 1986 World Championship golds to make the broader **"Five Consecutive Championships" (五连冠)** sequence; the English Wikipedia article does not enumerate the full five and the 1985/1986 results in the lineage are queued for backfill from CVA / Chinese-language federation sources [yuan-weimin-wikipedia-bio]. After his coaching career, Yuan became Director General of China's General Administration of Sports and chairman of the Chinese Olympic Committee in 2000 [yuan-weimin-wikipedia-bio]. He authored *My Way of Teaching*, described as a bestseller in China; specific philosophical detail is not elaborated in this Wikipedia source [yuan-weimin-wikipedia-bio].

## Key claims / ideas

- Full name: Yuan Weimin (袁伟民; pinyin: Yuán Wěimín)
- Born July 8, 1939, Suzhou, Jiangsu Province, China
- Head coach, China women's NT, **1976–1986** (decade tenure; mandate to rebuild post-Cultural-Revolution)
- Transformed a 16th-ranked team (1974) into "a super power"
- **1981 FIVB Women's World Cup — gold medal**
- **1982 FIVB Women's World Championship — gold medal**
- **1984 Los Angeles Olympic Games — gold medal**
- Chinese-language sources add 1985 World Cup and 1986 Worlds golds for the **五连冠 ("Five Consecutive Championships")** narrative — backfill queued
- Post-coaching: Director General, China General Administration of Sports; Chairman, Chinese Olympic Committee (2000–mid-2000s)
- Author, *My Way of Teaching* (bestseller in China)
