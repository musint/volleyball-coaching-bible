# What Is a Libero in Volleyball? (Gold Medal Squared, 2022 Sweet 16 datapoint)

## Summary

A general-audience GMS explainer on the libero position that contains a high-impact empirical anchor for the modern libero-at-LB consensus: **"All 16 teams in the 2022 round of 16 had their libero in volleyball play in the left-back position."** Methodology: examined each Sweet 16 team's second-round victory in the 2022 NCAA tournament.

The piece also reports heat-map data on ball-contact location: in women's college volleyball, approximately **45% of balls land or pass through central court zones**; high school 2022 patterns show similar concentration in the middle of the court, left of the middle, and area 2.

The 2022 Sweet 16 datapoint is the strongest single citable claim for libero-at-LB as the modern women's-game default. It is GMS-attributed and abstract-level (the underlying chart and methodology aren't published in the public-facing article), but it is the data point most-frequently cited in contemporary coaching content for the libero positioning trend.

## Key claims / ideas

- **2022 NCAA Sweet 16: 16 of 16 teams positioned libero at LB.** Methodology: Sweet 16 round-of-16 second-round-victory matches.
- **~45% of women's college balls land or pass through central court zones** (heat-map data).
- High school 2022 patterns show similar central-zone heavy attack distribution.
- Libero-at-LB rationale (per the article's seven listed reasons): defending hard cross-court OH attack (zone 5 lands the most attacks); facilitating setter responsibilities; preserving back-row attack options from outside hitters; (other reasons not fully detailed).
