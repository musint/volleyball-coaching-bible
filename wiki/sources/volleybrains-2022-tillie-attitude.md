# Laurent Tillie: 'The right attitude is everything' — VolleyBrains, July 2022

## Summary

Interview-style profile of Laurent Tillie published by VolleyBrains on 18 July 2022, captured during his Osaka Bluteon (Japan) tenure shortly after his decade as France men's NT head coach. The piece collects Tillie's voice on practice design, attitude, and game execution. It also documents his methodological lineage: Tillie cites the influence of American coaches, particularly [[carl-mcgown]] of [[gold-medal-squared]], whose game-realistic-training methodology he found markedly different from prevailing French training conventions.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Practice design as situation-design:** Tillie's stated coaching concept is "putting you in the right situation so that you, the player, will find the right answer." Practice is meant to engineer reps where players solve, not where coaches drill in solutions.
- **Skill is not the goal of practice:** "Skill is important, but skill is not the goal of the practice. The first thing to do is to play and win." Tillie privileges competitive game-realistic training over isolated technique reps.
- **The best receive drill is a full rally:** "The best drill for receiving is serving, receiving, pass, set, attack." A direct expression of integrating skill within the actual sequence of play.
- **Game execution as trained reaction, not deliberation:** "The game doesn't allow you to think." Volleyball is "a game that's all about implementing a few well trained reactions on the right moment." His on-court directive: "When the game starts, don't think play, play, play. … Act like an animal, without thinking."
- **Attitude precedes outcome:** "Attitude is everything." Tillie does not punish mistakes provided "attitude and body language is on point," and frames body language as the lever that "can even change your state of mind."
- **GMS / Carl McGown influence:** Tillie identifies American coaches, especially Carl McGown, as influences whose game-realistic training methodology departed from previous French training norms.
