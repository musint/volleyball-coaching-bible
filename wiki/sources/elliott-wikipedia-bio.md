# Jerritt Elliott — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
The Wikipedia biography of Jerritt Elliott compiles his playing career (Pepperdine, Hawai'i, Cal State Northridge), his pre-collegiate coaching path through Westside Volleyball Club and Marymount HS, his five-year USC ascent under Lisa Love (1995–2000) ending with two interim-HC seasons, and his 25-season Texas head-coaching tenure (2001–present) [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The page enumerates his three NCAA national championships (2012, 2022, 2023), 11 Final Four appearances, 15 Big 12 conference titles, AVCA National Coach of the Year (2012), seven AVCA Southwest Region COY honors, and Texas's 2024 move from the Big 12 to the SEC. It serves as the central biographical anchor for Elliott because Texas's official athletics-page bio is paywalled / structured as a directory rather than a full bio when accessed from outside the program.

## Key claims / ideas
- Elliott was born April 28, 1968, Pacific Palisades CA; played at Pepperdine (1986–88), Hawai'i (1989–90), graduated Cal State Northridge 1991.
- Texas career record: 685–137 (.833); Big 12 tenure: 635–125 (.836).
- **Three NCAA national championships at Texas: 2012, 2022, 2023** — back-to-back in 2022 and 2023.
- 11 Final Four appearances in 25 Texas seasons.
- 15 Big 12 conference titles: 2007–2009, 2011–2015, 2017–2023 (every year from 2017 to 2023, ending the Big 12 era).
- Texas joined the SEC in 2024; finished 20–7 (13–3 SEC) that year and 26–4 (13–2) in 2025.
- AVCA National Coach of the Year 2012; 7x AVCA Southwest Region COY (2004, 2007, 2010–11, 2013, 2021–22); 9x Big 12 Coach of the Year.
- Pre-Texas USC interim HC (1999–2000): 50–12 (.806), Pac-10 shared title, first USC Final Four in 15 seasons; recruiting classes #1 nationally in 2000 and 2001 (program firsts).
- USA Volleyball roles: 2001 Junior NT assistant; 2008 USA Volleyball A2 Team coach.
- High school teammate of Olympic gold medalist Kent Steffes at Palisades Charter HS.
