# Coach-Athlete Relationships, Self-Confidence, and Psychological Wellbeing (Coussens et al. 2024)

## Summary

Coussens and colleagues (2024) tested how two distinct forms of coach support — **perceived support** (athlete believes support is available if needed) and **received support** (support actually delivered) — relate to athletes' self-confidence and psychological wellbeing. In a large cross-sectional sample of 537 athletes, the authors examined whether the **coach-athlete relationship** mediates these support-outcome links and, critically, whether the two support types follow distinct mechanisms.

The headline findings are clean: perceived support uniquely predicts **self-confidence** through coach-athlete relationship quality; received support uniquely predicts **psychological wellbeing** through the same relational pathway. The two support types are not interchangeable — they predict different outcomes through the same mediator. The implication for coaches: building the relationship is the load-bearing first step (the mediator), and then both support types are needed — visible availability for confidence, actual delivery for wellbeing. Concrete behaviors that materialize received support include open-communication policies and consistent, visible help during stressors. The study is one of the cleanest mechanistic tests of how coach behavior translates into athlete psychological outcomes and is directly transferable to volleyball coaching contexts.

## Key claims / ideas

- Perceived coach support → self-confidence, mediated by coach-athlete relationship quality.
- Received coach support → psychological wellbeing, mediated by coach-athlete relationship quality.
- The two support types are distinct constructs with distinct outcomes through the same mediator.
- Coach-athlete relationship is a true mediator, not just a correlate.
- "Being available" and "actually showing up" are not interchangeable — coaches must do both.
- Practical: tell athletes what kinds of support are available (builds confidence), then deliver concrete support during stressors (builds wellbeing).
