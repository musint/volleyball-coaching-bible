# Training an aggressive approach for a more powerful swing

## Summary
Mark Barnard (Oregon State) advocates a four-step approach as the default for most hitters because it allows a "fast-fast-faster" momentum build, gives more adjustment time, and protects maximum speed at takeoff.

## Key claims / ideas
- Great hitters terminate in all situations; the approach is where termination is built.
- Four-step approach preferred over three-step: more room for "fast-fast-faster" acceleration.
- Three-step can work but leaves less time to reach maximum speed and less time to adjust.
- The non-negotiable regardless of step count: keep accelerating through the approach, never lose momentum before contact.

## Topics covered
- Four-step vs three-step approach tradeoff
- Acceleration profile through the approach
- Momentum preservation to contact
- Step-count decision as a coach
