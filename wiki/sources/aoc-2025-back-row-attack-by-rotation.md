# Running a back row attack by rotation

## Summary
Beth Launiere (Utah HC 1990-2025) frames back-row attacking as an increasingly important part of the modern game and recommends running back-row offense by rotation to surface the attack-pattern weaknesses that need work. She provides a 12-player rotational drill.

## Key claims / ideas
- Back-row attack is a growing portion of today's game.
- Time and effort invested in back-row offense should be calibrated to the **strengths of your hitters** — assess before committing.
- Running back-row offense rotation-by-rotation is a smart diagnostic: it surfaces the patterns you need to work on.
- Rotational back-row attack drill:
  - Purpose: practice back-row offense in different rotations.
  - Setup: two teams on the court in Rotation 4.
  - Execution: both sides play the rally with every set going to the back-row attacker. Instruct the BR hitter to hit an A, Bic, Pipe, or D shot depending on their ability and court position. Cycle to other rotations.
  - Requirements: 12 players, balls, coach, full court.
- Back-row attack shot-name inventory mentioned: A, Bic, Pipe, D.

## Topics covered
- Rotational diagnosis of back-row offense
- Back-row shot vocabulary (A / Bic / Pipe / D)
- Practice drill design — forcing every set to the back row
