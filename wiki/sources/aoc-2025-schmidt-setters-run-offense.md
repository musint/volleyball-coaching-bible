# Attacking vs. blocking: Are you teaching your setters how to run an offense?

## Summary
Simon Fraser head coach Gina Schmidt argues setters must be trained to read the opposing block — not just distribute to hitters. Teach setters to identify favorable matchups (best hitter vs. weakest blocker), read what the middle blocker is doing (gap open on 31 → set middle; middle fronting → back-set to isolated right side), and talk openly with the team about why certain sets keep coming.

## Key claims / ideas
- Setters must look at the other side of the net — specifically, the block — to decide who to attack.
- Best-hitter vs. weakest-blocker matchup is usually the right answer.
- "It's OK to talk with your whole team about why the setter is doing what they're doing."
- Read the middle blocker: gap open on a 31 → set the middle; middle fronting → back-set the isolated right side.
- "If it ain't broke, don't fix it" — keep running a winning matchup.

## Topics covered
- Setter tactical decision-making
- Reading the block (specifically the middle blocker)
- Hitter-blocker matchups
- Team-wide transparency around set allocation
