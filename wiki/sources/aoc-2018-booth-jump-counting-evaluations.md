## Summary

This is AOC curricular content; defer to qualified AT/CSCS/MD on programming specifics. Kirsten Bernthal Booth (Creighton head coach at the time of publication; later LOVB President of Business Operations) presents jump counting plus daily player wellness surveys as complementary injury-prevention tools. Data lets coaches throttle back players who aren't 100% so they're ready for the next match.

## Key claims / ideas

- Injury prevention requires daily data, not just training volume judgment.
- Two tools: jump counts and daily wellness/self-report surveys.
- Acting on the data means restricting a partially-compromised player's activity — intentional rep reduction, not push-through.
- Intent: match-day readiness, not generic "rest" — load management is strategic.

## Topics covered

- Jump counting as a standing practice
- Daily wellness surveys for player self-report
- Load management for match-day readiness
- Data-driven injury prevention
