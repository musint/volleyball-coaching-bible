# Setting drills to do at home – from Olympian Alisha Glass

## Summary
Rio 2016 bronze-medalist and Penn State 3-time NCAA champion setter Alisha Glass shares two simple at-home drills — lie-down catch/release progressions and progressive-distance wall sets — to train setter release and follow-through mechanics. The piece also points to Tod Mattox's chair/"freeze big hands" drill.

## Key claims / ideas
- "The release is a really important part of setting, and a lot of times we make it more complicated than it is."
- Lying-on-back setting isolates arms and hands from footwork — useful for young players dialing in release.
- Wall sets should progress from 2 ft → 4 ft → 8-10 ft to develop release, follow-through, and wrist/arm strength.
- Mattox's add-on: "freeze big hands" for two seconds after release to groove contact shape.
- Setting is one of the few volleyball skills a player can genuinely self-practice.
