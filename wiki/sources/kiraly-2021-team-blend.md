# USA coach Karch Kiraly talks with VolleyballMag.com: "I think we have a nice blend"

## Summary
A pre-Tokyo interview published June 9, 2021 on VolleyballMag.com, in which Kiraly addresses roster decisions for the U.S. Women's Olympic team — most notably the cut from three setter candidates (Jordyn Poulter, Micha Hancock, Lauren Carlini) down to the two-setter Olympic cap. The interview is a useful source for two reasons: first, it shows Kiraly's willingness to be publicly candid about difficult roster cuts rather than hiding behind press-release language; second, it demonstrates his habitual tone of public calibration — refusing to overclaim from regular-season success (a 9-0 Nations League run) heading into the Olympics.

The article captures Kiraly weeks before the team would go on to win its first-ever Olympic gold in Tokyo, so the modest framing contrasts sharply with the eventual outcome. For wiki pages on culture, press behavior, or roster construction, this is the right pre-Tokyo primary cite.

## Key claims / ideas
- Kiraly publicly acknowledged the difficulty of the setter cut and the reasonable arguments for all three candidates: "We made the best decision we could but I'm sure there are people who are upset with us or angry with us or frustrated with us. You could make good arguments about any of those three and it's just difficult that we only get to choose two."
- Refused to read Olympic hopes from VNL results: "Just take it with a grain of salt. 9-0 is nothing to be crazy about."
- Final Tokyo setter pairing: Jordyn Poulter + Micha Hancock; Lauren Carlini off the Olympic roster.
- Evidence of a "calibrated confidence" public voice — refusing to inflate regular-season results ahead of the main event.
