# Dani Busboom Kelly — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

The Wikipedia biography of Dani Busboom Kelly compiles her player-then-assistant-then-head-coach arc inside the Nebraska coaching tree [busboom-kelly-wikipedia-bio]. Identified by John Cook at age 14, she played as both setter and libero at Nebraska (2003–2006), graduated with 1,281 digs and 2,925 assists, and won the 2006 NCAA championship as a senior [busboom-kelly-wikipedia-bio]. After assistant stops at Tennessee (2009–2010), Louisville (2011), and Nebraska (2012–2016, including the 2015 NCAA title under Cook), she took the Louisville head-coaching job in 2017 [busboom-kelly-wikipedia-bio]. Her eight-season Louisville tenure produced a 203–44 record (.822), four ACC championships, a 2021 undefeated regular season with No. 1 coaches' poll ranking and Final Four appearance, the 2022 NCAA championship-match appearance (lost to Texas), and the 2023 Final Four [busboom-kelly-wikipedia-bio]. She was named Nebraska head coach on January 29, 2025 to succeed Cook on a six-year, $4.575M contract; her 2025 first season produced a 33–1 record and Big Ten championship [busboom-kelly-wikipedia-bio]. Awards include 2021 AVCA National Coach of the Year, 4x AVCA East Region Coach of the Year, 3x ACC Coach of the Year, and 2025 Big Ten Coach of the Year [busboom-kelly-wikipedia-bio].
