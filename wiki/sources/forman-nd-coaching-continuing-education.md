# Coaching continuing education (Forman, CoachingVB.com)

## Summary

John Forman's comparative analysis of volleyball coach-education enforcement
across USA Volleyball (CAP), Volleyball England, German Bundesliga A-license,
and FIVB. Forman — PhD, Level 3 Volleyball England, CAP III holder, founder of
coachingvb.com (2013), former NCAA D1 head coach at Brown and Rhode Island,
professional coaching in Sweden, presently working at a national-team / indoor
performance-direction level — writes as a credentialed coach inside the U.S.
system rather than as an outside critic. That positioning is what makes the
piece a usable secondary source: Forman holds the top CAP tier and still flags
its structural weakness.

## Key claims / ideas

- **CAP is entirely voluntary in practice.** "Since no one actually requires CAP
  for coaching, though, doing it really comes down to personal motivation."
  Junior-club coaches must complete IMPACT; CAP beyond that is not mandated by
  employers. Re-certification (every four years) is nominally required but not
  enforced.
- **U.S. scholastic floor is thin.** In Forman's state, the only required
  certification for high-school coaching was "a standard Red Cross first aid
  course. That was it. Nothing at all about actual coaching."
- **England also under-enforces.** Level 2 was theoretically required for
  National League coaches, but "it wasn't enforced." Insurance coverage was
  nominally contingent on certification; no one tested the contingency.
- **Germany represents the strong-enforcement model.** A-license is mandatory
  for Bundesliga coaches, with continuing-education courses required for
  license renewal.
- **FIVB certification is development-targeted.** Aimed at parts of the world
  "where there isn't a strong educational effort," with no re-certification
  requirement.
- **The overall diagnosis.** Forman characterizes the global picture as "this
  disparate set of educational offerings and requirements" and argues the
  inconsistency motivates interest in a global coaches association.
- **Forman does NOT call CAP bureaucratic.** His critique is structural and
  two-part: voluntary-participation-undermines-quality-control, and tier-front-
  loading (covered in his companion GMS/AOC/CAP comparison post). This
  distinction matters when citing the piece — it corroborates a lack-of-
  enforcement critique, not a bureaucratic-gatekeeping critique.

## Topics covered

- USAV CAP voluntary-certification structure (pre-2024 Coach Academy transition).
- Comparative volleyball-federation coach-education enforcement.
- Bundesliga A-license as enforcement benchmark.
- FIVB development-targeted certification.
