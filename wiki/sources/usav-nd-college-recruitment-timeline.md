# College Recruitment Timeline (USA Volleyball)

## Summary

USA Volleyball's official plain-language explainer of the NCAA recruiting timeline as it applies to volleyball. The page synthesizes NCAA Bylaw 13 for families — specifically defining the three operational floors that matter most for club-coach recruiting-support conversations: the earliest date a college coach may initiate communication (June 15 after sophomore year for D1 and D2), the earliest date a recruit may take unofficial or official campus visits (August 1 before junior year, with a five-official-visit lifetime cap), and the earliest date a recruit receives a written offer / NLI (mid-November of senior year, operationalized in the annual NCAA signing-dates document). USAV's page is the canonical public USAV reference for this timeline; AVCA historically published a parallel explainer at avca.org/education/resources/recruiting but that URL returns a 404 as of April 2026.

## Key claims / ideas

- NCAA Division I and II coaches cannot directly initiate communication with a women's volleyball recruit or family until **June 15 after the recruit's sophomore year** of high school. (Recruits face no restriction on outbound contact at any time; coaches may only respond substantively once the prospect crosses the June-15-after-sophomore threshold.)
- For D1 volleyball programs (men's, women's, and beach), **official and unofficial visits cannot happen until August 1 before the recruit's junior year**.
- Recruits may take a maximum of **five official visits** across all programs (D1 rule).
- Verbal commits carry no binding legal weight; the binding written offer / National Letter of Intent arrives at a recruit's school in **mid-November of senior year** (operationalized by the NCAA signing-dates PDF's initial signing date for "All Other D1 and D2 Sports" — Nov 12, 2025 for the 2025-26 cycle).
- The "September 1 of junior year" contact floor commonly repeated in older volleyball-parent forums applies to other D1 sports (basketball, football, certain Olympic sports) but not to women's volleyball under current Bylaw 13 legislation.
