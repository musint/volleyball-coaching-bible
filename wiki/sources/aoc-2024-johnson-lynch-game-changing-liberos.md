## Summary

Christy Johnson-Lynch (Iowa State) and Kirsten Bernthal Booth assert that a great libero is as critical to team success as a great setter. The video catalogs characteristics of a game-changing libero, serve-receive positioning principles, and a menu of ball-control, passing, defense, and setting drills used to train them.

## Key claims / ideas

- A great libero ranks alongside a great setter as the two most critical positional hires on a team.
- Core libero attributes: platform / ball handling, reaction time, consistency, second-ball setting, serve toughness, leadership, resilience.
- Libero training must explicitly cover serve-receive reading and where to position the libero on the court within the formation.
- Libero development requires reps across ball-control, passing, defensive, and setting categories — not just passing.
- Leadership is a trained skill for the libero, not an incidental one.

## Topics covered

- Libero attributes & hiring rubric
- Serve-receive formations & positioning decisions
- Ball-control drills, passing drills, individual-defense drills, setting drills
- Second-ball setting by libero
- Leadership of back-row unit by libero
