# 2022 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia tournament reference for the 20th FIVB Men's Volleyball World Championship — August 26 – September 11, 2022, jointly hosted by Poland and Slovenia after Russia lost hosting rights following its February 2022 invasion of Ukraine. **Italy defeated defending champion Poland 3–1 in the final** for Italy's fourth World Championship title. Polish head coach **Nikola Grbić** (appointed January 12, 2022) earned the silver in his first major Polish tournament after replacing Vital Heynen. Italian head coach Ferdinando De Giorgi reciprocally won his first major Italy result. Poland captured **four All-Star nominations** (Semeniuk best OH, Bieniek co-best MB, Kurek best opposite) plus tournament participation honors.

## Key claims / ideas

- Tournament dates: August 26 – September 11, 2022; co-hosts Poland + Slovenia.
- Russia hosting rights revoked due to Ukraine invasion.
- Three arenas: Spodek (Katowice), Gliwice Arena (Gliwice, Poland), Arena Stožice (Ljubljana, Slovenia).
- Format: 24 teams, six preliminary pools, top 2 + four best 3rd-place into knockout.
- **Final result: Italy def. Poland 3–1 — Italy's fourth World Championship title; Polish silver as defending champions.**
- Medal podium: Gold Italy (HC Ferdinando De Giorgi); Silver Poland (HC Nikola Grbić, his first major Polish tournament); Bronze Brazil; 4th Slovenia.
- Tournament MVP: **Simone Giannelli** (Italy) — first setter to win Worlds MVP since Vyacheslav Zaytsev in 1982.
- All-star team: Best Setter Giannelli (Italy); Best OHs Yoandy Leal (Brazil) + Kamil Semeniuk (Poland); Best MBs Mateusz Bieniek (Poland) + Gianluca Galassi (Italy); Best Opposite Bartosz Kurek (Poland); Best Libero Fabio Balaso (Italy).
- 194,612 total spectators across 52 matches; ~3,743 per-match average.
- **Coaching note**: Grbić's first major tournament as Polish head coach yielded silver — replacing Heynen (who departed after Tokyo 2020).
