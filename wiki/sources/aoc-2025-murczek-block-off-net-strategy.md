# Blocking training: Strategy for off-the-net sets

## Summary
Emporia State coach Ken Murczek teaches that when the opponent's pass goes 10+ feet off the net, the middle blocker should cheat toward the left-side attacker because a heavy swing is unlikely and the left-side gets the most sets.

## Key claims / ideas
- Middle blockers should cheat left when the opponent passes 10 feet or more off the net.
- Off-the-net sets limit opponents' ability to get a heavy swing.
- Cheating on bad passes creates a better chance for a solid two-person block.
- Statistically the left-side player gets set the most, absent an experienced setter or strong opposite.

## Topics covered
- Middle blocker positioning on bad opponent passes.
- Out-of-system blocking strategy.
- Two-person block priority versus left-side attackers.
