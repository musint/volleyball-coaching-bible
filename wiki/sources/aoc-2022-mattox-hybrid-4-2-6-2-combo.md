# Hybrid systems: 4-2 and 6-2 combo

## Summary
Tod Mattox (The Bishop's School) presents a 4-2/6-2 hybrid as a bridge system for young teams: 4-2 with front-row setter for serve-receive (covers inconsistent passing with 3 passers), flip to 6-2 on free balls (3 front-row hitters), and gradually layer in more back-row-setter situations as the team matures.

## Key claims / ideas
- Default to 4-2 with a front-row setter in serve-receive — accommodates inconsistent young passers with 3 back-row passers.
- Front-row setter can knock a tight ball over to keep a play going.
- When the team routinely gets free balls, switch to 6-2 (back-row setter) for free-ball offense → three front-row hitters.
- Progression plan: start with free-ball-only 6-2, then layer on other transition situations.
- Drill: 6v6 with front-row setter start; on opponent free ball, setter comes out of back row, offense flexes into 6-2.
