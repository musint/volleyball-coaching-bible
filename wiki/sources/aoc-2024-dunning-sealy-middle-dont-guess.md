# Middle blocker training: Don't guess where the set is going

## Summary
Dunning and Sealy correct a common middle-blocker fault — guessing and stepping the wrong way. Middles should wait, watch the setter, and see the set before moving; they have more time than they think.

## Key claims / ideas
- Wrong-direction lateral steps are the most common middle-blocker mistake.
- Fix: don't guess, wait and watch the setter set the ball.
- Middles have a split second longer than they think (Sealy).
- Better to go late during training than early and wrong-way.
- Patience is programmable via intentional repetition.

## Topics covered
- Middle blocker reading and eye work.
- Fixing the wrong-direction lateral step fault.
- Blocking patience and timing.
