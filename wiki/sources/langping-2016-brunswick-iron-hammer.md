# The Iron Hammer — Lang Ping (Brunswick Review)

## Summary
A reflective profile of Lang Ping (郎平) — "The Iron Hammer" — built around her Rio 2016 gold-medal run as China Women's head coach. The piece traces how Lang balances traditional "volleyball spirit" with modern scientific training: "Spirit alone will not bring victories. We also need scientific training and management systems." It also covers her cross-cultural coaching arc — China, University of New Mexico, USA Women's National Team (2005–2008, silver at Beijing), Italy, Turkey, and back to China — and argues that her durability comes from adapting motivation to culture while keeping a three-pillar philosophy: teamwork, perseverance, daring to win despite adversity.

Lang became the first person in volleyball history (male or female) to win Olympic gold as both player (1984 LA) and coach (2016 Rio). In Rio, after the Netherlands semifinal, she told her players to shake off previous results and play "in the moment."

## Key claims / ideas
- Three pillars: teamwork, perseverance, daring to win despite adversity.
- "The power of teamwork is greater than that of personal strengths."
- Spirit + science: tradition alone is insufficient without scientific training.
- Cross-cultural adaptation — motivation must be tuned to the cultural context of the athletes.
- "In the moment" play for elimination-round composure.
