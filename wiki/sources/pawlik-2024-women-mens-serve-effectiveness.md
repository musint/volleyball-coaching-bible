# Pawlik, Bobula & Mroczek 2024 — Polish Tauron Liga + PlusLiga Serve-Effectiveness Census

## Summary

A full-season match-analysis study of elite Polish pro volleyball: 314 matches (132 women's Tauron Liga + 182 men's PlusLiga), 53,513 serves, 2021/22 season. The study extracted serve-type distribution, serve-outcome percentages (ace / positive / negative / error), and an overall serve-effectiveness index for each gender — providing the strongest available citable evidence on how the women's game and men's game differ in serve selection and serve outcomes at the pro level.

The headline pattern: **the women's pro game has consolidated around the jump-float as the workhorse (86%) with jump-topspin as a deliberate change-up (12%); the men's pro game inverts this — jump-topspin as default (67%), jump-float secondary (~25%).** Women's overall serve-effectiveness exceeds men's (22% vs. 15%), driven primarily by lower error rates (12% vs. 21%) and a better ace-to-error ratio (0.498 vs. 0.358). The serve-game asymmetry is one of the most decisive structural differences between elite women's and men's indoor volleyball.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Serve-type distribution (women's elite pro):** jump-float 86%, jump-topspin 12%, standing/spot 2%.
- **Serve-type distribution (men's elite pro):** jump-topspin 67%, jump-float ~25%, fakes/standing remainder.
- **Serve-effectiveness index (women): 22%** vs. **men: 15%** — women's serves more effective on this composite.
- **Ace-to-error ratio:** women 0.498, men 0.358 — women trade peak velocity for far better risk-management discipline.
- **Error rate:** women 12%, men 21% — the structural gender gap in serve-error tolerance.
- The women's-game serve is a deliberate non-decisive disruptor, not an ace-rate maximizer; the decisive women's-game lever is attack after first-touch, not the serve itself.

## Topics covered

- Serve-type distribution (jump-float, jump-topspin, standing) by gender at elite pro level.
- Serve-outcome statistics (ace, positive, negative, error) by gender.
- Ace-to-error ratio and overall serve-effectiveness comparison between women's and men's pro game.
- Set-by-set match analysis on full-season Polish pro data.
