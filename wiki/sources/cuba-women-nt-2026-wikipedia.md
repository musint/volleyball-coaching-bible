# Cuba women's national volleyball team — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia reference page for the program George and Perdomo built — full Olympic, World Championship, World Cup, NORCECA and Pan American results, plus a chronological coach list (George 1976–1996; Perdomo 1998–2007; Calderón 2000–2006; Fernández 2018–present) and the team nickname **"Las Espectaculares Morenas del Caribe"** ("The Spectacular Caribbean Girls"). The page also documents the **eight-tournament winning streak** from 1991 to 2000 — World Cup '91, Olympics '92, Worlds '94, World Cup '95, Olympics '96, Worlds '98, World Cup '99, Olympics 2000 — and notes Cuba "was the first team to break the domination of the USSR and Japan in the world women's volleyball" with the 1978 World Championship.

Used on [[eugenio-george]] as the dynasty-era result anchor, on [[antonio-perdomo]] as the head-coach period anchor (1998–2007), and on [[cuban-school]] for the team-history backbone.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Olympic golds: 1992, 1996, 2000; bronze 2004; 4th 2008.**
- **World Championship golds: 1978, 1994, 1998; silver 1986.**
- **World Cup golds: 1989, 1991, 1995, 1999.**
- **Eight-tournament FIVB winning streak 1991–2000.**
- Team nickname "Las Espectaculares Morenas del Caribe."
- Coaching timeline:
  - **Eugenio George Lafita 1976–1996.**
  - **Antonio Perdomo 1998–2007.**
  - **Luis Felipe Calderón 2000–2006** (overlap with Perdomo).
  - Tomás Fernández 2018–present.
- 1978 WC win = "first team to break the domination of the USSR and Japan."
- Pan American Games: 8 golds (1971–2007).
- 1992 Olympic Gold roster: Mireya Luis, Regla Torres, Regla Bell, Idalmis Gato, Lilia Izquierdo.
- 1998–2000 dynasty roster adds Marlenys Costa, Mirka Francia, Yumilka Ruíz, Martha Sánchez.
