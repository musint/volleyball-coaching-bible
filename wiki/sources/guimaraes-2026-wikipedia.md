# José Roberto Guimarães — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Reference-level Wikipedia biography of **José Roberto Guimarães** (b. 31 July 1954, Quintana, São Paulo) — one of only a handful of head coaches to have won Olympic gold with both a men's and a women's national program. Article documents a playing career as a setter (1967–1988, including the 1976 Montréal Olympics), a Brazil men's NT coaching stint 1992–1996 that delivered Brazil's **first-ever Olympic men's gold (Barcelona 1992)**, Brazilian and international club assignments through 2003, and a Brazil women's NT tenure from 2003/2004 to present that produced back-to-back Olympic golds (Beijing 2008, London 2012), plus Tokyo 2020 silver and Paris 2024 bronze.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Olympic gold count as coach:** 3 — 1992 men's (Barcelona), 2008 women's (Beijing), 2012 women's (London). Additional Olympic medals: silver 2020 women's, bronze 2024 women's.
- **1992 Barcelona gold:** Brazil's first-ever Olympic men's volleyball gold medal, coached by Guimarães.
- **Playing career lineage:** played as setter at 1976 Montréal Olympics; club career across 8+ Brazilian sides (1967–1988).
- **Dual-program Olympic gold:** Guimarães is one of few head coaches to deliver Olympic gold with both a men's and a women's program representing the same country. (The [[bernardinho]] page notes Bernardinho as the only head coach with Olympic medals across both programs — Guimarães does not fit that specific framing because his medal set is gold-and-bronze-and-silver across programs, but the article positions Guimarães as a dual-program Olympic coach.)
- **International club work:** Scavolini Pesaro (Italy) 2006–2009; Fenerbahçe (Turkey) 2010–2012 — both women's assignments concurrent with Brazil women's NT duties.
- **Adhemar Ferreira da Silva Trophy (2024):** Brazilian sport lifetime-achievement honor — making Guimarães and Bernardinho two of the rare volleyball coaches to receive that award (Bernardinho received it in 2016).
- **Women's program ceiling:** four World Championship silvers under his tenure (2006, 2010, 2022; 2022 post-Tokyo) and two bronzes (2014, 2024). No World Championship gold for the program ever.
