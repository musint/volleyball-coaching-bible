# Passing formations

## Summary
AOC primer on passing formations: three-passer back-row is the default, responsibilities must be pre-assigned, passers communicate on short/deep and seams every serve, and two higher-level techniques are pinching (weaker passer gets minimal area) and shifting (decoy and fill at toss).

## Key claims / ideas
- Three back-row passers is the most common configuration.
- Passing responsibilities must be set before games, not improvised.
- Communication is per-serve: who has short, who has deep, who shades for seams.
- Pinching: cover more court with two passers when the third is weaker.
- Shifting: players start in one formation and move as the server tosses to deceive.

## Topics covered
- Basic passing formations
- Pinching and shifting
- Passer communication protocols
