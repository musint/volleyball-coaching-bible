# How to run a slower middle attack

## Summary
Tod Mattox (Bishop's) runs a slower middle attack by setting antenna-high just past center: a "push" from the middle of the net or a "back" from the shank-left position — but the set always lands in the same fixed spot left of center. Works with 2- or 3-hitter formations. Preferred middle shots: cutback (hard Zone 1), power (hard Zone 5), recycle (tip-and-cover), plus lift tips, tools, and roll shots.

## Key claims / ideas
- A slower middle attack sets antenna-high just past center for a Zone-1 finish.
- Nomenclature: "push" from middle-net setter; "back" from shank-left — same landing spot.
- Works in 2- or 3-hitter formations.
- Preferred shots: cutback to Zone 1, power to Zone 5, recycle tip into the block and cover, plus lift tips, tools, roll shots.
