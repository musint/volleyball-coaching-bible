# Brazil men's national volleyball team — Wikipedia article

## Summary

Reference-level Wikipedia article on the Brazil men's national volleyball team — the *Canarinhos* — the most-titled national men's volleyball program in FIVB history with **69 total titles** across main FIVB-sanctioned competitions. The article frames Brazil as a three-Olympic-gold program (1992 Barcelona, 2004 Athens, 2016 Rio) with three additional silvers (1984 LA, 2008 Beijing, 2012 London), three FIVB World Championship titles (2002, 2006, 2010), nine World League golds, three World Cup golds, and five World Grand Champions Cups. The 2001–2017 Bernardinho era is characterized as the dominant period, with a 2006 assessment from unnamed commentators calling the team "the best and most consistent men's volleyball team of all time."

## Key claims / ideas

- **Medal taxonomy.** Olympic golds 1992, 2004, 2016. Silvers 1984, 2008, 2012. World Championship golds 2002, 2006, 2010 (all under Bernardinho). Nine World League golds (1993, 2001, 2003–2007, 2009, 2010). Three World Cup golds (2003, 2007, 2019). Five World Grand Champions Cups (1997, 2005, 2009, 2013, 2017).
- **Bernardinho as transformative figure.** Appointed after the Sydney 2000 6th-place finish; "spent six years coaching the women's national team" prior; delivered three golds in his first year (2001 World League, America's Cup, South American Championship).
- **2002 World Championship.** Won "for the first time ever."
- **2003 World Cup.** Described as "the last title it had not won yet."
- **All-time-best framing.** "This team was considered by some commentators [as] the best and most consistent men's volleyball team of all time" — 2006 assessment.
- **Most-titled program framing.** "Brazil is the national volleyball team that has won the most titles overall in the main FIVB sanctioned competitions."
- **Nickname and cultural identity.** *Canarinhos* ("Saffron finches"), alternate monikers "Galactic" and "Best of All Times."
- **Current state.** FIVB ranking 3rd (as of October 5, 2025); Bernardo Rezende reappointed as head coach after 2023 return.
- **Post-peak adjustment.** Tokyo 2020 4th; 2025 Nations League bronze.
