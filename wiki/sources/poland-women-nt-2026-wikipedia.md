# Poland Women's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia results-and-history reference for the Poland women's national volleyball team — governed by [[pzps-poland]], the same federation that administers the [[polish-school]]'s men's-NT program. The program's medal record splits cleanly between two compressed peaks: a Soviet-era 1950s-1960s peak (1952 World Championship silver, 1956 + 1962 Worlds bronze, 1964 Tokyo + 1968 Mexico City Olympic bronzes) and a modern Lavarini-era resurgence (2003 + 2005 European Championship golds, plus three consecutive Nations League bronzes 2023-2024-2025 under [[stefano-lavarini]]). The intervening 1970-2008 period is documented as a long competitive trough — only one Olympic appearance in 40 years (Beijing 2008, 9th, 1-4) and no global medal between the 1968 Olympic bronze and the 2003 European gold. Wikipedia frames the modern peak with Lavarini's 2022-present tenure as the program's most sustained competitive era since the 1960s; FIVB ranking #3 as of June 29, 2025 is the highest in program history.

## Key claims / ideas

- Olympic record: 4 appearances (1964 Tokyo, 1968 Mexico City, 2008 Beijing, 2024 Paris); 2 bronzes (1964, 1968); 2024 Paris quarterfinals 6th-place exit (2-2, 6-7 sets) under Lavarini.
- World Championship: 1952 silver, 1956 + 1962 bronzes — Soviet-era peak. No global medal post-1962 in 60+ years.
- European Championship: 2 golds (2003 Turkey, 2005 Croatia) under Andrzej Niemczyk's second tenure (2003-2006); plus 4 silvers (1950, 1951, 1963, 1967) and bronzes including 2009 home; 29 of 34 editions appeared.
- Modern era under Lavarini: 3 consecutive Nations League bronzes (2023, 2024, 2025); 2024 Paris Olympic 6th-place is the team's first Olympic appearance since 2008.
- FIVB World Cup: only 2 appearances (2003 8th, 2007 6th) of 14 editions — peripheral to the program's history.
- 2024 Paris squad core: captain-setter Joanna Wołosz, opposite Magdalena Stysiak, MBs Agnieszka Korneluk + Klaudia Alagierska + Magdalena Jurczyk, OHs Martyna Łukasik + Natalia Mędrzyk + Martyna Czyrniańska, libero Maria Stenzel.
- Coaching history is well-documented chronologically: Andrzej Niemczyk (1975-1978, 2003-2006) is the key 2000s name; Marco Bonitta (Italy, 2007-2008) was an earlier Italian-import experiment; Jacek Nawrocki (2015-2021) preceded the Lavarini hire; Stefano Lavarini (Italy, 2022-present) is the current modernization driver.
- The Lavarini hire mirrors the imported-Italian-HC pattern PZPS used on the men's side under Andrea Anastasi (2011-2013) — same federation, same playbook.
- The 2003 + 2005 European golds came back-to-back under Niemczyk, but Poland did NOT defend in 2007, 2009, or any subsequent Euro cycle — a single peak rather than a sustained Euro dynasty.
