# Defending the first swing: Rotation 3

## Summary
Sealy and Rockwell work through Rotation 3, where the opposing offense runs a hut, a 31 (quick behind the setter), and a go (fast outside). This triple threat overloads the right-side blockers, and the piece walks through three MB-shading options to contain it.

## Key claims / ideas
- Opponent's attack set in R3 for this breakdown: hut (outside), 31 (quick behind setter), go (fast outside) — designed to overload the blocking team on the right side.
- Three blocker options:
  - (1) MB shades toward the right-side pin if the 31 and go are bigger threats than the hut; RS blocker stays ready for the go but can step in to help with the 31.
  - (2) If the 31 is not a big threat, MB shades toward the left-side pin for the red.
  - (3) If the offense overloads the right side, the setter can intentionally create a one-on-one by pushing the ball to the hut attacker — i.e., exploit the overload's weakness.
