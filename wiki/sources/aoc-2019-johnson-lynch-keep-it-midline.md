## Summary

Christy Johnson-Lynch (Iowa State) teaches that passers should face the server, angle their arms toward the setter, and shuffle laterally to keep the ball at their body midline for any pass type (dig, serve-receive, free ball).

## Key claims / ideas

- Midline body position at contact is a teachable, universal passing principle.
- Shuffle footwork (not cross-over or reach) is the movement pattern that makes midline possible.
- Shuffle-to-target should be warmed up at every practice and pre-match.

## Topics covered

Midline passing mechanics; shuffle footwork; warmup design for passing.
