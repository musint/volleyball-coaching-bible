## Summary

This is AOC curricular content; defer to qualified AT/CSCS/MD on programming specifics. John Dunning (Stanford '01-'16) admits he didn't count his players' practice jumps for years, then instituted hard per-player jump caps after recognizing the health/performance cost. Terry Liskevych recommends 90-120 minute practices with reductions to ~60 minutes late in season. Liskevych's slogan: "Rest is best."

## Key claims / ideas

- Jump counting per player is essential for volleyball injury management — Dunning adopted this mid-career after previously not tracking.
- When a player hits her jump limit, she stops practicing — no negotiation.
- Practice duration target: 90 minutes to 2 hours (Liskevych).
- Late-season practices should shrink — an hour may be enough as the season nears its end.
- Liskevych mantra: "Rest is best."

## Topics covered

- Jump counting / jump caps
- Practice duration by season phase
- Rest and recovery in season planning
- Individual-level load management
