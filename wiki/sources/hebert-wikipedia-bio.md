# Mike Hebert (Wikipedia bio)

## Summary
Wikipedia's biography of Mike Hebert (Jan 7, 1944 – Oct 21, 2019), American NCAA women's volleyball head coach. Provides the most complete public accounting of his year-by-year career dates, school-by-school records, honors, and USA Volleyball assignments. Tier 2 because it is an encyclopedic summary rather than a primary publisher bio, but it aggregates the same facts carried on the Illinois HOF page ([[hebert-illinois-hof]]) and the USAV obituary ([[hebert-usav-obituary]]) — both Tier 1 — and is internally consistent with them on dates/records.

## Key claims / ideas
- Pittsburgh tenure 1976–1979 (128–53 women, 60–21 men); New Mexico 1980–1982 (60–57); Illinois 1983–1995 (291–133, 144–62 Big Ten, four Big Ten titles, two NCAA Final Fours); Minnesota 1996–2010 (381–137, 173–76 Big Ten, 11 NCAA Tournament appearances).
- AVCA Hall of Fame inducted 2006; USAV Donald S. Shondell All-Time Great Coach 2011; University of Illinois Athletics Hall of Fame 2023 (posthumous).
- AVCA Coach of the Year 1985; Big Ten Coach of the Year 1986, 1988 (Illinois), 1999, 2002 (Minnesota); Volleyball Magazine National Coach of the Year 2003.
- Career total 952–392 across 35 head-coaching seasons.
