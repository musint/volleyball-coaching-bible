# Organizing serve receive

## Summary
Jim Stone frames serve-receive pattern design as a two-lever optimization: the best passers should cover the most court, and the best hitters should be freed up to attack. He shows pattern shifts for teams that lean on a strong outside versus a strong right side.

## Key claims / ideas
- Two guiding principles: max area to best passers; minimal receive burden on best attackers.
- Strong outside → shift reception right; outside covers a small area, stays free to attack.
- Strong right side → outside takes more coverage, right side takes a sliver and stays free to hit.
- Patterns should be tailored to the personnel, not adopted off the shelf.

## Topics covered
- Serve-receive pattern design
- Passer / hitter role tradeoffs
- Pattern shifts by strongest attacker
