# Learn different formations for strategic serve receive

## Summary
Pitt's Dan Fisher and UNC's Joe Sagula discuss serve-receive formation design — how to match the SR pattern to your team's strengths. Key themes: pre-serve communication, seam responsibilities, depth-based coverage variations, strategic stacking of stronger/weaker passers, and 4-person SR schemes against top-spin servers.

## Key claims / ideas
- Pre-serve communication — particularly seam responsibilities — is essential before each serve (Fisher).
- Passing variation: middle-back deeper with left-back and right-back pinched in → efficient coverage of both deep and short balls.
- Strategic stacking:
  - Stack stronger passers to maximize court coverage.
  - Bunch weaker passers into a smaller area with less room.
  - **Subtlety matters** — a one-step adjustment is preferable to an obvious reshuffle because it protects the mental state of weaker passers (Fisher).
- Four-person SR scheme introduced by Sagula as a defensive strategy against strong top-spin servers.
- Video-backed (Vimeo/AOC Premium) — two-coach dialog.

- Serve-receive formation design
- Pre-serve communication and seam assignments
- 3-passer vs. 4-passer SR schemes
- Passer stacking strategy and its psychological considerations
- Coverage patterns for deep and short balls
