# Kim Yeon-koung — Wikipedia Biography

## Summary
Wikipedia biography for Kim Yeon-koung (born 1988-02-26, 192 cm), South Korea's most decorated women's-volleyball player and the long-tenured captain through Tokyo 2020. Anchors the player figure most associated with the modern Korean school: 2012 Olympic MVP (first non-medalist Olympic MVP in volleyball history; 207-point Olympic scoring record), career trajectory across Korean / Japanese / Turkish / Chinese leagues, and direct coaching relationship with Stefano Lavarini at both Eczacıbaşı club and Korean NT.

## Key claims / ideas
- 2012 London Olympic MVP — **first non-medalist OMVP in volleyball history**; Olympic scoring record at **207 points**
- Career club path: Heungkuk Life → JT Marvelous (Japan) → Fenerbahçe → Shanghai → Eczacıbaşı → Heungkuk → Shanghai → Heungkuk Pink Spiders
- 2011-12 CEV Champions League MVP (first Asian player)
- 2014 Asian Games gold; multiple Asian Championship best-OH selections (2015/17/19)
- Korean NT career 2005-2021; captain through Tokyo 2020
- Coached by Stefano Lavarini at both Eczacıbaşı (club) and Korea (NT)
- Style profile preserves the cited descriptor "Russian physical strength, American power, Korean technique, and Brazilian agility" — illustrative of a player who functions as a precision-and-power outside hitter inside the Korean tradition's structural-height context
