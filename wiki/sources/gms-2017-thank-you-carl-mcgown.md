# Thank You Carl McGown

## Summary
GMS's tribute page for Carl McGown compiles testimonials from coaches he
mentored and from his family (sons Chris and Paul McGown, wife Susan). The
page articulates the methodological commitments that underpin the GMS school:
evidence-based coaching grounded in motor-learning science, specificity in
skill training, and the rejection of unproven constructs such as "general
athletic ability."

The tribute is useful as a primary statement of what GMS itself characterizes
as the McGown legacy — particularly its organizing principle that coaching
has a correct form that is empirically determinable, and its ethic of direct
feedback delivered with genuine care for the learner.

## Key claims / ideas
- Stated McGown principle: "First we have to teach them how to move. Then, we
  need to teach them how to see."
- Data-driven / principle-based approach to coaching, not opinion-based.
- Skeptical stance on "general athletic ability"; advocacy of specificity.
- Influence cited: University of Portland blocking program; AVCA 2010 whole-
  versus-part training publication; international clinic work.
- Teaching style: direct feedback, high expectations, accessibility to
  coaches at every level.

## Topics covered
- McGown's motor-learning-grounded coaching philosophy.
- Specificity and skill-transfer beliefs.
- Mentorship ethic within the GMS school.
- Organizational legacy claims (publications, international reach).
