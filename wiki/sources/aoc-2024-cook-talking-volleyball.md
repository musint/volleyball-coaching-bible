# Talking volleyball with John Cook (AOC, 2024)

## Summary
Don Patterson interviews John Cook for Art of Coaching Volleyball, published August 22, 2024 — the season before Cook's retirement. Cook covers the 2023 stadium-attendance record, his fundamentals-first training approach, the rationale for indoor-team beach training (January–March), serving as the most important practice drill, his agreement with assistant Tyler Hildebrand on arm-swing teaching for juniors, his caution on tight-set training for younger players (citing Lindsay Krause's lost season), Big Ten conference dominance, the new pro-league landscape, and advice for up-and-coming coaches. The interview is the strongest single capture of Cook's training-philosophy voice in this wiki's corpus.

## Key claims / ideas
- The Volleyball Day in Nebraska match (92,003 attendance) "came out of competitive fire" after Wisconsin broke Nebraska's prior attendance record at Milwaukee's NBA arena.
- Cook's training foundation comes from football, specifically watching Tom Osborne practices at Nebraska (1988–90 as volleyball assistant): "Football is all about fundamentals... they don't scrimmage for three hours."
- 1996 Russia tour with Wisconsin taught Cook that fundamentals erode without daily work: "Fundamentals have to be worked on every day. If you don't, they're going to go away."
- Cook will rebuild a college player's mechanics from the ground up: "We're going to reinvent your game, and we're going to start with: this is where you put your right foot, this is where you put your left foot, this is where you put your platform."
- Cook draws teaching analogies from learning guitar, flying, team roping — all required formal instruction and a step-by-step sequence; volleyball coaching is the same.
- **Indoor team plays beach from January through end of March every year.** Rationale: middle blockers/liberos get to do everything, athletes need variety, Brazil model requires all youth to do all skills, Karch Kiraly proved the cross-training value (gold medals in indoor and beach).
- Cook trains every player to set every day: "At some point, everybody is going to have to set a ball in a match."
- Conditioning advice: best Nebraska players have been multi-sport HS athletes; volleyball produces lateral imbalance (100 swings one arm, 0 the other) so balance work and varied conditioning matter.
- **"I tell our players every day that the most important drill we will be doing is serving."** Multiple serving bursts per practice. Serving cost Nebraska the 2023 NCAA final vs Texas (3–0 loss).
- Arm-swing teaching: agrees with Hildebrand against high-elbow emphasis at juniors. "If you go to Brazil, everybody has the same arm swing. They're teaching all the kids to do it the same way."
- Shoulder impingement frequency in juniors traced to elbow above shoulder in arm swing: "You don't see baseball players do that. You don't see quarterbacks do that in football."
- Cook is wary of training tight sets in juniors after Lindsay Krause's lost season from a tight-play ankle sprain.
- Big Ten dominance: every program made commitment to volleyball investment by 2021; Big Ten Network TV deal critical; coach-accountability standards rose.
- Up-and-coming coach advice: be around great coaches (Cook called Charlie Brande, watched Liskevych and Dunphy in San Diego, ran men's-team scrimmages against Liskevych's USA team); be a lifelong learner.
