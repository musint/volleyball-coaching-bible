# John Cook — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
The Wikipedia biography of John Cook compiles his career arc from Francis Parker School (San Diego, 1981–88) and UC San Diego assistant work into a 25-season Nebraska head-coaching tenure (2000–2024) [cook-wikipedia-bio]. The page enumerates his **four NCAA national championships** (2000, 2006, 2015, 2017), four runner-up finishes (2005, 2018, 2021, 2023), three AVCA National Coach of the Year awards (2000, 2005, 2023), 2017 AVCA Hall of Fame induction, the 2008 USA Volleyball All-Time Great Coach Award, and the 2023 "Volleyball Day in Nebraska" Memorial Stadium match that set a 92,003-attendance world record for women's sports [cook-wikipedia-bio]. It also documents his earlier Wisconsin head-coaching tenure (1992–98, 161–73), 1995 NIVC title, and 1997 Big Ten Co-Coach of the Year award. Cook retired in January 2025 and helped select successor Dani Busboom Kelly. The page serves as a one-stop secondary anchor for Cook's award and championship record.

## Key claims / ideas
- Cook was born April 19, 1956, Chula Vista, CA; BA history (USD 1979); MA teaching/coaching effectiveness (San Diego State 1991).
- Total career head-coaching record: **883–176 (.834)**.
- Nebraska tenure 2000–2024: **722–103 (.875)** over 25 seasons.
- **Four NCAA national championships at Nebraska:** 2000 (34–0), 2006 (33–1), 2015 (32–4), 2017 (32–4).
- Four NCAA runner-up finishes: 2005, 2018, 2021, 2023.
- 8 NCAA championship-final appearances; 12 NCAA semifinal appearances; 23 top-10 finishes; 66 All-Americans coached.
- Big 12 conference titles (Nebraska): 2000–02, 2004–08, 2010 (9 total).
- Big Ten conference titles: 1997 (Wisconsin), 2011, 2016, 2017, 2023, 2024 (6 total).
- **Volleyball Day in Nebraska (2023)** at Memorial Stadium drew **92,003** spectators — world record for a women's sports event.
- 3x AVCA National Coach of the Year (2000, 2005, 2023).
- AVCA Hall of Fame (2017); USA Volleyball All-Time Great Coach Award (2008).
- Pre-Nebraska HC: Francis Parker School (162–18 over six seasons, two state titles, 90-match win streak); Wisconsin (161–73; 1995 NIVC champions; 1997 Big Ten Co-COY).
- Retired January 2025; Dani Busboom Kelly succeeded him.
