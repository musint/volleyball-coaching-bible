# Wave: A fast-paced serve receive drill

## Summary
Karin Keeney (Hebron HS) runs "Wave" — teams of 3, two minutes, race to 10 good passes to a setter target. Constraint: servers can't hit the same spot twice or same passer twice in a row. Overpass or shank resets team score to zero.

## Key claims / ideas
- Four teams of 3 — two serve, two receive, on opposite sides of net
- Servers can't hit same court spot twice (penalty: burpees/pushups)
- Servers can't serve the same passer twice in a row — builds serve accuracy
- Target player/coach at net counts to 10 good passes
- Shank or overpass resets score to zero
- After 3 good passes, passers rotate positions within their own 3-person line (L→R, R→M, M→L)
- Two-minute timer; miss the 10 = sprints
- Every player passes (setters, middles included) — cultivate all-around skills
- Fast = fun: engagement via rapid-fire serves + competitive race

## Topics covered
- Wave drill mechanics
- Serving accuracy training via constraints
- Position rotation within SR line
- Everyone-passes philosophy
