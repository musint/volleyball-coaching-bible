# Arm swing reading drill: Back, bent, straight

## Summary
Diane Flick-Williams (Western Washington) shares a reading drill in which defenders call out "back," "bent," or "straight" based on the hitter's arm swing, tying each visual cue to a predicted shot type before digging.

## Key claims / ideas
- Arm-back swing signals an aggressive hard-driven swing.
- Bent elbow signals a roll shot.
- Straight arm signals an off-speed shot.
- Verbalizing the read ("back/bent/straight") forces defenders to commit eye work to the hitter's arm before playing the ball.
- Setup: middle and left-back defenders; a hitter feeds continuous balls so defenders call the cue and dig.

- Defense drill with verbal read prompts
