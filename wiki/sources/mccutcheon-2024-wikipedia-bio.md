# Hugh McCutcheon — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Biographical and career reference covering McCutcheon's trajectory from New Zealand junior national team player, through BYU (NCAA titles 1999, 2001 as assistant under Carl McGown), Vienna Hotvolleys, USA Men's head coach (2005–2008, Beijing gold), USA Women's head coach (2009–2012, London silver + three Grand Prix), University of Minnesota (2012–2022, three Final Fours, 277–74), to his post-coaching roles as Minnesota Assistant AD (2023) and FIVB Secretary General (elected Nov 2024).

Also captures the Beijing 2008 family tragedy (father-in-law Todd Bachman killed, mother-in-law Barbara seriously injured at Drum Tower), which shaped the narrative of his gold-medal run. Lists Halls of Fame (International Volleyball 2018, USAV 2024, AVCA 2024 — the "HOF trifecta") and summarizes his stated philosophy: athlete-centered, holistic, questioning the all-powerful coach.

## Key claims / ideas

- Combined head-coaching record: 490–146 (.770) across Minnesota, USA Men, USA Women.
- Only the second coach in history to medal with both the men's and women's sides (per volleyhall.org cross-reference).
- Minnesota program: highest winning percentage in program history (.789).
- Secretary General of FIVB as of November 2024.
- MNZM (Member of the New Zealand Order of Merit) 2016 for services to volleyball.
