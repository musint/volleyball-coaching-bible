# Partner passing in volleyball – tips and fundamentals

## Summary
Cary Wendell Wallin (former USAV national team, Stanford NCAA Player of the Year) teaches partner passing fundamentals with specific keys for short serves (drop body, raise platform) and deep serves (shuffle back, hunch shoulders, lower platform angle, pivot-side platform if over the head).

## Key claims / ideas
- Terry Liskevych quote: "In my 50-plus years coaching, the importance of passing has never changed... Passing determines the level you play on."
- Young players need lots of touches; partner passing = tons of reps in a few minutes
- Three drill emphases: solid platform, fundamental footwork, handling serves not hit at you
- "Feet to the ball" cue — get ball to midline
- Short-serve keys: drop lower body, raise platform
- Deep-serve keys: shuffle back, hunch shoulders, lower platform angle; if can't get back, pivot and create a side platform

- Partner passing drill design
- Short vs. deep serve response
- Platform management per serve depth
- Beginner passing fundamentals
- Underhand toss as teaching tool
