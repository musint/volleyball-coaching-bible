# 2014 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia tournament reference for the 18th FIVB Men's Volleyball World Championship — August 30 to September 21, 2014, hosted by Poland across seven cities (Warsaw, Wrocław, Katowice, Gdańsk, Kraków, Łódź, Bydgoszcz). **Poland defeated Brazil 3-1 in the final under head coach Stéphane Antiga**, winning their second World Championship title and first in 40 years. Poland became "the third team in the history of the competition to be crowned as champions in their own country" (after the Soviet Union and Czechoslovakia). Mariusz Wlazły won tournament MVP and Best Opposite. The championship drew **563,263 fans across the 18-day event, smashing the previous attendance records** in the competition's history — a data point that anchors the Polish-volleyball-as-national-priority claim.

## Key claims / ideas

- Tournament dates: August 30 - September 21, 2014; host: Poland (7 cities).
- **Final result:** Poland def. Brazil 3-1; Poland's second World Championship title, first in 40 years (since 1974).
- Poland the third nation to win the men's Worlds at home (after USSR and Czechoslovakia).
- Tournament MVP: **Mariusz Wlazły** (Poland) — also Best Opposite.
- Best Middle Blocker (co-): **Karol Kłos** (Poland).
- Other awards: Best Setter Lukas Kampa (Germany); Best OHs Ricardo Lucarelli + Murilo Endres (Brazil); Best Libero Jenia Grebennikov (France).
- Bronze: Germany (HC Vital Heynen — pre-Polish appointment).
- 4th: France.
- **Attendance record:** 563,263 fans across 18-day event — single-tournament attendance record in competition history.
