## Summary

Coach Connection LIVE session with Garrett Minyard (Northeast Volleyball Club) and Mike Garcia (Austin Junior Volleyball) on organizing and running effective tryouts at a competitive club. Covers tryout length, court density, evaluator selection, skill-vs-scrimmage split, offer communication, and follow-up tracking.

## Key claims / ideas

- Tryouts are one of the highest-leverage decisions of the club season.
- Court density (number of players per court) has a direct effect on evaluation quality.
- Who evaluates — head coach alone vs external evaluators — is a structural tradeoff worth naming.
- Split between small-group skills and scrimmage time is tunable; both are needed.
- When a player asks "who else will be on the team?" before accepting an offer, coaches should have a pre-planned answer.
- Early expectation-setting with players and parents prevents midseason conflict.
- Offer tracking and follow-up is a workflow, not a memory task.

## Topics covered

Tryout design; evaluator selection; skill-vs-scrimmage balance; offer communication; expectation setting; promotion of tryouts; parent management.
