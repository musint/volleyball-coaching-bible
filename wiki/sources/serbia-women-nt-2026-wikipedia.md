## Summary

Wikipedia article on the Serbia women's national volleyball team, captured 2026-04-24. The article documents the program built primarily under [[zoran-terzic]] from 2002 onward — 8 gold / 5 silver / 9 bronze in major FIVB and CEV competition, including back-to-back World Championship golds in 2018 and 2022 (the latter undefeated 12-0), 2016 Rio Olympic silver, 2020 Tokyo Olympic bronze, three European Championship golds (2011, 2017, 2019), and three European League golds (2009-2011). Lists rosters from peak cycles, FIVB ranking history, and tournament-by-tournament records.

## Key claims / ideas

- Terzić has been the primary architect of the program since its earliest competitive period — approximately two decades of head-coaching tenure (with a 2022-2024 Russia detour).
- The 2022 World Championship campaign (Netherlands/Poland) is the program's signature: 12 matches, 12-0 record, 36-5 sets — full-tournament dominance.
- The 2018 World Championship gold came on a 13-match, 11-2, 34-10 sets run.
- Olympic Committee of Serbia named the women's volleyball squad "Team of the Year" 13 times between 2006 and 2023.
- Top-10 FIVB rankings sustained across multiple cycles.
- Roster anchors of the peak era: setter Maja Ognjenović (captain), opposite Tijana Bošković, outside Brankica Mihajlović, libero Teodora Pušić.
