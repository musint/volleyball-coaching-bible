# John Dunning's 3-box drill

## Summary
Stanford's John Dunning runs a team drill with three coach-boxes at the net that bundles blocking cutbacks, digger defense, transition, fast middle moves on "Go"/"Hut" cues, and scramble recovery — with an assistant timing the middle's cue-to-hands-over-net so players can benchmark against opponent attack speed.

## Key claims / ideas
- Three boxes at the net for coach-initiated attacks.
- Multiple objectives per rep: block cutback, dig (often setter), transition attack, middle footwork on Go/Hut, scramble recovery.
- Time the middle from verbal cue to hands over the net on the outside.
- Compare that time to the opponent's setter-to-hitter delivery time — gives players a data-driven reason to get faster.
- Session objectives chosen from recent match notes.

## Topics covered
- Stats-informed middle blocker training.
- Multi-purpose team drill design.
- Block → dig → transition flow.
- Go/Hut terminology for quick and outside sets.
