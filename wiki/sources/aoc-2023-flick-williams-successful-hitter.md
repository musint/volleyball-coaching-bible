# What makes a successful hitter?

## Summary
Western Washington's Diane Flick-Williams argues hitting percentage is a cognitive skill, not just a mechanical one — built on role clarity and the habit of "taking inventory" after every swing.

## Key claims / ideas
- Tip 1: Understand current role — 6-rotation passer, pure point-scorer, or decoy — and optimize contribution to that role.
- Tip 2: If you want a bigger role (more sets, passing), commit to learner mode and target the specific skill gaps.
- Tip 3: "Take inventory" after every swing — note which defender dug, which zone was empty, what the other team revealed about their scheme.
- Example inference: if the off blocker dug a cross-court hit, the belly of the court is open.
- Match-long orientation: defense you see in the first five points will shift — keep gathering information for crunch time.

## Topics covered
- Hitter role clarity
- Post-swing defensive read / "taking inventory"
- Role progression through targeted skill work
- Match-long information gathering
