## Summary

Tod Mattox opens practice with serving because "serving is important to us." Players form three short lines on each endline and serve at each other. The featured drill emphasizes short serves inside the 10-foot line. After a minute of reps, the drill becomes a 2-3 player team competition — first team to 5 serves inside the 10-foot line wins. The serve-receive layer can be added by placing passers on each side. The message: if you put serving first, you tell the team serving matters.

## Key claims / ideas

- Opening practice with serving signals its importance and gives extra reps without sacrificing other blocks.
- Short serves (inside the 10-foot line) are a trainable skill worth isolating.
- Small-team (2-3 player) race-to-5 turns pure serving into a competitive warmup.
- Passers can be layered in for a serve-receive variation.
- Warmups reveal priorities: what you put first is what you say matters.
