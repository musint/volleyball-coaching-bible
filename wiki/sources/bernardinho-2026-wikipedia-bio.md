# Bernardo Rezende — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Reference-level biography of **Bernardo Rocha de Rezende** — universally known as **Bernardinho** — born August 25, 1959 in Rio de Janeiro. Establishes two careers: outside hitter / receiver on the Brazil men's NT (1979–1985, silver at Los Angeles 1984 as a player), then the architect of Brazil's modern volleyball era as head coach — first of the Brazil women's NT (1990–2000, bronze in Atlanta 1996 and Sydney 2000), then of the Brazil men's NT (2001–2017), winning Olympic gold in **Athens 2004** and **Rio 2016** plus three FIVB World Championships (2002, 2006, 2010). Returned to the Brazil men's program in 2023 after a 2021–2022 stint with France's men's NT.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Medal count as of 2021:** 48 medals across all national-team competition as coach — the article characterizes Rezende as "the most successful team-sport coach of all time" and as the first and only head coach to win **six Olympic medals** leading national teams.
- **Architect of the Brazilian men's dynasty:** continuous 2001–2017 tenure with the men's NT produced four consecutive Olympic medals (gold 2004, silver 2008, silver 2012, gold 2016) — one of the longest podium streaks in men's volleyball history.
- **Two-program coach:** uniquely coached both Brazil NTs to Olympic medals (women's bronze Atlanta 1996 and Sydney 2000; men's medals 2004/2008/2012/2016).
- **Pedigree as a player:** 1984 Los Angeles Olympic silver as a Brazil men's NT player — the cohort that preceded Brazil's first men's Olympic gold (1992 Barcelona, a team he did not coach).
- **Family:** first wife Vera Mossa (Brazilian NT player); son **Bruno Rezende**, current captain and setter of Brazil men's NT; second marriage to setter Fernanda Venturini (1999–2020).
- **Honors:** International Volleyball Hall of Fame induction (2022); multiple "best coach" awards (2008, 2011, 2012 ESPN / Volleyball Globe / Superliga).
- **Business / philanthropy:** Delirio Tropical restaurant chain (co-founder, 1983); Bodytech fitness chain; Instituto Compartilhar youth-sport NGO; eduK online education.
