## Summary

Russ Rose (Penn State, 1979-2021) taxonomizes three drill types for raising practice competitiveness: player-vs-player (1-on-1 across the net), team offense-vs-defense (with side-out rate targets like 60-70% in 3-hitter rotations), and end-of-game scenarios (tied at 9-9, 11-11, 13-13 to 15).

## Key claims / ideas

- Competitive practice requires structured 1-on-1 exposure so players learn 1v1 scenarios AND the rest of the team learns to "tune in" to help.
- Offense-vs-defense team drills should bake in measurable skill-level goals (side-out percentage by rotation type).
- Specific side-out rate target for Penn State's conference: 60-70% in three-hitter rotations, slightly lower in two-hitter rotations.
- Scenarios worth rehearsing include setter digging first ball, first-ball shank, and serves to a seam.
- End-of-game drills starting from tied scores (9-9, 11-11, 13-13 to 15) build clutch habit.
- For less experienced teams, goal is 1-2 wins in a 3-game pressure set; starters may need to be split to balance teams.
