# Female Athlete Triad / RED-S Review (Coelho et al. 2021)

## Summary

Coelho and colleagues (2021, *Revista Brasileira de Ginecologia e Obstetrícia*) provide a comprehensive clinical review of the Female Athlete Triad and its broader successor framework, Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S). The paper traces the evolution from the original ACSM 1993/1997 triad (disordered eating + amenorrhea + osteoporosis) to the IOC's 2014 RED-S framework, which broadened the model to recognize that the "low energy availability" (LEA) state damages many systems beyond bone and reproduction — metabolic rate, immunity, protein synthesis, GI, cardiovascular, growth, and psychological function are all affected. The operational definition of LEA is energy availability below ~30 kcal/kg fat-free-mass per day (computed as energy intake minus exercise energy expenditure, divided by FFM). Performance impact includes reduced endurance, strength, training response, coordination, judgment, and increased injury risk — meaning LEA is a performance problem, not just a health problem. The IOC's RED-S Clinical Assessment Tool (CAT) is the recommended screening instrument, looking for menstrual irregularity, stress fractures, weight loss, mood change, and restrictive eating. First-line treatment is nutritional rehabilitation (300–600 kcal/day above current intake) plus interdisciplinary care (physician, RD, PT, psychologist); hormonal therapy provides limited bone protection and is not a substitute for restoring energy availability. For the wiki, Coelho 2021 anchors the entire fueling/RED-S/triad area — relevant especially in leanness-pressured sports including volleyball, and a coaching responsibility because coaches see meal patterns and practice intensity that nutritionists never see.

## Key claims / ideas

- Female Athlete Triad (1993/1997) → RED-S (2014, IOC) reflects a broader, system-wide model of LEA harm.
- LEA threshold ≈ <30 kcal/kg FFM/day; affects metabolism, menstrual function, bone, immune, GI, cardiovascular, growth, and psychological systems.
- LEA is a performance problem — strength, endurance, coordination, training response, and injury risk all worsen.
- IOC RED-S Clinical Assessment Tool (CAT) is the consensus screening instrument.
- First-line treatment is nutritional rehabilitation + interdisciplinary care; OCPs are not a substitute for restoring EA.
- Implication for staff: pre-participation screening with CAT + athletic-trainer-led fueling education + clear referral pathways should be standard at HS/college programs.
