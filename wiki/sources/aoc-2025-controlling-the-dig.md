# Controlling the dig with body positioning

## Summary
Jim Stone analyzes Morgan Hentz controlling a hard-driven attack by dropping to her knees, getting her hips under the ball, and keeping her torso upright so the platform can manage dig trajectory and produce a settable ball.

## Key claims / ideas
- Get hips under the ball so the torso can remain upright on hard-driven attacks.
- An upright torso lets the defender manage both trajectory and platform angle.
- The dig goal is to make a hittable ball out of the attack — reliable trajectory control enables a set and transition to counterattack.
- Dropping to the knees is a legitimate advanced defensive posture, not a failure mode.

## Topics covered
- Defensive body positioning on hard-driven balls
- Dig trajectory control
- Libero / back-row defensive mechanics
- Transition from dig to settable ball
