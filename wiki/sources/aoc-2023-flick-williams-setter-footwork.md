# Setting drill to improve setter footwork

## Summary
Diane Flick-Williams (Western Washington) drills setter footwork: coach tosses, setter sets to a spot, moves there, sets again to zone 4. Cues are "toes and nose" facing target, fewest steps possible, final "left, right, walking through," and — critically — "circle the ball" instead of running straight at it.

## Key claims / ideas
- Squaring up efficiently is the prerequisite for a good ball to the OH.
- Two emphases: (1) toes and nose facing the target at contact, (2) feet to ball in fewest steps possible.
- Final step pattern: "left, right, walking through."
- Inexperienced setters run straight at the ball — pivot becomes much harder.
- Fix: "circle the ball" — start moving in the direction of the set early so you arrive already squared.
- Competitive extension: set from the farthest off-net point while still delivering accurate Zone 4 ball.
- Advanced challenge: require sets from right half of court behind the 10-foot line.
