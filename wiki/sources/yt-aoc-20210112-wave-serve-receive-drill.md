# Wave: A fast-paced serve receive drill

## Summary

AoC-distributed drill from Karin Keeney (Hebron HS, Texas), published 2021-01-12 on The Art of Coaching Volleyball. The companion article is publicly accessible and describes the full drill structure, scoring, and coaching rationale; the embedded YouTube video's direct captions were not accessible via WebFetch, so the underlying source here is the public AoC article page, not a scraped transcript. Content is specific and actionable enough to reference for practice-plan pages, with Keeney named as the originating coach.

## Key claims / ideas

- Three groups of three passers run a fast-paced serve-receive: one team serves, one receives, one mirrors on the other side.
- Servers may not target the same spot twice in a row — penalty (burpees, pushups) for repeats. This forces serve variety.
- Receivers must hit 10 quality passes to target; overpasses or drops reset the count to zero.
- Rotate receivers (L->R, R->M, M->L) every three successful passes so every player sees every angle.
- Two-minute rounds; consequence conditioning if a team doesn't reach 10.
- Keeney quote: "It doesn't matter if you are a setter or a middle, all my kids pass."
