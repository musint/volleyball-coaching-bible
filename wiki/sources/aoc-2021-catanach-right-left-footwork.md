# Beginner volleyball: Training "right-left" attacking footwork

## Summary
Chris Catanach isolates the final right-left of the attacker's approach as the adjustment window. Attacker starts on the 10-foot line; coach tosses sets varying in location (front, behind, left, right). The attacker adapts using only the final right-left and lands light, loaded, and ready.

## Key claims / ideas
- The final two steps of the approach are the adjustment window, not the early steps.
- Light-and-loaded landing is the expected finish even when the set is off-location.
- Tossing varied-location sets forces the attacker to problem-solve, not just rehearse.
- Beginner-appropriate isolation drill: no serving, no passing — just footwork to set.
