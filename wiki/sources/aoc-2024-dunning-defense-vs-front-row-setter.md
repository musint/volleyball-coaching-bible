# Defense options against a front-row setter offense

## Summary
Russ Rose, John Dunning, and Mike Sealy work a 6v6 drill on defending a front-row setter offense. Key defensive ideas: left-side commits with the middle while MB and RS kick out to pins; commit "big" when the opposing middle jumps; read and adjust for the slide; clear communication so the middle knows when she has help on the "One" — and when she does, block straight over rather than reaching.

## Key claims / ideas
- Against a front-row setter, left-side blocker can commit with the middle while MB and RS kick out to pin hits.
- "If you're committing, when the opposing middle jumps, you go big." Commitment fails when the LS hedges.
- LS must read and adjust to the slide.
- Communication is the key net mechanic: if middle has help on the "One," she blocks straight over, doesn't reach.
- Setter dump options include middle of court and near-sideline — defense must honor both.

## Topics covered
- Defending a front-row setter (3-hitter) offense
- Commit blocking decisions
- Slide-read adjustments for the left-side blocker
- Net-player communication
