# Marco Bonitta — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Biographical and career reference for Marco Bonitta (b. September 5, 1963, Ravenna), Italian women's volleyball coach best known as head coach of the Italy women's national team during its only senior World Championship win (2002 Germany). Tracks his two stints with the Italy women's federation (March 2001–September 2006; 2014–2016), his foundational women's-club tenure at Foppapedretti Bergamo (1997–2000) — the era's dominant Italian women's club — plus subsequent work as head coach of the Poland women's NT (2007–2008), the Slovenia women's NT (2022–2023/24), and most recently the Egypt men's NT (2025–). The English Wikipedia article is sparse on methodology and quotes; the Italian Wikipedia (cross-referenced into the raw snapshot) adds club-history detail and the September 2006 player-conflict departure context but is similarly thin on doctrinal material.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Italy women's NT, first cycle (March 2001–September 2006):** appointed in March 2001 succeeding Angelo Frigoni; resigned September 2006 after reported player requests for his removal. **2002 World Championship gold (Germany)** is Italy women's only senior FIVB Worlds title to date.
- **Italy women's NT, second cycle (2014–2016):** head coach through Rio 2016 Olympics.
- **Other senior medals under Bonitta:** 2001 European Championship silver (Bulgaria), 2005 European Championship silver (Croatia), 2004 World Grand Prix silver, 2005 World Grand Prix silver, 2006 World Grand Prix bronze.
- **Foppapedretti Bergamo (1997–2000):** back-to-back **CEV European Champions Cup** titles (1998–99, 1999–2000), two Italian Serie A championships (1997–98, 1998–99), one Coppa Italia (1997–98), and three Italian SuperCups (1997, 1998, 1999) — the dominant Italian women's club program of the late 1990s and the foundation of Bonitta's national-team appointment.
- **Italy U20 men:** 2012 CEV U20 European Championship gold — a separate youth-men contribution within his overall Italian-federation résumé.
- **Italy state honor:** Officer of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic (Cavaliere Ufficiale OMRI), awarded 2002 after the World Championship gold.
- **Methodology / philosophy / quotes:** essentially absent. Wikipedia frames Bonitta's record as career-results-and-titles; on-record coaching-doctrine material is not present in either the English or Italian articles.
- **Disambiguation note:** the user-provided brief mentioned "Modena" as Bonitta's defining club. Wikipedia does not record Bonitta as a head coach at Modena. His defining women's-club tenure is **Bergamo 1997–2000**, not Modena. (Massimo Barbolini, by contrast, is Modena-born and was Velasco's vice coach at Panini Modena men's 1985–1989 — but Barbolini is the Modena-adjacent figure, not Bonitta.)
