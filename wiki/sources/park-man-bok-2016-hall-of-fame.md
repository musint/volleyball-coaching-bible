# Man-Bok Park — International Volleyball Hall of Fame

## Summary

Official enshrinement biography (Hall of Fame, inducted 2016) of Man-Bok Park (also rendered Park Man-bok / 박만복), the first Korean person inducted into the International Volleyball Hall of Fame. Establishes the primary biographical dates (1936–2019), the Korean coaching tenure (Soong Eui Women's College 1965–1968; Korean Air 1969–1972; Korea women's national team 1973), the 1973 FIVB World Cup bronze medal in Uruguay with Korea, and the much longer Peruvian women's national team tenure (1974–1992 + 1999–2001) anchored by the 1988 Seoul Olympic silver and the 1982 home-soil World Championship silver.

The record is the cleanest source for dates and titles. Tactical-philosophy material is absent (the document is a results-record listing rather than a doctrinal exposition), so the Korean-school lineage's specific debt to Park is documentable at the level of dates, results, and the player roster he developed (Cecilia Tait, Gabriela Pérez del Solar) but not at the level of training-method specifics. Pair with Korean-language reporting around his 2016 induction for any philosophy quotes.

## Key claims / ideas
- Park is the first Korean inducted into the International Volleyball Hall of Fame (2016)
- Korean head-coach tenure ran 1965–1973 across Soong Eui Women's College, Korean Air, and the Korea women's NT
- 1973 FIVB World Cup bronze medal (Uruguay) is his Korean-NT signature result
- 1974 transition to Peru established a near-two-decade transnational coaching second career
- 1988 Seoul Olympics silver vs. USSR is the high-water Peruvian competitive result
- He developed Hall of Fame Peruvian players Cecilia Tait and Gabriela Pérez del Solar
- The 1976 Montreal Korean women's bronze is **not** his — by 1976 he was already head coach of Peru, finishing 7th in Montreal; the Korean head coach in Montreal was a different figure not surfaced in this document
