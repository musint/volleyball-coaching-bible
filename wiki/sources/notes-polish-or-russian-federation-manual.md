# Eastern-bloc federation coaching manual — Platonov, *My Profession: The Game* (composite)

## Summary

This composite documents the Eastern-bloc federation-coaching-literature
target of the cluster brief, anchored on **Vyacheslav Platonov's *My
Profession: The Game*** — the legendary Soviet men's coach's final book,
translated to English by the Lebedew family and released February 2014. This
is the single most-substantial English-available Eastern-bloc coaching manual
in volleyball, written by the architect of the 1977-1985 Soviet men's
dominance: Olympic gold (1980 Moscow), World Championship golds (1978 Italy,
1982 Argentina), and **five consecutive European Championship golds 1977-1985**.

Per the cluster-brief instruction ("Pick whichever has more accessible
material"), the composite documents Platonov as the primary anchor with
PZPS-side Polish federation literature documented at composite level with
`[unsourced]` / `[translation-needed]` flags. No widely-distributed

Hubert Wagner's biographical record describes his methodology but no

tie-break* is a Polish-language memoir, not a federation methodology manual.

## Key claims / ideas

**Platonov anchor — *My Profession: The Game*:**

- **Theory + practice without instructional drills.** Per the coachingvb.com
  review, the book is a coaching-philosophy and program-methodology manual,
  not a drill-book. Coaches expecting drill prescriptions will be disappointed;
  pair with a drills-oriented Eastern-bloc reference.
- **Five-axis structure:** developing your own coaching style; building a
  team; qualities of a successful coach; training and preparation methods;
  in-game coaching strategies.
- **Blocking-fundamentals chapter.** A single in-depth technical chapter on
  blocking is the structural exception — the technical area Platonov treated
  as the program's most distinctive doctrinal contribution, consistent with
  the Russian-school identification of block-and-defense as the program's
  center of gravity.
- **Coach as program-builder.** The book is structured as advice from one
  head coach to another; the program-building frame, not match-tactician
  frame, dominates.
- **Pre-rally-scoring context.** Some content reflects the pre-2000 game;
  Platonov anticipated certain rule and tactical changes, but readers must
  contextualize against the era.
- **"Multiple readings" recommendation.** Reviewer frames the book as
  rewarding re-engagement; "bite-sized chunks" structure suits busy coaches.
