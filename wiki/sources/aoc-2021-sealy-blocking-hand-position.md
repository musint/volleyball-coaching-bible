# The first step to teaching blocking: Hand position

## Summary
Mike Sealy's first building block for blocking: start with hands together and elbows close, shooting straight up. Wide-hand starts that collapse inward at the top of the jump create holes.

## Key claims / ideas
- Teach hand position FIRST.
- Elbows close, hands together at the start.
- Hands shoot straight up; do not reach inward mid-jump.
- Wide start → holes at the top; solid wall requires tight-to-center start.

- Hand position fundamentals.
- Block wall integrity.
- Common beginner block faults.
