# PlusLiga (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia institutional reference for PlusLiga, Poland's top men's professional volleyball league. Founded June 30, 2000 (as "Polska Liga Siatkówki"), renamed PlusLiga in 2008 after telecom sponsor Plus, administered by Polska Liga Siatkówki SA. 14-team competition running October to April/May; regular season + playoffs; winner takes Mistrzostwo Polski (Polish Championship). Functions as Poland's primary domestic player-development environment and a top-three European club league alongside Italian Serie A and Russian Super League. Polish clubs participate in CEV Champions League, CEV Cup, CEV Challenge Cup. Title concentration: Skra Bełchatów (9), ZAKSA Kędzierzyn-Koźle (7), Jastrzębski Węgiel (4), Resovia Rzeszów (3), Bogdanka LUK Lublin (1, 2024-25). Venue capacity ranges from 1,500 to 11,200 (Trefl Gdańsk's Ergo Arena).

## Key claims / ideas

- PlusLiga is "one of the best volleyball leagues in Europe," confirmed by clubs' performance in CEV Champions League and other European club competitions.
- Founded June 30, 2000; Polish Volleyball League system dates to 1929 (predecessor competitions).
- 14 teams in regular-season + playoff format October-April/May.
- **Skra Bełchatów** (9 titles) and **ZAKSA Kędzierzyn-Koźle** (7 titles) are the modern dynastic clubs; **Jastrzębski Węgiel** and **Resovia** complete the traditional powerhouse tier.
- **Bogdanka LUK Lublin** (HC Stéphane Antiga) won the 2024-25 championship — first title.
- Venue scale: regional 1,500-seat arenas to Ergo Arena (Trefl Gdańsk) at 11,200.
