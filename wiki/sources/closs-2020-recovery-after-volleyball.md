# Closs et al. (2020) — Recovery after volleyball (narrative review)

## Summary

Peer-reviewed narrative review published in *The Physician and Sportsmedicine* covering recovery modalities for volleyball: nutrition, sleep, mental / psychological techniques, cold-water immersion (CWI), and laser therapy. **Strongest evidence is for nutrition and sleep** — the authors describe these as "definitively shown to benefit volleyball players." **CWI and laser therapy show promise but lack volleyball-specific definitive evidence** — adoption is reasonable, but coaches shouldn't treat them as settled best practices. Identifies the evidence gap on **combined-modality recovery strategy for rapid turnaround** (multi-match days, tournament play) as a research priority. Frames volleyball's match-density problem as the central recovery design challenge: short bouts of high-intensity work with limited inter-match windows.

## Key claims / ideas

- Sleep and nutrition are the highest-evidence recovery interventions — the foundation of any program.
- Cold-water immersion shows promise but volleyball-specific evidence is mixed; reasonable but not mandatory.
- Laser therapy lacks robust volleyball evidence — used by some programs, not yet a settled best practice.
- Tournament / match-density recovery is the highest-leverage application of recovery strategy.
- Combined-modality recovery (e.g., CWI + sleep + nutrition stack) is under-studied — coaches should monitor and individualize.
- Recovery affects both performance and injury risk in volleyball — not optional.
