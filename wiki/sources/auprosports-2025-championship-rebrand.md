# AU's own announcement of the 2025 AU Pro Volleyball Championship rebrand

## Summary
Athletes Unlimited's first-party press release (May 2025) announcing the rebrand of its volleyball arm to the AU Pro Volleyball Championship, with new branding by Doubleday & Cartwright, the new two-host-city tour format (Omaha + Madison), 44-athlete roster, and an explicit positioning as "the first of the modern pro women's volleyball leagues to launch in the U.S. back in 2021." A useful corroborating Tier 2 source for the [[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]] page alongside Wikipedia.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Self-positioning:** AU as the first modern-era U.S. women's pro volleyball league (2021), preceding PVF (2024) and LOVB Pro (2025)
- **2025 rebrand:** Athletes Unlimited Pro Volleyball → **AU Pro Volleyball Championship**
- **Format retained** — weekly draft, four weekly captains, individual scoring, five-week season, individual champion
- **Two-host-city tour:** Omaha (Oct 1–15) and Madison (Oct 22–Nov 3) — both also LOVB markets
- **44 invited athletes** drawn from LOVB, PVF, Brazil's Superliga, Turkey's Sultanlar Ligi
- **30 contests; 15 on ESPN platforms**
- **Cassidy Lichtman (VP) quote:** the championship is intended to bring together "the very best from the top domestic and international leagues"
