# Athletes Unlimited Pro Volleyball — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia institutional record for Athletes Unlimited Pro Volleyball — the first U.S. women's pro indoor league of the post-2010s wave, launched February–March 2021 in Dallas. Operates the distinctive "AU model": no fixed teams, weekly captains-and-draft, individual point-based champion. Five seasons completed (2021–2025) with champions Jordan Larson (2021), Bethania de la Cruz (2022, all-time AU points record 4,652), Leah Edmond (2023), Brittany Abercrombie (2024), Jordan Thompson (2025). Rebranded the volleyball arm to AU Pro Volleyball Championship in May 2025 with two-host-city format (Omaha + Madison). Anchors the [[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]] page.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Inaugural season Feb–Mar 2021** in Dallas — first stable U.S. women's pro indoor league of this generation
- **Parent org founded 2020** by Jon Patricof (CEO) and Jonathan Soros
- **Athlete-led structure:** "no team owners; league investors capping their returns; athletes share in league profits and daily decision making"
- **No fixed teams** — players switch teams every week through a weekly draft
- **Weekly captains:** top 4 players each week become captains and draft new teams
- **Individual champion** — most points at season end wins
- **Scoring** combines team match points + individual stat-line points
- **Champions and points:**
  - 2021: Jordan Larson (4,569)
  - 2022: Bethania de la Cruz (4,652 — all-time AU volleyball record)
  - 2023: Leah Edmond (4,313)
  - 2024: Brittany Abercrombie (4,521)
  - 2025: Jordan Thompson (3,563)
- **2025 reformat:** AU Pro Volleyball Championship — two-host-city tour through Omaha (Liberty First Credit Union Arena, Oct 3–13) and Madison (Veterans Memorial Coliseum, Oct 23–Nov 2); 5-week season Oct 1–Nov 3; 44 players invited; 24 contests; 15 on ESPN platforms
- **Calendar:** Fall (October–November) — does not overlap PVF/LOVB winter–spring season, so players can do both
- **Athletes Unlimited College Sports** is a separate AU initiative for college-sport individual-scoring tournaments; AU Pro Volleyball is the original 2021 pro tier
