# Karch Kiraly — USA Volleyball Official Coach Bio

## Summary

The official USA Volleyball coach biography for Karch Kiraly documents his transition in October 2024 from head coach of the U.S. Women's National Team (a role he held from September 2012) to head coach of the U.S. Men's National Team. The page is the canonical USAV-approved factual record of his coaching and playing resume, and it is the right source to cite for any claim about his coaching record, medal haul, or appointment dates.

As head coach of the women, Kiraly compiled a 327-81 record over 12 years, with Olympic gold (Tokyo 2020, played 2021), silver (Paris 2024), and bronze (Rio 2016); the first-ever U.S. Women's World Championship title in 2014; three FIVB Volleyball Nations League titles (2018, 2019, 2021); the 2013 FIVB World Grand Champions Cup; the 2013 NORCECA Continental Championship; the 2015 Pan American Games; and the Pan American Cup 2017-2019.

The page also summarizes his playing career (FIVB greatest player of the 20th century; indoor Olympic gold 1984 and 1988; beach Olympic gold 1996; 148 beach tournament wins; 6x AVP MVP; UCLA 124-5 with three national titles) and Hall of Fame credentials (International Volleyball Hall of Fame 2001; U.S. Olympic Hall of Fame 2008; UCLA Hall of Fame 1992).

## Key claims / ideas

- Kiraly's women's NT coaching record: 327-81 over 12 years (2012-2024).
- Only player to win Olympic medals in both indoor and beach volleyball.
- Appointed head coach of U.S. Men's National Team on October 24, 2024.
- Named by FIVB as the greatest volleyball player of the 20th century.
