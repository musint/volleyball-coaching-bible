# Biomechanical analysis of serving and passing using stats

## Summary
Jim Stone explains how serve geometry creates different passing problems (straight-to-LB is shortest; crosscourt-to-RB forces body-angle adjustment), and walks through the 4-3-2-1-0 passing grade scale most coaches use for stat tracking.

## Key claims / ideas
- Straight serves at LB = shortest travel = fastest arrival time.
- Crosscourt serves at RB force the passer to angle the body to get the ball to setter.
- Serving patterns can be picked strategically based on which serve is hardest to receive.
- Standard passing scale: 4 = perfect, 3 = in-system, 2 = out-of-system but playable, 1 = freeball pass, 0 = error/unplayable.

## Topics covered
- Serve geometry and passing difficulty
- Biomechanics of receiving by angle
- Passing grade scale (0-4)
