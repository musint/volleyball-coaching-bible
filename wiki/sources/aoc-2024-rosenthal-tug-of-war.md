## Summary

Brandon Rosenthal (Lipscomb head coach) designs a 6v6 "Volleyball Tug of War" around a shared tug-of-war score. Both teams start at 3 points on a single scoreboard. Team A wants to push the score up to 6; Team B wants to drive it down to 0. Each rally shifts the single score one point in the winning team's direction. The winner of a rally also earns the next downball entry. Consecutive wins can be multiplied (double or triple points) to amplify momentum. Rosenthal's coaching point: the drill isn't about reaching game point but about closing out — finishing the game.

## Key claims / ideas

- A single shared score that both teams push in opposite directions makes every rally directly consequential to both.
- Close-out mental training is the explicit goal — reaching game point is easy; closing is hard.
- Momentum multipliers (consecutive-win bonuses) amplify the feel of a run, matching real-match psychology.
- 3-3-6-0 range is narrow enough that the drill resolves in a few minutes — can be run as a competitive block multiple times per practice.
- Hitters must swing to finish, not to keep the ball in play — the scoring system rewards kill intent.
