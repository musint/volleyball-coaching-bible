# The mental side of hitting: Helping hitters understand their strengths

## Summary
Western Washington's Diane Flick-Williams argues hitter development is as mental as technical — each attacker must identify her "superpower" skill rather than trying to mimic elite players wholesale.

## Key claims / ideas
- Mental preparation is equal in weight to technical training for hitters.
- Hitters must accept they won't play like their heroes, but can import specific attributes.
- Three archetypal "superpower" candidates: (1) shot variety across court areas, (2) raw hitting velocity, (3) block-tool skill.
- Coach's role: help each athlete identify and maximize her specific superpower.
- Framing combats the "be good at everything" trap and supports one-shot-forcing-defense logic.

## Topics covered
- Hitter psychology and identity formation
- Archetypal hitter strengths
- Coach-led strength identification
- Mental-technical parity in development
