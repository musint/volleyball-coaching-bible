# Defending with a short blocker

## Summary
Mike Sealy and Salima Rockwell discuss defensive strategy when you have a short blocker — push the pin blocker all the way to the pin to shrink the attack window, cheat the line digger in, and serve/tip to limit the hitter's options.

## Key claims / ideas
- Push the short pin blocker all the way to the pin to shrink attack angles; middle releases early and closes tight.
- This leaves tiny line shot and large, coverable cross court.
- Move line digger in a step — hitters rarely hit perfectly down the line.
- Hitters often make mistakes trying to exploit a small blocker (long, wide).
- Short-serve the left-side hitter to prevent full approach.
- Tip toward the left-side hitter to force her to play the first ball, denying her the transition attack.

## Topics covered
- Short-blocker defensive strategy.
- Block-defense coordination and angle shrinking.
- Serving and tipping strategy around a weak blocker.
