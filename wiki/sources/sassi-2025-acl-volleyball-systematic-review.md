# Understanding ACL injuries in volleyball: a systematic review of epidemiology and risk factors

## Summary

Sassi and colleagues (2025) is a 15-study systematic review of ACL injury patterns in volleyball, encompassing roughly 3.3 million athletes aged 10–70. It is the most current consolidated source on volleyball ACL epidemiology, mechanism, and prevention. The review's signature finding: volleyball ACL injury is overwhelmingly a non-contact, spike-landing event — 97% of cases via non-contact mechanisms and ~62% specifically during landing after an attack. Risk concentrates predictably: female athletes have 2–4× higher rates than males; adolescent female players are most vulnerable (high-school prevalence 9.3% vs. 4.4% collegiate); and outside hitters carry the highest position-specific incidence due to spike-landing volume and ground reaction forces. On prevention, the review converges on neuromuscular training programs of 15–20 minutes, 2–3×/week, progressing from bilateral to unilateral landings with sport-specific spike-landing simulations — the same dose-response pattern documented in PEP, FIFA 11+, and KIPP-style programs. For the wiki, this paper anchors any claim that landing mechanics, neuromuscular control, and progressive jump-landing training are the dominant ACL prevention levers in volleyball — and that adolescent female outside hitters should be the priority target for any prevention program.

## Key claims / ideas

- 97% of volleyball ACL injuries occur via non-contact mechanisms; ~62% during landing after a spike.
- Female athletes have 2–4× higher ACL injury rates than males.
- Adolescent female players (HS-age) show greatest vulnerability; HS prevalence ~2× collegiate.
- Outside hitter is the highest-incidence position due to spike-landing volume.
- 15–20 minute neuromuscular training, 2–3×/week, with progressive bilateral → unilateral landing drills, is the consensus prevention dose.
- Adolescent neuromuscular maturation and structured conditioning gaps explain part of the HS-vs-college risk differential.
