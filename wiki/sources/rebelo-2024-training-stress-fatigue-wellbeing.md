# Rebelo et al. (2024) — Training stress, neuromuscular fatigue, and well-being in volleyball (systematic review)

## Summary

PRISMA-compliant systematic review published in *BMC Sports Science, Medicine and Rehabilitation* synthesizing 31 articles (from 2,535 screened) on training-load monitoring, neuromuscular fatigue assessment, and well-being tracking in competitive volleyball. Documents **what to monitor, how often, and how to interpret** — converging on a stack: daily session-RPE for internal load (used in 77% of studies), daily jump count / height for external load via wearable or video (IMU adoption rising at 16%), weekly countermovement-jump (CMJ) for neuromuscular fatigue (most-utilized fatigue test in the literature), and daily Hooper Index for well-being. Pre-season shows progressive load → performance decrement (a planned and expected pattern); in-season shows wave-distribution across weekly cycles. Documents systematic over-loading of middle blockers vs other positions in uniform programs and confirms HR is unreliable for prescription in volleyball given its anaerobic profile. The reference work for what a coach-supportable monitoring program looks like.

## Key claims / ideas

- sRPE is the field-standard internal load metric — used in 77% of reviewed studies.
- CMJ is the workhorse for neuromuscular fatigue tracking; force-plate FT:CT ratio preferred where available, averaged jump height as a fallback.
- Daily wellness via Hooper Index (fatigue / stress / soreness / mood / sleep) is sensitive enough to detect overtraining signals.
- Sleep ≥ 7 hours nightly correlates with reduced injury risk.
- Pre-season produces progressive load → transient performance decrement — coaches should plan around this rather than mistake it for a problem.
- In-season runs as wave distribution across weekly cycles — concentrate load at lower-priority blocks.
- Middle blockers are systematically over-loaded relative to other positions in uniform programming — position-specific load planning is mandatory.
- HR is a poor load proxy in volleyball; prescribing intensity from HR is not supported.
- Travel / congestion windows require explicit recovery programming (rest, hydration, routine).
