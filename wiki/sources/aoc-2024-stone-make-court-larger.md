# How to make the court larger

## Summary
Jim Stone (Ohio State, USA Youth NT) describes three offensive techniques for "enlarging" the court against a stationary block: hitting high hands, hitting around or through holes in the block, and tooling the edges of the block.

## Key claims / ideas
- High hands: deflect the ball high off the blockers' hands so it lands outside court perimeter, forcing defensive scramble.
- Avoiding the block: attack around the block or find seams/holes.
- Tooling: hit off block edges with off-speed contact that's hard to read and defend.
- All three techniques widen the effective playing area beyond the 9m sideline by leveraging the block itself.

## Topics covered
- Using the block as an offensive weapon
- Tooling and off-speed shots
- Making the court larger vs. a set block
- Game strategy in attacking
