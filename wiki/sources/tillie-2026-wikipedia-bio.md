# Laurent Tillie (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article covering the career of French coach Laurent Tillie (b. December 1, 1963, Algiers). Documents his playing career as a France-NT outside hitter (1982-1995, 406+ caps, two Olympic appearances) and his coaching career culminating in the 2020 Tokyo Olympic gold medal as France men's NT head coach. Subsequent appointments include Osaka Bluteon (Japan, 2020-present) and the Japan men's national team (2025-).

## Key claims / ideas

- Born December 1, 1963 in Algiers, Algeria; height 1.93m / 6'4"; outside hitter.
- France national team player 1982-1995, 406+ caps, captain 1991-92.
- Player results: 1985 European bronze; 1987 European silver; 1988 Seoul + 1992 Barcelona Olympics.
- Coaching career: AS Cannes (2001-2012, French champions 2005); Czech Republic NT (2005-06); **France men's NT (2012-2021)**; RC Cannes (2016-17); Osaka Bluteon (2020+); **Japan men's NT (2025-)**.
- France HC results: 2015 European Championship gold; 2015 + 2017 World League gold; 2018 VNL silver; 2021 VNL bronze; **Tokyo 2020 Olympic gold (defeated ROC 3-2 in five sets, August 7, 2021)**.
- 2021 CEV Coach of the Year.
- Wife: Caroline Keulen (former Dutch NT captain). Three sons: Kévin (volleyball, NCAA + France NT), Kim (basketball), Killian (basketball, NBA).
