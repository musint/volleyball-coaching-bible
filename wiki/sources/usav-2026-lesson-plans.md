# Lesson Plans

## Summary

USA Volleyball's Lesson Plans page provides age-banded 12-day curricula covering hitting, overhead passing/setting, serving, forearm passing, and blocking. Plans are available for Kindergarten-2nd grade, 3rd-5th grade, 6th-8th grade (junior high), and 9th-12th grade, each including objectives, glossaries, cue words for new instructors, time allocations, and SHAPE standards alignment.

The curriculum is explicitly grounded in motor-learning principles: specificity (drills should closely resemble game conditions with a net, rope, or ribbon), random-over-blocked training, and whole-over-partial practice (pass-set-hit combinations rather than isolated skill reps). John Kessel frames coaching success broadly, including not just wins and skill improvement but "how many kids play volleyball again the following season."

## Key claims / ideas

- "Random training is superior to blocked training."
- Practice should mirror complete game sequences (pass-set-hit) rather than isolated skill drills.
- Drills should closely resemble game conditions, ideally with a net (or rope/ribbon/person as substitute).
- Lesson plans run 12 days per age band and align to SHAPE standards.
- Success metrics include player retention — "how many kids play volleyball again the following season."
- Each plan provides cue words so new instructors can deliver consistent instruction.

## Topics covered

- 12-day lesson plan structure by age band (K-2, 3-5, 6-8, 9-12)
- Five fundamental skills (hitting, setting, serving, forearm passing, blocking)
- Motor-learning framework (specificity, random vs. blocked, whole vs. partial)
- Game-like drill design with net substitutes
- Player-tracked scoring within drills
- SHAPE standards alignment
- Coach-success metrics beyond wins
