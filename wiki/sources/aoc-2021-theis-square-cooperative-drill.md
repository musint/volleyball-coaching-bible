## Summary

Ryan Theis (University of Florida) uses a cooperative 4v4 "square" drill to train communication, primary/secondary setter roles, and ball control. Four players form a square on each side of the court. Right back is the primary setter; left back is the secondary setter. The only sets called are huts and reds. Each time a ball crosses the net, both teams rotate one full rotation. Digs must be sent high to the middle. The team target is 10 controlled attacks over the net before the ball drops.

## Key claims / ideas

- Cooperative 4v4 with a rotation every kill-attempt rehearses position learning and communication at speed.
- Primary setter (right back) plus secondary setter (left back) trains the second-setter decision every team faces.
- Hut and red sets only — the drill specifically isolates outside-pin sets.
- High-to-middle digging is the standard target regardless of set call.
- 10 controlled attacks is the target — not a point count but a sustained-rally goal.
