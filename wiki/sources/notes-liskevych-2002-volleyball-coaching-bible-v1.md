# The Volleyball Coaching Bible (Volume 1) — 2002

## Summary

The 2002 AVCA-affiliated Human Kinetics anthology, edited by **Don Shondell
and Cecile Reynaud** (NOT Liskevych — the slug retains "liskevych" because
Task B.12 specified it, but the source-page frontmatter reflects the actual
editorship). 24 contributing coaches, 24 chapters, 369 pages. Foundational
US-NCAA coaching literature — the single best snapshot of US coaching
consensus circa 2002.

The wiki already has the Vol 2 source page from a parallel B.8 ingest. This
Vol 1 page captures the earlier edition. Vol 1 + Vol 2 together are the
canonical citable AVCA-aligned coaching reference texts in print.

## Key claims / ideas

The book's 24 chapters split into:

**Part 1 — Coaching Fundamentals (12 chapters):** Pavlik (model coach),
Hebert (principles + goal-setting), Braden (direction + inspiration), Beal
(excellence), Pettit (program promotion), Dunning (college-program building),
D. Shondell (HS-program building), Pingel (junior-club building), Rose
(practice planning), Peppler (teaching technique), Neville (drill design),
Cook (conditioning).

**Part 2 — Skills & Strategies (12 chapters):** Wise (serving), S. Shondell
(receiving), Clemens (setting), Weishoff (attacking), Stone (digging),
Suwara (blocking), Sturm (offensive components), Hanson (talent-based
offense), **Liskevych (dominating defensive systems — Ch. 21, the canonical
Liskevych defensive-systems treatment in published form)**, Waite
(competitive edge), Coleman (scouting), Scates (game situations).

Specific load-bearing chapters for the wiki:
- **Rose Ch. 9** — Penn State practice-design framework in print.
- **Hebert Ch. 2** — precursor to *Thinking Volleyball* (2013).
- **Neville Ch. 11** — drill-design principles (intent, transfer,
  repetitions, tempo); canonical drill-construction source.
- **Wise Ch. 13** — Wise's only stand-alone published technique chapter.
- **Liskevych Ch. 21** — defensive-systems treatment that tracks his AOC
  defensive articles but is more comprehensive.
- **Peppler Ch. 10** — one of the rare published Peppler pedagogy
  chapters.
- **Beal Ch. 4** — gold-medal-coach reflection on program-building from a
  national-team lens.

## Topics covered

- Coaching principles, goal-setting, and program-building
- Practice-planning frameworks (Rose)
- Drill design (Neville)
- All six core skills (serving, passing, setting, attacking, digging,
  blocking)
- Offensive systems (Sturm, Hanson)
- Defensive systems (Liskevych)
- Scouting and game management (Coleman, Scates)
- Conditioning and physical prep (Cook)
- College, HS, and junior-club program development (Dunning, D. Shondell,
  Pingel)
