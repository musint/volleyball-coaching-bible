## Summary

Crowd-sourced list from top coaches of player behaviors that tank a tryout: blaming the setter, phones in the gym, cliques, quitting on balls, talking over the coach, lazy footwork, attending to parents on the sideline, carrying mistakes into the next play, refusing to try another position, and projecting negative body language.

## Key claims / ideas

- Body language is observable data coaches use to rank comparable skills.
- Player-to-player blame (esp. toward the setter) is disqualifying.
- Willingness to try another position signals coachability.
- Phones, parents, and cliques are social anti-signals that coaches weight heavily.
