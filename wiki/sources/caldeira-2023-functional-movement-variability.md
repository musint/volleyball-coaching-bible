# How functional movement variability facilitates successful skill adaptation during the volleyball attack

## Summary

Caldeira and colleagues (2023), with Davids and Araújo on the author list, deliver a head-to-head comparison of **constraints-led approach (CLA)** vs. **traditional approach (TA)** practice on the volleyball attack against a live opposition block. Young male players were randomly assigned to a 6-week intervention in either condition; the kinematic outcomes (centre-of-mass deviation at the planting step and at ball contact) and the percentage of successful attacks against the block were the measured outcomes.

The CLA group's successful-attack rate **rose significantly more** than the TA group's. The TA group showed only a small improvement. Successful attacks were predicted by **lateral CoM deviation at the planting step** and **longitudinal CoM deviation at ball contact** — i.e., it was not the arm-swing that distinguished successful from unsuccessful attacks, but the hitter's ability to *adjust* their whole-body approach to the live constraint of the block.

For coaches, this is one of the cleanest demonstrations that variability in approach footwork and contact position is **functional** — players who could move their CoM laterally and longitudinally to solve the block beat players grooved into a fixed approach pattern. It provides volleyball-specific empirical support for CLA over a "model technique" approach to teaching the spike.

## Key claims / ideas

- 6-week CLA group's successful-attack rate against a live block improved significantly more than TA group's.
- Two specific kinematic predictors of successful attacks: **lateral CoM deviation at the planting step** and **longitudinal CoM deviation at ball contact**.
- Variability is **functional** — adjustment of approach footwork and contact position to the live block constraint, not random noise.
- Strong evidence for CLA over "model technique" coaching of the spike.
- Connects directly to the representative-learning-design literature: practice tasks must contain a live block for these CoM adjustments to develop.
