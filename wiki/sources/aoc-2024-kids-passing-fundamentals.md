# Volleyball training for kids: Teaching the fundamentals of passing

## Summary
Ruth Nelson outlines the key teaching points for introducing passing to kids aged 4-10, emphasizing hand-eye coordination, footwork, and body position, and framing passing as the foundational skill for keeping rallies alive.

## Key claims / ideas
- Passing is one of the most critical skills for young players; accuracy and consistency are the core outcomes.
- Kids need three building blocks: hand-eye coordination, footwork, and body position.
- Start with tennis balls or small pink balls, then progress to regulation or Baden Softlight volleyballs.
- Platform fundamentals: flat arms; wrists, thumbs, and hands together; arms slightly off chest; knees bent; shoulders over front foot.
- Follow through by extending arms toward the target and holding the finish.

## Topics covered
- Passing platform technique for young players
- Progression from low-impact balls to regulation
- Parent-involved practice (BYOP model)
- Serve receive vs. free-ball vs. front-/back-court passing
