# League One Volleyball (LOVB Pro) — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia institutional record for League One Volleyball — the U.S. women's pro indoor league founded as a youth-volleyball club network in 2020 and launched as LOVB Pro in 2025 with six teams (Atlanta, Austin, Houston, Madison, Omaha/Nebraska, Salt Lake). Single-entity league owned by the parent organization, with founders Katlyn Gao, Peter Hirschmann, and Olympian Kevin Wong; co-founder Lindsey Berg. Six head coaches include two current USA Olympic team assistants (Matt Fuerbringer, Tama Miyashiro) and the USA Women's National Team head coach (Erik Sullivan, Austin). Inaugural champion: LOVB Austin (April 2025). Anchors the [[lovb]] page.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Pro league launched 2025** (parent youth-club organization founded 2020; LOVB Pro formally announced October 19, 2021)
- **Six inaugural teams:** Atlanta, Austin, Houston, Madison, Omaha (rebranded Nebraska Aug 2025), Salt Lake
- **Single-entity ownership** — distinguishes LOVB from PVF
- **Tour-and-residency model:** players live in market cities; play in-arena home matches
- **Youth-club integration:** 77 LOVB youth clubs in 28 states (July 2025), 22,000+ youth athletes
- **Heavy USA Olympic talent:** nine 2024 Olympic-roster members signed; founding athletes include Jordan Larson, Justine Wong-Orantes, Lauren Carlini, Haleigh Washington, Jordyn Poulter, Carli Lloyd, Micha Hancock, Jordan Thompson, Kelsey Robinson-Cook, Fabiana Claudino
- **Series A and B fundraising totaling $51.75M+** (Sept 2022 + 2023); investors include Billie Jean King, Kevin Durant, Lindsey Vonn, Jayson Tatum, Candace Parker
- **Inaugural champion:** LOVB Austin
- **Format:** four matches/week, head-to-head + "Weekend with LOVB" homestand; LOVB Classic in-season tournament; April finals
- **2027 expansion:** LA, Minnesota, San Francisco
