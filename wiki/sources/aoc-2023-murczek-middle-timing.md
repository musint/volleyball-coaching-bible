# Middle hitter timing

## Summary
Emporia State's Ken Murczek frames middle-attack timing around the "out of the setter's hands" ideal and advocates for an off-speed tip into the donut area as a secondary scoring tool.

## Key claims / ideas
- Ideal middle contact: right out of the setter's hands (Tempo 1-equivalent).
- After the hard-swing fundamental is mastered, train soft shots — particularly a tip over the top of a swing block into the donut area.
- Even a dug tip yields a likely free ball, so tips create positive expected value regardless of landing.
- The swing block creates a predictable soft-shot window that the middle can exploit.

## Topics covered
- Middle-attack timing ideal
- Tip over swing-block as middle-specific weapon
- Expected-value framing of off-speed attacks
- Progression from hard contact to soft contact
