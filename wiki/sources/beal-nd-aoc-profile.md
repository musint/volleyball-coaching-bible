# Doug Beal — AOC coach profile

## Summary

The Art of Coaching Volleyball (AOC) coach-profile page for Doug Beal is a curated biographical capsule plus a directory of AOC content Beal has appeared in from 2012 through 2025. The biographical capsule is the most concise high-trust summary available: it names the "Triple Crown" framing of the 1984 Olympic + 1985 World Cup + 1986 World Championship golds, gives the USA Men's international ranking at 19th when Beal took over in 1977, and credits him with Midwest Collegiate Coach of the Year in 1972 and 1973. The content directory lists six AOC pieces featuring Beal, the most recent being the February 2025 "Don't train passing in isolation" piece co-authored with Ben Josephson.

## Key claims / ideas

- Beal is described as the first U.S. National Coach to win Olympic Gold.
- The U.S. Men were ranked 19th internationally when Beal took over the national team in 1977.
- "Triple Crown" framing ties 1984 Olympic + 1985 World Cup + 1986 World Championship gold medals.
- Midwest Collegiate Coach of the Year in 1972 and 1973 during the Ohio State head-coach tenure.
- Over 200 national team international matches as a player.
- Five USVBA All-American honors and 1975 Player of the Year as a player.
- National Team Center Director at the San Diego facility 1985-1987.
- CEO of USA Volleyball 2005-2017.
- AOC content featuring Beal covers: passing (2025 with Josephson), state of American volleyball (2017), growing the game (2018), general coaching advice (2013), and coach-development stories (2012).
