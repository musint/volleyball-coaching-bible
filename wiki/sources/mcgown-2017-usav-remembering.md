# "A Wonderful Volleyball Coach;" Remembering Carl McGown

## Summary
USA Volleyball's January 2017 remembrance of Carl McGown documents the career
of one of the most internationally-experienced U.S. volleyball coaches of the
20th century: seven consecutive Olympic Games (1984–2008) and seven
consecutive FIVB World Championships (1974–2002) as a member of U.S. staff,
three Olympic gold medals (1984, 1988, 2008) earned while he was on staff,
and two NCAA men's championships at BYU (1999, 2001).

The piece frames McGown as the pioneer of the "scientific" (motor-learning
grounded) approach to volleyball instruction in the U.S. It quotes colleagues
and former players including Hugh McCutcheon, who described McGown as
"fundamentally sound" while remaining adaptive to evolving game dynamics. The
article is a primary biographical source for dates, honors, and relationships
in the GMS lineage.

## Key claims / ideas
- McGown attended seven consecutive Olympics (1984–2008) and seven consecutive
  FIVB World Championships (1974–2002) as a U.S. staff member.
- Led BYU to NCAA men's titles in 1999 and 2001; AVCA Coach of the Year in
  both seasons; 225-137 record in 13 years as BYU head coach.
- AVCA Hall of Fame 2011; James E. Coleman National Team Award 2014.
- Coaching emphasis captured in the phrase "developing comfort with
  discomfort"; championed training efficiency and specificity.
- Mentored coaches who later led Olympic programs (e.g., McCutcheon).

## Topics covered
- Biographical timeline (1970 FIVB assistant → 2014 Coleman Award).
- Coaching philosophy (scientific approach, training efficiency).
- Lineage / influence on subsequent Olympic coaches.
