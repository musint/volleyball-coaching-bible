# Vital Heynen (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article on Vital Heynen (b. June 12, 1969, Maaseik, Belgium), Belgian setter-turned-coach who built a club legacy at Noliko Maaseik (2006-2012, four Belgian titles), then took Germany men's NT to 2014 World Championship bronze, before assuming Poland men's NT in February 2018 and winning the **2018 FIVB World Championship** in his first major tournament — Poland's second consecutive Worlds gold. Subsequent Polish results: 2019 World Cup silver, 2019 + 2021 European Championship bronzes. Polish Coach of the Year 2018. Currently head coach of China men's NT (since 2024).

## Key claims / ideas

- Belgian setter-turned-coach; Noliko Maaseik playing career 1995-2005, immediate transition to Maaseik HC role 2006-2012.
- German men's NT HC 2012-2016 (2014 Worlds bronze) — built reputation as European-system coach before Polish appointment.
- Polish men's NT HC February 7, 2018 - 2021. **2018 World Championship gold (3-0 over Brazil)** in his debut major — Poland's second consecutive Worlds title.
- 2019 World Cup silver, 2019 + 2021 European bronzes — sustained excellence even after 2018 peak.
- Polish Coach of the Year 2018; Belgian Coach of the Year 2009 + 2011.
- Club coaching: VfB Friedrichshafen (Germany), Sir Safety Perugia (Italy), Tours VB (France), Nilüfer Belediyespor (Turkey women's).
- Currently China men's NT HC since 2024.
