# Polish Volleyball Federation (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia institutional reference for the Polish Volleyball Federation (Polski Związek Piłki Siatkowej, PZPS), the national governing body for volleyball in Poland. Founded in 1928, headquartered in Warsaw; 16 provincial associations; FIVB membership since 1947 and CEV affiliation since 1963. Manages all domestic men's competitions (PlusLiga + amateur tiers), all women's competitions (Tauron Liga + tiers), national-team programs across senior + U23/U21/U19 age categories, and the Polish Cup / SuperCup. Current president (since 2021): Sebastian Świderski (former Polish NT player).

## Key claims / ideas

- PZPS founded **1928**, predating the modern era of international volleyball governance.
- Joined FIVB in **1947** (founding-era member; FIVB itself founded 1947) and CEV in **1963**.
- Federation oversees 16 provincial volleyball associations, organizing competitions "from professional to amateur" across men's and women's divisions.
- President Sebastian Świderski (2021-present) is a former Polish NT player.
- Notable historical presidents: Tadeusz Brzósko (1957-1969); Mirosław Przedpełski (2004-2015).
- Manages senior + U23 + U21 + U19 national teams across both genders.
