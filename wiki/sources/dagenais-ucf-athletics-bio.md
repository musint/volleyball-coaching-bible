# Todd Dagenais — UCF Athletics coach bio

## Summary
Institutional bio maintained by UCF Athletics during Dagenais's 15-year (2008-2023) tenure as head volleyball coach. Captures career accomplishments (308-159 record, 5 consecutive AAC titles, 6 NCAA Tournament bids, 4 AAC Coach of the Year awards), prior coaching stops (USC 2005-2007 assistant on a Final Four team; Michigan State 1998-2003 associate head coach; Northern Michigan 1998 assistant), playing background (Central Michigan men's club team), and an unusually explicit philosophy statement crediting three mentors — Chuck Erbe, Toshi Yoshida, and Mick Haley — for disciplined, internationally-informed, people-centered coaching.

## Key claims / ideas
- UCF head coach from January 2008; 308-159 overall (.660), 183-85 AAC (.683), 155-61 home, through the 2022-23 season.
- Five consecutive AAC titles (2019-2023); four AAC Coach of the Year awards; AAC's winningest all-time conference coach.
- Six NCAA Tournament appearances (2014, 2018, 2019, 2020-21, 2021, 2022); first tournament win over Florida State 3-2 in 2019.
- Player development outputs: 14 AVCA All-America honorees, 24 All-Region selections, 38 all-conference honors.
- Academic consistency: team 3.0+ GPA every semester since May 2008; 62 conference academic honors.
- Prior coaching: USC assistant 3 seasons (Trojans 29-5, 2007 Final Four); Michigan State associate head coach 1998-2003 (NCAA Tournament each of five seasons); Northern Michigan 1998 assistant.
- Playing and education: B.S. Sports Medicine (Exercise Physiology emphasis), Central Michigan 1995; four years on CMU men's club team; certified athletic trainer.
- Explicit three-mentor philosophy synthesis: Chuck Erbe (discipline), Toshi Yoshida (international perspective), Mick Haley (creative people management, maximizing individual talent toward collective goals).
- USA Volleyball involvement: 2016 Collegiate Women's NT co-head coach; 2018 Collegiate NT selection committee; U.S. Women's Junior A2 (2011-2012, bronze at 2011 USAV HP Championships); 2004 Olympic Games coaching delegation; lead court coach/director of USA HP tryouts.
