# Karch Kiraly: Mini games to practice serving

## Summary
Karch Kiraly (USA W-NT) teaches servers to deliver "flean" (flat and clean) serves under pressure via mini-games that start at 22-22 and require a flean serve for game-point to count, plus variants with doubled points for flean serves.

## Key claims / ideas
- "Flean" = flat and clean: low trajectory, little to no spin.
- Games start at 22-22 to manufacture late-set serving pressure every rally.
- At game point, the serve must be flean or the game continues.
- Variation: every rally counts double for serving team if they serve flean.
- Variation: run as offense-vs-defense with the serve as the drill's entry condition.
- Central coaching focus is toughness — not high-arc safe serves.

## Topics covered
- Serving under pressure
- "Flean" serving principle (flat and clean)
- Mini-game design for late-set simulation
- Team-level serving competition formats
