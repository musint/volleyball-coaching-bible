# Getting your middles extra reps (while keeping everyone else involved)

## Summary
Trinity Western's Ryan Hofer and Doug Reimer demonstrate a single-court drill that targets middle-blocker attack reps while keeping defenders, passers, and setters in active roles, solving the single-court rep allocation problem.

## Key claims / ideas
- Problem framed: on one court with limited time, middles routinely get too few quality reps.
- Solution: design roles for passers/setters/defenders so the drill reps more middle attacks per unit time without parking teammates.
- Technique cues for middles: get off the net quickly, read the pass, time approach dynamically.
- Reimer cue: lean into movement as the pass is made; plant as the setter touches the ball.
- Hofer cue: location when the setter has the ball determines timing and consistency.

## Topics covered
- Middle-blocker attack rep allocation on a single court
- Read-the-pass timing cues
- Plant-on-setter-touch timing
- Practice structure for position-specific work
