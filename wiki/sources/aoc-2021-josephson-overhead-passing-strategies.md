# Strategies for better overhead passing

## Summary
Ben Josephson argues the overhead pass has a lower error rate than the forearm pass. Drill: three passers must use hands on all receives, first 10 in normal positions, next 10 with 1-2 passers moved toward the net to open up the back.

## Key claims / ideas
- Overhead pass has a lesser chance of error than forearm passing (men's game)
- Drill setup: 3 passers, 1 setter, rest serve
- All receives must use the hands (overhead) — no forearm passes allowed
- First 10 balls: normal positions
- Next 10 balls: one or two passers move several feet closer to the net, opening the back
- Experiment with passer depth based on player skill set

- Overhead passing (hand-passing) for SR
- Men's volleyball serve-receive
- Passer depth experimentation
- Forced-hands-only drill constraint
