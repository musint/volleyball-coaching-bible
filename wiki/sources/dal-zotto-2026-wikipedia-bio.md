# Renan Dal Zotto — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article for **Renan Dal Zotto**, Brazilian volleyball
outside hitter (playing career 1979–1989) and later the post-Bernardinho
**head coach of the Brazil men's national volleyball team from January
2017 through 2023**. The article documents his playing-era Olympic record
— 1980 Moscow (5th), **1984 LA silver** (the "Silver Generation" alongside
Bernardinho, coached by Bebeto de Freitas), 1988 Seoul (4th) — plus
multiple Pan American and South American Championship medals. Inducted
into the International Volleyball Hall of Fame in 2015.

As head coach, Dal Zotto led Brazil men's NT through a seven-year rebuild
cycle after Bernardinho's departure following the Rio 2016 home gold. His
headline coaching title was the **2019 FIVB World Cup gold**. A 2018
FIVB-disciplined incident (intentionally rolling a ball onto the court
mid-rally during a VNL match against Russia) is also documented.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Position: outside hitter (#6)** during playing career.
- **Playing Olympic results:** 1980 Moscow (5th place), **1984 LA
  silver**, 1988 Seoul (4th place).
- **Silver Generation member** alongside Bernardinho — 1984 LA silver as
  teammate on the Bebeto de Freitas-coached Brazil men's NT.
- **Multiple Pan American and South American Championship medals** in
  playing career (1979–1987 window).
- **International Volleyball Hall of Fame induction: 2015**.
- **Appointed Brazil men's NT HC: January 2017**, replacing Bernardinho
  (who had "been in office during the last sixteen years").
- **Tenure: 2017–2023** (7 years), ending when Bernardinho returned for a
  second stint.
- **2019 FIVB World Cup gold** — Dal Zotto's headline HC title.
- **One season in Italy** as a club coach; otherwise primarily Brazilian
  clubs (recent: Funvic Taubaté).
- **2018 FIVB disciplinary incident** — intentionally rolled a ball onto
  the court mid-rally during a VNL match against Russia; did not
  apologize or admit fault.
