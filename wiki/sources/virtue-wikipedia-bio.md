# Erin Virtue — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
The Wikipedia biography of Erin Virtue compiles her playing career as a setter at Illinois (2001–2004, AVCA Honorable Mention All-American before a junior-year-ending ACL tear in 2004), her assistant-coaching arc through Loyola, Cincinnati, Michigan, and Northwestern (2006–2018), her seven-season tenure as USA Women's National Team assistant under Karch Kiraly (2017–2024), her parallel role as Director of the USA National Team Development Program from 2019, and her current Michigan head-coaching tenure (2023–present, 47–46 through 2025) [virtue-wikipedia-bio]. The page documents that Virtue was on the Tokyo Olympics staff that won the program's first-ever Olympic gold medal in 2020 (delivered 2021) [virtue-wikipedia-bio]. Erin Virtue does not yet have a Wikipedia page comparable in length to other coaches in her peer set, so this biography is the primary biographical anchor for her in the captured corpus.

## Key claims / ideas
- Erin Elizabeth Virtue, born April 13, 1983, St. Charles IL.
- St. Francis HS (Wheaton IL) — back-to-back Suburban Catholic Conference titles, 2000 all-state team.
- Illinois 2001–2004; junior season she was 2nd in the Big Ten in assists per game (13.56), 1,627 single-season assists, AVCA Honorable Mention All-American, first-team All-Big Ten.
- Senior season 2004 ended Sept 17 with a torn ACL vs. Ball State; no fifth year of eligibility.
- Assistant arc: Loyola (2006), Cincinnati (2007–2010, 101–36 over period), **Michigan (2011–2015 — recruiting + offensive coordinator; helped four NCAA tourneys including program's first Final Four in 2012)**, Northwestern (2016–2018, associate HC).
- **Named USA women's national team assistant coach March 3, 2017** under Karch Kiraly.
- Served on the staff that won the **2020 Tokyo Olympics gold medal** — USA women's first-ever Olympic gold (delivered 2021).
- Director, USA National Team Development Program from 2019 — oversees U15–U23 development pipeline.
- Returned to Michigan as head coach in 2023; record through 2025: 47–46 (.505) overall, 24–36 (.400) Big Ten, NCAA second-round appearance in 2025.
