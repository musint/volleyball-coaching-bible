# Volleyball: Steps to Success (Kenny + Gregory, 2006/2009; Schmidt 2nd ed., 2016)

**AUTHORSHIP NOTE**: The plan slug references "Pauley" — research confirms there is no Bonnie
Pauley volleyball book. The actual *Volleyball: Steps to Success* lineage in Human Kinetics' Steps
to Success Activity Series is **Bonnie Kenny + Cindy Gregory** (1st ed., 2006 with 2009 reprint;
ISBN 9780736063371) and **Becky Schmidt** (2nd ed., 2016; ISBN 9781450468824). Slug retained for
plan consistency; cite as `notes-pauley-2009-volleyball-steps-to-success`. The 2009 plan year
selects the Kenny + Gregory edition as the primary referent.

## Summary

*Volleyball: Steps to Success* is the volleyball entry in Human Kinetics' long-running Steps to
Success Activity Series — a self-evaluating skill-progression workbook for beginning to
intermediate players, youth/HS coaches, and PE-program instructors. The 1st edition (Kenny +
Gregory, 2006) organizes the sport into 12 "Steps," each presenting a teaching progression with
diagrams, drills, and a point-scored success-check rubric that gates progression to the next step.
Distinctively, the 1st edition treats **posture-and-movement (Step 1)** and **transitioning (Step
10)** as standalone gateway competencies — a pedagogical choice most other technique books
implicitly embed into other skills. The 2nd edition (Schmidt, 2016) compresses to 10 Steps and
folds those movement/transition concepts back into the skill steps. Bonnie Kenny brings 20+ years
of D-I head coaching at South Carolina, Massachusetts, and Delaware (~400 wins); Becky Schmidt won
Hope College's first NCAA D-III National Championship (2014) and carries a .762 career winning
percentage. The book is one of the few self-study + curriculum texts that covers the full canon
(six skills + team systems + out-of-system play + competitive readiness) in a single progression
volume.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Posture and movement are prerequisite skills** (1st ed Step 1) — players who can't move and
  recover athletically can't string the six skills in live play; movement is its own scaffold, not
  an embedded subskill.
- **Transitioning deserves explicit treatment** (1st ed Step 10) — skill-to-skill recovery
  and re-positioning is a teachable competency separate from any single skill.
- **Out-of-system play is teachable** (1st ed Step 11) — recovery-pass setting, free-ball downball
  offense, tip/roll-shot defense; a refreshingly explicit treatment in beginner-to-intermediate
  literature.
- **Self-evaluation rubrics gate progression** — every Step ends with a point-scored drill the
  reader executes and scores against a published threshold; pedagogically aligned with criterion-
  referenced mastery learning.
- **The competitive volleyball player** (1st ed Step 12) — mental and competitive readiness as the
  final scaffolding step before live competition.

## Topics covered

- Athletic posture and movement; ready position; gather steps and crossover
- Six fundamental skills: serving, forearm passing, setting, attacking, blocking, digging
- Team offense, team defense, transition
- Out-of-system play
- Competitive-readiness mental skills
- Self-evaluation and progression-rubric design
