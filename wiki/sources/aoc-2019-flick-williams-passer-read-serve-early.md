# Training passers to read the serve early

## Summary
Flick-Williams isolates the perceptual skill of reading the server. A caller 10 feet inside the court faces the server and must shout the target passer and depth (short/deep) as soon as the ball leaves the server's hand — training the eye to extract pre-contact cues.

## Key claims / ideas
- Reading the serve is a separable cognitive skill that can be drilled without passing.
- Caller stands in court facing server; must immediately name passer and depth.
- Calls happen the instant the ball is released — forces early cue extraction.
- 10 reps, then rotate — keeps the drill brief and focused.

## Topics covered
- Serve reading / early perception training
- Pre-contact cue extraction
- Serve-receive cognition drill
