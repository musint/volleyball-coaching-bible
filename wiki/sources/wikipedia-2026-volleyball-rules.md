# Volleyball (Wikipedia) — rules extract

## Summary

The Wikipedia entry on volleyball aggregates FIVB-aligned rules of the modern indoor game: court dimensions, net heights, contact rules, libero restrictions, rally scoring, serving rules, and net/centerline rules. It captures specific NCAA divergences where they exist — NCAA libero-serving (since 2004) vs. FIVB ban, dual-libero rule adoption (NCAA 2024, FIVB earlier), and the 2008 set-to-25 change. It is useful as a cross-check reference and for high-level claims about the modern rulebook, but citation-critical numerical values should be re-verified against the current USAV Indoor Rulebook and the NCAA Women's Volleyball Rules Book.

## Key claims / ideas

- Court is 9 m × 18 m; the attack (3-meter) line divides front/back row.
- Net height is 2.43 m for men and 2.24 m for women; varies for veteran and junior play.
- Teams may use up to three contacts per possession.
- Double-contact is an error EXCEPT on the team's first contact, where multiple contacts by a single player are legal if part of a single play (2000 rule change).
- Rally scoring replaced side-out scoring (FIVB 1999-2000; NCAA 2001). Sets are played to 25 points, best-of-five, with the deciding fifth set to 15 points.
- NCAA changed games to "sets" and reduced to 25 points in 2008.
- Scholastic (US high school) often plays best-of-three to 25 (varies by state federation).
- The 8-second serve clock starts on the first referee's whistle.
- Service area is anywhere behind the end line (within the theoretical sideline extensions) since 2000; a served ball may touch the net and still be in play.
- The libero cannot block or attack a ball when it is entirely above net height.
- The libero must stand behind (not on) the 3-meter line to perform a legal overhand set whose resulting ball is attacked above the net in front of the 3-meter line.
- FIVB bans the libero from serving; NCAA allows it in one specific rotation (since 2004).
- Dual-libero designation: FIVB already permits it; NCAA women's adopted it for the fall 2024 season.
- A ball over the net must pass completely between the antennae.
