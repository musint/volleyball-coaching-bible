# Karch at 60

## Summary
Published by FIVB on Karch Kiraly's 60th birthday (November 3, 2020), this retrospective article covers his playing legacy, his coaching tenure with the USA Women's National Team through 2020, and includes direct quotes from Kiraly on his proudest coaching moment and on managing the team through the pandemic. Because FIVB is the international federation for the sport, it is a useful tier-1 biographical anchor for any wiki page that needs a compact, authoritative career summary.

The article captures Kiraly mid-tenure, after the 2014 World Championship gold and the 2018/2019 Nations League titles, but before the 2021 Tokyo gold. The "pandemic adjustments" quote is a small but unusually candid window into how Kiraly viewed remote / distanced team management.

## Key claims / ideas
- Kiraly's proudest coaching memory as of 2020 was the 2014 World Championship: "Watching our indoor women winning their first-ever major championship, the 2014 World Championship, was really special."
- Team-chemistry hurdle during COVID: adjusting to "radically reduced amounts of live, in-person human to human interaction."
- Head-coach record at time of article: 222-47 across 8 seasons (82.5% win rate).
- Career highlight list: 2014 World Championship gold; 2015 World Grand Prix title; Rio 2016 bronze; 2018 & 2019 Volleyball Nations League titles.
- Playing legacy: only player (man or woman) to win Olympic gold in both indoor (1984, 1988) and beach (1996); named FIVB's greatest volleyball player of the 20th century; 148 beach tournament titles across 29 seasons.
