# Defending the spread offense & the three blocking postures

## Summary
Ben Josephson (Texas Associate HC, former men's coach) addresses the defensive problem of a spread offense — where all three attackers are legitimate threats including seam/31 plays — and outlines two middle-blocker positioning adjustments plus a taxonomy of three blocking postures.

## Key claims / ideas
- Two MB/RS-blocker options against a spread offense that attacks the 31:
  - (1) MB "front reads" by taking a half-step toward the right-side blocker to cover the 31, staying balanced enough to still recover to either pin.
  - (2) MB stays neutral and the right-side pin blocker shades a half-step into the court to become the primary 31 blocker.
- Three blocking postures — each with value, each chosen based on the blocker's size, athleticism, and quickness:
  - Under the ball
  - On the ball
  - Over the ball

## Topics covered
- Defending against a spread offense (three-attacker threat)
- Middle-blocker positioning vs. the 31 (quick-behind-the-setter)
- Right-side blocker coverage adjustments
- Blocking posture taxonomy (under/on/over the ball)
