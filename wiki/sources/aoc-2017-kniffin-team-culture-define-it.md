## Summary

Debbie Kniffin (Iowa State middle blocker 2008-11, former Badger Region program director) argues "culture" is volleyball's most overused word and that coaches owe their players a specific, context-anchored definition before using the term.

## Key claims / ideas

- "Culture" without a per-program definition is meaningless jargon.
- Coaches should define what *gritty / healthy / winning* culture specifically means in their gym (point-to-point playstyle, peer accountability, etc.).
- Test the stated definition against the observed culture; correct either the definition or the culture to align them.
- Clearly-communicated culture language lets players and staff buy into concrete expectations rather than vague slogans.

## Topics covered

- Team culture (definitional clarity)
- Coach-to-player communication
- Program values & standards
