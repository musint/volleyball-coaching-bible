# Russia Women's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia results-and-history reference for the Russia women's national volleyball team and its USSR / Unified-Team predecessor — governed by the Russian Volleyball Federation and currently FIVB-suspended following the 2022 invasion of Ukraine. The article documents the deepest medal record of any women's volleyball program: 4 Soviet-era Olympic golds (1968 Mexico City, 1972 Munich, 1980 Moscow, 1988 Seoul), 6 Olympic silvers across USSR + Russia eras, 7 World Championship golds (1952, 1956, 1960, 1970, 1990 USSR; 2006, 2010 Russia), 20 European Championship golds, and a 1973 World Cup gold — a total Wikipedia tabulates as 36 major-tournament golds, 21 silvers, 16 bronzes. The post-Soviet program continued at world-class level through the 2010 Worlds gold, then declined through the 2010s before the 2022 FIVB suspension closed the competitive era. [[Nikolay-karpol|Nikolay Karpol]]'s Sverdlovsk-based Uralochka Yekaterinburg pipeline (founded 1966, club + NT-feeder identity) is the structural anchor of the post-Soviet program; Wikipedia does not tabulate Karpol's full NT-HC tenure dates here, but his coach page documents the USSR + Russia head-coach period.

## Key claims / ideas

- 4 Soviet-era Olympic golds: 1968 Mexico City, 1972 Munich, 1980 Moscow, 1988 Seoul. 2 Soviet-era Olympic silvers: 1964 Tokyo, 1976 Montreal.
- 1992 Barcelona Olympic silver as the Unified Team (CIS post-USSR transitional roster).
- 2 post-Soviet Olympic silvers: 2000 Sydney, 2004 Athens. No Olympic gold in the Russia era (1996-present).
- 7 World Championship golds: 1952, 1956, 1960, 1970, 1990 (USSR era); 2006 Japan, 2010 Japan (Russia era). The 2006 + 2010 back-to-back is the modern dynasty's defining run.
- 20 European Championship golds — the deepest European medal record of any women's program. Soviet-era 14 golds (1949-1991), Russia-era 6 golds (1993-2015).
- 1 World Cup gold (1973 Uruguay), 2 silvers (1989, 1999), 4 bronzes — peripheral relative to Olympics + Worlds.
- 3 World Grand Prix golds (1997 Kobe, 1999 Yu Xi, 2002 Hong Kong) under the post-Soviet program before WGP discontinuation.
- 1997 World Grand Champions Cup gold; 2001 silver; multiple 4th-place finishes 2013-2017.
- Wikipedia documents the 2019 World Cup HC as Sergio Busato (Italy) — another data point for the imported-Italian-HC pattern visible in [[stefano-lavarini]] (Poland), Marco Bonitta (Italy women's NT, prior Polish women's HC), and historical Italian methodology export.
- FIVB and CEV suspended Russia following the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine; HC vacant; FIVB ranking "Not Ranked" as of June 29, 2025; Russia banned from 2022-2025 Worlds and 2023 + 2026 Euros.
- Total tabulated medal count across all FIVB + CEV competitions: 36 Gold, 21 Silver, 16 Bronze — the deepest record in women's volleyball.
- Nataliya Goncharova-led 2019 squad was the last Russia roster to compete in a major FIVB event before the suspension.
