# South Korea Women's National Volleyball Team — Wikipedia Biography

## Summary
Wikipedia institutional record for the South Korea women's NT, a program with sustained competitive presence in three distinct eras: the 1970s (1973 World Cup bronze, 1976 Montreal Olympic bronze, 1977 World Cup bronze, 1974 World Championship bronze); the 1990s (1994 Asian Games gold; multiple Asian Championship medals); and the 2010s (2012 London Olympic 4th, 2014 Asian Games gold, 2020 Tokyo Olympic 4th). Anchors the dates and result records cited on the [[korean-school]] page and on individual coach pages. Tier 2 because Wikipedia for national-team articles is well-maintained but tactical/methodology descriptors are sparse — those require external coaching-feature material.

## Key claims / ideas
- 1976 Montreal: women's bronze (Korea's only Olympic women's volleyball medal to date)
- 1972 Munich, 2012 London, 2020 Tokyo: 4th-place Olympic finishes
- 1973 / 1977 FIVB World Cup bronzes
- 1967 / 1974 FIVB World Championship bronzes
- 1994 Hiroshima + 2014 Incheon Asian Games gold
- 12 total Olympic appearances 1964-2020; did not qualify Paris 2024 (relegated from VNL 2025)
- Documented head coaches in chronological order (Park Man-bok 1973 → ... → Kim Hyung-sil 2012 → Stefano Lavarini 2019-2021 → César Hernández González 2022-2024 → Fernando Morales / Cha Sang-hyun 2024-present)
- Captain lineage: Kim Sa-nee (2012) → Kim Yeon-koung (2014-2021) → Park Jeong-ah (post-2021 onward)
