## Summary

Wikipedia article on the Serbia men's national volleyball team, captured 2026-04-24. The men's program is recognized by FIVB and CEV as the inheritor of records from the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (1948–1991) and Serbia and Montenegro (1992–2006), achieving full Serbia identity in 2006. Aggregate record: 28 medals (5 gold / 7 silver / 16 bronze) across Olympic, World Championship, European Championship, World League, World Cup, and World Grand Champions Cup — including the 2000 Sydney Olympic gold (defeating Russia in straight sets), 1996 Atlanta Olympic bronze, three European Championship golds (2001, 2011, 2019), 1998 World Championship silver, 2010 World Championship bronze, and 2016 World League gold. Lists six head coaches (Gajić → Travica → Kolaković → Grbić → Kovač → Kolaković return → Crețu).

## Key claims / ideas

- Yugoslavia / Serbia and Montenegro / Serbia continuity: FIVB and CEV consider Serbia the inheritor of SFR Yugoslavia and Serbia and Montenegro records.
- 2000 Sydney gold characterized as "dominating three-set win over Russia" — the program's signature competitive moment.
- Kolaković tenure (2007–2014) produced 2010 World Championship bronze + 2011 European Championship gold; resigned September 2014.
- Grbić tenure (2014–2019) produced 2016 World League gold (first ever after 5 silvers) + 2019 European Championship gold.
- Kovač tenure (2019–2021) sustained 2019 European Championship gold inheritance.
- Wikipedia characterizes the program as a "balanced, tactically disciplined team" emphasizing strong middle-blocking defense, consistent serving and passing, and coaching continuity under European-trained strategists.
- Yugoslav Olympic Committee named the 2000 gold-medal team best male team of the year; Serbia's Olympic Committee repeated this honor in 2010 and 2013.
