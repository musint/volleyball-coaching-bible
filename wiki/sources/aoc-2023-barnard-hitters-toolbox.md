# Refining your hitter's toolbox

## Summary
Oregon State's Mark Barnard argues that most hitters don't need a complete arsenal — owning one defense-forcing shot is enough, because the opponent's adjustment to stop it will create other scoring windows.

## Key claims / ideas
- Elite hitters have a wide arsenal (power, tips, roll shots, tools) but this is rare at most levels.
- Each hitter only needs to be "great at one shot" — if that shot forces the defense to adjust.
- Example: a reliable sharp-angle attacker forces the defense to shift; the shift opens a line shot.
- Reframes hitter development as defensive-displacement theory rather than generalist skill accumulation.
- Implication for practice design: develop each hitter's one reliable weapon before broadening.

## Topics covered
- Hitter toolbox development philosophy
- Defense-shift / one-shot economy
- Sharp-angle vs. line-shot interplay
- Implications for individualized hitter training
