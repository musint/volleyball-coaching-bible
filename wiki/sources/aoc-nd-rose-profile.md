# Russ Rose — AOC Coach Profile

## Summary

AOC's founder profile of Russ Rose, framing the full scope of a 42-year head-coaching tenure at Penn State (1979-2021). The page summarizes both the headline record (1,309 wins — first all-time among NCAA D1 head coaches at retirement — and 7 NCAA titles, including four straight from 2007-2010) and the development record beneath it (50 All-Americans, 110 total All-America honors, 4 AVCA Players of the Year, 14 Big Ten Players of the Year, 213 Academic All-Big Ten, 12 Academic All-Americans).

The profile underlines a few less-cited data points: Penn State participated in every one of the first 40 NCAA D1 Women's Volleyball Championships; Rose led back-to-back 38-0 seasons in 2008 and 2009; and the program's baseline, in any fully-competed year, was 22 wins. Rose came from George Williams College's 1974 NAIA national championship team (he was captain in 1975). Personal detail: married Lori Barberich, a three-time Penn State All-American, in 1986.

The page, used in conjunction with the 2017 "10 Things" piece, grounds Rose's authority as an AOC co-founder.

## Key claims / ideas

- 1979-2021 head coach, Penn State — 42 seasons.
- 1,309 career wins; 7 NCAA titles (4 consecutive 2007-2010); 17 Big Ten championships.
- Back-to-back 38-0 seasons (2008 and 2009).
- Never fewer than 22 wins in fully-completed seasons.
- Penn State competed in all 40 NCAA D1 Women's Volleyball Championships through Rose's tenure.
- 3 AVCA National Coach of the Year awards (5 total); 5 Big Ten Coach of the Year awards; AVCA Hall of Fame 2007.
- 50 All-Americans / 110 All-America honors; 4 AVCA Players of the Year.
- 213 Academic All-Big Ten; 12 Academic All-Americans.
- Graduated George Williams College 1975 (NAIA championship team 1974; captain 1975).
- AOC co-founder.
