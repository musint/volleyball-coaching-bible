# Running the middle from center court

## Summary
Tod Mattox (The Bishop's School) trains the middle attack off an antenna-high "push" set when the setter is at or behind the 10-foot line. The drill locks in approach cadence — "soft right, hard left, step, close, jump, hit" — with hard left timed to ball-in-setter's-hands.

## Key claims / ideas
- Setting is harder from behind the 10-foot line; train it with reps.
- Setter starts at 10-foot line just right of center, tosses, delivers an antenna-high push to the middle of the net.
- Hitter's first (soft) right step happens just before ball reaches setter.
- Hitter's hard left step lands when ball is in the setter's hands.
- Remaining approach completes after the set: step, close, jump, hit.
- Full cadence: soft right, hard left, step, close, jump, hit.
- Variation: hitters call "push" during the approach to cue the setter.
