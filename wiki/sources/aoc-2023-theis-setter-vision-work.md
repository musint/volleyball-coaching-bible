# Setter vision work

## Summary
Ryan Theis (Florida) runs a vision drill that forces the setter to scan the court continuously: passers and hitters flash visual cues that the setter must pick up and call out while running the offense, training peripheral awareness and decision-speed.

## Key claims / ideas
- Setter efficiency depends on constant awareness of what's happening on the court.
- Vision/scanning is trainable through drills that force the setter to identify visual cues from teammates.
- Calling out what the setter sees closes the loop and makes the scan checkable.

## Topics covered
- Setter peripheral vision and scanning
- Court awareness for setters
- Drill design — visual cue recognition
