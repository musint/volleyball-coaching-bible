# Developing young setters — Erin Virtue (AOC, 2015)

## Summary
Erin Virtue, then USA Junior National Team and Michigan assistant coach, narrates her framework for developing younger setters in this Art of Coaching Volleyball premium video clip (March 25, 2015). The piece is short but high-signal: it captures the screening principles she uses for identifying a young setter (first-step quickness over height, vocal leadership traits visible by age 12) and her training-design preference (extra one-on-one time with the setter, video study because young athletes are visual learners). Because the AOC clip is the only captured Virtue-narrated content in the corpus, it is the primary methodological anchor for her position-development reputation.

## Key claims / ideas
- **First-step quickness is the primary physical screen for a young setter**, not height. First-step quickness allows setters to cover more ground and use their hands more often.
- **Especially at younger ages, height should not necessarily be a determining factor** in identifying setters.
- **Vocal ability** is the leadership trait to look for — usually apparent even at age 12.
- Two diagnostic questions to ask of a young setter: "What is the setter doing between plays to make the next play better?" and "How good is the communication when things go well or poorly?"
- **Repetition leads to consistency** — spend extra one-on-one time "with the quarterback" before or after practices.
- **Younger players are typically visual learners** — sharing video of top-level setters is itself a developmental input.
