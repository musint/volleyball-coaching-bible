# Kelly Sheffield — Wikipedia biography

## Summary
The Wikipedia biography of Kelly Sheffield is the secondary-source anchor for his complete head-coaching résumé — Albany (2001–2007), Dayton (2008–2012), and Wisconsin (2013–present) — plus his 2021 NCAA national championship and three NCAA championship-final appearances (2013, 2019, 2024). The page enumerates his AVCA regional awards, Big Ten coaching honors, conference titles at all three head-coaching stops, and the All-Americans he has coached at Wisconsin (Dana Rettke, Sarah Franklin, Lauren Carlini, Sydney Hilley, Mimi Colyer). Where official AVCA pages do not surface a coach-by-coach award history, this Wikipedia compilation is the cleanest single page to anchor citation traffic.

## Key claims / ideas
- Sheffield is from Muncie, Indiana (b. July 14, 1970), Ball State alumnus.
- Three D-I head-coaching stops: Albany (142–79), Dayton (131–33), Wisconsin (348–73 entering 2026).
- Overall career head-coaching record: 621–185 (.770).
- Wisconsin won the **2021 NCAA national championship** by defeating Nebraska in five sets in the title match.
- Wisconsin reached the NCAA championship-final match three additional times under Sheffield: 2013 (lost to Penn State), 2019, and 2024.
- Five Big Ten regular-season titles at Wisconsin (2014, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022).
- Three Big Ten Coach of the Year honors (2014, 2019, 2022); Volleyball Magazine National Coach of the Year (2013).
- Five AVCA Northeast Region Coach of the Year (2010, 2012, 2014, 2019, 2022) plus AVCA North Region COY 2025.
- Dana Rettke was AVCA National Player of the Year in 2021; Sarah Franklin was AVCA NPOY in 2023.
- Pre-Wisconsin assistant stops: Houston (1997), Virginia (1998–99), Clemson (2000).
