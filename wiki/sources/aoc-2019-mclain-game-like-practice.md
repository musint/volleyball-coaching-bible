## Summary

Kim McLain (Corban University) recommends opening practice with a competition to fuel competitive spirit from minute one, and running last-game (5th-set) scenarios nearly every practice to prime players for clutch moments.

## Key claims / ideas

- Opening drill should be a competition — prevents "practice lag."
- Running 5th-set (decision-game) scenarios nearly every practice normalizes late-match pressure.
- Clutch capability is trainable through repeated exposure, not willpower.

Competition-first practice opens; 5th-set scenario rehearsal; game-like practice design.
