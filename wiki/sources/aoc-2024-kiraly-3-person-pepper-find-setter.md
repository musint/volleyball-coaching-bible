## Summary

Karch Kiraly's 3-person pepper variation adds setter-location awareness to a classic ball-control drill. One player stands in the middle between two outside players. The rally is pass-set-hit: each outside player passes the ball to the setter, who sets it back, who hits it to the other outside player. Between rallies, the setter relocates — behind the players, wide off the side of the court, or right beside them — forcing the digger to read and redirect the pass to a moving target instead of a fixed spot.

## Key claims / ideas

- The digger's job in real volleyball is to put the ball on the setter, wherever the setter happens to be — not to a fixed location.
- Moving the target between reps trains reading and direction of the dig, not just the dig itself.
- Three players and a ball is all that's required — fits a warmup or small-group block.
- Works as both communication and accountability work: the digger owns the outcome of finding the setter.
