# Volleyball blocking fundamentals: Positioning and posture

## Summary
Joe Sagula (North Carolina '90-'22) covers static-blocking fundamentals — distance from the net, hand position at eye level, athletic posture, net penetration, and balanced landing — as the first line of defense.

## Key claims / ideas
- Start roughly six inches from the net with hands ready in front of the face at eye level.
- Hands stay up (face level), never waist-level or lower, for blocking effectiveness and speed.
- Athletic posture with slightly bent knees is essential for effective blocking.
- Two keys to first-line defense: (1) jump and reach over the net (penetration); (2) land balanced and ready for transition.
- A well-executed landing is emphasized as core to block execution, not an afterthought.

## Topics covered
- Static blocking setup and distance from the net
- Hand height and readiness position
- Athletic posture for blockers
- Net penetration
- Balanced landing and transition readiness
