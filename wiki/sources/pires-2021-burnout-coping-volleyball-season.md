# Burnout and Coping in Volleyball Players Across an Annual Season (Pires & Ugrinowitsch 2021)

## Summary

Pires and Ugrinowitsch's (2021) longitudinal study in the *Journal of Human Kinetics* tracked **54 professional volleyball athletes** (28 men, 26 women; mean age 25.57) across **four measurement points spanning 10 months** of a competitive season. Researchers measured burnout (Athlete Burnout Questionnaire — physical/emotional exhaustion, reduced sense of accomplishment, sport devaluation) and coping skills (Athletic Coping Skills Inventory) at each point and tested how these states evolved across the season.

The findings reveal a specific pattern: **reduced sense of athletic accomplishment** rose significantly from season start through season end — players felt progressively less effective. **Sport devaluation** (loss of meaning) also rose with competition intensification. By contrast, **physical and emotional exhaustion** stayed relatively stable. Burnout in volleyball, then, is not primarily a fatigue story — it is an erosion of meaning and efficacy. **Coping strategies did not change** across the four measurement points; athletes' coping repertoires were fixed while their stress profiles rose. Confidence and motivation showed moderate inverse correlations with burnout dimensions at every measurement point. Practical implication: coaches should periodize *psychological* load alongside physical load, and confidence/motivation monitoring is a defensible leading indicator of burnout risk.

## Key claims / ideas

- Burnout in volleyball is progressive across a season, not episodic.
- The dimensions that rise are *reduced accomplishment* and *sport devaluation* — meaning and efficacy, not just fatigue.
- Athletes' coping skills do not adapt across a season without intervention; coping repertoire is fixed while load rises.
- Confidence and motivation are inversely correlated with burnout — they buffer burnout risk.
- Practical: periodize psychological load. Build confidence/motivation check-ins into mid- and late-season staff rhythm.
