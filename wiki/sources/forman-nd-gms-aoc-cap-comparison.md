# Best learning platform for new volleyball coaches — GMS, AOC, or CAP? (Forman)

## Summary

John Forman's direct comparison of the three dominant U.S. volleyball coach-
education platforms — Gold Medal Squared, Art of Coaching Volleyball, and
USAV's Coaching Accreditation Program. Written as a new-coach recommendation,
the piece is rare for offering a single credentialed coach's side-by-side
evaluation of all three at once.

## Key claims / ideas

- **New coaches: start with GMS.** "If a newer volleyball coach asked me this
  question I would likely tell them to do GMS first" — because GMS "provides a
  comprehensive platform" covering all game aspects with a science- and
  evidence-based approach. GMS gives a template the coach can customize as
  they learn.
- **CAP is best for coaches without competitive playing experience.** CAP I
  covers fundamentals thoroughly, so it is well-matched to coaches who don't
  have a playing background to fall back on.
- **CAP tier-structure is front-loaded for experienced players.** "You have to
  suffer a bit through the basics in CAP I before you can get to the meatier
  stuff in CAP II and III." The "meatier stuff" is not disputed; the sequencing
  is the obstacle for experienced coaches. This is the most concrete critique
  of CAP's tier structure from a CAP III holder.
- **AOC is problem-solving layer, not foundation.** AOC is useful "from more
  of a problem-solving perspective, offering ideas on how to address specific
  things" — fills out a framework rather than providing one.
- **Forman does NOT characterize CAP as bureaucratic or a gatekeeping barrier.**
  His critique is pedagogical (sequencing) and (in his companion continuing-
  education post) structural (voluntary enforcement). Citing this piece for a
  "bureaucratic gatekeeping" claim would misrepresent the source.

## Topics covered

- GMS as comprehensive-framework platform.
- AOC as problem-solving layer.
- CAP as introduction-heavy tiered program with pedagogical-sequencing obstacle
  for experienced players.
