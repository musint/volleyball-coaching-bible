# Building consistent passers through accountability and reps

## Summary
Mike Lingenfelter of Munciana Volleyball Club demonstrates how he teaches passing through competitive drills, high rep volume, and progressions from controlled tosses to live serves. Central mantra: "reps equal confidence."

## Key claims / ideas
- Every passing drill should have a winner and a loser to build accountability
- Progressions: controlled tosses with feedback → tosses over the net → live serves
- Balance work (passing on one foot), quiet platform training (one-armed passes, side-to-side moving passes)
- Pass-and-cover: pass then sprint to a designated spot to prevent passers from watching their pass
- Avoiding jammed passes: train passers to hop back and take the ball lower on the platform
- Communication after each pass — what each passer will do depending on serve situation

## Topics covered
- Competitive passing drill design
- Rep-based confidence building
- Platform management under pressure
- Pass-and-cover habits
- Handling balls that jam the passer
