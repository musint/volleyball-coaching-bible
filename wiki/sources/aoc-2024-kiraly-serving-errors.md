# Karch Kiraly’s 2-minute take: Serving effectively means making a few errors

## Summary
Video-backed, minimal text body. Kiraly argues the risk-reward math of the serve: at most levels, a team getting every serve in isn't applying enough pressure. Going for harder, flatter serves and accepting some misses is the expected-value play.

## Key claims / ideas
- If your team lands every serve, you aren't pressuring the opponent enough.
- Serving is "a huge point-scoring weapon" — treat it as offense, not just ball-start.
- The right target is harder, flatter serves with an accepted error rate.
- The framing is EV: some misses are the price of serves that actually disrupt opponent reception.
