# France Women's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia results-and-history reference for the France women's national volleyball team. Functions in this wiki as the structural counter-evidence for the [[french-school]]'s "women's program structural gap" critique — France women hold no Olympic, FIVB World Championship, FIVB World Cup, or CEV European Championship medal across the program's recorded history. The article documents only 4 World Championship appearances (1952, 1956, 1974, and 2025); only 1 Olympic appearance (2024 Paris as host, 11th place, 0-3 pool stage); and a European Championship record peaking at 6th place (2023). The program's two genuine bright spots in modern records are the 2022 CEV European Volleyball League title and the 2023 FIVB Challenger Cup gold — second-tier silverware that secured top-flight pathways. As of June 2025 the team is FIVB-ranked #16 with [[cesar-hernandez-gonzalez]] as head coach (the same Spanish coach who built [[vakifbank-istanbul]]'s mid-2010s era), succeeding the 2024 Olympic interim of Belgian coach Émile Rousseaux.

## Key claims / ideas

- France women made their Olympic debut at Paris 2024 — the program's first-ever Summer Games appearance (lost all three pool matches, 0-9 set count). Prior to 2024, the program had no Olympic appearance from 1964-2020.
- World Championship best result is 7th place at 1952 (Soviet Union, the team's inaugural appearance); 2025 Thailand quarterfinals 8th-place finish (3-2, 11-9 sets) is the modern best.
- Wikipedia documents NO Olympic, World Championship, World Cup, or European Championship medal in program history. This is the on-record fact behind the [[french-school]] "women's program structural gap" critique callout.
- 2022 CEV Women's European Volleyball League: France won the championship — the program's most significant senior-level title on record.
- 2023 FIVB Challenger Cup: France won gold, securing FIVB Volleyball Nations League promotion.
- VNL results: 14th (2024), 9th (2025) — top-flight competition exposure but no medal contention.
- Current squad core (Paris 2024): captain Héléna Cazaute (OH), Iman Ndiaye (OPP), Lucille Gicquel (OPP), Nina Stojiljković (S), libero Amandine Giardino.
- The team is governed by [[ffv-france]] (Fédération française de volley-ball) and competes under CEV.
- Documented head coaches: Émile Rousseaux (Belgium, 2024 Olympic interim) and current HC Cesar Hernández González (Spain, 2024-present). The article does not provide a full chronological coaching list — the program's pre-2020s coaching history is sparsely covered.
- The article notes explicitly that France's women's program "differs significantly from the country's men's program" in international success — Wikipedia's own framing of the asymmetry that drives the [[french-school]] critique callout.
