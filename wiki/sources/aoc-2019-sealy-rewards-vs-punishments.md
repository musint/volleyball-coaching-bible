## Summary

Mike Sealy (UCLA '10-'22) and Russ Rose (Penn State '79-'21) present contrasting approaches to practice consequences. Sealy generally avoids punishments but will use consequences when focus lags. Rose ties consequence magnitude to margin of loss — lose by 10 reps, run 10 suicides — so "every play matters."

## Key claims / ideas

- Two legitimate coach philosophies on consequences exist: Sealy's low-punishment default with situational escalation vs. Rose's proportional consequences.
- Scaling consequence to margin of loss (Rose) teaches that every play matters.
- Unknown consequence (not telling players what the punishment is until after the loss) raises intensity per rep.
- Competition without consequences can go soft; consequence design is a coach choice worth thinking about.

## Topics covered

Consequence design; proportional consequences; motivation via unknown stakes.
