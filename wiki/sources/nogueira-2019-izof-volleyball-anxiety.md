# IZOF Model for Anxiety and Self-Efficacy in Volleyball Athletes (Nogueira-Filho et al. 2019)

## Summary

Nogueira-Filho and colleagues (2019) tested **Hanin's Individual Zone of Optimal Functioning (IZOF) model** in seven male professional volleyball players in Brazil across an entire competitive season. Each player completed a multidimensional pre-match anxiety inventory (CSAI-2 reduced) and a volleyball-specific self-efficacy scale before every match; performance was extracted via Data Volley scouting at end of match. The study confirmed Hanin's foundational IZOF claim — optimal pre-match anxiety differs substantially between athletes — in volleyball specifically, and extended IZOF logic from anxiety to self-efficacy. Two players (a middle blocker and an opposite) had cleanest in-zone profiles and were also the season's highest performers. The practical implication for coaches: pre-match psychological state should be profiled per athlete, not handled with uniform "calm down" or "fire up" pre-match talks.

## Key claims / ideas

- IZOF is empirically applicable to professional volleyball — pre-match anxiety levels matter, but optimal levels differ across athletes.
- Optimal anxiety zones differ by 2-12 points across players within the same team — individualization is essential.
- IZOF logic extends from anxiety to self-efficacy: each player has an optimal self-efficacy zone, not "more is always better."
- Within-zone matches outperform out-of-zone matches for the same athlete — within-athlete IZOF is also empirically supported.
- Coach implication: profile each starter's pre-match psychological state, then design personalized pre-match interventions.
