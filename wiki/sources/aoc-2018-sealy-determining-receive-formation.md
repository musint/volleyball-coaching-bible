# Determining the serve-receive formation that's right for your team

## Summary
Mike Sealy (UCLA) walks through the formation taxonomy: two-passer, three-passer (traditional, sliver, show-and-take), four-passer (cup), and five-passer (W) — with tradeoffs and specific use cases for each.

## Key claims / ideas
- Two-passer: best two split the court; they cover a lot of space.
- Three-passer traditional: each takes ~10 feet of court evenly.
- Three-passer "sliver": pinch three to one side; one player gets only ~5 feet, creating an optical illusion that often makes servers decelerate.
- Three-passer "show and take": middle (or sideline) passer moves to a decoy spot, then fills the opposite space as server tosses.
- Four-passer "cup": protects a targeted LS hitter and improves short-serve coverage.
- Five-passer "W": 3 front + 2 back with setter hiding behind a front; whole-court coverage; seams are challenging and demand communication.

- Formation tradeoffs (coverage, hitter protection, deception)
- Formation-by-personnel selection
