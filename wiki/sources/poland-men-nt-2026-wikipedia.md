# Poland Men's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical and results reference for the Poland men's national volleyball team ("Biało-Czerwoni"), tracing the program from its 1974 World Championship and 1976 Montreal Olympic gold-medal era, through a long competitive plateau, a 2009 European Championship breakthrough under Daniel Castellani, the Andrea Anastasi 2011-2013 modernization that lifted Poland from 11th to 3rd in FIVB rankings, the Stéphane Antiga 2014 home-soil World Championship gold, the Vital Heynen 2018 World Championship defense, and the Nikola Grbić 2022-present era that delivered 2023 European Championship gold, 2023+2025 Nations League gold, and 2024 Paris Olympic silver — Poland's first Olympic medal in 48 years. Establishes Poland as one of three programs (with Italy and Brazil) at the very top of men's international volleyball through the 2010s-2020s.

## Key claims / ideas

- Poland's modern men's program is "one of the most successful national teams" with three World Championship titles (1974, 2014, 2018), two European Championship titles (2009, 2023), Olympic gold (1976) and silver (2024).
- As of October 5, 2025, Poland holds the **#1 FIVB world ranking**.
- Andrea Anastasi (Feb 23, 2011 - Oct 2013) inherited a team ranked 11th and produced three medals in 2011 alone (World Cup silver, World League bronze, European bronze) plus 2012 World League gold.
- Stéphane Antiga (Oct 24, 2013 - Dec 31, 2016) won the 2014 World Championship at home, defeating Brazil 3-1 in the final — Poland's first World title in 40 years and first hosted World Championship win since the USSR and Czechoslovakia.
- Vital Heynen (Feb 7, 2018 - 2021) defended the World title in 2018 (3-0 over Brazil in Italy/Bulgaria), produced 2019 World Cup silver and back-to-back European bronzes.
- Nikola Grbić (Jan 12, 2022 - present) delivered 2022 World Championship silver in his first major tournament, then 2023's triple (Nations League, European Championship, Olympic qualification), 2024 Paris silver, 2025 Nations League gold and World Championship bronze.
- Team identity is described as "strong attacking capability with excellent serve reception."
- Notable players: Bartosz Kurek, Michał Kubiak, Mariusz Wlazły, Karol Kłos, Paweł Zatorski, Krzysztof Ignaczak.
