# Gold Dust: Remembering the US Men at Beijing 2008 (USA Volleyball)

## Summary

USA Volleyball's own retrospective on the 2008 gold-medal campaign of the USA Men's National Team under Hugh McCutcheon — the program's first Olympic gold since 1988. The piece frames the tournament around two storylines: on-court excellence capped by a finals win over Brazil, and the off-court tragedy in which McCutcheon's father-in-law Todd Bachman was killed and mother-in-law Barbara seriously injured in an attack at Beijing's Drum Tower during the opening ceremony.

During McCutcheon's three-match absence, Assistant Coach Ron Larsen led the team with consulting support from John Speraw, Marv Dunphy, and Carl McGown. McCutcheon returned August 16 for the China match. In the final, middle blocker David Lee blocked decisively and opposite Clay Stanley — tournament MVP — delivered the clinching kill.

## Key claims / ideas

- Quote (McCutcheon on returning): "I wouldn't have come back if I wasn't ready. It was great to get back into the game."
- Quote (McCutcheon on culture): "It's never been a one-person show. We've always been talking about team — and that goes for the coaches, too."
- Quote (Lloy Ball): "For 12 guys and a 10-person staff to come together and become the world's best is not a small accomplishment."
- USA's first men's Olympic gold since 1988, third overall.
- Same quadrennial as USA's first-ever FIVB World League title (2008).
- Coaching succession during crisis: Ron Larsen as acting head coach; staff-of-many support model.
