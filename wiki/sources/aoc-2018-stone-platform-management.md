# Managing Your Platform When Passing

## Summary

An AOC Quick Hits video tip from Jim Stone (Ohio State, 1982-2007; USA Youth National Team, 2008-2019) on a small but high-leverage detail in serve receive: when to form the platform. Stone argues passers should not pre-form the platform on the way to the ball — instead, they should stay relaxed and let the lower body arrive first, forming the platform at the last moment so the angle can still be adjusted to the serve's actual path.

The second theme is posture: "A passer should never be stiff." Because the serve path keeps evolving until contact, a tight passer is a passer who can't re-solve the geometry. Stone specifically frames this as a habit to build in youth development — teach the calm, late-platform read early and it sticks.

## Key claims / ideas

- "Keep the arms relaxed, and don't prematurely form your platform until your lower body has adjusted to the serve location." — enables last-second angle adjustment.
- "A passer should never be stiff, as they constantly have to readjust to the serve before actually making contact."
- Late platform formation is a trainable habit; best installed during youth development.
