# Deciding to set the overload play or the isolation play

## Summary
Ryan Hofer (Trinity Western) and Gina Schmidt frame every offensive play as containing both an overload and an isolation — the setter's job is to read which will hurt the opposing block and choose accordingly based on hitter strength vs. blocker weakness.

## Key claims / ideas
- Every play contains both an overload and an isolation; the setter picks one.
- Overload = setting the attacker against the bunched blockers.
- Isolation = setting the attacker left with only one blocker because the bunch is on the other side.
- Choose based on "strongest attackers vs. least effective blockers" — e.g., tall OH against a small blocker → isolate.

- Overload vs. isolation decision-making
- Matchup identification
- Setter tactical reading
