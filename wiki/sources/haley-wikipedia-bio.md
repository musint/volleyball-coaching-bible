# Mick Haley — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

The Wikipedia biography of Mick Haley documents a four-stop NCAA-and-NT coaching arc spanning Kellogg Community College (1973–1979), the University of Texas (1980–1996), the U.S. Women's National Team head-coach role (1997–2000), and USC (2001–2017) [haley-wikipedia-bio]. Haley won three NCAA national championships across Texas and USC — Texas 1988 (the program's first NCAA title) and USC 2002 and 2003 — with the 2003 USC team going undefeated 35–0 as the first NCAA repeat champion to do so unbeaten [haley-wikipedia-bio]. The page documents his AVCA Hall of Fame induction and 2003 AVCA National Coach of the Year award, his 522–137–1 record at Texas, and his Sydney 2000 Olympic head-coach appearance (USA Women fourth-place finish) [haley-wikipedia-bio]. He played setter at Ball State University under Don Shondell, winning MIVA titles in 1964 and 1965 [haley-wikipedia-bio]. Haley anchors the pre-Elliott era at Texas and is the historical comparator for any modern Texas head-coach evaluation.

## Key claims / ideas

- Born August 18, 1943, Angola, Indiana
- Played setter at Ball State; MIVA titles 1964, 1965; B.A. Ball State (1965); M.A. Southern Illinois (1966)
- Kellogg Community College HC (1973–1979): 251–51; women's NJCAA national titles 1978, 1979
- Texas HC 1980–1996 (17 seasons): **522–137–1**; AIAW championship 1981; **NCAA championship 1988**; Final Fours 1986, 1987, 1995
- USA Women's NT head coach (1997–2000): Sydney 2000 Olympic fourth place
- USC HC 2001–2017: **NCAA championships 2002 and 2003**; 2003 team 35–0 (first NCAA repeat champion to go undefeated)
- AVCA Hall of Fame; 2003 AVCA National Coach of the Year
