## Summary

Terry Liskevych — former Oregon State head coach and USA women's Olympic coach — walks through his library of pepper variations. His core teaching point: pepper should actively train spatial awareness, not just groove the three basic contacts. The variations he demonstrates include digging overhand (to rehearse reading high balls), jumping on the attack (to progress toward a live swing), and rotating partners to keep partners and angles changing.

## Key claims / ideas

- Pepper is one of the most widely used drills in volleyball, and its value scales with the variations you layer on.
- Spatial awareness should be an explicit training target during pepper reps.
- Overhand dig variation builds the read for balls above the shoulder that would otherwise become bad forearm platforms.
- Jumping on the attack progresses pepper toward a live swing and adds conditioning.
- Rotating partners varies spacing, angles, and tempo so the rep doesn't become rote.
