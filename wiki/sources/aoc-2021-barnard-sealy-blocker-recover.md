# How a blocker can recover from misreading the set

## Summary
Barnard and Sealy give two recovery options when a middle misreads: if within 3-4 feet of the pin blocker, throw up a block to seal the seam; if more than 4 feet away, drop off to cover tip or shank. Alternative: always block and shift the middle-back into the wide seam.

## Key claims / ideas
- 3-4 feet from pin blocker → still jump and try to seal/force seam hit.
- >4 feet from pin blocker → drop off net, stay low, cover tip or shank off inside hand.
- Alternative scheme: commit to always blocking, rotate middle-back defender wide.
- Decision rule hinges on closing distance, not effort.

- Middle blocker recovery on misread.

- Coordinated middle-back coverage of wide seam.
