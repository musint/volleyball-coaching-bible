## Summary

Terry Liskevych's "Drill of 15" — adapted from USA National Team practice. 4v4 with players in middle back, left back, left front, and setter; extras line up behind middle back. Teams must dig-set-hit cross court to zones 5 and 6. Target: 15 contacts in sequence without an error. Any error (out of bounds, double contact) resets to zero. "Neutral" rallies (in play but off-sequence) neither add nor reset, so players have to maintain sequence discipline under rising pressure. Variations: downball entry for younger teams, hard-driven hits only for higher levels.

## Key claims / ideas

- Drill of 15 is USA National Team-pedigree — Liskevych brought it back to the college/club level.
- Cooperative count-to-15 builds both ball control and mental resilience; mental pressure grows with the count.
- The "neutral" rally classification is a key design choice — the count doesn't advance, but it doesn't reset. Players must self-regulate under ambiguity.
- Cross-court-to-5-and-6 is the specific shot discipline; the ball must go to the high-percentage read zone.
- Standing downballs make it age-appropriate for 14U and below; hard-driven-only scales it to elite levels.
