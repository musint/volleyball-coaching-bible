# Antonio Perdomo — Olympedia biographical entry

## Summary
The cleanest single Tier-2 record for Antonio Perdomo Estrella (June 13, 1943 – November 9, 2009): full name, birth/death dates and places, role-as-coach (not player), and Olympic head-coach assignment at **2008 Beijing** with the **Cuban women's national volleyball team** (4th place). Olympedia's record reflects only Olympic appearances and does not catalogue assistant-coach assignments at earlier Games — so Perdomo's documented career as assistant during Barcelona/Atlanta/Sydney is documented in Spanish-language obituaries [[perdomo-2009-cuban-media-tribute]] and the women's NT Wikipedia [[cuba-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]].

This source is also the **disambiguation anchor**: confirms Perdomo's career was with the **women's national team**, not the men's program — correcting the implementation-plan premise that anchored him to "1976 Montreal Olympic men's bronze era."

## Key claims / ideas
- **Full name: Antonio Perdomo Estrella.**
- Born June 13, 1943, Havana, Cuba.
- Died November 9, 2009 (age 66), Havana, Cuba.
- **Coach (not a player).**
- 2008 Summer Olympics, Beijing — head coach, Cuban **women's** national volleyball team — 4th place.
