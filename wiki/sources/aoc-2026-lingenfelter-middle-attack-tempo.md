# Training middle attack tempo

## Summary
Mike Lingenfelter (Munciana Volleyball Club) defines middle-attack tempo with three plant-timing benchmarks (Tempo 1/2/3) and shares a progression of individual and team drills — pull-off-and-approach, platform-to-net, and Pin-to-Pin/Repeat Recovery — to train them.

## Key claims / ideas
- Tempo 3 = high ball to pins; middle plants left foot once ball has left setter's hands.
- Tempo 2 = middle plants left foot while ball is in setter's hands.
- Tempo 1 = middle is in the air while ball is in setter's hands (true quick).
- All three tempos use the same three-step approach; the difference is plant timing.
- Training progression: start at the net, pull off, approach on coach toss; then add passer/setter.
- Team-level tempo drills: Pin to Pin and Repeat Recovery.

## Topics covered
- Middle-attack tempo definitions (1/2/3)
- Three-step approach mechanics
- Plant-timing cues for middles
- Progression from coach toss to live pass-set
- Team drills for tempo (Pin to Pin, Repeat Recovery)
