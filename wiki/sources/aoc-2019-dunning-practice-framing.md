## Summary

John Dunning (Stanford '01-'16) argues every drill, practice, and conversation should be framed — stating purpose explicitly — to prevent practices where players do not know why they are doing what they are doing.

## Key claims / ideas

- Frame everything: drills, practices, conversations.
- An unframed drill produces wasted reps because players default to arbitrary cues.
- Framing is a coach discipline, not a drill type.

## Topics covered

Framing philosophy; drill purpose statement; coach communication design.
