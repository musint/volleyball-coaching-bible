# Cues for reading the set and anticipating the attack

## Summary
Christy Johnson-Lynch (Iowa State) outlines reading cues that let defenders and blockers predict the attack from the set location, hitter's arm swing, and body position.

## Key claims / ideas
- Tight set → likely a tip; defense creeps toward center court.
- Set outside the antenna → hitter forced into a sharp angle; blockers shift several feet toward the middle of the net.
- Arm swing and body position reveal attack tempo.
- Set behind the hitter with a backward lean on contact typically produces a weak/soft attack.
- Reading cues should be trained in practice by deliberately varying sets for outside, right-side, and middle hitters so defense and blockers make real adjustments.

## Topics covered
- Set-location-based defensive and blocking adjustments
- Hitter arm-swing and body-position reads
- Tip vs. hard-swing prediction
- Training reading within team practice
