# Bernardinho — IVHF Class of 2022

## Summary

Official **International Volleyball Hall of Fame** (Holyoke MA) induction
biography for Bernardo "Bernardinho" Rezende — inducted in the Class of
2022. The bio provides a credentialed, results-focused tenure record that
complements the Wikipedia biography: 1984 Olympic silver as a player,
1990–2000 Brazil women's NT tenure (Atlanta 1996 and Sydney 2000 bronzes),
2001-onward Brazil men's NT run (Athens 2004 gold, London 2012 silver, Rio
2016 gold, three FIVB World Championships in 2002/2006/2010, record ninth
FIVB World League title in 2010). Totals: 30+ major titles, 48 team
championships.

The IVHF bio's distinctive value for the wiki is its **direct
corroboration of the Italian-coaching stints** mentioned only briefly in
the Wikipedia biography: Bernardinho "went overseas to take the helm of
the women's team Perugia and then the men's team Modena in Italy" before
returning to Brazil to coach the women's NT in 1990. This establishes
Bernardinho's Italian-Brazilian methodology-exchange axis at both the
women's (Perugia) and men's (Modena) club-level — a concrete data point
for the Brazilian-school bidirectional-exchange claim.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Class of 2022 IVHF induction** as a coach (separate from player
  inductions) — Tier-2-credentialed endorsement of the dynasty record.
- **1984 Los Angeles silver** as a Brazil men's NT player — the "Silver
  Generation" cohort (shared with Dal Zotto and Bebeto de Freitas as HC).
- **Italy stints: Perugia (women's) + Modena (men's)** before returning
  to Brazil 1990 — corroborates and extends Wikipedia's Pallavolo Sirio
  Perugia 1990–1992 men's Serie A reference. Two separate Italian clubs
  across both gender programs.
- **1990–2000 Brazil women's NT:** 1990 FIVB World Championship silver;
  1990 FIVB World Grand Prix gold (inaugural edition); 1996 Atlanta and
  2000 Sydney Olympic bronzes.
- **2001-on Brazil men's NT:** two major international gold medals in his
  first season (2001); fourth FIVB World League title + Olympic gold
  Athens 2004; four international gold medals in 2005; record ninth FIVB
  World League title in 2010; London 2012 silver; Rio 2016 gold.
- **Totals:** 30+ major titles, 48 team championships as head coach.
