# Types of serves

## Summary
AOC primer cataloging the main serve types — float, short serve, jump top spin, and second tempo — and noting that coach-to-server hand signals are used at higher levels to direct serve location.

## Key claims / ideas
- Float serve: little to no spin, producing an unpredictable ball path.
- Short serve: lands inside the 10-foot line, forcing front-row players to pass.
- Jump top spin: fast, spinny serve that drops short.
- Second tempo: targeted down the middle between front-row and back-row passers (seam serve).
- Serving signs (hand signals from the bench) direct servers to locations and are more common at higher levels of play.

## Topics covered
- Taxonomy of serve types
- Serve trajectory/spin characteristics
- Serve placement to attack seams and short zones
- Coach-to-server tactical signaling
