## Summary

Mark Rosen (University of Michigan head coach, 1999-2022) demonstrates a progression for teaching swing-blocking footwork to beginners. Players line up at the 10-foot line in a middle-blocker starting posture. One at a time, each player swing-blocks to the left — first doing the footwork without a jump, then layering the jump on top once the steps are comfortable. Rosen's cue is to penetrate the hands over the net and take aggressive, long steps. The drill needs just one player and a half court, so it fits into an individual or small-group warmup.

## Key claims / ideas

- Swing blocking is taught as a footwork-first skill; the jump is added only after the step pattern is reliable.
- Aggressive, long steps are preferred to short choppy footwork once players understand the pattern.
- Hand penetration over the net is a non-negotiable coaching point even at the beginner stage.
- The 10-foot line serves as a starting reference for the middle-blocker position before the swing.
- This is low-equipment (half court, one player) and fits a warmup, pre-practice, or individual-defense block.
