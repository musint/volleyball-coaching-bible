# Post-game talk: Stay consistent, win or lose

## Summary
Ben Josephson (Texas associate HC) and Terry Liskevych argue that post-game talks should feel the same whether the team won or lost — the coach's consistency is what turns a result into learning.

## Key claims / ideas
- Post-game tone should not swing with the scoreboard; consistency signals stability to the team.
- If emotionally inflamed, wait 24-48 hours before the substantive talk.
- The goal is a strong, simple message focused on growth, not disappointment.
- Framing question: "What did we learn, and how do we move forward?"
- Applies to both win and loss conversations — emotional regulation as a coaching skill.
