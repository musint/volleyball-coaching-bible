# Strategies for better overhead passing

## Summary
Ben Josephson (Texas associate head coach, former men's coach) argues overhead passing has a lower error rate than forearm passing and drills it with a serve-receive format where passers must use hands. Variation: shift 1-2 passers up to probe how passing depth changes outcomes.

## Key claims / ideas
- Overhead passing has a lesser chance of error than forearm passing (a men's-game perspective).
- Drill: 3 passers + setter + servers; passers must use hands.
- Vary passer depth (first 10 balls normal, next 10 with 1-2 passers closer to net) to explore tradeoffs.

## Topics covered
- Overhead (hand) passing in serve-receive
- Passer-depth variation
- Overhead-pass drill format
