# 5-3-5-3 passing drill with Terry Liskevych

## Summary
Terry Liskevych's time-boxed passing streak drill: in a limited window (he uses 4 minutes), passers must hit 5 good passes, then 3 in a row, then another 5, then 3 in a row. Same pattern as the later "5-3-5-3-5" variant, without the final 5.

## Key claims / ideas
- Time-boxed pressure replaces fixed serve counts with a ticking clock.
- Alternating non-consecutive "5 good" and consecutive "3 in a row" goals trains both volume and streak consistency.
- Coaches should calibrate what counts as a "good pass" to the team's skill level.
- Origin-drill for later Barnard variant (aoc-2019-barnard-53535-passing-drill) and Mattox adaptations.

## Topics covered
- Time-boxed passing drill
- Streak goals for consistency
- Adjustable difficulty by passing-grade threshold
