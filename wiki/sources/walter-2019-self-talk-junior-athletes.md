# Effects of Self-Talk Training on Junior Sub-Elite Athletes (Walter et al. 2019)

## Summary

Walter, Nikoleizig, and Alfermann's (2019) randomized controlled trial in *Sports* (MDPI) examined how **self-talk (ST) training** affects junior athletes' psychological states and performance — and crucially, **how training duration moderates the effect**. They randomly assigned 117 athletes (ages 13-23, sub-elite competitive level) to either a one-week ST intervention (3 sessions), an eight-week ST intervention (3 sessions per week), or a no-training control. Outcomes (state anxiety, self-confidence, self-efficacy, volitional skills, coach-rated performance) were collected pre-intervention, post-intervention, and at 5-6 weeks follow-up.

The headline result: **duration matters substantially**. Eight-week ST training reduced somatic state anxiety, increased state self-confidence, improved general self-efficacy with sustained gains at follow-up, improved self-optimization (a volitional/goal-setting skill), and produced measurably better coach-rated performance throughout the study. One-week ST training produced transient effects on self-efficacy that did not persist; control showed no improvement. The implication for volleyball coaches at HS / college / club levels is concrete: a one-shot pre-match self-talk script is unlikely to produce durable change. Self-talk works as a sustained 8+ week training block, not as a match-week tool. The use of coach-rated performance (not just self-report) addresses a methodological criticism common in self-talk research.

## Key claims / ideas

- Self-talk is a trainable skill, not a one-time tool — duration of training is critical.
- Eight-week ST training reduced anxiety, raised self-confidence, raised self-efficacy with durability, and improved coach-rated performance.
- One-week ST training produced transient effects only; long-term training produced lasting change.
- Coach-rated (not self-rated) performance improvement strengthens the methodological case.
- Practical for volleyball: serve-routine self-talk and pre-match self-talk should be embedded in 8+ week training blocks, not introduced match-week.
