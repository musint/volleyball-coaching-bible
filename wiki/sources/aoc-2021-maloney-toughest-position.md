## Summary

Luke Maloney (assistant, Ohio State Men's Volleyball) argues the libero is the hardest position in volleyball because of its asymmetric scoring math: liberos can only stay neutral or go negative in the points column, while every other position has upside (kill, block, assist) to offset errors. The piece prompts coaches to redefine libero success and reduce avoidable mental pressure.

## Key claims / ideas

- The libero is the most mentally taxing position because they have no kill/block/assist upside to offset errors — errors can only be corrected by not making another one.
- Libero performance is measured in only two categories (4-point pass grade, digs/set); neither translates directly to the points column.
- The analogy to hockey/soccer goalkeepers is fair: defensive-only, rare scoring, perceived-by-team-success.
- NCAA programs systematically under-scholarship back-row players despite libero being one of the few positions in every set every year.
- Coaches often recruit liberos as afterthoughts to hitters and setters, undervaluing ball-control assessment.
- Coach action items: clearly define libero success, coach for mental load explicitly, avoid rotation churn that breeds self-doubt.
