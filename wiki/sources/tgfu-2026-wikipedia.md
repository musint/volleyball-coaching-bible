# Teaching Games for Understanding — Wikipedia

## Summary

The English-language Wikipedia article on Teaching Games for Understanding (TGfU) names the approach's three credited developers — Peter Werner, David Bunker, and Rod Thorpe — and reproduces the canonical six-stage TGfU model as presented in the Werner, Thorpe, and Bunker (1996) *JOPERD* article: game form, game appreciation, tactical awareness, decision making, skill execution, and performance. The page also notes that TGfU was "adopted globally in 2002 as a global agenda for scholarly inquiry in the field of teaching with the help of games."

## Key claims / ideas

- TGfU is a physical-education approach credited to Peter Werner, David Bunker, and Rod Thorpe.
- The canonical six-stage TGfU model is: (1) game form, (2) game appreciation, (3) tactical awareness, (4) decision making, (5) skill execution, (6) performance.
- TGfU was formally adopted as "a global agenda for scholarly inquiry" in 2002.
- The 1996 *JOPERD* article by Werner, Thorpe, and Bunker is a primary canonical reference; a Barba-Martín 2020 systematic review is cited as a more recent synthesis.
