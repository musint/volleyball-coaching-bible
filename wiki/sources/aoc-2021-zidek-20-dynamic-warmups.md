## Summary

Marie Zidek — DePaul University coach, CSCS, master's in sports medicine — presents AOC's canonical 20-exercise dynamic warmup library. The prescription: a dynamic warmup should be 5-10 exercises, 5-10 minutes total. Include at least one exercise from each of three components — balance, core, dynamic stretch. The exercises rehearse volleyball movement patterns (shuffle, lunge, hip hinge, rotation, explosive plyo) rather than generic cardio. The article includes a printable PDF for gym use.

## Key claims / ideas

- Good warmup duration: 5-10 exercises, 5-10 minutes total.
- Three mandatory components: balance, core, dynamic stretch (pick at least one from each).
- Warmup should rehearse volleyball-specific movement patterns, not generic jogging.
- Lunge elbow tuck with hamstring stretch is the "perfect all-in-one" exercise (hips, ankles, posterior chain, mid-back rotation, hamstrings).
- Plyometric exercises (high skips, speed skater) should come after the body is warmed up.
- Shuffle variants (basic, sumo, karaoke) train lateral movement patterns used in defense and blocking.
- Jump-preparation exercises (hopscotch, high knees, high skips) build toward approach-jump explosive capacity.
