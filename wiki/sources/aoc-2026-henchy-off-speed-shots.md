# Drill to improve off-speed shots

## Summary
UCSB's Michael Henchy teaches a 4v4 rotation drill for off-speed hitting, framed around disguise — hitters start every attack with a bent elbow so tips are indistinguishable from full swings.

## Key claims / ideas
- Disguise principle: every off-speed shot begins with a bent-elbow arm position identical to a hard swing.
- Four-role rotation (digger → setter → hitter → sub) keeps players rotating through every attacking phase each rally.
- Target-area progression: donut first, then short down the line, then high-line roll shots over the setter's head.
- Off-speed is trained as an aggressive scoring tool, not a default safety option.

## Topics covered
- Off-speed hitting drill structure
- Disguise and arm-swing mechanics for tips
- Shot placement targets for tactical tipping
- Small-group (4v4) hitting progressions
