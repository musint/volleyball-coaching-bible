# Rethinking the classic back row attacking drill

## Summary
Tod Mattox (The Bishop's School) reframes back row attacking as a primary offensive option, not just a backup, and shares a queen-of-the-court variant that trains early release on bad passes, setter-dump defense, and aggressive transition play.

## Key claims / ideas
- Back row attacks should be treated as a legitimate first-option play, not a bail-out.
- Back row attackers must prepare their approach the moment they read a bad pass.
- Early release lets the team stay aggressive even when out of system.
- Queen-of-the-court structure drives competitive reps on back-row attack plus setter-dump defense.

- Back-row attacking as offensive weapon
- Out-of-system transition offense

- Early release timing for back row hitters
- Defending the setter dump
