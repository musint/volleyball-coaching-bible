# Chasing Greatness

## Summary
*Chasing Greatness* is Karch Kiraly's 2023 self-improvement book, co-authored with Don Patterson (former executive editor of Volleyball Magazine). Published via the Art of Coaching Volleyball store, the book is billed as "a blueprint for self-improvement by the head coach of the U.S. women's 2021 Olympic gold medal team" and explicitly targets not only coaches and players but also business professionals and parents — anyone seeking personal improvement.

Structurally, it is 190 pages across 15 chapters, with a mental/developmental focus rather than an X's-and-O's focus. Its core themes — purposeful practice, adversity management, willpower, time optimization, self-confidence, teamwork, and relative-vs-absolute greatness — align closely with the "we are all learners" culture Kiraly installed with USA Women's Volleyball (see `kiraly-2020-all-learners`). In that sense, *Chasing Greatness* is the book-length codification of a philosophy he had been articulating in AOCVB columns and interviews for years. It is the second of Kiraly's three authored books; the first, *Championship Volleyball* (1996), is primarily technical.

## Key claims / ideas
- Greatness is relative, not absolute: the measurable pursuit is improving against your own prior self, not chasing an external benchmark.
- Purposeful (deliberate) practice is the limiting resource for improvement — volume without quality does not produce mastery.
- Adversity is not an obstacle to growth; embracing it *is* the growth mechanism ("Embracing Adversity").
- Willpower is a developable capacity, not a fixed trait.
- Time management is a performance skill ("Making Every Minute Count").
- Teamwork and teammate behavior can be deliberately trained.
- Mental performance — managing less productive thoughts, preparing to be your best — is coachable and modellable.
