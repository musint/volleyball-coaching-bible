# Texas Longhorns women's volleyball — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia's program-history page for Texas Longhorns women's volleyball. Used to corroborate the head-coach lineage (Lampley → Lyman → Conradt → Lowery → Haley → Moore → Elliott), the program's five national titles (1981 AIAW; 1988, 2012, 2022, 2023 NCAA), Final Four appearances under Elliott, the SEC transition date (July 1, 2024), and award details for Eggleston, Skinner, O'Neal, and Hooker.

## Key claims / ideas
- Jerritt Elliott head coach since 2001; three NCAA titles (2012, 2022, 2023).
- Texas's most recent title was 2023 — the 2024 team did NOT win the title; eliminated by Creighton in the 2024 NCAA regional semifinal.
- Texas joined the SEC effective July 1, 2024.
- Logan Eggleston: 2022 AVCA National Player of the Year; 2023 NCAA Woman of the Year.
- Madisen Skinner: 2023 NCAA tournament Most Outstanding Player; transferred from Kentucky.
- Asjia O'Neal: AVCA First-Team All-American 2022 and 2023.
- Destinee Hooker: 2009 Final Four MVP, 34 kills in the championship match.
- Texas took the No. 1 spot in the 2022 VolleyballMag.com NCAA recruiting class rankings.
- Tonya Johnson — Texas associate HC; later Georgia Tech HC (2009–) and now LSU HC (2022–).
- Home venue: Gregory Gymnasium (capacity 4,000); first regular-season match at the Moody Center came in the 2025 season (state-record 10,899).
