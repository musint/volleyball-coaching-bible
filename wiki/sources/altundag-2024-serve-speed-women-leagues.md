# Altundag, Soylu & Akyildiz 2024 — Turkish League Serve Speed Analysis

## Summary

A four-season multi-league analysis of serve velocity in Turkish professional volleyball: 15,347 serves total (6,943 female + 8,404 male), spanning the Sultans League (top women's tier), Women's 1st League, Women's 2nd League, and corresponding men's tiers. The study quantified the effects of player position, set number within a match, and league tier on serve speed, with two-way and three-way interaction analyses. Three main effects were highly significant: position (F=375.8, p<0.001), set (F=17.6, p<0.001 — within-match fatigue effect), and league type (F=356.9, p<0.001 — top-tier serves faster). The three-way interaction was not significant.

## Key claims / ideas

- **League-tier scaling:** women's serve speed scales with league tier — Sultans League > Women's 1st > Women's 2nd. Men's 1st League had the highest speeds; women's 2nd League had the lowest.
- **Within-match fatigue:** serve speed declines significantly across sets — late-set serving is measurably slower than first-set serving for the same athletes.
- **Position effects:** large and significant — position-specific serving curricula are statistically supported.
- **Two-way interactions:** position×set, position×league, set×league all significant.
- **Three-way interaction:** not significant (p=0.179) — the main effects are largely additive.

## Topics covered

- Serve velocity differences across women's vs men's leagues.
- Position effects on serve speed.
- Within-match fatigue effect on serving.
- Multi-league analysis methodology in Turkish pro volleyball.
