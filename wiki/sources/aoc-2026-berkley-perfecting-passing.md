# Teaching passing fundamentals with clear cues and daily drills

## Summary
Amanda Berkley (Loyola Chicago head coach) teaches passing with a seven "S" cues framework — Soon, Simple, Strong, Stick, Shoulders, Space, Steps — anchored by her daily "Karch Kiraly" drill and a 6v6 "Susie" pass-rating game.

## Key claims / ideas
- Seven S cues for passing: Soon (early platform), Simple (clean, minimal), Strong (firm, can't be pushed), Stick (hold platform), Shoulders (down, ball forward), Space (gap between platform and body), Steps (move feet)
- "Karch Kiraly" drill is a daily staple: overhead tosses across net with feet planted, then with footwork shuffles (forward angle, side, back), stay low and shoulders forward, progress to live serves deep/short/hand passing
- "Susie" drill (named for sister at Wisconsin-Milwaukee): 6v6, passes rated 0-3; a "3" pass = 3 points, add a kill = 2 more points (5 total)
- Daily pass reps anchor practice

## Topics covered
- Coaching cues for passing
- Platform technique (strong, space, shoulders)
- Footwork patterns in passing
- Daily team passing drill (Karch Kiraly drill)
- Pass-rating scoring games (Susie)
