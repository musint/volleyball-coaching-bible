# John Speraw — USA Volleyball Coach Profile

## Summary
The USA Volleyball federation profile for John Speraw, covering his dual-role era (2012–2024) as UCLA men's head coach and USA Men's National Team head coach, through his September 2024 appointment as USA Volleyball President and CEO. Speraw is the only individual in men's volleyball history to win an NCAA championship as head coach, assistant coach, and player: he won NCAA titles as a UCLA middle blocker (1993, 1995), three NCAA titles as head coach at UC Irvine (2007, 2009, 2012), and two more as UCLA's head coach (2023, 2024). He served as assistant to Hugh McCutcheon on the USA Men's 2008 Beijing gold-medal staff.

Internationally, Speraw's USA Men won gold at the 2014 FIVB World League and 2015 FIVB World Cup, three NORCECA titles (2013, 2017, 2023), and bronze medals at the 2016 Rio and 2024 Paris Olympics. He founded First Point Volleyball to expand men's and boys' volleyball in the U.S.

## Key claims / ideas
- Only person in men's volleyball to win NCAA titles as player, assistant, and head coach.
- Culture flows top-down: how the coaching staff operates trickles into how the team trains and plays.
- Equitable ≠ equal — tailor approaches to individual circumstances.
- Evolve from rigid rules toward flexible guidelines (Wooden influence).
- Ethical leadership (2008 Pillar Award).
- Advocacy for men's/boys' volleyball growth via First Point Volleyball.
