## Summary

Two HS coaches — Tod Mattox (The Bishop's School) and Tina Kinkead (La Jolla Country Day) — debate ideal roster size at the AOC Champions Clinic. Kinkead advocates 12 as ideal for Varsity (10-14 range across JV/V, up to 16 novice no-cut). Mattox settles at 13-14, prioritizing "team experience over winning experience," with explicit bubble-player conversations for roster-spot #13/14.

## Key claims / ideas

- Novice rosters can carry up to 16 when philosophy is development, not winning.
- JV sits at 12-14; Varsity ideal 12 even numbers for scrimmages.
- Mattox trades ~4-6% winning probability to give 4 more players a roster spot.
- Bubble-player speech: explicit honesty that playing time will be near zero; if the athlete can't accept it without bad attitude, cut them now.
- Make a big deal out of supporting roles — starters who disrespect subs get called out.
- Subs have the toughest role: one error can get them pulled; starters get leeway.
