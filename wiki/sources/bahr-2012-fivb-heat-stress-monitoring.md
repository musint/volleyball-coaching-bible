# Bahr & Reeser (2012) — FIVB Heat Stress Monitoring Programme

## Summary

Peer-reviewed primary research published in the *British Journal of Sports Medicine* documenting the FIVB's three-year (2009–2011) heat-stress monitoring program across 51 international beach-volleyball events. **WBGT measured pre-match at every event; 48 of 51 events captured complete data.** Findings: **9 events exceeded the Navy Black Flag threshold** (WBGT > 32.3°C), 2 more exceeded 31°C, and yet **only 1 heat-related medical forfeit** occurred across the entire 3-year program (and that case had GI complications). The authors conclude that **existing heat-stress guidelines may be too conservative** for elite acclimated beach-volleyball athletes. The finding is specific to top-tier acclimated populations and does **not** generalize to junior, club, or non-acclimated players — for whom WBGT > 31–32°C remains a meaningful flag. Useful as an evidence anchor for indoor-tournament heat protocols and travel-acclimation planning.

## Key claims / ideas

- FIVB monitored 51 events over 3 years (2009–2011); 48 events with full WBGT data.
- 9 events exceeded WBGT 32.3°C (Navy Black Flag); 2 additional above 31°C — yet only 1 heat-related medical forfeit total.
- Existing heat-stress guidelines may be too restrictive for elite acclimated beach-volleyball athletes.
- Finding does NOT generalize to junior / club / non-acclimated players — conservative limits should remain in those populations.
- WBGT is the appropriate environmental-heat-stress index for volleyball.
- Documentation of program-level heat protocols is good practice particularly for poorly ventilated indoor tournament venues during summer travel.
- Acclimation, hydration, and shade-protocol matter even below clinical heat-illness threshold.
