# The setter tip: Smart ways to score with the dump

## Summary
Jed Stotsenberg maps dump zones by setter position: middle-court "donut" and the Zone-2-to-3 seam yield high-percentage tips depending on net position. Mechanically, tip higher (not low into the block), angle toward the left sideline, "tap down" rather than "lift and drop," hold setting shape until the last instant, and learn to tip with both hands.

## Key claims / ideas
- High-success dump zones: middle-court "donut" (from right-pin or middle-of-net setter); Zone 2-3 seam (from middle-of-net or past-middle setter); Zone 3 (from left-pin setter).
- Common setter error: contact too low → ball goes into the block.
- Correct: take the ball higher, angle toward the left sideline.
- "The goal is not to lift and drop. It's just to tap it down."
- Hold setting shape until a split-second before contact to preserve disguise.
- Setters should tip with BOTH hands — left when the ball is off the net, right when the ball is tight.
