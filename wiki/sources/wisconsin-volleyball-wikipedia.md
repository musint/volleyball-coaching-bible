# Wisconsin Badgers women's volleyball — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia's program-history page for Wisconsin Badgers women's volleyball. Used to corroborate the full head-coach lineage (Pedersen → Carney → Lowe → Fitzpatrick → Cook → Waite → Sheffield), the Sheffield-era NCAA finals appearances (runner-up 2013, 2019; champion 2021), the Big Ten regular-season title ledger, the Dana Rettke five-time-First-Team-All-American distinction, and the UW Field House attendance leadership (2nd nationally in 2021 at 7,540 average).

## Key claims / ideas
- Sheffield arrived 2013 from Dayton (prior stint at Albany). Wisconsin had not won a national title before his arrival.
- Sheffield-era NCAA finals: 2013 (lost to Penn State), 2019 (lost to Stanford), 2021 (won over Nebraska in five sets at Nationwide Arena).
- Big Ten regular-season titles under Sheffield: 2014, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022 — five in nine seasons of the Sheffield era.
- John Cook was Wisconsin's HC from 1992–1998 before moving to Nebraska in 2000. This matters for inter-program lineage: the same coach who built modern Nebraska also coached Wisconsin in the 1990s.
- Dana Rettke is described as "the nation's only ever five-time first team All-American" (2017–2021).
- Lauren Carlini and Dana Rettke both won Olympic silver with Team USA in Paris 2024.
- 2021 NCAA championship MVP: Anna Smrek.
- UW Field House capacity 7,540; 2021 attendance led to a No. 2 national ranking among Division I women's volleyball programs.
