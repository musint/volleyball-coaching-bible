# Massimo Barbolini — Wikipedia biography

## Summary

Biographical and career reference for Massimo Barbolini (b. August 29, 1964, Modena), one of the most-decorated club coaches of the modern Italian women's-volleyball era and head coach of the Italy women's national team 2006–2012. The era under Barbolini delivered Italy women's two European Championship golds (2007, 2009) and two World Cup golds (2007, 2011) — the program's peak between Marco Bonitta's 2002 World Championship cycle and Davide Mazzanti's 2017–2024 rebuild. At club level Barbolini holds four CEV Champions League / Champions Cup titles across four different clubs (Matera 1995–96, Sirio Perugia 2005–06, Casalmaggiore 2015–16, AGIL Volley / Igor Novara 2018–19), the 2016 FIVB Club World Championship with Eczacıbaşı, and a decade-long Sirio Perugia tenure (1997–2007) yielding three Serie A1 titles. He returned to the Italy women's program in 2024 as vice coach under [[julio-velasco]] for the Paris 2024 Olympic gold cycle. The English Wikipedia article is sparse on methodology and quotes; the Italian Wikipedia (cross-referenced into the raw snapshot) adds club-history detail and the early-career Velasco apprenticeship at Panini Modena men's 1985–1989 but is similarly thin on doctrinal material.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Italy women's NT head coach (2006–2012).** **2007 European Championship gold; 2009 European Championship gold; 2007 World Cup gold; 2011 World Cup gold; 2009 Grand Champions Cup gold.** Four consecutive Grand Prix bronzes (2006, 2007, 2008, 2010). No Olympic medal: Beijing 2008 quarterfinal exit (vs. USA, 2–3); London 2012 quarterfinal exit (vs. South Korea).
- **Italy women's NT vice coach (2024–).** Returned to the federation under Julio Velasco for the Paris 2024 cycle; Italy women's took Olympic gold at Paris 2024.
- **Turkey women's NT head coach (2013–2015).**
- **Velasco apprenticeship.** Vice coach under Julio Velasco at Panini Modena men's 1985–1989 — the four-consecutive-Serie-A1-titles core of the [[julio-velasco]] / [[italian-school]] men's-volleyball ascent. Barbolini is therefore a direct methodological descendant of the Velasco men's program who carried the lineage into Italian women's volleyball as a head coach across the next four decades.
- **Four CEV Champions League / Champions Cup club titles, four different clubs:** 1995–96 Matera; 2005–06 Sirio Perugia; 2015–16 Volleyball Casalmaggiore; 2018–19 AGIL Volley (Igor Gorgonzola Novara). Plus 2016 FIVB Club World Championship with Eczacıbaşı.
- **Sirio Perugia 1997–2007 (10 seasons):** 3 Serie A1 titles (2003, 2005, 2007), 4 Coppa Italia, 2005–06 Champions League — Barbolini's longest single-club tenure and the foundation for his 2006 federation appointment.
- **Individual honors:** Luigi Razzoli Award (Italian best coach) 1995, 2003, 2005, 2007; CEV Coach of the Year (women's club) 2019; Palma d'oro al Merito Tecnico 2019.
- **Methodology / philosophy / quotes:** essentially absent. Wikipedia frames Barbolini's record as career-results-and-titles; on-record coaching-doctrine material is not present in either the English or Italian articles.
- **Disambiguation note:** the user-provided brief mentioned "Bergamo" and "VakıfBank Istanbul (assistant)" in Barbolini's club résumé. Wikipedia does not place Barbolini at either. Bergamo women's volleyball of the late 1990s was Marco Bonitta's club, not Barbolini's. The Turkish-club affiliations in Barbolini's record are Galatasaray (2012–2015 head coach; 2025–2026 head coach) and Eczacıbaşı (2016–2017 head coach), not VakıfBank.
