# Akarçeşme et al. (2022) — Heart rate and blood lactate during the volleyball match

## Summary

Peer-reviewed primary research published in *Scientific Reports* (Nature) quantifying physiological response in 24 elite female volleyball players across competitive match play, segmented by position. **Whole-sample mean HR 117.2 ± 13.9 bpm; lactate 2.7 ± 1.2 mmol/L.** Position differences are stark: **liberos run highest** (130.6 bpm; 17.9% of match at ≥80% HRmax) while **setters run lowest** (101.3 bpm; 1.9% at ≥80%). Spikers and middles fall between. Lactate range 1.5–7.0 mmol/L was non-significant between positions but trending highest in liberos. **Over 50% of match time at ≤60% HRmax for all positions** — the sport is intermittent, with most time spent recovering between rallies. Pairs with Akyildiz et al. (2022) and Rebelo et al. (2024) on the case for position-specific conditioning prescription rather than uniform programming.

## Key claims / ideas

- Match-mean HR for elite female volleyball ≈ 117 bpm; lactate ≈ 2.7 mmol/L.
- Liberos work hardest (130.6 bpm; 17.9% at ≥80% HRmax) — by far highest cardiovascular load.
- Setters work least (101.3 bpm; 1.9% at ≥80% HRmax) — by far lowest cardiovascular load.
- Middles and spikers fall in between (118.8 / 116.9 bpm).
- > 50% of match time below 60% HRmax for all positions — volleyball is intermittent, not steady-state.
- Lactate alone doesn't separate positions; sRPE + jump count are better load proxies.
- Position-specific conditioning prescription is supported by physiology, not just convention.
