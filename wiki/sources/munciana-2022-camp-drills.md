# 2022 Munciana Camp Drills (Coach Library) — Mike Lingenfelter

## Summary

A 64-minute video tutorial in which [[mike-lingenfelter]] walks Munciana camp staff through every drill they will run during a summer Munciana youth camp. The intended audience is camp coaches running Munciana programming — the explicit framing in the opening minutes is "I'm going to go over every drill that will possibly be in your camps to give you a library so you don't have to feverishly scribble down drills and can actually watch your team participate in the camp and make assessments." Drills are demonstrated in the order they would be coached: ball-control / passing → defense → serving → setting → hitting → blocking → 6v6 game-like work.

This is a primary source: the speaker is the program's co-director instructing his own staff on his own drill library, captured in his own words. Trust-tier 1.

## Key claims / ideas

- **Pursuit-and-recovery as the foundation.** The library opens with a two-man shuffle drill: hold low, shuffle down and back, no ball — pursuit footwork as the first thing camp athletes do.
- **Closure on the ball.** Lingenfelter repeatedly emphasizes "simple closure on the ball" before adding passing volume — straight-line passing is built around centering the body and finishing on balance.
- **Triangles as the multi-skill format.** Triangle passing, triangle passing off a box B, and the later "tight triangles" setting/hitting drills all use the same three-corner geometry to combine multiple skills in a small space, a Munciana signature.
- **Defense begins with speedball.** Speedball is named explicitly as "the intro to defense" in the camp progression — defense is taught from a games-based ball-up-everywhere starting point, not from isolated platform work.
- **The Three P's of defense — get back, get low, get a touch.** Articulated as a coaching shortcut for the defensive ready position and pursuit decision.
- **Figure-eight defense and "teacher" drills.** A coaching-driven movement progression with the coach feeding from a fixed location, athletes flowing through a figure-eight pattern that forces the full pursuit footwork inventory.
- **Aim small, miss small for serving.** Munciana's serving philosophy is target-driven from day one: "all too often in serving we just serve to serve, and it becomes somewhat of a dumpster" — instead, every serve is at a finite target (down to a 2-inch area) so athletes hold themselves accountable to placement.
- **No-gut serving + five-before-ten.** Rotating serving drills daily under aggressive-mentality framing — repeated emphasis that camp practice must rehearse the *aggressive* serving mindset, not just the mechanic.
- **Tight triangles for setting/hitting integration.** The post-serving section opens with tight triangles as the first setting/hitting drill — small-court, fast-feedback geometry consistent with the rest of the library.
- **Camp progression mirrors a full practice arc.** Pass → defend → serve → set → hit → block → 6v6 mirrors the practice flow Munciana would run with its own teams; the camp library is the club's drill library, not a separate camp-only inventory.
