## Summary

Asymmetric-score pressure drill: one side starts at 21, the other at 17. Team with 21 serves throughout and can only score on their own serve. If the trailing team sides out, they earn 1 point plus a free ball for a possible 2nd. Trains aggressive end-of-set side-out play.

## Key claims / ideas

- Simulating late-set scoreline pressure forces aggressive passing / swinging even when behind.
- Constraining serve side to "points only on their serve" rewards tough serving over safe serving.
- Bonus-free-ball after successful side-out models the real-match rhythm of chaining points.
