# Blocking drill progression: Keeping the blocker engaged

## Summary
Springfield College's Moira Long presents a blocking drill progression that uses boxes, color/number cards on hitter shoulders, and a "ball, setter, hitter" eye sequence to train blockers to stay visually locked on the hitter.

## Key claims / ideas
- Use "ball, setter, hitter" verbal cue to reinforce eye-tracking sequence.
- Hitter's shoulder is the key visual cue for attack angle.
- Color/number cards on hitter shoulders force blockers to keep eyes on the hitter's body, not the ceiling.
- Math problems on cards increase attention and reaction load.
- Reading is a team-wide habit — not just blockers.
