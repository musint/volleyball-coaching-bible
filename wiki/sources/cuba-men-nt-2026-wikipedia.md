# Cuba men's national volleyball team — Wikipedia

## Summary
Reference page for the Cuban men's NT, used on [[eugenio-george]] for context on his pre-1968 men's-program coaching (1963 junior, 1966 CAC gold), and on [[antonio-perdomo]] as the **disambiguation source** that confirms Perdomo was NOT the 1976 Montreal head coach (the Wikipedia article does not list a comprehensive head-coach chronology, and no external source connects Perdomo's name to the men's program in 1976).

Documents the **1976 Montreal Olympic bronze** (Cuba's only Olympic medal in men's volleyball to date) without naming the head coach. The 1976 roster is preserved (Leonel Marshall, Victoriano Sarmientos, Ernesto Martínez, Víctor García, Carlos Salas, Raúl Vilches, Jesús Savigne, Lorenzo Martínez, Diego Lapera, Antonio Rodríguez, Alfredo Figueredo, Jorge Pérez Vento). Orlando Samuels Blackwood (per other sources outside this file) was head coach 1989–1994 and 2007–2013, post-1976.

## Key claims / ideas
- **1976 Montreal — Olympic bronze (Cuba's only Olympic men's volleyball medal).**
- World Championship: silver 1990, 2010; bronze 1978, 1998.
- World Cup gold 1989; silvers 1981, 1991, 1999.
- NORCECA Championship: 16 titles, 1969–2019.
- Pan American Games: 5 golds (1971, 1975, 1979, 1991, 1999).
- World League gold 1998.
- World Grand Champions Cup gold 2001.
- **Wikipedia does not provide a comprehensive head-coach chronology**; the article does not name the 1976 men's NT head coach.
- "After 2001, many players defected from Cuba" — affecting team composition.
