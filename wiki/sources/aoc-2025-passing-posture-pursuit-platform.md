# Passing technique: Posture, pursuit, platform

## Summary
Kansas head coach Ray Bechard breaks passing into three teachable components — posture, pursuit, and platform — to be trained in isolation and then recombined under game-like conditions.

## Key claims / ideas
- Posture: bent knees, shoulders over the knees, right foot slightly ahead of the left; this stance enables quick lateral and front/back motion.
- Pursuit: the ability to chase the ball in any direction depends on staying low and ready.
- Platform: thumbs held parallel produce a flat passing surface.
- Drill progression should isolate each component before combining them in realistic scenarios.

## Topics covered
- Passing posture and stance
- Ball pursuit and movement off the platform
- Platform construction (thumbs parallel)
- Component-then-integration teaching progression
