# Japan Women's National Volleyball Team (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia results-and-history reference for the Japan women's national volleyball team (火の鳥NIPPON / "Hinotori Nippon," the modern nickname). The program is the historical anchor of [[japanese-training]] — the school whose 1964 Tokyo Olympic gold under [[daimatsu-hirobumi]] became the founding event of organized women's international volleyball outside the USSR. The article documents 70 total major-tournament medals across 14 Olympic appearances, 18 World Championship appearances, and 13 World Cup appearances: 2 Olympic golds (1964 Tokyo, 1976 Montreal), 2 silvers (1968 Mexico City, 1972 Munich), 2 bronzes (1984 Los Angeles, 2012 London); 3 World Championship golds (1962, 1967 home, 1974), 3 silvers (1960, 1970, 1978), 1 bronze (2010 home); 1 World Cup gold (1977), 2 silvers (1973, 1981). As of June 2025 the team is FIVB-ranked #5 with Turkish-national Ferhat Akbaş as head coach and Mayu Ishikawa (b. 2000) as captain.

## Key claims / ideas

- 1964 Tokyo Olympic gold ("Oriental Witches" / Daimatsu era) was won 5-0 with 15 sets won and 1 dropped — defeating the heavily favored Soviet Union; captain Masae Kasai is the era's iconic figure. This is the founding result for [[japanese-training]] as a school of thought.
- 1976 Montreal Olympic gold was a perfect 5-0 with 15-0 sets — the program's only flawless Olympic gold and the second-greatest result in program history.
- Three consecutive World Championship golds 1962-1967-1974 (with 1962 a perfect 9-0, 27-1; 1967 perfect 3-0 home; 1974 perfect 11-0, 33-2). Wikipedia frames this as the "6 major world titles" 1960s-1970s peak.
- 2012 London bronze under unspecified HC ended a 28-year Olympic-medal drought (since 1984 LA bronze) — Wikipedia frames this as "a critical program resurgence."
- 2010 World Championship bronze on home soil (8-3, 28-17) is the most recent global-medal result in the program's record.
- Tokyo 2020 host-nation 10th-place pool-stage exit (1-4) is the lowest Olympic result; Paris 2024 followed at 9th (1-2 pool stage).
- Japan's all-time head-to-head record vs. Brazil is 1-13 across Olympics + Nations League — the most unfavorable matchup on the table; vs. Italy 2-5; vs. China 5-8; vs. USA 5-8; vs. Soviet Union 3-3 (notable parity).
- "Oriental Witches" nickname reflects domestic media characterization of the 1964 team's "tactical intelligence and teamwork, in contrast to Western volleyball's perceived reliance on raw athleticism" — a framing that maps directly to the [[witches-of-the-orient]] page and the Daimatsu methodology.
- Modern HC succession: Tadayoshi Yokota documented in 1994; Ferhat Akbaş is current. Wikipedia's coverage of 1964/1976-era HCs is incomplete in the article body (Daimatsu's 1964 tenure is acknowledged via era-naming rather than tabulated).
