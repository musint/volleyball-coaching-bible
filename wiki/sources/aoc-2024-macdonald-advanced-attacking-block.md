# Advanced attacking: Using the block

## Summary
Kerry MacDonald (Volleyball Canada Chief Sports Officer, former UBC Men's head coach) and Gina Schmidt (Canada Youth NT) outline three ways attackers can weaponize the block: vision (read strength/location/holes), finding holes/seams, and using the block itself (high hands, tooling, wipeoffs, tipping).

## Key claims / ideas
- Train attackers to see the block: strength, location, penetration, any holes.
- A late middle blocker opens a seam between two blockers; attack that seam into dig gaps.
- Hit high off a tall blocker's hands to force scrambling, unpredictable digs.
- Work around the block by swiping, tipping, or tooling the edges.
- These are advanced attacking tactics — vision precedes tactical execution.

## Topics covered
- Vision training for hitters
- Reading the block (holes, strength, penetration)
- High-hands attacking
- Tooling, swipe, tipping
- Attacker-defender seam exploitation
