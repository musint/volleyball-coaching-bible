# Serve receive: Dividing the court

## Summary
Andrew Clifton on receive positioning: push passers inward to challenge servers to hit sidelines, then adjust with a shuffle and a sideline-platform angle. Court-division is personnel-driven — strong passer covers more, weak passer gets a sliver — and passers should read the server's toss and arm speed for early cues.

## Key claims / ideas
- Moving inward forces servers to a small, difficult sideline target.
- Passers counter sideline serves with shuffle steps and a platform-angle-to-setter adjustment.
- Court-area allocation scales with passer skill — more area for the best passer, less for the weakest.
- Reading toss height: a server arching to reach the toss usually produces loopy serves.
- Reading arm swing: fast swing usually produces quick, deep serves.

## Topics covered
- Court-division strategy in serve receive
- Passer positioning and shuffle-to-sideline
- Early server reads (toss, arm speed)
