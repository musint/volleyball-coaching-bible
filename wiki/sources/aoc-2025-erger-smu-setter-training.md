# SMU's comprehensive setter training

## Summary
SMU associate head coach Sam Erger describes an end-to-end setter development model that stacks fundamentals, tempo training, decision-making on good and bad passes, and live-ball integration with hitters. The emphasis is on efficiency, consistent reps, and investing heavily in the setter at every level because of the outsized influence the position has on the court.

## Key claims / ideas
- Setter influence on the court justifies disproportionate training investment at every level.
- Efficiency and consistent reps matter more than volume without intent — make every rep count.
- Tempo training, entry patterns, and middle-attack precision are trainable through structured progressions.
- Out-of-system decision-making should be rehearsed, not improvised in matches.
- Setters should be developed to be athletic, smart, and adaptable — not just accurate hands.

## Topics covered
- Setter training program structure (fundamentals through live play)
- Tempo and entry patterns
- In-system vs. out-of-system decision-making
- Setter-hitter integration in live-ball reps
- Middle attack execution
