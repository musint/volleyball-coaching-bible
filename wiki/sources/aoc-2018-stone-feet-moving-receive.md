# Keep your feet moving in serve receive

## Summary
Jim Stone's split between lower- and upper-body roles in serve receive: feet must stay light and multi-directional; the platform stays the quietest, steadiest piece adjusting calmly to ball location.

## Key claims / ideas
- Lower body = light, quick, ready to move any direction.
- Upper body = loose but quiet; platform is the steadiest component.
- Separating "mover" (legs) from "striker" (platform) is the organizing principle.
- The platform should adjust calmly to ball location, not jerk to it.

## Topics covered
- Serve-receive footwork
- Platform stability
- Upper-lower body task separation
