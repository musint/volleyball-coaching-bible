## Summary

Republished Championship Performance article arguing that superior athletes develop half-hearted work ethic when under-challenged, and offering drill-asymmetry mechanisms: 1-on-2 drills for one superstar, weaker-side extra players, handicaps, and limited-outs structures borrowed from multiple sports.

## Key claims / ideas

- Under-challenged stars regress toward mediocrity through half-hearted practice.
- Drill asymmetry (1v2, extra players on weaker side, handicaps) is the mechanism, not coaching harder.
- Competitive balance across uneven rosters requires explicit handicap design.
- Starter expectation: "If you're a starter, you work harder." The asymmetry is the workload signal.

## Topics covered

Elite-player challenge design; roster-asymmetry handling; cross-sport handicap examples; starter workload philosophy.
