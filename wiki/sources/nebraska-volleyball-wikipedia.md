# Nebraska Cornhuskers women's volleyball — Wikipedia

## Summary
Wikipedia's program-history page for Nebraska Cornhuskers women's volleyball. Used to corroborate the head-coach lineage (Sullivan → Pettit → Cook → Busboom Kelly), the five-title NCAA championship history (1995, 2000, 2006, 2015, 2017), the six runner-up finishes (1986, 1989, 2005, 2018, 2021, 2023), the Memorial Stadium attendance record (92,003 on August 30, 2023), and the documented Cook coaching tree.

## Key claims / ideas
- Terry Pettit (1977–1999): 694–148–12; led Nebraska to its first national title in 1995.
- John Cook (2000–2024): 722–103; four titles as head coach (2000, 2006, 2015, 2017). Retired after the 2024 season.
- Dani Busboom Kelly named head coach January 29, 2025; eight years at Louisville (203–44, .822); won 2024 AVCA National Coach of the Year before returning. Six-year, $4.575M contract.
- "Volleyball Day in Nebraska" at Memorial Stadium, August 30, 2023, drew 92,003 — described in coverage as "one of the highest-attended women's sporting events ever."
- 337-match home sellout streak at Devaney Center (and previously the NU Coliseum), dating to 2001.
- Cook coaching tree (corroborated by Omaha World-Herald and Lincoln Journal-Star reporting): Dani Busboom Kelly (Louisville → Nebraska); Chris Tamas (Illinois HC); Christy Johnson-Lynch (Iowa State HC); Craig Skinner (Kentucky HC); Dan Meske (Louisville HC after Busboom Kelly); Dan Conners (UC Davis HC); Tyler Hildebrand (Long Beach State HC, then USA Volleyball beach).
- Notable program alumnae: Jordan Larson (4× Olympic medalist), Sarah Pavan (4× First-Team AVCA, Honda Sports Award winner), Kelsey Robinson (3× Olympic medalist), Lexi Rodriguez (2021 AVCA Freshman of the Year).
- Devaney Center floor dedicated in Pettit's honor.
