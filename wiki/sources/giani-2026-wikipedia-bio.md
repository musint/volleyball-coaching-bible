# Andrea Giani (Wikipedia)

## Summary

Wikipedia biographical article covering the career of Italian coach and former player Andrea Giani (b. April 22, 1970, Naples). Documents his Generazione di Fenomeni-era playing career (Italy NT 1988-2005, 474 caps, three FIVB World Championship golds 1990/1994/1998) and his subsequent coaching career, currently head coach of France men's NT (2022-present), where he won 2024 Paris Olympic gold.

## Key claims / ideas

- Born April 22, 1970, Naples, Italy. Father Dario was an Olympic rower (1964).
- Began volleyball at age 14 (1985) with Sabaudia.
- Club career: Parma (1985-1996, three Italian titles); Modena (1996-2007, two CEV Champions League titles 1996-97 and 1997-98, three Italian titles).
- Italy NT player 1988-2005, 474 caps; member of Velasco-era "Generazione di Fenomeni."
- Player honours: three FIVB World Championship golds (1990, 1994, **1998**); four European Championship golds (1993, 1995, 1999, 2003); silver Olympic 1996 and 2004; bronze Olympic 2000; five-time Olympian.
- IVHF induction: 2008.
- Coaching: Modena (2007-08), Roma Volley (2008-12), Calzedonia Verona (2013-16), Slovenia NT (2015-16, 2015 European Championship final), Power Volley Milano (2017-19), Germany NT (2017-21), Modena Volley (2019-23), **France NT (2022-present)**, ZAKSA Kędzierzyn-Koźle (2024-).
- France results: 2022 + 2024 VNL gold; **2024 Paris Olympic gold (defeated Poland 3-0 in final, sets 25-19, 25-20, 25-23, August 10, 2024)**.
