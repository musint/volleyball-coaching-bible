# Offensive & defensive strategies for Ro.1

## Summary
Diane Flick-Williams (Western Washington) and Mark Barnard (Oregon State) outline rotation-1 serve targeting and receive adjustments: "serve to disrupt the pattern," shift lineup if the RS is a weak passer, and read opponent receive shifts in real time.

## Key claims / ideas
- "Serve to disrupt the pattern": put the ball between the setter and a nearby teammate.
- Target weakest passer, or serve straight at the middle to complicate their attack transition.
- Ro.1 requires the RS hitter to pull back to pass; setter releases from behind her.
- If the RS is a weak passer, shift so the LF passes first before becoming a hitter.
- Always align the lineup so the strongest passers receive, especially when stuck siding out.
- Monitor the opponent's receive shifts and adjust serve targeting in real time.

## Topics covered
- Rotation-1 serve targeting
- Receive formation adjustments by passer strength
- In-match rotation reading
