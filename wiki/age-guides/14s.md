# Age Guide: 14s — late-fundamentals → specialization onset

This guide is the operational layer for the wiki's primary coaching context: **14U girls club volleyball at a nationally-recognized program**. It assumes you have already read [[age-lens-14u]], which sets the developmental philosophy, age-appropriate ceilings, and source canon for this age band [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules][usav-2009-cap-game-like-training]. This guide goes one layer deeper: match-spec milestones, position-by-position skill targets, [[munciana-volleyball-club]]-style operational depth on practice design, and the promotion-criteria language that drives the [[tryout-rubric-14u]] scoring scale. Adjacent age-guides are [[13s]] (the runway in) and [[15s]] (the runway out).

## Overview & development phase

Phase: **late-fundamentals → specialization onset.** A 14U athlete in their second year of organized 6s should be running stable forearm-pass mechanics, contacting overhead sets with hand-hinge rather than chest-trap, executing a reliable standing-topspin serve to zone, and beginning to defend live attacks with a recognizable platform-and-pursuit identity [aoc-2024-kids-passing-fundamentals][aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge][aoc-2024-kids-serving-fundamentals]. The fundamentals are no longer being installed for the first time — they are being *consolidated under tournament pressure* while position identity emerges underneath. By late season a coach should be able to name, for almost every athlete, a primary position and a secondary position they could legitimately play at 15s.

The developmental load is heavy and uneven: pre-adolescent to mid-adolescent growth is rapid and asymmetric, and coordination, strength, and proprioception shift week to week [till-2022-ltad-practitioner-knowledge]. What 14s adds beyond [[13s]] is **specialization onset** — a deliberate narrowing of the developmental fan-out. The libero-candidate pool starts running serve-receive-heavy reps separately from the OH pool [aoc-2024-kiraly-training-libero]; the setter pool gets dedicated tempo work [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-attack-tempo]; the middle pool starts learning the three-tempo footwork even if Tempo 1 is months away [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-tempo]. This is the age where Hugh McCutcheon's "generalized specialists" pipeline begins meaningfully — broadly skilled athletes with one or two emerging strengths, not early-narrowing single-position specialists [usav-2026-hp-clinic-lessons-shared].

## Skill milestones

### Passing
- **Milestone:** Forearm pass is reliable from a tossed serve and a moderate-pace standing-float serve, and *introducing* under live tougher serves [aoc-2024-kids-passing-fundamentals].
- **Anchor framework:** Kiraly's 4-keys — stopped and balanced; thumbs together with platform formed late; angle set with the shoulders, not the arms; simple one-way motion through the ball [aoc-2013-kiraly-4-keys-forearm-pass].
- **Coaching cue layer:** Lingenfelter's quiet-platform / pass-and-cover micro-skills as named drill targets, not assumptions — balance trained on one foot, quiet platform via one-armed and lateral-shuffle reps, pass-and-cover trained by sprinting to a designated spot after the contact rather than admiring the pass [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing].
- **Late-14s technical target:** athletes should hop back and take a jamming ball low on the platform rather than reaching up [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing].
- **Overhead pass:** introduced for free balls and high-arcing serves; the [[passing-overhead]] decision (forearm vs. hands) becomes a deliberate read, not an accident.
- **Failure mode:** athlete steps into the ball with arms first instead of stopping balanced and forming the platform second.
- See [[passing-cues]].

### Setting
- **Milestone:** Setter candidates demonstrate hand-hinge and side-of-ball contact from day one — bad mechanics ingrained at 14 are extremely hard to retrain at 17 [aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge][aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].
- **Late-14s deliverables:** stable outside set off a reasonable pass, competent backset without telegraphing, workable jump-set from in-system passes [setting-jump].
- **Tempo vocabulary (install, not execute):** a 14s setter should *understand* Lingenfelter's three-tempo framework — Tempo 3 (high to the pins, plant left foot after the ball leaves the setter's hands), Tempo 2 (plant while the ball is in the setter's hands), Tempo 1 (middle airborne while the ball is in the setter's hands) [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-tempo][aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-attack-tempo].
- **Match execution ceiling:** only Tempo 3 and rare Tempo 2 sets executed in matches at this age.
- **Failure mode:** chest-trap on the overhead set — setter contacts low and pushes through with chest instead of hand-hinge with side-of-ball contact.
- **Named drill target:** [[target-setting]] with hands-only constraint to enforce hand-hinge.
- See [[setting-cues]].

### Hitting
- **Approach milestone:** Three-step approach is reliable from in-system passes, with Schmidt's "start slow, accelerate the last two steps" tempo cue producing a visible deceleration-then-explosion pattern on the penultimate step [aoc-2024-attack-approach][aoc-2021-teach-spiking].
- **Arm-swing framework:** MacDonald's torque-and-high-hand emphasis paired with Mattox's Sight-90 / Elbow-Lead / Lift-and-Whip keywords [aoc-2024-attack-arm-mechanics].
- **Late-14s shot inventory:** the [[hitting-shot-selection]] trio (big cross, line, tip) is in the inventory off a workable in-system set.
- **Female-specific approach coaching:** women approach at slower speeds and use shorter penultimate steps than men in matched-elite samples — strength and power do not fully explain the gap, meaning approach-velocity and penultimate-step length are *technical* leverage points coachable at 14s, not weight-room-only [fuchs-2019-male-female-spike-biomechanics].
- **Failure mode:** penultimate step too long, plant too far from the ball, OH approaches into the net rather than parallel to it.
- **Anti-cue:** do not say "snap your wrist" — over-emphasizes wrist instead of the arm-and-hand dynamic with high-hand torque.
- See [[hitting-cues]].

### Blocking
- **Primary footwork pattern:** Static-and-shuffle is the primary pattern at 14s.
- **Swing-block introduction (footwork-only):** introduced via Rosen's 10-foot-line progression — steps without a jump, then steps with a jump after the steps are comfortable [aoc-2026-swing-blocking-footwork-beginners].
- **Coaching anchor:** hand-press emphasis with net penetration; closing the block on the outside-foot-high stance — which Lingenfelter explicitly notes "doesn't happen by accident, it has to be taught" [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Ceiling:** full swing-blocking execution remains an [[age-lens-14u]] ceiling [aoc-2023-swing-blocking-when].
- **Critical injury caveat:** Zahradnik's female-specific landing study found run-back-style block landings (where a blocker breaks down and sprints back to play backcourt defense) produced significantly higher knee-valgus moments than step-back landings (p=0.019) [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus].
- **Default landing pattern:** teach the controlled stick or step-back landing as the default at 14s; treat run-back as a recovery-only pattern, not a coachable habit [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus].
- **Failure mode:** run-back landing as default — highest knee-valgus moment landing pattern. Hard rule: any athlete with prior knee-pain history practices stick-only landings until cleared.
- See [[blocking-cues]].

### Serving
- **Headline milestone:** Standing topspin reliable; jump-float introduction.
- **Primary serve:** Standing-float remains the primary serve, with Mattox's Lift/Step/Swing cue and Dunning's toss-first emphasis ("tossing is the worst skill in volleyball" — drill it in isolation) [aoc-2025-float-serve-3-keys][aoc-2024-float-serve-toss].
- **Standing-topspin target:** reliable to zone for 60-70% of athletes by late 14s.
- **Jump-float is the bridge skill:** introduce mid-season for athletes whose toss is consistent and whose standing-float is reliable; do not run as a match-default until late-14s or [[15s]].
- **Ceiling:** no jump-topspin yet — that is a [[15s]]–[[16s]] target.
- **Accountability framework:** Munciana's "aim small, miss small" philosophy applies from day one — every serve targets a finite area (down to a 2-inch noodle target or a single floor box) so athletes hold themselves accountable to placement, not just clearing the net [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Failure mode:** toss-first failure — athlete spends all the rep load on the contact and tosses inconsistently; reverse to drilling the toss in isolation, no contact, until the toss lands repeatably in the same spot [aoc-2024-float-serve-toss].
- See [[serving-cues]].

### Defense
- **Body-positioning anchor:** Hips-under-ball for hard-driven attacks (Stone framework) [aoc-2025-controlling-the-dig].
- **Read diagnostic:** Look/See/Decide as the diagnostic framework for read [aoc-2025-training-reading-look-see-decide].
- **Coaching shortcut:** Munciana's Three P's — get back, get low, get a touch — work as a coaching shortcut for the ready position and pursuit decision [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Late-14s read milestone:** athlete recognizes shot-vs-swing in the hitter's approach and adjusts depth pre-contact, not post-contact [aoc-2024-kiraly-reads-every-skill].
- **Failure mode:** platform formed early instead of late on the dig contact.
- See [[defense-cues]].

### Transition
- **Block-to-approach recovery footwork installed:** defaulting to step-back, not run-back, per the landing-mechanics caveat above [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus].
- **Dig-to-approach footwork:** live for OHs and opposites.
- **Out-of-system framing:** Munciana's "you're going to be out of system about 80 percent of the time" framing is age-appropriate at 14s [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **OOS training block:** the setting-and-hitting block trains non-setter setters, dig-then-set patterns, and team out-of-system flows on the assumption that a perfect first-contact pass is the exception, not the rule.
- **Failure mode:** panic-set with hands when a tight pass arrives instead of hopping to a forearm-set or bumping to a teammate.
- See [[transition-cues]].

## Systems repertoire

**[[offense-4-2]] is the default**, simple to install, two setters, slide-capable, manageable rotation tracking [aoc-2022-4-2-system]. **[[offense-6-2]] is viable** when two bodies are setter-capable and rotations track solidly without a whiteboard [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners] — most nationally-ranked 14U teams will run a simple 6-2 by mid-season. [[offense-5-1]] is reserved for late-14s teams with a clearly dominant setter and a clear pin-attacker rotation behind her [aoc-2022-4-2-system]. [[offense-6-6]] is appropriate only for true-beginner 14s where rotating through every position is the developmental point [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]; nationally-ranked club 14s have moved past it.

Serve-receive: [[serve-receive-4-player]] is the default; [[serve-receive-3-player]] introduced late-14s once passing identity has settled into the libero-and-two-OHs core. Defense: [[defense-perimeter]] is primary because it carries fewer decisions and a simpler reading load; [[defense-rotation]] introduced as a concept only. Blocking: [[commit-blocking]] is the primary read scheme because the decision is simpler (commit to the middle on every play); [[read-blocking]] is introduced as a concept, not as the match-default scheme. Bunch-read blocking with full-decision-tree variants stays an [[age-lens-14u]] ceiling — HS+ material [aoc-2023-swing-blocking-when].

## Athletic profile

Pre-adolescent to mid-adolescent growth is rapid and asymmetric. The CSCS owns programming; the coach owns movement quality and rep-count discipline. Vertical-jump targets are not coachable end-points at 14s — coaching attention belongs on the *technical* leverage points (approach velocity, penultimate-step length, plant angle) where female-specific biomechanics evidence shows real coachable gain [fuchs-2019-male-female-spike-biomechanics].

**Specialization is itself an injury-prevention lever at 14s.**
- Post 2019's matched basketball/soccer/volleyball youth comparison (ages 12-18) showed high-specialization volleyball youth carried OR ~2.3 for overuse injury (p=0.02) [post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball].
- >8-month-per-year volleyball participation carried OR ~2.0 in the same comparison [post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball].
- Volleyball was the *only* sport in the comparison where specialization significantly raised overuse risk [post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball].
- Practical implication: multi-sport encouragement at 14s is evidence-based injury prevention, not just developmental philosophy.

**Landing-mechanics priority for adolescent female athletes.**
- Volleyball ACL injury is overwhelmingly a non-contact spike-landing event (97% non-contact, ~62% during landing-after-attack) [sassi-2025-acl-volleyball-systematic-review].
- Adolescent female outside hitters carry the highest position-specific incidence — high-school prevalence 9.3% vs. 4.4% collegiate [sassi-2025-acl-volleyball-systematic-review].
- Coach-actionable lever: neuromuscular training programs of 15-20 min, 2-3×/week, progressing bilateral-to-unilateral with sport-specific spike-landing simulations.
- Plyometric dose (coordinate with CSCS): meta-analytic evidence supports moderate-volume, moderate-frequency programming at ~2,000 jumps over 8 weeks at 2 sessions/week, with diminishing returns and rising injury risk above that envelope [ramirez-campillo-2020-plyometric-meta-analysis].
- Balance-board work in warm-ups is RCT-supported for any 14s player with prior ankle-sprain history [verhagen-2005-balance-board-ankle-prevention].

**Silent overuse is real at this level.**
- Junior-elite volleyball cohorts (ages 15-19) show clinical jumper's-knee structural change with neovascularization in ~10/57 *asymptomatic* tendons — "no pain yet" does not mean "no problem yet" [gisslen-2005-jumpers-knee-elite-junior-volleyball].
- The same overuse logic applies to the shoulder: even at sub-symptomatic volume, the playing-shoulder adaptations (depression, scapular lateralization, posterior-capsule tightness) are detectable from 14s onward [kugler-1996-shoulder-pain-volleyball-attackers].
- Practical implication: posterior-shoulder mobility plus rotator-cuff strengthening belong in arm-care routines from 14s.

**Female-athlete fueling awareness.**
- Volleyball's leanness pressure makes Female Athlete Triad / RED-S coach-relevant from early-adolescent onward [coelho-2021-female-athlete-triad-reds].
- Coaches see meal patterns and practice intensity that nutritionists never see; recognition of red flags (menstrual irregularity, stress fractures, weight loss, mood change, restrictive eating) is the coach's share.
- Referral path: any concerns route to a physician + registered dietitian, not coach-prescription.
- See [[physical]].

## Mental & coachability markers

A late-14s athlete who is HS-tryout-ready demonstrates four mental markers in matches and in practice:

1. **Self-correcting after a clear coach-cue.** Not silent compliance, not defensive reactivity — visible re-anchoring on the next rep.
2. **Consistent effort independent of score.** Munciana's "every drill has a winner and a loser" culture only works when athletes don't cash out at 14-22 in the wash drill [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing]; at 14s this should be installed even when fragile.
3. **Communication during plays.** Calling balls, calling seams, calling switches in real time, not retrospectively.
4. **Visible engagement during teammate reps.** Watching, not phone-checking, between drill turns.

**Self-talk is a sustained block, not a one-shot tool.** For 14s-and-older athletes who can engage with sport-psychology work, 8-week self-talk interventions reduced somatic anxiety, increased self-confidence, improved general self-efficacy with sustained gains, and produced measurably better coach-rated performance in junior sub-elite athletes; 1-week interventions produced only transient effects [walter-2019-self-talk-junior-athletes]. The implication: a pre-tournament motivational session is not the lever — the lever is integrating self-talk practice into the season-long routine. **Self-feedback capability** is itself a developmental milestone with a concrete age — Munciana names this explicitly as a marker that distinguishes older from younger teams [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide].

## Promotion criteria

These promotion criteria are the contract for moving from 14s to 15s and the input scoring criteria for [[tryout-rubric-14u]]. Each criterion is observable in a 60-90-minute tryout session and is graded on a 1-5 scale (1: does not demonstrate; 3: consistent at 14s; 5: 15s-ready).

- **Passing:** Forearm pass to a 4-meter target zone is reliable (≥60% in-system) from a moderate-pace standing-float serve. Quiet platform under jam-balls with the hop-back adjustment, not the chest-reach [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing]. Overhead-pass decision is deliberate. Pass-and-cover habit visible — sprints to coverage spot after contact, not still admiring the pass [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing]. Promotes to 15s if **3 across all four sub-criteria**.
- **Setting:** For setter candidates only — hand-hinge mechanics with side-of-ball contact, no chest-trap [aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge]; outside set delivers a hittable ball from in-system pass; backset competent without telegraphing; understands and can demonstrate Tempo 3 footwork on tossed balls (Tempo 2 nice-to-have, Tempo 1 not required) [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-tempo]. Promotes if **3 across hand-hinge / outside set / backset / Tempo 3 demo**.
- **Hitting:** Three-step approach with visible "start slow, accelerate the last two" tempo [aoc-2024-attack-approach]; arm swing with elbow-lead and high-hand contact, not a flat sidearm [aoc-2024-attack-arm-mechanics]; in-shot-selection inventory of at least two of {big cross, line, tip}. Female-specific marker: penultimate-step length and approach velocity coachable from cue, not strength-bound [fuchs-2019-male-female-spike-biomechanics]. Promotes if **3 across approach / arm swing / shot selection**.
- **Blocking:** Static-and-shuffle footwork reliable; outside-foot-high closing position [munciana-2022-camp-drills]; hand-press with net penetration; default landing pattern is stick or step-back, not run-back [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus]; swing-block footwork demonstrable without jump (Rosen 10-foot-line progression) [aoc-2026-swing-blocking-footwork-beginners]. Promotes if **3 across footwork / hand position / landing pattern**.
- **Serving:** Standing-topspin to zone reliable (≥60% in target zone). Jump-float introducible (toss consistency is the gating criterion, not contact) [aoc-2024-float-serve-toss]. Aim-small target accountability: athlete can name where she is serving before each serve, not just "deep" [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Promotes if **3 on standing-topspin and target accountability; jump-float a bonus, not required**.
- **Defense:** Three P's (get back, get low, get a touch) visible as a habit, not as a coach-prompted reset [munciana-2022-camp-drills]; Look/See/Decide read on shot-vs-swing pre-contact [aoc-2025-training-reading-look-see-decide]; platform formation late, not early [aoc-2025-controlling-the-dig]. Promotes if **3 across three P's / read / late platform**.
- **Transition:** Block-to-approach footwork (step-back default), dig-to-approach footwork for OHs and opposites; recognizable out-of-system protocol — does not panic-set with hands when a tight pass arrives, hops to a forearm-set or bumps to a teammate [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Promotes if **3 across recovery footwork and OOS protocol**.
- **Mental & coachability:** Self-corrects after one cue (not three); consistent effort across the score; communicates in-rally; engages during teammate reps. **This is non-negotiable** — a player below 3 on coachability does not promote, regardless of skill scores.

## Common coaching corrections

- **Reach-and-pop on the platform.** Athlete steps into the ball with arms first; coach reverses to "stopped and balanced first, platform second" — Kiraly's first key [aoc-2013-kiraly-4-keys-forearm-pass][aoc-2023-passing-shuffle-not-reach]. Drill: [[munciana-straight-line-passing]] for the closure-on-balance reset.
- **Chest-trap on the overhead set.** Setter contacts low and pushes through with the chest; coach reverses to hand-hinge with side-of-ball contact [aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge]. Drill: [[target-setting]] with hands-only constraint.
- **Penultimate-step too long, plant too far from the ball.** OH approaches into the net rather than parallel to it; coach reverses to "start slow, accelerate the last two" with the penultimate-step shortening implied [aoc-2024-attack-approach][fuchs-2019-male-female-spike-biomechanics]. Drill: [[approach-and-swing]] with a tape line marking the plant.
- **Wrist-snap as the cue for hitting.** Over-emphasizes wrist; modern teaching uses arm-and-hand dynamic with high-hand torque [aoc-2024-attack-arm-mechanics]. Reverses to "high hand, lead with the elbow" — Mattox's keywords. Anti-cue: do not say "snap your wrist." See [[hitting-cues]].
- **Run-back landing after a block.** Highest knee-valgus moment landing pattern — never the default [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus]. Coach reverses to step-back-and-recover, not sprint-back. Hard rule: any athlete with prior knee-pain history practices stick-only landings until cleared.
- **Toss-first failure on the float serve.** Athlete spends all the rep load on the contact and tosses inconsistently. Reverses to drilling the toss in isolation, no contact, until the toss lands repeatably in the same spot [aoc-2024-float-serve-toss]. Drill: any of the [[munciana-5-6-seam-noodle]] / [[munciana-5-6-seam-strike]] target series.
- **Standing in line during reps.** Munciana's design: "we'll touch more balls in a five-minute period than some people will pass in an hour" [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Reverses to short-window drills with rapid scoring resets and no standing lines [usav-2009-cap-game-like-training].
- **Verbal cueing at the wrong granularity.** Moran's youth replication of the OPTIMAL-theory cue paradigm found *no significant differences* between external, internal, analogy, and neutral-control cues on whole-body explosive tasks — and neutral-control was actually superior to internal cueing on vertical jump [moran-2023-verbal-coaching-cues-youth]. The "always external" rule is over-stated for youth. External focus stays defensible for **arm-swing-specific** cues where 3-D motion-capture data on young female spikers does support an external-focus advantage on arm-swing velocity [slovak-2023-external-focus-volleyball-spike] — but coach to the player, observe, and retain what works for that athlete.

- **Hitting:** [[approach-and-swing]], [[pass-set-hit]] with target zones.

- **Serving:** [[serve-targets]], [[zone-serving]], [[munciana-5-6-seam-noodle]], [[munciana-5-6-seam-strike]], [[munciana-no-gut-serving]], [[munciana-five-before-ten]] for aggressive-mentality target work [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Defense:** [[munciana-speedball]] (the intro to defense), [[munciana-three-way-release]], [[munciana-mountain-d]], [[munciana-seat-to-seam]], [[munciana-pursuit-attack]] for movement-first defensive identity [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Competitive 6v6:** [[munciana-biggie-smalls]] (3-in-a-row), [[munciana-prove-it]] (must-serve-and-score), [[munciana-momentum]] (escalating point values), [[munciana-tug-of-war]], [[munciana-finish-strong]] (19-19 reset on miss) — the chassis varies, the technique stays constant; the principle is that competitive 6v6 trains psychological habits that show up in real matches [munciana-2022-camp-drills].

**Practice-design note** (Munciana operational depth): drill volume in tight windows is a stated design principle — drills are short, scoring resets fast, athletes accumulate reps inside small geometric formats (triangles, tilt-chains, two-ball patterns) rather than long lines [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Always run left and right — symmetry is the default, not an option [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. **Differential-learning schedules** (every-rep-different) significantly out-performed both repetitive and passive-control groups across underhand pass / overhand pass / overhand serve in matched adolescent female absolute-beginners — supporting noise-injection variability for 14s skill sessions [apidogo-2021-differential-learning-volleyball]. **Implicit-then-explicit progression** for serve decision-making: front-load perception-based reps before layering on the verbal "rules of where to serve" [lola-2012-implicit-explicit-volleyball-serving].

## Recommended cues

Cue dictionaries (to be filled in alongside this guide): [[passing-cues]], [[setting-cues]], [[hitting-cues]], [[blocking-cues]], [[serving-cues]], [[defense-cues]], [[transition-cues]]. Anchor cues for 14s, by skill — see [[mike-lingenfelter]] for the Munciana voice and [[karch-kiraly]] for the USA-CAP voice:

- **Passing:** Kiraly's 4 keys — stopped & balanced / thumbs together / set the angle / simple one-way motion [aoc-2013-kiraly-4-keys-forearm-pass]. Lingenfelter quiet-platform / pass-and-cover [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-accountability-passing].
- **Setting:** Hand-hinge / side-of-ball contact / vision-on-target [aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge]. Lingenfelter Tempo 1/2/3 by left-foot plant timing [aoc-2026-lingenfelter-middle-tempo].
- **Hitting:** "Start slow, accelerate the last two" (Schmidt) [aoc-2024-attack-approach]. Sight-90 / Elbow-Lead / Lift-and-Whip (Mattox) [aoc-2021-teach-spiking]. High hand, torque (MacDonald) [aoc-2024-attack-arm-mechanics].
- **Blocking:** Outside-foot-high — "this doesn't happen by accident, it has to be taught" (Lingenfelter/Munciana) [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Read-then-jump. Penetrate-not-cover.
- **Serving:** Aim-small-miss-small (Munciana) [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Lift/Step/Swing (Mattox). Toss-first ("tossing is the worst skill in volleyball" — Dunning) [aoc-2024-float-serve-toss]. Aggressive-mentality framing (Munciana five-before-ten) [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Defense:** Three P's — get back, get low, get a touch (Munciana) [munciana-2022-camp-drills]. Hips-under-ball (Stone) [aoc-2025-controlling-the-dig]. Look/See/Decide (Josephson) [aoc-2025-training-reading-look-see-decide]. Read the hitter, not the ball [aoc-2024-kiraly-reads-every-skill].
- **Transition:** Block-to-approach (step-back default, never run-back as default) [zahradnik-2014-block-landing-grf-valgus]. OOS detect/call/bail-to-pin [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
