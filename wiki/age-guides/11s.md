# Age Guide: 11s

## Overview & development phase

The 11U athlete is at the **introduction phase transitioning toward fundamentals** — past the movement-discovery window of ages 3–9 [usav-2026-youth-volleyball-tips], not yet at the durable skill-acquisition stage where 12U technique milestones become non-negotiable [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals]. For most clubs 11U is the bridge year: a player who was only loosely organized at [[10s]] starts forming a recognizable platform, attempts an overhand serve for the first time, and meets a real 6v6 entry-level offense. Calendar-age-wise the 11U athlete is typically 10–11 by USAV calendar age, pre-pubertal through early puberty.

Two USAV instruments shape this phase. **Simplified Youth Rules** still nominally cover ages 3–11 — the 11U athlete is in USAV's last codified small-sided window, where 2v2 / 3v3 / 4v4 with 7-foot nets, lightweight youth balls, and unlimited substitution remain the official defaults [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]. **Growing Kids' Volleyball** carries the 12U-and-under philosophy that engagement, pair-based practice, and over-the-net-first principles drive retention [usav-2026-growing-kids-volleyball]. A competitive 11U program straddles both: USAV's small-sided defaults remain the practice-design baseline, while **6v6 elements are introduced progressively** so a player can graduate to [[12s]] with rotations recognized, an overhand serve rehearsed, and a real platform under construction.

The "introduction" label means: technique and systems are *introduced*, not mastered. Ingrained mechanical errors at 11U are costly to retrain later, so the coach's job is to install good shapes (platform, hand triangle, approach footwork) before high-volume reps cement bad ones [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals]. The recency-leaning operating model on this wiki [[age-lens-14u]] applies in compressed form: modern coaching language (Kiraly's 4 keys, Munciana's pursuit-and-closure framing, Mattox's accelerate-the-last-two-steps) lands more cleanly on a beginner than legacy phrasing. The Munciana club-wide-alignment principle — an 11U player already speaks the same vocabulary an 18U player speaks [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide] — implies serious programs install the *right* cues at 11U, not the *easy* ones.

## Skill milestones

### Passing

- **Platform shape:** thumbs together, wrists straight, arms slightly off the chest, knees bent, shoulders over the front foot — recognizable on every contact, even off-center [aoc-2024-kids-passing-fundamentals].
- **Kiraly's 4 keys frame the cognitive load:** stopped and balanced before contact, thumbs together, set the angle with the platform, simple one-way motion [aoc-2013-kiraly-4-keys-forearm-pass].
- **Highest-yield 11U focus:** "stopped and balanced" — beginners reach instead of move; Munciana opens its drill library with a no-ball pursuit shuffle for exactly this reason [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Milestones:**
  - Pass a tossed or slow-served ball to setter target with consistent arm shape.
  - "Shuffle, don't reach" footwork on balls outside the body.
  - Hold the platform finish a beat after contact.
  - Recognize overhead-pass territory vs. forearm territory (concept only).
- **See:** [[passing-cues]], [[passing-forearm]].

### Setting

- **Default progression:** catch-and-set for most athletes; a small number of taller / coordinated kids ready for live setting by season's end [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].
- **Hand shape (same one elite setters use):** triangle above the forehead, thumbs not touching, elbows slightly forward, feet shoulder-width with right foot forward, knees bent [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].
- **Hand-hinge:** wrist cocks back and snaps forward as legs extend — the day-one setter skill in modern AOC coaching [aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge].
- **Milestones:**
  - Catch-and-set 10 reps with hand triangle and no thumb-collision.
  - For setter-track candidates: live-set a tossed ball 5–10 feet above the net to target (Nelson's "ready to transition from passing to setting") [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].
  - Left-to-right shuffle footwork.
  - Legs-extend-up power source, not "all wrist."
- **Reality check:** most 11U athletes will not be cleanly live-setting 5–10 feet by season end; ingraining the right hand shape in catch-and-set reps counts.
- **See:** [[setting-cues]], [[setting-hands]].

### Hitting

- **Three-stage progression (Nelson):** down-ball no-jump → 2-step modified approach → 3-step introduction late season for advanced athletes [aoc-2024-kids-attacking-fundamentals].
- **Workhorse stance:** 2-step "Flamingo" [aoc-2024-kids-attacking-fundamentals].
- **Tempo cue (Schmidt):** start slow, accelerate the last two steps [aoc-2024-attack-approach].
- **Milestones:**
  - Down-ball arm swing from puppet position (elbow up, hand high, contact in front).
  - 2-step approach with isolated jump on accurate toss.
  - 3-step approach attempted by season's end where coordination supports it.
  - Contact at peak of jump (concept, not consistent execution).
- **See:** [[hitting-cues]], [[hitting-approach]], [[hitting-arm-swing]].

### Blocking

- **Scope:** single-blocker concept under USAV simplified rules [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules].
- **Skill focus:** hand-on-the-net ready position and a controlled stationary jump — no swing-blocking footwork yet.
- **Reality:** most 11U attacks are free balls and down-balls; blocking practice is more about **net awareness and not getting injured** than stopping attacks.
- **Milestones:**
  - Hands above the net with thumbs up, fingers spread, balanced pre-jump posture.
  - Stationary 2-foot jump straight up without net contact.
  - Recognize when to block vs. back up to defense.
- **See:** [[blocking-cues]], [[blocking-footwork]], [[blocking-hand-position]].

### Serving

- **The 11U headline:** the year the overhand serve is introduced — at 10U underhand is default and overhand is exploratory [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]; at 12U standing-float and standing-topspin should be the defaults [aoc-2024-kids-serving-fundamentals]; 11U is the bridge.
- **Nelson's five-serve taxonomy at this age:** static floater is the workhorse; topspin (mirrors hitting swing) and Japanese floater (rewards wrist control) introduced as concepts only [aoc-2024-kids-serving-fundamentals].
- **Entry sequence:** Newkirk's "Deliver and Pop" — reach + drop + pop [aoc-2021-youth-15-drills].
- **Milestones:**
  - Underhand serve consistently clears the net (10U baseline preserved).
  - Overhand serve attempted from developmentally-appropriate distance (2–3 feet inside the end-line for smaller athletes; back up over weeks).
  - Toss-first emphasis: consistent toss precedes any swing coaching.
  - Aggressive intent — **serve at a target**, not just over the net [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **See:** [[serving-cues]], [[serving-float]].

### Defense

- **Operating mode:** read-and-pursue more than platform precision.
- **Munciana's Three P's:** get back, get low, get a touch — frame the defensive ready position and pursuit decision [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Platform mechanics transfer from passing;** the new content is the **read**.
- **Reading is not a separate drill (Kiraly):** "reading is not separate from skill; it is part of every skill" — embed reading questions ("where is the setter looking?") into every defensive rep rather than running isolated reading drills [aoc-2024-kiraly-reads-every-skill].
- **Milestones:**
  - Ready position (knees bent, weight forward, hands in front).
  - Pursue with a recovery touch (not a clean pass — a touch that keeps the rally alive).
  - Recognize free balls vs. attacks (concept).
- **See:** [[defense-cues]], [[defense-platform]], [[defense-reading]].

### Transition

- **Single 11U concept:** after a contact, get back to your ready spot.
- **Out of scope:** block-to-attack and defense-to-attack patterns (not yet executable).
- **Unit-level skill:** pass-set-hit as a *shape* — passer to setter, setter to hitter, hitter swings — with every player attempting all three contacts in 6v6 cooperative play.
- **Milestones:**
  - Recover to ready position after every contact.
  - Pass-set-hit shape executed cooperatively (low success rate is fine; the shape is the point).
  - Urgency awareness (concept).
- **See:** [[transition-cues]].

## Systems repertoire

- **Offense:**
  - [[offense-6-6]] (every player rotates through every position) remains the developmentally-correct practice default for 11U athletes still figuring out where they belong [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules].
  - **Simple [[offense-4-2]] is the entry-level competitive offense** — front-row setter in all six rotations, two designated setters opposite each other, three front-row attackers [aoc-2022-4-2-system].
  - Mattox's framing applies most strongly at 11U: front-row setter is always the same, the two setters are interchangeable, rotation tracking is minimal — "easier for less experienced players to learn than 5-1 or 6-2" [aoc-2022-4-2-system].
  - **Out of scope at 11U:** the Slide and back-row Bic that Mattox cites as 4-2 weapons at HS+ levels — timing too tight, approach too complex.
  - **The 11U 4-2 is the *simple* 4-2:** setter sets, front-row OH or middle hits.
  - **Ceiling:** simple 4-2 entry by season's end. [[offense-6-2]] and [[offense-5-1]] explicitly out of scope [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners][aoc-2022-4-2-system].
- **Serve receive:** 3-passer or 4-passer U or W shape with overlap rules taught conceptually [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners] — front-row players ahead of back-row partners, back-row players behind their front-row partners, server "not on the court" [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners].
- **Defense:** [[defense-perimeter]] is the only viable shape — each athlete owns a zone, simple geometry, low reading load.
- **Blocking:** single-blocker only per USAV simplified rules [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]; [[commit-blocking]] as the conceptual framing.
- **Positions:** no specialization at 11U.
  - Some athletes trend toward setter, hitter, or libero profiles by season's end and the coach **notices** signals — but assigning a position is premature [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules][aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide].
  - Rotate every athlete through every position across the season.

## Athletic profile

11U is mostly pre-pubertal with some early-puberty athletes. Growth is uneven; coordination shifts underneath the player. Jump-training and plyometrics are a **CSCS conversation, not a coach conversation** — see [[physical]].

- **Movement quality first:** multi-directional ready-position footwork (shuffle, drop step, cross-over) drilled in warm-ups; jumping mechanics taught for safe landing more than vertical gain.
- **Multi-sport encouragement (evidence-backed, not philosophical):** Post et al. (2019) is unambiguous — high volleyball specialization at the youth level produces OR ~2.3 for overuse injury, year-round (>8 months/year) OR ~2.0, with 45.7% of youth volleyball athletes reporting prior overuse injury (highest among three sports studied) [post-2019-specialization-overuse-volleyball]. Multi-sport play at 11U is injury prevention.
- **Load management literacy for parents:** most 11U overuse comes from accumulated practice load across sports; the coach's lever is communication.
- **No max-effort jump training:** plyometric-heavy conditioning is deferred to a CSCS.

## Mental & coachability markers

11U is the year a player starts processing feedback **with intent to apply it** rather than just nodding. Markers worth observing:

- **Self-correction within a rep cycle.** After feedback, does rep N+1 attempt the correction, or look identical to the prior one? The first is a coachability signal; the second is a "needs more reps" signal, not a deficit.
- **Tolerance for repetition.** Does the athlete accept that platform shape needs many reps, or push to "play games" before fundamentals are stable?
- **Asks questions vs. nods** — asking is a coachability strength at 11U.
- **USAV's 3–11U feedback stance:** **FeedForward** (encouraging reminders about *what to do next* rather than criticism of past errors) is the dominant feedback mode, and "Catch them doing it right" outweighs error-correction [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules].
- **Cue-language evidence is honest about its limits:** a multi-site youth replication of the Wulf external-focus paradigm on jumping and sprinting found **no significant differences** between external, internal, analogy, and neutral-control cues — and *neutral control was actually superior to internal cueing on vertical jump* [moran-2023-verbal-coaching-cues-youth].
- **Implication:** don't over-engineer cue selection. Rep volume and consistent vocabulary outweigh cue-type optimization at this age.
- **Munciana's rep-based confidence principle:** "reps equal confidence" — anxious 11U athletes are usually under-repped, not under-talented [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide].

## Promotion criteria

Promotion criteria specify what a player must demonstrate to graduate from 11U to [[12s]]. **The corresponding [[tryout-rubric-11s]] translates these criteria into a 1-5 scoring scale**, so the milestones below are the unifying spine of 11U evaluation. Each criterion is observable in a 60-90-minute tryout setting or across season-end practices.

**Passing — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Recognizable platform on every contact, even off-center balls (thumbs together, wrists straight, knees bent).
- Shuffle-not-reach on balls outside the body for at least 60% of trials in tryout-pace reps.
- Can pass a tossed or slow-served ball to the setter target with the platform shape intact for a majority of reps.

**Setting — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Hand-triangle shape above forehead with no thumb collision in catch-and-set reps.
- For setter-track candidates: live-set a tossed ball 5–10 feet above the net to a target [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].
- Recognition of overhead-pass vs. forearm-pass territory.

**Hitting — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Down-ball arm swing from puppet position with elbow-up, contact-in-front shape.
- 2-step approach onto an accurate toss with isolated jump.
- Attempt at a 3-step approach (form not yet expected to be clean).

**Blocking — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Hands above the net in pre-jump posture without net contact.
- Recognition of when to block vs. when to back up.

**Serving — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Reliable underhand serve (the 10U baseline holds at 11U-promotion).
- **Overhand serve attempted at a developmentally appropriate distance, with successful contact in at least 30% of attempts.** This is the load-bearing 11U-specific milestone — the overhand attempt does not need to score, but it needs to exist as an attempted skill, not a hypothetical one.
- Toss consistency on the overhand attempt (same arc each time, ball coming down to a repeatable contact zone).

**Defense — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Ready position held during opponent's offensive contact.
- Touches a pursued ball with intent (recovery touch counts — does not need to be a clean dig).
- Recognizes free ball vs. attack at concept level.

**Transition — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Recovers to ready position after contact rather than admiring the ball.
- Participates in a pass-set-hit shape with teammates without freezing.

**Mental & coachability — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Attempts coach feedback on the next rep (self-correction signal).
- Tolerates repetition without protest.
- Engages with teammates positively during cooperative play.

**Systems — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Recognizes own position in a simple 4-2 lineup.
- Knows which way to rotate after a side-out.
- Understands "stay" vs. "switch" at concept level (overlap rules taught conceptually, not rigorously) [aoc-2021-teach-rotations-beginners].

**Athletic — promotion-ready demonstrates:**
- Multi-directional movement (shuffle, drop step) without falling or losing balance.
- Lands jumps with knees-over-toes and bent-knee absorption.

A player meeting most of these criteria is ready for [[12s]], where the platform must become stable, the standing-float serve must be reliable, basic 4-2 must be executable in real games, and overhead passing is added.

## Common coaching corrections

- **Reaching with the platform instead of moving the feet.** Cue: "Move your feet first, platform second" — Munciana's pursuit-shuffle drill addresses this directly [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Setter thumbs colliding above forehead.** Cue: "Triangle window — see the ball through the window" [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals].
- **Hitter approaching too fast and out of control.** Cue: "Start slow, accelerate the last two steps" [aoc-2024-attack-approach].
- **Overhand server tossing inconsistently.** Drill the toss alone — 10 tosses, no swing, ball must land at the same spot each time [aoc-2024-kids-serving-fundamentals].
- **Players standing flat-footed on defense.** Cue from Munciana's Three P's: "Get low" before the ball comes [munciana-2022-camp-drills].
- **Reaching back to the net during blocking.** Hands above net, fingers spread, do not retreat — backing up creates the gap.
- **Server cleating the net or court.** Serve from a closer distance until contact is clean, then back up.

## Recommended drills

The 11U list inherits the 10U warm-up + cooperative ball-control core and adds **overhand-serve drills, simple shuttle-passing (2-line), and 2-step approach drills**. Full curated list at [[11s-drills]]; must-do core:

- **Pursuit & footwork:** [[munciana-two-man-shuffle]], [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], [[jump-warmup]] (movement quality, not max-vertical).
- **Passing:** [[butterfly-passing]] (shortened distance), [[two-line-passing]], [[shuttle-passing]], [[pepper]]. Lingenfelter's short-ball/long-ball warmup with 3 passers is age-appropriate [aoc-2021-lingenfelter-short-ball-long-ball].
- **Setting:** [[target-setting]] (catch-and-set for most, live for ready candidates). Newkirk's toss-up → fizzles → four-sets progression is well-suited [aoc-2021-youth-15-drills].
- **Hitting:** [[approach-and-swing]] with 2-step Flamingo [aoc-2024-kids-attacking-fundamentals]; down-ball reps from puppet position.
- **Serving:** [[serve-targets]] with closer-distance progression, [[zone-serving]] as concept. Newkirk's "Deliver and Pop" works for the overhand introduction [aoc-2021-youth-15-drills].
- **Mixed / cooperative:** [[cooperative-25-goal]] (Kessel-style net game) [usav-2026-growing-kids-volleyball], [[pass-set-hit]] in slow form, [[loser-becomes-the-net]] when nets are unavailable [usav-2026-youth-volleyball-tips].
- **Selectivity note:** Munciana's drill library is selectively appropriate at 11U — opening pursuit drills work cleanly, but 6v6 psychology games (Biggie Smalls, Prove It, Tug of War) are 13U+ material — cognitive pressure exceeds 11U capacity.

## Practice ratio across the season

The blocked / small-group / 4v4 mix for 11s runs **40/55/5 → 30/55/15 → 30/50/20 → 25/45/30 → 35/50/15** across preseason, mid-season, late-season, pre-tournament-peak, and taper respectively. 11s shares 10s's small-group dominance (50-55% bucket 2 baseline) but with skill development pulling ahead — the platform and contact mechanics are stable enough to graduate beyond catch-and-set, so blocked work is more efficient per minute [aoc-2024-kids-passing-fundamentals]. Bucket 3 ceiling at 30% in pre-tournament-peak reflects that 11s competition still caps at 4v4 brackets [usav-2026-simplified-youth-rules]. Drill-format anchors: `[[butterfly-passing]]`, `[[serve-targets]]` (blocked); `[[serve-receive-3v3]]`, `[[queen-of-the-court-passing]]`, `[[wash-drill]]` 3v3, `[[cooperative-25-goal]]` (small-group); 4v4 simulation (4v4). See `[[practice-ratios]]` for the full matrix, bucket definitions, and adjacent-age trajectories.

## Recommended cues

The 11U cue library is intentionally **small and consistent**. Per Moran et al. 2023, cue-type optimization is not robustly supported by youth data — consistent vocabulary repeated until the motor pattern is stable matters more [moran-2023-verbal-coaching-cues-youth].

- **Passing:** "Stopped and balanced" (Kiraly) [aoc-2013-kiraly-4-keys-forearm-pass]; "Thumbs together"; "Move your feet first, platform second"; "Quiet platform" (Munciana) [aoc-2025-cc-skill-training-clubwide]. See [[passing-cues]].
- **Setting:** "Triangle window above the forehead"; "Legs make the set, hands shape it" [aoc-2024-kids-setting-fundamentals]; "Hand-hinge" for ready candidates [aoc-2024-setting-hand-hinge]. See [[setting-cues]].
- **Hitting:** "Start slow, accelerate the last two steps" (Schmidt) [aoc-2024-attack-approach]; "Elbow up, hand high"; "Contact in front." See [[hitting-cues]].
- **Blocking:** "Hands above the net, fingers spread"; "Don't back up." See [[blocking-cues]].
- **Serving:** "Toss to the same spot every time"; "Aim small, miss small" (Munciana) [munciana-2022-camp-drills]; "Reach up, drop, pop" [aoc-2021-youth-15-drills]. See [[serving-cues]].
- **Defense:** "Get back, get low, get a touch" (Munciana Three P's) [munciana-2022-camp-drills]; "Eyes up." See [[defense-cues]].
- **Transition:** "Where do you go next?"; "Recover to your spot." See [[transition-cues]].
- **Recency bias:** the cue library leans toward **modern AOC + Munciana** language as primary, with USAV CAP defaults as the developmental scaffold. Legacy cues ("snap your wrist" for hitting; isolated-platform passing language) are avoided at 11U because they install patterns that become anti-cues later.
