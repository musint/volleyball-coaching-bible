# 10s Drill Pick-List

## Purpose

This pick-list is the operating drill rotation for [[10s]] — first-organized-volleyball athletes in the introduction phase, typically 8-10 by USAV calendar age. The master library at [[index]] is built mostly around `levels: [14u]` defaults; this list down-scales those drills for an age cohort whose proprioception, hand-eye, and attention windows are still forming. The bias is heavy on warm-up, cooperative ball-control, and target-driven introductions to setting and serving. Penalty-heavy wash drills, competitive 6v6 elimination formats, and high-jump-volume conditioning are deliberately absent — those belong from 12s upward, with full game-like wash from 13s onward (see [[age-lens-14u]] for the upper-band reference).

The unifying principle is USAV's youth philosophy: keep the ball in the air, every athlete touches it, "catch them doing it right" beats penalty resets, and grills (game-like drills with a clear objective) replace standing lines without inflating cognitive load. Where Munciana drills appear, they are used in their gentlest variants — footwork primer, free-tilt straight-line passing — chosen because the underlying movement pattern is age-portable even though the source library is camp-grade.

## Must-do drill list

**Warm-up (every session)**
- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — ball-in-hand 6-8 minute opener; teaches movement vocabulary (carioca, A-skip, hip-hinge) before any ball-contact load.
- [[munciana-two-man-shuffle]] — shorter 3-4 m shuffle; the day's footwork primer; "low, lower, lowest" cue does most of the teaching.
- [[partner-pepper-warmup]] — hold at the bump-bump and bump-set-bump phases only; do not add the hit until pairs sustain 10+ controlled contacts.

**Ball-control (cooperative)**
- [[ball-control-warmup]] — catch on contact 2 for the first minute; live sets only when the catch-and-place shape is clean.
- [[butterfly-passing]] — modified 14U adaptation: tosses only, 3 m short distance, target 6 cleans in a row before extending the pattern.
- [[munciana-straight-line-passing]] — 4-5 m run-in only; emphasize stopping before contact; "quiet platform" is the cardinal cue at this age.

**Skill introductions**
- [[two-line-passing]] — tosses only at 5 m, simple "mine" call before each rep; target grades aloud so the passer hears feedback before shagging.
- [[target-setting]] — catch-and-set version: large bucket or wide hoop, coach-tossed passes, good-pass shape only for the first month before introducing seam/overthrow.
- [[serve-targets]] — underhand-only version with three big zones (1, 5, 6); in-zone or adjacent both count; target 40% on-zone before tightening.

**Cooperative culmination**
- [[cooperative-25-goal]] — target 15 in a row (not 25), attacks may be roll shots or directed throws; the count itself is the teaching tool, not the kill.

## When-in-season-to-use mapping

The 10s season is shorter and gentler than the 14U arc — most clubs and rec leagues run 8-12 weeks of practice with a small handful of cooperative jamborees rather than a tournament-stacked schedule. The composite list above is the season default; the seasonal weighting shifts as follows:

- **Preseason (weeks 1-2).** Lean almost entirely on warm-up + ball-control: [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], [[munciana-two-man-shuffle]], [[partner-pepper-warmup]] (bump-bump only), [[ball-control-warmup]] (catch on contact 2). Introduce [[target-setting]] catch-and-set in week 2.
- **Mid-season (weeks 3-8).** Full pick-list rotation. Hold serving at underhand-only [[serve-targets]] for the entire mid-season unless a player demonstrates safe overhand mechanics independently — overhand introductions belong on the [[11s]] guide.
- **Pre-jamboree (1-2 weeks before play).** Add [[cooperative-25-goal]] with the goal-of-15 adaptation; this is the closest a 10s session should get to a "scrimmage" — keep it cooperative, never elimination-style.
- **Match-day / jamboree day.** Warm-up only ([[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] + abbreviated [[partner-pepper-warmup]]); no new teaching, no penalties, no last-minute fixes.
- **Postseason (final 1-2 sessions).** Re-run the cooperative favorites; introduce one [[10s]]-appropriate "graduation" reference to the [[11s]] curriculum (overhand serve attempt, 4-2 entry concept) so families and athletes see the on-ramp.

## Adaptations

- **Court size.** Run on a shortened court when available (USAV's small-sided framing applies — 4v4 on a half-court teaches more than 6v6 on a full court at this age).
- **Ball.** Lighter youth ball (Volley-Lite / VolleyLite or equivalent) for all passing, setting, and serving drills. Reduces shoulder load and increases successful contacts per minute.
- **Net height.** USAV-recommended 7'0" for 10s competition; lower in practice if it accelerates successful overhead contact.
- **Group size.** Pair or trio formats beat 6v6 — every athlete should touch a ball at least every 10-15 seconds for the warm-up and ball-control blocks.
- **Coaching language.** Lead with FeedForward ("next time, low platform first") instead of error correction; "catch them doing it right" is the operating cue. The youth-cue evidence is honest at this age — a multi-site replication of Wulf's external-focus paradigm on jumping found no significant differences between external, internal, analogy, and neutral-control cues at the youngest age bands, so plain-language descriptive cues are entirely defensible.
- **Penalty-heavy formats.** Avoid. Counter-resets (the [[cooperative-25-goal]] mechanic) replace push-ups, sprints, or sit-out punishments at 10s; the cognitive stage is too early for anxiety-loaded reps to compound.
- **Position rotation.** Every athlete cycles through every position every session; specialization belongs from [[13s]] onward per the [[age-lens-14u]] framing.
