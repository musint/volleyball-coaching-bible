# 13s Drill Pick-List

## Purpose

This pick-list is the operating drill rotation for [[13s]] — the late-fundamentals / early-specialization bridge year, typically 12-13 by USAV calendar age. The 13s cohort runs into the 14U club season covered by [[age-lens-14u]] one season later, so the 13s build deliberately previews 14U technique and tempo without the full 14U practice load. Where 12s introduces wash at low intensity, 13s graduates to **real transition reps, position-specialized setter work, and aggressive-target serving**. This is the first age where the rotation looks like a club practice rather than a developmental clinic.

Three defining additions: (1) **transition-rally + free-ball-to-offense** as the first true transition-block work — earned-point counting, three-step transition logic, block-and-recover footwork; (2) **setter-specific work** beginning to separate the setter track from all-position rotation — [[target-setting]] with seam/back-2 targets and an introductory [[three-setter-rotation]]; and (3) **Munciana 5-6 seam serving drills** — accountability-driven aggressive serving in the 5-and-6 seam, the highest-leverage zone in modern girls serve-receive.

## Must-do drill list

**Warm-up (every session)**

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — 8-10 minute ball-in-hand opener; movement-quality trumps pace at this age.
- [[munciana-two-man-shuffle]] — full 5 m shuffle with "low, lower, lowest"; pursuit-footwork primer for the day's transition work.

**Ball-control (passing-forward)**

- [[butterfly-passing]] — full distance, tossed entries graduating to standing-float by mid-season; passers grade contact aloud before shagging.
- [[shuttle-passing]] — 2-line shuttle, 6 m, mandatory shuffle-not-reach footwork (Kordes cue); season-long passing-identity volume.
- [[three-person-serve-receive]] — entry to formation passing; seam communication ("mine / yours / shared") and platform-angle to setter, not yet competitive scoring.

**Setter-specific (the 13s separator)**

- [[target-setting]] — full live-set with seam, high-4, and back-2 targets; setter candidates take 60% of reps, all-position rotators 40%. Hand-hinge is the priority — bad shape at 13 is hard to retrain at 17 (Stone framing in [[age-lens-14u]]).
- [[three-setter-rotation]] — **introduction-level only**. Two-station 14U adaptation (high-4 and back-2; defer the 31 station to 14U). Coach-tossed; the matched rotation itself is the point.

**Hitting + integrated**

- [[approach-and-swing]] — 3-step approach with Schmidt's "start slow, accelerate the last two steps"; coach-tossed sets, advancing to live setter mid-season.
- [[pass-set-hit]] — the three-skill chain connecting ball-control to live offense; cooperative "complete the chain" target rather than kill counting.

**Serving (the Munciana seam introduction)**

- [[serve-targets]] — full-court standing float to three big zones (1, 5, 6); 40-50% on-zone target before tightening.
- [[munciana-5-6-seam-bounce]] — first 5-6 seam-driven aggressive serve drill; bounce variant builds toss-and-target accountability without high failure cost. Lingenfelter's "aim small, miss small" applies from rep one.
- [[munciana-5-6-seam-noodle]] — partner-tracker variant where the receiving-side noodle defines the seam; teaches passers and servers simultaneously how the 5-6 seam attacks a rotation.

**Transition (the 13s headline addition)**

- [[free-ball-to-offense]] — entry to transition work; coach-tossed free balls, mandatory base-to-defense-to-base footwork, setter-release timing as cardinal cue. Five clean reps before scoring.
- [[transition-rally]] — **14U adaptation**: free balls only, accept any successful transition attack regardless of quality. Cap rallies at 8-10 transition points to manage cognitive load.

## When-in-season-to-use mapping

The 13s season runs 5-7 months on the modern club calendar — preseason late fall, regional play through winter, qualifiers in spring. The composite list is the season default; weighting shifts as follows:

- **Preseason (weeks 1-3).** Heavy on warm-up + ball-control + setter-specific. Run [[butterfly-passing]], [[shuttle-passing]], [[target-setting]] every session. Defer [[transition-rally]] and 5-6 seam serving until week 3-4 once mechanics hold under pressure.
- **Mid-season (weeks 4-12).** Full pick-list rotation. Build [[three-setter-rotation]] from coach-tossed to live-pass. Layer [[munciana-5-6-seam-bounce]] in week 4-5, advance to [[munciana-5-6-seam-noodle]] by week 7-8. [[transition-rally]] weekly by week 6.
- **Pre-tournament (1-2 weeks out).** Compress to game-like blocks: [[pass-set-hit]] full chain, [[transition-rally]] with shorter caps, [[free-ball-to-offense]] for setter-release rhythm. Hold serve-receive reps in [[three-person-serve-receive]] and [[serve-targets]] formation.
- **Tournament / match-day.** Warm-up only — no new teaching, no penalty resets. [[age-lens-14u]] tournament-pacing rule applies one age earlier.
- **Postseason (final 2-3 sessions).** Re-run cooperative favorites; preview [[14s]] curriculum (full wash penalty structure; full three-station [[three-setter-rotation]] including the 31) so promotion-bound athletes see the on-ramp.

## Adaptations

- **Court size.** Full court for competitive reps; small-sided 4v4 acceptable for ball-control but not transition or 6v6 wash.
- **Ball.** Standard adult ball; lighter youth ball is no longer indicated except for athletes recovering from shoulder strain (defer to athletic trainer / CSCS).
- **Net height.** Train at 13s competition height (matches 14U standard).
- **Group size.** Setter-specific work runs in pods of 3 matched setter candidates plus 2-3 catchers; transition work needs full 12 athletes for the 6v6 frame.
- **Coaching language.** FeedForward as the operating mode. External-focus arm-swing cues are defensible per [[age-lens-14u]] — "long arm, high contact, see the line of the ball" beats internal anatomical cues. Setter-specific cues stay descriptive: "hand-hinge first, target second, decision last" (Liskevych ordering).
- **Penalty structure.** Real wash at 13s — earn-the-point logic, server side resets a partial point, defenders earn the other half by transitioning. Elimination formats still age-inappropriate; wash provides accountability without anxiety-loaded reps.
- **Position rotation.** Setter candidates begin specialization (60/40 reps); other positions still rotate every 2-3 sessions. Full-specialization libero training waits for late-14U per [[age-lens-14u]].
- **Volume guard.** 90-minute session cap; jump-volume drills deliberately absent. Jump-warmup work belongs more aggressively at 14s — at 13s, cap formal jump-warmup at 5-7 minutes with CSCS-aligned movement quality as the gate.
