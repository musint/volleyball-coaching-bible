# 15s Drill Pick-List (Composite)

## Purpose

Fifteens is the bridge year. Athletes are still inside the [[14u]] club calendar by USAV age but are arriving at — or finishing — their freshman HS varsity season; they have caught the post-puberty growth curve, the setter is reaching above the net for real, the OH approach has its full four steps, and team-defense systems start to need actual rotation discipline rather than coverage by hustle. This pick-list packages a season-spanning composite drill menu that holds onto the [[14u]]-foundation ball-control drills while introducing five new building blocks the age requires: **jump-warmup**, **jump-setting-progression**, **transition-hitting**, **six-player-defense**, and **reactive-jumping**. The age-lens cross-reference is split: most ball-control content still maps to [[age-lens-14u]] guidance, while the new system-execution and jump-loaded blocks map to [[age-lens-hs]] expectations. Treat this list as the floor for what a national-track 15s practice should be capable of running by mid-season.

## Must-do drill list

**Warm-up + jump-prep (2 drills)**
- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — non-negotiable session opener. Movement-prep, ladder, court-shape rehearsals; 10-12 minutes; scales identically across 14u/hs/college.
- [[jump-warmup]] — *new at 15s.* Pogo bounces → squat jumps → tuck jumps → flamingo plant → 3-step approach-and-land. Always after [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], never before. CSCS-disclaimed; volume-capped.

**Passing + serve-receive (2 drills)**
- [[butterfly-passing]] — the canonical pass-target-pass loop; remains the workhorse. Run with serve initiation by mid-season instead of toss.
- [[serve-receive-3v3]] — competitive 3-passer receive in the libero-split-readying formation. Forces communication discipline ahead of the [[serve-receive-libero-split]] install.

**Setting (3 drills)**
- [[target-setting]] — accuracy floor. Stationary then short-shuffle; the 7-of-10 standard before any setter touches the next progression.
- [[jump-setting-progression]] — *new at 15s.* Four-stage gate: stationary → step-and-set → approach-jump-set → live-pass jump-set. Only setters who clear stage 1 advance. See [[setting-jump]] for the readiness check.
- [[three-setter-rotation]] — three-setter circuit teaching front-set, back-set, and out-of-system reps under tempo pressure.

**Hitting (1 drill)**
- [[transition-hitting]] — *new at 15s.* Off-the-net retreat → live set → full approach. The single highest-leverage drill of the year for OH/Opp development; without it, 16s+ transition offense never installs.

**Serving (1 drill)**
- [[pressure-serving]] — points-on-the-line serving block. Carries the Kiraly serving-pressure frame: aggressive but accountable, error-rate visible.

**Defense (2 drills)**
- [[six-player-defense]] — *new at 15s.* Full team in defensive base with a coach-on-box attacker; introduces line/angle responsibilities, block-and-defend coordination, immediate-transition behavior. The system-installer.
- [[munciana-figure-eight-defense]] — Lingenfelter's figure-eight pursuit pattern; trains low-base footwork and read-and-go off a coach feed. Pairs cleanly as a pre-block to [[six-player-defense]].

**Competition (2 drills)**
- [[wash-drill]] — gateway to game-like training [usav-2009-cap-game-like-training]. Wash-style scoring (two consecutive scores to bank a point) installs the "no free balls" culture earlier than 16s.
- [[queen-of-the-court]] — three-team rotation with kill-or-leave-the-court stakes. Trains transition urgency without needing a full 6v6 install.

**Conditioning (1 drill, CSCS-supervised)**
- [[reactive-jumping]] — *new at 15s, CSCS disclaimer required.* Color-cued vertical → directional → approach jump bursts. Volume capped; quality-first; use as a finisher, not a workout. Defer programming load to the staff CSCS or athletic trainer; this is a reactive-movement template, not a plyometric program.

## When-in-season-to-use mapping

- **Tryout / preseason:** [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], [[butterfly-passing]], [[target-setting]], [[munciana-figure-eight-defense]] daily. [[jump-warmup]] introduced once (low-volume) toward the end of preseason. [[serve-receive-3v3]] for tryout evaluation. Skip [[reactive-jumping]] until baseline conditioning is in.
- **Early season:** add [[jump-setting-progression]] (stage 1-2 only), [[three-setter-rotation]], [[transition-hitting]] at the 14u-adapted level, [[pressure-serving]] as a 10-minute weekly block. [[wash-drill]] introduced at low intensity once per week.
- **Mid-season:** full menu in rotation. [[transition-hitting]] and [[six-player-defense]] in every practice that has 12+ athletes. [[jump-setting-progression]] stages 3-4 for setters who have cleared the gate. [[queen-of-the-court]] and [[wash-drill]] take 25-35% of practice volume. [[reactive-jumping]] introduced at low volume per CSCS guidance.
- **Pre-tournament:** lean on [[serve-receive-3v3]], [[pressure-serving]], [[six-player-defense]], [[wash-drill]]. Cut [[reactive-jumping]] in tournament-week tapers.
- **Match-day:** short [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] + abbreviated [[jump-warmup]] + on-court warm-up routine; no skill drills.
- **Postseason / off-season:** [[butterfly-passing]], [[target-setting]], [[jump-setting-progression]] for setter-position-development; [[reactive-jumping]] only if CSCS-supervised and athletes are uninjured.

## Adaptations

- **Down-scale to early-15s / late-14u club teams:** keep stages 1-2 of [[jump-setting-progression]], use the 14u adaptations on [[transition-hitting]] and [[six-player-defense]], cap [[reactive-jumping]] at 5 minutes total, and run [[wash-drill]] in cooperative (not punitive) scoring.
- **Up-scale to advanced 15s / freshman varsity:** advance [[jump-setting-progression]] to live-pass stage 4, run [[transition-hitting]] with all three front-row hitters live, install libero-split mechanics inside [[serve-receive-3v3]], allow [[wash-drill]] full punitive scoring.
- **Setter-only block:** combine [[target-setting]] → [[jump-setting-progression]] → [[three-setter-rotation]] as a 25-30 minute setter-development sequence on light-volume days.
- **CSCS supervision:** [[reactive-jumping]] and the higher-volume reps of [[jump-warmup]] should run only with a CSCS, AT, or qualified strength coach involved in volume and exercise selection. The volleyball coach owns the drill geometry; the CSCS owns the load. Athletes with growth-plate, joint, or recent-injury concerns clear with medical staff first.
- **Resource-thin clubs:** the warm-up + ball-control + competition core ([[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]], [[butterfly-passing]], [[target-setting]], [[wash-drill]], [[queen-of-the-court]]) carries practice quality on its own; defer the jump-loaded drills if you cannot supervise them.
