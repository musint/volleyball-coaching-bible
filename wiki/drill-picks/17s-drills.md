# Drill Picks: 17s

## Purpose

This pick-list is the working backbone for a competitive 17s practice — the year that sits one tournament season away from college. By 17s, athletes are recruited or recruiting, systems are settled, and practice is mostly about **deciding faster, swinging harder under contact, and finishing pressure rallies the way a college freshman has to**. Tactical depth replaces fundamental volume. The lift is competitive 6v6 work — king-of-the-court formats, out-of-system tactical reps, and conditioning that rehearses the back end of a third set rather than building base. Munciana's competitive 6v6 corpus is heavily featured here because the program's senior-team library (prove-it, finish-strong, momentum, hand-to-hand-combat) is purpose-built for exactly this developmental window. Cross-link [[17s]], [[age-lens-hs]], and [[age-lens-college]] — 17s is the bridge year and pulls from both sides of that arc.

## Must-do drill list

**Game-like 6v6 competitive (the backbone — most of practice lives here)**
- [[king-of-the-court]] — the round-robin format Kiraly ties to "compete in practice" culture; rotates teams on/off the queen's court, keeps every rep stakes-loaded.
- [[queen-of-the-court]] — same format, women's variant naming; belongs in the same slot as king-of-the-court depending on terminology preference.
- [[gold-medal-scrimmage]] — long-rally scoring rules that punish casual play and reward serving aggression; the Munciana-meets-USAV game-like default for the 17s-and-up roster.
- [[wash-drill]] — wash-style scoring (must win two sequential rallies to score) that disproportionately rewards out-of-system finishing and clean transition; a 17s-appropriate wash is intensity-heavy and teaches finishing.
- [[munciana-net-six]] — Munciana's 6v6 net competition format; the program's senior in-season standard rep.

**Tactical out-of-system to attack**
- [[out-of-system-to-attack]] — the technique drill that converts a high-set bail-out into a finishable swing; the leverage skill HS-to-college bridge.
- [[munciana-team-out-of-system]] — Munciana's team-level out-of-system rep; trains the setter-hitter connection when the pass is bad.

**Pressure serving**
- [[pressure-serving]] — Kiraly-frame serving under simulated match pressure: the server's job is to give the team a chance, not to ace [aoc-2024-kiraly-serving-pressure]. 17s is where this gets installed as a real practice block, not a warm-up.
- [[jump-serve-progression]] — for athletes with the physical tools and CSCS-cleared shoulder load; introduce or progress the jump-topspin pathway in 17s.

**Tactical hitting**
- [[line-vs-angle-shot]] — shot-selection toolbox work; line vs. angle vs. tip vs. roll under different defensive looks.
- [[transition-hitting]] — the modern HS+ transition-attack rep; OH back-row pipe entries from Zone 6, slide work, quick-tempo reps.
- [[hitting-vs-block]] — live-block reads, swing decisions, recycle off the block.

**Defense**
- [[six-player-defense]] — full-team defensive system rep with rotation/middle-back base defense; libero-in-6 alignment by default at 17s.

**Conditioning**
- [[conditioning-court-sprints]] — court-sprint conditioning rehearsing third-set physical demands; programmed in CSCS-aligned doses, not as punishment volume [usav-2026-coach-academy].

**Mental toughness rep**
- [[munciana-prove-it]] — Munciana's competitive-rep accountability drill; trains the "compete in practice" ethos directly [aoc-2023-kiraly-competing-in-practice].

## When-in-season-to-use mapping

- **Preseason / install:** lighter king-of-the-court rotations to surface decision-making gaps; out-of-system technique work; jump-serve-progression as install. Conditioning court-sprints front-loaded.
- **Mid-season:** the heavy block — full-rotation gold-medal-scrimmage, wash, prove-it, net-six daily; pressure-serving twice per week; tactical hitting (line-vs-angle, transition-hitting, hitting-vs-block) cycled through.
- **Pre-tournament / qualifier week:** taper conditioning; tighten 6v6 rep volume but raise stakes (every rep scores against rotation order). Out-of-system reps simulate the bad-pass third-set scenario.
- **Match-day shake-out:** brief king-of-the-court at moderate intensity for rhythm; pressure-serving five-before-ten; no conditioning.
- **Postseason / handoff:** prove-it and momentum-style finishing reps to close the season culture-positive; reduced jump-serving volume (shoulder/knee taper).

## Adaptations

- **Down-scale to 16s** when the 17s plan runs hot: cut hitting-vs-block live-swing volume, replace some jump-serve-progression reps with serving accountability (zone-serving, no-gut-serving), shorten conditioning court-sprint blocks.
- **Up-scale toward 18s / college-bridge:** add arm-care-routine load (post-practice maintenance), increase out-of-system to college-tempo expectations (faster setter decisions, expect off-the-net redirects), introduce hitting-vs-block at college net-height block reads.
- **CSCS gate.** Conditioning court-sprints, jump-serve-progression, and any high-volume jumping is programmed by a CSCS, not a volleyball coach [usav-2026-coach-academy]. The 17s year is where ACL-prevention landing mechanics and jump-load tracking become non-optional — see [[age-lens-hs]] for the load-monitoring stack.
- **Roster-size shifts.** With ≤10 healthy bodies, run 6v6 as 6v3+wave (3 transition-side, 3 rotating in); king-of-the-court stays viable down to 9. Below that, collapse to small-sided wash and tactical-hitting station work.
