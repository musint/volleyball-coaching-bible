# 11s Drill Pick List

## Purpose

The working drill backbone for an [[11s]] practice. Built on the [[10s-drills]] baseline — every cooperative ball-control rep at 10s still works at 11s — with two age-band pivots: an **overhand serve introduction** prioritizing posture, toss, and contact over reaching the back line, and a **simple shuttle / two-line passing** progression that lets athletes touch a real serve-receive lane without the 6v6 load. The lens is [[age-lens-14u]] dialed two years younger: ball-control first, reading embedded, no jump training, equal reps, smaller spaces, lighter loads, zero penalty-driven competition. 11s is still inside USAV's "Growing Kids' Volleyball" small-sided band — keep courts manageable, rotations egalitarian, and the ball moving.

## Must-do drill list

Twelve drills. Each links to its full page; per-line rationale describes the **11s adaptation**.

### Warm-up and ball-control

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — Movement-prep template. At 11s, abbreviate the dynamic block to ~6 minutes and skip any plyometric add-on; mobility plus light skipping/lateral shuffles is plenty for the warm-up window.
- [[ball-control-warmup]] — Self-contact and partner-tap progressions. Stay on the cooperative side of the drill; no penalties for drops. The metric is "how long can the partnership keep it alive," not "who wins."
- [[partner-pepper-warmup]] — Pass-set-hit pepper at half-volume. Substitute a controlled forearm-down attack (no swing) for the hit phase until forearm-platform contact is consistent.

### Footwork and movement

- [[munciana-two-man-shuffle]] — Lateral shuffle paired with a partner-toss catch. The "Tilt" cue (lower hip toward the ball) lands well at 11s because it pre-loads platform formation without requiring a live pass yet.

### Passing — shuttle and two-line additions

- [[two-line-passing]] — **The 11s headline passing drill.** Two short lines facing a single target across a 5-7 m gap; coach feeds from the target side rather than serving across the net. Simplest possible "real" passing rep: read a tossed ball, platform to target, get out.
- [[shuttle-passing]] — Add once two-line is rhythmic. At 11s use coach-tosses only; pull the gap to 7-8 m and keep tosses high and slow.
- [[butterfly-passing]] — Modified: one half-court only, tossed serves only, target is a hoop or a coach catching with two hands. Full butterfly with a real overhand serve is a 12s+ drill.
- [[munciana-straight-line-passing]] — Munciana's "free tilt" line drill, run as feeder-passer-target-shagger with relaxed scoring; value is rep volume in a stable lane.

### Setting

- [[target-setting]] — Use the **catch-and-set version**: setter catches the toss with hand-hinge contact, freezes for a one-count to feel hand position, then releases the next ball as a real set. The freeze drops out as touch develops; most 11s setters need it for at least the first month.

### Serving — overhand introduction

- [[munciana-kneel-single-leg-serve]] — **The 11s overhand-serve gateway.** Kneel ~3 m back from the net and serve overhand to a partner-catcher, then progress to single-leg, then standing short. Goal is *contact* — ball clears the net with a real swing — not depth or zone accuracy.
- [[serve-targets]] — Run from the 3 m or attack line, not the endline. Target the entire receiving half (no zone calls yet) so every clean serve over the net counts. Walk the start line back 1 m per session as the toss-step-swing rhythm consolidates.

### Cooperative competition

- [[cooperative-25-goal]] — Adapted: any pass-set-attempted-attack scores regardless of landing zone; goal lowered to 15-20; "in-system" defined loosely. Stays cooperative — no losing team, no penalties — ends when goal is reached.

## When-in-season-to-use mapping

- **Preseason / first 4-6 weeks.** Warm-up cluster (dynamic, ball-control, partner-pepper) plus two-line-passing and target-setting catch-version. Introduce kneel-and-single-leg overhand serving in week 1; earlier swing exposure banks more reps.
- **Mid-season.** Shuttle-passing and butterfly-passing (modified) become the passing backbone; serve-targets from the 3 m line replaces the kneel-progression once the swing is consolidated. Cooperative-25-goal opens once basic in-system continuity exists.
- **Pre-tournament / match-day prep.** Warm-up, two-line-passing as the pass-rep block, two minutes of partner-pepper for activation, short cooperative-25-goal block. Skip everything else.

## Adaptations

- **Smaller / less-experienced teams.** Drop butterfly-passing; double-dose two-line-passing. Keep all overhand serving at the kneel/single-leg progression all season — depth is not the priority, the swing pattern is.
- **More-experienced teams (second-year, older-end-of-band).** Move serve-targets to the endline with zone calls; allow shuttle-passing to take real coach serves. Layer in a Munciana directional-pass variant.
- **Setter-track athletes.** Pull them for an extra [[target-setting]] block with the freeze removed and a hoop at zone 4. Hand-hinge mechanics at 11 are the cheapest investment a setter ever makes.
- **Penalty / consequence design.** Per the [[age-lens-14u]] downscale: no push-ups, no sprints, no losing-team consequences. Replace with "challenge rounds" — bonus reps for leaders, no punishment for trailers.
