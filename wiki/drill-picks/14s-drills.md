# 14s — Drill Pick List

Parent context: [[14s]] (age-guide), [[age-lens-14u]] (the club lens this list operationalizes).

## Purpose

This is the canonical 15-drill backbone for a 14s practice. The age band sits at the late-fundamentals → specialization-onset hinge: passing platforms have stabilized, hand-hinge setting is in place, the three-step approach is reliable, and standing-float serving is the daily-driver. The drill list reflects that arc — heavy ball-control, repeated reading reps embedded in everything, Munciana movement-and-pursuit drills as the pace backbone, and one transition / out-of-system primer to bridge into 15s.

The selections honor the drill-mix ratio defended in [[age-lens-14u]] — roughly 30–40% ball-control, 20–30% small-group competitive, 20–30% game-like, 10% warm-up — and Swan's CAP rule that if it isn't game-like, it doesn't earn court time. All 15 drills are tagged `levels: [14u, ...]` in their drill pages and are within the cognitive load and physical envelope appropriate for adolescent female athletes per the injury and motor-learning evidence cited on the parent age-lens.

## Must-do drill list

**Warm-up (2 drills).**

- [[dynamic-warmup-volleyball]] — ball-in-hand dynamic warmup; opens every practice. Movement preparation, joint mobility, and ball-touches in one block.
- [[jump-warmup]] — light approach-and-jump primer; CSCS-compatible volume; preps neuromuscular landing patterns before the first hitting block.

**Passing (4 drills).**

- [[butterfly-passing]] — the canonical 14s passing anchor; forces feet-beat-hands and target-grading aloud.
- [[two-line-passing]] — partner-grade reps with high feedback density; pairs cleanly after butterfly.
- [[munciana-three-man-tilt]] — Munciana movement-based passing; passers tilt the platform while the ball is in flight, embedding the centering-not-reaching cue Kordes emphasizes.
- [[munciana-rush-and-push]] — two-balls-one-passer pursuit drill; trains the second-ball read and the recovery footwork that wins long rallies.

**Setting (2 drills).**

- [[target-setting]] — hand-hinge fundamentals to a static target; the entry rep that locks side-of-ball contact in.
- [[munciana-five-and-five-setting]] — set-and-cover sequence introducing out-of-system reads; the late-14s bridge to live setter-hitter timing without committing to a 5-1.

**Hitting (1 drill).**

- [[approach-and-swing]] — three-step approach with Schmidt's "start slow, accelerate the last two" tempo cue; tossed entry, then setter-fed; isolates the swing without imposing setter-timing load.

**Composite skill (1 drill).**

- [[pass-set-hit]] — the universal 14s rep: pass to target, target sets, attacker swings. Trains the three-contact rhythm that 6v6 success rests on.

**Serving (2 drills).**

- [[serve-targets]] — accuracy-first standing-float serving to numbered zones; reps Dunning's toss-first emphasis without pressure-load.
- [[munciana-no-gut-serving]] — light-pressure serving with consequence; introduces a pressure-acclimatization element appropriate at 14s without the all-or-nothing stakes of a wash drill.

**Defense (1 drill).**

- [[munciana-go-stay]] — read-based defense: the defender either pursues or holds based on a triggered cue; the cleanest 14s introduction to Look/See/Decide diagnosis on real attacks.

**Game-like / cooperative (1 drill).**

- [[cooperative-25-goal]] — Kessel-style cooperative target; teams chase a shared count of clean three-contact sequences. The grills-not-drills test in one rep.

**Transition primer (1 drill).**

- [[munciana-mia-drill]] — defender sets out-of-system to an attacker; the late-14s gateway into transition reads. Run as a concept block, not a stress-tested install.

## When-in-season-to-use mapping

The pick-list is tagged `season-context: composite` because at 14s the seasonal arc is itself a development arc — a "preseason-only" backbone would shortchange a club calendar that runs November through May. Use the list as follows:

- **Preseason (Nov–early Dec).** Lean into the warm-up + ball-control block (dynamic-warmup-volleyball, jump-warmup, butterfly-passing, two-line-passing, target-setting, approach-and-swing). Re-establish movement quality and reset platform/hand-hinge fundamentals. Run cooperative-25-goal as the closing rep — chemistry-first, per Rose's club-rules framing.
- **Mid-season (Dec–Feb, local-qualifier window).** Add the Munciana movement drills (munciana-three-man-tilt, munciana-rush-and-push, munciana-five-and-five-setting, munciana-go-stay). These raise the read-and-pursue density without imposing wash-drill anxiety on under-construction motor programs.
- **Pre-tournament (regionals, March–April).** Add munciana-no-gut-serving for low-stakes pressure-acclimatization, and munciana-mia-drill to install the out-of-system look. Keep cooperative-25-goal as the closer; a high-confidence final block matters more than a final stress test at 14s.
- **Taper / match-day.** Pull the Munciana pursuit and out-of-system drills out; keep the warm-up block, butterfly-passing, target-setting, approach-and-swing, serve-targets, and cooperative-25-goal. Reps that build confidence; nothing that introduces new failure modes the day before competition.
- **Tryout / team-formation week.** Run the warm-up block, butterfly-passing (graded), target-setting, approach-and-swing, and serve-targets — the rubric-friendly, scorable subset.

## Adaptations

- **Newer 14s teams (first-year-of-6s, late tryouts).** Cap pursuit drills (rush-and-push, go-stay) at half-volume; substitute extra reps of two-line-passing and target-setting. Skip munciana-mia-drill until late-season.
- **Veteran 14s teams (second-year, nationally competitive).** Increase munciana-five-and-five-setting volume; add a pressure-tier to munciana-no-gut-serving (consequence reps); run pass-set-hit with a live setter rather than a target.
- **Mixed-experience rosters.** Run butterfly-passing and target-setting as the levelers; veterans grade newer passers aloud; cooperative-25-goal mixes the lines so the chemistry rep is real.
- **Short-session days (60-min practice).** Keep warm-up + butterfly-passing + target-setting + approach-and-swing + cooperative-25-goal. Five drills; complete arc; no skill block neglected.
- **Athlete-load caution (post-tournament, growth-spurt visible).** Pull jump-warmup volume; pull munciana-rush-and-push (the highest-pursuit drill) entirely. The injury-prevention frame on the parent age-lens applies at the daily-plan level, not just the macrocycle.
