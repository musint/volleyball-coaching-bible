# Chris McGown

## Overview

Chris McGown is a co-founder and current head coach of [[gold-medal-squared]] (GMS) — the motor-learning-grounded coaching methodology business founded in 1985 by his father [[carl-mcgown]] alongside [[marv-dunphy]] and [[doug-beal]] [gms-nd-about-us]. Chris McGown is the family lineage-carrier of the GMS school: after Carl McGown's death in December 2016 he is one of the primary voices keeping the organization's camps, clinics, and online coach-education operation running as a continuing methodology-propagation business [gms-nd-about-us] [gms-2017-thank-you-carl-mcgown]. The BYU men's volleyball program lists him in its coach-lineage affiliations as part of the broader McGown coaching tree [unsourced].

## Coaching career

**Gold Medal Squared — co-founder and current head coach.** The GMS public About page identifies Chris McGown as a co-founder of the GMS coaching business and as a current head coach in the organization [gms-nd-about-us]. GMS today functions as (1) an instructional methodology with named commitments (specificity, random/variable practice, reading-and-reaction, external-focus cues, skill-simplification), (2) a coaching network anchored by Chris McGown and including multiple Olympic-staff coaches and a large roster of sitting NCAA Division I head coaches, and (3) a delivery business running camps, clinics, and online coach education [gms-nd-about-us] [gms-2018-clinic-report] [gms-nd-structure-practice].

**BYU lineage and continuation.** The [[byu]] men's volleyball program lists Chris McGown among its affiliated coaches — the family-lineage carrier of his father's 1988-2006 BYU head-coaching tenure and two NCAA men's national championships (1999, 2001) [unsourced]. ## Core teaching principles

Chris McGown's public teaching voice is primarily through GMS organizational output (About page, clinic reports, practice-structure articles) rather than through personally bylined books or interviews in the current source set [gms-nd-about-us] [gms-2018-clinic-report] [gms-nd-structure-practice]. The school's organizing commitments — which Chris McGown continues to propagate — are documented on the [[gold-medal-squared]] school page: **motor-learning science over opinion, specificity of training, random/variable practice over blocked practice, reading-and-reaction primacy, external-focus cues, intentional feedback, and deliberate skill simplification** [gms-nd-structure-practice] [gms-2017-thank-you-carl-mcgown]. ## Contributions to the game

**GMS continuation through the 2020s.** Carl McGown's death in December 2016 created a succession question for the GMS organization; Chris McGown is the public face of that continuation, keeping the camps, clinics, and online coach-education operation running as the primary institutional carrier of the motor-learning-grounded American coaching tradition [gms-nd-about-us] [gms-2017-thank-you-carl-mcgown]. The continuity matters at a school-level: GMS remains the cleanest articulation of "maximize meaningful touches per hour" in U.S. coach education.

**Bridge between Carl McGown's teaching and the current coaching network.** The current GMS coaching network — including Mike Wall, Rob Browning, Luka Slabe, John Speraw, Courtney Thompson, and a large NCAA D-I head-coaching roster — sits downstream of the methodology-propagation business Chris McGown helps run [gms-nd-about-us]. That network's continuing reach into USA men's and USA women's coaching staffs means the GMS school's methodological footprint on the modern American coaching landscape runs through the institutional infrastructure Chris McGown is part of.
