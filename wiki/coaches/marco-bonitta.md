# Marco Bonitta

## Overview

Marco Bonitta (b. September 5, 1963, Ravenna) is the Italian women's volleyball coach who delivered Italy women's only senior FIVB World Championship title — **2002 Germany, gold** [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. He held the Italy women's national-team head-coaching post across two non-contiguous cycles: **March 2001 to September 2006** (his peak medal cycle) and **2014–2016** (a second cycle ending with the Rio 2016 Olympics) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. His foundational women's-club tenure is **Foppapedretti Bergamo (1997–2000)**, where he won back-to-back **CEV European Champions Cup** titles (1998–99, 1999–2000), two Italian Serie A championships, one Coppa Italia, and three Italian SuperCups in three seasons [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. Within the [[italian-school]] tradition he occupies the women's-NT-results bracket between the [[julio-velasco]] men's-program 1990s era and the women's club-internationalist generation that produced [[giovanni-guidetti]]; his 2002 World Championship gold remains the canonical Italian women's NT achievement of the post-Velasco-men's-era. Bonitta has subsequently coached the Poland women's NT (2007–2008), the Slovenia women's NT (2022–2023/24), the Italy U20 men's team (2012 CEV U20 European gold), and most recently the Egypt men's NT (2025–) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio].

## Coaching career

**Italian club origins (1990–1997).** Bonitta's early coaching career is in his hometown's Porto Ravenna Volley youth program (1990–1996) followed by a one-season head-coach role at Olimpia / Big Power Ravenna (1996–1997) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 1990–96 youth-coaching apprenticeship inside an Italian Serie A men's club's pipeline places his foundational coaching education inside the Italian-federation system at the height of the Velasco men's-team era.

**Foppapedretti Bergamo (1997–2000) — the defining women's-club tenure.** Bonitta was hired to head-coach the Bergamo women's senior team in 1997. Across three seasons the club won every domestic and continental trophy contested: **2 Italian Serie A championships (1997–98, 1998–99), 1 Italian Cup (1997–98), 3 Italian SuperCups (1997, 1998, 1999), and back-to-back CEV European Champions Cup titles (1998–99, 1999–2000)** [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 1998–2000 Bergamo dynasty is the era's defining Italian women's club program and the credential that produced the Italy federation's interest in 2001.

**Italy women's NT, first cycle (March 2001–September 2006).** Bonitta succeeded Angelo Frigoni in March 2001 [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. His first major event delivered silver: 2001 European Championship in Bulgaria [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 2002 FIVB World Championship in Germany delivered the program's defining result — **gold**, Italy women's only senior Worlds title to date [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The cycle continued with three consecutive World Grand Prix podiums (2004 silver Reggio Calabria, 2005 silver Sendai, 2006 bronze Reggio Calabria) and a second European silver in 2005 (Croatia) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. Bonitta resigned in September 2006 following reported player requests for his removal [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio] — a departure note worth recording because it indicates the cycle ended over locker-room dynamics rather than results.

**Inter-cycle and post-2016 career (2006–present).** A Poland women's NT head-coach term (2007–2008), the Italy U20 men's **2012 CEV U20 European Championship gold**, a sequence of Italian Serie A men's clubs at Ravenna (Consar / Porto Robur Costa, multiple stints 2012–2024), AZS Olsztyn in Poland (2021), the Slovenia women's NT (2022–2023/24), LOVB Austin (2025), and the Egypt men's NT (2025–) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio].

**Italy women's NT, second cycle (2014–2016).** Bonitta returned for a Rio 2016 cycle [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The cycle did not produce a senior medal at Worlds, Euros, or the Olympics on the public record captured here.

**Italian state honor.** Officer of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic (Cavaliere Ufficiale OMRI), awarded 2002 after the World Championship gold [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio].

## Contributions to the game

**The 2002 Italy women's World Championship.** Italy's only senior FIVB Women's Worlds gold to date, won under Bonitta in Germany 2002 [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. This is the canonical women's-NT achievement of the Italian federation's post-Velasco-men's-era and the result that situates Italy among the small set of nations to have won a senior Women's World Championship.

**Foppapedretti Bergamo (1998–2000) as the era's defining Italian women's club program.** Back-to-back CEV European Champions Cup titles plus a domestic clean sweep [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio] established Bergamo as the late-1990s benchmark for Italian women's club volleyball — the program-shape later inherited and internationalized by VakıfBank under [[giovanni-guidetti]].

**Two-cycle senior-NT tenure structure.** Bonitta is one of a small group of national-team head coaches to have been re-appointed for a second cycle after a multi-year gap (2001–06; 2014–16) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The data point matters because it reflects a federation pattern (re-engaging a known head-coach voice for an Olympic year) more than a single doctrinal contribution.

**Italian-school cross-federation export.** Bonitta's later head-coaching of three other federations' women's programs (Poland 2007–08, Slovenia 2022–23/24) plus a men's program (Egypt 2025–) [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio] reflects the broader [[italian-school]] export pattern that also describes [[julio-velasco]] (Argentina men, Iran men, Italy women), [[giovanni-guidetti]] (six federations), and [[andrea-anastasi]].

**2012 CEV U20 European Championship gold (Italy U20 men).** A youth-men result inside the Italian federation's pipeline [bonitta-2026-wikipedia-bio] — significant because it indicates the Italian federation continued to use Bonitta in development roles between his two senior women's NT cycles.
