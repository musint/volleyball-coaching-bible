# Cassidy Lichtman

## Overview

Cassidy Lichtman is the founding athlete-leader of [[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]] and the architectural figure for the AU model's athlete-governance framework — the first athlete to sit on the Athletes Unlimited Pro Volleyball Board of Directors and the league's Director of Volleyball since 2022 [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. Born May 25, 1989 in Poway, California, the 6'1" outside hitter played at Stanford 2007–2011, earning first-team AVCA All-American honors as a junior and senior, four Pac-10 championships, and dual Stanford degrees (B.A. political science, M.A. history) completed in four years [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. She represented the USA Women's National Team 2011–2016 across the 2014 FIVB World Grand Prix, the 2011 and 2015 Pan American Games, and multiple Pan American Cups, then played five years overseas (Poland, Switzerland, Azerbaijan, France, China; final stop Sichuan 2015) before joining AU as a founding athlete in 2021 [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. Her on-court AU career includes a fourth-place finish in Season 2 (2022); her off-court contribution is the structural one — she is the athlete-leader figure used to frame the AU governance model in public communications [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. AdWeek named her among its Most Powerful Women in Sports in 2022; in 2026 she was elected Chair of the USA Volleyball Board [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. She co-founded the nonprofit P/ATH (2020), focused on developing empathy and empowerment in sports [lichtman-wikipedia-bio].

While Lichtman's primary classification in this wiki is athlete-leader / sports administrator rather than head-coach in the conventional NCAA / NT sense, her [[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]] governance role qualifies for inclusion in the coaching-and-program-leader corpus because AU is a head-coach-less competitive structure where her role substitutes for the franchise-head-coach institution.

## Contributions to the game

- **First athlete on the AU Board of Directors.** This is the structural innovation in U.S. women's pro volleyball governance: Lichtman is the named athlete-director who anchors the AU equity-in-governance commitment [lichtman-wikipedia-bio][[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]]. The role has been influential — PVF's transparent salary disclosure and LOVB's prize-pool framing both implicitly respond to AU's earlier articulation that athletes deserve a defined share of league economics [[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]].
- **AU Director of Volleyball role since 2022.** Lichtman's AU executive position is the on-record voice for AU public communications, including the 2025 championship rebrand framing [lichtman-wikipedia-bio][[athletes-unlimited-volleyball]]. The structural significance: AU's governance commitments are not anonymous policy — they have a named athlete-leader behind them.
- **USA Volleyball Board Chair (2026–).** Lichtman's election to the USA Volleyball Board Chair role brings the athlete-leader experience built at AU into the federation's governance level [lichtman-wikipedia-bio]. This is the highest U.S. domestic-volleyball governance role to be held by a former WNT player and AU founding athlete.
- **Stanford academic-athletic-dual-track example.** Completing a B.A. and M.A. in four years while playing first-team AVCA All-American volleyball is itself a model that some Stanford athletic-recruiting conversations cite, though specific recruiting-conversation citations are not in the Wikipedia source [unsourced — recruiting-conversation citations are not in this corpus].
- **P/ATH co-founder (2020).** The nonprofit's empathy-and-empowerment focus extends Lichtman's athlete-leader pathway into youth-and-community sport-development work [lichtman-wikipedia-bio].
