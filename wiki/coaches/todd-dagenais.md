# Todd Dagenais

## Overview

Todd Dagenais is the winningest head coach in UCF women's volleyball history (308-159 across fifteen seasons, 2008-2023), the winningest coach in American Athletic Conference history, and — after leaving college — the founding head coach of the Pro Volleyball Federation's Atlanta Vibe (19-5, 2024 regular-season championship) and now Director of League Operations at League One Volleyball (LOVB) [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio] [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure] [dagenais-wikipedia-bio]. Within the Art of Coaching Volleyball (AOC) content network he is best known for a 2018 piece on "coaching the iY generation" that cites Mack Brown (Texas football) and [[hugh-mccutcheon]] (Minnesota / USA national teams) as cross-sport influences on communicating with modern athletes [aoc-2018-coaching-iy-generation].

## Coaching career

**Playing and entry into coaching.** Dagenais is a native of Michigan's Upper Peninsula [dagenais-wikipedia-bio]. He earned a B.S. in Sports Medicine with an exercise-physiology emphasis from Central Michigan University in 1995, where he played four years on the Chippewas' men's club team and qualified as a certified athletic trainer [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. He entered the coaching side of the sport as an assistant at Northern Michigan in 1998 (NCAA Division II), serving as recruiting and travel coordinator [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio].

**Michigan State (1998-2003).** Dagenais spent five seasons as associate head coach at Michigan State, during which the Spartans reached the NCAA Tournament all five seasons [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. This is the stretch in which his tactical and recruiting chops were shaped under a Big Ten program; the available sources do not name the head coach he served under [unsourced].

**USC (three seasons, mid-2000s).** Dagenais then served three seasons as associate/assistant head coach at USC, helping the Trojans to a 29-5 record and the 2007 NCAA Final Four, with the program finishing third in the final AVCA poll [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. USC at that time was coached by Mick Haley, whom Dagenais later identifies as one of his three primary coaching influences [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]; the direct working relationship there is the source-supported connection [unsourced] to some of Haley's people-management framing later visible in Dagenais's own teams.

**UCF head coach (2008-2023).** Dagenais took over UCF in January 2008 and ran the program for fifteen seasons [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. He finished 308-159 overall (.660), 183-85 in AAC play, and 155-61 at home [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. In AAC play he was the winningest volleyball coach in conference history with 227 total wins and 142 conference wins across the tenure [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure]. Awards and postseason results: four AAC Coach of the Year awards; six NCAA Tournament appearances (2014, 2018, 2019, 2020-21, 2021, 2022); five consecutive AAC titles (spanning 2018-2022 per the departure announcement, or 2019-2023 per the later UCF bio, depending on where one draws the tenure cut) [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio] [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure]. The program's first NCAA Tournament victory came over Florida State, 3-2, in 2019 — the match that made him UCF's all-time winningest volleyball coach [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. Player-development output across the tenure: 14 AVCA All-America honorees, 24 All-Region selections, 38 all-conference honors [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio].

**Academic culture.** UCF volleyball maintained a minimum 3.0 team GPA every semester under Dagenais from May 2008 onward, earning 62 conference academic honors across the tenure; by the end of his fifteen seasons the combined team GPA was 3.68 — the highest among all UCF athletic programs at the time of his departure [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure]. Athletic director Terry Mohajir framed the tenure as "building UCF volleyball into a national program" [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure].

**USA Volleyball involvement.** Dagenais was co-head coach of the 2016 USA Collegiate Women's National Team, served on the 2018 Collegiate National Team selection committee, and coached U.S. Women's Junior A2 programs in 2011-2012 (bronze at the 2011 USAV High Performance Championships) [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. He was part of the 2004 Olympic Games coaching delegation and has worked as lead court coach and director at USA High Performance tryouts [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio].

**Pro Volleyball Federation — Atlanta Vibe (2024).** Dagenais left UCF after the 2022-23 season to "help start a professional league," in his own words [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure]. He was hired as the founding head coach of the Pro Volleyball Federation's Atlanta Vibe and led the team to a 19-5 inaugural-season record and the PVF regular-season championship [dagenais-wikipedia-bio].

**League One Volleyball (current).** He now serves as Director of League Operations for League One Volleyball (LOVB) [dagenais-wikipedia-bio].

**Honors.** Upper Peninsula Sports Hall of Fame inductee, 2019 [dagenais-wikipedia-bio].

## Core teaching principles

**Three-mentor synthesis as explicit coaching identity.** The single clearest statement of Dagenais's coaching philosophy — from his own UCF bio — credits three mentors for the way his teams are built: Chuck Erbe, for whom "the discipline of the game is paramount"; Toshi Yoshida, who supplied an international-level tactical and training perspective; and Mick Haley, whose contribution is "creative people management and maximizing individual talent toward collective goals" [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio]. Unlike many coaches at his level, Dagenais is on record naming this synthesis as the core of his approach rather than a single school — discipline plus international framing plus people management, in that explicit stack [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio].

**Generational adaptation in coach-to-player communication.** In his 2018 AOC piece on "coaching the iY generation," Dagenais argues that athletes raised by Generation X parents bring distinct communication expectations — protective upbringings, desires for belonging, and instant-gratification patterns — that require coaches to adjust style rather than expect the athlete to adjust [aoc-2018-coaching-iy-generation]. He names Mack Brown (former Texas football) and [[hugh-mccutcheon]] (then Minnesota women's head coach; earlier USA men's and women's national team head coach) as the two sources he has leaned on for communication guidelines with modern athletes [aoc-2018-coaching-iy-generation]. The AOC video body itself is paywalled, so the specific communication-guideline content beyond the intro paragraph is not publicly captured in our source base [unsourced].

**Academic culture as a program standard, not a side-benefit.** The every-semester 3.0+ GPA standard held across fifteen consecutive seasons, plus the 3.68 exit GPA being the highest among all UCF athletic programs, is evidence that academic performance sits inside Dagenais's program definition rather than beside it — but the available sources state the outcome without laying out the practice-level and recruiting-level mechanics that produced it [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure] [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio] [unsourced].

**Program-building at a non-traditional volleyball school.** UCF was not a historical volleyball power when Dagenais arrived in 2008; the AAC-winningest record and five consecutive conference titles followed, from a base that required him to build recruiting, identity, and staffing from the ground up [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio] [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure]. That the program-building was durable enough to transfer into a founding-season pro championship (PVF Atlanta, 19-5) one year later is the strongest external signal that the methodology is operational rather than roster-dependent [dagenais-wikipedia-bio].

## Contributions to the game

**UCF as a repeatable mid-major-to-national-program case.** Before Dagenais, UCF women's volleyball had no NCAA Tournament victories; by 2023 it had six NCAA Tournament appearances, five consecutive AAC titles, and the program's first NCAA win [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio] [dagenais-2023-ucf-departure]. This makes Dagenais one of a small group of non-Power-5 coaches whose program trajectory serves as a working blueprint for other mid-major athletic departments contemplating volleyball investment [unsourced].

**Founding coach of a viable women's pro franchise in the US.** The Pro Volleyball Federation launched in 2024 as a serious attempt to establish a sustainable top-tier women's indoor league in the United States. Atlanta Vibe under Dagenais won the inaugural regular-season title, establishing an early template for what coaching in that league looks like [dagenais-wikipedia-bio]. As a founding head coach who also moved from there into league-operations work at LOVB — the competing U.S. pro league — Dagenais now sits inside the institutional design of American women's pro volleyball itself [dagenais-wikipedia-bio].

**AOC content network — single-issue contribution on generational coaching.** Dagenais's public coach-education footprint is narrower than that of coaches like [[karch-kiraly]] or [[terry-liskevych]], but his 2018 "iY generation" piece is one of the earliest AOC videos to treat generational differences in athletes as a first-class coaching problem rather than a soft-skill aside — framing it with cross-sport references to Mack Brown and McCutcheon, and locating communication style as something the coach must adapt to the athlete rather than vice versa [aoc-2018-coaching-iy-generation].

**USAV collegiate and high-performance roles.** Co-head coaching the 2016 USA Collegiate Women's National Team, sitting on the 2018 Collegiate NT selection committee, running the U.S. Women's Junior A2 programs in 2011-2012, and serving as lead court coach at USA High Performance tryouts places Dagenais inside the USA Volleyball pipeline-selection apparatus, with visibility on collegiate talent at a level most college head coaches do not have [dagenais-ucf-athletics-bio].
