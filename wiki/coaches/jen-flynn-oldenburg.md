# Jen Flynn Oldenburg

## Overview

Jen Flynn Oldenburg is the head coach of Ohio State women's volleyball and an [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] contributor whose public coaching voice is concentrated on out-of-system (OOS) offense [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill] [aoc-2024-oldenburg-double-take]. Across the AOC drill set she published in late 2024 she identifies herself as "Buckeye coach" running drills inside the Ohio State gym [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill]. This profile is brief by design — the available source base is limited to six short AOC drill articles, so biographical scope stays close to what those articles substantiate. Broader biographical material (playing career, hire date, full coaching résumé) is [unsourced] in this wiki and flagged in [[unsourced-queue]].

## Coaching career

**Head coach, Ohio State.** Oldenburg coaches the Ohio State Buckeyes women's program and is identified as head coach in AOC drill captions [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill] [aoc-2024-oldenburg-double-take] [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-drill]. ## Core teaching principles

**1. OOS setting is a team skill, not a setter-only skill.** Oldenburg's most-repeated thesis across the AOC cluster: every player on the court must be able to set an out-of-system ball cleanly enough that the team still gets a quality swing. "Everybody should be able to dig a ball. Everybody should be able to set an out-of-system ball. Everybody should be able to hit a ball hard to a corner. We want our liberos taking swings at the ball" [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-attacking]. The "Double Take" drill — whoever digs the first ball *must* also set the second — operationalizes the principle by forcing every position to rehearse the dig-set handoff [aoc-2024-oldenburg-double-take] [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-double-take].

**2. Non-setter OOS setting must be bold.** Hitters should still "take rips" on out-of-system balls, which requires whoever is setting OOS — setter or not — to be *bold with the contact* rather than cautious [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill] [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3in-3oos-drill]. Tentative bump-setting is a failure mode: if the emergency setter short-arms the contact, the hitter can't rip.

**3. Buy time with height.** Her OOS setting drill teaches diggers and setters to set and dig *higher* when scrambling — extra time is the most valuable thing a first contact can give a hitter in a chaotic rep [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-drill].

**4. Hands finishing to target.** On OOS 2-handed setting she teaches "hands finishing to target" — finishing the setting motion pointed where the ball should land — so that hitters read ball flight earlier and commit to a swing window [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-drill]. This is consistent with the broader AOC read-first framing voiced by [[karch-kiraly]] [aoc-2024-kiraly-reads-every-skill].

**5. Alternate in-system and out-of-system reps in drill design.** The "3 in-system, 3 out-of-system" team drill keeps rally quality match-like by alternating controlled free-ball initiations with chaotic coach-bounce deep balls, because real matches oscillate between the two constantly; the 6-in-a-row target creates sustained pressure without becoming an open-ended slog [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill].

**6. Cover habits travel with the set.** On every OOS rep, both the digger and the emergency setter step up to cover an imaginary hitter near where the ball will land [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-drill]. Cover is trained as an automatic next move, not a separate drill.

## Contributions to the game

**An OOS-setting curriculum for non-setters.** Oldenburg's AOC cluster assembles an integrated progression for teaching *team-wide* OOS setting capability — a skill area older drill libraries typically leave to the setter alone. The progression runs from the foundational dig-and-set drill [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-drill], to the same structure with attacking layered on [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-attacking], to the Double Take variant forcing same-player dig-then-set [aoc-2024-oldenburg-double-take], to the full 6-on-6 alternating in-system / out-of-system team drill [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill]. That chain is a teachable four-step progression adaptable from 14U through college and is the clearest single-voice statement of "OOS as universal skill" in the current AOC catalog.

## Quotes & representative passages

On universal OOS capability: "Everybody should be able to dig a ball. Everybody should be able to set an out-of-system ball. Everybody should be able to hit a ball hard to a corner. We want our liberos taking swings at the ball" [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-attacking].

On bold contact: "We encourage our hitters to take rips at the ball, so our players who are setting out of system – whether it's a setter or not – are going for it. They have to be bold with that contact" [aoc-2024-oldenburg-3-in-3-out-drill].

On the teaching cue: "Hands finishing to target" — on OOS setting, to help hitters get a better idea where the ball is going to end up [aoc-2024-oldenburg-oos-setting-drill].
