# Mark Barnard

## Overview

Mark Barnard is an Australian volleyball coach best known for a seventeen-year run as head coach of the Oregon State women's program (2005–2022) and an ongoing affiliation with the Australian Volleyroos men's national team [aoc-2023-back-row-attacking-basics] [aoc-2023-barnard-single-double-triple] [aoc-2023-barnard-left-vs-left]. On The Art of Coaching Volleyball platform he has emerged as one of the most prolific clinic voices on hitter development — attack approach, arm-swing mechanics, shot selection, and the setter-hitter connection — with an adjacent body of drill design covering pressure passing, zone-aware serve-receive, and 6v6 competitive hitting formats [aoc-2023-barnard-armswing-technique] [aoc-2019-barnard-53535-passing-drill] [aoc-2023-barnard-left-vs-left].

## Core teaching principles

**The approach is where termination is built.** Barnard's default hitter prescription is a four-step, "fast-fast-faster" approach, preferred over three-step because it leaves more room to reach maximum speed at takeoff and more time to adjust [aoc-2023-barnard-aggressive-approach]. The non-negotiable across step counts is acceleration *through* contact: "keep accelerating through the approach, never lose momentum before contact" [aoc-2023-barnard-aggressive-approach]. For outside hitters, once the approach begins it doesn't stop until contact; set height dictates *when* to start, not *how* to swing [aoc-2025-barnard-go-big-mindset].

**Arm-swing: load, align, torque — never pike.** After the jump, both arms load behind the body parallel to the floor; the non-hitting shoulder sits closest to the net; the elbow starts back-and-open and rises naturally through extension [aoc-2023-barnard-armswing-technique]. Power is primarily body-rotation torque combined with arm action, and piking is explicitly discouraged because it lowers contact point and bleeds off torque [aoc-2023-barnard-armswing-technique]. A 2026 follow-up elevates the non-hitting arm to equal billing — it drives the jump, initiates rotation, serves as the setter's visual target, and helps accelerate the hitter into the ball [aoc-2026-barnard-left-arm-power-swing].

**One great shot beats a deep toolbox.** Barnard rejects the generalist-hitter premise for most non-elite players: "each hitter only needs to be great at one shot" — provided that shot forces the defense to adjust [aoc-2023-barnard-hitters-toolbox]. A reliable sharp-angle attacker forces a defensive shift; the shift opens a line shot [aoc-2023-barnard-hitters-toolbox]. The block itself is reframed as a scoring opportunity: tooling, wiping the outside hand, and ricocheting off high hands are trained as intentional scoring modes [aoc-2023-barnard-scoring-off-block].

**Back-row attack: setter leads the hitter.** Barnard's simplest teaching principle is that the setter *leads* the hitter — placing the set in front of the 10-foot line so the hitter is forced into an aggressive takeoff from behind the line and a landing in the front row [aoc-2023-back-row-attacking-basics]. The minimal introductory drill runs a Zone-6 file of hitters with a tosser off to the side; setter delivers in front of the line; attacker jumps from behind, hits, lands in the front row [aoc-2023-back-row-attacking-basics]. The principle anchors [[hitting-back-row-attack]] and is cited on [[age-lens-14u]] as the recommended entry point for exposing late-14U hitters to back-row attacking without set-tempo complexity.

**Setter-middle connection is a read, not a default.** Barnard's paired drills address a common failure: the "textbook" middle set delivered to the spot the hitter *isn't* standing. Setter-find-the-middle frees the middle to move anywhere along the net and forces the setter to scan in real time; middle-find-the-ball runs back-to-back reps — coach-tossed direct hit, then setter-delivered attack — to train the middle's visual hand-off between cue sources [aoc-2023-barnard-setter-find-middle] [aoc-2023-barnard-middle-find-ball].

**Competitive drills with ranking teeth.** Barnard's 6v6 hitting drills double as depth-chart sorters: head-to-head left-vs-left (and its middle-vs-middle and right-vs-right generalizations) forces a known set into a known block, so the hitter has to score against a defense that *knows* the set is coming [aoc-2023-barnard-left-vs-left]. The single-double-triple block progression uses kill/play/error scoring (+1/0/-1) to train finishing under escalating block density [aoc-2023-barnard-single-double-triple]. On pressure passing, 5-3-5-3-5 uses a 40-serve budget with alternating non-consecutive (5) and consecutive (3) perfect-pass thresholds — the streak requirement is the consistency lever [aoc-2019-barnard-53535-passing-drill]. Serve-receive-angles rotates servers through Zones 1/6/5 so passers learn angle, spin, and travel-time cues from every origin [aoc-2019-barnard-serve-receive-angles].

## Contributions to the game

**Back-row-attack teaching accessibility.** The "setter leads → takeoff behind the line → land in the front row" framing is one of the cleanest introductory treatments of back-row attacking available at the club-through-college level [aoc-2023-back-row-attacking-basics], durable enough that this wiki cites it at both the technique and age-lens-14u levels.

**AOC hitter-development canon.** Across 2023–2026, Barnard authored the densest single-coach cluster of hitter-development AOC pieces in this wiki's corpus — approach step-count tradeoffs, loaded-arm contact position, left-arm mechanics, toolbox economy, post-block resilience, scoring off the block [aoc-2023-barnard-aggressive-approach] [aoc-2023-barnard-armswing-technique] [aoc-2026-barnard-left-arm-power-swing] [aoc-2023-barnard-hitters-toolbox] [aoc-2025-barnard-go-big-mindset] [aoc-2023-barnard-scoring-off-block].

**Rotation-4 front-row-setter strategies.** The 2018 Portland clinic with Flick-Williams produced a clear public treatment of 5-1 rotation-4: slide-capable-middle-stacking near the setter to disguise the offense, a conditional "right-front can pass from left-back" rule, and a serving-team counter targeting the front-row non-designated-defender [aoc-2019-barnard-flick-williams-rot4-front-setter].

## Quotes & representative passages

On approach: the fast-fast-faster four-step approach is preferred because "great hitters terminate in all situations," and the approach is where termination is built [aoc-2023-barnard-aggressive-approach].

On hitter toolbox economy: each hitter need only be "great at one shot" — the defensive adjustment to stop that shot opens the next scoring window [aoc-2023-barnard-hitters-toolbox].

On outside-hitter mindset: "I'm going to get this point. I'm going to save this team." After being blocked, the anti-pattern is tipping the next ball — the prescription is "swing again, swing harder, swing higher" [aoc-2025-barnard-go-big-mindset].

On back-row attack, paraphrased: the setter must lead the hitter so the takeoff is behind the 10-foot line and the landing is in the front row [aoc-2023-back-row-attacking-basics].
