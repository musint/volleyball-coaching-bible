# Kirsten Bernthal Booth

## Overview

Kirsten Bernthal Booth is the head coach of Creighton University women's volleyball and a sustained Art of Coaching Volleyball (AOC) contributor whose public footprint in the ingested sample is concentrated on libero training (often co-clinicked with [[christy-johnson-lynch]]), OH out-of-system work, jump-counting player evaluation, and shot-work repetition design [aoc-2018-booth-jump-counting-evaluations] [aoc-2024-jl-booth-libero-training] [aoc-2026-booth-outside-out-of-system]. ## Core teaching principles

**Measurable-evaluation emphasis.** Booth's 2018 piece on jump-counting-for-evaluations treats practice-time load tracking as a player-evaluation tool, not just a conditioning tool — jump-count data feeds decisions about which players are ready for higher-load reps and which need to be protected [aoc-2018-booth-jump-counting-evaluations].

**Libero as a trainable, coachable skill group.** The Johnson-Lynch-Booth libero co-clinic material treats libero play as a distinct technical domain requiring specific platform, pursuit, and read teaching — not as a catch-all residual of "defensive player" [aoc-2024-jl-booth-libero-training] [aoc-2024-johnson-lynch-booth-libero-training].

**OH out-of-system skill work.** Booth's 2026 OH-OOS material frames out-of-system hitting as its own teachable skill set with distinct shot menus and approach footwork from in-system OH work [aoc-2026-booth-outside-out-of-system] [aoc-2026-booth-outside-shot-work].

## Contributions to the game

**Libero pedagogy co-authorship.** Booth's co-clinic contributions with Johnson-Lynch have produced AOC material that other head coaches use as a libero-training curriculum [aoc-2024-jl-booth-libero-training].
