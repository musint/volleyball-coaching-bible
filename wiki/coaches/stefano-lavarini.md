# Stefano Lavarini

## Overview

Stefano Lavarini (born 1979, Omegna, Italy) is the **first foreign head coach** of the South Korea women's national team (2019-2021) and the figure most associated with the modern Italian-school export into Korean and Polish women's volleyball [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature] [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure]. His career arc — Italian and Turkish club coaching → Korean NT 2019-2021 (Tokyo 2020 4th place) → Polish women's NT 2021-onward (Paris 2024 bronze, Korean cycle parallel) — places him at the center of the post-Velasco Italian-school women's coaching diaspora alongside [[giovanni-guidetti]] and [[cesar-hernandez-gonzalez]].

For this wiki, Lavarini is the **modern bridge figure between the [[italian-school]] methodology and the [[korean-school]] tactical context**: hired by KVA in summer 2019 explicitly as a foreign head coach to import Italian-school scouting, serving discipline, and psychological player empowerment into a Korean program that had been Korean-led continuously since [[park-man-bok]]'s 1973 World Cup bronze tenure [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature] [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature].

## Coaching career

**Italian club coaching (pre-2019).** Lavarini's pre-Korean tenure includes head-coaching positions at Italian Serie A women's clubs in the post-Velasco era [unsourced — specific club / date detail not in ingested English-language sources]. His Italian-school methodological lineage is implicit in the federation's 2019 hiring rationale and in his subsequent public framing of "Italian volleyball culture" [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature].

**Eczacıbaşı VitrA, Turkish women's SuperLig (2018-2020 documented overlap).** Lavarini coached [[kim-yeon-koung]] at Eczacıbaşı during the 2018-2020 cycle, the club tenure that established the player-coach relationship he would later carry into the Korean NT [kim-yeon-koung-2026-wikipedia]. The Turkish-club tenure is the structural anchor for his later "Italian-school methodology applied to a non-European roster" pattern.

**South Korea women's national team head coach (2019-2021).** Lavarini was hired in **summer 2019 as the first foreign head coach** of the Korean women's NT [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]. His tenure delivered an immediate result-arc:

- **2019 VNL: 15th place** (the inherited baseline) [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]
- **2019 World Cup: 6th place** [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]
- **Tokyo 2020 Olympics: 4th place**, including a **3-2 quarterfinal upset over 4th-ranked Turkey** [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]

Lavarini declined a contract renewal in October 2021, citing a preference for European-only work; KVA appointed his assistant **[[cesar-hernandez-gonzalez]]** as successor [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure].

**Poland women's national team head coach (2021-2024).** Following the Korean tenure, Lavarini took the Polish women's NT head-coach post for the Paris 2024 cycle [poland-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The tenure produced **three consecutive FIVB Volleyball Nations League bronze medals (2022, 2023, 2024)** plus a **Paris 2024 Olympic quarterfinal appearance** — the deepest Olympic finish for Poland women since the program's only Olympic medal at Mexico City 1968 (bronze) [poland-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The bronze cluster represents the most decorated multi-cycle stretch in Polish women's-NT history and is the second documented Italian-school-into-non-Italian-NT export of Lavarini's career, following the Korean tenure [korea-women-nt-2026-wikipedia][poland-women-nt-2026-wikipedia].

## Core teaching principles

The Korea-era source base (Korea Times, Volleyball World) preserves three doctrinal axes Lavarini imported into the Korean program [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature] [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]:

**1. Heavy video / opponent scouting.** Yang Hyo-jin: *"He spends so much time watching and studying videos (of opponents) to prepare. Before matches, he talks about how we should execute our game plan to a T."* [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature]. The methodology is the Italian-school-into-Korean-NT signature import — a high-detail per-rotation scouting discipline contrasting with the prior less-systematized Korean approach [unsourced].

**2. Aggressive serving as physical-disadvantage equalizer.** Lavarini: *"We are skilled, we are good technically...But for sure, to play versus such good physical teams like Turkey...we need to serve very strong. Serving is our first goal."* [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature]. The principle is the tactical primary against opponents with size advantages — applied during the Tokyo 2020 quarterfinal over Turkey and called from the bench via signed serving zones [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature].

**3. Psychological player empowerment.** Lavarini: *"If they believe in what they can do, they can go really far. Everything is in their hands."* [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature]. The team-puzzle metaphor and "create a good volleyball culture inside the team and infused it with the Korean tradition" framing — the cleanest articulation of the modern fusion identity of [[korean-school]] [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature].

**4. Methodology over technical overhaul.** Lavarini explicitly framed his Korean tenure as a **psychological / cultural blend** rather than a technical reset: *"In Europe, USA and South America, it's very physical volleyball with emphasis on power, height and speed. So we have to create strategies to face the opposition."* [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]. The framing is consistent with the [[italian-school]] tradition's tactical-prep emphasis over technique-rebuild posture.

## Contributions to the game

**First foreign head coach of Korean women's NT.** Lavarini's 2019 hire ended approximately five decades of Korean-only NT head coaching since [[park-man-bok]]'s 1973 World Cup bronze tenure [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature]. The institutional opening became the template for [[cesar-hernandez-gonzalez]]'s 2022-2024 tenure [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure] [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment].

**Tokyo 2020 4th place.** Korea's second Olympic semifinal of the 21st century after [[kim-hyung-sil]]'s 2012 London 4th place [korea-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The result-arc — 2019 VNL 15th to Olympic semifinalist in two years — is the strongest single trace of Italian-school-methodology productivity on a non-European roster [lavarini-2021-volleyballworld-feature].

**Italian-school export beyond Italy.** Lavarini's Korea-then-Poland career places him alongside [[giovanni-guidetti]] in the post-Velasco Italian-coaching diaspora [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure]. His Polish women's NT tenure documents the methodology as repeatable across federations.
