# Igor Kolaković

## Overview

Igor Kolaković (born 4 June 1965, Titograd / now Podgorica, SR Montenegro, SFR Yugoslavia) is the Montenegrin setter-turned-coach who delivered Serbia men's volleyball's most dominant European Championship title — the **2011 European Championship gold** in Austria/Czech Republic, won on a perfect **6-0 tournament record** [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. Kolaković is one of the few coaches to have led the Serbia men's national team across two separate appointments (2007-2014/2015 first stint, 2022-2024 second stint), bracketing the [[nikola-grbic]] (2014/2015-2019) and [[slobodan-kovac]] (2019-2021) head-coaching eras [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The first-stint résumé also includes a **2010 World Championship bronze** (Italy host) and back-to-back **2008-2009 World League silver medals**, plus European Championship bronzes in 2007 and 2013 — a continuous-medals-program output across an eight-year tenure [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia].

Beyond Serbia, Kolaković has coached the **Iran men's national team (2017-2020)** to the 2019 Asian Championship gold and 2017 World Grand Champions Cup bronze, plus club tenures across Slovenia, France, Poland, and Turkey [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. His career spans the post-Yugoslav coaching diaspora that fed Italian, Polish, and Iranian men's volleyball with European-trained head coaches across the 2010s.

## Coaching career

**Player career (1979-1997).** Kolaković played as a setter from 1979 to 1997 — 18 years across two clubs: **Budućnost Podgorica** (1979-1987, 1991-1993, 1994-1997, three separate stints) and **Partizan Beograd** (1987-1991, 1993-1994) [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. As a Partizan player he won Yugoslav championships in 1989-90 and 1990-91, plus a **CEV Challenge Cup silver in 1989-90** [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. The setter-position playing background is the methodological lens through which his subsequent head-coaching voice operates — a player-perspective tactical orientation he shares with [[nikola-grbic]] (also a setter) and contrasts with the OH-perspective tactical lens of [[slobodan-kovac]].

**Budućnost Podgorica head coach (1997-2009).** Immediately after retiring as a player, Kolaković took the Budućnost Podgorica head-coach role and held it for 12 years [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. The tenure produced multiple Montenegrin championships, including 2007 and 2008 — the most-recent-Montenegrin-championship period referenced in the Wikipedia biography [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. Specific aggregate-titles count across the 12-year tenure is not in the sources currently on file [unsourced]. The continuity of single-club head-coaching for over a decade gave Kolaković the same long-arc club-coach foundation that anchors many of the modern men's NT head coaches in Europe.

**Serbia men's NT head coach, first stint (2007-2014/2015).** Kolaković replaced Ljubomir Travica as Serbia men's head coach in 2007 [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. The eight-year first stint delivered the program's most-decorated medium-term result period:

- **2007 European Championship bronze** [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia].
- **2008-2009 World League silver medals** — back-to-back finals appearances [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia].
- **2010 World Championship bronze** (Italy host) [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia].
- **2011 European Championship gold (perfect 6-0 record)** — Serbia's most dominant European-title run; Kolaković's signature head-coaching result [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia].
- **2013 European Championship bronze** [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia].

The tenure ended in 2014 (per the kolakovic-2026-wikipedia bio) or 2015 (per the serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia program page) [unsourced — minor source disagreement on tenure end-date]. Specific cycle-end context (resignation, contract expiry, or federation decision) is not in the sources on file [unsourced].

**ACH Volley Slovenia (2010-2012).** Concurrent with the Serbia first-stint NT role, Kolaković held the ACH Volley head-coach role and delivered Slovenian championships and cups in 2011 and 2012 [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. The dual NT-and-club arrangement — a CEV-region pattern especially common across the 2010s — placed him at one of the top Slovenian men's clubs at the same time he was running Serbia's NT.

**AS Cannes (2013-2016).** French SuperLeague tenure; specific titles from this French-club tenure are not in the sources currently on file [unsourced].

**Iran men's NT head coach (2017-2020) — 2019 Asian Championship gold.** Kolaković took the Iran men's NT head-coach role in 2017, succeeding [[julio-velasco]]'s long Iran tenure. The three-year tenure delivered:

- **2019 Asian Championship gold** — top Asian regional title [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia].
- **2017 World Grand Champions Cup bronze** — a major-tournament medal in the FIVB-region competitive ladder [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia].

The Iran tenure overlapped Kovač's 2014-2015 Iran NT tenure earlier, completing a Serbian-coach pattern at the top of Iran's men's program across the 2010s [kovac-2026-wikipedia].

**Warta Zawiercie Poland (2020-2022).** PlusLiga head-coach tenure; specific results not in the sources currently on file [unsourced].

**Serbia men's NT, return (2022-2024).** Kolaković returned for a second Serbia men's NT head-coach tenure in 2022 [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia]. Specific results from the return tenure are not in the sources on file [unsourced].

**Halkbank Ankara (2024-2025) and ACH Volley return (2025-present).** Kolaković's most recent club roles. Specific results are not yet documented in the sources currently on file [unsourced].

The pattern is not unique to Kolaković but is characteristic of the Serbian/post-Yugoslav coaching lineage and the broader CEV-region men's NT coach selection logic [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [unsourced].

## Contributions to the game

**The 2011 European Championship gold (perfect 6-0).** Serbia's most dominant European Championship title — undefeated across the 2011 tournament [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The result placed Serbia at the top of the European-region men's competition standings entering the London 2012 Olympic cycle.

**The 2010 World Championship bronze.** Serbia men's first World Championship medal under standalone Serbia identity (post-2006 federation-rebrand), a foundational result for the Serbian-school men's-program identity [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia].

**The Iran 2019 Asian Championship gold.** Continuation of the post-Velasco Iran men's program development and the second-major Iranian-NT title under Serbian/post-Yugoslav coaching across the 2010s (after Kovač's 2014 Asian Games gold) [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [kovac-2026-wikipedia].

**Two-stint Serbia men's NT head-coaching career.** Kolaković is one of few men's NT head coaches in modern volleyball with two separate appointments at the same national team — a 2007-2014/2015 first stint and a 2022-2024 return [kolakovic-2026-wikipedia] [serbia-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The pattern speaks to federation-level confidence in the same head coach across multiple cycles.
