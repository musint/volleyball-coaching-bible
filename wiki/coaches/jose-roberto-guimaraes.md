# José Roberto Guimarães

## Overview

José Roberto Guimarães (b. 31 July 1954, Quintana, São Paulo) is one of the most decorated volleyball coaches in the Olympic era — the head coach who delivered **Brazil's first-ever men's Olympic gold** at **Barcelona 1992**, and the subsequent architect of the Brazil women's Olympic back-to-back at **Beijing 2008** and **London 2012**, giving him three Olympic golds as a head coach across both programs [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia]. Alongside [[bernardinho]], Guimarães is one of the two anchor program-builders of the modern [[brazilian-school]] — the speed-first, tactically creative, psychologically resilient tradition that has positioned Brazil as the most-titled men's national program in FIVB history and one of the strongest women's programs of the 2000s-2020s [brazil-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [brazil-women-nt-2026-wikipedia].

## Coaching career

**Playing career (1967-1988).** Guimarães played as a setter, including at the **1976 Montréal Olympics** on the Brazil men's national team, with a domestic club career spanning eight-plus Brazilian sides [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia].

**Brazil men's NT head coach (1992-1996).** Delivered the program's first Olympic gold medal at **Barcelona 1992** and the 1993 FIVB World League gold [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia]. The 1992 Barcelona campaign is the foundational moment in the men's [[brazilian-school]] lineage — the generation after [[bebeto-de-freitas]]'s 1984 Los Angeles silver and before [[bernardinho]]'s 2001-2017 men's dynasty.

**International and domestic club assignments (1996-2003).** Multiple club roles including at Scavolini Pesaro (Italy) and Brazilian SuperLiga sides; this is the stretch that reinforced Italian-Brazilian methodology exchange [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia] [unsourced].

**Brazil women's NT head coach (2003/2004-present).** Took over the Brazil women's program in 2003-2004 and has held the position through multiple Olympic cycles [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia]. Olympic medal record: **gold Beijing 2008, gold London 2012, silver Tokyo 2020, bronze Paris 2024** [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia] [fivb-2021-guimaraes-dal-zotto-paris-2024]. Four FIVB World Championship silvers (2006, 2010, 2022) and two bronzes (2014, 2024) — the notable program ceiling being that Brazil women have never won a World Championship gold under his tenure [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia].

**Concurrent club work.** Scavolini Pesaro (Italy) 2006-2009 and Fenerbahçe (Turkey) 2010-2012 — both women's assignments running concurrent with Brazil women's NT duties [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia].

## Contributions to the game

**Dual-program Olympic gold coaching.** Guimarães is one of a very small number of head coaches in volleyball history to win Olympic gold with both a men's and a women's national program representing the same country [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia]. That dual-program distinction places him alongside a handful of Olympic-era peers and is a structural anchor for how Brazilian coach-education frames cross-gender program design.

**1992 Barcelona as the men's-program pivot.** Brazil men had never won Olympic gold before Barcelona 1992; the win under Guimarães reset international expectations of the program and seeded the confidence platform on which Bernardinho's 2001-2017 men's dynasty was subsequently built [brazil-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia].

**Two-decade women's program stewardship.** Guimarães has held the Brazil women's NT head-coaching role across roughly twenty years — an unusual tenure length at the international level — giving the program the institutional continuity that produced back-to-back Olympic golds and three Olympic finals across five consecutive Summer Games [guimaraes-2026-wikipedia].
