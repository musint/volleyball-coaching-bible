# Stéphane Antiga

## Overview

Stéphane Antiga (born February 3, 1976, Suresnes, France) is the French outside hitter (France men's NT 1998–2010, 306 caps, captain) who delivered Poland's **2014 FIVB Men's World Championship gold on home soil — the program's first World title in 40 years** [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio] [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The September 21, 2014 final against Brazil — won 3–1 with Mariusz Wlazły as tournament MVP — was Antiga's first major tournament as head coach, less than 11 months after his October 24, 2013 appointment [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio] [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The 2014 Worlds gold reset the modern Polish men's-program ceiling and created the structural baseline on which [[vital-heynen]] (2018 Worlds gold) and [[nikola-grbic]] (2024 Paris silver) subsequently built [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [2018-fivb-mens-worlds-2026-wikipedia].

For this wiki, Antiga sits at the structural pivot of the modern [[polish-school]]: a French-national-team-captain-turned-head-coach who took the program from [[andrea-anastasi]]'s 2011–2013 medal-but-no-gold elevation to a full World Championship in his very first major. His subsequent decade in Polish-club coaching (Onico Warsaw, Developres Rzeszów, LUK Lublin) — including a 2024–25 PlusLiga title — keeps him inside the Polish system into the 2025–26 season [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. He is also a first-tier link to the [[french-school]] coaching tradition through his 12-year French national-team playing career.

## Coaching career

**French playing roots (1998–2010).** Antiga played outside hitter for the French men's national team for 12 seasons, accumulating **306 caps** as team captain [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. His national-team medals: 2002 World Championship bronze (Argentina), 2003 European Championship silver (Germany), 2006 World League silver (Moscow), 2009 European Championship silver (Turkey); 2004 Athens Olympics participant [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 2009 European Championship "Best Receiver" individual award [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio] is structurally important: it is a passing-skill credential that maps onto his later coaching emphasis (the 2014 Polish squad's serve-receive depth was a critical factor in the home-soil run).

**Polish-club playing era (2007–2014).** Antiga's bridge to Polish coaching is biographical: he played for **Skra Bełchatów** in two stints (2007–2011 and 2013–2014), winning **5 Polish championships** (2007–08, 2008–09, 2009–10, 2010–11, 2013–14), plus FIVB Club World Championship silvers in 2009 + 2010 [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 2007–2014 Polish-league playing tenure embedded Antiga in the PlusLiga ecosystem before any coaching role — preparation that made the October 2013 head-coach appointment less of a foreign-import gamble than it would otherwise have been.

**Poland men's NT (October 24, 2013 – December 31, 2016).** Appointed on a three-year contract with Philippe Blain as deputy [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. Antiga's tenure produced:

- **2014 FIVB Men's World Championship gold (September 21, 2014, Poland host)**: Poland def. Brazil 3–1 in the final; Mariusz Wlazły MVP; Karol Kłos best Middle Blocker — the program's second World title overall and first in 40 years (since 1974) [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio] [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia].
- 2015 FIVB World Cup: bronze (Japan) [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio].
- Coached the program through the 2016 Olympic Games qualification cycle [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio].

The 2014 World Championship gold defines Antiga's place in the wiki — both as the architect of Poland's first hosted-Worlds win since the USSR and Czechoslovakia, and as a coach who delivered tournament gold in his first major. **2014 Coach of the Year (co-recipient with Łukasz Kruczek)** plus **Polish Men's Volleyball Team named "Best Team of 2014"** are the contemporaneous public recognition [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. Antiga was awarded the **Knight's Cross of the Order of Merit of the Republic of Poland** by President Bronisław Komorowski in 2014 (for contributions to Polish sport), and the **Médaille d'or de la jeunesse, des sports et de l'engagement associatif** by France [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio].

**Canada men's NT (2017–2018).** Antiga led Canada to **2017 FIVB World League bronze (Curitiba)** during a brief one-year national-team appointment between Polish-NT and Polish-club roles [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The Canadian bronze is structurally similar to Vital Heynen's 2014 World Championship bronze with Germany — a coach demonstrating tournament-medal results from a non-major program, ratifying the methodology rather than the program.

**Polish-club era (2017–present).** Antiga has stayed inside the Polish-league ecosystem nearly continuously since 2017 [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio]:

- **Onico Warsaw (2017–2019)**: Polish Championship silver 2018–19.
- **Developres Rzeszów (2019–2024)**: Polish SuperCup 2021–22 + 2022–23; Polish Cup 2021–22; **Polish Championship gold 2024–25**.
- **Pallavolo Scandicci (2024)** — brief Italian-women's club role.
- **LUK Lublin (2025–present)**: Polish SuperCup + Cup 2025–26 winner; PlusLiga title in his first LUK Lublin season per polish-school cross-reference [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio].

## Contributions to the game

**2014 FIVB Men's World Championship gold (host nation).** The defining contribution: Poland's second World title overall, first in 40 years, on home soil under Antiga's first-major-tournament campaign [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio] [poland-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The win is the structural pivot of the modern Polish men's program — without it, Anastasi's 2011–2013 elevation likely reads as a multi-medal but no-gold cycle; with it, Polish men's volleyball joins Italy and Brazil as the top tier of the modern men's game.

**French-Polish coaching bridge.** Antiga is the most prominent coaching link between [[french-school]] and [[polish-school]] in the modern era — a French national-team captain who became Polish men's-NT head coach. The lineage continuity supports the broader pattern of European cross-national coaching mobility (Anastasi Italy-to-Poland, Heynen Belgium-to-Poland, Grbić Serbia-to-Poland).

**Sustained Polish-club coaching career.** Antiga's 2017–present run through three Polish-league clubs — including a 2024–25 PlusLiga title with Developres Rzeszów and ongoing 2025–26 with LUK Lublin — extends his coaching footprint across more than a decade inside the same league system, comparable in continuity to Andrea Anastasi's 2014–2022 Polish-league tenure [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio].

**Knight's Cross + state recognition.** Antiga is the recipient of state-level recognition from both Poland (Knight's Cross of the Order of Merit of the Republic of Poland, 2014) and France (Médaille d'or de la jeunesse, des sports et de l'engagement associatif, 2014) — a dual-state honors record matched by few volleyball coaches [antiga-2026-wikipedia-bio].
