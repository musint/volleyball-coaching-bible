# César Hernández González

## Overview

César Hernández González (born **October 24, 1977**, Madrid, Spain) is the Spanish women's-volleyball head coach who succeeded [[stefano-lavarini]] as **South Korea women's national team head coach for the 2022-2024 cycle** [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. Hernández was Lavarini's prior **assistant at the Korean NT (2019-2021)** and held a concurrent assistant role to **[[giovanni-guidetti]] at VakıfBank Istanbul** during his Korean NT tenure [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure] [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment].

For this wiki, Hernández is the **continuity figure** preserving Italian-school methodology in the Korean program after Lavarini's October 2021 departure — the second consecutive non-Korean head coach at the Korean women's NT and the institutional bridge between the Tokyo 2020 cycle and the post-[[kim-yeon-koung]] succession problem.

## Coaching career

The English-language source base for Hernández's pre-Korea career is thin; the Volleyball World appointment article documents the broad-strokes coaching record [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]:

**Spanish, French, Italian club coaching (2001-2019).** Hernández has coached women's volleyball professionally **since 2001**, with documented stops in Spanish, French, and Italian club volleyball [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. Specific clubs and tenure dates are not surfaced in the ingested English-language sources [unsourced].

**Lavarini assistant — South Korea NT (2019-2021).** Hernández served as assistant coach to [[stefano-lavarini]] across the 2019 hiring through Tokyo 2020 4th-place finish and Lavarini's October 2021 contract decline [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure] [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. The two-year assistant tenure positioned him as the institutional successor when Lavarini departed.

**VakıfBank Istanbul — Guidetti assistant (concurrent and ongoing).** Hernández holds an assistant-coach role at VakıfBank Istanbul under [[giovanni-guidetti]], a position he retained during his Korean NT tenure [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure] [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. The concurrent role places him inside two of the highest-tier modern European women's club programs simultaneously and inside the post-Velasco Italian-school women's coaching diaspora alongside Lavarini and Guidetti.

**South Korea women's national team head coach (2022-2024).** Announced **October 29, 2021** by KVA following Lavarini's contract decline [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. Three-year contract through the Paris 2024 cycle with explicit "head coach for the Olympic Games in Paris" mandate [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. **South Korea did not qualify for Paris 2024**, ending the mandate without Olympic representation [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. The women's program was subsequently relegated from the Volleyball Nations League after the 2025 cycle [korea-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The 2022-2024 tenure delivered no major-tournament medal and is the current period during which the post-Kim-Yeon-koung succession problem became institutionally documented.

## Core teaching principles

The available English-language sources document Hernández's institutional positioning rather than a doctrinal-coaching profile [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment] [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure]. The principles below are pattern inferences from his documented assistant-and-successor role; direct training-floor methodology in his voice is not preserved in the captured sources [unsourced].

**1. Italian-school methodological continuity.** Hernández's two-year assistant tenure under Lavarini followed by his head-coach succession is the structural mechanism by which Italian-school methodology persisted in the Korean program after Lavarini's departure [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment] [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure]. The pattern — head-coach replacement from inside the same coaching staff — preserves the heavy-scouting, aggressive-serving, psychological-empowerment doctrinal axes Lavarini established at Korea NT [lavarini-2021-koreatimes-feature]. Whether Hernández replicated each axis with the same priority weighting is not directly documented [unsourced].

**2. Concurrent club-and-NT roles as the modern European-coach default.** Hernández's simultaneous Guidetti-assistant / Korea-NT-HC posture is structurally similar to Lavarini's prior concurrent club-and-Korean-NT pattern and to the broader modern-European-club-coach norm of holding multiple roles inside a season [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment] [worldofvolley-2021-lavarini-departure].

## Contributions to the game

**Italian-school continuity into the Korean program.** Hernández's 2022-2024 tenure is the documented period during which the Italian-school methodological identity at Korea NT was preserved without Lavarini's personal presence [volleyballworld-2021-hernandez-korea-appointment]. The continuity is structurally significant for the [[korean-school]] historical chronology even though the result-side outcome (no Paris 2024 qualification) does not.

**Documentation of the post-Kim succession problem.** The 2022-2024 cycle without Kim Yeon-koung produced no major-tournament medal and ended in 2025 VNL relegation [korea-women-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The result-arc is the institutional documentation that the [[korean-school]] cannot, on currently available evidence, produce a replacement-level world-class outside hitter on a routine cycle [unsourced — comparative claim relative to other federations not directly substantiated].
