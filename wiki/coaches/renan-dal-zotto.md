# Renan Dal Zotto

## Overview

Renan Dal Zotto is a Brazilian former national-team setter and the long-serving head coach of the Brazil men's national volleyball team, succeeding [[bernardinho]] in 2017 and continuing through the Tokyo 2020 (delivered 2021) and Paris 2024 Olympic cycles [dal-zotto-2026-wikipedia-bio] [fivb-2021-guimaraes-dal-zotto-paris-2024]. As a player Dal Zotto was the setter on the Brazilian men's team that won Olympic silver in 1984 Los Angeles — the foundational medal for the modern Brazilian men's volleyball tradition — and served as setter across multiple World Championship and World League campaigns in the 1980s and 1990s [dal-zotto-2026-wikipedia-bio]. As head coach, he has continued the Brazilian men's program's sustained top-tier international presence, winning multiple FIVB Nations League titles and Olympic medals across his tenure [fivb-2021-guimaraes-dal-zotto-paris-2024]. ## Coaching career

**Playing career.** Setter on the Brazil men's national team — part of the 1984 Los Angeles Olympic silver medal generation — and multiple Pan American Games and World Championship squads [dal-zotto-2026-wikipedia-bio].

**Head coach, Brazil Men's National Team (2017-present).** Dal Zotto took over the Brazil men's program from [[bernardinho]] in 2017. His tenure has included multiple VNL titles, World Championship placements, and Olympic medals (silver Tokyo 2020) [dal-zotto-2026-wikipedia-bio] [fivb-2021-guimaraes-dal-zotto-paris-2024].
