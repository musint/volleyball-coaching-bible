# Tod Mattox

## Overview

Tod Mattox is the longtime head coach of The Bishop's School (La Jolla, CA) girls volleyball program and one of the highest-volume individual contributors to the Art of Coaching Volleyball (AOC) drill catalog. His public footprint in the ingested AOC sample is built around high-school-appropriate system design (hybrid 4-2 / 6-2, modern 4-2 revival), short-practice warm-up drills, setter development, and out-of-system / OOS dig-set-hit work [aoc-2015-mattox-kamikaze-scrimmage] [aoc-2019-mattox-hs-practice-plan] [aoc-2019-mattox-hs-designing-systems] [aoc-2022-mattox-hybrid-4-2-6-2-combo]. Mattox's bylined AOC pieces consistently pair a clear scoring scaffold with a tight rep-per-minute design — tools engineered for the high-school-practice constraint of limited court time and short attention cycles [aoc-2023-mattox-2-contact-challenge] [aoc-2023-mattox-dynamic-footwork-patterns]. ## Core teaching principles

**System design scaled to high-school reality.** Mattox's hybrid 4-2 / 6-2 piece argues that the modern 4-2 is re-emerging specifically because it scales to less-experienced players better than the conventional 5-1 while preserving more attack options than a simple front-row-setter system [aoc-2022-mattox-hybrid-4-2-6-2-combo]. The hybrid framework gives a high-school head coach a blueprint for matching the offense to the roster rather than forcing the roster into an offense.

**Short-practice discipline.** Mattox's warm-up library is built for a 90-minute practice window where every minute has to carry both teaching and conditioning load — dynamic-footwork warmups, serving warmups, defensive-intensity warmups that collapse traditional warm-up / skill / conditioning phases into single multi-purpose blocks [aoc-2022-mattox-dynamic-warmup-short-practice] [aoc-2023-mattox-defensive-intensity-warmup].

**Scoring scaffolds for practice reps.** Mattox's drill pieces almost always specify a concrete scoring frame — number of consecutive contacts required, losing-team penalty, run-multiplier — that turns ambiguous practice reps into counted, consequence-bearing competitions [aoc-2023-mattox-2-contact-challenge] [aoc-2015-mattox-kamikaze-scrimmage].

## Contributions to the game

**Hybrid 4-2 / 6-2 high-school offensive template.** Mattox's 2022 piece contributes a documented offensive-system blueprint specifically aimed at high-school programs that don't have the setter depth for a pure 5-1 but want more attack capability than a pure 4-2 [aoc-2022-mattox-hybrid-4-2-6-2-combo]. The template is cited on the [[offense-4-2]] and related systems pages.

**AOC drill-catalog volume.** Beyond any single contribution, Mattox's steady-state cadence of bylined AOC pieces functions as a working drill library that other head coaches use as practice-plan raw material. The ingested sample covers warm-ups, skill drills, small-group and team drills, setting, OOS, and season-arc content [aoc-2019-mattox-hs-practice-plan].
