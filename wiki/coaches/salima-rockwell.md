# Salima Rockwell

## Overview

Salima Rockwell is one of the most durable voices on The [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] platform and a direct descendant of the [[russ-rose]] Penn State coaching lineage — a former Penn State and U.S. National Team setter who has since transmitted Rose-era standards of reading, competitive practice, and setter IQ through more than a decade of AOC clinic content [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts] [aoc-2021-rockwell-cues-unexpected-plays]. The 2015 Vancouver Clinic serves as her flagship AOC appearance, producing the bulk of her video catalog on setting, pepper progressions, 5-on-5 defense, and the Virus variation — a body of material that AOC has continued to resurface under her byline for ten straight years [aoc-2015-rockwell-training-setters] [aoc-2015-rockwell-pepper-series] [aoc-2015-rockwell-five-on-five] [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals]. At the time of her 2018 AOC "3 setting do's and don'ts" article she was Penn State's associate head coach; by her 2023 blocking-fundamentals piece and her 2024-25 whiteboard-drill articles, AOC identifies her as representing the University of Notre Dame [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts] [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals] [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill].

For a coach working at the 14U, HS, or college level, Rockwell's corpus is the most accessible modern entry point into how Penn State trained its setters and how that same Rose-era coaching DNA translates into defense and team-IQ drills. Her distinguishing feature against peers on the AOC platform is a near-equal split between setter pedagogy on one side and defense-and-blocking pedagogy on the other — mirrored in the volume of her AOC catalog across both topic clusters [aoc-2015-rockwell-training-setters] [aoc-2016-rockwell-defending-angle] [aoc-2018-rockwell-acceleration-drill] [aoc-2021-rockwell-defensive-iq-setter].

## Coaching career

**Penn State player (early 1990s).** Rockwell played setter at Penn State under Russ Rose and went on to represent the U.S. National Team as a setter — AOC's 2018 "3 setting do's and don'ts" article describes her in both capacities: "a former setter (at Penn State and on the U.S. national team) who now works closely with setters as a coach" [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts]. She is consistently identified across AOC's catalog as a "former national team player" when her playing résumé is summarized [aoc-2021-rockwell-cues-unexpected-plays] [aoc-2021-rockwell-defensive-iq-setter].

**Penn State associate head coach (through 2018).** By April 2018, Rockwell is publicly credited as Penn State's associate head coach on AOC, making her one of the senior assistants under Rose in the final stretch of Rose's 42-season run at Penn State [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts]. Her 2016 AOC write-up of the Virus variation is headlined "from Penn State's Salima Rockwell," confirming her Penn State affiliation across the 2015-2018 AOC content window [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation].

**Notre Dame head coach (most recent tenure documented on AOC).** From at least 2023 onward, AOC identifies Rockwell as representing the University of Notre Dame [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals] [aoc-2024-rockwell-whiteboard-think] [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill]. Her 2024 collaboration with Mike Sealy on defending with a short blocker and her 2025 re-release of the whiteboard drill both list her under the Notre Dame byline [aoc-2024-sealy-rockwell-short-blocker] [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill]. Specifics of hire date, title (head coach vs. associate), and intermediate stops between Penn State and Notre Dame are not documented in the AOC corpus reviewed here [unsourced].

**AOC clinic-content lineage.** The 2015 Vancouver Clinic was Rockwell's flagship AOC appearance — AOC has continued to categorize many of her individual-defense, blocking, and team-drill clips under "2015 Vancouver Clinic" through subsequent re-releases [aoc-2018-rockwell-acceleration-drill] [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals]. The breadth of that single clinic — setting technique, setting drills, pepper progressions, cross-court pepper, 5-on-5, the Virus variation, and the acceleration drill — is unusual on the AOC platform for a single coach, and it is a meaningful part of why Rockwell's voice has remained in the AOC content rotation across a decade [aoc-2015-rockwell-training-setters] [aoc-2015-rockwell-train-setter] [aoc-2015-rockwell-pepper-series] [aoc-2015-rockwell-cross-court-pepper] [aoc-2015-rockwell-five-on-five] [aoc-2015-rockwell-virus-variation-vancouver] [aoc-2018-rockwell-acceleration-drill].

## Core teaching principles

**1. Train setters by coupling technique to decision-making in the same session.** Rockwell's setter pedagogy refuses to silo "setting technique" and "setter drills" into separate blocks — her 2015 Vancouver setter work is packaged as a unified progression from technique cues into decision drills, and AOC's video catalog file markers reflect that design [aoc-2015-rockwell-training-setters] [aoc-2015-rockwell-train-setter]. The "Go Against the Flow" drill operationalizes this — two pin hitters plus two setters on the net against a single middle blocker, with the setter required to redirect every set away from the middle's sneak direction — so that physical redirection is trained against a live opposing cue rather than in isolation [aoc-2022-rockwell-setter-think-drill].

**2. Setter-specific fundamentals — hands up early, jump timing, no back-pedaling.** Rockwell's three named juniors-level setter anti-patterns are (1) hands up late before contacting the ball, (2) jumping late on jump sets, and (3) back-pedaling to set balls passed behind — with Penn State setters demonstrating the fixes [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts]. The implicit teaching standard — decelerate the ball cleanly, pre-set jump timing, turn-and-redirect on balls behind the body — is transferable across every level from 14U through NCAA.

**3. Pepper as structured warmup, not free play.** Rockwell's 2015 Vancouver pepper series is explicit that pepper progressions must scale from basic to dynamic without losing the control-first foundation, and that pepper belongs inside structured warmup rather than as "play around" time [aoc-2015-rockwell-pepper-series]. Her cross-court pepper variant constrains ball trajectory to cross-court so hitters and diggers drill game-realistic angles rather than the convenient straight-line defaults [aoc-2015-rockwell-cross-court-pepper].

**4. Defense is read-first.** Rockwell teaches defensive IQ through specific read cues rather than generic "see the ball." Four setter-read cues: (a) pass location reveals setter options — off-net pulls the middle off the table, tight passes open the quick or dump; (b) setter body language, chest-to-net signals dump threat while a backpedal signals a set-behind; (c) a setter running toward the ball is unlikely to back-set to the pin behind her; (d) coaches should vary toss location and tempo in drills so defenders train reads rather than spot-feeds [aoc-2021-rockwell-defensive-iq-setter]. Three additional cues for emergency plays: middles should anticipate the overpass and practice overpass-conversion; defenders should keep hitters in peripheral vision while the pass is flighting; and ball-speed-off-platform on a shank is a leading indicator of the setter's options [aoc-2021-rockwell-cues-unexpected-plays]. Blocking sits inside the same framework — Rockwell's blocking-fundamentals clinic treats eye sequence as the central key for effective blocking at the net [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals].

**5. Accelerate through the ball.** In Rockwell's acceleration drill, three coaches feed quick balls and the defender must move forward through contact rather than catch the ball flat-footed [aoc-2018-rockwell-acceleration-drill]. The multi-feeder format pushes rep density and forces faster recovery between touches — the drill's embedded coaching point is that forward momentum is a trainable defensive habit.

**6. 1-on-1 blocking: hold the outside angle.** In a head-up hitter-blocker matchup, the hitter's primary option is cross-court, so the blocker's job is to hold the outside without cheating center — cheating center opens the line [aoc-2016-rockwell-defending-angle]. Rockwell enforces this with a 5-on-5 drill where hitters must swing (no tips) and winner stays, so block-and-dig decisions happen under real hitter pressure rather than soft contact [aoc-2016-rockwell-defending-angle].

**7. Compete in practice — especially if you coach female athletes.** Rockwell's 2018 AOC clinic lecture on "Creating a competitive culture for female athletes" frames competitive disposition as a trainable construct, not an assumed byproduct of talent or work ethic — filed by AOC under the Competitiveness and Team Building tags [aoc-2018-rockwell-competitive-culture]. The specific prescriptions inside that paywalled lecture are not captured in the reviewed AOC corpus [unsourced], but the thematic orientation — competitive culture as deliberate construction — aligns with Rose's Penn State program-as-standards model.

**8. Constrained-contact drills force communication under pressure.** Rockwell's Virus variation is a 6-on-6 drill where the coach shouts "1," "2," or "3" when tossing, indicating how many contacts remain before the ball must cross; "three" demands an immediate over-the-net play [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation]. Coaching emphasis rides on three communication cues: who sets if the setter takes the first contact, which hitters are available, and that a third contact must be a committed swing rather than a soft send [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation] [aoc-2015-rockwell-virus-variation-vancouver]. The scoring rotates sides after two points to prevent fatigue from favoring a single side [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation].

**9. Whiteboard constraints force setter IQ and flip into defensive IQ.** In Rockwell's whiteboard drill, the coach writes four to five specific sets on a whiteboard (outside, middle, right-side, back-row) and each team must score on every listed set — first to check every box wins [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill] [aoc-2024-rockwell-whiteboard-think]. Setters must track which sets have already scored to call ones that haven't; as the drill advances, it morphs into defensive IQ, because the opposing team anticipates which sets still need to be checked off [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill]. The mechanism is compact: a lightweight cognitive constraint changes offensive variety and blocker anticipation at once, without altering scoring rules.

**10. Defending a short blocker — shrink the window, trust the cover.** Together with Mike Sealy, Rockwell teaches that the right move with a short pin blocker is to push her all the way to the pin rather than inside it; if the middle releases early and closes tight, the line shot is tiny and the cross-court becomes a large, coverable area, so the line digger moves in a step because hitters rarely execute the down-the-line [aoc-2024-sealy-rockwell-short-blocker]. Rockwell adds that hitters often over-correct when trying to exploit a small blocker — hitting long or wide — "so it may not be that big of a disadvantage if you're playing it properly" [aoc-2024-sealy-rockwell-short-blocker].

## Contributions to the game

**Russ Rose lineage transmission to AOC.** Rockwell is one of the most visible conduits through which Rose-era Penn State coaching culture — reading, competitive practice, standards held regardless of scoreboard — has reached coaches outside Happy Valley via the AOC platform [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts] [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation] [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill]. The 2015 Vancouver Clinic functioned as the pipeline event: AOC has continuously re-released Rockwell's Vancouver clips under new packaging for a decade, keeping her methodology in the content rotation long after she left Penn State [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals] [aoc-2018-rockwell-acceleration-drill].

**Defense-and-blocking pedagogy distinct from most setter-focused voices.** Unlike peers on AOC who specialize narrowly in either offense or defense, Rockwell is one of the few senior voices on the platform whose AOC catalog is roughly evenly split between setter pedagogy (training-setters, train-setter, 3 do's-and-don'ts, Go Against the Flow) and defense-and-blocking pedagogy (acceleration drill, defending the angle, blocking fundamentals, setter-reads, emergency cues, short-blocker defense) [aoc-2022-rockwell-setter-think-drill] [aoc-2018-rockwell-3-setting-dos-donts] [aoc-2018-rockwell-acceleration-drill] [aoc-2016-rockwell-defending-angle] [aoc-2023-rockwell-blocking-fundamentals] [aoc-2024-sealy-rockwell-short-blocker]. This breadth mirrors the Penn State expectation that a setter must also be a competent back-row defender and a blocking-zone communicator.

**Constrained-contact drill design.** Rockwell's Virus variation and whiteboard drill are two of the more widely circulated constrained-contact team drills in AOC's catalog — both impose a cognitive constraint on team offense (contact count in one case, required-set checklist in the other) and both flip into defensive-IQ training as the drill evolves [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation] [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill]. The broader pattern — using a lightweight constraint to change what players pay attention to without changing scoring rules — is a recognizable Rockwell drill-design signature across her AOC catalog.

## Quotes & representative passages

On the whiteboard drill: "I love this drill because it keeps players thinking. As you get further along in the drill, it becomes a defensive drill" [aoc-2025-rockwell-whiteboard-drill].

On defending a short blocker, explaining why the disadvantage is often overstated: "It may not be that big of a disadvantage if you're playing it properly. (The small blocker) is hanging out on the pin, giving (the hitter) a visual ... but there's not a ton of space to go behind her" [aoc-2024-sealy-rockwell-short-blocker].

On emergency-play reading: "Always see your hitters in your peripheral vision while the pass is reaching the setter and adjust accordingly. Always be prepared to shift directions with the hands or the lower body" [aoc-2021-rockwell-cues-unexpected-plays].

On setter reading by pass location: "If she's pulled off the net, she's given much fewer options for offense — and likely won't set the middle. If she's shoved towards the net with the pass, she'll also have limited options and is more likely to set a quick set or dump" [aoc-2021-rockwell-defensive-iq-setter].

On the three communication priorities in the Virus variation: "Who sets if the setter takes the first contact. Which hitters are available. When it's a third contact, swing" [aoc-2016-rockwell-virus-variation].
