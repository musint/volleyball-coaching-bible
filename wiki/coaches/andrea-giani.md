# Andrea Giani

## Overview

Andrea Giani is the Italian volleyball player turned coach who, after a five-Olympic, three-world-title playing career as a core member of [[julio-velasco]]'s "Generazione di Fenomeni," won Olympic gold for the first time as head coach of the France men's national team at Paris 2024 — France's second consecutive Olympic title, secured with a 3-0 final over Poland (25-19, 25-20, 25-23) on 10 August 2024 [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio] [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia] [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold]. As a player Giani earned 474 caps for Italy across 1988-2005 — a national-team record at the time of his retirement — collecting three FIVB World Championships (1990, 1994, 1998), four European Championships (1993, 1995, 1999, 2003), seven World League titles, the 1995 World Cup, and three Olympic medals (silver 1996 and 2004, bronze 2000) [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio] [cev-2022-giani-france-appointment]. He was inducted into the International Volleyball Hall of Fame in 2008 [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. As coach he has led the Italian club Modena Volley (twice), Calzedonia Verona, M. Roma Volley, Power Volley Milano, and currently ZAKSA Kędzierzyn-Koźle in Poland (2024-), in parallel with the Slovenian (2015-2016), German (2017-2021), and French (2022-present) men's national teams [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The Paris 2024 gold was his most-cited public moment; Giani's post-final reaction was, "I'm very happy, it was my dream. I've been waiting for this medal for 35 years" [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold].

Giani sits at the intersection of two coaching traditions documented in this wiki — the [[italian-school]] he was formed inside as a player under [[julio-velasco]], and the [[french-school]] now in its second back-to-back-Olympic-gold cycle that he inherited from [[laurent-tillie]] (via Bernardinho's brief 2021-2022 stint) [france-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment]. Together with [[andrea-anastasi]] (Italy and Poland) and Velasco (Italy women's resurgence under Italian methodology), Giani is the third major case in this wiki of a Generazione di Fenomeni-era figure exporting Italian-school coaching to a non-Italian top federation in the 2020s.

## Coaching career

### Playing career as the foundation

Giani was born 22 April 1970 in Naples, the son of 1964 Tokyo Olympic rower Dario Giani [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. He started volleyball at age 14 with Pallavolo Sabaudia (1982-1985), then moved to Maxicono Parma (1985-1996) where he won three Italian Championships (1989-90, 1991-92, 1992-93) and multiple CEV Cups, and Modena Volley (1996-2007) where he added another Italian Championship (2001-02) and two CEV European Champions Cups (1996-97, 1997-98) [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. His 1988 Italian national-team debut against Finland began the 17-season international career that the CEV summarized as "five-time Olympian, three-time Olympic medallist, three-time world champion (1990, 1994, 1998), four-time European champion (1993, 1995, 1999, 2003)…seven World League victories" [cev-2022-giani-france-appointment]. He won 1994 World League MVP, 1995 World Cup MVP, and 1998 World League Best Blocker [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. His position polyvalence — middle blocker by training, transitioning to power attacker, passer, and at points opposite spiker — became part of his coaching vocabulary later [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 2008 induction into the International Volleyball Hall of Fame in Holyoke, Massachusetts confirmed him as one of the defining international players of his generation [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio].

The "Generazione di Fenomeni" affiliation matters here: Giani played the entirety of his prime years inside Velasco's 1989-1996 program (and continued under successive Italian head coaches through 2005), absorbing the Italian-school commitments to psychological leadership, team unity over specialization (the "square"), meticulous match preparation, and tempo-pressure offense from the inside [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. This is the formation that travelled with him into coaching.

### Club coaching, 2007-present

Giani's coaching career began at Modena Volley in 2007-2008, immediately winning the CEV Challenge Cup 2007-08 [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. He then coached M. Roma Volley (2008-2012, with the Serie A2 Championship in 2009-10), Calzedonia Verona (2013-2016, CEV Challenge Cup winners 2015-16), Power Volley Milano (2017-2019), Modena Volley again (2019-2023, CEV Cup winners 2022-23), and ZAKSA Kędzierzyn-Koźle in the Polish PlusLiga from 2024 onward [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The 15-year club arc gave him the longest continuous Italian Serie A coaching tenure of any Generazione-era player and a working knowledge of how Polish, Italian, and (through Power Volley Milano's CEV runs) German club opposition was prepared and structured [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio].

### National-team coaching

**Slovenia men's NT (May 2015 – 2016):** Giani's first national-team head-coaching role, taken on top of his Calzedonia Verona club tenure [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. Slovenia won the 2015 European Volleyball League and reached the **final of the 2015 European Championship** — losing to France, the country's first medal at any major tournament [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The Slovenian silver is significant in the broader story: Giani's losing-finalist team handed Tillie's France its 2015 European title, the result that began legitimizing Tillie's tenure publicly. Six years later Giani would inherit the program he had once lost to.

**Germany men's NT (January 2017 – 2021):** Replacing the outgoing Vital Heynen (who moved to Poland), Giani won **silver at the 2017 European Championship** in Poland [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The five-year stint produced no further medals but kept Germany competitive at the European level during a period of tactical reshaping in men's volleyball. The Germany experience also extended Giani's working knowledge of mid-tier European pro players, the demographic France draws on through CEV club leagues.

**France men's NT (March 2022 – present):** Appointed by the FFV in March 2022, replacing the brief one-year tenure of Brazilian coach Bernardo Rezende [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment] [france-men-nt-2026-wikipedia]. The mandate ran through Paris 2024 and has since extended forward [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment]. Giani's stated goals at appointment were direct: "It gives me real satisfaction to be the new coach of this world-class team, the reigning Olympic champions, who have the potential to win all the competitions in which they participate. … My objective will be to prepare them well physically and technically, and the goal will always be to win" [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment]. The CEV's parallel rendering captures the same substance with the line, "I want to prepare them well physically and technically, because the goal will always be to win!" [cev-2022-giani-france-appointment]. The titles followed: **2022 FIVB Volleyball Nations League gold** in Bologna; **2024 FIVB Volleyball Nations League gold** in Łódź; and the **2024 Paris Olympic gold** [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio] [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia].

The Paris 2024 run is the headline. France finished Pool A at 2-1 (wins over Serbia 3-2 and Canada 3-0; loss to Slovenia 2-3) [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia]. The knockout path then went 3-2 reverse-sweep over Germany in the quarterfinal, 3-0 over reigning World Champion Italy in the semifinal, and 3-0 over Poland in the gold-medal match [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia] [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold]. France dropped only one set across the three knockout matches — the second set of the German quarterfinal — and emerged as the third nation to defend Olympic men's volleyball gold and the first to do so in 36 years [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia]. In the final, Jean Patry led scoring with 17 points, Trévor Clévenot added 11, Earvin N'Gapeth contributed 8, and Quentin Jouffroy delivered 4 aces from the bench [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold]. The defining coach quote of the night was Giani's: "I'm very happy, it was my dream. I've been waiting for this medal for 35 years" [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold] — a reference to the start of his senior international career under Velasco in 1989, after which three Olympic medals had never produced gold.

## Core teaching principles

**The Italian-school formation as default frame.** Giani was a Generazione di Fenomeni player under [[julio-velasco]], earning three world titles in eight years inside the program that became the [[italian-school]] anchor [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. The Italian-school commitments — psychological leadership, the "square" of team unity over specialization, meticulous match preparation, aggressive service plus tempo-combination offense, and brotherhood-style internal accountability — are the working assumptions Giani carries into every coaching posting. He has not articulated those commitments in publicly circulated long-form interviews to the same degree as Velasco himself, but his program-management language at the France appointment ("prepare them well physically and technically … the goal will always be to win") and his selection of N'Gapeth, Clévenot, Patry, and Toniutti as the program's identity anchors are consistent with the Italian-school playbook applied to French-school personnel [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment] [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold]. The full theoretical exposition of his methodology is widely-discussed in Italian-language coaching media (CEV technical post-tournament reports, Italian federation interviews) but has not been ingested in detail in this wiki [unsourced].

**Player-pool-aware program management.** Giani's most concrete public articulation is structural rather than tactical: "There is a large pool of very high-level players in France, who play in big clubs, and this will allow me to manage the team in a timely manner depending on the competitions" [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment]. The principle is that the head-coach's job in a federation with strong club-level player development is rotation management and selection across competitions rather than universal-system enforcement. France's roster spread — Toniutti and Le Goff in Pro Ligue, N'Gapeth and Patry in Italian Serie A, Clévenot in Polish PlusLiga, Chinenyeze in CEV-tier Italian and Polish clubs across his career — is not a problem to standardize but a resource to manage [france-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment]. This is methodological continuity with Tillie, not a departure: "France's large pool of very high-level players … who play in big clubs" was the structural condition both coaches inherited [tillie-2026-wikipedia-bio].

**Continuity-as-strategy on inherited golden-era rosters.** Giani's tactical posture at France was explicit: he inherited "the reigning Olympic champions" and the goal was to "win all the competitions in which they participate" [volleyballworld-2022-giani-france-appointment]. The emphasis is continuity rather than reinvention. The Tokyo-2020 captain (Toniutti), MVP (N'Gapeth), and offensive identity (multidirectional attack with high-frequency outside-hitter back-row) all carried into Paris 2024 [france-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia]. The strategic decision to defend rather than rebuild is itself a documented coaching choice — and one that the result validated. The further claim that Giani's tactical fingerprints on the Paris 2024 squad center on tighter blocking organization and more aggressive jump-serving (the Italian-school and the German-tradition twin influences) is plausible editorial synthesis but is not directly substantiated in the English-language sources currently ingested here [unsourced].

**Resilience under in-tournament adversity.** Trévor Clévenot's post-final observation — that the quarterfinal reverse-sweep over Germany "pushed the team to play more aggressively" in the semifinal and final — is the in-tournament tactical lesson the squad explicitly acknowledged [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold]. Whether this was Giani's intentional intervention between matches or an emergent player-led adjustment is not documented, but the team's improvement curve from the Pool A loss to Slovenia, through the QF reverse-sweep, to the SF/F sweeps is in the public match record [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia]. France improved across the tournament rather than peaking and degrading.

**The 35-year horizon.** Giani's "I've been waiting for this medal for 35 years" quote is more than a personal celebration — it is a coaching-career framing [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold]. The horizon starts at his 1988 Italy debut against Finland and ends at the Paris 2024 final. Three Olympic medals as a player (silver, bronze, silver), three world titles, four European titles, seven World League titles — and yet no Olympic gold until coaching France against his own native federation's primary regional rivals. The implied principle is that coaching legitimacy and competitive longevity are accumulated through decades of unbroken work inside elite competition, not through single-tournament breakthroughs [volleyballworld-2024-france-paris-gold].

## Contributions to the game

**The first Italian-school coach to win Olympic gold abroad with a non-Italian men's program.** Velasco won World Championships with Italy and women's Olympic gold with Italy in 2024; Anastasi reached World Championship gold with Poland in 2014; Giani is the first Generazione di Fenomeni-era figure to win **Olympic men's gold** as a head coach with a non-Italian program. The 2024 Paris result is therefore a methodological export milestone for the Italian school comparable to the 2014 Polish World Championship under Antiga (a French-coached Polish program) but at a higher tournament tier [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia].

**France's back-to-back Olympic golds.** Tillie won the first; Giani won the second. The continuity of the program across the head-coach handoff — same captain, same MVP, same offensive identity, same player-pool-management philosophy — is the structural achievement [france-men-nt-2026-wikipedia] [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia]. France joined the Soviet Union (1964-1968 and 1976-1980 with boycotts) and the United States (1984-1988) as the only nations ever to win consecutive Olympic men's volleyball golds [paris-2024-mens-volleyball-2026-wikipedia].

**The Generazione-to-modern-coaching bridge as a propagation channel.** Giani's coaching career validates the Generazione di Fenomeni roster as a generational source of head coaches — a parallel to the Yugoslav-era 1990s/2000s women's basketball pipeline or the Brazilian beach-volleyball-to-coaching pipeline [unsourced]. Bernardi (briefly), Lucchetta, Zorzi, and several Generazione players have moved into media, federation, or administrative roles, but Giani is the figure who has fully made the player-to-international-head-coach transition with podium results.

**Recognitions.** International Volleyball Hall of Fame inductee 2008; Knight of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic (2000); Officer of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic (2004) [giani-2026-wikipedia-bio]. As coach, his first Olympic gold (Paris 2024) is the headline achievement; as a player his three world titles and five Olympic appearances are the program's reference point.
