# John Cook

## Overview
John Cook is the recently retired head coach of the Nebraska Cornhuskers women's volleyball program, where he served 25 seasons from 2000 through the 2024 season [cook-wikipedia-bio]. He won **four NCAA national championships** at Nebraska — 2000, 2006, 2015, and 2017 — and reached the championship-final match four additional times as runner-up (2005, 2018, 2021, 2023) [cook-wikipedia-bio]. His career head-coaching record is 883–176 (.834), with his Nebraska tenure alone going 722–103 (.875) [cook-wikipedia-bio]. He is a three-time AVCA National Coach of the Year (2000, 2005, 2023), a 2017 AVCA Hall of Fame inductee, and the 2008 USA Volleyball All-Time Great Coach Award winner [cook-wikipedia-bio]. In August 2023 he organized "Volleyball Day in Nebraska" at Memorial Stadium, which drew **92,003 spectators** — the world attendance record for a women's sports event [cook-wikipedia-bio][aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. He retired in January 2025 and helped select Dani Busboom Kelly — a Nebraska native, former player, and former Cook assistant — as his successor [cook-wikipedia-bio].

## Coaching career

### Early years (1981–1991)
Cook started coaching at Francis Parker School in San Diego in 1981 and went 162–18 across six seasons there, winning two state titles and producing a 90-match winning streak [cook-wikipedia-bio]. He was an assistant at UC San Diego (D-III) in 1983–84, including the 1984 NCAA Division III national championship team [cook-wikipedia-bio]. From 1989–91 he served as a volleyball assistant at Nebraska under Terry Pettit — the foundation experience that returned him to Lincoln nine years later as head coach [cook-wikipedia-bio]. Cook had wanted to be a football coach when he started teaching, and he describes watching Tom Osborne's Nebraska football practices during those assistant years as the formative event for his fundamentals-first training methodology [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### Wisconsin (1992–1998)
Cook served as Wisconsin's head coach for seven seasons and went 161–73 (.688) [cook-wikipedia-bio]. The 1995 team won the National Invitational Volleyball Championship; the 1997 team went 30–3 and shared the Big Ten title, with Cook named Big Ten Co-Coach of the Year [cook-wikipedia-bio]. He produced four All-Americans, nine AVCA All-District winners, and 11 All-Big Ten honorees during his Wisconsin tenure [cook-wikipedia-bio]. The 1996 Russia tour during this period taught Cook the "fundamentals erode without daily work" principle that became a permanent training axiom: "By the end of the tour, I noticed that our fundamentals really went downhill... fundamentals have to be worked on every day. If you don't, they're going to go away" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### Nebraska (2000–2024)

#### Four NCAA championships
- **2000:** 34–0 perfect season; Cook's first year at Nebraska as head coach; AVCA National Coach of the Year [cook-wikipedia-bio].
- **2006:** 33–1; Sarah Pavan AVCA National Player of the Year [cook-wikipedia-bio].
- **2015:** 32–4 [cook-wikipedia-bio].
- **2017:** 32–4 [cook-wikipedia-bio].

#### Four NCAA runner-up finishes
2005 (33–2; AVCA NCOY), 2018 (29–7), 2021 (26–8), 2023 (33–2; AVCA NCOY) [cook-wikipedia-bio]. Cook attributed the 2023 championship-match loss to Texas (3–0) directly to serving: "Serving completely took us out of the Texas match" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

#### Conference dominance across two leagues
Cook's Nebraska teams won 9 Big 12 titles (2000–02, 2004–08, 2010) before Nebraska moved to the Big Ten in 2011 [cook-wikipedia-bio]. He then added 5 more Big Ten titles at Nebraska (2011, 2016, 2017, 2023, 2024) plus his earlier 1997 Wisconsin Big Ten share — six total Big Ten titles across both head-coaching stops [cook-wikipedia-bio].

#### Career totals
Across all stops: 883–176 (.834), 8 NCAA championship-final appearances, 12 NCAA semifinal appearances, 23 top-10 finishes, 66 All-Americans coached, 800th win in 2022 [cook-wikipedia-bio].

#### National recognition
Three AVCA National Coach of the Year awards (2000, 2005, 2023); six AVCA Region COY (1997, 2000, 2005, 2008, 2016, 2023); USA Volleyball All-Time Great Coach Award (2008); AVCA Hall of Fame (2017) [cook-wikipedia-bio].

### Volleyball Day in Nebraska — August 30, 2023
Cook organized a regular-season match at Memorial Stadium in Lincoln to reclaim the attendance record Wisconsin had set at Milwaukee's Fiserv Forum [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The football field's nine-inch crown required hundreds of thousands of dollars of stage construction to create a flat playing surface; the game took six days of stadium prep [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The match sold out in a single day at 92,003 attendees — the world record for a women's sporting event of any kind [cook-wikipedia-bio][aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Cook frames the match as a women's-sports inflection point: "If you look at women's sports this year — softball, gymnastics, basketball, volleyball — there's a huge uptick in ratings. I think the stadium match had a big impact in getting people to think that women's sports are pretty cool" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### Retirement and succession
Cook retired in January 2025 after 25 Nebraska seasons and assisted in selecting his successor — Dani Busboom Kelly, a Nebraska native who had played for and assisted under him [cook-wikipedia-bio]. The 2024 final season ended at 33–3 with an NCAA semifinal appearance [cook-wikipedia-bio].

## Core teaching principles

### 1. Fundamentals every day, and fundamentals first
Cook's training methodology is explicitly old-school in framing: heavy on reps and drills, skeptical of pure game-teaches-the-game [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The foundation came from watching Tom Osborne's Nebraska football practices in the late 1980s: "Football is all about fundamentals. It's about breaking things down. They don't scrimmage for three hours. So that was my foundation as a coach, and I really believe in that" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Cook will rebuild a college player's basic mechanics — foot placement, platform construction — when she arrives with poor habits: "We're going to reinvent your game, and we're going to start with: this is where you put your right foot, this is where you put your left foot, this is where you put your platform" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### 2. Serving is the most important drill in practice — every day
"I tell our players every day that the most important drill we will be doing is serving. We try to do several different serving bursts during practice, so we're not just doing it once and leaving it" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Cook attributes the 2023 NCAA final loss to Texas directly to serving and frames the modern women's game as catching up to the men's-game pattern of all-out aggressive serving: "In the men's game, it's been that way forever. They're just bombing as hard as they can because they know that if all they do is get it in, the other team will probably side out at 90 percent. So the men have had that mindset, and now I think it has transferred to the women's game" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### 3. Beach as cross-training for indoor athletes (January–March)
Nebraska indoor players play 2-on-2 beach from January through end of March every year [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The rationale is that 2-on-2 forces every player to do everything: "What's great about 2-on-2 beach is that the middle blockers get to do everything... they're all wannabe setters, and they get to set. The liberos get to hit. When do they ever get to hit indoors?" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Cook cites Karch Kiraly (gold in indoor and beach) and observed Brazilian club practices where every youth player must rotate through every position [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The principle is variety as a developmental input, not a recreational add-on.

### 4. Every player sets every day
"At some point, everybody is going to have to set a ball in a match. How many times have you seen a ball go to a non-setter and they just gaff it because they're not prepared? I've seen matches determined by some middle trying to set a ball at match point. We train everybody to set every day because at some point you're going to have to set a ball" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Cook explicitly recommends this for lower levels of the game as well [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### 5. Multi-sport HS athletes outperform single-sport ones
"The best players we've had at Nebraska have been multi-sport athletes in high school. So let them play other sports. If you run 800-meter track or high jump or play basketball or tennis, you're conditioning your body in other ways and helping your body stay balanced" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The lateral imbalance from volleyball — 100 swings one arm, 0 the other — is the specific failure mode Cook flags [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Coaches at the club level are the gatekeepers here: the principle implies club coaches should not pressure athletes into year-round single-sport specialization [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### 6. There is a right way to teach the arm swing — and a high elbow is wrong
Cook agrees with assistant Tyler Hildebrand against the high-elbow emphasis common in juniors instruction [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. "There is a definite way that the elite athlete does an arm swing, and there are biomechanical studies that prove that there is a certain way to teach arm swings, so that should be a major emphasis in teaching young kids... If you go to Brazil, everybody has the same arm swing. They're teaching all the kids to do it the same way" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The injury argument: "I can't tell you the number of times I've heard, 'She has a shoulder impingement.' That's because the elbow is getting higher than the shoulder. You don't see baseball players do that. You don't see quarterbacks do that in football" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### 7. Be a lifelong learner — outside coaching too
Cook explicitly draws on guitar, flying, and team-roping as parallel learning frames: each required formal instruction and a step-by-step sequence; volleyball coaching is no different [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. His advice to up-and-coming coaches is to be around great coaches in person — Cook himself called Charlie Brande's Orange County club, watched Terry Liskevych and Marv Dunphy practice in San Diego, and organized men's-team scrimmages against Liskevych's USA team to learn [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. "Be a lifelong learner. I continue to learn and get out of my comfort zone... that keeps things fresh and prevents you from burning out" [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

## Contributions to the game

### Four NCAA national championships and a defended-program identity
Cook's four titles — 2000, 2006, 2015, 2017 — span 17 years inside the same head-coaching tenure, an achievement matched by very few D-I coaches in any sport [cook-wikipedia-bio]. Combined with four runner-up finishes (2005, 2018, 2021, 2023), Cook reached the NCAA championship match in 8 of his 25 Nebraska seasons [cook-wikipedia-bio]. The decade-spanning reload pattern is the on-paper case that Nebraska's program model is durable across recruiting classes, not a single-cohort peak.

### Volleyball Day in Nebraska — 92,003 attendance
The 2023 stadium match is, on the record, the largest-attended women's sporting event in world history [cook-wikipedia-bio][aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. Cook frames it as a deliberate competitive response to Wisconsin's prior attendance record [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The structural significance is that women's volleyball has now demonstrated — in a single match — that it can fill an FBS football stadium, which is a market-credibility shift the sport had not had before [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### A defended fundamentals-first methodology
Cook's training-design voice — heavy reps, daily fundamentals, footwork-and-platform reset for new arrivals — is one of the most fully captured in the captured corpus [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. His framing that fundamentals "go away" without daily work is a mechanically usable principle for coaches at any level: ball-control errors that build during a tournament weekend are the team-level version of Cook's individual-player erosion claim [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The 1996 Russia tour is the formative datum he cites [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball].

### Beach cross-training as institutional practice
Cook's January–March beach window for the Nebraska indoor team is a documented training-design choice, not an off-season recreation [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. The pairing — winter beach + spring indoor + competition fall — is the closest documented analog in the women's D-I record to the Brazilian dual-system Cook references explicitly [aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. For coaches studying training-design alternatives to the standard fall-only model, the Nebraska annual-rhythm is a high-signal data point.

### Mentorship contribution
Cook's coaching tree includes Tyler Hildebrand (USC associate head coach, 2024+) and Dani Busboom Kelly (Nebraska successor) [cook-wikipedia-bio][aoc-2024-cook-talking-volleyball]. A comprehensive Cook coaching-tree inventory is not in the captured sources — but the fact that he handed off the Nebraska program to a former player and assistant is itself a continuity signal [cook-wikipedia-bio].
