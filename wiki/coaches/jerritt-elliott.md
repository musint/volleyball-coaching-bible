# Jerritt Elliott

## Overview
Jerritt Elliott is the head coach of the Texas Longhorns women's volleyball program, a post he has held since 2001 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. He has won three NCAA national championships — **2012, 2022, and 2023** — including back-to-back titles in 2022 and 2023 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. His Texas career record stands at 685–137 (.833) over 25 seasons [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. He is the architect of a modern Texas dynasty: 15 Big 12 conference titles, 11 NCAA Final Four appearances, every NCAA tournament except one since taking the job, and the recruiting and development engine that turned out the program's first-ever AVCA National Player of the Year in 2022 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. He moved Texas into the SEC in 2024 and the program has continued to win at the same rate (20–7 in 2024, 26–4 in 2025) in the new conference [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

## Coaching career

### Early years (1991–1995)
Elliott began coaching while still in college: Palisades Charter High School in 1991, then a sequence of LA-area club and high-school posts including Westside Volleyball Club (director/HC, 1992–95), Forum Team Cup VC (HC, 1992–93), and Marymount High School (HC, 1992–94) [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. He completed his BA in kinesiology at Cal State Northridge in 1991 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. In 1993 he served as an assistant on the Cal State Northridge men's team that reached the NCAA national championship game (lost to UCLA) [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The compressed early-career résumé — multiple roles in parallel — is recognizable as the LA-volleyball assistant arc that produced several modern D-I head coaches.

### USC (1995–2000)
Elliott joined USC in 1995 as an assistant under Lisa Love and was promoted to interim head coach for the 1999 and 2000 seasons [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. As interim HC he went 50–12 (.806), led USC to its first NCAA Final Four in 15 seasons, and shared the Pac-10 title [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The 2000 USC team featured two All-Americans, including future Olympic beach gold medalist April Ross [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. Elliott's USC recruiting classes were ranked #1 nationally in both 2000 and 2001 — program firsts — and that recruiting reputation is the through-line into the Texas hire [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

### Texas (2001–present)
Elliott was hired at Texas in April 2001 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. Through 25 seasons his team has made every NCAA tournament except one, reached 11 Final Fours, and won three national titles [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

#### Three NCAA national championships
- **2012:** 29–4. Elliott was named AVCA National Coach of the Year [elliott-wikipedia-bio].
- **2022:** 28–1, sixth consecutive Big 12 title; the season produced the first AVCA National Player of the Year in Texas program history and six All-Americans [elliott-wikipedia-bio].
- **2023:** 28–4, defeating Nebraska in the championship match — back-to-back titles [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

#### Big 12 dominance (2007–2023)
Texas won 15 Big 12 conference titles under Elliott: 2007–2009, 2011–2015, and seven straight 2017–2023 — the latter the longest single-conference title streak in his career [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. He was named Big 12 Coach of the Year nine times (2004, 2007, 2010–12, 2014, 2020–22) [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

#### SEC era (2024–)
Texas joined the SEC in 2024 and Elliott's first SEC season went 20–7 overall, 13–3 in conference [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The 2025 season produced 26–4 overall, 13–2 in SEC play [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The program's win-rate has not collapsed in the conference shift, which is itself a non-trivial result given the SEC's compressed travel and elevated overall depth.

### National recognition
Elliott has been named AVCA National Coach of the Year (2012) and AVCA Southwest Region Coach of the Year seven times (2004, 2007, 2010–11, 2013, 2021–22) — he also won AVCA Pacific Region Coach of the Year at USC in 2000 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. He has been Big 12 Coach of the Year nine times [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

### USA Volleyball involvement
Elliott served as an assistant coach with the USA Junior National Team in 2001 and was head coach of the USA Volleyball A2 Team in 2008 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The collegiate-head-coach + USA-developmental-staff combination is consistent with the modern Tier 1 women's coach pattern.

## Core teaching principles

### 1. Recruit to win national championships, not to fill a depth chart
Elliott's stated recruiting principle is to recruit "to win national championships" — not to fill perceived depth-chart needs of a current team [unsourced — attributed to NCAA.com 2021 feature on Texas, not in this wiki's ingested source corpus]. The factual support for the principle is the recruiting record itself: USC #1 nationally in 2000 and 2001 (program firsts), and 25 years of Texas recruiting that produced 11 Final Fours, three national titles, and the program's first AVCA National Player of the Year in 2022 [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The cumulative record of 18 AVCA All-Americans coached at Texas through 2022 is the throughput-side of that principle [unsourced — figure attributed to Texas program reporting; not directly in the Wikipedia bio captured here].

### 2. Develop the player who makes others better
Elliott has stated he looks for players "able to make the players around them better, one that is able to speak the language of the coaches, one that had just a great feel of what is going on around them and able to make those players change and develop and be united" [unsourced — quote from NCAA.com 2021 feature, not yet ingested into this wiki's source corpus]. The operational implication for coaches at any level is that the recruiting screen is multi-dimensional — not just physical ceiling and skill output but the player's effect on teammates and her ability to absorb and transmit coaching language [unsourced — same source].

### 3. Hold the standard across a 25-year arc
The strongest defensible claim about Elliott's program-design model is its sustained quality: 11 Final Fours in 25 years, three national titles spaced across an 11-year span (2012, 2022, 2023), and a conference shift (Big 12 → SEC) that did not collapse the win rate [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. Programs that produce one championship and then taper are common; programs that produce championships separated by decade-plus gaps and remain in the title conversation across that span are rare [unsourced — comparative tenure claim outside corpus].

### 4. Back-to-back championship execution
The 2022 → 2023 back-to-back is a separate methodology data point from a single-title run [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. Reigning national champions in women's volleyball typically face roster turnover from graduation/portal, opponent counter-scouting throughout the season, and target-on-the-back match psychology. Texas's 2022 (28–1) → 2023 (28–4) sequence — both championship-winning seasons — speaks to a program-management capability around defending a title that is difficult to inherit; comprehensive published analysis of that capability is not yet ingested into this wiki [unsourced].

### 5. Coach-language fluency as a recruiting screen
Elliott's framing — that he wants players "able to speak the language of the coaches" — implies a teaching model in which coach-player communication bandwidth is itself a competitive weapon [unsourced — quote per above]. For coaches at lower levels, the translatable mechanic is: when you evaluate a recruit, evaluate not just her current skill but her capacity to absorb and execute coach-language at speed.

## Contributions to the game

### A modern Texas dynasty in women's volleyball
Texas under Elliott has become a top-tier women's college volleyball program on every measure: NCAA tournament appearance every year except one since 2001, 11 Final Fours, three national championships, and the program's first AVCA National Player of the Year [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The 25-year tenure at one school in women's D-I volleyball is itself uncommon and places Elliott in a small peer set of multi-decade single-program coaches [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

### Back-to-back NCAA championships (2022, 2023)
Defending an NCAA championship is structurally difficult; doing it back-to-back in the modern transfer-portal era is rarer [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. Texas's 2022 → 2023 sequence is one of only a handful of back-to-back women's volleyball titles in the post-2010 NCAA era [unsourced — frequency claim outside corpus]. The 28–1 / 28–4 records across those two seasons are the strongest defense-the-title data points in this wiki's ingested record [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

### Big 12 conference dominance during transition era
Texas won every Big 12 title from 2017 to 2023 — the program's seven-year capstone in a conference whose volleyball depth had risen substantially over that span (Baylor reached the NCAA championship final in 2019, Kansas reached the Final Four in 2021) [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. The Big 12-into-SEC transition itself was a major realignment for Texas; that the on-court production has continued at the same rate is the strongest available evidence that the program model is conference-independent [elliott-wikipedia-bio].

### USC turnaround as a separate proof
Elliott's 1999–2000 interim HC stretch at USC produced the program's first Final Four in 15 seasons and shared Pac-10 title — entirely separate from the Texas tenure [elliott-wikipedia-bio]. For coaches studying turnaround mechanics, the USC two-year sample plus the Texas 25-year sample together represent two different programs reaching the national elite under his direction.

### Recruiting reputation as program infrastructure
Both stops — USC and Texas — built recruiting-class rankings (USC #1 nationally in 2000 and 2001; Texas's 18 All-Americans through 2022) into program infrastructure rather than treating recruiting as a one-class peak [elliott-wikipedia-bio][unsourced — All-American figure noted above]. For coaches at any level, the model is recruiting as a recurring cycle rather than a hit-or-miss event.
