# John Speraw

## Overview

John Speraw is the outgoing head coach of the U.S. Men's National Team (2013–2024) and concurrently served as UCLA men's volleyball head coach (2012–2024) before stepping into the role of USA Volleyball President and CEO on October 1, 2024 [speraw-2024-usav-profile] [kiraly-2024-usav-announcement]. He is the only person in men's volleyball history to have won an NCAA Division I men's championship as a player, as an assistant coach, and as a head coach [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. His coaching identity fuses the UCLA lineage he competed in as a middle blocker with the USA Men's staff culture he learned from Hugh McCutcheon during the 2008 Beijing gold-medal cycle [mccutcheon-2008-beijing-gold-usav] [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

## Coaching career

**Player, UCLA (early 1990s).** Speraw played middle blocker at UCLA and was part of the Bruins' 1993 and 1995 NCAA championship teams [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. This grounding in UCLA volleyball shapes both the cultural register of his coaching and the specific networks (UCLA alumni, West Coast men's volleyball) he worked through for the next thirty years.

**Head coach, UC Irvine (through 2012).** At UC Irvine Speraw built a dominant men's program, winning three NCAA Division I Men's Volleyball Championships — 2007, 2009, and 2012 — before moving across the LA Basin to UCLA for the 2012–13 season [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. The UC Irvine era is when he made his name as a program-builder in the notoriously small national pool of Division I men's volleyball teams.

**Assistant coach, USA Men's National Team — Beijing 2008.** Speraw was on Hugh McCutcheon's staff for the USA Men's 2008 Beijing Olympic gold-medal campaign, the program's first men's Olympic gold since 1988 [mccutcheon-2008-beijing-gold-usav]. During the tournament, after McCutcheon left the team temporarily following the Drum Tower attack on his in-laws, Speraw was one of the senior voices — alongside Marv Dunphy and Carl McGown — supporting Assistant Coach Ron Larsen as acting head coach for three matches [mccutcheon-2008-beijing-gold-usav]. That experience on McCutcheon's staff appears repeatedly in his later framing of coaching-staff operation and culture [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**Head coach, UCLA (2012–2024) and USA Men's National Team (2013–2024).** Speraw took over UCLA for the 2012–13 season and the U.S. Men's National Team in 2013, holding both jobs simultaneously for roughly a decade — an unusual arrangement in modern high-performance volleyball, where club/national dual roles are more common in Europe than the U.S. [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. At UCLA he added two more NCAA titles as head coach (2023, 2024), bringing his total NCAA head-coaching championships to five [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. With the USA Men he won gold at the 2014 FIVB World League and the 2015 FIVB World Cup; three NORCECA Championships (2013, 2017, 2023); and Olympic bronze medals in both 2016 Rio and 2024 Paris [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. The 2020 Tokyo cycle is not highlighted as a medal in the USAV profile [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**Transition to USAV President and CEO (October 2024).** Speraw was named USA Volleyball's President and CEO on September 26, 2024, stepping into the role on October 1 and handing the USA Men's head-coach position to Karch Kiraly for the LA 2028 cycle — Kiraly himself moving across from women's to men's in the federation's highest-profile coaching transition in a decade [kiraly-2024-usav-announcement] [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. In the USAV announcement, Speraw endorsed Kiraly in language that underlines the staff-operation theme central to his own coaching voice: "I have complete and total trust in Karch's process. He has a relentless work ethic, brilliant intellect..." [kiraly-2024-usav-announcement].

**GMS network affiliation.** Speraw is listed on the Gold Medal Squared coaching roster, placing him inside the extended Carl McGown–Doug Beal–Marv Dunphy lineage that already ran through the USA Men's 2008 staff he was part of [gms-nd-about-us] [mccutcheon-2008-beijing-gold-usav]. The GMS connection is lineage and network rather than explicit methodological endorsement — the USAV profile does not attribute his on-court teaching to a named school [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

## Core teaching principles

**1. Culture flows top-down from coaching-staff behavior.** Speraw argues that how the coaching staff operates — its internal dynamics, communication, fairness, accountability — trickles into how the team trains and plays [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. The staff's conduct is the team's first lesson in what excellence looks like [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**2. Equitable is not the same as equal.** He distinguishes treating players "equally" (identical rules for everyone) from treating them "equitably" (tailoring approach to the individual athlete's circumstances). Fairness, in this framing, is calibrated, not uniform [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**3. Leadership through example, with caution about offloading accountability to players.** Speraw is explicit that coaches should embody the qualities they want their team to display, and he has publicly cautioned against placing full peer-to-peer accountability on the athletes — the staff cannot abdicate that weight [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**4. Evolve from rigid rules toward flexible guidelines (Wooden influence).** He cites the John Wooden lineage — itself embedded in UCLA's coaching culture — in describing an arc from prescriptive team rules early in a coach's career toward more flexible, situation-specific guidelines as experience accumulates [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**5. Ethical leadership as a core coaching credential.** The 2008 Pillar Award for "outstanding achievement in ethical leadership" is cited as an anchor of his coaching identity, not just an external honor [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. Ethics, in this frame, is practical — staff conduct, fairness calibration, accountability placement — rather than abstract.

**6. Player-development and defensive-systems orientation.** [unsourced] His reputation inside the U.S. men's volleyball community emphasizes player development and defensive systems, but the available Tier 1 source does not spell out the technical content of his teaching (e.g., block schemes, dig reads, side-out structure).

## Contributions to the game

**First Point Volleyball Foundation.** Speraw founded First Point Volleyball to expand opportunities for boys' and men's volleyball in the United States, where the men's game is historically thin at the high school level and funneled through a small set of Division I programs [speraw-2024-usav-profile]. This advocacy vehicle is treated in the federation profile as a signature contribution distinct from his on-court coaching record [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**Unique NCAA treble (player / assistant / head coach).** Speraw is the only individual in men's volleyball history to have won an NCAA Division I Men's Volleyball Championship in all three roles — player (UCLA, 1993 and 1995), assistant (not specified in the available source [unsourced]), and head coach (UC Irvine 2007, 2009, 2012; UCLA 2023, 2024) [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**Sustaining the USA Men's program through three Olympic cycles.** From 2013 through 2024 Speraw kept the USA Men's program competitive internationally — bronze medals in 2016 and 2024, world-level titles in 2014 and 2015, and three NORCECA gold medals across the decade — while simultaneously rebuilding UCLA into a two-time NCAA champion program [speraw-2024-usav-profile].

**Federation leadership.** Stepping into the USAV President and CEO role in October 2024 puts Speraw in position to shape USA Volleyball's structural priorities — coaching education, men's-side investment, national-team pipelines — for the LA 2028 cycle and beyond [speraw-2024-usav-profile] [kiraly-2024-usav-announcement].
