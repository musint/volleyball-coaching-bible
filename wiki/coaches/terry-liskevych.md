# Terry Liskevych

## Overview
Terry Liskevych is a lifelong American coach-educator whose career sits at the
hinge between the modern USA women's national team era and the teach-the-coaches
infrastructure that followed it. He served as head coach of the U.S. Women's
National Team from 1985 to 1996 — three Olympic cycles culminating in a bronze
medal at Barcelona 1992 [liskevych-2021-volleybrains-no-shortcuts] — then
returned to college coaching at Oregon State and, in retirement from the bench,
co-founded [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] and edited AVCA's *The Volleyball
Coaching Bible* volumes [liskevych-2015-aoc-10-things]. In the current wiki's
terms, he is both a practitioner and a primary vehicle by which American
volleyball IP is packaged and transmitted: the ASICS Skill Series alone is a
rare coach-delivered full fundamentals set [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-passing].

## Coaching career
Liskevych's trajectory runs through three distinct phases and bridges the
collegiate and national-team sides of American volleyball.

- **College, pre-NT (1970s–1985).** Head coach at the University of Pacific
  through the late 1970s and early 1980s. This is the era in which a young John
  Dunning, with no volleyball background, slept in his car to attend a
  Liskevych clinic in Stockton and absorbed the fundamentals that seeded his
  own Hall-of-Fame career [aoc-2017-liskevych-dunning-tribute]. The Pacific
  period established Liskevych's reputation as a clinician whose teaching
  frameworks traveled.

- **USA Women's National Team (1985–1996).** Appointed in 1985 to rebuild a
  program gutted by retirements after the 1984 silver-medal team. Liskevych
  inherited a roster that lost all seven matches and no sets against Korea's
  third-ranked club team; within a decade he had the program at world #1, with
  bronze at Barcelona 1992, a silver at the 1990 World Championships, and gold
  at the 1995 World Grand Prix [liskevych-2021-volleybrains-no-shortcuts].
  Three Olympic quads (1988 Seoul, 1992 Barcelona, 1996 Atlanta) sit in this
  span [liskevych-2015-aoc-10-things].

- **Post-NT and Oregon State (1996–present).** Returned to Oregon State as
  head coach and later built out the educator-coach side of his work:
  co-founding [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] with John Dunning and Russ Rose
  [aoc-2017-liskevych-dunning-tribute], editing *The Volleyball Coaching Bible*
  volumes for AVCA/Human Kinetics [liskevych-2021-volleybrains-no-shortcuts],
  and producing the full ASICS Skill Series covering all six contact skills
  [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-passing][aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-setting][aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-hitting][aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-blocking][aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-serving][aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-defense].
  He remains an active clinician, podcast guest, and mentor, and is an open
  critic of USA Volleyball's current organizational leadership
  [aoc-2024-liskevych-growing-sport].

## Core teaching principles

**Controlling feelings before controlling play.** Liskevych frames the mental
game as a trainable technique, not a personality trait. Players and coaches
own their emotional state or are owned by it; untreated negative emotion
becomes team drag [aoc-2017-liskevych-controlling-feelings]. The coach's job
is to help athletes convert feelings into a usable attitude, and the principle
applies equally to the bench — post-game tone should not swing with the
scoreboard [aoc-2026-post-game-talk]. This is the root of the "no emotional
leakage" throughline in his work.

**Build systems around players, not players into systems.** "Perfect practice
makes perfect. Design systems for your players rather than forcing them into
rigid frameworks. Build technical skills before tactical complexity"
[liskevych-2015-aoc-10-things]. Player-first system design is the organizing
principle of his USA NT tenure and the reason his AOC teaching emphasizes
skill-before-tactic sequencing. It is also why he admires John Dunning's
2016 mid-season conversion from a 6-2 to a 5-1 to accommodate injuries —
flexibility serves the roster, not the schematic [aoc-2017-liskevych-dunning-tribute].

**Measurement beats gut feel — with the right window.** Liskevych argues that
full-match hitting percentages obscure within-match momentum. His named
heuristic, the **Liskevych-5**, judges a player by her most recent five
contacts rather than the cumulative line, revealing who is hot or cold *now*
and driving substitution and set-distribution decisions [aoc-2022-liskevych-five].
Rolling-window evaluation is a signature piece of the Liskevych stats
sensibility and a direct rebuke of box-score coaching. He extends the same
analytic habit into video work — scouting, match analysis, and embedded
practice review — packaged with his "Highlight Videos," "Use of Video in a
Practice Setting," and "Scouting 101" handouts [aoc-2020-stone-liskevych-using-video].

**Lifelong-learner orientation; cross-sport reading.** Paired with Kiraly,
Liskevych converges on a single first principle: "Learning is Job One" for
head coach, staff, and players alike [aoc-2024-legends-liskevych-kiraly]. His
recommended-reading list is telling: of ~35 titles, only one (Pettit's *A Fresh
Season*) is volleyball-specific — the rest are leadership, mental-game, and
talent-development texts from football, basketball, tennis, and performance
science (Walsh, Wooden, Gallwey, Coyle, Epstein, Dweck, Kerr, Orlick)
[aoc-2013-liskevych-top-references]. Coaching IP, in his view, comes from
outside the sport.

**Player-first program philosophy and roster/staff complementarity.** Who you
surround yourself with caps the program's ceiling. Roster and staff selection
are strategic, not administrative [aoc-2018-liskevych-players-and-staff].
Hire to complement your weaknesses; culture fit flows from selection rather
than correction [liskevych-2015-aoc-10-things]. In the perennial
players-vs-coaches debate for who drives the sport, he picks players —
coaches build the framework, but players create the product
[aoc-2024-liskevych-growing-sport].

**Fundamentals-first skill teaching across all six contacts.** The ASICS Skill
Series documents a coherent teaching progression the length of the game:
forearm platform and angle-to-target in [[passing-forearm]]
[aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-passing]; hand shape, footwork, and target alignment
in [[setting-hands]] [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-setting]; approach, armswing,
and contact in [[hitting-approach]] [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-hitting]; toss,
contact, and routine in [[serving-float]] [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-serving];
stance, penetration, and hand work at the net in blocking technique
[aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-blocking]; reads, posture, and pursuit in
[[defense-platform]] [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-defense]. In blocking
specifically, he is explicit: hands are the primary teaching point (block with
hands, not arms), balance sets up the hands, penetration beats reach, and
swing blocking is "flavor of the month" relative to durable traditional
footwork [aoc-2016-liskevych-blocking-what-matters].

## Contributions to the game

**The USA women's NT rebuild (1985–1996).** Taking over a program hollowed out
after 1984, Liskevych delivered three straight Olympics and a bronze, silver,
and gold across world-level competitions in the first half of the 1990s
[liskevych-2021-volleybrains-no-shortcuts]. The rebuild is his most
consequential on-court contribution and the reference case for his "there are
no shortcuts" thesis — everything takes longer than you think, and nothing
worth doing is easy.

**Co-founding [[art-of-coaching-volleyball]] (AOC).** With John Dunning and
Russ Rose, Liskevych launched AOC as a coach-development platform — the
educator-coach infrastructure that now distributes much of American volleyball
teaching at scale [aoc-2017-liskevych-dunning-tribute]. The ASICS Skill
Series, the "Liskevych 5" article, the controlling-feelings lecture, and the
Dunning tribute are all AOC products of this second act.

**Editing *The Volleyball Coaching Bible* (AVCA / Human Kinetics).** The
multi-volume AVCA coaching anthologies — the "coaching bible" this wiki riffs
on — were edited by Liskevych [liskevych-2021-volleybrains-no-shortcuts]. As
editor he gave the sport its canonical multi-author reference, assembling
contributions from the coaches his generation produced.

**The **Liskevych-5** heuristic.** A named, portable rolling-evaluation tool
for in-match player reads [aoc-2022-liskevych-five]. Used as shorthand by
coaches who want a last-five-contacts lens without running full stats during
play.

**The ASICS Skill Series.** A rare coach-delivered full fundamentals set
covering all six skills by a former national-team head coach — useful at the
[[age-lens-hs]] and [[age-lens-14u]] levels where consistent skill vocabulary
matters more than tactical sophistication [aoc-2017-liskevych-asics-passing].

**Mentorship as a coaching legacy.** Liskevych frames his post-bench decades
explicitly as a debt repayment: "I've learned so much about how not to do
things that I can help people cut down on some of the errors"
[liskevych-2021-volleybrains-no-shortcuts]. The Dunning career itself — from
a Stockton clinic to five NCAA titles — is the most-cited proof of concept
[aoc-2017-liskevych-dunning-tribute].
