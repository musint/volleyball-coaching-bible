# Mark Rosen

## Overview

Mark Rosen is a longtime Division I head coach (Michigan 1999-2022; later Creighton [unsourced]) and a sustained Art of Coaching Volleyball (AOC) contributor whose public footprint in the ingested sample is concentrated on transition training, swing-blocking footwork, OH shot selection based on block, and competitive serve-pass work [aoc-2016-rosen-training-transition-in-out-system] [aoc-2017-rosen-training-transitions] [aoc-2021-rosen-oh-shots-based-on-block] [aoc-2026-rosen-swing-blocking-footwork]. Rosen's AOC pieces share a throughline: teach transition as the skill that determines high-level offensive success, and teach blocking-and-defense with footwork specificity ahead of aggression [aoc-2016-rosen-training-transition-in-out-system] [aoc-2026-rosen-swing-blocking-footwork]. ## Coaching career

**Head coach, University of Michigan Women's Volleyball (1999-2022).** Rosen led the Wolverines to multiple Big Ten finishes and NCAA tournament appearances across a 23-season tenure [unsourced]. At Michigan, Rosen developed the transition-and-swing-block methodology that forms the bulk of his AOC publishing output [aoc-2016-rosen-training-transition-in-out-system].

**Head coach, Creighton University Women's Volleyball (2025-present [unsourced]).** Rosen moved to Creighton after his Michigan tenure ended; his Creighton-era AOC work has emphasized ball-control and efficient hitting-lines drills [aoc-2025-rosen-creighton-ball-control] [aoc-2025-rosen-efficient-hitting-lines].

**AOC contributor (2016-present).** Rosen's output spans transition training (2016-2017), OOS offense and defense (2020), OH shot selection (2021), swing-blocking footwork (2026), and ball-control and hitting-lines drills (2025) [aoc-2016-rosen-training-transition-in-out-system] [aoc-2017-rosen-training-transitions] [aoc-2021-rosen-oh-shots-based-on-block] [aoc-2025-rosen-creighton-ball-control].

## Core teaching principles

**Transition as the trainable gap between good and great teams.** Rosen's 2016 and 2017 transition pieces argue that high-level in-system attacking is a relatively mature skill in NCAA volleyball, while transition attacking — the block-to-attack chain, the dig-to-attack chain, OOS pin-attack decisions — is where teams separate [aoc-2016-rosen-training-transition-in-out-system] [aoc-2017-rosen-training-transitions].

**Swing-blocking footwork as a teachable technical sequence.** Rosen's 2026 piece treats swing-blocking as a specific footwork pattern — not a generic aggression prescription — and reduces its teaching to discrete steps that can be drilled and graded [aoc-2026-rosen-swing-blocking-footwork].

**Shot selection driven by block read.** Rosen's 2021 piece places the OH's shot selection downstream of reading the block's shape; the hitter's menu shrinks or opens based on a pre-contact block read rather than a post-jump decision [aoc-2021-rosen-oh-shots-based-on-block].

## Contributions to the game

**Transition-training curriculum for NCAA-level programs.** Across multiple AOC pieces, Rosen has published a durable transition-training curriculum — in-system → out-of-system transitions, block-OOS, dig-set-hit shapes — that other head coaches use as practice-plan raw material [aoc-2016-rosen-training-transition-in-out-system] [aoc-2017-rosen-training-transitions].

**Swing-blocking methodology bridging European and American traditions.** Rosen's swing-blocking work gives U.S. coaches a footwork-first teaching path into a blocking style that originated in the European professional tradition [aoc-2026-rosen-swing-blocking-footwork].
