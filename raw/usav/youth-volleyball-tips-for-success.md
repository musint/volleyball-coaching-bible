# Youth Volleyball Tips for Success

**Author:** John Kessel

---

## Overview

Drawing from nearly 40 years of coaching experience at the 12U level, this resource addresses strategies for expanding youth volleyball programs, particularly for children ages 6-12.

## Key Topics

### Fitting Volleyball Into Other Sports Programs

The article suggests maximizing facility usage by sharing gymnasium space with basketball programs. Rather than requesting an entire gym, coaches can request a single court section and establish multiple smaller courts using specialized net systems.

The "Maximum Contact Net System" enables six different sports on one structure at various net heights:

- Volleyball (older kids): 6'6"
- Volleyball (younger kids): 5'
- Badminton: 5'
- Tennis: 3'
- Sitting Volleyball (men): 3'9 1/4"
- Sitting Volleyball (women): 3'7 1/4"
- Pickleball: 3'

Alternative scheduling solutions include training during non-traditional hours, such as early morning or evening sessions.

### Addressing Coach Shortages

The resource recommends leveraging existing personnel, including physical education teachers who manage large class ratios effectively. The article emphasizes supervised open gym sessions where "the game teaching the game" allows children to develop leadership and decision-making skills independently.

Club programs should develop internal coaching development initiatives, encouraging experienced players to mentor newer participants.

### Training the Youngest Players (Ages 3-9)

Incorporate diverse movement experiences through "games" rather than traditional drills. The author suggests introducing multiple sports as warm-up activities and creating movement discovery sessions using varied equipment like different-sized balls and foam noodles.

### Game-Based Learning ("Grills")

Children prefer games over drills. The resource advocates for "game-like drills with scoring" that teach competitive and cooperative skills simultaneously. One innovative approach involves using "the loser becomes the net," playable with 3-12 participants without requiring an actual net.
