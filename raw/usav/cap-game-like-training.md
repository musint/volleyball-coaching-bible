# CAP Article: Game-Like Training

**Author:** Rick Swan

**Publication Date:** Originally published in 2009

---

## Content

### Introduction

Game-like training represents the principle that athletes develop volleyball proficiency more effectively by practicing within authentic competitive contexts rather than isolating individual skills. Athletic success depends not on isolated skill execution but on team performance within actual match conditions.

### The Coach's Journey

Rick Swan describes receiving guidance at a coaching clinic that fundamentally transformed his approach. He explains: "If it wasn't game-like we weren't going to do it." This philosophy eliminated traditional drills—standing lines, pepper warm-ups, and repetitive exercises.

The implementation proved dramatically successful. Within weeks, players demonstrated remarkable improvement in ball control, court movement, and decision-making. A young, relatively inexperienced roster advanced to fourth place at the state tournament, then won the school's first state championship the following year.

### Core Philosophy

Swan identifies reading and reacting as volleyball's most critical skill—more fundamental than passing or serving alone. Artificial practice situations create problematic habits that must be unlearned before competition.

**Examples of ineffective artificial drills:**
- Tossed balls in hitting lines
- Hitting from isolated boxes
- Coach-directed ball striking at diggers
- Wall spiking exercises
- Partner passing or setting without net context

Effective practice mimics actual game situations through one-on-one, two-on-two, and competitive formats that demand genuine decision-making.

### Key Practice Principles

1. Eliminate non-game-like activities
2. Incorporate competitive scorekeeping
3. Establish clear drill objectives
4. Create pressure through consequences
5. Vary daily practice structure
6. Empower players to manage drills
7. Maintain active engagement without standing lines
8. Practice unpredictable, chaotic situations
9. Prioritize play over coaching
10. Emphasize enjoyment throughout
