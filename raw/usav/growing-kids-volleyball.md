# Growing Kids' Volleyball

## Overview

This comprehensive guide from USA Volleyball focuses on youth volleyball coaching methods for children 12 and under. The author, drawing from 40 years of coaching experience, advocates for practices that emphasize game understanding and player engagement rather than traditional line-based drills.

## Key Coaching Approaches

**Pair Activities Over the Net**

The guide recommends practicing receive-set-attack combinations in pairs across a net or ribbon system. As noted, this method keeps all students active simultaneously rather than having "one player touching the ball while 23 watch."

**Two vs. Zero Games**

This cooperative warm-up develops genuine ball control. Players work together to achieve consecutive successful net crossings before transitioning to competitive play.

**Modified Net Games**

"Loser Becomes the Net" variations work with three or more players, where unsuccessful players stand in the middle with raised arms. This teaches net awareness and blocking fundamentals across various group sizes.

**Small-Sided Formats**

Four nets on a rope systems allow 8-16 doubles teams to compete simultaneously on standard courts. Round-robin competitions ensure competitive matches regardless of skill level.

## Additional Recommendations

- **Two-contact games** introduce chaotic elements that increase reading opportunities
- **Sitting volleyball** provides practice alternatives when gym space is limited
- **Peer coaching** where older children (11-12) teach younger groups (7-10)
- **Coed play** accelerates skill development through mixed-experience competition
- **Field trips** to high school or college matches inspire player engagement

The resource emphasizes that coaches should observe technical understanding without relying solely on game performance, while maintaining focus on over-the-net play and competitive enjoyment.
