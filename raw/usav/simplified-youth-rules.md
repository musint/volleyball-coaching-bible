# Simplified Youth Rules - USA Volleyball

**Author:** John Kessel

---

## Overview

USA Volleyball provides simplified rules for youth players ages 3-11, designed to make the sport more accessible and enjoyable for younger athletes. These guidelines follow the Domestic Competition Regulations while allowing flexibility in court setup, team composition, and scoring.

## Team Composition

Teams may consist of 2, 3, or 4 players per side and can be single-gender or coed. The document notes that "substitutions are unlimited, and often should be done by circulation," allowing players to rotate in and out during play.

## Court Options

The rulebook offers multiple court configurations:

- **Two youth courts on one adult net:** 4 meters wide with a 2-meter safety buffer
- **Four courts on two linked nets:** 4 meters wide and 4.5-6 meters deep
- **Racquetball courts:** Ideal for younger players to prevent excessive ball chasing
- **Tennis courts:** Using rope or net dividers down the center
- **Alternative surfaces:** Grass, concrete, or sand are acceptable if consistent for both teams

## Playing Format

Three ways to score points:
1. Opponent makes an error
2. Opponent receives a conduct penalty
3. Team puts the ball down on opponent's court

Sets are played to 25 points with a required 2-point lead. Matches can be single games or best-of-three format, with tiebreaker sets to 15 points.

## Key Rules

**Service:** Players may use overhead, jump, underhand, or windmill serves. Two toss attempts are permitted before the serve must be executed. Serves occur from the end line or up to 5 meters behind it.

**Ball Handling:** Maximum three contacts per side before returning over the net. The first contact may be a double hit; unintentional double hits are allowed. The ball cannot be thrown or carried.

**Net Play:** Players may not touch the net unless the ball drives it into them. The ball may hit the net and continue over as long as it passes fully between antennas.

**Blocking:** Blocked balls don't count toward the team's three-hit limit. Youth teams should use single-player blocks rather than double blocks.

## Coaching Philosophy

The document emphasizes developmental principles:

- "Catch them Doing it Right" – Use encouragement over punishment
- Focus on performance over outcome to encourage learning
- Treat mistakes as teaching opportunities
- Emphasize positive errors (high, safe passes) over negative ones (into the net)
- Use "FeedForward" coaching—providing guidance on upcoming actions rather than criticizing past mistakes

## Special Considerations

**Uniforms:** Must be numbered 1-99 and match, though identical tops and shorts aren't strictly required for youth programs.

**Injury Protocol:** Stop play immediately and call a replay. Players with hard casts cannot participate; only small padded finger splints are permitted.

**Equal Playing Time:** The guidelines encourage rotating players to ensure everyone gets court time, including opportunities to serve and play front row positions.

**Officiating:** While a referee is ideal, the document states that "a referee is not required, as long as both teams are willing to call their own faults."

## Adaptive Volleyball

The resource encourages coaches to identify talented young players for USA's Paralympic teams, including sitting volleyball and beach ParaVolley programs, as well as USA Deaf National teams competing in the Deaflympics.
