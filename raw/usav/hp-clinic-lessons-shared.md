# HP Clinic Lessons Shared

**Author:** John Kessel

---

## Overview

This article recounts key teachings from the USA Volleyball High Performance Coaches' Clinic held at the Olympic Training Center, featuring presentations by Hugh McCutcheon, Tom Hogan, and Peter Vint.

## Core Teaching Principles

The coaches emphasized three foundational concepts for skill development in passing and other techniques:

**Repeatability** - Players must consistently execute skills successfully across varying competition levels, not just occasionally.

**Variance** - Minimizing unnecessary movement through economical, consistent technique increases performance reliability. "Simpler is better, consistency comes with such economy of movement."

**Risk Management** - Strategic positioning reduces errors. For example, placing the setter's target five to six feet off the net allows greater margin for error while keeping balls playable.

## Philosophy on Development

McCutcheon stressed that coaching effectiveness depends on foundational science rather than individual coaching styles. He advocated for developing "generalized specialists"—players skilled across all game areas but exceptional in one or two—rather than early specialization.

On gender differences in training, he argued that physics and sport principles remain universal: "Don't we live on planet Earth and the laws of physics apply?"

## Key Training Insights

- Opponents excel at executing fundamentals, maintaining biomechanical efficiency, making in-system adjustments, and making tactical decisions
- Fun and positive reinforcement drive long-term player retention and development
- Over-the-net, game-like training approaches prove most effective
