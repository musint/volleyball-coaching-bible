# Lesson Plans - USA Volleyball

## Overview

USA Volleyball provides comprehensive lesson plans designed for teachers and coaches to instruct volleyball's fundamental skills: hitting, overhead passing/setting, serving, forearm passing, and blocking.

## Key Principles

The organization emphasizes several motor learning concepts:

- **Specificity**: Drills should closely resemble game conditions, ideally incorporating a net (or rope, ribbon, or person as alternatives).

- **Random vs. Blocked Training**: "Random training is superior to blocked training," reflecting how actual matches present unpredictable scenarios rather than repetitive patterns.

- **Whole vs. Partial Training**: Practice should mirror complete game sequences (pass-set-hit combinations) rather than isolated skill drills.

As coaching director John Kessel notes, "There are many ways to define success for a volleyball coach...how many kids play volleyball again the following season."

## Program Structure

Each lesson plan includes:
- Gamelike drills with varied distances, angles, and player movements
- Net incorporation
- Multi-skill combinations
- Player-tracked scoring
- Time allocations
- SHAPE standards alignment
