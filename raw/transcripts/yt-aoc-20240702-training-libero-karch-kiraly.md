# Training Your Libero with Karch Kiraly

**Presenters:** Karch Kiraly (then USA Women's National Team head coach) and Luka Slabe
**Publish date (companion article):** 2024-07-02

[transcript-unavailable — YouTube video embedded on AoC page; captions not exposed via WebFetch. Content below drawn from the public AoC companion article summarizing the video.]

## Content summary

Instructional video featuring Karch Kiraly and Luka Slabe walking through drills for developing libero and back-row defensive skills.

## Drills presented

1. **Blitz the Bro** — A 6v6 game where servers intentionally target the libero. Kiraly notes the USA national team uses this drill weekly to challenge liberos against multiple serves.

2. **Fire Drill** — Players rotate positions after each point in a 6v6 format with half-speed attacks. Kiraly: "The main point of this drill is giving the players some empathy for how hard it is to play other positions."

3. **Neville's Pepper Drill** — Awards points for successful digs and rally wins, with all attacks originating from the back row. Can be modified so attacks target Zone 5 where the libero plays.

4. **Setter-Rotation Game** — Requires the libero to set when the setter plays the first ball.

5. **Game-Planning Exercise** — Teaches liberos to coordinate defensive positioning with the blocking line.

## Quoted

- Kiraly (on Fire Drill): "The main point of this drill is giving the players some empathy for how hard it is to play other positions."
- Slabe: drill modifications should always reward quality defensive play.
