# Terry Liskevych: "There are no shortcuts" (VolleyBrains Podcast)

## Core Coaching Philosophy
- **Embracing failure as learning**: "Failure is tuition for the future." Losses and skill mistakes are essential components of development rather than setbacks.
- **Patience and process**: "Everything takes longer than you think, and nothing in life that is worth doing is easy." His philosophy rejects shortcuts, insisting that sustained effort and realistic timelines are prerequisites for meaningful achievement.

## USA Women's National Team (1985–1996)
Liskevych transformed the program from rebuilding to dominance. When he assumed the role in 1985, following retirements from the 1984 silver-medal team, the squad faced immediate challenges — losing all seven matches and no sets against Korea's third-ranked club team. Within a decade, the team reached world number one status. Highlights:
- Bronze medals at 1992 Barcelona Olympics and 1990 World Championships
- Gold at 1995 World Grand Prix
- 300+ international victories across 12 years

## Leadership Philosophy
Rather than pursuing financial gain exclusively, Liskevych prioritizes mentoring: "I enjoy mentoring coaches and business people … because I've learned so much about how not to do things that I can help people cut down on some of the errors."

## AOC Co-Founder
One of three founders of The Art of Coaching Volleyball (2011) with John Dunning and Russ Rose.

## Editorial Role
Liskevych served as editor of *The Volleyball Coaching Bible* (Human Kinetics, AVCA series) — the canonical multi-author coaching reference that compiles chapters from dozens of elite coaches on philosophy, program management, skills, systems, and practice.
