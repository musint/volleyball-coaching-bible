# VolleyBrains Podcast Episode 30 — Hugh McCutcheon: "It's about committing to a standard of competitive excellence."

Podcast episode summary/show-notes extraction from VolleyBrains.com. [transcript-unavailable] — full verbatim audio transcript not fetched.

## Summary

McCutcheon describes his entry into coaching: initially a means to fund graduate studies in exercise science, statistics, and business administration at BYU. Rather than a predetermined calling, he was drawn to coaching through a series of "unique experiences" and opportunities — including Olympic coaching roles — which allowed him to apply the same principles across different contexts.

## Core philosophy quote (per show notes)

> "It's about committing to a standard of competitive excellence."

## Key tenets discussed

- Focus on **winning through excellence** rather than winning itself
- Help athletes develop on and off the court during formative years
- Provide a "launching pad" for the next life phase

## Advice to younger coaches

Two critical lessons:

1. **Patience.** Younger coaches often expect immediate results; development takes time.
2. **Intellectual humility.** Coaching exists in "gray" areas rather than absolute certainties. He cautions against polarizing opinions and advocates evaluating ideas through "scientific rigor" rather than tradition alone. Coaches need "guiding principles" to distinguish substantive approaches from those merely based on opinion or tradition.

## Career touchpoints mentioned

- USA Men's Olympic gold (2008)
- USA Women's silver (2012) and three Grand Prix titles
- Minnesota Gophers head coach since 2012
- International Volleyball Hall of Fame (2018)
