# Wave: A Fast-Paced Serve Receive Drill

**Presented by:** Karin Keeney (Hebron High School, Texas) via The Art of Coaching Volleyball
**Publish date (companion article):** 2021-01-12

## Drill Overview

Drill developed by Karin Keeney at Hebron High School in Texas. Designed to make serve-receive practice engaging through competitive, fast-paced gameplay.

## Drill Structure

**Team Setup:** Divide players into groups of three. One team serves while another receives in a line formation, with a third team mirroring this setup on the opposite court side.

**Serving Rules:** Players serve one at a time from behind the end line at varying positions. A key constraint: "Servers are not allowed to serve the same spot on the court twice; if they do, they have a penalty (burpees, pushups, etc.)."

**Scoring:** Receiving teams must execute 10 quality passes to a target player (usually the setter) at the net. Overpasses or balls hitting the floor reset the score to zero.

**Rotation:** After every three successful passes, receivers rotate positions (left→right, right→middle, middle→left), ensuring all players practice multiple receiving angles.

**Duration & Consequences:** Two-minute rounds. Teams failing to reach 10 passes perform sprints or conditioning exercises.

## Key Benefits

Develops serving accuracy while maintaining receiver engagement through rapid-fire challenges.

## Quoted

Keeney on inclusivity: "It doesn't matter if you are a setter or a middle, all my kids pass."
