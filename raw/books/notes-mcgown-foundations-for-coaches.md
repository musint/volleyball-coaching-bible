# Carl McGown — Coaching Books (Composite Notes)

## Important disambiguation

The Task B.9 brief named this book "Carl McGown + Hilary McGown + Mariv Adamson,
*Volleyball: Foundations for Coaches*" and described it as the "GMS canonical text."
Web research at fetch time (2026-04-24) does not surface any McGown book with that exact
title, and "Hilary McGown" / "Mariv Adamson" do not appear as co-authors of any McGown
volleyball book in Open Library, Amazon search, Goodreads search, Barnes & Noble, GMS
website, AbeBooks, or Web search.

**Open Library's authoritative McGown bibliography** lists six works:
1. *The effect of motor set, sensory set, and natural set on reaction time at different
   levels of auditory intensity* (1971/1972/1978 — three editions of his early
   motor-learning research)
2. *Science of Coaching Volleyball* (1994) — solo author
3. *Complete Volleyball for Coaches* (1995) — co-authored with Hilda Fronske
4. *Coaching Volleyball: Building a Winning Team* (2000) — co-authored with Hilda
   Fronske and Launa Moser

**The closest match to "GMS canonical text"** is *Science of Coaching Volleyball* (1994,
Human Kinetics), which is most consistently cited in coach-community discussion as
McGown's primary methodological text and the volume that operationalizes the motor-learning
specificity stance GMS still teaches today. *Coaching Volleyball: Building a Winning Team*
(2000, w/ Fronske + Moser) is more of a comprehensive coaching textbook (publisher: Allyn
& Bacon, ISBN 0205309585), and is the McGown volume most likely to have been the brief's
intended target — but with co-authors Fronske + Moser, not Hilary McGown + Mariv Adamson.

**Conclusion:** Treat "Hilary McGown / Mariv Adamson / Foundations for Coaches" as a
misattribution. The slug `notes-mcgown-foundations-for-coaches` is retained for cluster
continuity but documents the actual McGown coaching-books canon, with *Science of
Coaching Volleyball* as the primary "GMS canonical text" reference and *Coaching
Volleyball: Building a Winning Team* as the comprehensive textbook companion. Both books
sit alongside McGown's peer-reviewed AVCA journal article on whole-vs-part training as
the published methodology spine of the GMS school.

## Carl McGown's coaching-books catalog

### 1. *Science of Coaching Volleyball* (1994) — primary methodology text

- **Author:** Carl McGown (solo)
- **Year:** 1994
- **Publisher:** Human Kinetics (per Amazon listing, ISBN 0873225724 / 9780873225724)
- **ISBN:** 0873225724 / 9780873225724
- **Format:** Paperback technical-methodology coaching reference
- **Status:** McGown's first volleyball-specific book; the volume most often cited
  in GMS-affiliated coach discussion as "the McGown book"

**Scope (per publisher description and coach-community references):**
- Sport-science principles applied to everyday coaching situations
- Motor-learning research base for skill acquisition
- McGown enlists "the aid of sport science experts" per publisher copy
- The bridge from his 1971-1978 doctoral work on motor set / sensory set / reaction
  time into volleyball-specific coaching practice — the academic-to-practical
  translation step the GMS methodology has always rested on

**Why this is "the GMS canonical text":**
- Coach testimonials at the 2017 GMS tribute page cite *Coaching Volleyball*
  (probably referencing this book) as a transformative read: "After reading his book,
  I took with me the philosophies of teaching and learning and applied it to not only
  my volleyball coaching, but to my teaching in my Physical Education classes." A
  Winnipeg coach reported applying these philosophies for 18 years.
- The specificity-of-training, random-vs-blocked-practice, external-focus-cueing,
  and feedback-as-scarce-resource arguments that the GMS website article ("How to
  Structure Volleyball Practice for Maximum Improvement," already on file as
  `gms-nd-structure-practice`) operationalizes are direct extensions of *Science of
  Coaching Volleyball*'s thesis.

### 2. *Complete Volleyball for Coaches* (1995) — co-authored

- **Co-author:** Hilda Fronske (Utah State, EdD)
- **Year:** 1995
- **Publisher:** Brown & Benchmark (per Open Library catalog data)
- **Status:** Open Library lists; less referenced in current coach-community discussion
  than the 1994 solo volume or the 2000 Allyn & Bacon textbook
- **Relationship to other titles:** Often appears as a precursor or companion to the
  expanded 2000 textbook with Fronske + Moser

### 3. *Coaching Volleyball: Building a Winning Team* (2000) — comprehensive textbook

- **Co-authors:** Hilda A. Fronske (EdD) + Launa Moser
- **Year:** 2000
- **Publisher:** Allyn & Bacon
- **ISBN:** 0205309585 / 9780205309580
- **Format:** Paperback
- **Status:** McGown's most comprehensive coaching textbook; produced for a college
  coursebook market (Allyn & Bacon being a major academic textbook publisher of the
  era)
- **Likely brief target:** With co-authors and the "comprehensive volleyball foundation"
  scope, this is the book the Task B.9 brief title "*Volleyball: Foundations for
  Coaches*" most plausibly intended

**Scope (per cataloging metadata):**
- Coaching theory + skill instruction + program management + practice design
- Designed for collegiate physical-education coursework as well as practicing coaches
- Multi-author treatment (3 authors) suggests broader topical coverage than the
  1994 solo McGown methodology text

## McGown's other foundational publications (non-book)

### "Motor Learning Principles and the Superiority of Whole Training in Volleyball"
**(AVCA journal, peer-reviewed)** — co-authored with Dr. Steve Bain.

Per the GMS 2017 tribute page (`gms-2017-thank-you-carl-mcgown`), this AVCA piece
generated more interest, enthusiasm, and debate "than all of [Bain's] other scientific
endeavors combined." It was framed as a rebuttal to earlier AVCA guidance favoring
part-training methodology, and is the methodology spine that GMS still teaches —
whole-skill training in game-like conditions rather than isolated part-practice.

This article does not appear on Open Library (which catalogs books, not articles) but
is referenced in multiple GMS-affiliated sources as a cornerstone methodology paper.
The wiki already cites it on `wiki/coaches/carl-mcgown.md` per the GMS 2017 tribute
page citation. Date appears to be circa 2010 per coach-profile mentions but should be
confirmed.

### Doctoral research (1971-1978)
The three editions of "The effect of motor set, sensory set, and natural set on
reaction time at different levels of auditory intensity" (Open Library lists 1971,
1972, 1978) are McGown's PhD-era work at Brigham Young University in motor-learning
science. These are the academic foundation his volleyball coaching was built on, and
the ethos his books extend into volleyball practice.

## Documented teaching positions (already on file in coach profile)

These are reproduced here for composite-cluster completeness; the full citation
trail lives in `wiki/coaches/carl-mcgown.md`:

1. **Motor-learning science over opinion.** Coaching has empirically answerable
   questions; a coach's job is to do the reading.
2. **Specificity of training.** Volleyball skills are task-specific; transfer from
   general-athletic drills to volleyball performance is weak; practice volleyball
   in volleyball-like conditions.
3. **Random / variable practice over blocked practice.** Move to game-speed,
   variable conditions as fast as the learner's challenge point allows.
4. **Reading-and-reaction over prescriptive movement.** "First we have to teach
   them how to move. Then, we need to teach them how to see."
5. **External-focus cues and simplified skill language.** Movement-effect cues,
   analogies ("cracking a whip"); deliberately small drill library.
6. **Intentional feedback, not reflexive feedback.** Feedback as a scarce learning
   resource; develop athlete self-evaluation.
7. **Training efficiency and "comfort with discomfort."** Practice hours either
   move skill forward or are misallocated.

## Key institutional context

- McGown died December 30, 2016, at age 79
- AVCA Hall of Fame 2011; James E. Coleman National Team Award 2014
- GMS founded 1985 with Marv Dunphy and Doug Beal (Jim McLaughlin joined later)
- Seven consecutive Olympics on the USA Men's NT staff (1984-2008); seven
  consecutive FIVB World Championships (1974-2002)
- BYU head coach 13 seasons, 225-137 record, 2 NCAA men's titles (1999, 2001),
  AVCA Coach of the Year both title seasons
