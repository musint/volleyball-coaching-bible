# Hugh McCutcheon — Recent Coaching Writings & Speaking (2022 → 2025)

## Why this note exists

The Bible already has a dedicated source page for *Championship Behaviors*
(2022, Triumph Books). McCutcheon has **not published a second book** as of
April 2026. He is, however, the single most-cited modern US-volleyball coaching
voice on the podcast and speaking circuit. This composite captures the **post-
2022 corpus** — his FIVB role, recurring podcast theses, and speaking-circuit
material — so wiki pages can cite a second McCutcheon source where citing the
book again would be redundant.

The conceptual through-line across the post-book corpus is consistent:
**deliberate practice with intentional design, skill acquisition + skill
application as a paired requirement, transfer as the central test of any
drill**. The book made the argument; the post-book corpus refines and
operationalizes it.

## Career update (2022 → 2025)

- **Sept 2022:** stepped down as Minnesota head coach, transitioned to
  **Assistant Athletics Director / Sport Development Coach** at the
  University of Minnesota.
- **Nov 2022:** *Championship Behaviors* published.
- **2023:** joined FIVB as **Senior Advisor**.
- **November 2024:** elected **Secretary General of the FIVB** at the 39th
  World Congress in Porto, Portugal. (Source: Star Tribune; FIVB World
  Congress release; FIVB CV PDF dated Dec 2024.)

The FIVB Secretary General role is global-game governance, not coaching — but
the appointment matters for the wiki because it makes McCutcheon's writings
on coach development institutionally significant rather than just personal.

## Recurring podcast theses (2023 corpus)

### GAINcast Episode 275 (HMMR Media, October 2023)

The most coaching-substantive of the post-book interviews. Six themes
McCutcheon develops:

1. **Time as the binding constraint (≈11:15).** "Time is the most difficult
   constraint that we have to manage." Practice hours are finite; intentional
   design beats volume.
2. **Skill acquisition AND skill application (≈11:15).** Coaches must train
   both — isolated drills for acquisition, game-realistic variability for
   application. Acquisition without application doesn't transfer.
3. **Fundamental mastery at the elite level (≈14:00).** "Everybody at the
   highest levels has the physical tools to be great. So it comes down to
   fundamental mastery" — replicating skills under pressure.
4. **Against controlled-input practice (≈17:15).** "Coaches tend to want to
   operate with controlled inputs leading to controlled outputs… But it's not
   the game. It's not controlled." Realistic variability is non-negotiable.
5. **Practice ≠ exercise (≈17:15).** "Practicing without intention is working
   out and you can do that on a treadmill down at the gym."
6. **Rules vs. principles for young coaches (≈38:00).** "Lots of rules
   produce rule followers… we're controlling, we're not empowering." Young
   coaches should develop guiding principles grounded in truth, not tradition.

He also touches on **gendered approach defaults (≈33:30)**: male athletes
"from a position of ego" need vulnerability work; female athletes "from a
position of insecurity" often need confidence reinforcement. These are
*defaults to test against*, not stereotypes to apply uniformly — McCutcheon
explicitly frames them as starting hypotheses.

### Buy-in & rationale (≈36:45)

A recurring McCutcheon line in the GAINcast interview and elsewhere: coaches
must "help the athlete understand why the change is going to improve their
performance." Behavior change without rationale produces compliance, not
ownership.

### Other 2023 podcast appearances

- **The Organizational Health Advantage S2E1 (March 2023):** organizational-

- **Changing the Game Project #315 (2023):** youth-coaching-focused; book

## TEDx Fargo: "Championship Behaviors"

YouTube-archived TEDx talk, condensed-public-speaker version of the book
thesis. The TEDx is the cleanest single-source citation for the
"championship results require championship behaviors" line and the
work/learn/compete + teach/coach/mentor split.

## Speaking-circuit positioning

McCutcheon's championshipbehaviors.com About page lists him as available for
**corporate keynote and clinic speaking** on:
- Deliberate coaching
- Championship behaviors
- Sport leadership and team culture
- Olympic high-performance coaching

He is not running a recurring column or written-content product — the post-
book footprint is **podcast appearances + speaking + book sales + FIVB**, not
serial article publication.

## Reception of the post-book corpus

- Terry Pettit's review (terrypettit.com) emphasizes McCutcheon's distinction
  between **process-oriented and outcome-oriented coaching** as the book's
  most useful contribution to the volleyball-coaching literature.
- VolleyballMag treats *Championship Behaviors* as the modern US-coaching
  reference text alongside Hebert's *Thinking Volleyball*.
- The combined book-plus-corpus position McCutcheon as the central voice
  bridging **motor-learning research and team-culture frameworks** in
  contemporary US volleyball.

## What the corpus is NOT

- **Not a second book.** No follow-up volume has been announced.
- **Not a written-article series.** No regular column at AVCA, AOC, GMS,
  Triumph Books, or FIVB.
- **Not new methodology.** The post-book corpus refines and applies the book
  thesis; it does not introduce a new framework.
