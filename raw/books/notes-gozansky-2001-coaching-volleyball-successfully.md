# Sue Gozansky — Coaching Volleyball Successfully (composite book note, 2001) — Book Notes

**TITLE / PUBLISHER CORRECTION**: The plan slug is
`notes-gozansky-2001-coaching-volleyball-successfully`, with publisher noted as "Human Kinetics."
Research confirms:

- Sue Gozansky did NOT publish a Human Kinetics book in the *Coaching Successfully* series. Human
  Kinetics' *Coaching Volleyball Successfully* line covers other authors.
- Gozansky's two canonical books are:
  1. **Championship Volleyball: Complete Book of Techniques and Drills** (Parker / Prentice Hall,
     1983/2nd-ed; ISBN 0131276212) — drills compendium, 296 pp.
  2. **Volleyball Coach's Survival Guide: Practical Techniques and Materials for Building an
     Effective Program and a Winning Team** (Parker Publishing, Paramus NJ, 2001; ISBN 0130207578;
     344 pp.) — adopted as USAV CAP Level II textbook.

Slug retained for plan consistency. This note treats Gozansky's *2001 coaching corpus* — primarily
the **Volleyball Coach's Survival Guide (2001)**, supplemented by *Championship Volleyball
Techniques and Drills* — as the substantive "coaching volleyball successfully" body of work.

## Author Bio — Sue Gozansky
- **39 seasons** as head coach, **UC Riverside women's volleyball** (1970–2009)
- **674–498–16 career record** (as of retirement)
- **Three national championships**:
  - AIAW Small College Championship (1977)
  - NCAA Division II National Championships (1982, 1986)
- **20 consecutive NCAA tournament appearances**
- **CCAA Coach of the Year**: 5× (1981, 1982, 1988, 1989, 1996)
- **AVCA Hall of Fame** inducted 2006 (Final Four, Omaha)
- **UC Riverside Athletics Hall of Fame** (2011)
- **Riverside Sport Hall of Fame**
- **Southern California Indoor Volleyball Hall of Fame** — Lifetime Service Award
- International coaching instructor for **FIVB and USA Volleyball** — 100+ clinics in 35+
  countries (Germany, Spain, Dominica, Dominican Republic, China, Belize, etc.)
- Cal Poly Pomona — multi-sport athlete (volleyball, basketball, badminton, softball, tennis,
  track), back-to-back athlete-of-the-year; B.A. PE/social sciences, 1968.

## Volleyball Coach's Survival Guide (2001)
- **Publisher**: Parker Publishing (Paramus, NJ)
- **ISBN**: 0130207578
- **Pages**: 344 (xiii + 344)
- **Format**: 28 cm hardcover; practical-reference handbook
- **Adoption**: textbook for the **USA Volleyball Coaching Accreditation Program (CAP) Level II
  Course** (2001-era CAP, prior to 2024 reorganization into the USAV Coach Academy).
- **Subtitle / scope**: "Practical Techniques and Materials for Building an Effective Program and
  a Winning Team."

### Theme & approach
A **program-management** handbook rather than a drills compendium. Pairs Gozansky's tactical
expertise with administrative/operational coaching tools — practice planning templates,
season-management schedules, scouting forms, parent/team-handbook templates, and code-of-conduct
frameworks. The "Survival Guide" framing is literal: it codifies the off-court coaching workload
that determines whether a program persists past one or two seasons.

### Key topic areas (synthesized from CAP-syllabus alignment + reviewer summaries)
- Coaching philosophy and program vision
- Practice planning and seasonal periodization
- Team-management materials (handbooks, codes of conduct, parent communication templates)
- Practical techniques for the six skills
- Drills, scrimmage formats, scouting and statistics
- Building team chemistry and team-concept culture
- Coach career development and professional growth

## Championship Volleyball: Complete Book of Techniques and Drills
- **Publisher**: Parker Publishing / Prentice Hall (later editions)
- **ISBN**: 0131276212
- **Reputation**: top-selling drills compendium of its era; widely cited in collegiate-level
  practice-design references throughout the 1990s–2000s.
- Companion to the *Survival Guide* — *Techniques and Drills* is the on-court playbook; *Survival
  Guide* is the program-management workbook. Together they form a complete coaching reference.

## Coaching Philosophy & Teaching Principles (from interviews + clinic record)
- **Team concept first.** Gozansky's signature emphasis — the Volleyball Coaching Wizards podcast
  episode is titled "Developing the Team Concept" — frames team-building strategy as a coaching
  deliverable, not an emergent property.
- **Coach career progression as a discipline.** Treats coaching itself as a developable skill set
  with explicit growth phases.
- **Knowledge sharing.** 100+ international clinics in 35+ countries; Gozansky operates as an
  educator-of-coaches as much as a head coach.
- **Player-relationship management.** Explicit coaching attention to coach-athlete relationship
  quality as a performance variable.
- **Strategic implementation of training planning.** Periodization and structured seasonal
  planning rather than week-to-week practice improvisation.

## Significance for the Wiki
- **CAP-era authoritative anchor.** The Survival Guide's adoption as USAV CAP Level II textbook
  makes it the definitive 2001-era cite for program-management, season-planning, and
  practice-planning hubs.
- **Female head-coach voice.** Among the small set of book-length female head-coach references in
  the volleyball coaching canon (alongside Reynaud, Mary Wise, Russ Rose's contemporaries).
- **Pre-rally-scoring perspective.** 2001 publication date precedes the 2003 NCAA rally-scoring
  conversion; useful as a primary source for understanding what "championship volleyball" thinking
  looked like in the side-out-scoring era.
- **Career duration.** 39 seasons makes Gozansky one of the longest-tenured collegiate head
  coaches in the AVCA Hall of Fame; cross-references to longevity / program-sustainability
  themes.
