# Mary Wise — Coaching Writings & Public Material (1998 → 2025)

## Why this note exists

Mary Wise retired Feb 6, 2025 after 34 seasons at Florida (987-150, .868). She
is the **winningest female head coach in Division I women's volleyball
history** with 1,068 total wins (counting Iowa State 1981-1984). The Bible
already has the Wise *Character and Leadership* leadership profile and the
Wikipedia bio captured. **What was missing was the book-publication footprint.**
This note captures it: she has co-edited one Human Kinetics book, contributed
a chapter to another, and produced an instructional video. No memoir.

## Published works (verified)

### *Volleyball Drills for Champions: Mastering Key Skills with 7 Winning Coaches* (1998, Human Kinetics)

- **Editor:** Mary Wise (then in year 8 of her Florida tenure).
- **Format:** 192 pages, paperback. ISBN 9780880117784.
- **Structure:** seven chapters mapped to the six core skills + drill-design
  principles, with **one contributing coach per skill**:
  - **Serving** — Russ Rose (Penn State)
  - **Passing** — Lisa Love (USC)
  - **Setting** — John Dunning (Pacific, later Stanford)
  - **Attacking** — Brad Saindon (Australia National Team)
  - **Blocking** — Greg Giovanazzi (Michigan)
  - **Digging** — Jim Stone (Ohio State)
  - **Drill design / editorial framing** — Mary Wise (Florida)
- **Drill format (Wise's editorial spec):** Purpose / Procedure / Key Points /
  Variations / Equipment Needed. ~73 drills total; ~12 per primary-skill
  chapter.
- **Editorial significance:** at the time of publication this was one of the
  first multi-coach NCAA-D1 drill anthologies; Wise's editorial choice to pull
  drills from seven different programs (rather than write a single-program
  book) is consistent with her later "find your superpower" philosophy.

### *The Volleyball Coaching Bible* Vol 1 (2002, Human Kinetics; Shondell + Reynaud editors)

- Wise contributed **Chapter 13: Serving**. (Note: the same chapter authorship
  is credited as "Serving" in the ToC; some external lists rename it
  "Receiving Serves" — that chapter is actually Steve Shondell's Chapter 14.
  Wise = serving chapter.)
- Single-skill specialist authorship. Detailed contents are part of the Vol 1
  source page (`notes-liskevych-2002-volleyball-coaching-bible-v1`).

### *Foundations for Successful Volleyball* (1999, instructional video)

Listed by Wikipedia and ESPN press-room bio. Production-era VHS/DVD instructional
material. Not currently in raw/.

## What does NOT exist

- **No memoir.** As of April 2026, no Wise-authored memoir, autobiography, or
  retrospective book has been announced or published.
- **No regular column.** Wise has not run a recurring AVCA, AOC, GMS, or
  VolleyballMag column.
- **No academic publications** of note.

The retirement coverage (Feb 2025) and AVCA Hall of Fame material together
function as the **de facto memoir** — direct quotes from Wise on her
philosophy, her relationships approach, and her career assessment.

## Career résumé (clean)

- **Player:** Purdue setter, 1977-1980.
- **Iowa State head coach:** 1981-1984 (81-63 record).
- **Kentucky assistant:** 1986-1990.
- **Florida head coach:** 1991-2024, **34 seasons** (987-150, .868;
  conference 522-57, .902).
- **NCAA Final Fours:** 8 (1992, 1993, 1996, 1997, 1998, 2002, 2003, 2017).
- **NCAA Championship runner-up:** 2003, 2017.
- **SEC regular-season titles:** 25 (most by any SEC coach in any sport).
- **AVCA National Coach of the Year:** 3× (1992, 1996, 2017).
- **SEC Coach of the Year:** 13×.
- **AVCA "All-Time Great Coach":** 2006.
- **Former AVCA president.**
- **2003 record streak:** 105 consecutive wins during the season — then an
  NCAA record.

  stretch.

## Coaching philosophy (composite from retirement-era sources + Character & Leadership profile)

Wise's coaching philosophy is well-articulated by direct quotes from the
retirement coverage and the Character and Leadership profile. Six themes
recur:

### 1. Relationships are the answer

> "The answers are always in relationships."

This is Wise's most-quoted single line and the organizing principle of her
Florida program. She has reframed it across decades — from relationships with
players, to relationships with parents, to relationships with assistants, to
relationships with the broader Florida-volleyball community.

### 2. Find each player's superpower

> "Help every player find and hone their superpower."

A differentiation principle, not a uniformity principle. Wise's recruiting
and development tradition treats every roster spot as a unique developmental
project rather than a standardized funnel.

### 3. Shared ownership

> "It's your team — what do you expect?"

The mantra Wise uses to push players into co-authorship of standards. Pairs
with open-door access (players bring non-volleyball issues to her office)
and "family meeting" framing for team business.

### 4. Competitive intensity + genuine care are NOT in tension

A recurring observation from Florida assistants and players: Wise is
simultaneously "competitive, fiery, passionate" AND deeply caring. She
explicitly rejects the dichotomy between hard-driving and player-first
coaching styles.

### 5. Family meetings, not team meetings

Wise frames team gatherings as **family meetings** — emphasizing continuity
beyond graduation. Florida alumnae return to practices and team events at
unusually high rates compared to peer programs.

### 6. Measure success by impact on lives, not win totals

A retirement-era theme: Wise has explicitly framed her career assessment in
terms of player life trajectories rather than the 987-150 record. The career
record is mentioned almost as a footnote in her own retirement statement.

## Reception and context

- The 987-150 Florida record is the **highest-winning percentage in Division I
  women's volleyball coaching history among coaches with 1,000+ matches**.
- Florida's 34 consecutive NCAA Tournament appearances under Wise is the
  longest active streak in Division I women's volleyball at her retirement.
- AVCA Hall of Fame induction: Wise is in the AVCA Hall of Fame; specific year
  to be confirmed against AVCA records.
- The retirement coverage (Feb 6-7, 2025) is the **best single source for
