# Vôlei: Aprendendo a Jogar + Pensar Bem... Sai Bem — Bernardinho

## Publication overview

This citation key covers **two earlier Bernardinho books** that predate
his major 2006 leadership volume *Transformando Suor em Ouro* (already
ingested separately as `bernardinho-2006-transformando-suor-em-ouro`).
The two earlier titles operate in different genres and are bundled here
because they share a publication context — Bernardinho's late-1990s /
early-2000s Brazilian-public profile after taking over the Brazil men's
NT in 2001.

- **Vôlei: Aprendendo a Jogar** ("Volleyball: Learning to Play") —
  approx. 2000. A volleyball-instruction book aimed at Brazilian
  beginning-to-intermediate players and youth coaches. Typical content
  for the genre includes basic passing/setting/hitting/serving
  technique progressions, court positions and rotations, basic
  6-2/5-1/4-2 systems, and youth-coaching priorities. The book is the
  Brazilian counterpart to USA's *Volleyball: Steps to Success*
  (Pauley) format. Specific publisher, ISBN, and year [unsourced]
  pending Brazilian-retailer fetch.
- **Pensar Bem... Sai Bem** ("Think Well... Come Out Well") —
  approx. 2000s. A leadership/mindset shorter volume in the same
  Brazilian motivational-business genre as *Transformando Suor em Ouro*
  but predating it. Specific publisher, ISBN, and year [unsourced].
  The title plays on Bernardinho's signature framing that mental
  preparation drives competitive performance — "thinking well" → "coming
  out well" in pressure moments.

## Themes (extrapolated from Bernardinho biographical context)

### Vôlei: Aprendendo a Jogar (technique manual)

Inferred content based on the genre and Bernardinho's known coaching
emphases at the time of writing:

- **Brazilian fundamental-skills sequencing.** The Brazilian men's
  program of the 1990s–2000s prioritized fundamentals (passing,
  setting, defensive footwork) before tactical complexity. This book
  is expected to articulate that progression for youth coaches
  [unsourced].
- **Speed-first offensive tempo.** The Brazilian-school identity
  pillar (per [[brazilian-school]] page) is faster middle-attack tempo
  than other national programs. Beginner-level introduction to tempo
  systems is expected here [unsourced].
- **6-2 and 5-1 system introductions.** Standard youth-volleyball
  curriculum structure for Brazilian club coaches in the 2000s
  [unsourced].
- **Position-skill expectations.** OH/MB/setter/libero role
  introductions at the youth level [unsourced].

### Pensar Bem... Sai Bem (leadership/mindset)

Inferred content based on Bernardinho's well-documented teachings:

- **Mental preparation precedes physical execution.** The book's title
  encodes the through-line — disciplined thinking under pressure is the
  precondition for performing well. Anchors with the Wheel-of-Excellence
  pressure-management dimension formalized later in *Transformando Suor
  em Ouro* (2006) [unsourced].
- **Pressure as trainable.** Composure under match-point conditions is
  framed as a discipline rather than a temperament trait — the same
  framing the 2006 book formalizes [unsourced].
- **Athlete mindset for non-athletes.** The book is read in Brazilian
  business contexts as a leadership primer aimed beyond the volleyball
  audience — a precursor to the broader cross-domain framing of
  *Transformando Suor em Ouro* [unsourced].
- **Commitment, complicity, discipline.** The three-word team-culture
  shorthand documented in the 2006 book likely originates or is
  prefigured in *Pensar Bem... Sai Bem* [unsourced].
