# Chasing Greatness (fair-use notes)

NOTE: Fair-use notes compiled from the Art of Coaching Volleyball store page for Kiraly's book *Chasing Greatness*. No book body text is reproduced; only publisher-facing description and the chapter list, both public.

## Bibliographic details
- Title: *Chasing Greatness*
- Author: Karch Kiraly, with Don Patterson (co-author; executive editor of Volleyball Magazine)
- Publisher: Art of Coaching Volleyball / Kiraly self-published via AOC (per AOC store)
- ISBN: 978-1-7347652-9-8
- Format: Paperback, 190 pages, 8 × 5.25 × 0.6 in
- Price: $29.99
- Publication: referenced as 2023 in Wikipedia bibliography for Kiraly.

## Publisher description
"A blueprint for self-improvement by the head coach of the U.S. women's 2021 Olympic gold medal team. The book explores methods for unlocking potential in sports and life, applicable to individuals of any age or skill level."

## Core themes
Purposeful practice; adversity management; willpower development; time optimization; self-confidence building; teamwork; understanding relative versus absolute greatness.

## Target audience
Coaches, players, business professionals, parents, and anyone seeking personal improvement.

## Reception
AOC store: 4.83/5 stars, 6 reviews. Praise centers on humility, practical insights, accessibility for coaches at all experience levels.

## Why this source matters for the wiki
- *Chasing Greatness* is the book-length articulation of the USAWNT learning-culture and purposeful-practice philosophy Kiraly has espoused in shorter form across AOCVB articles (see `karch-2019-smarter-training-better-volleyball.md`, `karch-2020-we-are-all-learners.md`). It consolidates the mental/developmental side of his coaching system, complementing the more technical material in his 1990/1996 book *Championship Volleyball*.
- Of particular interest for the wiki: chapters on Practicing Purposefully, Embracing Adversity, Being a Good Teammate, and Managing Less Productive Thoughts — these align directly with teachable-moment themes that appear in Kiraly interviews.
