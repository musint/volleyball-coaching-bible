## Premise (from publisher page)

"Championship results require championship behaviors — it's as simple as that." McCutcheon proposes a framework for competitive excellence grounded in motor learning, psychology, and decades of coaching experience.

Quoted from publisher site:
> "We won't always have five-star talent, but we can often make up the difference by being five-star teachers, learners, and competitors."

## Dual responsibility framework

The book builds on a split of responsibilities between athletes and coaches:
- Athletes must **work, learn, and compete**.
- Coaches must **teach, coach, and mentor**.

(This same language is echoed on the AVCA resource landing page for McCutcheon's Championship Behaviors seminar.)

## Chapter structure (per third-party review at coachingvb.com)

- Goal setting
- Motivation and mindset

- **The Championship Model for Skill Acquisition and Application** — described by reviewer as ~100 pages, "over 40% of the book"; reviewer notes it could have been subdivided for easier reading.

Each chapter ends with player interviews that provide a practitioner perspective; reviewer notes the book includes "former players who discuss how they initially questioned McCutcheon's process before experiencing success."

## Core ideas (as reported by review and publisher)

- **Holistic integration.** Technical, physical, mental, and team development should be integrated rather than siloed.
- **Pattern recognition over randomness.** Athletes should be taught to evaluate competitive situations and reduce uncertainty by recognizing patterns, not treating every rally as a random event.
- **Practice-to-competition transfer.** A central, repeated question: do your practice activities actually transfer to what competition demands? Effectiveness is emphasized over tradition.
- **Questioning the all-powerful coach.** Amazon/publisher copy reports the book "questions the notion of the all-powerful coach, debunks antiquated ideas around gender, discusses handling disruptive players with empathy, and emphasizes the mental and physical health of players and the learning opportunities that stem from failure."
- **Failure as information.** Learning from failure is highlighted as a core theme.
