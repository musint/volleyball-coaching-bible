## Scope per publisher description
From Simon & Schuster / Goodreads / Amazon descriptions:
- Described as a comprehensive skills course covering both indoor and beach volleyball.
- Intended for players at multiple levels, presented from the perspective of Kiraly as captain of the 1984 and 1988 gold-medal US men's indoor teams.
- Coverage spans:
  - Fundamental techniques (passing, setting, attacking, serving, blocking, digging)
  - Team strategy and plays
  - Training and workout routines
  - Coaching principles and team management
  - Indoor-vs-beach considerations ("On and Off the Beach")

## Why this source matters for the wiki
- This is Kiraly's earliest full-length coaching/technique book, predating his women's NT coaching tenure by ~16 years. It captures his technical vocabulary and his bridge between indoor and beach volleyball — a thread that runs through the rest of his corpus (see *Beach Volleyball* 1999, and his later AOCVB/USA Volleyball content).
- Of particular interest: the book is one of the earliest public sources tying fundamental forearm-pass and platform mechanics to Kiraly's name, years before the Art of Coaching Volleyball video series formalized his "4 keys to the forearm pass."
