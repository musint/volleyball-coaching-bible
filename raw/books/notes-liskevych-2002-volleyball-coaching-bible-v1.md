# The Volleyball Coaching Bible (Vol 1) — Shondell + Reynaud (eds.), 2002

## Publication

- **Title:** The Volleyball Coaching Bible
- **Editors:** Donald S. Shondell + Cecile Reynaud (in association with the
  American Volleyball Coaches Association — AVCA)
- **Publisher:** Human Kinetics
- **Year:** 2002 (July 15 release)
- **ISBN-13:** 9780736039673
- **Pages:** 369 (xiii intro + 369 main)
- **Edition note:** sometimes cataloged as 384 pages (paperback formats
  differ); 369 is the Internet Archive scan length

This is the **earlier** of the two AVCA "Coaching Bible" anthologies. Vol 2
followed in 2014 (also Shondell + Reynaud eds.) and is being ingested in
parallel by Task B.8 of the same plan as `notes-shondell-2014-volleyball-
coaching-bible-v2`. Vol 1 is foundational US-NCAA coaching literature; Vol 2
updates and broadens it.

## Editorship clarification (read me)

The plan brief (Task B.12) attributed Vol 1 editorship to **Taras Liskevych**.
That attribution is wrong. Liskevych is a **Chapter 21 contributor** to Vol 1
("Dominating Defensive Systems"), not the editor. **Both volumes of *The
Volleyball Coaching Bible* are edited by Don Shondell and Cecile Reynaud.**
This source page reflects the corrected editorship in frontmatter while
keeping the planned slug for commit-history continuity.

## Why this anthology matters to the wiki

Vol 1 is the **single best snapshot of US-NCAA coaching consensus circa
2002**. It captures:

- **Russ Rose** on practice planning (Chapter 9) — the original published
  Penn State practice-design framework.
- **Mike Hebert** on principles and goal-setting (Chapter 2) — the precursor
  to *Thinking Volleyball* (2013).
- **Doug Beal** on excellence (Chapter 4) — gold-medal-coach reflection on
  program-building from a national-team lens.
- **John Dunning** on college-program development (Chapter 6) — Pacific-era
  Dunning before the Stanford championships.
- **Mary Wise** on serving (Chapter 13) — Wise's only stand-alone published
  technique chapter.
- **Taras Liskevych** on defensive systems (Chapter 21) — the canonical
  Liskevych defensive-systems treatment in print form.
- **Mary Jo Peppler** on teaching technique (Chapter 10) — Peppler is one of
  the canonical women's-volleyball coaching voices and this is one of her
  rare published chapters.
- **Bill Neville** on drill design (Chapter 11) — drill-construction
  principles that underlie the 1990s-2000s US drill tradition.

## Key claims / ideas (composite, by chapter)

### Coaching fundamentals (Chapters 1-12)

- **Pavlik (Ch. 1):** the coach's responsibility extends beyond performance
  to character development of athletes. Modeling behavior is central.
- **Hebert (Ch. 2):** principles must precede tactics; goal-setting is a
  professional discipline, not a motivational gesture. (See `notes-hebert-
  thinking-volleyball-2013` for the expanded 2013 treatment.)
- **Braden (Ch. 3):** coaching has emotional cycles; sustainability requires
  intentional practice of finding direction during plateaus and losses.
- **Beal (Ch. 4):** excellence is a system, not an event; the 1984 Olympic
  gold and the program-building work that preceded it.
- **Pettit (Ch. 5):** program promotion is a coach's job; budget visibility
  buys methodological autonomy. Pettit is canonical Nebraska-program voice.
- **Dunning (Ch. 6):** college-program development is roster, recruiting,
  scheduling, and culture. Pacific-era Dunning before Stanford.
- **D. Shondell (Ch. 7):** HS-program development with the Indiana lens.
- **Pingel (Ch. 8):** junior-club development; this is one of the earlier
  published treatments of club-program building in US volleyball.
- **Rose (Ch. 9):** practice planning at Penn State — the Penn State practice
  framework in print.
- **Peppler (Ch. 10):** teaching technique — verbal cues, demonstration
  sequencing, error correction. Foundational pedagogy.
- **Neville (Ch. 11):** making drills more beneficial — drill-design
  principles (intent, transfer, repetitions, tempo). One of the canonical
  drill-construction sources for the wiki.
- **Cook (Ch. 12):** conditioning for high performance — strength,
  conditioning, and physical-prep integration.

### Skills & strategies (Chapters 13-24)

- **Wise (Ch. 13):** serving as a strategic skill, not a starting ritual.
- **S. Shondell (Ch. 14):** serve-reception fundamentals.
- **Clemens (Ch. 15):** setting from the Washington University tradition.
- **Weishoff (Ch. 16):** attacking from a USA Olympic-medalist player-coach
  perspective.
- **Stone (Ch. 17):** digging and ball control from the Ohio State tradition.
- **Suwara (Ch. 18):** blocking — pre-swing-block era.
- **Sturm (Ch. 19) + Hanson (Ch. 20):** offensive-system construction and
  talent-based offensive design.
- **Liskevych (Ch. 21):** dominating defensive systems — Liskevych's signature
  defensive treatment in published form. Tracks closely with his AOC
  defensive-system articles (already in raw/articles/) but more comprehensive.
- **Waite (Ch. 22):** competitive edge — game-prep psychology.
- **Coleman (Ch. 23):** scouting opponents and evaluating team performance —
  pre-Data Volley scouting methodology.
- **Scates (Ch. 24):** handling game situations — Al Scates is the UCLA-
  dynasty coach, 19-time NCAA championship-winning men's coach.

## Use to the wiki

This anthology is one of the most-citable foundational sources for technique
and systems pages. Each chapter author has a coach page in the wiki (or
should). Vol 1 grounds:

- Coach pages: Hebert, Beal, Pettit, Dunning, Rose, Peppler, Neville, Cook,
  Wise, Stone, Suwara, Liskevych, Waite, Scates.
- Technique pages: serving, passing, setting, attacking, digging, blocking
  (each with a Vol-1 chapter to cite).
- System pages: offense (Sturm + Hanson), defense (Liskevych).
- Drill pages: Neville Ch. 11 is the primary drill-design citation source.

## Differences from Vol 2 (2014)

The Shondell + Reynaud (eds.) Vol 2 (2014) is a deliberate update, not a
replacement:

- Vol 2 adds **modern motor-learning frameworks** (Hebert, McGown influence).
- Vol 2 adds **video-analysis and Data Volley** treatment.
- Vol 2 adds **20+ years of NCAA-rule changes** (libero rules, rally scoring,
  net-touch rules, challenge system).
- Vol 2 broadens **non-US contributing voices** (some international coaches
  added).
- Vol 2 keeps **most original-author voices** but rotates ~30% of chapter
  authors based on who was active in 2013-2014.

For wiki purposes: **cite Vol 1 for 1990s-2000s methodology and the Penn
State / Pacific / Florida / Pacific tradition**; cite Vol 2 for 2010s
methodological updates.
