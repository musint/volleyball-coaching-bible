## Series Pedagogy ("Steps to Success" template)
Every Human Kinetics "Steps to Success" book follows the same pedagogical scaffold:
- Each "Step" introduces a new skill/concept with a clear teaching progression.
- Each Step ends with a **success-check / self-evaluation rubric** (typically point-scored
  drills) that gates progression to the next step.
- Diagrams are heavily used; the series target is the player or coach learning a sport
  systematically rather than browsing a reference.
- Each book is dual-purpose: instructional self-study text AND a curriculum scaffold for a
  PE/coaching-class instructor.

## Pedagogical Significance
The 1st edition's inclusion of a dedicated **Step 1: Posture and Movement** and a dedicated
**Step 10: Transitioning** is unusual and pedagogically deliberate — most volleyball technique
books treat both as embedded into other skills rather than as gateway competencies. Kenny +
Gregory frame ready-position athletic stance, gather steps, crossover, and skill-to-skill
transitions as a *prerequisite* skill set; players who can't move and recover can't string skills
in live play. The 2nd edition (Schmidt) collapses these back into the skill steps — a defensible
streamlining for advanced readers but arguably a step backward for beginner pedagogy.

The book is one of the few standalone player-and-coach instructional volumes in the modern
volleyball-publishing market that treats the 6-skill canon plus team systems plus out-of-system
play in a single self-evaluating progression. It is widely cited in PE-program syllabi and youth
coaching certifications.

## Key Topic Areas
- **Six fundamental skills**: serving, forearm passing, setting, attacking, blocking, digging — all
  with technique cues and drilled progressions.
- **Movement/footwork as foundation** (1st ed Step 1) — pre-skill athleticism.
- **Team offense / defense / transition** — system overlay onto fundamentals.
- **Out-of-system play** (1st ed Step 11) — a refreshingly explicit treatment of recovery-pass
  setting, free-ball downball offense, and tip/roll-shot defense.
- **Competitive volleyball player** (1st ed Step 12) — mental and competitive-readiness wrap-up.

## Review Notes
Widely adopted in collegiate PE volleyball-instruction courses and quoted in clinic syllabi.
Series-format consistency makes it predictable; coaches tend to praise the Step 1 and Step 11
inclusions in 1st ed but find the Schmidt 2nd ed "tighter for self-study" and "more current on
modern systems" particularly defensive rotations and serve-receive.
