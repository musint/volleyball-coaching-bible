# Thinking Volleyball (Mike Hebert, 2013) — Book Notes

**IMPORTANT DISAMBIGUATION**: This is Mike Hebert's *Thinking Volleyball* (Human Kinetics, 2013), NOT Hugh McCutcheon's *Thinking Volleyball* (2022). They share the title. Cite this one as `hebert-2013-thinking-volleyball`.

## Publisher / Bibliographic
- **Author**: Mike Hebert (Illinois, Minnesota, New Mexico, Pittsburgh)
- **Publisher**: Human Kinetics
- **Year**: 2013 (Nov 27)
- **ISBN**: 9781450442626

## Central Theme
The broad theme is "being ready, willing, and able to think beyond the conventional." This requires understanding conventional wisdom's origins, strengths, and limitations — not merely taking risks. The book emphasizes critical thinking about volleyball strategy and coaching philosophy rather than presenting drill compilations.

## Structure (10 chapters)
- Only two chapters focus on technical/tactical aspects (offense and defense).
- The remaining eight explore broader coaching dimensions: program management, organizational structure, team playing style, gym culture, trust, match coaching, team policy, goals, event management, injured-player management, communication systems.

## Key Topic Areas
- Offensive and defensive philosophy (including Hebert's signature reading-defense approach)
- Program management and organizational structure
- Developing a team's playing style
- Gym culture, team dynamics, learned optimism
- Trust-building within programs
- Match coaching approaches
- Modern statistical analysis — Hebert, initially a "stats evangelist," later questioned whether time spent gathering stats justified their value, suggesting alternative analytical approaches instead

## Reading Defense
Hebert's best-known tactical contribution. Introduces strategies behind his reading-defense approach: players read the play and posture/location based on set/hitter tendencies rather than a rigid rotational base defense.

## Author Bio
- 35-year coaching career: Pittsburgh men's/women's, New Mexico women's, Illinois women's, Minnesota women's
- Career record 952–392
- 5 Final Four appearances
- AVCA National Coach of the Year (1985)
- AVCA President (1987–1990)
- 30+ years with USA Volleyball; inducted into Minnesota and AVCA halls of fame

## Review Notes (coachingvb.com)
The reviewer notes personal anecdotes are prevalent but somewhat biased toward positive outcomes. Recommended for coaches at all levels; most readers will find at least one chapter prompting critical reflection about their coaching practices.
