# The Volleyball Coaching Bible, Volume II (AVCA / Reynaud, ed., 2015) — Book Notes

**EDITORIAL CORRECTION**: The plan slug is `notes-shondell-2014-volleyball-coaching-bible-v2`, but
research confirms Don Shondell did NOT co-edit Volume II. Volume I (2002) was edited by Don
Shondell + Cecile Reynaud. Volume II (2015) is edited by Cecile Reynaud alone, sponsored/credited to
the **American Volleyball Coaches Association (AVCA)**. Slug retained for plan consistency; treat
this note as covering AVCA / Reynaud, ed., 2015.

- **Editor**: Cecile Reynaud (with AVCA)
- **Publisher**: Human Kinetics, Champaign, IL
- **Copyright Year**: 2015 (often listed as 2014/2015)
- **ISBN**: 9781450491983 (paperback); 9781492585275 (eBook)

- **Format**: Anthology — 20 chapters across 4 parts, each chapter authored by a different

## Editor Bio — Cecile Reynaud
- 26-year head coach at Florida State University (1976–2001)
- 635 career wins; seven conference championships
- Two-time ACC Coach of the Year
- AVCA Hall of Fame; multiple hall-of-fame inductions
- Doctorate in athletic administration; serves on volleyball boards

## Volume Lineage
- **Volume I (2002)** — Don Shondell + Cecile Reynaud, eds. (the original AVCA "coaching bible").
- **Volume II (2015)** — Reynaud, ed.; explicitly framed as a complement / successor anthology with
  20 of "the top volleyball minds in the game today." Vol II is not a revision of Vol I but a fresh
  set of contributions reflecting the post-2002 evolution of the sport.

## Central Theme & Editorial Stance
Volume II privileges **program-building, culture, and positional training** over generic
technique. The opening "Coaching Priorities" part (passion → expectations → culture → growth
mindset) frames every subsequent tactical chapter; the AVCA's editorial choice to lead with
mindset/culture before X's-and-O's is itself a statement that program-level fundamentals predict
outcomes more than tactical novelty.

## Key Topic Areas
- **Growth mindset** — Carol Dweck-aligned framing of player development (Ch. 4); contemporary
  with the surge of growth-mindset adoption in elite NCAA programs.
- **Sand/beach program startup** (Ch. 7) — captures the 2010–2015 NCAA emerging-sport explosion.
- **Position-specific training** — dedicated chapters per position (middle, outside, setter,
  libero) provide explicit progressions and drill prescriptions.
- **Deliberate practice** (Ch. 15) — Ericsson-aligned framework for purposeful repetition;
  bridges to motor-learning literature.
- **Five-year planning** (Ch. 10) — makes long-cycle strategic planning a coaching deliverable, not
  just a coordinator-level task.
- **Statistics for coaching** (Ch. 17) — practical statistical literacy without requiring a
  separate analyst; complements/contrasts Hebert's stats-skepticism in *Thinking Volleyball* (2013).
- **On-court decision making** (Ch. 20) — match-management as a teachable skill.

## Endorsements
Doug Beal (former USA Volleyball CEO; 1984 Olympic gold-medal coach) and Gabrielle Reece (pro
beach player) both blurb the volume. Beal's endorsement signals USAV-aligned credibility; Reece's,
the beach/sand crossover.

## Target Audience
Coaches at all levels — high school, college (D-I/II/III), beach, club, youth, Olympic-track.
Particularly aimed at coaches running or starting a program, where position-by-position and
program-management chapters carry direct operational value.
