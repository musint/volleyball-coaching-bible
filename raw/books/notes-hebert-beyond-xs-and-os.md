# Mike Hebert — "Beyond X's and O's" (Composite Notes)

## Important disambiguation

The Task B.9 brief named this book "Mike Hebert, *Beyond X's and O's*" and asked it to be
treated as "distinct from his *Thinking Volleyball* (2013) which is already on file." Web
research at fetch time (2026-04-24) does not surface any Mike Hebert title called *Beyond
X's and O's*. The only place that exact phrase shows up in Mike Hebert's published-work
record is the publisher description of *Thinking Volleyball* itself:

> "*Thinking Volleyball* goes beyond the Xs and Os and skill instruction by offering
> in-match strategy and insights from legendary coach Mike Hebert."
> — Human Kinetics publisher copy and Russ Rose blurb (2013)

Wikipedia's Mike Hebert entry says he "is the author of two books, including a 1993
co-written autobiography (with Dave Johnson) titled *The Fire Still Burns*" — naming only
*Fire Still Burns* explicitly, with the second unnamed book understood across multiple
catalogs to be *Thinking Volleyball* (2013). Hebert's earlier 1991 *Insights and Strategies
for Winning Volleyball* (Leisure Press) is a third book that Wikipedia did not include in
its "two books" count.

**Conclusion:** "Beyond X's and O's" is a *marketing tagline* for *Thinking Volleyball*,
not a separate book. The slug `notes-hebert-beyond-xs-and-os` is retained for cluster
continuity but the source page it grounds will document the Hebert coaching-writings
canon with *Thinking Volleyball* as the primary text and explicit clarification of the
mistitling. The pre-existing wiki source page `[[hebert-2013-thinking-volleyball]]`
already covers *Thinking Volleyball* in detail and is the canonical reference; this new
source page complements rather than duplicates it.

## Mike Hebert's full coaching-writings catalog

### 1. *Insights and Strategies for Winning Volleyball* (1991)

- **Publisher:** Leisure Press (Champaign, Illinois)
- **Year:** 1991
- **ISBN:** 0880114231 / 9780880114233 (HC); 0880115327 / 9780880115322 (later printing)
- **Page count:** 210 pages (~230 with front matter)
- **Format:** Hardcover technical-tactical coaching manual
- **Status:** Hebert's first published book; an earlier-career systems and tactics
  manual rather than the philosophical text he later produced

The Internet Archive listing confirms publisher and bibliographic details but lacks a
visible table of contents. The book was Hebert's first attempt to articulate the
tactical-systems thinking that would later mature into his *Thinking Volleyball*
philosophical reflection. Subjects per IA cataloging: "Volleyball -- Coaching."

This is the book the Task B.9 brief's "Beyond X's and O's" naming most plausibly meant,
if it intended a Hebert volume distinct from *Thinking Volleyball*. Including it here
preserves cluster-research integrity even though it is not the brief's named title.

### 2. *The Fire Still Burns* (1993, autobiography)

- **Co-author:** Dave Johnson
- **Year:** 1993
- **ISBN:** 0915611775 / 9780915611775
- **Format:** Hardcover autobiography
- **Status:** Wikipedia explicitly names this as one of Hebert's two books

**Content (per Web search summaries):**
> "In this candid autobiography, Hebert talks about the frustrations his family faced
> after his father was declared MIA during the Korean War, about his personal
> activities as an anti-war protestor and Peace Corps volunteer in Nigeria during
> the Vietnam War, and of the difficulties he and his children faced as a result of
> his bi-racial first marriage."

The autobiographical material is biographical and contextual rather than a
methodological volleyball text — relevant to the wiki's Hebert profile (already on
file at `wiki/coaches/mike-hebert.md`) but not a source of new tactical or training
methodology. The 1993 publication date sits between Hebert's 1991 *Insights* and
his 2013 *Thinking Volleyball*, capturing the mid-Illinois-tenure period when he
was AVCA President and won the 1985 AVCA National Coach of the Year.

### 3. *Thinking Volleyball* (2013) — the "Beyond X's and O's" volume

- **Publisher:** Human Kinetics
- **Year:** December 2, 2013
- **ISBN:** 1450442625 / 9781450442626
- **Page count:** 280 pages
- **Status:** Hebert's culminating coaching statement; the book the Task B.9 brief
  most plausibly intended under the "Beyond X's and O's" label
- **Already covered in:** `wiki/sources/hebert-2013-thinking-volleyball.md` — the
  canonical wiki source page for this book
- **Already raw'd in:** `raw/books/notes-hebert-thinking-volleyball-2013.md`

**Why "Beyond X's and O's" is a tagline for this book specifically:**
- Publisher description: "*Thinking Volleyball* goes beyond the Xs and Os..."
- Of 10 chapters, only 2 are tactical (offense, defense); 8 are program management,
  organizational structure, gym culture, trust, match coaching, team policy, goals,
  event management, injured-player management, and communication systems
- The book's organizing thesis ("ready, willing, and able to think beyond the
  conventional") is itself the "beyond X's and O's" thesis

### Editorial blurbs and reception (additional material not yet on the existing source page)

**Russ Rose (Penn State, AVCA Hall of Fame head coach):**
> "*Thinking Volleyball* is a must-read. Coaches will benefit from Mike Hebert's
> years of experience and success in building programs at every level."
[hebert-thinking-volleyball-publisher-blurbs]

**Kelly Sheffield (Wisconsin head coach, AOC contributor):**
> "Others that have written analysis have not come as close to the essence of
> coaching women's volleyball as Mike has in *Thinking Volleyball*. The chapters
> on gym culture and trust are remarkable. Unlike other volleyball coaching books
> of its kind, it digs far deeper, and comes very close to the heart of coaching
> excellence."
[hebert-thinking-volleyball-publisher-blurbs]

The Sheffield blurb is the strongest contemporary peer endorsement of the book's
non-tactical chapters (gym culture, trust) — the chapters most often cited as the
"beyond X's and O's" content the publisher headline points to.

## Hebert death and the existing wiki record

Mike Hebert died October 21, 2019. The wiki already documents this on the coach
profile (`wiki/coaches/mike-hebert.md`) and via the AOC tribute (`aoc-2019-hebert-a-coach-who-mattered`)
and USAV obituary (`hebert-usav-obituary`). The 2013 *Thinking Volleyball* is therefore
his final published coaching statement, predating his death by six years.

## Key teaching positions captured across all three books

These are documented in the existing source page `[[hebert-2013-thinking-volleyball]]`
and in the Hebert coach profile. Reproducing here for cluster-completeness:

1. **Think beyond convention — but only after understanding why the convention
   exists.** Innovation has a prerequisite of fluency in received tradition.
2. **Program management is a first-class coaching skill.** Eight of ten *Thinking
   Volleyball* chapters are program/culture/management; only two are tactical.
3. **Reading defense over rigid base defense.** Hebert's signature tactical
   contribution; players read set/hitter tendencies and posture accordingly
   rather than playing fixed rotational positions.
4. **Gym culture and trust rival systems in program outcomes.** A well-designed
   system executed by a fractured group produces worse outcomes than an ordinary
   system executed by a cohesive group.
5. **Statistics skepticism from a former evangelist.** Hebert began as a stats
   advocate, later questioned whether time cost of detailed stat collection
   actually paid back; proposed alternative analytical approaches.
6. **Learned optimism as a coaching variable.** Hebert treats it as something a
   coach builds into the program, not as athlete personality trait.
