# Ramirez-Campillo et al. (2020) — Plyometric jump training on volleyball vertical jump (meta-analysis)

**Citation (APA).** Ramirez-Campillo, R., Andrade, D. C., Nikolaidis, P. T., Moran, J., Clemente, F. M., Chaabene, H., & Comfort, P. (2020). Effects of plyometric jump training on vertical jump height of volleyball players: A systematic review with meta-analysis of randomized-controlled trial. *Journal of Sports Science & Medicine*, 19(3), 489–499.

## Abstract (summary)

Systematic review with meta-analysis of randomized-controlled trials evaluating plyometric jump training (PJT) effects on volleyball-specific vertical jump height.

## Methods

- **Search.** PubMed, MEDLINE, Web of Science, SCOPUS through August 2019. 7,081 records → 14 RCTs included.
- **Sample pooled.** 322 participants in PJT arms across 20 experimental groups.
- **Outcome.** Pre-to-post vertical jump (countermovement jump primarily).
- **Quality.** PEDro scale 4–6 / 10 (training-research blinding ceiling).

## Key findings

- **Pooled effect size** Cohen's d = 0.82 (p < 0.001) — moderate-to-large benefit on jump height vs control.
- **No publication bias** (Egger's test p = 0.59); moderate heterogeneity (I² = 34.4%).
- **Frequency:** ≤2 sessions/week (ES = 0.83) and >2 sessions/week (ES = 0.78) **equivalent** — more is not better.
- **Volume:** <2,000 jumps total (ES = 0.79) ≈ >2,000 jumps (ES = 0.76) — lower-volume programs effective.
- **Duration:** ≤8 weeks (ES = 0.79) ≈ >8 weeks (ES = 0.87) — meaningful gains within an 8-week block.
- **Sex:** Female ES = 1.3 vs male ES = 0.5 — females demonstrated larger response in this dataset.
- **Age:** ≥19 (ES = 0.89) and <19 (ES = 0.70) both effective.
- **Safety:** zero adverse events reported across the 14 RCTs.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Lower volume and frequency are sufficient.** ~2,000 total jumps across an 8-week block at 2 sessions/week generates the bulk of available adaptation. Adding volume above this trades little gain for more injury risk.
2. **Plyometrics work at every level.** Effect is robust across age, sex, training duration, and program structure — no single moderator is decisive.
3. **Position-tailored.** Authors recommend individualized prescription (libero may tolerate higher loads than mids).
4. **Programming anchor.** PJT belongs as a structured component of the team's S&C plan, not an ad-hoc add-on.
