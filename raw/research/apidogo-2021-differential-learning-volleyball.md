# Repetition without Repetition or Differential Learning of Multiple Techniques in Volleyball?

**Authors:** Julius B. Apidogo, Johannes Burdack, Wolfgang I. Schöllhorn
**Journal:** International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health
**Year:** 2021
**Volume/Issue/Pages:** 18(19): 10499
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph181910499
**PMID:** 34639799

## Abstract (verbatim, condensed)

The study compared three approaches to acquiring three volleyball techniques in parallel. Female secondary school students (N = 42; 15.6 ± 0.54 years), absolute beginners in volleyball, were pre-tested on the underhand pass, overhand pass, and overhead serve (accuracy outcome) and parallelized into three protocols: a **repetitive learning group (RG)**, a **differential learning group (DG)**, and a **control group (CG)**. After 6 weeks / 12 intervention sessions, all participants completed a posttest, and a retention test was administered after a further 2 weeks. Statistically significant differences favored DG over RG and CG for each individual technique and for the combined three-technique score (each p < 0.001). Through differential learning — where no movement is repeated identically — the absolute-beginner adolescent girls experienced a broader spectrum of movements, allowing the neuro-motor system to adapt more efficiently across all three techniques.

## Key claims / ideas
- **Differential learning (DL)** — Schöllhorn's "noise-injection" framework where each rep is intentionally varied so no two are identical — beat repetitive learning AND a passive control on **all three** volleyball techniques tested.
- Effect held across underhand pass, overhand pass, AND overhead serve **AND** their combination — i.e., no skill was hurt by parallel practice when the practice was differential.
- Population was 42 adolescent female absolute beginners (~15.6 yr) — directly relevant to early-HS / late-club beginner ingest.
- Retention-test (2 weeks post-intervention) results were the load-bearing comparison — DG advantages held into retention, not just acquisition.
- Mechanism (per authors): broader sampling of movement solutions → better neuro-motor adaptation than repetitive grooving of a "model" technique.

## Topics covered
- Differential learning / "repetition without repetition" (Bernstein)
