# APPLICATION OF IZOF MODEL FOR ANXIETY AND SELF-EFFICACY IN VOLLEYBALL ATHLETES: A CASE STUDY

**Authors:** Antonio Nogueira-Filho et al.
**Journal:** Revista Brasileira de Medicina do Esporte
**Year:** 2019

## Abstract / study overview

The study examined the validity of the **Individual Zone of Optimal Functioning (IZOF) model** from a multidimensional perspective of anxiety with seven male professional volleyball players in Brazil. Players completed the Individual Self-Efficacy Scale for Volleyball and the Competitive State Anxiety Inventory (CSAI-2 reduced) before every match throughout a season. Athletic performance was extracted via the Data Volley scouting program at the end of each match.

## Key findings

- IZOFs differed substantially **inter-individually** (cognitive anxiety zones ranged 3-5 pts; somatic anxiety 2-7 pts; self-confidence 2-14 pts; self-efficacy 12-54 pts) — there is no universal "optimal anxiety" for volleyball.
- IZOFs also differed **intra-individually** across matches, but pre-match scores within the personalized zone strongly tracked match performance.
- Two players (Middle Blocker 1, Opposite 1) presented the cleanest IZOF profiles — variables in zone in the majority of matches — and these were also the two best-performing players over the season.
- Self-efficacy showed clear within-zone vs out-of-zone performance separation, suggesting IZOF logic extends from anxiety to self-efficacy.
- Practical implication: pre-match anxiety/confidence assessment and personalized regulation work (not blanket "calm down" coaching) is supported for elite volleyball.

## Key claims / ideas

- IZOF (Hanin) is empirically applicable to professional volleyball.
- One athlete's "optimal" can be another athlete's "in the red zone" — confirming Hanin's core thesis in this sport.
- IZOF logic extends beyond anxiety to self-efficacy.
- Coach implication: profile each starter's pre-match psychological state, don't apply uniform pre-match interventions.
