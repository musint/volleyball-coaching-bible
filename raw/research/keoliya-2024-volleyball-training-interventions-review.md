# Keoliya et al. (2024) — Volleyball training interventions: agility, power, strength

**Citation (APA).** Keoliya, A. A., Ramteke, S. U., Boob, M. A., & Somaiya, K. J. (2024). Enhancing volleyball athlete performance: A comprehensive review of training interventions and their impact on agility, explosive power, and strength. *Cureus*, 16(2), e53273. https://doi.org/10.7759/cureus.53273

## Abstract (summary)

Systematic literature review across 10 peer-reviewed intervention studies (2012–2023) evaluating which training modalities improve agility, jump performance, and strength in volleyball players. Spans plyometrics, resistance training, core stability, agility ladder / shuttle protocols, FIFA 11+ injury-prevention warm-ups, and novel modalities.

## Methods

- **Databases.** PubMed, PEDro, Semantic Scholar, Google Scholar.
- **Yield.** 186 initial → 10 included after screening.
- **Range of modalities.** Plyometrics, traditional vs cluster resistance training, core stability, agility ladder / shuttle, FIFA 11+, dance-video training, sand vs court training surfaces.

## Key findings

- **Cluster resistance training > traditional resistance training** for total-strength gains across multiple measures — short intra-set rest preserves bar speed and improves strength outcomes vs straight-set protocols.
- **Plyometrics + agility protocols both improve agility** consistently — shuttle-run, agility ladder, and core training all delivered statistically significant agility gains.
- **Sand training > court training for agility outcomes** — surface variability transfers to better COD performance on hard surfaces.
- **Core training has direct transfer** — RCT evidence of significant CMJ, vertical jump, and pro-agility improvements after isolated core programming.
- **Novel modalities show promise** — dance-video games functioned as a viable agility intervention in one trial.
- **Strength → serve-receive accuracy link** — power generation supports mid-air body stabilization needed for accurate platform and overhead control.

## Limitations noted by authors

- Small sample sizes across most included studies.
- "Low quality of evidence" overall — calls for larger, better-controlled volleyball-specific RCTs.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Cluster sets are a programming upgrade** for in-season strength maintenance — preserves bar speed and recovery while still building strength.
2. **Surface variability matters** — sand drills are not just conditioning, they're an agility-training modality.
3. **Core stability is a transferable target** for jump and agility, not just lower-back insurance.
4. **Cautious interpretation** — use review as a signal of direction, anchor specific prescriptions to the underlying RCTs.
