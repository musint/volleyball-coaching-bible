# Tavakkoli et al. (2022) — Block vs traditional periodization in volleyball

**Citation (APA).** Tavakkoli, M. J., Abbaspoor, M., & Nikooie, R. (2022). The effect of 8 weeks of block and traditional periodization training models on practical factors in volleyball players. *Scientific Journal of Sport and Performance*. https://doi.org/10.55860/YMMU8246

## Abstract (summary)

Randomized comparison of two periodization models — block periodization vs traditional (linear) periodization — on practical performance in young male volleyball players over 8 weeks of in-court training (3 sessions/week).

## Sample / setting

- **Subjects.** 13 male volleyball players, age 17 ± 0.70 years.
- **Groups.** Block periodization (n = 6) vs traditional periodization (n = 7).
- **Duration.** 8 weeks. Three court sessions per week. Pre / mid / post testing.

## Methods

- **Dependent variables.** Vertical jump, Illinois agility, pro-agility shuttle, T-agility, 300-yard shuttle (aerobic endurance), 60-second side jumps (power endurance), hand-grip strength.
- **Statistics.** Group × time ANOVA. Effect sizes reported.

## Key findings

- **No significant between-group differences** for vertical jump, agility (any test), power endurance, or aerobic endurance — both models worked equivalently well on these outcomes (p > 0.05).
- **Both groups improved significantly over 8 weeks** (p < 0.05) — the periodization choice was less important than the fact of structured training.
- **Block periodization was superior for hand-grip / forearm strength** at post-test — sequenced concentrated loading produced larger upper-body strength gains than mixed daily distribution.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Periodization choice is not the primary lever for jump and agility in adolescent males** — both block and linear models drive equivalent gains over 8 weeks. The training stimulus matters more than the scheduling philosophy at this level.
2. **Block periodization may be the better pick when upper-body / forearm strength is the priority** (e.g., jump-serving programs, attacker arm conditioning).
3. **Programming flexibility for HS / club coaches** — defaulting to a simpler traditional / linear model in-season is defensible, with block reserved for targeted strength blocks.
