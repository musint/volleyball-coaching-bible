# Palao, Ureña, Moreno & Ortega-Toro 2024 — U-14 Female Rule Modifications

## Citation

Palao JM, Ureña A, Moreno MP, Ortega-Toro E. Effect of changes in the net height, court size, and serve limitations on technical-tactical, physical, and psychological aspects of U-14 female volleyball matches. *Frontiers in Psychology*. 2024;14:1341297. https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2023.1341297

Open access via PubMed Central: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10806054/

## Sample & design

- **N:** 29 U-14 female volleyball players from 3 regional Spanish club teams.
- **Anthropometrics:** age 13.4 ± 0.68 yrs; height 1.63 ± 0.96 m; weight 55.5 ± 7.9 kg.
- **Country:** Spain.
- **Design:** Quasi-experimental three-tournament design.
- **Data:** 5,315 ball actions across 9 matches.

## Conditions

1. **Control Tournament:** Official rules — net 2.10 m, 9×9 m court, unlimited serves.
2. **Tournament 1:** Net reduced to 2.00 m, court unchanged (9×9 m), no jump serves, max 2 serves per player.
3. **Tournament 2:** Net 2.00 m, court reduced to 8×8 m, no jump serves, max 2 serves per player.

## Headline findings

**Serve-type distribution under official rules (U-14 Spanish female):**
- Jump serves: **21.5%** of total serves.
- "23% of serves" exceeded the 2-per-rotation threshold (by multiple-server players).

**Tournament 1 (net 2.00 m only):**
- Serve efficacy increased significantly.
- Reception efficacy decreased.
- Block efficacy significantly lower than Control (p<0.01).
- Defense quality improved.

**Tournament 2 (net 2.00 m + court 8×8 m + serve limits):**
- Serve efficacy decreased (mean 1.74 vs 1.97 control).
- Reception efficacy improved (mean 1.64 vs 1.46 control).
- Significantly more attacks (p<0.01, ES=0.588).
- Significantly more blocks (p<0.001).
- Side-out efficacy increased significantly.
- Game continuity: 84.55% of rallies vs 79.46% control.

## Authors' recommendation

> "Scaling the net and court and adapting the serve rules (Experimental Tournament 2) resulted in game dynamics for these U-12 teams [sic] that were more similar to those of posterior stages of player development."

The combined modifications — lower net + smaller court + serve limits — produced more balanced serve-reception dynamics, more attacks, more blocks, and more rallies. Net-only modification (Tournament 1) skewed too heavily toward serve dominance.

## Coach-relevant interpretation

For U-14 female programs, the **21.5% jump-serve uptake under official rules** is a substantial pre-puberty-finished figure — confirming that the technique enters the developmental pipeline well before senior pro years even when not roster-default. This anchors the wiki's "introduce jump-float at top-club 13s, rotation-ready at 14s" guidance with empirical U-14 female data. The rule-environment finding (lower net alone tilts toward serving; lower net + smaller court + serve limits rebalances) gives clubs design levers for developmentally appropriate intra-club competition.

## Caveats

- Spanish regional sample, n=29 — limited statistical power on subgroup effects.
- Quasi-experimental design (no random assignment).
- The "U-12 teams" reference in the recommendation is likely a typo for U-14 in the published article; methods clearly state U-14.
