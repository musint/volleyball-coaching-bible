# Evolving the validity of a mental toughness measure: Refined versions of the Mental Toughness Questionnaire-48

**Authors:** Masato Kawabata, Toby G. Pavey, Tristan J. Coulter
**Journal:** Stress and Health
**Year:** 2021

## Abstract / study overview

The original 48-item Mental Toughness Questionnaire (MTQ48), built on Clough's 4Cs model (Challenge, Commitment, Control, Confidence), is the most widely used mental-toughness instrument in sport but has long faced criticism for poor factorial validity. Kawabata and colleagues tested the original MTQ48 in two large independent samples (N=2,186 and N=3,209 university students) using confirmatory factor analysis and exploratory structural equation modeling, found that one-, four-, and six-factor models with all 48 items fit poorly, and developed two refined versions: an 18-item Short MTQ and a 6-item Very Short MTQ.

## Key findings

- The original MTQ48 fit poorly — one-, four-, and six-factor models all failed to satisfy fit criteria.
- The 18-item Short MTQ achieved excellent fit and preserves both a global MT score and the six subdimensions.
- The 6-item Very Short MTQ is suitable for time-constrained settings (in-season check-ins, brief screening).
- Both refined versions were validated across two large samples — strong cross-validation evidence.
- Implication: when measuring mental toughness in volleyball or any sport context, use Short MTQ or Very Short MTQ rather than the original 48-item version.

## Key claims / ideas

- The 4Cs model of mental toughness (Clough) survives, but the original 48-item operationalization does not.
- Refined short forms are now the recommended MT measurement tools.
- Mental toughness is a multidimensional construct; brief global scores can be defended only when validated against the full multifactor structure.
