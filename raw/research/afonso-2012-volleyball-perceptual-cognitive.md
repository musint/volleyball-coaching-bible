# The Perceptual Cognitive Processes Underpinning Skilled Performance in Volleyball: Evidence From Eye-Movements and Verbal Reports of Thinking Involving an in Situ Representative Task

**Authors:** José Afonso, Júlio Garganta, Allistair McRobert, A. Mark Williams, Isabel Mesquita
**Journal:** Journal of Sports Science & Medicine
**Year:** 2012

## Abstract / study overview

The study examined 27 female volleyball players performing defensive tasks in an **in situ representative task** — a live-action setting preserving the perception-action coupling of actual play — rather than a decoupled video simulation. Researchers combined eye-movement tracking with concurrent verbal reports of thinking to characterize the perceptual-cognitive processes underpinning volleyball defense expertise.

## Key findings

- **Highly-skilled players** demonstrated more exploratory visual search, directing more fixations to a greater number of display locations than their less-skilled counterparts.
- Expert players allocated more attention to **functional spaces between display areas** (i.e., not only on the ball or hitter, but on relational information between players, setter, and attackers).
- Highly-skilled participants produced substantially more **"condition concepts"** (observations about situational constraints) during verbal reports, and at higher levels of sophistication.
- Experts showed enhanced attunement to **opponent-related information** versus ball-only cues.
- Combining eye-tracking with verbal reports provided complementary views of expertise: superior performance involves both strategic information pickup and deeper situational understanding.

## Key claims / ideas

- Expertise in volleyball defense is perceptual-cognitive, not merely motoric.
- Attunement to between-object / relational affordances (setter–hitter–blocker geometry) distinguishes experts.
- **In situ representative tasks** reveal expert-novice differences that decoupled video tasks may miss, supporting the representative learning design framework.
