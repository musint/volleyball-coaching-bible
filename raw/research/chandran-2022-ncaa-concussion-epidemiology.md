# Epidemiology of Concussions in National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Sports: 2014/15–2018/19

**Authors:** Avinash Chandran, Adrian J. Boltz, Sarah N. Morris, Hannah J. Robison, Aliza K. Nedimyer, Christy L. Collins, Johna K. Register-Mihalik
**Journal:** American Journal of Sports Medicine
**Year:** 2022 (February)

## Abstract / study overview

Five-season analysis of 3,497 sport-related concussions across 23 NCAA sports drawn from over 8.4 million athlete-exposures in the NCAA Injury Surveillance Program (ISP).

## Key findings

- **Overall concussion rate:** 4.13 per 10,000 athlete-exposures across all sports.
- **Highest-risk sports:** men's ice hockey (7.35 / 10,000 AEs) and women's soccer (7.15 / 10,000 AEs).
- **Volleyball trend:** women's volleyball concussion rates increased over the 2015/16–2018/19 window — flagged as a "concerning trajectory" requiring prevention focus.
- **Competition vs practice:** competition rates substantially higher than practice rates across nearly all sports.
- **Mechanism by sex:**
  - Men's sports: 77% of concussions from player-to-player contact.
  - Women's sports: 39.2% from contact with equipment/apparatus (ball, net, post, floor) — distinct mechanism profile from men's.
- **Recurrence:** most concussions were new injuries (84.3% men, 81.1% women); ice hockey had notable recurrence rate.

## Key claims / ideas

- Women's volleyball is one of the rising-concussion-rate sports — coaching staff cannot treat concussion as a football/hockey-only problem.
- Mechanism profile (equipment/floor contact) means coaching attention to floor-defense technique, net-pole padding, and ball-to-head awareness is the right prevention vector — not "tackling" mechanics.
- Implication: adopt or co-develop a concussion protocol with athletic training staff before any season, not after the first incident.
