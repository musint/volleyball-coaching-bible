# Effects of Self-Talk Training on Competitive Anxiety, Self-Efficacy, Volitional Skills, and Performance: An Intervention Study with Junior Sub-Elite Athletes

**Authors:** Nadja Walter, Lucie Nikoleizig, Dorothee Alfermann
**Journal:** Sports (Basel)
**Year:** 2019

## Abstract / study overview

A randomized controlled intervention study examining how **self-talk (ST) training** affects junior athletes (ages 13-23, N=117) over different durations. Athletes were assigned to a one-week ST intervention (3 sessions), an eight-week ST intervention (3 sessions per week), or a no-training control. Outcome measures (state anxiety, self-confidence, self-efficacy, volitional skills, coach-rated performance) were collected at three points: pre-intervention, post-intervention, and 5-6 weeks follow-up.

## Key findings

- **Anxiety reduction:** ST training reduced somatic state anxiety and increased state self-confidence, especially with longer-duration training.
- **Self-efficacy:** Both intervention groups improved post-training; only the eight-week group sustained the gain at follow-up.
- **Volitional skills:** Self-optimization (goal-setting/self-regulation) significantly improved only in the eight-week group.
- **Coach-rated performance:** Eight-week group rated as performing measurably better throughout study; one-week and control groups did not improve.
- **Duration matters:** Long-term ST training was substantially more effective than short-term across virtually every outcome.
- Practical implication: a one-shot pre-match self-talk script does not produce durable change. Sustained 8+ week practice does.

## Key claims / ideas

- Self-talk is a trainable skill, not a one-time tool.
- Longer-duration ST training produces durable performance and psychological benefits, while short-duration ST training produces transient effects only.
- The intervention's effect on coach-rated performance (not just self-report) addresses a common methodological criticism of ST research.
- Useful generalization to volleyball: serve-routine self-talk should be embedded in 8+ week training blocks, not introduced match-week.
