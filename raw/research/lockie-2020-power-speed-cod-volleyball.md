# Lockie et al. (2020) — Lower-body power, sprint, and change-of-direction in NCAA D-I women's volleyball

**Citation (APA).** Lockie, R. G., Dawes, J. J., & Callaghan, S. J. (2020). Lower-body power, linear speed, and change-of-direction speed in Division I collegiate women's volleyball players. *Biology of Sport*, 38(2), 195–202. https://doi.org/10.5114/biolsport.2020.96944

## Abstract (summary)

Cross-sectional study of NCAA D-I women's volleyball players quantifying correlations between vertical and horizontal lower-body power tests and linear-speed / change-of-direction (COD) performance.

## Sample / methods

- **Sample.** 15 NCAA Division I collegiate women's volleyball players (height 178.13 ± 8.96 cm; mass 70.18 ± 7.58 kg).
- **Battery.** Vertical jump, two-step approach jump, standing broad jump, 20-m sprint (10-m and 20-m splits), pro-agility shuttle.

## Key findings

- **Vertical-jump and power-to-body-mass correlate with 20-m sprint** (r = −0.534 to −0.557) — vertical jump predicts longer-distance sprint speed.
- **Standing broad jump and relative broad jump correlate strongly with pro-agility shuttle** (r = −0.589 to −0.729) — horizontal-power tests are the best predictors of COD.
- **No significant correlations** between any power measure and 10-m acceleration — early-acceleration mechanics are likely a separate quality.
- **Volleyball-specific note.** Most on-court movements happen in 3–5-m windows; standard 20-m and pro-agility tests may overstate the value of long-distance speed for the sport.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Horizontal jumping is the highest-leverage power target for COD** — broad jumps and bounding belong in any volleyball strength program prioritizing court agility.
2. **10-m acceleration is its own quality** — vertical jump won't carry it; sprint-mechanics work and short-distance sprint exposure are needed.
3. **Train COD specifically** — vertical-jump volume alone won't maximize court agility.
4. **Test design matters** — coaches using a 5-m shuttle or pro-agility get better volleyball-relevant signal than from 40-yard / 20-m sprint times.
