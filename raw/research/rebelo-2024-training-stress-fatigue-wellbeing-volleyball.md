# Rebelo et al. (2024) — Training stress, neuromuscular fatigue, and well-being in volleyball

**Citation (APA).** Rebelo, A., Pereira, J. R., Cunha, P., Coelho-e-Silva, M. J., & Valente-dos-Santos, J. (2024). Training stress, neuromuscular fatigue and well-being in volleyball: a systematic review. *BMC Sports Science, Medicine and Rehabilitation*, 16, 14. https://doi.org/10.1186/s13102-024-00807-7

## Abstract (summary)

PRISMA-compliant systematic review of monitoring strategies for training load, neuromuscular fatigue, and well-being in competitive volleyball. Synthesizes the evidence base for what to monitor, how often, and how to interpret signals during the season.

## Methods

- **Databases.** PsycINFO, MEDLINE/PubMed, SPORTDiscus, Web of Science, Scopus through March 2023.
- **Yield.** 2,535 records → 31 articles included.
- **Populations.** 22 professional / 7 collegiate / 2 youth studies; 22 male / 9 female samples.

## Key findings

- **sRPE dominates internal-load monitoring** — used in 77% of included studies as the daily quantification method.
- **IMUs / accelerometry growing** — used in 16% of studies for external load (jump count, distance, accelerations).
- **CMJ is the workhorse fatigue test** — most-utilized neuromuscular assessment across the literature.
- **Wave-distribution patterns appear in-season** — training load oscillates across weekly cycles rather than running flat.
- **Pre-season pattern: progressive load → performance decrement** — coaches should plan for transient reductions during the build phase.
- **Middle blockers carry significantly higher jump counts and internal loads** than other positions across multiple studies.
- **Hooper Index sensitivity** — daily wellness questionnaires capture training stress, sleep, soreness, and mood reliably.
- **Avoid HR-based intensity prescription in volleyball** — the sport's anaerobic profile makes HR a poor load proxy.

## Recommended monitoring stack (paper's bottom line)

1. **Daily internal load:** sRPE after every session and match.
2. **Daily external load:** jump height, jump count, kinetic energy via wearable or video.
3. **Weekly neuromuscular check:** CMJ via force platform (FT:CT ratio) or, if no platform, averaged jump height.
4. **Daily wellness:** Hooper Index — fatigue, stress, soreness, mood, sleep duration.
5. **Sleep target:** ≥ 7 hours nightly; lower correlates with injury risk.
6. **Travel / congestion buffers:** schedule active recovery, hydration, and routine maintenance during high-density windows.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Monitoring is now a Tier-1 best practice** — peer-reviewed recommendation set, not anecdote.
2. **Position-aware load planning is mandatory** — middles are systematically over-loaded by uniform programming.
3. **Wave the in-season load deliberately** — concentrate harder weeks at lower-priority blocks of the schedule and pull back before key matches.
4. **The CMJ is the field-test gold standard** for fatigue tracking when force plates are out of reach for HS / club coaches.
