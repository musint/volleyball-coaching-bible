# Optimising long-term athletic development: An investigation of practitioners' knowledge, adherence, practices and challenges

**Authors:** Kevin Till, Rhodri S. Lloyd, Sam McCormack, Graham Williams, Joseph Baker, Joey C. Eisenmann
**Journal:** PLoS One
**Year:** 2022

## Abstract / study overview

Mixed-methods survey of 236 practitioners (sport coaches, PE teachers, S&C coaches, administrators) examining knowledge of and adherence to long-term athletic development (LTAD) frameworks, especially the NSCA's "10 Pillars of Successful LTAD."

## Key findings

- **Definition gaps:** only 2.7% of practitioners incorporated all four required components of "athleticism" (movement, physical qualities, psychological elements, multiple environments) — most operate with incomplete definitions of what they're developing.
- **Pillar adherence — strong:** Pillar 5 (health and wellbeing) — high self-reported adherence.
- **Pillar adherence — weak:**
  - Pillar 1: accommodating individualized, non-linear development (low).
  - Pillar 8: monitoring and assessment tools (low).
  - Pillar 9: systematic progression and individualization (low).
- **Model awareness:** Bayli/Balyi LTAD model best-known; NSCA 10-Pillars framework less recognized despite being the more current/research-grounded framework.
- **Eight implementation challenges identified:** governance conflicts, resource limitations, insufficient education, early-specialization pressures, excessive training volumes, poor staff communication, parental misconceptions, youth motivation issues.

## Key claims / ideas

- LTAD-aware practitioners overestimate their own adherence — particularly weak on individualization, monitoring/assessment, and systematic progression.
- Early specialization pressure and excessive training volumes are practitioner-identified problems, not abstract concerns from research.
- Implication for club programs: regular maturity-status screening, individualized loading, and explicit progression frameworks (rather than calendar-age progression) are the underused levers.
