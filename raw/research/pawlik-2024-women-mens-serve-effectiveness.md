# Pawlik, Bobula & Mroczek 2024 — Polish Tauron Liga Serve Census

## Citation

Pawlik D, Bobula G, Mroczek D. The effectiveness and types of serves used in elite women's and men's volleyball in the 2021/2022 season. *Scientific Reports*. 2024;14:17286. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-68262-5

Open access via PubMed Central: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11283524/

## Sample & methods

- **Total:** 314 matches, 53,513 serves analyzed.
- **Women's leg:** 132 matches, 244 sets, 22,378 serves — Tauron Liga (Polish women's top professional league), 2021/22 season.
- **Men's leg:** 182 matches, 348 sets — PlusLiga (Polish men's top professional league).
- **Statistics:** One-way ANOVA, Bonferroni post-hoc tests, Spearman correlations; α=0.05; 95% CIs reported; ICC for reliability.

## Headline findings — women

**Serve-type distribution (women's elite pro):**
- Jump-float (JF): **86%**
- Jump-serve / jump-topspin (JS): **12%**
- Standing / spot (W): **2%**

**Serve-effectiveness index (women): 22%**
- Aces: 5%
- Positive (+) outcomes: 22%
- Negative outcomes: 49%
- Errors: 12%
- Ace-to-error ratio: **0.498**

## Headline findings — men (for contrast)

**Serve-type distribution (men's elite pro):**
- Jump-serve / jump-topspin (JS): 67% (dominant)
- Jump-float (JF): ~25%
- Fake jump-float / fake jump-serve: small percentages
- Standing: <1%

**Serve-effectiveness index (men): 15%**
- Errors: 21% (notably higher than women's 12%)
- Ace-to-error ratio: 0.358 (lower than women's 0.498)

## Coach-relevant interpretation

The women's pro game has consolidated around the jump-float as the workhorse (86%) with jump-topspin as a deliberate change-up (12%). Men's pro inverts this — jump-topspin dominant (67%), jump-float secondary. Women's overall serve effectiveness exceeds men's (22% vs 15%) because women's serve game trades peak ball velocity for substantially lower error rates (12% vs 21%) and a better ace-to-error ratio (0.498 vs 0.358). The structural gender split is on a full league season of pro data, not a small-sample tournament snippet.

## Caveats

- **Generalization to NT-level play untested in this paper.** The data is Tauron Liga (Polish pro), likely generalizable to FIVB women's pro level but not formally validated against USA NCAA D1 or Olympic / Worlds / VNL data.
- **No direct ace-rate-by-serve-type decomposition for women separately** in the abstract — the 5% ace rate is overall women's. Sub-decomposition by serve type may be in the full text but isn't extracted in the public summary.
- **2021/22 season may underrepresent the most recent shifts** (e.g., 2024 World Cup tactical patterns).
