# Within-week differences in external training load demands in elite volleyball players

**Citation (APA).** Akyildiz, Z., de Oliveira Castro, H., Çene, E., Laporta, L., Parim, C., Altundag, E., Akarçesme, C., Guidetti, G., Miale, G., Silva, A. F., Nobari, H., & Clemente, F. M. (2022). Within-week differences in external training load demands in elite volleyball players. *BMC Sports Science, Medicine and Rehabilitation*, 14, 188. https://doi.org/10.1186/s13102-022-00568-1

**Author affiliations (relevant).** Giovanni Guidetti is listed with affiliation **"Vakıfbank Women's Volleyball Team, Istanbul, Turkey."** Several co-authors (Akyildiz, Çene, Parim, Altundag, Akarçesme) are Turkish sport-science and statistics researchers; Clemente, Silva, and Nobari are international collaborators in sport-science training-load research.

## Abstract (summary)

Peer-reviewed, open-access study analyzing within-week (microcycle) differences in external training intensity in elite women's volleyball, segmented by playing position. Uses Local Positioning System (LPS) technology across a full competitive season.

## Sample / setting

- **Team.** 14 elite female volleyball players from a "world champion club team" competing in the Turkish Volleyball Federation league and the CEV Champions League. The affiliation line plus the season match counts (see below) identify the team as **VakıfBank Istanbul**, with Guidetti as head coach and co-author.
- **Season.** 2020–2021: 10 friendly matches, 41 league matches, 11 CEV Champions League matches.
- **Positions measured.** Left hitter (outside), right hitter (opposite), middle blocker, setter, libero.

## Methods

- **Technology.** KINEXON LPS system, 12 antennas, millimeter-accurate positioning.
- **External-load metrics tracked per player per session.**
  - Total distance covered
  - Jump count
  - Accelerations and decelerations
  - High metabolic load distance (HMLD)
  - Workload
  - Acute load, chronic load, acute:chronic workload ratio
  - Monotony
  - Strain
- **Analysis design.** Days segmented relative to match day (MD-4, MD-3, MD-2, MD-1) and compared across positions. Moderate-to-large effect-size comparisons reported.

## Key findings

- Training intensity systematically **decreases approaching match day** across nearly all variables, consistent with a tapered microcycle.
- **Significant differences with moderate-to-large effect sizes** in load by day relative to match day.
- **Middle blockers show notably lower workload values compared to other positions** throughout all microcycles — implying that the outsides, opposite, and libero carry higher session load than middles at VakıfBank, and that periodization must respect these position-specific demands.
- "A high jump count during a training session may lead to the feeling of fatigue and a reduction in jump height" — practical implication for jump-count monitoring at elite volleyball clubs.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Direct evidence of sports-science integration at VakıfBank.** VakıfBank is instrumented with KINEXON LPS; per-player external load (distance, jumps, accelerations, HMLD, workload, strain) is tracked across sessions and match days. Guidetti is a co-author on the paper, establishing that this data-driven periodization is not incidental but coach-sanctioned.
2. **Position-specific periodization.** The study supports a per-position load-management model — specifically that middle blockers are programmed with lower training load than outsides, opposites, and liberos. This is concrete methodology, not folk wisdom.
3. **Elite-club reference.** The paper is a primary peer-reviewed source for how a top-five European women's club structures its weekly microcycles, jump counts, and acute:chronic ratios.
