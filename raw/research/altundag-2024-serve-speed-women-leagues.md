# Altundag, Soylu & Akyildiz 2024 — Turkish League Serve Speed Analysis

## Citation

Altundag E, Soylu C, Akyildiz Z. Multidimensional analysis of serving speed in volleyball players by position, sets, and league types: interactions and statistical differences. *BMC Sports Science, Medicine and Rehabilitation*. 2024;16:240. https://doi.org/10.1186/s13102-024-01031-z

Open access via PubMed Central: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11622488/

## Sample & methods

- **Total serves analyzed:** 15,347 (6,943 female + 8,404 male) across 4 seasons.
- **Player positions covered:** setters, middle blockers, opposites, wing spikers.
- **Female leagues sampled:** Sultans League (top tier), Women's 1st League, Women's 2nd League (Turkey).
- **Male leagues sampled:** corresponding men's tiers.
- **Players:** ≥18 years, ≥2 years of playing experience.
- **Statistics:** ANOVA on three factors (position, set, league type) plus two-way and three-way interactions.

## Headline findings

- **Player position effect:** F = 375.844, p < 0.001 — position significantly influences serve speed.
- **Set effect:** F = 17.624, p < 0.001 — serve speed declines significantly across sets within a match (fatigue effect).
- **League-type effect:** F = 356.914, p < 0.001 — higher-tier leagues serve faster than lower-tier leagues; on the women's side, Sultans League > Women's 1st > Women's 2nd.
- **Two-way interactions significant:** position × set; position × league; set × league.
- **Three-way interaction not significant:** p = 0.179.
- **Highest serve speeds:** Men's 1st League. **Lowest serve speeds:** Women's 2nd League.

## Coach-relevant interpretation

Women's serve speed scales with competition tier — top-tier women serve faster than lower-tier women, in line with what's expected from physical-development and selection effects. The within-match fatigue effect (significant decline across sets) confirms that serve-speed coaching at top-club level should account for late-set fatigue tolerance, not just opening-set peak velocity. Position-specific serve-speed differences are large and significant, supporting position-specific serving curricula (the wiki already differentiates setter vs OH vs MB vs OPP serving expectations).

## Caveats

- The full text doesn't appear to give absolute serve-speed numbers (km/h) by tier in the abstract; specific velocity comparisons require full-text access.
- Turkish-league sample — generalizability to North-American HS / NCAA / club populations untested.
- No direct serve-type effect controlled (athletes likely use a mix of jump-float and jump-topspin; the effect estimates aggregate across types).
