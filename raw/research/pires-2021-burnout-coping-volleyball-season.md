# Burnout and Coping Perceptions of Volleyball Players Throughout an Annual Sport Season

**Authors:** Daniel Alvarez Pires, Herbert Ugrinowitsch
**Journal:** Journal of Human Kinetics
**Year:** 2021

## Abstract / study overview

A longitudinal study tracking 54 professional volleyball athletes (28 men, 26 women; mean age 25.57) across **four measurement points spanning 10 months** of a competitive season. Researchers measured burnout (Athlete Burnout Questionnaire — physical/emotional exhaustion, reduced sense of accomplishment, sport devaluation) and coping skills (Athletic Coping Skills Inventory) at each point and tested how these states evolved across the season.

## Key findings

- **Reduced athletic accomplishment** rose significantly from season start through season end — players feel less effective as the season grinds on.
- **Sport devaluation** rose as competition intensified — meaning was eroded by load, not just performance dips.
- **Physical and emotional exhaustion** stayed relatively stable — burnout's affective dimensions, not just the fatigue dimension, are what shift longitudinally.
- **Coping strategies did not change** across the four measurement points — athletes maintained whatever coping repertoire they started with.
- Confidence and motivation showed **moderate inverse correlation** with burnout dimensions at every measurement point — high confidence/motivation buffered burnout.
- Practical implication: coaches should periodize psychological load, not just physical load. Monitoring confidence/motivation is a leading indicator of burnout risk.

## Key claims / ideas

- Burnout in volleyball is progressive, not episodic, and it specifically erodes meaning/efficacy rather than just adding fatigue.
- Without intervention, players' coping skills do not adapt across a season — fixed coping toolkit meets a rising stress profile.
- Confidence/motivation maintenance is a defensible psychological-monitoring metric for season-long burnout prevention.
- Implication: build motivation-targeted check-ins (mid-season + late-season) into staff rhythm.
