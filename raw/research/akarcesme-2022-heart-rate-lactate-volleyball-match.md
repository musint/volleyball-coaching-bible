# Akarçeşme et al. (2022) — Heart rate and blood lactate during volleyball match

**Citation (APA).** Akarçeşme, C., Cengizel, E., Şenel, Ö., Yıldıran, İ., Akyildiz, Z., & Nobari, H. (2022). Heart rate and blood lactate responses during the volleyball match. *Scientific Reports*, 12, 14831. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-022-19687-3

## Abstract (summary)

Quantifies cardiorespiratory and metabolic load during competitive matches in 24 elite female volleyball players, segmented by playing position (setter / spiker / middle blocker / libero).

## Sample / methods

- **Subjects.** 24 senior elite female players (Turkish Super League / 1st League).
- **Protocol.** 3 friendship matches (3 sets each); continuous telemetric HR; 12 blood-lactate samples per player at defined intervals.
- **Equipment.** Telemetric HR; portable lactate analyzer.

## Key findings — match-mean physiology

- **Whole-sample mean HR:** 117.2 ± 13.9 bpm; lactate 2.7 ± 1.2 mmol/L.

**Heart rate by position (mean during match):**
- Setters: 101.3 ± 5.2 bpm — lowest
- Spikers: 116.9 ± 11.9 bpm
- Middle blockers: 118.8 ± 6.6 bpm
- Liberos: 130.6 ± 9.8 bpm — highest

**Time at high intensity (≥ 80% HRmax):**
- Setters: 1.9%
- Spikers: 8.2%
- Middle blockers: 12.3%
- Liberos: 17.9%

**Lactate range across positions:** 1.5 → 7.0 mmol/L — non-significant between positions but trending highest in liberos and lowest in setters.

**Time at ≤ 60% HRmax:** > 50% of match for all positions — the sport is intermittent, with most time spent recovering between rallies.

## Practical implications for the wiki

1. **Setter ≠ libero conditioning.** Liberos run a near-2× higher HR-load and far more time at high intensity. Position-specific conditioning is supported by physiology, not just convention.
2. **Volleyball is intermittent / mostly low-intensity** — > 50% of match time below 60% HRmax. Aerobic base matters but the priority is repeated-effort capacity (ATP-PCr + glycolytic recovery), not steady-state aerobic.
3. **HR is a reasonable monitor in match play** but Rebelo et al. (2024) caution against using HR for prescription — pair with sRPE.
4. **Lactate alone doesn't separate positions** — internal-load monitoring should rely on sRPE + jump count, not lactate.
