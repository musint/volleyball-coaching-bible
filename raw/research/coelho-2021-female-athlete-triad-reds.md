# The Female Athlete Triad/Relative Energy Deficiency in Sports (RED-S)

**Authors:** Alexandra Ruivo Coelho, Gonçalo Cardoso, Marta Espanhol Brito, Inês Neves Gomes, Maria João Cascais
**Journal:** Revista Brasileira de Ginecologia e Obstetrícia (Rev Bras Ginecol Obstet)
**Year:** 2021

## Abstract / study overview

Comprehensive review of the Female Athlete Triad and its broader successor framework Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S). Covers definition evolution, low-energy-availability physiology, multi-system consequences, screening tools, and treatment.

## Key findings

- **Triad definition (1993/1997 ACSM):** disordered eating + amenorrhea + osteoporosis.
- **RED-S evolution (IOC 2014):** broadened framework — the "low energy availability" (LEA) state has consequences beyond bone and menstrual function: metabolic rate, immunity, protein synthesis, GI, cardiovascular, growth, and psychological systems all affected.
- **Energy availability formula:** EA = (energy intake − exercise energy expenditure) / fat-free mass [kcal/kg FFM/day]. Threshold for LEA generally <30 kcal/kg FFM/day.
- **Performance impact:** reduced endurance, strength, training response, coordination, judgment, and increased injury risk — i.e., LEA harms performance, not just health.
- **Screening:** IOC RED-S Clinical Assessment Tool (CAT) recommended for annual athlete pre-participation screens; clinicians look for menstrual irregularity, stress fractures, weight loss, mood change, restrictive eating patterns.
- **Treatment:** nutritional rehabilitation (300–600 kcal/day above current intake) + interdisciplinary care (physician, RD, PT, psychologist) is first-line. Hormonal therapy (OCPs) provides limited bone protection; not a substitute for restoring EA.

## Key claims / ideas

- LEA is a system-wide problem, not a "thin athlete" cosmetic concern; it costs performance and health.
- Volleyball is a leanness-pressured sport where awareness is a coaching responsibility — coaches see meal patterns and practice intensity that nutritionists never see.
- Implication for staff: pre-participation screening with RED-S CAT, athletic-trainer-led nutrition fueling baseline education, and a referral pathway should be standard at HS/college programs.
